# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea to Create Over 160 Jobs, Invest $72M in Henry County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-06-22/gov-kemp-ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-create-over-160-jobs-invest-72m)  ·  `page_028964`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on June 22, 2023, that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs and bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This announcement marks the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced during his week-long trip to the Paris Air Show.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia's reliable infrastructure network and great workforce enable job creators to find success in every community.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp highlighted the incredible growth of Georgia's e-mobility and battery industries as a testament to the state's ability to support large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan, South Korea, and was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts, which it currently produces in Korea, Europe, and India.
- NVH Korea already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new Locust Grove facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States for manufacturers including Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia was chosen as the company's first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Cooperation between state agencies and NVH Korea staff helped guide the project from selection through planning.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Ja Kyum Koo noted that this project is the first step in NVH Korea's effort to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry.
- Mayor Robert Price of Locust Grove stated that NVH's investment will increase the city's manufacturing jobs base and integrate it into Georgia's electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, stated that the announcement will add quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and diversify the local economy.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that NVH Korea is establishing its first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that manufacturers creating jobs of the future are driving an economic transformation across Georgia's communities.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has attracted more than $22.7 billion in investments and created over 28,400 jobs along the electric vehicle supply chain.
