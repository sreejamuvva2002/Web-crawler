# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-25

**Source:** [Hyundai gets $1.8B in aid to build electric cars in Georgia | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2022/07/25/hyundai-gets-18b-in-aid-build-electric-cars-in-georgia)  ·  `page_009406`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia and local governments are giving $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to Hyundai Motor Group in exchange for the automaker building its first U.S. plant dedicated to electric vehicles near Savannah.
- The deal calls for Hyundai to invest $5.5 billion in its Georgia plant and hire 8,100 workers.
- The deal is the largest economic development deal in Georgia's history.
- The deal comes just months after Georgia closed another major deal with electric vehicle maker Rivian to build a factory in the state.
- State economic development commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the generational projects solidify Georgia's spot at the vanguard of the EV transition and ensure thousands of Georgians will benefit from future jobs.
- Hyundai executives and Gov. Brian Kemp announced the deal in May with a champagne toast at the project's 2,900-acre (1,170 hectare) site in Bryan County, west of Savannah.
- Hyundai plans to start construction on the plant next year and begin producing up to 300,000 vehicles per year in 2025.
- The new factory will also produce vehicle batteries.
- Officials declined to reveal the specific incentives promised to Hyundai until after the agreement was signed.
- The incentive package is worth roughly $300 million more than incentives previously promised to Rivian.
- The package amounts to Georgia and four counties in the Savannah area giving Hyundai about $228,000 per job created.
- Georgia officials insist the incentive package is a worthwhile investment.
- Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai's payroll at the new plant is expected to reach $4.7 billion over 10 years.
- Parts suppliers are expected to create thousands of additional jobs in Georgia.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, stated that the $1.8 billion in incentives is easily the largest subsidy package a U.S. state has ever promised for an automotive plant.
- Greg LeRoy characterized the deal as inherently super-risky because it bets a huge amount on one company and one facility.
- Local governments are giving Hyundai more than $472 million in property tax breaks.
- Hyundai will pay more than $357 million in lieu of taxes over a 26-year period starting in 2023.
- Hyundai will receive more than $212 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $5,250 per job over five years.
- If Hyundai did not owe enough state corporate income tax to cover the credits, Georgia would instead give the company personal income taxes collected from Hyundai workers.
- The state and local governments spent $86 million to purchase the plant site.
- The state will spend $200 million on road construction and improvements.
- The state will spend an additional $50 million to help fund construction, machinery, and equipment.
- Sales tax exemptions on construction materials and machinery expenses are estimated to cost $396 million.
- The deal requires Hyundai to pay back a portion of the incentives if the company falls below 80% of its promised investment or employment.
- Kia, a subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group, received more than $450 million in incentives for its plant in West Point, southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia has promised SK Innovation $300 million in incentives for a $2.6 billion, 2,600-worker battery plant being built northeast of Atlanta.
