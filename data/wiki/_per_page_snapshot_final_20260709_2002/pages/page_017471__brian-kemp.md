# Brian Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-17

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp to Visit Germany, Poland on Business Mission  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/gov-kemp-to-visit-germany-poland-on-business-mission/)  ·  `page_017471`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp is set to visit Germany and Poland next week on a weeklong business mission aimed at shoring up relationships with key partners and driving new investment relationships.
- Mr. Kemp recently outlined his legislative and budgetary priorities for 2025 in local appearances and the annual State of the State address, which focused on lowering taxes, boosting infrastructure spending, and achieving tort reform.
- The governor emphasized the importance of international partnerships to job-creation efforts across the state.
- Mr. Kemp stated that over the last six years, international companies have invested over $38 billion in Georgia, creating more than 58,000 new opportunities for Georgians in every corner of the state, with communities outside of metro Atlanta attracting over 80 percent of those jobs.
- The visit occurs as Germany faces a political crisis with snap elections scheduled for Feb. 23 and announced that 2024 brought a second straight year of recession.
- Despite struggles in Europe's largest economy and public issues with auto-makers like Volkswagen that have large plants in the Southeast, Georgia remains committed to the German market where it has been present for decades.
- Mr. Kemp said the mission is an opportunity to reinforce collaboration with Germany while opening dialogues with emerging markets to ensure a brighter future for Georgians as the state prepares to return money to taxpayers and invest in public safety and education.
- Automotive suppliers from Germany have invested more than $830 million in Georgia over the last two decades, according to the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Manufacturers like Volkswagen and BMW have set up shop in the region.
- Atlanta is home to the North American headquarters of both Porsche and Mercedes-Benz.
- Georgia’s European office was opened in 1973 by then-governor Jimmy Carter and is now located in Munich.
- This trip will be at least the third visit to Germany for Mr. Kemp.
- On a previous visit, Mr. Kemp visited Porsche in Stuttgart to see production of the all-electric Taycan.
- More recently, he traveled to Hamburg to view smelting operations of Aurubis, which just opened an $800 million smelting operation in Augusta, representing the state’s largest German investment project ever.
- At the grand opening, Mr. Kemp called Aurubis “one of my favorite projects ever,” noting it exemplifies the promise of Georgia’s global partnerships.
- Six other German investors are on Mr. Kemp’s public itinerary, and his office stated he will also attend diplomatic and relationship-building opportunities.
- BASF maintains a manufacturing presence in Georgia with operations in Attapulgus, Edgar, and Savannah.
- Gerresheimer established a production facility in Peachtree City in 1993 and expanded in 2009 with a Technical Competence Center (TCC), announcing additional expansions in December 2023 and May 2024.
- Carcoustics established Carcoustics USA South Inc. in Buford in 2017, including a central research center and manufacturing presence.
- KIRCHHOFF Automotive opened an assembly facility in Lawrenceville in 2023, set to create 73 jobs.
- GEDIA Automotive Group announced an advanced manufacturing facility in the greater Dalton area in 2020 that will begin production focusing on parts for electric vehicles.
- KIRCHHOFF Automotive and GEDIA Automotive Group are based in North Rhine-Westphalia, the only German state with a trade office in Georgia.
- The press release did not specify which firms the governor plans to visit in Poland, though a defense element is possible as Poland's trade with Georgia increased from $118 million five years ago to $1.2 billion in 2023 following delivery of Marietta-made Lockheed Martin C-130J aircraft.
- On previous overseas trips, Mr. Kemp has visited National Guard troops.
- Following the governor's return, the community will have two opportunities to hear from German Consul General Melanie Moltmann about Germany’s political and economic situation.
- Mr. Kemp attended his second appearance at the World Economic Forum’s annual conference in Davos, Switzerland, last January, with the event scheduled again for next week.
- Following that trip, he traveled to Switzerland to reinforce relationships with Swiss investors, accompanied by members of the Georgia team.
- The economic development department will accompany the governor on the Germany mission.
