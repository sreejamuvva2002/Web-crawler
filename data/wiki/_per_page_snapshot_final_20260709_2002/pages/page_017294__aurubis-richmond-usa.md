# Aurubis Richmond, USA

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-10

**Source:** [Aurubis AG: Aurubis builds recycling plant in the US and sets its sights on sustainable growth](https://menafn.com/1103142918/Aurubis-AG-Aurubis-builds-recycling-plant-in-the-US-and-sets-its-sights-on-sustainable-growth)  ·  `page_017294`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG is building the first secondary smelter for multimetal recycling in the United States in Augusta (Richmond County), Georgia.
- Construction of the new site, named "Aurubis Richmond, USA," will start in summer 2022.
- Commissioning of the plant is planned for the first half of 2024.
- Aurubis is investing about € 300 million in the facility.
- The plant will have a capacity for processing up to 90,000 t of complex recycling materials.
- It will produce 35,000 t of blister copper annually from circuit boards, copper cable, and other recycling materials containing metals.
- Aurubis will further process the intermediate products into various industrial and precious metals at its European smelter sites to a great extent, but also sell them directly in the US market.
- The facility is expected to generate an annual contribution to earnings of approx. € 80 million EBITDA starting fiscal year 2025/26 at full production capacity.
- CEO Roland Harings stated the investment sets a clear example of sustainable growth and positions Aurubis as a forerunner for multimetal recycling in the US.
- Supervisory Board Chairman Prof. Dr. Fritz Vahrenholt explained the US is a highly attractive recycling growth market with a lot of potential and competitive energy prices.
- The investment will directly create more than 100 jobs in the region.
- Aurubis signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the relevant US governmental authorities regarding economic support measures.
- The new site is located in a strategically advantageous location near the Port of Savannah and key economic regions of the US.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed Aurubis to Georgia, noting it is one of the largest German investments in the state.
- A rising sustainability awareness and significant increase in collection volumes lead to about 6 million t of recyclable, metal-bearing materials in the US annually.
- Expert estimates project the recycling volume in North America will increase by an average of 5 % annually in the next several years.
- Aurubis' objective is for its production to be carbon-neutral well before 2050 as part of its support for the European Green Deal and American Green New Deal plans.
- The recycling technology enables complex metal-bearing recycling materials to be processed and returned to the material cycle, supporting natural resource conservation.
- Aurubis' metals are crucial for global megatrends including digitalization, renewable energies, electric vehicles, and battery recycling.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into metals of the highest quality.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually, along with wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, flat rolled products made of copper and copper alloys, precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, zinc, sulfuric acid, and iron silicate.
- Aurubis has about 7,200 employees globally.
- The company has production sites in Europe and the US, and an extensive service and distribution system in Europe, Asia, and North America.
