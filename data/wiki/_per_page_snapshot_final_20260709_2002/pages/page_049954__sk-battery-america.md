# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-25

**Source:** [SK Battery pays $31M to family-owned business after ‘catastrophic’ fire in Commerce - Now Georgia](https://nowgeorgia.com/sk-battery-pays-31m-to-family-owned-business-after-catastrophic-fire-in-commerce-2/)  ·  `page_049954`

## Facts

- SK Battery America paid $31 million to Commerce-based recycling center Metro Site Inc. in late September as a settlement for a fire that destroyed the family-owned business's facility.
- The settlement was reached just before the case was set to go before a Jackson County jury.
- SK Battery spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated that SK Battery America added an extra layer of oversight to the disposal of unused material at the site more than a year ago.
- Collier noted that the agreement provides a generous settlement for Metro Site to rebuild and relaunch its business, and questioned why Metro Site continued arguing the case after agreeing to settle a month prior.
- Attorneys for Metro Site Inc. alleged that SK Battery America disguised more than 600 explosive, hazardous lithium-ion batteries as regular recyclable material inside a roll-off dumpster shipped to their non-hazardous recycling facility in Commerce.
- The delivery of the dumpster allegedly triggered a chemical reaction known as thermal runaway, causing a catastrophic fire at the Banks County facility.
- According to the lawsuit, the fire burned for almost a week before flames were extinguished, emitting toxic fumes for miles and depleting Commerce’s water reserves to unsafe levels.
- State Senator Bo Hatchett reported that Banks County used more than 3 million gallons of water over five days to extinguish the blaze.
- Plaintiff attorneys claimed this was allegedly the second time SK Battery tampered with evidence, citing a 2021 case where an International Trade Court judge determined SK systematically destroyed evidence in a patent dispute with LG Energy Solution, resulting in a settlement of over $1 billion.
- Collier disputed the evidence tampering claim, stating the referenced previous case was filed in 2019 before SK Battery America started manufacturing operations.
- Metro Site attorneys questioned the millions of dollars in tax breaks SK received to build its Commerce facility, with the lawsuit specifically citing more than $300 million in tax breaks for the Commerce-based gigafactory.
- Plaintiff attorneys cited 911 service call records showing an exorbitant number of internal fires at the gigafactory as evidence of a pattern of issues.
- Collier countered that the cited fire events occurred over three years, with many triggered only by smoke alarms without actual fires, and none resulted in significant damage or burn injuries to employees.
- SK Battery America established an on-site emergency response team in 2021 that is staffed around the clock by trained firefighters using sophisticated equipment, including a company-owned fire truck.
- A second lawsuit regarding reimbursement for resources expended during the blaze remains pending between SK Battery and Banks County government.
- Banks County retained the McDonald & Cody law firm for legal representation in the pending case, which was transferred to federal court before a motion was filed to move future proceedings to Jackson County Superior Court.
- Hatchett stated that during the fire, county officials had to monitor water going into a nearby treatment facility and evacuate a fire station due to extraordinary measures taken to protect government property.
- SK Battery is known as one of the largest employers in Jackson and Banks counties.
- Collier emphasized that SK Battery America maintains compliance with safety guidelines and has stringent procedures for shipping and disposing of production-related material.
