# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-02

**Source:** [Stellantis Invests Additional $55 Million In Archer Following Recent Flight Test Milestone | Corporate | Stellantis Media](https://www.media.stellantis.com/em-en/corporate/press/stellantis-invests-additional-55-million-in-archer-following-recent-flight-test-milestone)  ·  `page_016811`

## Facts

- Stellantis invested an additional $55 million in Archer following its achievement of a transition flight test milestone last month.
- This latest investment builds on $39 million worth of open market purchases of Archer stock completed by Stellantis earlier this year (March 2024).
- During 2023, Stellantis invested $110 million in Archer through a combination of open market stock purchases and investments under their strategic funding agreement.
- Construction is nearing completion on Archer’s high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA.
- The Georgia facility is on track to be completed later this year (2024).
- The first phase of the build-out consists of a ~350,000 square foot facility located on an ~100 acre site.
- The facility is designed to support production of up to 650 aircraft annually.
- This production capacity would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aircraft industry.
- Archer plans to leverage Stellantis as its contract manufacturer to support its planned commercial ramp-up.
- Carlos Tavares, CEO of Stellantis, commended the innovation and hard work of engineering and manufacturing teams from both companies.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO of Archer, cited Stellantis’s commitment in providing manufacturing expertise and capital to accelerate Archer’s business objectives.
- Stellantis has been a strategic partner to Archer since 2020 through various collaboration initiatives.
- Stellantis has served as an investor in Archer since 2021.
- Archer has utilized Stellantis’s manufacturing, supply chain, and design expertise for designing, developing, and commercializing its eVTOL aircraft.
- Archer aims to replace 60–90 minute car commutes with estimated 10–20 minute electric air taxi flights.
- The planned electric air taxi flights are intended to be safe, sustainable, low noise, and cost-competitive with ground transportation.
- Archer’s Midnight aircraft is a piloted, four-passenger aircraft designed for rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time between flights.
