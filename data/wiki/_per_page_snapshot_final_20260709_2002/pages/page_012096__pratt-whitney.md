# Pratt & Whitney

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-26

**Source:** [Pratt & Whitney to Invest $200M in Georgia Engine Manufacturing Site](https://thedefensepost.com/2026/02/26/pratt-whitney-manufacturing-site-expansion/)  ·  `page_012096`

## Facts

- RTX subsidiary Pratt & Whitney plans to invest $200 million to expand its Columbus, Georgia, manufacturing site.
- The investment will add new equipment to boost output for both military and commercial engines.
- The investment will fund a seventh isothermal forging press.
- The seventh isothermal forging press is expected online in 2028.
- The press will produce critical rotating compressor and turbine disks.
- The press could increase production of these parts by approximately 30 percent.
- The expanded production will support the geared turbofan (GTF) family and the F135 fighter engine, among others.
- This expansion follows a $70 million project at the adjacent Columbus Engine Center.
- The previous project added 81,000 square feet (7,525 square meters) for maintenance, repair, and overhaul.
- The previous project raised GTF engine service capacity by 25 percent.
- The Columbus site is located roughly 90 miles south of Atlanta.
- The site supports both defense and commercial programs.
- The site maintains GTF engines for the Airbus A320neo, A220, and Embraer E-Jets E2.
- The site services F117 and F100 engines used in the C-17, F-15, and F-16.
- Columbus Forge produces compressor airfoils and turbine disks for multiple engine programs, including the F135.
- The F135 engine powers the F-35 Lightning II.
- The expansion comes amid growing demand for narrowbody aircraft and sustainment services.
- To date, over 2,600 GTF-powered aircraft have been delivered globally.
- More than 1,300 F135 engines have been produced for international F-35 operators.
- The Columbus site employs more than 2,600 workers.
- The Columbus site has received over $1 billion in cumulative investment since 2008.
