# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: DAS Corp. to Bring 300 Automotive Jobs to Metter - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/654160593/gov-kemp-das-corp-to-bring-300-automotive-jobs-to-metter)  ·  `page_008286`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that DAS Corp. (DAS) will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter, Georgia.
- The facility will create 300 new jobs and generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- The initiative will involve collaborative indirect investments.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia's logistics assets, including a reliable network of rail lines and highways, connect companies like DAS to key business partners in all corners of the state and to markets across the world.
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987 and specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Sean Kim, Chief Operating Officer at DAS, stated that the strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon facilitated convenient access and played a pivotal role in the decision-making process.
- Sean Kim noted that Metter was deemed an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub thanks to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new DAS manufacturing facility will be located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- The facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Over the course of the next five years, DAS will be recruiting for assembly technicians.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- Metter Mayor Ed Boyd stated that the impact on the community will be transformative, stimulating investment in housing, creating other business opportunities, and improving the social economic wellbeing of citizens.
- Glyn Thrift, Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners, thanked DAS for choosing Candler County as its new home and stated they will provide a boost to spur more economic development.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated the authority is proud to support the growing automotive manufacturing presence in the state by driving economic activity and jobs.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented the department on this competitive project in partnership with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is well-positioned for the arrival of Hyundai’s supplier network as a result of years of consistent investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that DAS is another exciting addition to Georgia’s automotive community.
- Over the past century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DAS Corp. maintains a strong focus on innovation and quality to consistently provide premier automotive seating solutions tailored to evolving customer needs.
- Strategic collaborations, including partnerships with Hyundai Motor Group and Kia Motors, continue to drive progress within the constantly evolving automotive industry for DAS Corp.
- DAS Corp. garnered a total revenue of approximately $900 million USD during the fiscal year of 2022.
