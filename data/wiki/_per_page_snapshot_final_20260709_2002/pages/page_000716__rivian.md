# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Rivian approved for $5 billion manufacturing plant in Georgia](https://www.teslarati.com/rivian-approved-5-billion-plant-georgia/)  ·  `page_000716`

## Facts

- Rivian has gained approval to start construction on a new manufacturing plant outside of Atlanta, Georgia.
- The final approval for the project was granted by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDECD) and the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton counties.
- State officials announced the approval on Thursday via WSB-TV.
- The approval grants Rivian long-term rights to construct an automotive plant in the Stanton Springs industrial area east of Atlanta.
- Plans for the facility were first set in motion in 2021.
- The JDA and GDECD described closing and issuing bonds for the project as a generational opportunity for residents of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties.
- The factory is expected to manufacture Rivian’s R2 next-generation platform for future vehicles.
- The site is located near the I-20 corridor to Atlanta’s east, near the historic Walton County community Social Circle and touching on Morgan County.
- This facility will be Rivian’s second manufacturing plant, following its first factory in Normal, Illinois, which produces the R1T pickup and the R1S SUV.
- Rivian previously gained approval for its Economic Development Agreement (EDA) for the factory earlier this year.
- The new approval officially allows the automaker to begin construction.
- Shortly after Rivian’s initial 2021 commitment for the Stanton Springs North site, Georgia approved a $43 million EV battery recycling plant nearby.
- The Rivian location encompasses just under 2,000 acres at a site previously known as the East Atlanta Megasite.
- The facility is expected to create up to 7,500 jobs in the area.
- Rivian will invest $5 billion into building the facility.
- Rivian is targeting 2026 for initial production at the plant.
- The company eventually expects to reach an annual volume production of around 400,000 vehicles.
- Rivian is expected to accelerate charging station deployment in nearby shopping centers, state parks and near its Ponce City Market showroom.
- The production facility has prompted Georgia lawmakers to consider changes to direct sales laws that currently ban automobile sales outside of dealerships.
- Tesla holds an exemption from the direct sales ban but is limited to five statewide sales sites as of last year.
- Rivian is introducing a 24-month apprenticeship program selecting local students to support the Stanton Springs North facility.
- Georgia HOPE Grants will provide tuition for the apprenticeship, while Rivian plans to fund additional expenses including textbooks and other learning materials.
