# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-30

**Source:** [Economic Development Around The State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/11/30/economic-development-around-the-state-68/)  ·  `page_013468`

## Facts

- Georgia was named the No. 1 state for business by Area Development magazine, marking its ninth consecutive year receiving this recognition and making it the only state to do so.
- Georgia earned the top ranking in seven categories from Area Development magazine, including for its Quick Start workforce development program.
- Chicago-based NewCold Advanced Cold Storage Logistics is investing $333 million to build a temperature-controlled warehousing and distribution facility in Henry County.
- The NewCold Advanced Cold Storage Logistics investment in Henry County is the largest made by a single business in the county’s history and will create 170 new jobs.
- Belgium-based Solvay Specialty Polymers received a $178 million federal grant to build a companion facility to its existing plant in Augusta.
- The new Solvay facility in Augusta will produce battery components for electric vehicles.
- The Solvay Augusta project will create up to 500 construction jobs and 100 manufacturing jobs.
- New York-based Wheels Up Experience Inc., one of the largest private aviation companies in the country, is building a 34,000-square-foot operation center in Atlanta.
- The Wheels Up facility will bring 350 jobs to Atlanta.
- The Wheels Up facility is scheduled to open by spring 2023.
- Iowa-based animal proteins producer Essentia Protein Solutions is investing $100 million to open a broth factory in Dalton.
- The Essentia Protein Solutions facility will create 80 jobs.
- Clean Space recently opened a 60,000-square-foot manufacturing plant and warehouse in Flowery Branch.
- The Philadelphia-based Clean Space specializes in climate and humidity control for products like pharmaceuticals and electronics.
- Clean Space will initially employ 25 people at its Flowery Branch facility.
- Convenience store and gas station chain Wawa will open its first location in the state in 2024, with up to 20 more stores planned.
- Taiwanese EV software and charging-station company Noodoe recently broke ground on several charging stations for the city of Peachtree Corners.
- The Noodoe charging station project is the result of Peachtree Corners’ Curiosity Lab innovation center’s partnership with Taiwanese incubator Startup Terrace.
