# Hella Automotive India (HAI)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-04-29

**Source:** [Hella Automotive eyes two-wheeler segment - Auto Components India](https://autocomponentsindia.com/hella-automotive-eyes-two-wheeler-segment/)  ·  `page_055795`

## Facts

- Hella Automotive India (HAI), the Indian arm of German major Hella, has secured a niche in the four-wheeler electronics segment manufacturing auto sensors, actuators and horns.
- HAI is planning to foray into the two-wheeler segment.
- Naveen Gautam, managing director of HAI, said the two-wheeler segment is a big market in India that cannot be ignored anymore.
- Gautam noted that bikes today are linked to lifestyle change and users now aspire for car-like comforts.
- The company plans to capitalize on this changing trend.
- Electronic throttle is one feature HAI could see employed in motorcycles.
- Keys, which are currently mechanical, will see remote start coming up.
- Despite potential in the aftermarket, the company will focus more towards OEM based solutions.
- Hella’s current portfolio includes accelerator pedal sensor, climate sensor, rain and light sensors, head lamp levelling actuator, body actuator, switches and horns.
- HAI counts on companies like Tata Motors, Maruti Suzuki, Mahindra Reva, Ashok Leyland, Hyundai, Honda and many more as clients.
- HAI will soon make Body Control Modules (BCM) and remote control keys, which are likely to be launched in a couple of months for an Indian OEM.
- BCM is not intended to reduce manufacturing cost but to improve energy management and make the car more efficient.
- The intelligence of the BCM system will help reduce the complexity of wiring, thus reducing weight of the car and improving heat dissipation.
- Manufacturing BCM may not be the biggest project in India but is surely the most complex one, defining a new era for Hella and its India plant.
- As far as exports are concerned, HAI will firmly keep its focus on horns.
- India is the only place where Hella Automotive manufactures horns for the two-wheeler segment, making them the most exported products from the country.
- The company is planning to introduce electronic horns and multi-tone horns.
- At present, over 30 percent of HAI’s revenue comes from exports.
- Overall, HAI constitutes less than five percent of Hella’s global revenue with plans to reach 10 percent by 2020.
- Hella spends about 12 percent of global sales, roughly Euro 5 billion, on R & D.
- In India, Hella spends little more than 12 percent because it is in the development phase and leads responsibility for some products while offering support for others.
- Apart from a manufacturing plant in Dhankot and corporate office in Gurgaon, Hella has an R&D centre in Pune for electronics and one in Chennai for lighting.
