# Mid-America Trucking Show

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2009-03-30

**Source:** [Foreign Firms Use MATS as Way to Enter U.S. Market - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/foreign-firms-use-mats-way-enter-us-market)  ·  `page_013272`

## Facts

- Companies from China and Taiwan formed the biggest foreign contingent at the Mid-America Trucking Show in Louisville, Ky., for the second year in a row to enter the U.S. market.
- Some Chinese and Taiwanese companies expressed disappointment that their booths attracted fewer visitors than expected, citing the recession or their location in the Kentucky Exhibition Center as possible causes.
- Most Chinese exhibitors had small booths in the exhibit hall's vast West Wing because none came to sell major components or complete vehicles.
- Double Coin Tires is an option on International Trucks and displayed in the main halls.
- CIMC Vehicles Group Co., owner of Vanguard National Trailer Corp., displayed in the main halls where most major U.S. truck industry suppliers were located.
- Peipei Chang, export manager of Cheetah Automotive Products Co. Ltd. in Taichung, Taiwan, attended her company's first year at the show and expressed interest in the U.S. market despite lower-than-expected traffic.
- Yuhui Liu, project manager for truck manufacturer China FAW Group Import and Export Corp., attended MATS for the first time to find distributors and enter the U.S. truck market.
- FAW Group Corp. is a state-owned company that says it is the largest truck manufacturer in China.
- FAW sold 1.2 million vehicles in China in the first nine months of 2008, including 142,388 medium- and heavy-duty trucks, which was up 15% from 2007.
- FAW sells only to distributors or manufacturers, so its representatives went to other sections of the show to talk directly to exhibitors in their target market.
- Toby Young, president of Exhibit Management Associates Inc., organizes MATS and noted that companies are located according to how they are booked.
- Different Chinese agencies ordered blocks of booths and asked that they be grouped together.
- Fifty-four companies from China and Taiwan were at the show this year, making them the largest foreign contingent, while Canada had the next largest with 52 companies.
- Cherrie Cai, export manager for privately owned Fuhe Automobile Industrial Fittings Co. Ltd. in Foshan, China, stated her company makes trailer axles and is looking for a way to open up a path to the American market.
- Fuhe representatives first heard about MATS three years ago, studied truck shows for two years before deciding to attend, and thought it was unfortunate to attend during a difficult economy where not many businesses are seeking new products.
- Kim Tsai, president of Mobiletron Electronics Co., stated his company is based in Taiwan with a manufacturing plant in China, makes spare parts for trucks, tire pressure monitoring systems, and side-view mirrors, and employs 1,500 people.
- Mobiletron Electronics currently sells mainly to Taiwan and China but wants to sell to the U.S. market and needs distributors.
- Frith Zhou, sales manager for Yuhua Industrial Group in Ningbo, China, stated his company produces a wide variety of metal parts and joints for truck engines and frames.
- Yuhua Industrial Group already supplies original equipment manufacturers of trucks in Europe and wants to enter the U.S., attending its first time at MATS to find a business partner to supply.
- Zhou noted there were a lot of people attending the show but not a single buyer, though he made contacts by visiting other parts of the show.
