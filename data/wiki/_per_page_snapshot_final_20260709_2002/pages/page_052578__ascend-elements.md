# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-10

## Facts

- Ascend Elements has started Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings in the U.S.
- Investors had sunk nearly $900 million into Ascend Elements prior to the filing.
- CEO Linh Austin announced the decision in a LinkedIn post late Thursday night, stating the company faced “insurmountable” financial challenges.
- The bankruptcy filing comes amid a softening market for electric vehicles in the U.S.
- The Trump administration canceled a $316 million grant intended for an Ascend Elements facility under construction in Kentucky.
- Of the canceled grant, $204 million had already been disbursed to Ascend Elements before the cancellation.
- Ascend Elements was forced to seek additional capital to cover the shortfall left by the canceled grant.
- Electric vehicle sales in the U.S. surged prior to the end of tax credits in September last year but have not yet recovered.
- Analysts attribute the lack of recovery to customers pulling forward purchases to take advantage of expiring tax credits, which has heightened automakers' fears.
- Several automakers have reduced their plans for new electric vehicles in the U.S. due to market conditions.
- Volkswagen announced it is ending production of the ID.4 at its Chattanooga, Tennessee factory and shifting focus to the gas-powered Atlas.
- Ascend Elements developed a process to extract valuable critical minerals from scrap and end-of-life batteries.
- The company's process limits the number of steps required to transform shredded battery waste into precursor materials for new cathodes.
- Ascend Elements was constructing a 1 million-square-foot facility in Kentucky that has faced lawsuits and delays.
- Battery-related startups like Ascend Elements operate in a challenging and cutthroat industry with long automaker lead times and frequently changing specifications.
- Chinese manufacturers currently dominate the battery materials market, driving down costs through steady and generous state support.
- Competitor recycling startup Redwood Materials has pivoted to repurposing battery packs for grid-scale batteries capable of powering data centers.
- The market for stationary energy storage has exploded in recent years, allowing Redwood Materials to generate near-term revenue while continuing its recycling operations.
