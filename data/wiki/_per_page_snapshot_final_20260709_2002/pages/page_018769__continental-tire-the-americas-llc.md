# Continental Tire the Americas, LLC

## Facts

- Strauss Borrelli PLLC is investigating Continental Tire the Americas, LLC dba Continental Tires regarding a potential mass layoff at its Barnesville, Georgia facility.
- On January 28, 2026, Continental notified the Georgia Office of Workforce Development of its decision to conduct a mass layoff at its Barnesville, Georgia location.
- The investigation focuses on whether Continental failed to provide at least 60 days' advance notice before laying off 235 employees, potentially violating the federal Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act.
- The WARN Act is a federal law passed in 1988 that requires employers with 100 or more employees to provide a 60-day written notice for significant layoffs or plant closings.
- The WARN Act aims to reduce the impact of sudden job loss on workers and communities by ensuring they have sufficient time to transition, seek new employment, and pursue training or retraining opportunities.
- Generally, employees must receive a WARN Act notice if they are laid off or if their hours are cut by 50% or more in any six-month period because of a plant closing or mass layoff.
- A temporary layoff may still violate the WARN Act if it ultimately lasts longer than six months.
- Employers who do not follow WARN Act rules, either by giving notice too late or providing unclear notices, may have to pay employees back pay and benefits for the time they were in violation.
- Continental Tire the Americas is headquartered in Fort Mill, South Carolina, and operates as the North and South American tire business of Continental AG.
- The company designs, manufactures, imports, and distributes tires for passenger vehicles, light trucks, commercial trucks/buses, and select specialty applications.
