# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-27

**Source:** [Georgia Tech, Stryten install lead BESS for advanced research](https://evertiq.com/news/2025-06-27-georgia-tech-stryten-install-lead-bess-for-advanced-research)  ·  `page_050470`

## Facts

- The Georgia Institute of Technology and Stryten Energy, a Georgia-headquartered energy storage solutions provider, have announced the successful installation of Stryten Energy’s Lead Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) at the Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory (CNES).
- The installation aims to create a living-learning lab on campus that supports research and real-world applications of medium-duration energy storage solutions.
- Lead BESS was selected for this initial installation due to its cost-effectiveness, high discharge rates, and recyclability.
- Extensive research demonstrates the reliable performance of lead BESS, according to a media release.
- The CNES building is located in the North Avenue Research Area of the Georgia Tech campus and houses the Strategic Energy Institute (SEI), an interdisciplinary research institute focused on energy research, along with multiple research groups dedicated to renewable energy and energy infrastructure-related topics.
- The BESS integrates renewable energy sources into the existing power mix, providing stable and dependable backup power and reducing grid dependency during peak hours.
- With additional components and software, the system is capable of bi-directional charging, allowing current to flow into the battery for charging and out of the battery to power the grid or microgrid.
- Christine Conwell, SEI’s interim executive director, stated that Georgia Tech’s strategic plan envisions the campus as a dynamic laboratory and experimental test bed where sustainable practices are seamlessly integrated into operations.
- Through enduring partnerships with organizations like Stryten, Georgia Tech is creating mini ecosystems that yield valuable situational data to help chart a path for innovative energy research well beyond the campus.
- Scott Childers, vice president of essential power at Stryten Energy, noted that while large utility-scale implementations were clearly the focus when solar and other renewables hit the market years ago, behind-the-meter applications are now getting their day in the sun.
- Stryten Energy is particularly excited about deploying this unit in commercial and industrial microgrids and pairing it with EV charging stations to help the U.S. achieve its energy goals.
- Stryten Energy aims to demonstrate the advantages of lead BESS from cost savings, technology, environmental, and safety perspectives.
