# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-16

**Source:** [YKK expanding in Macon-Bibb County, bringing 100 new jobs to region](https://wgxa.tv/news/local/ykk-expanding-in-macon-bibb-county-bringing-100-new-jobs-to-region)  ·  `page_016202`

## Facts

- A $125 million expansion at YKK AP America Inc. in Macon-Bibb will bring 100 new jobs along with it.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the expansion on Tuesday.
- YKK AP, a tech-oriented manufacturer of commercial facade systems and residential windows and doors, is set to add a new manufacturing facility as well as machinery and equipment to its operation.
- YKK AP already supports 250 jobs in the Macon area.
- Governor Kemp stated that being the No. 1 state for business doesn't just mean attracting new investments, and it is a priority to ensure companies already operating in Georgia can grow and thrive.
- Governor Kemp noted that YKK AP recognizes the advantages of doing business in middle Georgia and has chosen to expand their relationship with the Macon community through 100 new jobs.
- The company entered the US market in 1991 in Dublin.
- YKK AP's headquarters are in Atlanta.
- Along with its Ontario-based subsidiary Erie Architectural Products, YKK AP employs more than 1,200 people in North America -- with 900 of those workers living in Georgia.
- The company's new facility will be located at the Macon-Bibb I-75 Industrial Park.
- Once construction is completed, YKK AP will relocate its operations and employees from its current location on Ocmulgee Blvd. to the new site.
- The facility will house YKK AP's residential division, which manufactures vinyl windows and doors for builders and homeowners.
- The new facility will also act as a model plant for YKK AP's future North American manufacturing facilities.
- Oliver Stepe, President of YKK AP America Inc., stated the company is excited to be able to increase its investment in the hometown of its residential products business, expand its workforce, introduce further vertical integration of vinyl extrusion, glass, and window production, and create enhanced efficiencies to manufacture the highest quality windows and doors for homebuilders and homeowners.
- Oliver Stepe added that this investment builds toward a long-term vision to be the leader in the residential window and door market and positions the company to expand its business in and beyond the Southeast region.
- Operation of the new site is expected to start in early 2024.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester Miller stated it's an especially great day in Macon-Bibb County when one of their longest running business partners decides to make another investment in their community and bring an additional 100 jobs to the area.
- Mayor Miller added that knowing the new state-of-the-art facility will be used as a blueprint for future facilities illustrates trust in the community and in Georgia as the best place to do business.
- The company is already hiring for several positions.
