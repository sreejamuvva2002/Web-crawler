# Denkai America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Denkai files for bankruptcy, plans to liquidate Camden plant | Camden | pmg-sc.com](https://www.pmg-sc.com/camden/article_cf6c0811-0866-52af-b176-dd5994c8cdfd.html)  ·  `page_019286`

## Facts

- Nippon Denkai Ltd., headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, is planning to dissolve and liquidate its American subsidiary, Denkai America Inc. (DAI), located in Camden, South Carolina.
- The dissolution was revealed in a letter announcing Nippon Denkai’s petition for “civil rehabilitation proceedings,” which are analogous to Chapter 11 bankruptcy in the U.S.
- The rehabilitation petition was filed in Tokyo District Court on Nov. 27.
- The company canceled an “extraordinary shareholders” meeting that had been scheduled for Nov. 29.
- DAI manufactures electrolytic copper foils that are added by other manufacturers of microchips for use in electric vehicle (EV) batteries.
- The company invested ¥30 billion (~$225 million) to build the Camden plant in 2018.
- DAI received $4.6 million in state tax credits from the South Carolina Economic Development Corp in 2019.
- Total investment for the facility is approximately ¥30 billion ($225 million).
- The company canceled debt of ¥10 billion (~$75 million) with SPX, a major customer.
- For the financial year ended March 31, 2026, DAI reported revenue of ¥1.5 billion (~$11 million) and a loss of ¥0.8 billion (~$6 million).
- The company is projecting a $1.7 billion (~$1.2 billion) investment for a new facility and a workforce of 2,000 employees.
- Camden County Economic Development Corp CEO Jonathan Slesson stated the county is “looking for a buyer” for the facility.
- Nippon Denkai CEO Richard Goddard said the company has been “losing money” since 2019.
- South Carolina Gov. Henry McMaster offered incentives to support the project.
- John B. Smith, Executive Director of the SC EDC, was involved in incentive discussions for the facility.
- SPX will continue to supply electrolytic copper foils to Nippon Denkai’s former customers.
- A new plant location is planned for Camden County, near Camden.
- Total debt as of March 31, 2026, was reported at approximately ¥1.5 billion (~$11 million).
