# Hyundai Motor Group’s Meta Plant America

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Northern Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A $5.54 billion electric-vehicle and battery manufacturing complex under construction in northern Bryan County, projected to open in the first half of 2025 and eventually employ about 8,100 people.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant is a major electric-vehicle and battery manufacturing hub that will rely on a new water supply system from four county-owned wells.

## Investment

- **$5.54 billion** — Construction of EV and battery manufacturing complex
- **$22.6 million** — Infrastructure spending for water and sewer service to support the plant and future homes
  - Approximately half funded by American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) money

## Jobs

- **8,100 employees** — First half of 2025
  - Areas: manufacturing, assembly, operations

## Timeline

- **July 5, 2024** — Bulloch County commissioners voted 6-0 to approve a purchase contract for the two-acre well site.
- **On or before Sept. 1, 2024** — Projected closing date for the land sale if conditions are met.
- **Early June 2024** — Commissioners approved an amendment to the Smart Bulloch 2040 Comprehensive Plan and rezoned a 22-acre parcel for a Hyundai supplier injection-molded plastic parts factory.
- **First half of 2025** — Projected opening of the Hyundai Meta Plant America EV and battery manufacturing complex.

## Key facts

- The plant is projected to cost $5.54 billion and employ about 8,100 people.
- Four wells (two owned by Bulloch County, two by Bryan County) will supply a combined 1.5 million gallons of water per day to the Hyundai plant.
- Bulloch County anticipates spending $22.6 million on water and sewer infrastructure to serve the plant and future residential developments.
- Approximately half of the infrastructure costs are expected to be funded with American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) money.
- A proposed Hyundai supplier injection-molded plastic parts factory on a 22-acre parcel could use 50,000 gallons of water per day, which Bulloch County plans to supply.
- Bryan County may pay a “host fee” for its wells located within Bulloch County.
- The two-acre well site is not intended for co-location of other facilities; a minimum of 3-5 acres would be needed for shared uses such as fire, EMS, or park space.
