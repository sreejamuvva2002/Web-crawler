# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Launches Largest Lithium-Ion Battery Recycling Facility in North America, Investing $50 Million in Georgia](https://evmagz.com/ascend-elements-launches-largest-lithium-ion-battery-recycling-facility-in-north-america-investing-50-million-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_052522`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a US-based company, has announced the opening of its $50 million commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility is the largest electric vehicle battery recycling facility in North America to date.
- The facility already began partial operation in August 2022.
- The facility can process up to 30,000 tonnes of batteries and manufacturing scrap per year.
- The company claims it can recover up to 98% of critical metals used in lithium-ion batteries.
- The recovery process is more efficient and environmentally friendly compared to other recycling processes.
- The company uses a unique approach called the ‘Hydro-to-Cathode’ process, which involves engineering the composition and microstructure of lithium-ion cathode particles during the direct precursor synthesis phase.
- This process delivers custom-made battery materials to precise customer specifications.
- Ascend Elements states that the process recovers 98% of critical battery metals in spent lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- The process produces new cathode material at half the cost compared to similar cathodes made with newly mined and refined metals.
- The process produces new cathode material with 90% lower greenhouse gas emissions compared to similar cathodes made with newly mined and refined metals.
- The company is planning to produce sustainable battery materials for electric vehicles in Kentucky.
- The Kentucky facility should be completed towards the end of the year.
- The Kentucky facility will manufacture enough sustainable cathode material and precursors for up to 250,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- Ascend Elements CEO Patrick Gruber stated that the facility demonstrates that sustainable battery production is possible and that a circular economy for lithium-ion batteries can be created.
- Patrick Gruber expressed excitement about leading the way in recycling and sustainable production.
- Patrick Gruber stated the company is committed to developing new technologies and processes that support a greener future.
