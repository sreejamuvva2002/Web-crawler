# Nippon Denkai

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Printed Circuit Design & Fab Online Magazine - Nippon Denkai to Close Last Remaining US Copper Foil Plant](https://pcdandf.com/pcdesign/index.php/editorial/menu-news/fab-news/18443-nippon-denkai-to-close-last-remaining-us-copper-foil-plant)  ·  `page_019278`

## Facts

- Nippon Denkai has decided to close and liquidate its copper foil manufacturing plant in Augusta, GA.
- The facility is the last remaining copper foil factory in the United States.
- Denkai America Inc., the US-based subsidiary of the Japanese parent company, will close just two years after the parent heralded the site as the future of foil production, with tens of millions of dollars in investments planned and hundreds of new jobs expected.
- The company cited financial problems stemming from semiconductor shortages, a decrease in exports of batteries manufactured domestically, lower demand for smartphones, and other factors for the decision.
- Continued losses coupled with loan payments coming due and dried-up sources of new capital have rendered the company insolvent.
- In 2022, Denkai America announced the planned investment of some $150 million in the plant to make copper foil.
- The company took combined losses in the 2023 and 2024 fiscal years of about $23 million tied to the plant.
- For the fiscal year ending March 2025, decreased sales and a rapid increase of copper prices, plus an impairment loss related to the Augusta plant, led to first-half consolidated ordinary losses of approximately 1.5 billion yen ($9.9 million).
- The same period resulted in a consolidated net loss of approximately 5 billion yen ($33 million).
- Cash flow gradually deteriorated due to reconstruction of manufacturing facilities at the subsidiary's previous plant in Camden, SC, and investments in foil manufacturing facilities in the Augusta plant.
- Attempts to find additional funding have stalled.
- The company filed a petition for commencement of civil rehabilitation proceedings last month.
- Under the supervision of the Tokyo District Court, Nippon Denkai will continue its business while seeking a buyer.
- Sumitomo Mitsui Banking is providing up to 2 billion yen as DIP financing for the company’s future fund-raising.
- Nippon Denkai is being delisted from the Tokyo Stock Exchange.
