# Georgia DOT, Kia Georgia, Panasonic and The Ray Connected Vehicle Partnership

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-09

**Source:** [Georgia DOT Demonstrates Safer, Connected Roadways with Kia Georgia and Panasonic Smart Mobility Technology on "The Ray Highway" - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/?p=269102)  ·  `page_060451`

## Facts

- GDOT, The Ray, Kia Georgia, and Panasonic demonstrated a fully operational connected vehicle environment along 18 miles of Interstate 85 known as “The Ray Highway.”
- The demonstration utilized the Cirrus by Panasonic connected vehicle data management platform that detects real-time roadway events.
- The platform communicates safety-critical messages to travelers about driving conditions.
- The partnership between the four organizations is focused on raising awareness of the life-saving potential of connected vehicle technology.
- The partnership aims to deploy real-world use cases with measurable benefits.
- GDOT and Kia Georgia vehicles equipped with vehicle-to-everything (V2X) technology were showcased during the demonstration.
- GDOT can now deliver real-time situational awareness to drivers on equipped roadways in this key logistics and manufacturing corridor, a fast-growing region of West Georgia.
- GDOT, The Ray, and Panasonic are providing “ride-along” experiences for stakeholders.
- Drivers participating in the ride-alongs will be warned of hazardous conditions ahead on the roadway before they come into view.
- The experiences showcase how connected vehicle technology can detect and warn drivers about weather events, work zones, hard braking, and vehicle crashes in a real-world highway environment.
- The experience includes an in-vehicle visualization of traveler warnings.
- The experience showcases the capabilities of V2X technology installed on and along roadways.
- The experience highlights the digital communication that enables the intelligence of connected roadways.
- The Cirrus cloud platform manages the data for GDOT to detect events and provide real-time alerts to drivers.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, stated it is important that Georgia DOT leads in the deployment of transportation technology that benefits all drivers.
- McMurry noted this technology helps move freight across the region more efficiently and safely.
- McMurry said advancing Georgia as the technology epicenter of the east coast is a goal.
- McMurry expressed determination to see connected vehicle technology come to life in Georgia as soon as possible.
- McMurry emphasized that the day is about the power of partnerships with organizations like Kia, The Ray, and Panasonic to advance roadways for Georgians.
- Stuart Countess, Kia Georgia’s president and CEO, stated Kia Georgia is proud to partner with each organization while showcasing products built by the Kia team in West Point, Georgia.
- Countess said customer safety is the number one priority for Kia Georgia team members.
- Countess described the V2X program as a forward-thinking initiative that puts Kia at the heart of solutions where vehicles and infrastructure communicate seamlessly to enhance travel safety and efficiency.
- Allie Kelly, Executive Director of The Ray, stated connected vehicle technology creates an opportunity to increase travel efficiency and driver safety simultaneously.
- Kelly noted investments in physical and digital infrastructure are necessary to enable connected and autonomous vehicles.
- Kelly said Georgia DOT and The Ray made those investments to improve and modernize Georgia’s transportation infrastructure.
- Kelly mentioned the goal is to offer the traveling public real-time, real-place warnings about critical driving conditions such as crashes, construction work zones, hard braking, and weather impacts like black ice, flooding, and hydroplaning.
- Chris Armstrong, Vice President of Cirrus at Panasonic’s Smart Mobility Office, stated the tech industry constantly talks about building technology with purpose.
- Armstrong expressed pride in partnering with mission-driven organizations in Georgia DOT, The Ray, and Kia Georgia.
- Armstrong highlighted solving transportation challenges by bringing forward perspectives from public, private, and philanthropic organizations united in improving safety.
- Armstrong stated each organization plays a unique role in championing the wide-scale adoption of this life-saving technology as quickly as possible.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is headquartered in Newark, NJ.
- Panasonic is a leading provider of Consumer Lifestyle technologies, Smart Mobility, Sustainable Energy, Immersive Experiences, and Integrated Supply Chain solutions.
- Panasonic is the principal North American subsidiary of Osaka, Japan-based Panasonic Holdings Corporation.
- Panasonic was named one of Interbrand’s Top 100 Best Global Brands of 2021.
- Panasonic serves as a leading technology partner and integrator to businesses, government agencies, and consumers across the region.
