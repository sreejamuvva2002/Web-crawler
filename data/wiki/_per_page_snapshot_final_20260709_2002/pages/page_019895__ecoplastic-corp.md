# Ecoplastic Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-06

**Source:** [Hyundai lure continues: Bulloch County lands second auto parts supplier - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2023/01/06/hyundai-lure-continues-bulloch-county-lands-second-auto-parts-supplier/)  ·  `page_019895`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic Corp. will build a new automotive parts plant in Bulloch County.
- The project represents a $205 million investment.
- The plant will create 456 jobs.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Thursday.
- The plant will supply the Hyundai electric vehicle manufacturing facility being built near Savannah.
- Ecoplastic was established in 1984.
- The company produces plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers and other suppliers.
- Products include bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Ecoplastic expects to begin operations by October of next year.
- The company will be filling positions in human resources, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development worked with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, the Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and the Technical College System of Georgia to land the project.
- Two months ago, auto parts manufacturer Joon Georgia announced it will invest $317 million in a new manufacturing plant in Bulloch County that will create 630 jobs.
- Joon Georgia is the Hyundai EV plant’s first confirmed supplier.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said, “We’re proud to welcome Ecoplastic to our growing ecosystem of manufacturers, logistics professionals, suppliers, and more, building on the record-breaking jobs and investments that are on their way to the Peach State.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp added, “This great company … will find a welcoming community of hardworking Georgians and close proximity to our ports system.”
