# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-23

**Source:** [South Korean supplier plans $40 million auto parts plant in Georgia near new Hyundai complex | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/hyundai-georgia-auto-supplier-hanon-systems-00a71f69f77fb36cf5f41f8e9a263fce)  ·  `page_001385`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems, a South Korean company, plans to build a $40 million factory in coastal Georgia to manufacture heating and cooling systems for automobiles.
- The company announced it will construct the facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- Hanon Systems plans to hire at least 160 new employees for the new plant.
- Hanon Systems is the eighth major supplier to locate in the region following Hyundai Motor Group's 2022 announcement of a $5.5 billion electric vehicle and battery assembly plant in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The Hyundai site in Ellabell could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Hanon Systems and seven other suppliers have collectively pledged to invest more than $2 billion and hire 4,800 people in the region.
- Hanon Systems CEO Ming Sun stated that the announcement aligns with the company's strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon Systems currently supplies heating and cooling systems to a Hyundai assembly plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and a Kia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Headquartered in Daejon, South Korea, the company manufactures heating and air conditioning systems at an existing facility in Shorter, Alabama, which opened in 2003 and has expanded at least three times.
- Production at the new Statesboro facility is planned to begin in May 2024.
- The new plant will be located in a business park on the southern outskirts of Statesboro.
- A majority of Hanon Systems is owned by a South Korean private equity firm, while the rest is publicly traded.
- Hanon Systems operates plants worldwide and was created by combining Halla Climate Control with related businesses from Visteon Corp., Cooper Standard Automotive, and Magna International.
- The company could qualify for $3.2 million in state income tax credits, at $4,000 per job over five years, as long as workers make at least $31,300 a year.
- The state will pay to train the new employees.
- Hanon Systems could qualify for other incentives, including property tax breaks from Bulloch County.
