# Battery Manufacturing & Grid Infrastructure

**Source:** [PJM Adds 9.3 GW to Meet Rising Demand // $100B Storage Investment // Clean Energy Cancellations](https://www.gridbrief.com/p/pjm-adds-9-3-gw-to-meet-rising-demand-100b-storage-investment-clean-energy-cancellations)  ·  `page_055031`

## Facts

- PJM Interconnection, the nation’s largest grid operator, has greenlit 51 generation projects totaling 9,361 MW of unforced capacity under its one-time Reliability Resource Initiative (RRI).
- The approved project mix includes a blend of gas (combined and simple cycle), battery storage, nuclear, coal, and a whisper of wind.
- 39 of the approved projects are capacity uprates at existing plants.
- 12 of the approved projects are new builds, consisting of 6 gas, 5 battery, and 1 nuclear facility.
- Nearly all approved projects are expected to be online by 2030.
- PJM’s shift from a 'first-come' to 'first-ready' interconnection queue has already yielded 18 GW of signed agreements.
- The American Clean Power Association and industry executives announced a $100 billion commitment to U.S.-based battery manufacturing by 2030.
- This fivefold jump in domestic investment could, in theory, cover 100% of U.S. storage demand without relying on Chinese supply chains.
- Tesla is building a refinery near Corpus Christi and an expanded Megapack facility in Nevada.
- Form Energy is constructing an iron-air battery plant in West Virginia.
- Multiple LG and Fluence facilities are located across the South and Midwest.
- The IRA supercharged storage economics with generous credits, but policy stability is now uncertain due to legal limbo and looming tariffs.
- ACP CEO Jason Grumet stated: 'Best investment conditions in a generation, highest uncertainty in a generation.'
- E2 reported that nearly $8 billion in clean energy projects were canceled or downsized in Q1 2025.
- Kore Power’s $1.2B Arizona battery plant was canceled.
- Freyr Battery’s $2.6B Georgia facility was canceled.
- Multiple solar, wind, and EV investments were halted or reversed during this period.
- Cancellations were driven by the Trump administration freezing IRA funds pending a federal review, increasing tariffs, and signaling hostility toward EV and solar subsidies.
- Courts temporarily reinstated some IRA funds, but investor sentiment has turned cautious.
- $1.6B in clean energy investments were announced in March alone.
- Freyr rebranded as T1 Energy and pivoted to solar despite canceling its battery plant.
- Quanta Services (PWR) is positioned as the dominant contractor for grid buildouts, benefiting from the need for new or upgraded transmission lines for PJM's fast-tracked generation projects.
- Utilities are shifting from speculative renewables to shovel-ready gas and nuclear, which will likely cause Quanta's backlog of lucrative, long-duration projects to balloon.
- Freyr just pulled the plug on its $2.6B Georgia battery plant due to the IRA freeze and tariff threats gutting expected incentives.
- Unlike Tesla or LG, Freyr lacks U.S. manufacturing scale or political leverage, and its semi-solid lithium technology remains unproven at scale.
- Freyr is now months behind its peers and hemorrhaging investor confidence, representing a retreat rather than a pivot.
