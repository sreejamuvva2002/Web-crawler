# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Gets $6.6B DOE Loan for New Georgia EV Factory | Automotive Manufacturing Solutions](https://www.automotivemanufacturingsolutions.com/electrification/rivian-strengthens-ev-ambitions-with-66b-federal-loan-to-develop-advanced-manufacturing-facility-in-georgia/534952)  ·  `page_008208`

## Facts

- Rivian secured a $6.6 billion loan from the US Department of Energy (DOE).
- The financing is part of the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing programme (ATVM).
- The loan is earmarked for constructing a 9 million sq. ft manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, near Social Circle, Georgia.
- The plant will focus on producing Rivian’s all-electric midsize platform, starting with the R2 SUV and R3 crossover.
- When fully operational, the facility is expected to manufacture up to 400,000 vehicles annually.
- Rivian founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated: “This additional capacity for our mass market products is key to US leadership in the electric vehicle industry.”
- Georgia has aggressively pursued automotive investments with attractive tax incentives, workforce training programmes, and robust infrastructure.
- The Stanton Springs site is located less than an hour from Atlanta.
- The project is set to generate 7,500 direct jobs by 2030, along with thousands of indirect roles.
- The project’s ripple effect is expected to boost the local economy while reinforcing the Biden-Harris administration’s focus on clean energy initiatives, all hinging, however, on the status of the controversial decisions expected to be taken by the Trump team.
- Rivian is collaborating with Georgia’s QuickStart technical training programme to prepare a skilled EV-capable and digitally-ready workforce.
- Twenty local fellows have already commenced training at Rivian’s Illinois facility and will return to Georgia upon completion.
- A DOE spokesperson stated: “This investment will provide thousands of jobs and strengthen America’s leadership in clean transportation.”
- The DOE’s ATVM programme previously aided Tesla in 2009.
- The loan’s dual-tranche structure allows Rivian to draw $3.35 billion during the first phase of construction.
- Rivian can draw $2.62 billion in the second tranche.
- The facility will incorporate advanced automation and renewable energy, setting a benchmark for sustainable manufacturing.
- The investment aligns with the Justice40 Initiative, which ensures 40% of certain climate-related federal investments benefit disadvantaged communities.
- Rivian plans to hire at least a quarter of its employees from local communities.
- The new facility will feature an R&D wing dedicated to advancing battery technology and refining manufacturing processes.
- Rivian delayed construction on its Georgia facility in 2024 initially to cut costs, a move now viewed as a strategic recalibration.
- Supply chain disruptions, inflation, and regulatory hurdles could complicate timelines.
- The first R2 SUVs are expected to roll off the line in 2028, two years after construction begins in 2026.
- The EVs manufactured in Georgia will reportedly save 146 million gallons of petroleum annually.
