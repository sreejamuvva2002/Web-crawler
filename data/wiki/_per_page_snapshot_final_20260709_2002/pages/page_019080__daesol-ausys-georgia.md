# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72M in Harris County - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/news/2023/08/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-invest-72-million-harris-county/)  ·  `page_019080`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on Wednesday, August 30th, 2023 that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The new facility will create more than 140 new jobs and represent $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and is excited for Daesol Ausys to join other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia's automotive industry currently employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose locations across the state.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017 and specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, expressed commitment to delivering world-class services and excitement about the project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- The new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant, Chairman of the Harris County Commission, stated that Daesol Ausys' decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- West Point and Harris County have a strong relationship with Daesol Ausys' sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials, and officials have worked closely to bring the new venture to West Point.
- Craig Greenhaw, Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority, welcomed Daesol Ausys as a corporate citizen and noted the successful partnership between West Point and Harris County bringing jobs and capital investment to the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is driven by the local level and congratulated community partners for helping bring Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- The automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state, with continued growth expected from Daesol Ausys.
- Georgia has positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
