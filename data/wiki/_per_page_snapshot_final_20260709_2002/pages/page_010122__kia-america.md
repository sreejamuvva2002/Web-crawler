# Kia America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-04

**Source:** [KIA AMERICA DONATES $150,000 TO THE AMERICAN RED CROSS FOR THOSE IMPACTED BY HURRICANE MILTON AUTOJUNCTION - Autojunction](https://autojunction.org/kia-america-donates-150000-to-the-american-red-cross-for-those-impacted-by-hurricane-milton-autojunction/)  ·  `page_010122`

## Facts

- Kia America announced a $150,000 donation to the American Red Cross for Hurricane Milton recovery efforts.
- This follows a previous $250,000 contribution from Kia America and Kia Georgia to the Red Cross for Hurricane Helene relief.
- Kia America launched a customer assistance program for customers in Florida, Georgia, South Carolina, and North Carolina who need to replace vehicles damaged beyond repair by either hurricane.
- Eligible customers with an insurance claim for total vehicle loss due to either storm will receive $500 toward the purchase or lease of a new Kia.
- The $500 customer assistance is valid for purchases or leases made between October 4, 2024, and January 2, 2025.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon serves as President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America.
- Hurricanes Helene and Milton caused catastrophic damage across Georgia, Florida, South Carolina, North Carolina, and Tennessee.
- Kia's donated funds will provide vital resources such as food, water, shelter, and emotional support to impacted individuals.
- Kia team members are raising internal funds to further support Red Cross relief efforts.
- Alicia Doherty serves as Regional Executive for the American Red Cross of Georgia.
- The donations are part of Kia's "Accelerate the Good" charitable program, which includes national and local initiatives.
- The "Accelerate the Good" program has provided more than $30 million to those in need since 2019.
- Program funds support causes including childhood cancer treatment, ending childhood hunger, youth homelessness shelter, natural disaster relief, military family support, animal sheltering, and environmental cleanup.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California.
- Kia was recognized as one of TIME's World's Most Sustainable Companies of 2024.
- Kia serves as the "Official Automotive Partner" of the NBA and WNBA.
- Kia sells gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and electric vehicles through a U.S. dealer network of over 775 locations.
- Several Kia cars and SUVs are proudly assembled in America using U.S. and globally sourced parts.
- The 2025 EV9 all-electric three-row SUV is among the models assembled in the United States.
- The 2025 Sportage, Sorento (excluding HEV/PHEV variants), and Telluride are also assembled in the United States.
- The $500 vehicle replacement support offer excludes the 2024 K5, 2024 Sorento Hybrid, and 2024 Sorento PHEV.
- Eligibility for the $500 offer requires proof of residency in FL, GA, NC, SC, or TN, along with proof of a valid insurance claim indicating total loss due to Helene or Milton on or after September 26, 2024.
- The $500 offer may be combined with cash incentives from Kia America and finance or lease offers from Kia Finance America, though not all incentive programs are compatible.
- Customers must take delivery of the new vehicle from retailer stock by January 2, 2025.
