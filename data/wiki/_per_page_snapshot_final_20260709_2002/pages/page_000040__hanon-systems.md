# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-23

**Source:** [Hanon Systems to Create 160 New Jobs in Bulloch County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/automotive-supplier-hanon-systems-create-160-new-jobs-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_000040`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs and invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County on May 23, 2023.
- The announcement was made while Governor Kemp was on an economic development trip to Israel.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as the state welcomes Hanon Systems.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that as Georgia furthers its position as the EV capital of America, new jobs and opportunities are coming to hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, expressed pleasure in contributing to the local economy of Bulloch County through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies for guiding the project.
- Min Sung stated that the announcement aligns with Hanon Systems' strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon Systems will build a new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County to assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, said Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen highlighted that the community is excited to welcome another automotive supplier working closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen noted that the decision exemplifies the resources offered by the community and region to help companies succeed.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated that the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch noted that GPA is proud to support the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers as they deliver jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at GDEcD’s Global Commerce team, represented the department on this competitive project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems can successfully support larger industry ecosystems in Georgia through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County, as part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, has been part of a growing success story and that Georgia’s history of partnership continues to create opportunity for current and future generations.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Hanon Systems was formed in 1986 and has grown into a leading supplier of automotive thermal and energy management solutions for electrified and conventional vehicles.
- Hanon Systems' offerings include heating ventilation and air conditioning, powertrain cooling, compressor, fluid transport, and electronics and fluid pressure solutions.
