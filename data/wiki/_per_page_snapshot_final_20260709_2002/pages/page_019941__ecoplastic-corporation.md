# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Press release, 2023 January 5 - Digital Library of Georgia](https://dlg.usg.edu/record/dlg_ggpd_i-ga-bg600-b-pp1-bp7-b2023-s1-h5-belec-p-btext)  ·  `page_019941`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs and invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County on Thursday, January 5, 2023.
- The new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro, Georgia.
- The plant will be capable of injection molding and painting.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers, garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- Ecoplastic expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is a Korea-based company established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd.
- The company established its first Technical Research Center in 1987 and was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia, manufacturing automotive interior and exterior plastic parts including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- The company currently employs more than 770 employees and recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
- Han Shang, President and CEO of Ecoplastic, stated the company is honored to establish a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County to provide a stable supply of high-quality products to HMGMA.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County Chair Billy Allen welcomed Ecoplastic to the community, citing location, workforce, and quality of life as success factors.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia is building the supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County and spreading suppliers across the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 in neighboring Bryan County for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state, with announced projects already exceeding this amount.
