# Amazon Web Services

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-09

**Source:** [AWS eyes ‘flexible’ data center expansion with $11bn Georgia investment | IT Pro](https://www.itpro.com/infrastructure/data-centres/aws-eyes-flexible-data-center-expansion-with-usd11bn-georgia-investment)  ·  `page_054728`

## Facts

- AWS pledged $11 billion for data centers in the state of Georgia.
- The announcement follows a similar $10 billion funding pledge for data centers in Ohio at the end of last year.
- Microsoft announced it will spend half of an $80 billion tranche of data center funding for 2025 in the US alone.
- Roger Wehner, vice president of economic development at AWS, stated that ongoing infrastructure investments across the United States demonstrate a commitment to powering customers' digital innovation through cloud and AI technologies.
- The growth in data centers has been spurred by the rise in generative AI.
- AWS said the new infrastructure will be designed to be flexible, working for traditional loads as well as meeting the demands of massive AI models.
- AWS data centers are flexible enough to efficiently run GPUs for traditional workloads or AI and machine learning models.
- AWS has invested $18.5 billion in Georgia data centers since 2010.
- Wehner noted that state and local leaders have cultivated an environment that enables companies like AWS to make bold, forward-looking investments.
- AWS predicted the initiative would create at least 550 new high-skilled jobs in the region.
- The initiative will also create thousands of non-technical roles such as construction.
- The investment is expected to focus on Butts and Douglas counties.
- Russ Crumbley, chairman of the Butts County Board of Commissioners, said the investment would be the "most significant in Butts County history."
- AWS did not detail how the new infrastructure would be powered.
- AWS noted it had previously made "significant investments" in renewable energy in the state, including eight solar farms.
- Once fully operational, these projects will generate enough carbon-free energy to power an estimated 123,000 US homes each year.
