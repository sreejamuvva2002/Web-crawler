# Georgia Department of Transportation

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Department of Transportation - Partners for Automated Vehicle Education (PAVE)](https://pavecampaign.org/georgia-department-of-transportation/)  ·  `page_017506`

## Facts

- Georgia’s AV readiness covers four principal facets: Legal, Technology, Signals, and Markings.
- In 2018, the Georgia Legislature passed two bills which were signed into law.
- One bill allowed AVs to travel legally on Georgia highways.
- The other bill changed state law concerning the definition of “tailgating” so that truck platoons wouldn’t be regarded as tailgating.
- Since 2018, Georgia has installed over 600 connected vehicle roadside units (RSUs).
- Plans are underway for Georgia DOT to install an additional 1,000 RSUs.
- Further plans exist for the Atlanta area MPO to install another 1,000 RSUs.
- Almost all of these RSUs are installed at signalized intersections and broadcast SpaT and MAP messages.
- Since 2015, Georgia DOT has had an active program to upgrade traffic signals statewide to the latest version of traffic signal firmware which can accommodate connected vehicle technology.
- Georgia DOT has upgraded traffic signal displays to provide separate indications for each lane.
- Georgia DOT has upgraded left-turn signals to flashing yellow arrow displays.
- Since 2015, Georgia DOT has invested nearly $30,000,000 a year to upgrade the condition of pavement markings to the latest in marking technology, including wider stripes.
- Georgia DOT continues to learn how best to work with the AV development community, noting that they speak different languages and must pay attention to ensure mutual understanding.
- AV development is described as an ongoing and not mature concept.
- As Infrastructure Owners and Operators, Georgia DOT is learning what matters most to the AV developer community.
- Georgia has limited knowledge of AV deployments due to the enabling stance of its state law concerning AVs.
- Georgia is aware of limited AV working done in a freight context.
- Georgia DOT is in conversations with AV developers about expanding testing.
