# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Commerce, an hour and a half drive from Atlanta, Georgia, United States. When you enter a small city.. - MK](https://www.mk.co.kr/en/business/11378665)  ·  `page_050056`

## Facts

- Commerce, Georgia, where the plant is located, is an hour and a half drive from Atlanta and has a population of 7,000.
- A road name sign "SK Boulevard" welcomes visitors to the area housing SK Battery America (SKBA).
- SKBA's Georgia plant serves as SKON's electric vehicle battery production base.
- Georgia's 1st and 2nd plants employ 3,000 workers, which is about half of Commerce City's population.
- The plants are locally called "human resources black holes" and operate 24/7 with lights not extinguished.
- SKBA operates as a security facility that strictly controls outsiders' access as an intensive force of advanced battery technology.
- Workers wear dustproof suits, masks, and gloves to prevent even the slightest dust contamination during operations.
- Countless machines and equipment operate nonstop to manufacture key battery materials including anode and cathode materials.
- A dedicated machine performs "stacking," a key process of making battery cells.
- Real-time production yields and targets are updated in minutes and seconds on large monitors installed for each line.
- Monitor screens show yield data at levels where almost no defective products could be found, such as 98 percent and 99 percent.
- SKBA's Georgia 1 and 2 plants have 12 lines in full operation.
- Nine of the operating lines are dedicated to Hyundai Motor Group.
- Hyundai Motor Group's North American electric vehicle production plant Metaplant is in full operation starting this year.
- SKBA is supplying most of the electric vehicle batteries required for Metaplant.
- Yield is the most important factor to produce high-quality batteries while meeting tight delivery dates.
- SKBA's Georgia plant is raising its overall average yield to around 95% through a continuous yield maximization strategy.
- Achieving results relied on local personnel hired in the United States rather than domestic personnel with excellent battery technology understanding or production know-how.
- Choi Yoon-sang, head of SKBA, stated that recruiting more than 3,000 unskilled local workers in Georgia and training them as skilled workers was extremely difficult.
- It was not easy for middle-aged workers in the United States, who have mainly been engaged in the service industry, to transition to manufacturing.
- All local residents who applied to SKBA previously worked in concentration industries such as chicken farms or at outlets and restaurants.
- SKBA was the first to introduce a two-week "boot camp" training system.
- Basic safety education and basic manufacturing training are systematically conducted and repeated before entering the production line.
- Many applicants left without enduring the first month due to high initial dropout rates, prompting additional hiring.
- SKBA introduced a strikeout system that allows people to leave the company three times for being late to inject Korean-style production DNA.
- Adapting American workers to accept working weekends if necessary without being late was a significant cultural challenge.
- Securing adequate manpower was critical because failing to complete roles would affect the entire line and make it impossible to increase yield above the target.
- Production equipment and process technology made to fit Korean body types have been modified to suit Americans.
- The eye level of the machine screen has been raised, and the size of the button to be pressed when working has been replaced much larger.
- Non-technical improvements that minimize human errors such as operator manipulation mistakes are equally important for maximizing yield alongside machine upgrades.
- Typhoons have hit every year due to abnormal climates, making it difficult for factories to control humidity.
- Battery quality can be shaken even if the humidity increases by only 1-2%.
- Flexibility to respond appropriately through comprehensive judgment is essential when external variables cannot be eliminated.
- Efforts to improve quality led to favorable reviews from local automakers and positive perceptions in the region, resulting in a virtuous cycle structure.
- Companies that have looked at SK-on's battery production system are positively considering new contracts by sending confidence in SK-on.
- The full operation of the plant could lead to a long-term, not temporary or one-time operation.
- Choi Yoon-sang stated that battery production has tripled compared to last year and efforts have paid off.
- SKBA is trying to deliver good news and achieve more production efficiency in the second half of the year.
- SKBA is working on more than 100 production improvement projects to further boost yields in the second half of this year.
- A "language service team" will be organized to solve language problems to facilitate communication between engineers and workers and to harmonize culturally.
- The good performance of SK-on North American production base is expected to be an opportunity for SK Group to rebound in its performance.
- SK Group aims to seize opportunities for growth as a future food, not a concern, by deriving tangible indicators and numbers in the battery business sector after previously lacking clear results.
