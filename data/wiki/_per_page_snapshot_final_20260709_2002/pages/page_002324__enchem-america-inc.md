# Enchem America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor Lee, Commissioner McWhorter Announce Enchem America Inc. to Locate US Manufacturing Operations in Brownsville](https://www.tn.gov/ecd/news/2023/6/26/-governor-lee--commissioner-mcwhorter-announce-enchem-america-inc--to-locate-us-manufacturing-operations-in-brownsville.html)  ·  `page_002324`

## Facts

- Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee, Department of Economic and Community Development Commissioner Stuart McWhorter and Enchem America Inc. officials announced that the company will invest $152.5 million to establish electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville, Tennessee.
- The new facility is located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park, a Select Tennessee Certified Site.
- The project will create 190 new jobs in Tier 4, At-Risk Haywood County.
- The Brownsville facility will operate similarly to Enchem’s U.S. headquarters in Commerce, Georgia.
- The facility will manufacture electrolytes for electric vehicle (EV) batteries as well as other EV battery-related services.
- Enchem America Inc. is a subsidiary of South Korea-based Enchem Co., Ltd.
- The company specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes and other additives for rechargeable batteries.
- With the additional Tennessee operations, Enchem will be able to further support its EV partners across the Southeast.
- Since 2019, TNECD has supported more than 50 economic development projects in the Southwest Tennessee region, resulting in nearly 9,300 job commitments and $7.2 billion in capital investment.
- Gov. Bill Lee stated that Enchem’s decision to invest more than $150 million in Tennessee is a testament to the state's unmatched business climate, highly skilled workforce, and position as a leader in the automotive industry, and thanked the company for creating 190 new jobs in Haywood County.
- TNECD Commissioner Stuart McWhorter noted that South Korea is one of Tennessee’s top 10 foreign direct investment partners and that Enchem is boosting the EV supply chain across Tennessee by investing in an At-Risk county.
- Jung Kang Oh, CEO and founder of Enchem Co. Ltd., expressed gratitude to Haywood County, the City of Brownsville, and professionals who assisted with the expansion, noting the remarkable growth of the EV market over the coming decade and confidence in capitalizing on it.
- Haywood County Mayor David Livingston welcomed Enchem, stating the economic opportunity will help transform Haywood County and West Tennessee with high-paying jobs, and thanked Governor Lee and TNECD for their partnership.
- Brownsville Mayor Bill Rawls stated that years of work and investment in I-40 Advantage Industrial Park fulfilled the goal of bringing high-quality job opportunities to the city and expressed a look forward to building an advanced manufacturing workforce.
- John Bradley, TVA senior vice president of economic development, congratulated Enchem America and noted TVA's pride in partnering with HTL Advantage, Brownsville-Haywood County Industrial Development Corporation, City of Brownsville, and TNECD.
- Sen. Page Walley (R-Savannah) welcomed Enchem America to Haywood County, stating the investment will boost the regional economy and establish the community as a hub of innovation in the rapidly growing industry.
- Rep. Johnny Shaw (D-Bolivar) cited the more than $152 million investment as an example of unprecedented growth in Brownsville, Haywood County, and West Tennessee, noting that BlueOval City lit the fuse for this economic explosion.
- The Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development’s mission is to develop strategies to make Tennessee the No. 1 location in the Southeast for high-quality jobs by attracting new corporate investment and facilitating expansion.
