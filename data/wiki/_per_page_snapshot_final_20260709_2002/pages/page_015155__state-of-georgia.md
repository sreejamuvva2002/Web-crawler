# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-03

**Source:** [Move over, Michigan: Georgia now leads in building next-generation cars • Georgia Recorder](https://georgiarecorder.com/2024/07/03/move-over-michigan-georgia-now-leads-in-building-next-generation-cars/)  ·  `page_015155`

## Facts

- Georgia is emerging as the undisputed national leader in the electrifying auto sector and EV manufacturing.
- According to data from the Clean Economy Tracker, Atlas Public Policy, and the CHARGE coalition, Georgia leads the nation in committed investments and permanent jobs in EV and battery manufacturing.
- Since November 2021, $22 billion and nearly 24,000 new private sector EV and battery manufacturing jobs have been announced for Georgia.
- Blue Bird’s factory in Peach County is utilizing the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law’s Clean School Bus program to shift production from diesel engines to electric buses.
- Blue Bird’s Fort Valley facility currently employs 2,000 workers in a town of only 9,000 residents.
- Blue Bird has announced plans to ramp up EV bus production from two per day to twenty.
- Blue Bird is developing a “Registered Apprentice Program” to train workers in partnership with local colleges, high schools, and trade schools.
- Hyundai operates several EV and battery-related facilities in Georgia.
- Hyundai entered into a memorandum of understanding with Savannah Technical College to provide training for prospective EV industry employees in shop operations, electrical principles, and servicing hybrids and EVs.
- Similar Hyundai workforce training programs now exist at Columbus and Augusta Technical Colleges.
- Hyundai’s $7.6 billion assembly plant in Ellabell is projected to create upwards of 2,000 construction jobs and 8,500 permanent jobs.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant will eventually produce 300,000 EVs annually.
- Rivian plans to build its second production facility in Georgia.
- Rivian's second Georgia facility is anticipated to employ 7,500 workers by 2030.
- The growth of Georgia's EV and battery industry is directly supported by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and tax credits from the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act.
- Norwegian battery company FREYR chose to build a $2.6 billion facility near Atlanta instead of Norway due to incentives under the IRA.
- The Atlanta metro area was selected for FREYR out of 130 options across 25 states because of its strong air, sea, and rail port connections and a robust engineering workforce trained at institutions like Georgia Tech.
- Governor Brian Kemp visited the Kia plant in West Point to celebrate hundreds of new jobs associated with production of the EV9.
- The Kia EV9 won North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- 97% of the nearly 24,000 EV and battery manufacturing jobs announced for Georgia since the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law are located in Congressional districts represented by Republican legislators.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated during his visit to West Point’s Kia factory: “We are working to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.”
