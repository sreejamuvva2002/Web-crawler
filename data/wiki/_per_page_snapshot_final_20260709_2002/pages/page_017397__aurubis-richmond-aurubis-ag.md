# Aurubis Richmond (Aurubis AG)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-08

**Source:** [Press release: First Lady of the United States visits Aurubis Richmond, the first ... | Presseportal](https://www.presseportal.de/en/pm/109932/5644621)  ·  `page_017397`

## Facts

- On November 8, 2023, First Lady Jill Biden visited Aurubis Richmond at its new site in Augusta (Richmond County), Georgia.
- Aurubis is investing around US$ 700 million to build the first secondary smelter for complex recycling materials in North America.
- Commissioning of the secondary smelter is planned for the second half of the coming year.
- An expansion of the plant is already under construction.
- The expansion will allow Aurubis Richmond to process a total of 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year as of 2026.
- The facility will process complex recycling materials such as printed circuit boards and insulated copper cable.
- The visit was part of the Biden-Harris Administration’s Investing in America agenda.
- Augusta is designated as one of five focus regions, known as Workforce Hubs, where a large number of jobs for skilled workers are to be created due to significant federal and private-sector investments.
- Aurubis is creating long-term, secure jobs for more than 200 people at the site.
- The metals returned to circulation by the facility are essential to the mobility and energy transition and an important contribution to the electrical vehicle ecosystem.
- The project aligns with Georgia’s Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance initiative.
- Aurubis has pledged a community benefit agreement with the Technical Colleges in Augusta and Aiken (South Carolina) as well as the Richmond County School System.
- As part of the community benefit agreement, Aurubis will invest more than US$ 200,000 in scholarships and job training programs for young workers over the next five years.
- The company actively promotes women in the metal industry through its international Women4Metals initiative.
- Increasing awareness for sustainability and a significant jump in collection volumes are generating roughly 6 million tons of recyclable, metal-bearing materials in the U.S. every year.
- The growing supply of recyclable materials is a main driver for Aurubis's investment decision.
- The goal is to establish a reliable and sustainable supply chain by returning more recycling materials to local cycles.
- Experts estimate that the recycling volume in North America will increase by an average of 5% annually in the coming years.
- Georgia offers ideal conditions for Aurubis due to its pro-business climate and excellent infrastructure, including logistics and a stable energy supply at competitive prices.
- Support from Georgia State officials has enabled Aurubis to contribute expertise and become a multi-metal recycling pioneer in the U.S.
- The Inflation Reduction Act offers great potential to accelerate Aurubis’s future U.S. growth and investment, helping position the Southeast as a leading ecosystem for EV and multi-metal recycling.
- Aurubis is in a strong financial position and ready to invest more in the U.S. in keeping with its ‘Metals for Progress: Driving Sustainable Growth’ strategy.
- In addition to strategic investments in its European sites, Aurubis recognizes great potential for further development in North America.
