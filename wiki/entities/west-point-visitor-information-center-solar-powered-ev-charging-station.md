# West Point Visitor Information Center Solar-Powered EV Charging Station

**Type:** Charging Infrastructure  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** West Point, Georgia, Troup County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Georgia’s first solar-powered photovoltaic electric vehicle (PV4EV) charging station installed at the West Point Visitor Information Center, providing level 3 quick-charge capability and powered by Spotlight™ Solar LIFT technology as part of “The Ray” sustainable highway initiative.

## EV supply-chain relevance

First solar-powered EV charging station in Georgia, intended to increase EV adoption and support travel along the I-85 corridor between West Georgia and Alabama.

## Key facts

- Georgia’s first solar-powered photovoltaic EV charging station.
- Level 3 quick-charge provides 80%+ battery charge in less than 45 minutes for most electric vehicles.
- Powered by Spotlight™ Solar LIFT technology, a solar PV system integrated with the charger.
- Part of “The Ray,” a 16-mile I-85 corridor project envisioned as a model sustainable highway of the future.
- Installation aims to promote broader EV adoption; Georgia ranks second nationally in registered electric vehicles.
- Students from Georgia Tech School of Architecture researched solar panels, energy-generating asphalt, and air-filtering plant walls for sustainable highways.
- The station’s location at West Point VIC improves travel convenience for EV drivers between West Georgia and Alabama.
- "Georgia ranks 2nd in the nation for registered electric vehicles..." - John Lanier, executive director of Ray C. Anderson Foundation.
- "Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia is proud to be a leader when it comes to taking care of our environment..." - Randy Jackson, KMMG senior vice president.
