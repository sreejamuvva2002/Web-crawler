# Duracell

## Facts

- Duracell is a subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway.
- Duracell maintains manufacturing operations across multiple locations worldwide, including facilities in the United States, Europe, and Mexico.
- The majority of Duracell’s manufacturing takes place in the United States.
- Duracell's LaGrange, Georgia plant is responsible for creating most of the AA and AAA alkaline batteries.
- US manufacturing plants are located in Cleveland, Tennessee; LaGrange, Georgia; and Lexington, North Carolina.
- Duracell's US operations and headquarters are based in Bethel, Connecticut.
- Duracell’s Lancaster, South Carolina plant was closed in 2016, shortly after Berkshire Hathaway’s acquisition of the company.
- In Europe, Duracell operates a manufacturing plant in Aarschot, Belgium, recognized as its most innovative facility and transformed into a “factory of the future” in two years.
- The company leases manufacturing plants in Mississauga, Ontario, Canada, and Wrexham, Wales.
- In Mexico, Duracell has a manufacturing plant located in Mexico City.
- The company collaborates with several third-party manufacturers to produce batteries in other countries.
- Duracell has a distribution center located in Heist-op-den-Berg, Belgium, which exports products to Europe and Africa.
- Duracell’s batteries are available in over 130 countries worldwide.
- Berkshire Hathaway oversees Duracell’s production of its own alkaline batteries.
- Duracell's manufacturing facilities have received ISO 9001 certification for their quality management systems.
- Duracell developed the Duralock Power Preserve Technology, enabling longer-lasting batteries.
- Duracell was originally founded as the P.R. Mallory Company in the 1920s by scientist Samuel Ruben and businessman Philip Rogers Mallory.
- In 2014, Procter & Gamble announced plans to spin off Duracell.
- Berkshire Hathaway purchased Duracell in an all-stock deal finalized in February 2016.
- The acquisition involved a cash investment of $1.8 billion from Procter & Gamble directly into Duracell.
- In 2016, Duracell announced that its executives and 60 other employees would relocate to Chicago.
- Duracell manufactures non-rechargeable (alkaline) and rechargeable batteries for consumer devices.
- Non-rechargeable batteries are available in AAA, AA, C, D, and 9V sizes with Simply Power, Plus, and Ultra Power variations.
- Rechargeable batteries are available in standard (Recharge Plus) and high-capacity (Recharge Ultra) types using NiMH and lithium-ion technologies.
- Duracell car batteries are manufactured by East Penn in Pennsylvania, USA.
