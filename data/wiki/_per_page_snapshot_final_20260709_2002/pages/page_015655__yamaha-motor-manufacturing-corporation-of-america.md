# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-12

**Source:** [Yamaha Celebrates 4 Million Units Assembled in the USA](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/yamaha-celebrates-4-million-units-193000034.html)  ·  `page_015655`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America (YMMC) and Yamaha Motor Corp., USA (YMUS) celebrated the production of 4 million vehicles with the assembly of a new 2021 Wolverine RMAX2 1000 Side-by-Side at its U.S. manufacturing facility in Newnan, Georgia.
- The milestone celebration took place on August 12, 2021, at the Newnan factory to recognize employees for their dedication to superior manufacturing processes and high-quality vehicle production.
- YMMC serves as Yamaha's hub for 4-wheel off-highway vehicle manufacturing, producing all of the company's Side-by-Sides, full-size ATVs, WaveRunners, and Golf Cars for worldwide distribution.
- Over the past 20 years, Yamaha has invested more than $380 million into U.S. manufacturing to create a world-class facility in Newnan, according to Taka Imanishi, president of YMMC.
- Since its founding in 1988, YMMC has made a significant impact within its community, with the facility covering 280 acres and containing more than 1.3 million square feet of manufacturing space.
- Between the YMMC facility and YMUS corporate offices in Marietta and Kennesaw, Georgia, Yamaha now employs over 2,000 people in the state of Georgia alone.
- The Georgia employment encompasses more than half of the 4,000+ individuals the company directly employs across the United States.
- The city of Newnan named May 18th "Yamaha Day" in 2011 to celebrate the company's positive economic impacts and ongoing job creation.
- Yamaha's impact on Newnan has been highlighted through recent disaster relief donations, ongoing community engagement programs, and a season 19 premiere of "Modern Marvels: Machines" on the HISTORY® Channel that went behind-the-scenes at the factory.
- Mike Martinez, YMUS vice president of motorsports, stated that Yamaha continues to add personnel and strengthen operations in Georgia as the Side-by-Side line expands with popular new models like the Wolverine RMAX 1000.
- Due to increasing demand for outdoor recreation, Yamaha expects continued growth of its operations in Georgia and across the country.
- The YMMC facility holds ISO9001 Quality Management System and ISO14001 Environmental Management System certifications for quality, safety, and environmental manufacturing standards.
