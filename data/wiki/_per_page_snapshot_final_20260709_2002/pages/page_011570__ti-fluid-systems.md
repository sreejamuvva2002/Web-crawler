# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [AIM2Flourish | Sustainable Development of TI Fluid Systems](https://aim2flourish.com/innovations/sustainable-development-of-ti-fluid-systems)  ·  `page_011570`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems is actively transforming its operations and product offerings to support a more sustainable future as the automotive industry shifts toward electrification.
- The company operates a company-wide transition strategy called “Taking-the-Turn,” which focuses on three pillars: Technology, Transformation, and Talent.
- The “Taking-the-Turn” strategy aims to reduce emissions, foster innovation, and improve workplace safety and inclusivity.
- TI Fluid Systems develops advanced products for electric and hybrid vehicles.
- Through its global E-Mobility Innovation Centers (eMICs), TI Fluid Systems works with OEMs to design lightweight and modular systems that improve thermal management, reduce vehicle weight, and enhance energy efficiency.
- The company is adopting more sustainable materials and advancing techniques like vertical integration to streamline production and lower environmental impact.
- TI Fluid Systems is modernizing its facilities with renewable energy, digital monitoring systems, and smart manufacturing practices.
- These efforts support the UN Sustainable Development Goals—particularly SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure) and SDG 13 (Climate Action).
- Brian David is the former Asia Pacific President and Managing Director of TI Fluid Systems.
- Brian David played a pivotal role in evolving TI Fluid Systems’ product portfolio to support cleaner mobility, including electric and hybrid vehicle technologies.
- His leadership included the development of lighter, more efficient systems and the integration of environmentally responsible practices across manufacturing sites.
- Brian David is now retired and channels his passion for sustainable development and leadership through coaching businesses.
- Brian David stated: “I lead with a couple of very simple dogmas in mind – ‘be the very best you can be’ and ‘always lead with integrity’. In this industry, to be the best and embody integrity means looking out for the environment. I am less focused on just the environment, but more on what can I do to better everyone and everything. Gone are the days when companies can sweep things under the rug and move on to the next environmentally damaging product. To innovate now is to build sustainable infrastructure and products – I don’t mean that’s the end-all, be-all, but a start to an industry-wide change. I lead by example, and I think what we have done here [TI Fluid Systems] is a pretty good example.”
- TI Fluid Systems reduced its Scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions by 15% year-on-year in 2024.
- The company’s Scope 1 and 2 GHG emissions are 28% lower than its 2021 baseline.
- Progress stems from procuring over 120,000 MWh of renewable electricity.
- The company implemented an energy efficiency program across global facilities.
- TI Fluid Systems is investing in new product development, including an electric coolant pump and modular EV thermal systems.
- TI Fluid Systems collaborates directly with original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) through its five e-Mobility Innovation Centers (eMICs).
- The company is tracking toward 2030 targets: a 50% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions and a 30% reduction in Scope 3 emissions.
- TI Fluid Systems secured €500 million in lifetime battery BEV bookings in 2024.
- The company secured €600 million for HEVs in 2024 alone.
- The electric coolant pump is set to enter production this year, completing the product offering for modular thermal systems.
- Modular thermal systems will be a critical growth area given the increasing complexity and cooling needs of EV platforms.
- Average training hours per salaried employee increased from 4 to 13 in 2024.
- Lost Time Injury frequency was cut nearly in half in one year, dropping from 3.0 to 1.76.
- The company's innovation alignment with global regulatory and consumer expectations has attracted positive ESG ratings and increased investor confidence.
- Nylon-based multi-layer refrigerant lines offer a 30–60% weight reduction compared to traditional materials, contributing to greater EV efficiency and range.
- The company diverted over 60,000 tons of CO2(e) emissions in 2024 by sourcing renewable energy through Energy Attribute Certificates.
- In 2024, the company expanded its Women’s Empowerment Networks globally.
- The company sponsored over 40 STEM scholarships for women in 2024.
- TI Fluid Systems serves over 24,600 employees across 95 manufacturing sites in 26 countries.
- TI Fluid Systems designs and produces essential vehicle components such as brake and fuel lines, fuel tanks, and thermal systems.
- These components cater to all types of propulsion—from traditional internal combustion engines (ICE) to hybrid (HEV) and battery electric vehicles (BEV).
