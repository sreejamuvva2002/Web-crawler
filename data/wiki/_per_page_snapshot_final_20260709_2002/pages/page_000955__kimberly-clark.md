# Kimberly-Clark

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kimberly-Clark backs neighborhood 3MW solar ranch in LaGrange, Georgia](https://list.solar/news/kimberly-clark/)  ·  `page_000955`

## Facts

- A 3MW solar photovoltaic (PV) project has been finished and marketed by United Renewable Energy at Kimberly-Clark's center in LaGrange, Georgia.
- The project is a partnership between Kimberly-Clark's center, United Renewable Energy LLC as the project developer and home builder, and NextEra Energy Resources, LLC (a subsidiary) as the long-term project owner.
- All output from the new solar center, along with all renewable energy credit reports (RECs), will be sold to Georgia Power as part of Georgia Power's Renewable Energy Development Initiative.
- The linked RECs will be retired on behalf of all of Georgia Power's consumers.
- The 3MW solar project includes more than 8,600 photovoltaic panels.
- Along with renting the land for the project, Kimberly-Clark has participated in a separate arrangement to acquire RECs from NextEra Energy Resources to counter its greenhouse gas emissions.
- Jeremy Cannady, mill supervisor at Kimberly-Clark's LaGrange center, stated that the growth of the project and acquisition of RECs demonstrate their dedication to renewable energy.
- The project provides satisfaction for LaGrange workers, and buying RECs from NextEra Energy Resources helps achieve a global objective of a 40% offset in greenhouse gas emissions by 2022.
- William Silva, CEO of United Renewable Energy, expressed pride in being part of the quality group including Kimberly-Clark and NextEra Energy Resources for the benefit of the LaGrange plant and community.
- Matt Ulman, vice president of distributed generation for NextEra Energy Resources, noted that collaborations with commercial customers focus on helping them achieve business objectives like saving on utility costs or meeting GHG emission goals, and that distributed generation properties allow commercial services to make an impact at their centers where they appear to stakeholders and staff.
