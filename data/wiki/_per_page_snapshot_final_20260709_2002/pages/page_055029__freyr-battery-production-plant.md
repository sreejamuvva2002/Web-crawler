# FREYR Battery production plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-17

**Source:** [$2.57 bn FREYR Battery production plant for construction in Georgia](https://constructionreviewonline.com/news/2-57-bn-freyr-battery-production-plant-coming-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_055029`

## Facts

- A $2.57 billion FREYR Battery production plant will soon be built in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The developer, FREYR Battery, recently purchased a 368-acre plot at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site for the project.
- The site location was selected based on key operational, logistical, and financial criteria.
- FREYR and its joint venture partner, Koch Strategic Platforms, have plans of building the new battery plant in multiple phases.
- Both companies are contributing a total of $70 million to commence the initial phase of the FREYR Battery production plant.
- Koch is investing $50 million for the initial phase.
- FREYR is providing the remaining 20 million for the initial phase.
- FREYR will also apply for U.S. Department of Energy funds and other grants to raise more funding.
- The focus of the new plant will be mostly on battery energy storage solutions.
- Energy storage is particularly important for wind and solar energy systems in the U.S.
- The project’s initial phase will include a cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- The cell production module was designed based on the next generation of 24M Technologies for the SemiSolid™ production platform in the U.S.
- The new Battery production plant is expected to create about 723 jobs.
- The project is likely to provide capital investments worth more than $2.6 a billion by 2029.
- FREYR is targeting a broader global plan of building up to 200-gigawatt hours of capacity by 2030.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed the developers to the state and expressed pleasure with the new plant.
- Governor Brian Kemp said FREYR was the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunities to communities in the Peach State.
- Governor Brian Kemp said he was looking forward to witnessing their growing impact on Coweta and the surrounding region.
