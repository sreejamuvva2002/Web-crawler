# Mercedes-Benz AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz Announces Expansion in Atlanta: Company to Establish Atlanta as North American Headquarters with Centralization of Key Corporate Functions and Investment in New Research & Development Hub](https://investor.wedbush.com/wedbush/article/bizwire-2025-5-22-mercedes-benz-announces-expansion-in-atlanta-company-to-establish-atlanta-as-north-american-headquarters-with-centralization-of-key-corporate-functions-and-investment-in-new-research-and-development-hub)  ·  `page_046260`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz is expanding its presence in Metro Atlanta by moving up to 500 corporate, technical, and high-tech roles to its existing “1MB” facility in Sandy Springs, Ga.
- The company is making a multi-million dollar investment in a new Research & Development hub in Metro Atlanta for technical teams from various U.S. locations.
- Today’s announcement establishes Atlanta as Mercedes-Benz’s headquarters in North America, centralizing and uniting key corporate functions driving the success of the U.S. market.
- Additional corporate roles will move to Sandy Springs from Farmington Hills, Mich.
- Financial services teams and corporate functions based in Farmington Hills will be co-located with current sales teams in Sandy Springs.
- The move of up to 500 roles further expands Mercedes-Benz’s longstanding presence in the Atlanta area.
- Jason Hoff, CEO of Mercedes-Benz North America, stated that thanking the State of Georgia for its support deepens roots and brings talented team members to the city.
- Hoff noted the expansion strengthens their position for continued growth, reinforces commitment to the U.S. market, enables agility, increases speed to market, and ensures the best customer experience.
- Mercedes-Benz opened its 1MB facility in 2018, making it an integral part of the Atlanta community.
- There are currently approximately 800 Mercedes-Benz employees in Atlanta.
- The move of up to 500 additional roles to Georgia is expected to be completed by August 2026.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia continues to lead in mobility and technical innovation, attracting world-class companies driving the automotive industry forward.
- Kemp expressed excitement that a job creator with close ties to Georgia is doubling down on its presence in the best state for business and opportunity.
- Mercedes-Benz is co-locating its Research & Development and customer lifecycle-focused activities in the U.S. as part of a broader strategy to enhance product excellence and customer experience.
- The strategic investment in Atlanta will allow the company to leverage the engineering talent pool around institutions like Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The investment will create new opportunities to collaborate with the region’s thriving startup ecosystem and established tech sectors.
- This announcement comes weeks after Mercedes-Benz announced it will bring production of the GLC SUV to its Tuscaloosa, Ala. facility.
- The U.S. is a vital market for Mercedes-Benz, with sales of 374,100 vehicles (passenger cars and vans) last year.
- The company is responsible for 11,100 direct jobs in the U.S., of which around 8,000 are in manufacturing.
- There are approximately 112,000 jobs across Mercedes-Benz’s business suppliers and service providers.
- Mercedes-Benz has a strong heritage dating back to 1888 in Long Island City, NY.
- As a major local investor, Mercedes-Benz has committed over $10 billion to production, infrastructure, R&D, design, and the dealer network.
- This commitment includes $7+ billion at its Tuscaloosa, Ala., site and $560+ million at its Charleston, S.C., plant.
- The company supports more than 163,000 jobs across the country, including 11,100 direct jobs (around 8,000 in manufacturing) and 28,000 dealership employees.
- Mercedes-Benz’s nationwide presence spans 385 dealer partners and 24 corporate locations in 13 states.
- More than 4.5 million vehicles have left the Tuscaloosa plant over the past 28 years.
- Roughly sixty percent of annual output from Tuscaloosa is exported.
- The United States is the second-largest global market for Mercedes-Benz Sprinter vans.
- Since 2006, Mercedes-Benz Vans, LLC (MBV Charleston) has assembled more than 400,000 vans for delivery across North America.
- Mercedes-Benz works with around 400 suppliers in the United States.
- The company is sourcing local demand for steel and aluminum almost exclusively in the United States.
- The Tuscaloosa operation is the global hub for SUVs including GLE, GLS, GLE Coupe, Mercedes-Maybach GLS, EQS SUV, EQE SUV, and Mercedes-Maybach EQS SUV.
- Mercedes-Benz is making electric vehicle infrastructure investments of approximately $1.2 billion to expand a high-speed charging network in North America over the next six to seven years.
- The company launched its first charging location in Sandy Springs, Ga. in November 2023.
- In 2024, Mercedes-Benz sold 324,500 passenger cars and 49,600 vans in the U.S.
- The United States is the second-largest global market for Mercedes-Benz cars and vans.
- Mercedes-Benz currently supports over 700,000 finance and lease customers in the United States with a portfolio of over $40 billion.
- The company has filed nearly 100 technology patents from the U.S.
- Mercedes-Benz introduced DRIVE PILOT, the first SAE-Level 3 system for conditionally automated driving approved for Nevada and California.
- The company actively supports communities with nearly 40,000 hours of company-supported volunteer work in 2024 alone.
- Mercedes-Benz AG is part of the Mercedes-Benz Group AG with around 175,000 employees worldwide.
- Ola Källenius serves as Chairman of the Board of Management of Mercedes-Benz AG.
- The company focuses on development, production, and sales of passenger cars, vans, and vehicle-related services.
- Mercedes-Benz aspires to be the leader in electric mobility and vehicle software.
- The product portfolio includes the Mercedes-Benz brand with Mercedes-AMG, Mercedes-Maybach, G-Class, all-electric models, and the smart brand.
- In 2024, Mercedes-Benz sold around 2.4 million passenger cars and vans worldwide.
- The company is continually expanding its worldwide production network with more than 30 production sites on four continents.
- Mercedes-Benz is constructing and extending its global battery production network on three continents.
- Sustainability is the guiding principle of the Mercedes-Benz strategy, focusing on creating lasting value for stakeholders through a sustainable business strategy of the Mercedes-Benz Group.
