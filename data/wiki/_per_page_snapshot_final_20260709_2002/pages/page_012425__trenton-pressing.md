# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-18

**Source:** [Metal forming company jumps into the thick of metal fabricating](https://www.thefabricator.com/thefabricator/article/lasercutting/metal-forming-company-jumps-into-the-thick-of-metal-fabricating)  ·  `page_012425`

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing is a metal forming company located in Trenton, Ga.
- The company operates as a longtime Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier of stamped parts and complex assemblies for automotive companies and golf cart manufacturers.
- During the pandemic, new owners purchased the business with the goal of diversifying its customer base beyond two consolidated industries.
- The new ownership added metal fabricating capabilities to the company's existing stamping, robotic welding, and assembly services.
- The strategic goal is to build full turnkey fabrications for customers rather than just supplying individual parts.
- Brad Coffman works for Trenton Pressing and brings 30 years of experience in the metal fabricating industry to the new direction.
- The company constructed a 100,000-sq.-ft. building on its campus specifically for warehousing and assembly activities.
- Constructing the new facility freed up space in Plant 1 to accommodate a laser cutting machine and a press brake.
- Plant 2 houses 15 robotic welding cells along with manual welding and assembly areas.
- Trenton Pressing purchased a BesCutter 20-kW fiber laser cutting machine with a 5- by 10-ft. bed and automatic loading/unloading capability from Automated Solutions in late 2023.
- The 20-kW laser cutting machine became operational a few months later in early 2024.
- The high-powered laser can cut up to 2-in. steel plate and uses compressed high-pressure air for economical cuts on 3/8- to ½-in. material.
- An automated load and unload gantry system was installed alongside the laser, utilizing vacuum suction cups for raw sheet metal and fork-style lifters for finished parts.
- The automated loading system is capable of running unattended for eight hours without requiring material reloading.
- The company purchased two cutting heads to allow quick switching during maintenance and prevent machine downtime.
- Trenton Pressing installed a Haco EuroMaster 275-ton CNC press brake with a 10-ft. bed in early 2025.
- The press brake features 6-axis backgauging, CNC crowning, automatic angle measurement during bending, hydraulic upper tool clamping, and offline programming capability.
- Brad Coffman plans to train one of the company's engineers to operate the press brake, creating two forming experts to help train other staff members.
- A box truck moves work-in-process between buildings at least three times per day using a kanban logistics system.
- Management refinished approximately 60% of the plant floors with polyurethane paint after moving assembly operations to the newly constructed building.
- The company's owners maintain high standards for the plants, viewing the clean facilities as a selling tool for potential customers.
