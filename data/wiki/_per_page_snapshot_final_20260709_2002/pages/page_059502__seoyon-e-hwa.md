# Seoyon E-HWA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-02

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier plans $76M Georgia plant, hiring 500, ETHRWorld](https://hr.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/industry/hyundai-supplier-plans-76m-georgia-plant-hiring-500/97539327)  ·  `page_059502`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA will invest $76 million in a new plant near Savannah to manufacture interior and exterior parts for Hyundai Motor Group.
- The new Georgia facility will produce door trim, headlining, and tailgate trim.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans to begin production at the new Georgia plant in October 2024.
- The new plant will hire more than 500 workers.
- The facility is located near Savannah to supply parts for Hyundai's upcoming assembly plant in nearby Ellabell.
- Seoyon E-HWA already operates a plant with 630 employees in LaGrange, Georgia.
- The existing LaGrange plant is located near Hyundai's assembly plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and Kia's plant in West Point.
- Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 manufacturing units worldwide.
- Seoyon E-HWA is the fourth Hyundai supplier to announce plans for a southeast Georgia plant since Hyundai announced its $5.5 billion electric vehicle and battery assembly plant in May.
- Hyundai's planned EV and battery plant could grow to employ 8,100 workers and is scheduled to begin turning out vehicles in 2025.
- Hyundai Mobis previously announced plans for a $926 million powertrain plant in Richmond Hill that will employ 1,500 workers.
- Joon Georgia announced plans for a $317 million plant near Statesboro that will employ 630 workers.
- Ecoplastic announced plans for a $205 million plant employing 456 workers in Register.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp has proclaimed his goal for his second term is to make Georgia the leading state for electric-powered vehicles.
- Trip Tollison, president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, described the new jobs as "well paying" but did not disclose specific wage details.
- Seoyon E-HWA currently imports parts through Savannah's port to supply its existing LaGrange plant.
- The state of Georgia will pay to train workers for the new facility, though the total incentive package from state and local governments was not immediately clear at the time of the announcement.
- Seoyon E-HWA could qualify for more than $3.1 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $1,250 per job over five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 annually.
