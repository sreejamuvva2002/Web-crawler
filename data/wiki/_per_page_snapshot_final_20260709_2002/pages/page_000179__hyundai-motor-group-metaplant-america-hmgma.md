# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces New Production Start Date at Metaplant America During Hyundai Day in Georgia - Alexandria Hyundai](https://www.alexandriahyundai.com/Hyundai-Announces-New-Production-Start-Date-at-Metaplant-America-During-Hyundai-Day-in-Georgia/)  ·  `page_000179`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America moves up start of production to Q4 2024.
- Georgia Governor Kemp, company, and state leaders officially marked Feb. 26, 2024 as Hyundai Day.
- Hyundai will deploy its XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck between HMGMA and its suppliers.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp, First Lady Marty A. Kemp, and José Muñoz celebrated Hyundai Motor Group’s positive impact on the State of Georgia at the event.
- The official start of production has accelerated to the fourth quarter of 2024 with the grand opening celebration targeted for the first quarter of 2025.
- Production was previously announced to begin in the first quarter of 2025.
- The General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to the State of Georgia and recognizing Feb. 26, 2024, as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles, including the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70, along with the hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck for logistics, were on display outside of the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that Georgia is like a second home to Hyundai.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai has been an incredible partner to the state and will help recruit businesses, give back to communities, and work with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Oscar Kwon, president and CEO of HMGMA, stated that 2024 is going to be a great year for the plant and called it the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Joint Development Authority provided continued support and cooperation for the project.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders, Department of Economic Development Director Pat Wilson, and local officials joined the celebration.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA, which will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia that will employ 3,500 workers.
- A recent Center for Automotive Research study expects Hyundai’s investments in Georgia to create nearly 40,000 jobs and generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- HMGMA continues hiring efforts and has developed educational partnerships with area colleges to create a pipeline for future employment.
- The Electric Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate (EVP TCC) is being offered at four area technical colleges as part of this collaboration.
- Students who successfully complete the EVP TCC program receive preferred status when applying for a position at HMGMA.
- Hyundai announced it is actively developing a hydrogen mobility value chain at HMGMA at CES 2024.
- Through the Clean Logistics Project in Georgia, the Metaplant will utilize XCIENT Fuel Cell tractors to manage logistics for the plant and its suppliers.
- The XCIENT is Hyundai’s commercialized class 8 6x4 fuel cell electric model with more than 6.25 million accumulated miles of real-world application.
- Hyundai is developing hydrogen refueling infrastructure needed to support this fuel cell activity.
- This collaboration is anticipated to stimulate economic growth and create job opportunities within the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Georgia facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant where order collection, procurement, logistics, and production are optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
