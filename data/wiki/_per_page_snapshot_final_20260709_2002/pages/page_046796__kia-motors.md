# Kia Motors

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-28

**Source:** [Carmaker Recalls Over 400,000 SUVs Over Crash Risk From ‘Unintended Vehicle Movement’ - Total News](https://totalnews.com/carmaker-recalls-over-400000-suvs-over-crash-risk-from-unintended-vehicle-movement/)  ·  `page_046796`

## Facts

- Kia America is recalling 427,407 Kia Telluride SUVs over concerns that a shaft misalignment could cause the vehicles to move while parked and cause a crash.
- The recall covers all 2020-2023 Telluride SUVs and some 2024 models.
- The intermediate shaft and right front driveshaft in each affected vehicle may not fully mate due to suspected improper assembly by the supplier.
- Affected vehicles were manufactured between January 9, 2019 and October 19, 2023.
- The recalled vehicles represent approximately 1% of all 2020 to 2024 Kia Telluride SUVs manufactured during the same period.
- Kia identified the affected vehicles by examining production records from both Kia and its supplier.
- The supplier responsible for the suspected improper assembly is MOBIS Alabama, LLC, based in West Point, Georgia.
- Dealers will install the latest electronic parking brake (EPB) software to prevent movement of parked vehicles.
- Dealers will also replace any damaged intermediate shafts found during the recall service.
- Kia has asked vehicle owners to move the gear shifter to the ‘P’ (Park) position before exiting the vehicle and to always use the manual EPB until the recall is complete.
- Kia has promised to refund vehicle owners who may have paid to resolve the issue independently.
- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) published a document stating that damaged shaft splines can cause the vehicle to move unintentionally while parked if the parking brake is not engaged.
