# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Georgia’s largest manufacturing presence is in the food-processing sector, employing over 60,000 people across producers like Coca-Cola, Anheuser-Busch, Frito-Lay, and General Mills.
- Transportation is the state’s second largest manufacturing industry, with companies like Kia, Porsche, and Auto Zone operating across Georgia.
- Aerospace manufacturers in Georgia include Gulfstream and Lockheed Martin, while heavy machinery includes Caterpillar, John Deere, and Kubota.
- Over 90 percent of Georgia’s exports come from its manufacturing sector, mainly going to Canada, China, Mexico, Japan, and the United Kingdom.
- Tom Croteau states that billions of dollars worth of goods are exported globally as a result of dedicated, skilled manufacturing in Georgia.
- Governor Nathan Deal launched the Georgia Competitiveness Initiative in 2011 to bring state government and business communities together for long-term economic development strategy.
- The initiative sought to improve competitiveness by asking the private sector what the state could do to help businesses thrive.
- Feedback was received from over 4,000 individuals regarding the Georgia Competitiveness Initiative.
- An overriding issue identified in the feedback was the state’s sales tax on electricity and energy, which the state decided to eliminate.
- Three years later, GDEcD and private industry teamed up through Governor Deal’s High Demand Career Initiative to address a skilled workforce shortage due to Baby Boomer retirements over the next five to ten years.
- The High Demand Career Initiative began with three large statewide meetings personally chaired by Governor Deal, involving private industry leaders and university/technical college/Board of Regents leaders.
- Companies were asked about their workforce needs for today, five years from now, and ten years from now.
- The resounding response from companies was a need for a skilled workforce capable of keeping up with emerging technology and automation globally.
- The initiative targets the educational system to generate awareness and interest in manufacturing careers and ensure graduates have degrees/diplomas matching modern manufacturing needs.
- The High Demand Career Initiative uses a multifaceted plan targeting students at college, high school, and middle school levels.
- A key issue identified is the younger generation's relative uninformed status regarding modern manufacturing careers.
- Generating interest and awareness at the middle school level into high school is seen as crucial for shaping career decisions.
- Steps taken include implementing apprenticeships at the high school level and integrating manufacturing-centric skills into core curriculum.
- Governor Deal recommended amending state policy to allow computer programming courses to satisfy core requirements (math, science, or foreign language) for a high school diploma.
- A press release stated that more than half of the projected job growth in STEM fields will be in computing occupations.
- Training in computing must begin prior to post-secondary education to fill rapidly increasing high-wage, high-skill positions.
- The Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia announced a new ‘Manufacturing Engineering’ degree to be available at Georgia Southern University.
- Manufacturers consistently reported a need for more engineers on plant floors in manufacturing and maintenance areas.
- Assistant engineers are needed where a 4-year degree isn't required, but a technical college degree could help solve minor technical issues.
- GDEcD worked closely with Walmart via their Made in America campaign to assist international suppliers in moving operations back to the U.S. or helping existing U.S. businesses grow.
- Governor Deal stated that they are working to keep Georgia the number one place in the nation for business and need a strong education system responding to company needs.
- Tom Croteau serves as Deputy Commissioner, Global Commerce for GDEcD, leading the team to increase jobs/investment through locating/expanding businesses and growing small businesses.
- The Global Commerce team includes statewide/existing industry project teams, small business, and Georgia’s international offices in ten strategic regions globally.
- Croteau joined GDEcD in 1996 and previously served as Division Director of Projects, Global Commerce.
- Recent company locations/expansions assisted by Croteau include Pilgrim’s Pride (Douglas-Coffee County), Medline Industries (Lithia Springs-Douglas County), Dollar Tree (Savannah), DeWaffelbakkers (McDonough-Henry County), and Starbucks (Augusta).
- Prior to GDEcD, Croteau worked in economic development divisions of MEAG Power (currently Electric Cities of Georgia) and the Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce.
- Croteau also worked for Ernst & Young and the Florida Chamber of Commerce.
- Croteau currently serves on the board of the Georgia Economic Developers Association and the Georgia Quick Start Industry Services Advisory Council.
- Croteau completed Georgia Tech’s Economic Development Course and attended the Economic Development Institute.
- Croteau holds a degree in Economics from Florida State University.
