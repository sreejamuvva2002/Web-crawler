# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Goodyear Still Evaluating Damage at Tupelo Plant | Modern Tire Dealer](https://www.moderntiredealer.com/retail/article/33003520/goodyear-still-evaluating-damage-at-tupelo-plant)  ·  `page_055161`

## Facts

- Remediation efforts are continuing at Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.'s Tupelo, Miss., plant.
- A Goodyear spokesperson says the company is still evaluating the extent of the damage at the Tupelo facility.
- The Tupelo plant has the capacity to build 42,000 passenger tires a day at full throttle.
- The Tupelo plant shut down on April 1 due to a tornado.
- From a daily production capacity standpoint, the Tupelo factory is the second biggest plant in Goodyear’s North American manufacturing network.
- The Tupelo plant was one of three plants acquired by Goodyear when the Akron, Ohio-based tiremaker finalized its purchase of Cooper Tire & Rubber Co. in summer 2021.
- A Goodyear distribution center in Whiteland, Ind., has successfully resumed full operations following an April 1 tornado there.
- None of the inventory already stored at the Whiteland distribution center was damaged.
- Goodyear expects no significant impact to customer shipments from the Whiteland distribution center.
