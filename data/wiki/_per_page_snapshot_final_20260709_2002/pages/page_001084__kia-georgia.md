# Kia Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-02

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Donates $100,000 to Technical College System of Georgia Foundation • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/georgia-news/kia-georgia-donates-100000-to-technical-college-system-of-georgia-foundation/)  ·  `page_001084`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia presented a $100,000 donation to the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) Foundation at an event in Atlanta celebrating TCSG’s top students and instructors.
- Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess presented the check at TCSG’s annual Georgia Occupational Award of Leadership (GOAL) and Rick Perkins Awards banquet in Buckhead.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated that Kia is a valued partner and remarkable corporate citizen in Georgia, and the funding will help technical college students remain on track to graduate and join Georgia’s workforce.
- Since 2016, Kia Georgia has provided funding to award the state GOAL winner with a brand-new Kia as the grand prize.
- The 2023 GOAL winner will receive a 2023 Kia K5, which is built at the Kia facility in West Point.
- Remaining funding from the donation supports The Last Mile Fund to provide gap funding for more than 131,000 technical education students served within TCSG.
- Stuart Countess remarked that TCSG undergirds workforce development by adapting to evolving technical needs of state industries and businesses, contributing to economic growth in manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare.
- In 2006, Kia established a state-of-the-art production facility in West Point, Georgia, representing one of the largest single foreign investments in the history of Georgia.
- The West Point facility has an annual production capacity of more than 340,000 vehicles.
- Kia Georgia produces the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV, Sportage SUV, and K5 midsize sedan at the West Point facility.
- Kia recently announced plans to produce the Kia EV9 at the West Point facility, launching its first battery-electric vehicle to be produced in the U.S.
- The West Point advanced manufacturing facility represents a $1.9 billion investment on a 2,200-acre site and operates 24 hours a day.
- Kia Georgia’s partnership with TCSG produced a Maintenance Certification Program through collaboration with Quick Start to upskill production team members for maintenance tasks on highly automated production equipment.
- The Maintenance Certification Program was expanded to West Georgia Technical College and integrated with the campus's standard offerings.
- The program provides a steady stream of qualified candidates to fill maintenance roles at Kia Georgia and other local manufacturers.
