# Savannah Economic Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-20

**Source:** [Announcements Archives - Page 2 of 8 - SEDA](https://seda.org/category/announcements/page/2/)  ·  `page_060472`

## Facts

- World Trade Center Savannah hosted its sixth annual Prosperity Through Trade Luncheon on Thursday, May 15, in conjunction with World Trade Month.
- Attendees at the luncheon included World Trade Center Savannah Board of Directors Chairperson John Coleman, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Chief Administrative Officer Brent Stubbs, and Georgia Southern University Vice President.
- The 2025 Savannah Economic Development Authority and World Trade Center Savannah meeting dates are listed, with all meetings held at 906 Drayton St. unless otherwise noted.
- SEDA Board of Directors Meetings are scheduled for 11 a.m. Jan. 15, 8:30 a.m., SEDA Annual Meeting at Savannah Convention Center on Feb. 11, April 17, and May 13.
- On November 20, 2024, the Regional Industry Support Enterprise, Inc. (RISE) held its first annual meeting, officially forming and appointing officers for its board of directors.
- The RISE board represents eight counties: Bryan, Bulloch, Candler, Chatham, Effingham, Evans, Liberty, and Screven.
- HydroFleet announced it is investing nearly $33 million in Pooler, Ga., to construct a hydrogen production and fueling station that will service zero-emission heavy-duty hydrogen fuel cell trucks.
- At the November 2024 Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) Board of Directors meeting, the board unanimously voted to increase the Savannah Entertainment Production Incentive rebate from $100,000 to $175,000 for feature films and from $250,000 to $300,000 for television or internet-distributed episodic productions.
- At the April 16, 2024 Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) board of directors meeting, staff provided an update on the naming and activities of a regional workforce organization announced in December 2023.
- The new workforce organization was a recommendation from the Regional Workforce Study released in November 2023.
- At the Jan. 4 Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) Annual Meeting, SEDA President and CEO Trip Tollison announced that the authority facilitated 11 company announcements in 2023 resulting in 3,731 new job opportunities and $2,631,994,199 in capital investment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Plastic Express, a leader in the plastic resins shipping industry, will build a second shipping facility in Savannah, Georgia, creating an estimated 200 new jobs and $80 million in investment in Chatham County.
- At the Savannah Economic Development Authority Board of Directors meeting, Vend Tech International, the company behind Naturals2Go vending machines and gourmet coffee equipment, announced it will relocate its headquarters, training center, assembly manufacturing, global distribution, and technology development facility to the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames, will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), creating over 500 jobs.
