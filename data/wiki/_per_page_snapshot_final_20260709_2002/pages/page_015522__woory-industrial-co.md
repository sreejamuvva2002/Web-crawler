## Facts

- Woory Industrial Co., a South Korean producer of heating, ventilation and air conditioning systems for vehicles, announced it will construct an $18 million factory in Dublin, Georgia.
- The new facility is located roughly 95 miles northwest of Hyundai’s Metaplant near Savannah.
- Woory Industrial Co. promised to hire 130 workers for the new plant.
- Hyundai suppliers have been moving to rural and Coastal Georgia to partner with the future EV manufacturing plant.
- Eight large parts companies, also known as tier one suppliers, have announced factories within the Metaplant’s orbit.
- The Pad Construction Company, a Joint Venture between Atlanta Department of Economic Development and Georgia Commerce Commission, is the largest supplier of this project, according to Woory Kim, President of Woory Industrial Co.
- Woory Kim stated that they are looking forward to expanding their component supply chain in Georgia as well as other Korean companies that have announced plans to expand operations there.
- The Wall Street Journal reported that Hyundai Motor Group will build a new factory near Dublin, County 404.
- Hyundai Motor Group is building a $5.54 billion electric vehicle factory near Savannah.
- Korean companies have pledged to create over 82,000 jobs and invest $22.7 billion in Georgia since 2021.
- This investment marks the first of several new investments in Georgia by Korean companies.
- Hyundai Motor Group will also expand its existing battery cell manufacturing facilities at the plant.
