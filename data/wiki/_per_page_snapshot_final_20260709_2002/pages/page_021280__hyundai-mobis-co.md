# Hyundai Mobis Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis to sell overseas bumper business for $325mn - 매일경제 영문뉴스 펄스(Pulse)](https://pulse.mk.co.kr/news/english/12089699)  ·  `page_021280`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis Co. is selling its 500 billion won ($324.5 million) overseas bumper business to three South Korean mid-sized auto parts makers.
- Hyundai Mobis is shifting its focus to robotics, software and other future growth businesses.
- According to industry sources on Thursday, Hyundai Mobis recently named Seoyon E-hwa Co., Ecoplastic Corp. and Plakor Co. as preferred bidders for different parts of the business.
- The assets include bumper subsidiaries and manufacturing facilities in Alabama and Georgia, the United States, Beijing, China, Slovakia, Brazil and Mexico.
- Global suppliers, including France’s OPmobility SE, competed for the assets.
- Hyundai Mobis chose Korean middle-market suppliers with long-standing business ties to Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp.
- Seoyon E-hwa will acquire the U.S. operations, considered the portfolio’s most valuable assets.
- The U.S. operations include plants in Montgomery, Alabama, and West Point, Georgia.
- Seoyon E-hwa will also acquire the Slovakia operation and the Beijing subsidiary.
- The Beijing operation had been viewed as a less attractive asset because of weak local sales by Hyundai Motor and Kia.
- Industry sources said Seoyon E-hwa agreed to acquire the China unit as part of the package after securing the U.S. operations.
- Ecoplastic was selected as the preferred bidder for the Mexico operation.
- Plakor was chosen for the Brazil business.
- The acquisitions will allow both companies to immediately join Hyundai Motor and Kia’s global supply chain.
- Suppliers located near vehicle assembly plants generally enjoy a strong competitive advantage after making the required upfront investment in production facilities as automotive bumpers are bulky and costly to transport.
- Hyundai Mobis sold its domestic bumper business in Asan, South Chungcheong Province, to domestic auto parts maker BMI last year.
- With the planned sale of its overseas operations, Hyundai Mobis will exit the bumper manufacturing business.
- The divestment reflects Hyundai Mobis’ strategy to reduce its reliance on traditional hardware manufacturing, once a key profit driver, and accelerate its transformation into a software-defined vehicle company focused on autonomous driving and software.
- Hyundai Mobis is also seeking to sell its automotive lighting business.
- The company and the preferred bidders are expected to sign share purchase agreements next month after completing due diligence.
