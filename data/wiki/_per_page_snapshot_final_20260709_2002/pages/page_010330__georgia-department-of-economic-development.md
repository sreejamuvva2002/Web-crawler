# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-04

**Source:** [2025 Economic Yearbook - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/03/31/2025-economic-yearbook/)  ·  `page_010330`

## Facts

- Between January 1 and December 4, 2024, Georgia recorded more than $13.7 billion in investments.
- These investments are expected to create 19,500 new jobs over the next few years.
- The state saw an estimated 360 new locations or expansions across its 159 counties during the same period.
- Investment sectors span technology, electric vehicle manufacturing, food processing, and life sciences.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Duracell is establishing a new global headquarters for research and development in Atlanta.
- PBS Aerospace will locate its North American headquarters, along with manufacturing and R&D operations, in Roswell.
- During the last fiscal year (July to December 4), 83% of expansions and new locations occurred outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Expansions outside the Atlanta metro area represented more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Georgia has been ranked the No. 1 state for doing business for a record 11 consecutive years.
- Misti Martin, deputy commissioner of global commerce for the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that strategic investments in workforce development and infrastructure combine with lasting partnerships to give Georgia consistent advantages as a global business gateway.
