# Kia Georgia

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** West Point, GA, Troup County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Kia Georgia launched the all-electric 2025 Kia EV9 at its West Point manufacturing plant, marking the first electric vehicle produced in the state and completing a $200 million expansion that added about 200 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

First EV produced in Georgia; expands electric vehicle manufacturing capacity in the state.

- **West Point Manufacturing Plant** — Site where the Kia EV9 was produced; part of a $200 million expansion.
- **Kia Georgia Assembly Plant** — Manufactures the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage SUVs and the all-electric EV9.
- **Kia Georgia Plant** — The facility recently completed a $217 million expansion, adding about 200 jobs.
- **Kia Georgia Manufacturing Plant** — Located on 2,200 acres; began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009; currently assembles EV9, Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs.
- **West Point Plant** — The plant reached a cumulative production of 5 million vehicles and produced the 2027 Kia Telluride hybrid, the first hybrid EV built in Georgia.
- **West Point Manufacturing Plant** — 3.2 million square feet of automotive hail protection and 10 MW of solar capacity have been installed to strengthen resilience and sustainability.
- **West Point Plant** — Sole global production site for the Telluride; operating since 2009 and has grown into one of the largest employers in the region.
- **Kia West Point Assembly Plant** — Will begin assembling the EV9 electric vehicle in 2024; originally built with a $1 billion investment in 2006 and expanded to $1.8 billion.
