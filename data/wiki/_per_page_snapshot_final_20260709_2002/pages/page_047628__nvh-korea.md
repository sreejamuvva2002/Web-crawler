# NVH Korea

**Source:** [협력업체 NVH 코리아, 조지아주 진출 - 애틀랜타 K 뉴스](https://atlantak.com/협력업체-nvh-코리아-조지아주-진출/)  ·  `page_047628`

## Facts

- Korean NVH Korea (Chairman Gu Ja-gyeom) is establishing its first US production facility in Georgia.
- On the 22nd, Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that auto parts supplier Korean NVH Korea will invest $72 million in Locust Grove, Henry County.
- The project is expected to create over 160 new jobs (subhead notes 150 jobs).
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia's remarkable growth in the e-mobility and battery industries has enabled support for large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- Governor Kemp, currently attending the Paris Air Show in France, noted this is the second economic development project secured by Georgia this week alone.
- Governor Kemp expressed pride in welcoming NVH Korea as a partner in Georgia, describing it as the center of the cutting-edge industrial revolution.
- NVH Korea is headquartered in Ulsan and was founded in 1984.
- The company currently operates production facilities in Korea, Europe, and India.
- NVH Korea specializes in manufacturing automotive noise, vibration, and heat control parts.
- The Georgia plant plans to supply EV battery parts to Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor EV plants.
- Chairman Gu Ja-gyeom expressed pleasure at selecting Georgia as the company's first EV battery parts manufacturing site.
- Chairman Gu stated this marks the first step in efforts to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry and thanked Henry County and Georgia for their cooperation.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive.
- Production at the new facility is scheduled to begin in the second quarter of next year.
