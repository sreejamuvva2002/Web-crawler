# Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-09-04

**Source:** [Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. to create 100 jobs in Newton County - On Common Ground News - 24/7 local news](https://ocgnews.com/nisshinbo-automotive-manufacturing-inc-create-100-jobs-newton-county/)  ·  `page_058263`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. (NAMI) will create 100 jobs and invest $72 million in an expansion of its Covington facility.
- New jobs at the facility will include positions in production and administration.
- The facility expansion is located in Newton County.
- NAMI has maintained a presence in Newton County for more than two decades.
- Along with its business development center in Sterling Heights, Michigan, NAMI’s Covington facility produces high-quality friction materials for automotive manufacturers and develops environmentally friendly, copper-free brake pads.
- NAMI CEO Shigeru Matsuki announced the expansion together with the Governor’s Office and the Newton County Industrial Development Authority.
- NAMI has been based in Covington for the last 20 years and hopes to grow there for the next 20 years and beyond.
- Select Newton Vice President of Economic Development David Bernd noted that more than $70 million in Foreign Direct Investment is being created for 100 well-paying advanced manufacturing careers.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Project Manager for International Investment Joseph Huntemann represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with Select Newton.
- Japanese-affiliated companies currently operate more than 500 facilities in Georgia that employ approximately 30,000 Georgians.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that NAMI’s success in Georgia is a combination of strong international relationships and a pro-business environment.
- Gov. Nathan Deal emphasized that Georgia provides the business climate, skilled workforce and thriving automotive sector necessary for manufacturers like NAMI to enjoy long-term growth.
- Gov. Nathan Deal noted that with this facility expansion in Newton County, NAMI will continue to benefit from close proximity to top vehicle manufacturers while also reaching customers across the nation and around the world with Georgia’s logistics infrastructure.
- Gov. Nathan Deal highlighted that international economic missions are delivering tangible results, as NAMI’s commitment directly reflects the strength of Georgia’s manufacturing environment for global industry leaders.
- Gov. Nathan Deal expressed appreciation for NAMI’s investment over the last 20 years and look forward to the future growth of this partnership.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that they had the opportunity to meet with NAMI leadership last year in Japan and are very excited to see the company expand in Newton County.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed looking forward to NAMI’s continued success.
