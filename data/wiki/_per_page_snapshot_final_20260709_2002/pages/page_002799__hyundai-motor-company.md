# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Announces 2025 Annual and Q4 Business Results](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-announces-2025-annual-and-q4-business-results-0000001116)  ·  `page_002799`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company announced its 2025 annual and fourth quarter business results on January 29, 2026.
- In 2025, the company sold 4,138,389 vehicles worldwide, nearly unchanged from 2024.
- In 2025, annual revenue reached a record-high of KRW 186.3 trillion, up 6.3 percent year over year.
- In 2025, annual operating profit totaled KRW 11.47 trillion, down 19.5 percent year over year due to global trade uncertainties including tariff impacts.
- In 2025, operating profit margin stood at 6.2 percent.
- In 2025, annual net profit amounted to KRW 10.36 trillion, down 21.7 percent year over year.
- Hyundai Motor achieved a historic milestone in the U.S., recording its first-ever one million units in annual wholesale.
- The U.S. sales milestone was driven by a robust SUV portfolio and accelerating hybrid electric vehicle sales.
- In Korea, full-year sales volume increased 1.1 percent year-over-year in 2025, driven by successful new model launches including the PALISADE SUV and its hybrid variant.
- In Q4 2025, Hyundai Motor sold 1,033,043 units globally, a 3.1 percent decrease from the year earlier.
- In Q4 2025, sales outside Korea totaled 855,547 units, down 2.4 percent year over year.
- In Q4 2025, sales in Korea came to 177,496 units, down 6.3 percent year over year, reflecting a lower number of business days in October.
- In Q4 2025, Hyundai Motor recorded its highest ever fourth-quarter revenue of KRW 46.84 trillion, up 0.5 percent year over year.
- Q4 2025 revenue growth was driven by solid sales of high-value models such as hybrids and Genesis brand vehicles, as well as favorable exchange rates.
- In Q4 2025, operating profit totaled KRW 1.7 trillion, down 39.9 percent year over year, reflecting the impact of U.S. tariffs and higher incentives amid intensified competition.
- In Q4 2025, operating profit margin stood at 3.6 percent.
- In Q4 2025, net profit including non-controlling interests amounted to KRW 1.18 trillion, down 52.1 percent year over year.
- For full-year 2025, Hyundai Motor sold 961,812 electrified vehicles globally, up 27 percent year-over-year.
- The 2025 global electrified vehicle sales comprised 275,669 EVs and 634,990 HEVs.
- Electrified vehicles accounted for approximately 23 percent of total global sales in 2025.
- In 2025, HEV sales grew 27.9 percent year-over-year, achieving a record-high share of 15.3 percent of total global sales.
- In Q4 2025, global electrified vehicle sales increased 12.1 percent year over year to 234,957 units.
- Q4 2025 electrified vehicle demand was supported by solid demand across major markets, including North America.
- In Q4 2025, EV sales totaled 56,625 units globally.
- In Q4 2025, HEV sales reached 168,002 units globally.
- HEVs represented a record-high 16.3 percent of Hyundai Motor’s global quarterly sales in Q4 2025.
- In the U.S. market in Q4 2025, HEVs reached an all-time quarterly high share of 22.6 percent.
- Hyundai Motor achieved its 2025 annual guidance, with revenue growth exceeding targets and operating margin remaining within guided ranges.
- The company delivered a 6.3 percent annual revenue increase in 2025, surpassing its 5 to 6 percent target range.
- For 2026, Hyundai Motor is targeting consolidated revenue growth of between 1 to 2 percent.
- For 2026, the company targets an annual operating profit margin of 6.3 to 7.3 percent.
- For 2026, the company aims for total vehicle sales of more than 4.16 million units.
- The company plans to expand its investment for 2026 total to KRW 17.8 trillion.
- The 2026 investment plan includes KRW 9 trillion in capital expenditure, KRW 7.4 trillion in research and development, and KRW 1.4 trillion in strategic investments.
- Hyundai Motor will pay a year-end dividend of KRW 2,500 per common share for full-year 2025.
- The company's total payout per common share for full-year 2025 is KRW 10,000.
- The company announced a share buyback plan of KRW 400.7 billion in line with its shareholder return policy.
- Hyundai Motor expects challenging global market conditions to continue in 2026 due to stagnant industry growth and intensifying competition across emerging markets.
- To navigate the market environment, the company is reinforcing its leadership in electrification and expanding localized production capabilities.
- The company is advancing cost-optimization across global operations and maintaining a flexible powertrain strategy meeting diverse customer needs for EVs, HEVs, or highly efficient internal combustion models.
- As new production bases ramp up operations, the company aims to accelerate sales growth and respond swiftly to regional market and supply-chain needs.
- Hyundai Motor Company was established in 1967 and is present in over 200 countries with more than 120,000 employees.
- The company invests in advanced technologies such as robotics and Advanced Air Mobility (AAM).
- Hyundai continues efforts to introduce zero emission vehicles equipped with industry-leading hydrogen fuel cell and EV technologies.
