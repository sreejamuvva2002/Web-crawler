# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-03

**Source:** [Rivian To Get $1.5 Billion In Public Incentives For Georgia Plant](https://insideevs.com/news/583448/rivian-get-1500-million-usd-public-incentives-georgia-plant/)  ·  `page_046716`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive will receive a total of $1.5 billion in state and local incentives and tax credits for the new EV plant it plans to build in Georgia.
- The incentive package is by far the largest Georgia has ever offered to a company investing in the state.
- Documents outlining the incentives were signed by Rivian and the state on May 2.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson described the size of the package as being "appropriate" for the largest single industrial announcement in Georgia history.
- The $5 billion plant is expected to employ some 7,500 people by 2028.
- The average annual salary will be $56,000.
- All employees will also receive equity in Rivian.
- The two parties estimate that the new plant will create 7,978 additional indirect jobs.
- Together, these 15,478 jobs are expected to generate over $1 billion in labor income annually.
- The Economic Development Agreement (EDA) was between the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties, the State of Georgia, and Rivian.
- The EDA was approved and signed by all parties on May 2, marking a milestone in the automaker's progress towards the development of its second US manufacturing plant.
- Rivian stated that the "long-term economic partnership promises to deliver value to Rivian, the people of Georgia and their kids' kids' kids."
- Rivian released the site plan of the future campus as well as an exterior rendering of the front of the plant building.
- The rendering shows planned green spaces filled with native plants.
- Over 50% of the site's acreage will remain unpaved when the plant reaches maximum production capacity.
- Rivian also operates a plant in Normal, Illinois.
- Approximately 5,000 people at the Illinois plant build Rivian R1T electric pickups, R1S electric SUVs, and electric vans for Amazon.
- Rivian expects to break ground at the new Georgia site as early as this summer.
- Rivian forecasts that production will start in 2024 at its Georgia plant.
- The project has faced opposition from residents east of Atlanta who say that the factory will spoil their rural quality of life.
- A press statement from Georgia state leaders notes that the agreement "ensures that the project follows locally required standards pertaining to water quality, groundwater recharge and runoff, and local ordinances."
