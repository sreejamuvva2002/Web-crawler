## Facts

- Yazaki North America specializes in automotive power, data, and display technologies within the automotive industry.
- The company offers a range of products including power distribution and control systems, wire harnesses, connectors, sensors, and driver information systems.
- Yazaki's products are designed to support hybrid and electric vehicles, providing essential connectivity and power solutions for vehicle applications.
- It was founded in 1966 and is based in Canton, Michigan.
- Yazaki North America operates as a subsidiary of Yazaki.
- Headquarters Location is 6801 N Haggerty, Canton, Michigan, 48187, United States.
- Yazaki North America has filed 19 patents.
- The 3 most popular patent topics include electrical connectors, capacitors, and coaxial connectors.
- A patent application dated 10/18/2022 was granted on 6/17/2025 for computer buses, electrical connectors, locks, locksmithing, and bones of the head and neck.
- According to a detailed NHTSA filing (Recall No. 25V769) dated Nov 14, 2025, a defect stems from a three-day period in July 2025 when automated crimping equipment was offline for maintenance, causing the backup manual process to produce inconsistent terminal connections.
- The issue affects vehicles built between July 31 and October 24, 2025, creating a risk of increased injury in certain frontal crashes.
- GMC's investigation shows that the defective wire harness, built by Yazaki North America, may interfere with the airbag's ability to receive deployment signals.
- Although some vehicles produced during the period were corrected before leaving the plant, 245 SUVs and 109 pickups remain at risk.
- GMC says owners may notice an airbag warning light, but not all vehicles will display one.
- Dealers will install a jumper harness with properly crimped connectors at no cost, with owner notifications scheduled to begin December 22, 2025.
- The issue was first flagged on August 21, 2025, when a loose wire was noticed during assembly of a Hummer EV SUV.
- GM opened a formal investigation days later and traced the problem to a manual crimping procedure used during supplier machine downtime.
- No field incidents or injuries have been reported, but GM opted to issue a safety recall after confirming inconsistent harness integrity.
- The airbag defect adds to a broader pattern of scrutiny surrounding GM's safety campaigns and legal pressure over past recalls.
- This latest recall will likely intensify conversations about quality control as GM scales its EV lineup.
