# SK battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK on’s Georgia battery plant runs at full capacity - 매일경제 영문뉴스 펄스(Pulse)](https://pulse.mk.co.kr/news/english/11378985)  ·  `page_050047`

## Facts

- SK on’s Georgia battery plant is now operating at full capacity, four years after it began production.
- The facility is located in Commerce, Georgia.
- The site operates as a high-security facility with strict external access controls.
- The facility was unveiled to the media for the first time during a recent visit by Maeil Business Newspaper.
- Robotic arms and machines assemble components, from manufacturing battery materials such as cathodes and anodes to the stacking process of battery cell assembly.
- Locally hired workers who completed systematic technical training programs monitor real-time production data and output via large screens installed along each production line.
- The monitors display a yield rate of around 95 percent, reflecting virtually defect-free production.
- SKBA is advancing its smart factory initiative by integrating artificial intelligence (AI) inspection systems into its battery manufacturing and quality control processes.
- Plants 1 and 2 are running at full capacity.
- The facility produces batteries sufficient for approximately 300,000 electric vehicles annually, equivalent to 22 GWh.
- The company overcame numerous challenges since the start of commercial operations in 2022 to reach full operational capacity within four years.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the automaker’s electric vehicle production hub in Georgia, is beginning operations earlier in 2025.
- SKBA is experiencing accelerated growth as a key battery supplier due to Hyundai's upcoming operations.
- SKBA Chief Executive Officer Yoonsang Chaey emphasized the company’s focus on process optimization and workforce training to achieve high productivity.
- CEO Yoonsang Chaey stated, “We worked hard to achieve top-level productivity by fine-tuning the compatibility between equipment and workers.”
- CEO Yoonsang Chaey added, “The yield rate represents field competitiveness built on technology, people, and countless trials, making this achievement even more meaningful given the challenges of U.S. manufacturing.”
