# BYD Company Limited

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- In Tbilisi, passing cars display the slogan “Build Your Dreams,” which is BYD’s branded motto.
- BYD is China’s largest automaker.
- Chinese vehicles are increasingly visible on streets in Tbilisi and other Georgian cities.
- Official data for January-July 2025 shows China accounted for just over 10 percent of Georgian imports, ranking fourth behind Turkey, the United States, and Russia.
- Passenger cars are Georgia’s largest import product, accounting for 20 percent of all imports.
- The auto sector is significant for domestic demand and Georgia’s role as a regional re-export hub.
- China’s increasing presence underscores its strategic interest in gaining a larger share of the country’s auto market.
- While Georgia’s annual imported car numbers have remained steady, the share of electric vehicles is surging.
- During the first half of this year, the country imported 3,616 EVs, an 88 percent jump from 1,922 during the same period last year.
- Until recently, China did not rank among Georgia’s top five car-supplying countries.
- China is now the second-largest EV supplier after the US.
- Overall imports of Chinese cars, both electric and conventional, have risen roughly 79 percent this year.
- Electric vehicles still make up a relatively small share of the overall auto market in Georgia, where nearly every second person owns a car.
- Most Georgian consumers have long relied on cheap, second-hand cars from the US that are easy to repair locally.
- EVs do not fit this local repair model, which helps explain why the US remains the country’s main car supplier.
- China has captured nearly 12 percent of Georgia’s EV market in just a couple of months.
- Demand for EVs is rising sharply due to strong appeal and lower running costs compared to gasoline-powered cars.
- Widespread EV adoption requires charging infrastructure, which Georgia currently lacks.
- Market signals suggest that charging infrastructure development is about to change.
- Chinese EVs are more affordable than their Western-made counterparts, positioning them to capture an increasing share of the Georgian market.
- Growing Chinese economic influence goes hand in hand with politics, specifically the ruling Georgian Dream party’s pro-China stance.
- Prime Minister Irakli Kobakhidze praised Communist China as the “best example of state modernization” and argued that Beijing sets a positive example for other global powers.
- The BYD Center in Tbilisi has recently taken over the former headquarters of the United National Movement, now a major opposition force.
- The building was built in 2010 for party operations and slipped out of the opposition’s hands amid financial and political pressures from the government.
- Transparency International Georgia noted in its latest report that as relations with the US and EU deteriorate, Georgian Dream is strengthening alliances with authoritarian regimes hostile to the US, such as China.
- Irakli Machaidze is a contributing writer covering the South Caucasus for Eurasianet.
