# Norma Precision Ammunition

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-11

**Source:** [Norma Precision Ammunition Relocates its U.S. Headquarters to Georgia | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/norma-precision-ammunition-relocates-its-us-headquarters-manufacturing-distribution)  ·  `page_006044`

## Facts

- Norma Precision Ammunition, a subsidiary of RUAG Ammotec, established its U.S. headquarters, manufacturing site, and warehousing and distribution operations in Chatham County, Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the relocation on April 11, 2022.
- The company is currently hiring for advanced manufacturing, distribution, management, and sales opportunities.
- RUAG Ammotec operates in 12 different countries.
- RUAG Ammotec develops and produces high-end ammunition for sports shooters, special operations forces, and peace officers worldwide.
- The company serves key markets in the military, law enforcement, hunting, and sporting goods sectors.
- In 2021, Norma Precision Ammunition imported over 400 containers of ammunition from RUAG Ammotec factories in Europe.
- In 2021, the company delivered over 30 million cartridges of ammunition made in the U.S.
- Norma Precision Ammunition has been manufacturing in the United States for over 10 years under the name RUAG Ammotec, USA.
- The four years prior to the announcement were marked by steady growth.
- Christoph Eisenhardt serves as President and CEO of RUAG Ammotec.
- Paul Lemke serves as President of Norma Precision Ammunition.
- The company's facility is located at 141 Prosperity Drive in Garden City, Georgia.
- Trip Tollison serves as President and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- Griff Lynch serves as Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Pat Wilson serves as Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- According to National Shooting Sports Foundation data, Georgia has become one of the country's top 10 states for firearm manufacturing.
- Georgia's GDP was $59.5 billion in 2020.
- The Georgia Department of Labor estimated there were 392,508 manufacturing jobs in the state as of the third quarter of 2021.
- RUAG Ammotec markets its product offerings under the Norma and Swiss P brands in the U.S.
- The company brings Swiss, German, Hungarian and Swedish high-tech offerings to the U.S.
