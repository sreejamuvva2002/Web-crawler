# Freyr

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Coweta County, Georgia, Coweta County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Norwegian battery firm Freyr announced plans for a new "Giga America" battery factory in Coweta County, Georgia that will produce lithium-ion cells primarily for energy storage applications.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant will supply batteries for stationary energy storage, a key component of the electric-vehicle ecosystem and grid integration.

- **Giga America** — Located on a 368-acre tract on the southwestern edge of the Atlanta metropolitan area; will use Boston-based 24M's SemiSolid manufacturing process.

## Investment

- **$1.7 billion** — First-phase construction
  - Targeted 34 GWh production capacity in the initial phase.
- **$2.6 billion** (by 2029) — Second-phase expansion
  - Adds additional cell production lines, bringing total investment to $2.6 B.

## Key facts

- Freyr plans a 368-acre "Giga America" battery factory in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The first phase targets 34 GWh of production capacity with a $1.7 billion investment.
- A second phase will add more cell lines and raise total investment to $2.6 billion by 2029.
- Freyr expects an incentive package from the State of Georgia, Coweta County, and tax credits under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The company is applying to the U.S. Department of Energy for a federal grant or direct loans.
- The plant will use Boston-based 24M's SemiSolid manufacturing process; technicians from 24M will work with Freyr staff from Norway.
- Freyr cites Rystad Energy estimates that combined U.S. and European energy-storage demand could reach 1.4 TWh by 2030, a 34-fold increase from 2022.
- Raw material price surges may delay further battery affordability gains until at least 2024.
