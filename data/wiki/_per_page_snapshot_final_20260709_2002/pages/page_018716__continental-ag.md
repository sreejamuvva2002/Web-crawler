# Continental AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Continental AG’s Closure of the Aldora Mills Plant in Barnesville, Georgia – 360News.com](https://360news.com/west-central-georgia-community-news/continental-ags-closure-of-the-aldora-mills-plant-in-barnesville-georgia/)  ·  `page_018716`

## Facts

- Continental AG issued a formal press release stating that it would discontinue operations at its textile manufacturing facility, Aldora Mills, in Barnesville, Georgia, by the end of 2026.
- The facility employs approximately 230 people.
- The plant produces “textile reinforcement materials”, including tire-cord fabric, hose yarn, and knitted fabric used by Continental’s Tires and ContiTech business units.
- The plant was acquired by Continental in 1988 and has roots going back long before that as a cotton mill and tire-cord fabric producer.
- According to the company’s release, the closure decision followed a “comprehensive business review” aimed at safeguarding profitability, competitiveness and business performance in the Americas region.
- Continental stated the plant “has not been cost competitive for many years despite continuous turnaround efforts.”
- Changes in global market dynamics and supply chains were cited as additional pressures making this decision necessary.
- The plant’s closure means the loss of a major local employer in Barnesville-Lamar County, with the layoffs timed by end-2026.
- For the region, such an announcement brings significant economic and social consequences including job loss, decreased local spending, ripple effects for suppliers, and the need for workforce transition support.
- The site had long been part of the local industrial base, operating since the early 20th century originally as a cotton mill, then shifting to tire-cord fabrics in the 1920s, sold to General Tire & Rubber in 1930, which later became part of Continental.
- Continental emphasizes its commitment to support affected employees during the transition through career counselling and access to employment resources in collaboration with local partners.
- The company reiterated that the U.S. remains a key market for Continental, noting that it has invested about US$1.5 billion in its U.S. tire manufacturing operations over the last decade.
- The official end of operations at the Aldora Mills plant is slated for late 2026.
- Over the coming months, Continental will work with local authorities, workforce organizations and employees to execute the transition plan including job counselling, potential redeployment, and managing the winding-down of the facility.
- Local leaders will likely face the challenge of attracting new investment or repurposing the site to mitigate job losses and fill the economic void.
- The closure of such a long-standing manufacturing facility is part of a broader trend of global supply-chain shifts, automation, cost pressures, and strategic consolidation in manufacturing industries.
- For Barnesville and the surrounding region which includes counties like Upson, Meriwether, Lamar, etc., the news underscores the importance of economic diversification and support for workforce transition.
