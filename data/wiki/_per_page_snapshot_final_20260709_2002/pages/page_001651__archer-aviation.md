# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer will manufacture eVTOL ‘Midnight’ in Georgia - Online EV Marketplace](https://onlineev.com/archer-will-manufacture-evtol-midnight-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001651`

## Facts

- California’s eVTOL startup Archer has chosen a site in Georgia for its first manufacturing facility in Covington, close to an airfield.
- Archer targets making 650 flying cabs a year from 2024.
- Production capability is expected to rise considerably within ten years.
- Archer plans to initially construct out a 350,000-square-foot manufacturing facility (about 32,516 sqm).
- The facility can be increased by an extra 550,000 sq. ft.
- The expanded capacity will support the manufacturing of up to 2,300 planes yearly.
- Archer expects to complete the primary stage in 2024.
- Archer intends to invest $118 million over the next ten years.
- Archer will receive an incentive bundle totaling 33% of its capital investment dedication from the state of Georgia.
- Georgia is ruled by Republican Brian Kemp.
- Georgia has attracted multiple investments correlated with Biden’s infrastructure funding and Inflation Reduction Act, including local incentives.
- Archer listed the expert local labor market, utility availability, costs, and logistics as “key issues” for its site selection.
- Archer also cited the power to conduct seamless flight test operations as a key factor.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s CEO, stated they are excited to pick Georgia after many months of debate and analysis.
- Governor Kemp said they were thrilled to welcome Archer to the state.
- Georgia's Fast Start workforce training program will help fill over 1,000 jobs in Covington long-term.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace firms.
- Georgia has a dozen high schools that offer training in the subject.
- Georgia has five technical schools with aviation programs.
- Several universities in Georgia offer degrees in aerospace engineering.
- Despite Georgia awaiting multiple battery firms like SK Innovation or Freyr to open cell factories, Archer opted for an abroad provider.
- Molicel of Taiwan will manufacture and provide the batteries for Archer’s eVTOL aircraft, Midnight.
- Molicel plans to establish its first Gigafactory in Taiwan.
- Molicel's Gigafactory is scheduled to ramp up manufacturing in 2023.
- The facility will have the capability to supply 1.8 gigawatt-hours (GWh) of battery capacity yearly.
- Archer’s CEO pointed to Molicel’s 40+ years of high-volume manufacturing expertise in the battery business.
- Archer claims it has selected more than 64% of its suppliers for its bill-of-materials for the eVTOL.
- Plans for such an eVTOL surfaced in May 2020.
- The Midnight, formerly known as ‘Maker’ in the prototype stage, is a four-seater aircraft.
- The Midnight initially targeted intra-city travel in the form of an air taxi of around 60 miles (slightly below 100 kilometres).
- The current announcement mentions back-to-back short-distance flights of about 20 miles with roughly 10-minute charging cycles in between.
- Archer disclosed no information regarding battery size this time.
- Early information had specified the usable battery capacity at 143 kWh.
- 80 kWh was meant for flight (“cruise”).
- 26 kWh was meant for hovering.
- 37 kWh was meant as a reserve.
- Archer updated its cooperation with United Airways.
- In February last year, Archer mentioned it needed to run 200 Archer eVTOLs finally.
- Archer revealed the first route: Downtown Manhattan to Newark Liberty International Airport.
- They estimate the ultra-short flight will take ten minutes using present infrastructure like the Downtown Manhattan Heliport.
- Michael Leskinen, President of United Airways Ventures, stated electrical air taxis will change how United customers experience comfort, convenience, and efficiency during airport commutes.
- The New York to Newark route marks a benchmark toward providing a carbon-neutral and safe commute that drastically reduces journey time and produces a fraction of the sound emitted by helicopters.
- Archer refers to new transport methods between the airport to the city centre as “trunk” routes.
- Once trunk routes are established, Archer will begin building out “branch” routes to connect to surrounding communities.
- The first part of this UAM network is targeted for launch in 2025.
