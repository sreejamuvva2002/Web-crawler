# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Ramps up Electrification Supply Chain](https://www.waste360.com/waste-recycling/georgia-ramps-up-electrification-supply-chain)  ·  `page_007866`

## Facts

- Georgia is on an aggressive trajectory to advance a full electric vehicle (EV) supply chain, with significant activity occurring in the past year alone.
- Ascend Elements recently launched an EV battery recycling plant in Georgia.
- Several EV and vehicle battery manufacturers announced plans to locate in Georgia around the same time as Ascend Elements' launch.
- Korean-based SungEel HiTech is preparing to establish operations in Georgia.
- SungEel HiTech will invest $37 million in a new battery recycling plant under its subsidiary, SungEel Recycling Park Georgia.
- Georgia state officials and SungEel HiTech declined to provide interviews regarding the venture.
- Georgia reports that more than 30 “EV-related” projects have brought in about $13.3 billion in investments since 2020.
- The state is home to operations from about 23 e-mobility companies.
- Georgia has more than 1,200 miles of federally designated EV-ready alternative fuel corridors along its interstates and highways.
- Georgia has plans for expansion leveraging $135 million in expected funding from the Biden Administration’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.
- SungEel's new plant will recycle end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap, including nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- SungEel utilizes a recycling process with a reported greater-than 95 percent metal recovery rate.
- SungEel's plant will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park in Toccoa, GA.
- The SungEel plant is projected to generate 104 jobs.
- The facility is certified under the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) program, indicating it has been vetted and approved for fast-track development.
- SungEel's operation has a slated launch date of 2024.
- Suk Jae Yim, representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, described SungEel's entry as “The last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.”
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the proximity of SungEel's new plant to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will be key to further success.
- School bus manufacturer Blue Bird rolled out an all-electric bus in 2018.
- EV parts manufacturer Solvay produces thermoplastic composites in the region.
- YKKUSA manufactures fasteners for EV interiors in the region.
- Hyundai Motor Group reportedly plans to break ground on an EV and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia in January 2023.
- The Hyundai facility is expected to operate at full capacity of about 300,000 units no later than mid-2025.
- EV manufacturer Rivian has sited a plant outside of Atlanta.
- The Rivian plant is reported to begin production in 2024.
- The Rivian plant will have an annual capacity of 400,000 vehicles.
- Incentives and policies are driving much of the activity, including an electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE) tax credit for businesses to buy and install public infrastructure.
- Georgia offers rebates for installing Level 2 chargers.
- A “plug-in electric vehicle charging” rate incentive is available for residents to encourage nighttime and very early-morning EV charging.
- Much of the growth is attributed to Korean companies setting up operations in the region, including Enchem, Duckyang, and EcoPro BM.
- Enchem, Duckyang, and EcoPro BM either manufacture technology around batteries and energy storage systems or supply materials like nickel cathode.
- Much of the activity is spurred by SK Battery’s expansion into Georgia.
- SK Battery America's $2.6 billion facility is now in operation while construction continues.
- The SK Battery America facility will eventually produce enough gigawatt hours annually to power 430,000 vehicles.
- Roger Lin, Ascend's vice president of Marketing and Government Relations, stated that Georgia was selected primarily because of its growing EV and battery ecosystem and the essential proximity to partners like SK Battery America.
- Ascend Elements' new Georgia plant is the largest of its kind in North America.
- The Ascend Elements plant can recycle 30,000 tons of lithium-ion battery materials and scrap per year.
- State officials anticipate an ongoing accelerated growth pattern for the state's EV supply chain.
- Governor Brian Kemp launched the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA) in 2021.
- EMIA is a statewide initiative between the public and private sectors and nonprofits to further scale electrification in Georgia.
