# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-21

**Source:** [Sustainable Battery Materials Provider Ascend Elements Raises $162 Million to Build new US Plant - ESG Today](https://www.esgtoday.com/sustainable-battery-materials-provider-ascend-elements-raises-162-million-for-new-us-plant/)  ·  `page_052553`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a closed-loop battery materials solutions provider founded in 2015 and headquartered in Westborough, Massachusetts.
- The company provides EV battery recycling and commercial-scale production of lithium-ion battery precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- Ascend Elements produces new cathode materials from spent lithium-ion cells using a proprietary technology that enables lower costs, improved performance, and reduced emissions.
- Ascend Elements announced it has raised $162 million in new equity investments to support building a new plant.
- The proceeds will fund a 1-million square foot sustainable lithium-ion plant in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The new Hopkinsville project is named Apex 1 and is anticipated to open in early 2025.
- Apex 1 will be North America’s first sustainable pCAM manufacturing facility.
- The facility will produce sustainable pCAM and CAM for up to 750,000 electric vehicles per year.
- The project is supported in part by the U.S. Department of Energy and the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O’Kronley thanked new and existing partners for investing in North America’s critical EV battery infrastructure and stated that recycling lithium-ion batteries and making new engineered battery materials with lower carbon emissions accelerates the global transition to zero carbon emissions.
- Major investors in the new $162 million funding round include Just Climate, Clearvision Ventures, and IRONGREY.
- The new equity financing brings the company’s 12-month funding total to $704 million.
- This follows a $542 million funding round led by Decarbonization Partners, Temasek, and Qatar Investment Authority in September 2023.
- Just Climate Managing Director Aruna Ramsamy stated that Ascend Elements' Hydro-to-Cathode technology transforms spent lithium-ion batteries into critical materials for new batteries and champions circularity in an industry poised to scale significantly.
- The company's first pCAM facility is currently under construction in the United States.
