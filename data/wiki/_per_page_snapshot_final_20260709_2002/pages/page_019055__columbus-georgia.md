# Columbus, Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-31

**Source:** [Prospects for Columbus - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2026/03/31/prospects-for-columbus/)  ·  `page_019055`

## Facts

- JS Link is building a magnet manufacturing facility that will employ 520 when it opens in 2027.
- Micromize has established its first semiconductor manufacturing facility in Columbus, creating around 20 jobs.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia – a key supplier to Kia, Hyundai and General Motors – opened a manufacturing facility in 2024 in neighboring Harris County, creating 140 jobs.
- Pratt & Whitney is investing $206 million to expand operations, which will create about 400 jobs by the end of 2028, including for engineers, mechanics and operators.
- BioTouch is expanding two existing facilities, creating 480 jobs over the next four years.
- Amazon opened a delivery station in 2025 that supports 200 jobs.
- Sierra Pacific Industries opened a manufacturing facility in nearby Phenix City in 2024 that will employ 300 new crew members this year, with aspirations of eventually reaching 500.
- Fort Benning is the area’s largest employer, with about 45,000 military personnel, contractors and civilians working there and an annual economic impact of $4.75 billion.
- Defense-friendly politics favor Fort Benning, which will continue to be an important driver of Columbus’ economy and an exceptional location for defense contractors, which should increase the high-skill jobs available.
- Veterans account for 12.7% of the region’s population compared to 6.1% of the U.S. population and add to the workforce.
- Labor force availability is a factor when selecting sites for economic development projects in the area.
- Columbus State University employs nearly 2,700 people and is planning a center for advanced technologies that will focus on robotics, engineering and space science to help attract high-tech businesses and spur entrepreneurial activity.
- The region's economy derives only 4.5% of GDP from exports, making it not especially vulnerable to sudden shifts in U.S. trade policies like high tariffs.
- Columbus offers a low cost of living, affordable homes, abundant natural amenities and an improving quality of workforce that supports economic development site selection.
