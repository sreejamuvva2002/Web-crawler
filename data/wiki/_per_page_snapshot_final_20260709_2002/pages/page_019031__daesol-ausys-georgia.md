# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-30

**Source:** [Grow in Harris County, Georgia](https://www.growinharris.com/)  ·  `page_019031`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The facility will create more than 140 new jobs.
- The facility represents $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp said e-mobility suppliers will continue to choose all corners of the state for future jobs.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017.
- The company specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon serves as the CEO of Daesol Ausys.
- Min Ho Kwon stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- The new facility will be located in the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- The new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Commission.
- Robert Grant stated Daesol Ausys’ decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- West Point and Harris County have a working relationship with Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials.
- Craig Greenhaw serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority.
- Craig Greenhaw extended a welcome to Daesol Ausys and expressed anticipation for a mutually beneficial partnership.
- Alyce Thornhill holds the title of Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson serves as the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Pat Wilson stated economic development is a team sport driven by the local level.
- Pat Wilson noted the automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain.
- Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a leading manufacturer of automotive components and a key supplier of luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The company has a long history as one of the top suppliers to Kia and Hyundai worldwide.
