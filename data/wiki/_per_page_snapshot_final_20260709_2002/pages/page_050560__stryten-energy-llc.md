## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a leading U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider.
- The company is based in Alpharetta, Ga.
- Stryten Energy will showcase its advanced battery energy storage solutions (BESS) at CES 2026 in Las Vegas, taking place January 6-9.
- Visitors to the Stryten Energy booth (6929) will learn how the company’s battery-first, technology-agnostic approach is driving energy resilience and independence for critical sectors.
- Mike Judd serves as the CEO of Stryten Energy.
- Mike Judd stated that at CES, Stryten is showing how it is driving transformation with advanced battery solutions built in the U.S., turning energy storage into a strategic advantage to strengthen communities, secure supply chains, and power America’s energy future.
- According to Battery Council International (BCI), U.S. electricity demand is projected to quadruple by 2030, primarily driven by AI adoption and the electrification of business sectors.
- Batteries support surging energy demand by storing excess energy, balancing supply and demand, and providing reliable backup power.
- Domestic battery production strengthens energy security by reducing reliance on foreign supply chains.
- Stryten Energy’s portfolio includes advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium technologies.
- The company uses a battery-first, technology-agnostic model to ensure customers have the right chemistry for the right application.
- Lead BESS delivers grid resilience and reliable backup power through rapid response, voltage stabilization, and a modular design that scales from microgrids to large industrial sites.
- Lead BESS integrates with renewable and hydrocarbon energy sources for peak shaving and safeguarding against power outages.
- The domestic circular economy of lead batteries makes them one of the most sustainable energy storage options available.
- Stryten’s Lithium Mobile Microgrid utilizes a modified Jeep to bring power to remote areas and provide disaster relief with emergency power resources.
- The hybrid diesel-electric vehicle uses proprietary software to efficiently manage carbon-based and renewable energy sources, storing power in lithium and/or lead batteries for use during prolonged power outages.
- Vanadium Redox Flow Battery Technology offers safe, scalable, long-duration energy storage for data centers and microgrids demanding 5-24+ hour resilience.
- Vanadium redox flow battery components are available through Stryten Energy’s joint venture, Storion Energy.
- Storion Energy offers domestically sourced, non-flammable vanadium electrolyte and high-performance stack power assemblies.
- Stryten Energy will host two in-booth panel discussions at CES 2026 addressing AI demand, microgrids, and distributed energy.
- The first panel, "Powering the AI Era: The Role of Batteries in Data Center Infrastructure," is scheduled for Tuesday, January 6, at 3:00 p.m.
- Panelists for the first session include John A. Howes (Principal, Redland Energy Group), Frank Sharp (Technical Director, EPRI), Erik D. Spoerke, Ph.D. (Senior Analytics Advisor, Energy Storage Division, DOE), Travis Torrey (Chief Technology Officer, Storion Energy), and moderator Scott Childers (Vice President, Essential Power, Stryten Energy).
- The second panel, "Local Power, National Impact, the Blueprint for Building Resilience Behind the Meter," is scheduled for Wednesday, January 7, at 11:00 a.m.
- Panelists for the second session include John A. Howes (Principal, Redland Energy Group), Dr. Vick Singh (COO, Dragonfly Energy), Erik D. Spoerke, Ph.D. (Senior Analytics Advisor, Energy Storage Division, DOE), and moderator Scott Childers (Vice President, Essential Power, Stryten Energy).
- Attendees will learn why distributed energy solutions are key to building resilient communities and transforming energy management amid growing grid challenges.
- The company's booth is located at #6929 in the West Hall of the Las Vegas Convention Center.
