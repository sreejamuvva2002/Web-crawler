# Valeo

## Facts

- Valeo’s EV Tech Days took place from 23rd to the 27th September, 2024, in Hungary.
- The event aimed to inform key customers and journalists from across Europe about the aftermarket division's role in electrification.
- The program was made up of a plenary conference in Budapest, onsite workshops, and a visit to Valeo’s Veszprém Factory.
- Valeo’s Veszprém Factory is a multi-products site that specializes in the production of high-voltage technologies and systems for well-known automotive car makers.
- The EV Tech Days showcased Valeo’s approach to High Voltage Powertrain Electrification, Thermal Management, 48V Hybrid Technology, and EV Training with Valeo's Tech Academy.
- By 2030, 93% of all new cars will include a form of electrification.
- One-third of the European car parc will consist of electrified vehicles (either fully electric or hybrid) by 2030.
- Electrification is emerging as the most viable, affordable, and energy-efficient solution to reducing the carbon footprint of the automotive industry, which currently stands at about 18% of global CO2 emissions.
- Current and future car parcs include multiple levels of electrification: low-voltage systems like 48V mild hybrids (combining small electric motors with internal combustion engines for improved fuel efficiency), high-voltage systems like 400V and 800V for both full and plug-in hybrids, and fully electric vehicles.
- Valeo’s strength lies in its ability to support all of these levels of electrification.
- As a pioneer in electrification for over 20 years, Valeo has continually introduced innovative solutions for electrified vehicles.
- In 2004, Valeo created the iStARS system, a belt starter/alternator that optimizes Stop-Start functionality.
- In 2012, Valeo launched its 48V Belt Starter Generator (iBSG), providing mild-hybrid solutions.
- In 2016, Valeo oversaw the creation of the Valeo Siemens eAutomotive JV.
- In 2024, Valeo is set to produce the next generation of “rare-earth-free” electric motors with Renault.
- As of today, one-third of the world’s vehicles are equipped with Valeo electrics, from alternators right through to high-voltage systems.
- To meet the evolving needs of the industry, Valeo launched the Valeo Tech Academy, an innovative training program that offers both digital and in-person learning.
- One in two mechanics is currently unable to maintain or repair electric vehicles.
- Valeo’s goal is to help build the sustainable mobility of tomorrow.
