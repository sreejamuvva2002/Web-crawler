# Rivian Stanton Springs EV Manufacturing Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Stanton Springs, Georgia, Morgan County; Walton County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Rivian's new electric-vehicle manufacturing plant being built by Clayco on a 1,800-acre site in Stanton Springs, Georgia, with an annual capacity of 400,000 vehicles.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Manufacturing of electric vehicles

## Investment

- **$5 billion** (2022) — Construction of EV manufacturing plant
  - Outlined in the Economic Development Agreement inked in 2022 between GDEcD and the Joint Development Authority.

## Jobs

- **7,500 jobs** — Early 2024 (construction start)
  - Average wage of $56,000 per job as specified in the Economic Development Agreement.

## Timeline

- **2022** — Economic Development Agreement inked between GDEcD and Joint Development Authority for Rivian project
- **October 2023** — Earthwork at the site reported to be almost 95% complete
- **Early 2024** — Plant construction expected to commence

## Related organizations & people

- [[clayco|Clayco]] — Construction contractor selected to build the plant
- [[rivian|Rivian]] — Project owner / EV manufacturer
- Jacobs — Engineer of record for the project
- Skidmore, Owings & Merrill (SOM) — Owner’s design architect
- [[georgia-department-of-economic-development-gdecd|Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD)]] — State agency partner in the Economic Development Agreement
- Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton & Walton Counties — Local development authority partner
- State of Georgia — Government partner supporting the project
- Takeda — Existing resident in the industrial park
- Facebook’s Newton Data Center — Existing resident in the industrial park
- Georgia Bioscience Training Center — Existing resident in the industrial park

## Key facts

- The plant will occupy a 1,800-acre complex dedicated to sustainable transportation.
- Annual production capacity is planned for 400,000 electric vehicles.
- Located about 30 minutes east of metro Atlanta along I-20 in a high-tech and bio-pharma industrial park.
- Site spans Stanton Springs North, approximately 40 minutes from Atlanta across Morgan and Walton Counties.
- Clayco will collaborate with Jacobs (engineer of record) and Skidmore, Owings & Merrill (owner’s design architect).
- The project is expected to significantly advance EV adoption and generate numerous job opportunities.
- Average wage for the projected 7,500 jobs is $56,000.
