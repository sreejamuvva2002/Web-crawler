# Magna International

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-09

**Source:** [Why Magna International Shares Are Falling on Thursday | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2019/05/09/why-magna-international-shares-are-falling-on-thur.aspx)  ·  `page_057469`

## Facts

- Shares of Magna International fell more than 10% on Thursday after the Canadian auto part supplier missed on earnings and lowered its full-year profit forecast.
- The share decline was attributed to high program costs and the impact of a slowdown in China.
- Magna reported first-quarter earnings of $1.63 per share on revenue of $10.59 billion, falling short of analyst expectations for $1.71 per share on sales of $10.7 billion.
- Total sales fell 2% year over year, which Magna noted compares well to total global light-vehicle production decreasing 7%.
- Reversing the impact of currency conversions and stripping out revenue from recent divestitures, revenue was up 3% from the first quarter of 2018.
- Magna now expects full-year net income to come in between $1.9 billion and $2.1 billion, down from earlier guidance for $2.1 billion to $2.3 billion.
- The company expects total 2019 light-vehicle production of 16.7 million units in North America, down from its earlier forecast for 17 million units.
- CEO Don Walker cited lower overall vehicle production, margin pressure in the power & vision segment related to higher program engineering and other costs, and a Getrag joint venture in China due to lower than previously anticipated sales as factors weighing on full-year results.
- Magna has long been seen as a best-of-breed auto supplier, with shares more than tripling in value over the past 10 years.
- The company has a broad portfolio of business, a wide range of customers, and has been aggressively moving into new areas including autonomous driving.
- CEO Don Walker stated that despite current issues, the company remains confident in its ability to outpace production in its markets, generate strong free cash flow, and create value for shareholders.
- Magna offers a 2.83% dividend yield.
