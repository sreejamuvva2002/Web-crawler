# Amazon Web Services, Inc.

## Facts

- Amazon Web Services (AWS) announced plans to invest an estimated $11 billion to expand its infrastructure in Georgia to support cloud computing and AI technologies.
- The investment is expected to create at least 550 new high-skilled jobs.
- AWS data centers will be flexible enough to efficiently run GPUs for traditional workloads or AI and machine learning models.
- Amazon has invested $18.5 billion in Georgia since 2010.
- Amazon has contributed $20.1 billion to Georgia's gross domestic product since 2010.
- Amazon currently supports 34,000 full- and part-time jobs in Georgia.
- Amazon currently supports 44,700 indirect jobs in Georgia.
- The planned investment is expected to create hundreds of new, direct AWS jobs in technical roles including data center engineers, network specialists, engineering operations managers, and security specialists.
- The investment is expected to support thousands of local construction jobs and positions in the data center supply chain.
- Amazon operates a Disaster Relief Hub in Atlanta.
- Amazon delivered more than 40 truckloads of over 2.3 million essential items supporting 32 relief organizations following hurricanes Helene and Milton.
- The majority of disaster relief items were delivered within 72 hours from the request.
- Amazon’s on-the-ground Response team used AWS technology to set up energy systems, establish connectivity, and conduct drone mapping of more than 3,000 acres in remote areas for search-and-rescue missions.
- Amazon has made significant investments in renewable energy, including eight solar farms.
- The Bulldog Solar farm was built in part by local solar technicians.
- Once fully operational, the solar projects will generate enough carbon-free energy to power an estimated 123,000 U.S. homes each year.
- Amazon MGM Studios regularly films series in Georgia, including The Bondsman, The Pickup, and I Want You Back.
- Roger Wehner serves as the vice president of economic development at AWS.
- Dr. Romona Jackson Jones serves as the Douglas County Commission Chairwoman.
- Russ Crumbley serves as the chairman of the Butts County Board of Commissioners.
- AWS plans to locate new data centers in Douglas County and Butts County, Georgia.
- AWS offers a Fiber Optic Fusion Splicing Workshop that trains individuals in new fusion splicing techniques and equipment.
- In the fall of 2022, dozens of participants completed the Fiber Optic Fusion Splicing Workshop at Augusta Technical College.
- Participants in the fiber optic workshop earn a fusion splicing certificate and are immediately connected to employers seeking talent in fiber broadband.
- AWS will bring signature science, technology, engineering, arts, and math (STEAM) education programs to Georgia.
- The STEAM programs include Girls’ Tech Day to inspire girls to pursue technology careers and Think Big Spaces for hands-on STEAM education.
- AWS chooses locations based on robust utility infrastructure, a skilled workforce, and opportunities to support public services through increased tax revenue.
- AWS is committed to making a positive impact in Georgia through job creation, educational partnerships, sustainability initiatives, and community reinvestment.
