# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia Job Creation & Momentum Remains Strong - Tifton CEO](https://tiftonceo.com/features/2024/08/georgia-job-creation-momentum-remains-strong/)  ·  `page_009253`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced sustained momentum for business recruitment and expansions in Fiscal Year 2024 (July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2024).
- GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations resulting in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new private sector jobs statewide.
- 83% of expansions and new locations are on their way to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp emphasized prioritizing growth in rural Georgia and investing in key areas like education, workforce development, and infrastructure despite national economic headwinds.
- Metro Atlanta attracted nearly $7.8 billion in investments as a logistics and business hub.
- Statewide, new locations account for $12.8 billion in investments and expansions are responsible for 9,800 new jobs.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted the need to ensure businesses have the necessary workforce, crediting the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships for building the workforce pipeline through training programs.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns noted historic investments in education, workforce development, and infrastructure alongside tax cuts and regulatory relief.
- Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland during FY2024, focusing on strengthening relationships with Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- In the prior year, Governor and First Lady Kemp led similar missions to Israel, France, and Switzerland.
- Georgia maintained its position as a global gateway for business, landing more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market during the past fiscal year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted the state's strong partnerships, diverse industry landscape, and range of locations as major advantages for businesses.
- The State of Georgia celebrated more than 50 years of continuous representation in Europe and Japan, which remain among the top sources of foreign direct investment.
- The coming fiscal year will mark 40 years of continuous representation in Korea, a partnership that earned GDEcD the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- All markets where Georgia maintains representation accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Kristi Brigman highlighted the state's transition from agribusiness and manufacturing to mobility and technology, noting a decade of excellence and project milestones across the state.
- Manufacturing, including aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- StandardAero operates in Richmond County.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer announced a recent expansion in Fayette County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens County.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support solar panel manufacturer Qcells’ operations.
- Investments in food processing and agribusiness totaled more than $1.3 billion in FY2024.
- Japanese probiotic beverage company Yakult located in Bartow County.
- Korean bakery CJ Foodville located in Hall County.
- Walmart will source milk from local dairy farmers at a future dairy processing facility in Lowndes County.
- Technology and corporate hubs are creating at least 4,400 new jobs as a result of wins in FY2024.
- Cargill Corporation is establishing a new office hub in the City of Atlanta.
- U.S. Soccer’s future headquarters and national training center will be located in Fayette County.
- Georgia earned the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for achieving significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
