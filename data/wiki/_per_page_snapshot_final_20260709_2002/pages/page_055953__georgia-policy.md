# Georgia Policy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-27

**Source:** [Friday Facts: September 27, 2024](https://www.georgiapolicy.org/news/friday-facts-september-27-2024/)  ·  `page_055953`

## Facts

- Georgia Ports Authority reported its eighth consecutive month of growth at the Garden City Terminal.
- A potential work stoppage is scheduled for October 1 due to stalled negotiations between the International Longshoremen’s Association and the U.S. Maritime Alliance.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced that automotive supplier Shinsung Petrochemical will invest $11.2 million in a new manufacturing facility in Toombs County.
- The new Shinsung Petrochemical facility is expected to create more than 30 new jobs.
- Established in 1974, Shinsung Petrochemical specializes in products like automotive sealant.
- Shinsung Petrochemical will serve as a key supplier for Hyundai Motor Group and the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Georgia Labor Commissioner Bruce Thompson called for bold action after the state’s unemployment rate rose to 3.6% in August, up from 3.4% in July.
- The number of unemployed Georgians increased by 6,933 to 192,612, marking the highest total since July 2021.
- Valdosta State University partnered with Hwashin Georgia to promote entrepreneurship and small business development by supporting ventures conceived, managed, and owned by students.
- Hwashin Georgia donated $10,000 to support the J. Donald Lee Center for Entrepreneurship’s annual Business Plan Competition at Valdosta State University.
- Brunswick and Chatham County will receive more than $4.8 million in state grants for infrastructure supporting 129 housing units through Governor Brian Kemp’s Rural Workforce Housing Initiative.
- The Rural Workforce Housing Initiative was launched by Governor Brian Kemp early last year during his annual State of the State address to the General Assembly.
- The grants for the Rural Workforce Housing Initiative are overseen by the OneGeorgia Authority.
- Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO Griff Lynch stated that the port is positioned as number one or two for the year, having caught up to traditionally number one Baltimore.
- Governor Brian Kemp directed the Georgia Emergency Management and Homeland Security Agency to activate the State Operations Center in preparation for Hurricane Helene.
- Governor Brian Kemp declared a State of Emergency for all 159 counties to enable emergency management teams to position resources ahead of the storm’s impact.
- Governor Brian Kemp asked FEMA to grant Georgia an emergency declaration to marshal additional resources before, during, and after the major weather event.
