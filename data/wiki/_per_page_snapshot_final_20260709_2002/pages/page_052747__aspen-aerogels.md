# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-14

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels ends construction of major facility in Bulloch County](https://www.yahoo.com/news/aspen-aerogels-ends-construction-major-212152867.html)  ·  `page_052747`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels' proposed electric vehicle parts plant in Bulloch County will no longer come to fruition.
- The plant was originally announced in 2022.
- The project represented the single largest investment opportunity in the history of Bulloch County, with $352 million going towards it.
- The proposed facility would have been 500,000 square feet.
- The facility would have focused on producing aerogel thermal insulating material used to assemble batteries for electric vehicles.
- An estimated 150 jobs would have been created from the project.
- Aspen announced the decision to end construction on Friday, Feb. 14, 2025.
- To meet long-term aerogel demand, Aspen will invest to maximize capacity at its East Providence manufacturing facility and utilize a flexible supply strategy.
- Aspen has stopped construction of its planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- The company will assess options to derive value from the Statesboro assets, including relocating some equipment to upgrade and expand its existing plant.
- This new path requires minimal capital.
- Ricardo C. Rodriguez serves as Aspen Aerogels' Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer.
- In early 2023, Aspen decided to right-time the construction of the Statesboro facility, pre-empting a reset in EV demand expectations.
- Aspen subsequently ramped up its external manufacturing capacity for its Energy Industrial business.
- The external manufacturing model has fully demonstrated its ability to efficiently increase aerogel supply.
- Aspen is confident that a capital-light and modular capacity plan provides the most efficient path to creating value.
- Aspen believes it is fully funded by a strong balance sheet, enabling it to evaluate opportunities for organic and inorganic growth, optimize its capital structure, and return capital to shareholders.
