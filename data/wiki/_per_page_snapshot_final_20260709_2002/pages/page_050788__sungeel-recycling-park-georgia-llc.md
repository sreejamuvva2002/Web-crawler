# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-15

**Source:** [Q&A with SungEel representatives, state and local officials - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/qa-with-sungeel-representatives-state-and-local-officials/)  ·  `page_050788`

## Facts

- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC is a subsidiary of Korea-based SunEel HiTech Co., LLC.
- The company plans to invest more than $37 Million in a new battery recycling facility at Hayestone Brady Industrial Park in Toccoa-Stephens County, Georgia.
- The project will bring 104 new jobs to Stephens County, including positions for technicians, operators, administrative and sales staff, with no management positions mentioned.
- Average pay for the new roles is above the county’s current average wage of $22.85 per hour.
- Groundbreaking was originally scheduled for March 2023 but has been delayed while the company upgrades equipment and selects a general contractor.
- A new groundbreaking date will be announced once the general contractor is selected.
- The company expects to start production in December 2023.
- SungEel plans to utilize North Georgia Technical College’s services for employee training and hiring.
- The company fully expects to hire locally qualified individuals to fill the new positions.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the project in August 2022.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development joined Governor Kemp in welcoming the facility.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that SungEel is at the cutting edge of sustainable technology and its proximity to Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to success.
- Stephens County Development Authority (SCDA) Chairman Brian Akin noted the SCDA had been working with SungEel for eight months prior to the announcement.
- SCDA President and CEO Brittany Ivey updated the timeline in September 2023 and discussed ongoing negotiations for a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU).
- The MOU includes incentives, job creation commitments, investment timelines, and land purchase terms.
- Unlike previous deals where land was given away, the SCDA is asking full price for the land in Hayestone Brady Industrial Park.
- If the MOU remains unchanged, the facility will be placed on the tax digest within the first two years of operation.
- The recycling process dismantles battery modules, heats cells to dry them, and shreds the material into battery powder that ships to Korea.
- The facility will use an electric-powered dryer instead of natural gas combustion or positive pressure type vessels.
- Equipment is being upgraded to vacuum-type vessels for US facilities to ensure safety.
- SungEel confirmed it has used this recycling process since 2008, with recent equipment upgrades.
- No incinerator will be used at the facility, and the process operates without combustion.
- All materials will be recycled for beneficial reuse, with nothing going to a landfill or city wastewater system.
- The Georgia facility will recover more electrolyte material compared to other proposals.
- An explosion in July 2022 at SungEel HiTech’s Hungary plant was caused by faulty equipment installation by an outside vendor during test operations, not facility operation.
- The process is fully compliant with Federal and State permitting regulations.
- An air permit will be obtained prior to starting operation, and the application will be available to the public upon submission.
- SungEel is actively working with Georgia’s Environmental Protection Division (EPD) and Department of Natural Resources (DNR) throughout the permitting process.
- No PFAs will be released, and emissions will not exceed state or federal regulations.
- The facility will install state-of-the-art fire suppressant systems that satisfy all building codes and insurance requirements.
- Local fire personnel will conduct employee fire precaution training every six months, as required at all other SungEel facilities.
- The site is not a storage facility but will have separate areas for raw materials and short-term finished goods storage.
