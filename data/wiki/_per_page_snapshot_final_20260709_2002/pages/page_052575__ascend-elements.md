# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-08

**Source:** [Poland Grants $320 Million to Support Ascend Elements’ Battery Plant - Battery-Tech Network](https://battery-tech.net/battery-markets-news/poland-grants-320-million-to-support-ascend-elements-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_052575`

## Facts

- The Ministry of Economic Development and Technology in Poland has extended a grant of up to USD $320 million (1.22 billion PLN) to Ascend Elements.
- The grant is intended to facilitate the establishment of a sustainable precursor cathode active material (pCAM) manufacturing facility in Poland.
- This subsidy represents one of the largest grants ever provided by the Republic of Poland.
- The grant aligns with the European Union’s Temporary Crisis and Transition Framework (TCTF), which aims to support Europe’s shift towards a net-zero economy.
- pCAM is a crucial material in the production of lithium-ion batteries used in electric vehicles and other advanced industrial applications.
- Ascend Elements has pinpointed a location in Poland for the construction of its first advanced pCAM manufacturing plant in Europe.
- CEO Linh Austin met with Poland’s Secretary of State Michał Jaros to formalize the grant offer.
- The meeting marks a significant advancement in Ascend Elements’ European expansion plans.
- Pawel Pudlowski, Ph.D., Vice President of the Management Board at the Polish Investment and Trade Agency (PAIH), stated that the project is among the largest U.S. investments in recent years.
- The Polish facility is expected to enhance Poland’s position within the global battery supply chain and promote regional development.
- The project will include advancements in technology and collaborations with local educational institutions.
- Ascend Elements intends to commercialize its Hydro-to-Cathode® technology at the new Polish facility.
- The technology facilitates the production of sustainable nickel, manganese, and cobalt (NMC) pCAM from recycled lithium-ion battery materials.
- The process is more efficient and environmentally friendly compared to traditional methods, resulting in reduced carbon emissions and particulate matter.
- A recent life cycle assessment highlighted that the Hydro-to-Cathode® process generates 49% lower carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e) emissions and 26% lower particulate matter (PM 2.5) emissions.
- The company aims to achieve further emission reductions by 2030 through additional decarbonization efforts.
- Ascend Elements is expanding its presence in the United States alongside its European operations.
- The company operates a major battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia.
- A new lithium recovery line at the Covington facility is expected to produce up to 3,000 metric tons of Lithium Carbonate annually.
- Ascend Elements is developing an advanced Hydro-to-Cathode® pCAM manufacturing facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Hopkinsville facility is projected to become North America’s largest NMC pCAM producer by late 2026.
- The Polish grant underscores Ascend Elements’ commitment to building a sustainable and circular battery materials supply chain.
- The investment supports both European and global efforts to meet strategic net-zero targets.
