# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- The electric vehicle market is projected to grow by more than 40 percent in the next few years.
- Korea-based energy company SK Innovation needed to build a plant in North America to serve its growing list of customers like Ford and Volkswagen.
- SK Battery America (SKBA) was formed to pursue the project.
- Georgia was selected as the site because it is the No. 1 state in the U.S. for doing business.
- Georgia’s infrastructure and supportive local and state governments were essential to the site selection process.
- Workforce training partner Georgia Quick Start was key to sealing the deal.
- SKBA spent $2.6 billion to build two facilities.
- The two facilities will manufacture lithium-ion batteries.
- The project will create nearly 2,600 direct new jobs.
- SKBA’s President Hwang Jun-ho stated that the Quick Start training was really crucial.
- This facility is SKBA’s first plant in the United States.
- With full support from Quick Start, SKBA can meet production timelines without any obstacles.
- SK Battery America is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- SK Battery America is a global leading manufacturer for electric vehicles.
- SK Battery America aims to produce the safest, fastest-charging, and longest-lasting batteries to create a cleaner and more sustainable future across the United States.
- Lanier Technical College is listed as a partner on the page.
- Timothy (Jun Yong) Jeong serves as the CEO of SK Battery America.
