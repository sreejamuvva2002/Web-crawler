# Hyundai Motor Company / Kia Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-03

**Source:** [Hyundai and Kia top US sales records in 2024 as new EVs arrive](https://electrek.co/2025/01/03/hyundai-kia-top-us-sales-records-2024-new-evs-arrive/)  ·  `page_021366`

## Facts

- Kia achieved its seventh consecutive year of higher consumer sales in the US in 2024.
- Kia broke its annual US sales record for the second straight year in 2024.
- Kia’s total US vehicle volume reached nearly 797,000 units in 2024.
- Sean Yoon, CEO of Kia North America, stated that the sales accomplishment validates “that our strategy is working to further elevate the Kia brand.”
- Kia’s growing EV lineup is cited as a major reason behind its US sales growth.
- Kia solidified its leadership position within the US EV market, largely thanks to the successful launch of its flagship EV9.
- The EV9 is Kia’s first three-row electric SUV, with deliveries beginning in late 2023.
- Kia sold over 22,000 EV9 models in the US in 2024.
- The EV9's 2024 US sales volume exceeded that of Kia’s first dedicated EV, the EV6, which sold 21,715 units in 2024.
- Hyundai set a new US sales record with over 836,800 vehicles sold in 2024, representing a 4% year-over-year increase.
- Hyundai credited growing demand for its EVs as a major growth driver for its 2024 US sales.
- The IONIQ 5 remained one of the best-selling EVs in the US, with 44,400 models sold in 2024, marking a 31% increase from 2023.
- Randy Parker, CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, expressed confidence that sales momentum will continue due to exciting new models and increased US production ramping up at its new EV plant in Georgia.
- Hyundai began building EVs at its new Georgia facility in October.
- The Georgia plant started production with the upgraded 2025 IONIQ 5.
- The upgraded 2025 IONIQ 5 features more range, better style, and a NACS port compatible with Tesla Superchargers.
- Hyundai is building the IONIQ 9 alongside the new IONIQ 5 at its Georgia (GA) facility.
- With US production ramping up at the Georgia plant, five Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EVs now qualify for the $7,500 federal tax credit for the first time.
- The 2025 Hyundai IONIQ 5 starts at $42,500 with up to 245 miles of driving range.
- The long-range model of the 2025 IONIQ 5 starts at $46,550 and offers up to 318 miles of range, an increase from 303 miles in the outgoing model.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 SE RWD Standard Range trim features a 168-horsepower rear motor, 245-mile range, and starts at $42,500.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 SE RWD trim features a 225-horsepower rear motor, 318-mile range, and starts at $46,550.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 SEL RWD trim features a 225-horsepower rear motor, 318-mile range, and starts at $49,500.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 Limited RWD trim features a 225-horsepower rear motor, 318-mile range, and starts at $54,200.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 SE Dual Motor AWD trim features a 320-horsepower dual motor, 290-mile range, and starts at $50,050.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 SEL Dual Motor AWD trim features a 320-horsepower dual motor, 290-mile range, and starts at $53,000.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 XRT Dual Motor AWD trim features a 320-horsepower dual motor, 259-mile range, and starts at $55,400.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 Limited Dual Motor AWD trim features a 320-horsepower dual motor, 269-mile range, and starts at $58,100.
