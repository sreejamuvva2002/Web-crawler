# Bonnell Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-02-28

**Source:** [Bonnell Aluminum | The Construction Source](https://www.theconstructionsource.net/2020/02/28/bonnell-aluminum/)  ·  `page_053046`

## Facts

- Bonnell Aluminum has its headquarters in Newnan, Georgia.
- The company operates state-of-the-art manufacturing facilities in Carthage, Tennessee; Niles, Michigan; and Elkhart, Indiana.
- Bonnell Aluminum employs a team of more than 1,300 people.
- The company specializes in the production of mill finish, anodized, painted, fabricated, and thermal-barrier assemblies of customized aluminum extrusions.
- Product applications include construction, pleasure crafts, heat sinks for LED lighting, photovoltaic panels, automotive engineering, walkways, windows, and shower enclosures.
- The company's work is featured in projects such as the Georgia Aquarium, Luxor’s pyramid-styled hotel & casino in Las Vegas, and the Philadelphia Eagles football stadium.
- William Bonnell acquired his first extrusion press in 1939, making his firm the first in the industry to have its own in-house extrusion capability.
- During World War II, scientists working on the Manhattan Project turned to William Bonnell for help developing a method of extruding radioactive uranium.
- In the early 1950s, William Bonnell served as superintendent and general manager of Trimedge, Inc., which later became known as Trimedge of Georgia, Inc.
- In 1955, he bought out patents and trademarks affiliated with Trimedge to form the William L. Bonnell Company.
- The company continued to thrive as a wholly owned subsidiary of Ethyl Corporation starting in 1965.
- Ethyl Corporation opened the Tennessee facility in 1969 and later acquired an aluminum window fabricator formerly known as Capitol Products.
- By the late 1980s, Ethyl consolidated its aluminum, plastics, and energy holdings into Tredegar Corporation, making Bonnell Aluminum a core business unit of that international, publicly-traded corporation.
- Duncan Crowdis serves as the President of Bonnell Aluminum.
- Imports from China, benefiting from heavy government subsidies, grew to capture as much as 20% of America’s market.
- Bonnell Aluminum joined forces with other dedicated extruders to file petitions with the U.S. Department of Commerce citing unfair trade practices involving China.
- Working with support from the U.S. Trade Commission, the team successfully maneuvered through bureaucratic processes culminating in an ultimate decision by the Department of Commerce to apply duties to extruded product imports from China.
- Following these duties, Chinese imports were reduced to a trickle compared to their volume in 2009.
- To diversify and gain fabrication capabilities, Bonnell strategically acquired Indiana-based extruder AACOA.
- The company sees particular optimism for opportunities in the automotive industry due to a push for vehicle lightweighting.
- Federal guidelines are prompting new fuel efficiency rates to be implemented by 2025, making aluminum vital as it is one-third the weight of steel but can offer appropriate strength for many applications.
- Transportation and future vehicle direction represent a significant growth area for the extrusion industry, including Bonnell Aluminum.
- Bonnell Aluminum works in conjunction with the Aluminum Extruder’s Council, a U.S. industry association, to educate designers on the benefits of using aluminum and aluminum extrusions.
- The company's culture emphasizes high integrity, long-term client relationships, and empowering employees to serve customers without personal agendas.
