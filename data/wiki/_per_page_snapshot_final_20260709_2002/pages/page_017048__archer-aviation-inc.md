# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-03

**Source:** [Archer Aviation unveils more details on its Miami network](https://aerospaceglobalnews.com/news/archer-aviation-miami-evtol-network-plans/)  ·  `page_017048`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation unveiled full details of its proposed Miami metropolitan area air taxi network.
- The plan combines newly-built vertiports and existing landing sites to create a citywide network for eVTOL aircraft.
- Archer's Midnight aircraft is a piloted aircraft designed to carry four passengers and perform high-frequency journeys with minimal charge time between flights.
- The company says the Midnight is ideal for built-up urban communities such as Miami, where traffic congestion can be a significant inhibitor to growth.
- The new network aims to connect major population and business centers of the greater Miami conurbation, including Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Boca Raton, and West Palm Beach.
- Flights between these four centers will be short, 10–20-minute eVTOL flights designed to bypass ground-based traffic congestion using the soon-to-be-certified Midnight aircraft.
- The network will link the four major centers with the area's three primary airports: Miami International Airport (MIA), Fort Lauderdale–Hollywood International Airport (FLL), and Palm Beach International Airport (PBI).
- Other strategically important general aviation airports in the area will also be linked to decrease transfer times while reducing road infrastructure traffic.
- Partnerships with property development, infrastructure, and real estate partners across the region will work on developing existing helipad networks and building newly constructed dedicated vertiports.
- West Palm Beach real estate developer Related Ross plans to partner with Archer to develop a vertiport within its under-construction West Palm Beach downtown development.
- Related Ross aims to create a new premium mobility hub in the region's fastest-growing commercial center.
- Archer intends to collaborate with Dragon Global, a private investment firm based in Miami, Florida, which invests in venture capital and real estate projects.
- Dragon Global's Magic City Innovation District in Miami's Little Haiti area plans to develop a vertiport site for air taxis in one of Miami's largest mixed-use technology and innovation communities.
- Hard Rock Stadium and Apogee Golf Club have said they are ready to adapt existing helipads to handle next-generation eVTOL aircraft such as the Midnight.
- Hard Rock Stadium is a premier sports and entertainment destination home to the Miami Dolphins NFL team, University of Miami Hurricanes, Formula 1 Miami Grand Prix, Miami Open, and other major global events.
- Apogee Golf Club is one of the US's most prestigious golf clubs featuring three 18-hole courses designed by renowned architects.
- Miami Mayor Francis Suarez commented that Miami attracts visionaries, embraces breakthrough technology, and turns bold ideas into real impact, noting his work with Archer to advance an air-taxi network that elevates Miami as a global capital for innovation and mobility.
- Steven Ross, CEO and Chairman of Related Ross, stated the partnership will accelerate South Florida region connectivity and improve access to key locations by integrating Archer's eVTOL across its locations.
- Bob Zangrillo, Founder of Dragon Global and partner in the Magic City Innovation District, said partnering with Archer advances a multimodal, future-ready transportation ecosystem for South Florida.
- Archer is slowly gaining a firm grip on global cities wishing to be at the forefront of eVTOL evolution, with plans for public passenger eVTOL flights in 2026 if all goes according to plan.
- At November's Dubai Airshow, Archer stated Abu Dhabi will become its "second home" in the near future and should see public eVTOL flights in 2026.
- Abu Dhabi will be the first place globally where the Midnight eVTOL passenger aircraft will be built outside of the United States.
- At Dubai Airshow 2025, Archer signed an exclusive tripartite deal with The Helicopter Company and Red Sea Global to facilitate eVTOL operations rollout across Saudi Arabia from 2026.
- The Dubai Airshow deal focuses on development, testing, and potential integration of Archer's eVTOL aircraft into Red Sea Global's operations as one of the first deployments in Saudi Arabia.
- In October, Korean Air signed an exclusive agreement with Archer Aviation to commercialize its Midnight aircraft in South Korea.
- Under the memorandum of understanding, Korean Air plans to purchase up to 100 Midnight aircraft for deployment across government and commercial applications.
- The collaboration supports South Korea's ambition, announced by the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, and Transport in 2022, to make eVTOL operations a national priority.
