# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: Auto parts manufacturer breaks ground on new facility - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/georgia-seoyon-e-hwa-savannah-groundbreaking/43622847)  ·  `page_059822`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA, a global auto parts supplier, plans to invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- On Monday, Seoyon E-HWA broke ground on its new facility in Chatham County.
- The groundbreaking comes less than three months after the company announced plans to hire 740 workers and invest almost $76 million at the new manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will be a supplier to the new Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated in February that he promised it was just the start in bringing even more jobs and opportunity to hardworking Georgians after announcing the largest economic development project in Georgia history last year.
- Seoyon’s facility is located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Established in 1972, Seoyon E-Hwa supplies door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen and other car manufactures.
- The new facility will produce automotive interior parts such as door trims, headlining, and tailgate trims.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Ryu Yang-seok said the establishment of Seoyon E-Hwa Savannah is hoped to provide new jobs in the region and contribute to making Savannah a central city for automobile development and production.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in October 2024.
- About 500 of the announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality and production personnel.
