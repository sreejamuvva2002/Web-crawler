# Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A.

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A. announced its participation in the Drawdown Georgia Business Compact.
- The Drawdown Georgia Business Compact is a statewide initiative facilitated by the Ray C. Anderson Center for Sustainable Business at the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The Compact was launched in October 2021 as a business-focused collaborative initiative focused on galvanizing climate action in Georgia.
- Martin Peters, Director of External Affairs for Yamaha U.S. Marine Business Unit, stated that Yamaha's support underscores its mission to achieve carbon neutrality within its facilities worldwide by 2035.
- This carbon neutrality goal includes Yamaha's facilities located in Newnan, Georgia, specifically Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America.
- Through the initiative, Yamaha will access research and collaborate with conservation-minded Georgia businesses to reduce carbon emissions.
- Drawdown Georgia was launched in 2020 as a research-based initiative born from a multi-university collaboration led by Georgia Tech.
- Research partners for Drawdown Georgia included Emory University, Georgia State University, and The University of Georgia.
- The research team released initial findings in October 2020 identifying 20 high-impact climate solutions to advance drawdown in Georgia.
- Michael Oxman, Managing Director of the Ray C. Anderson Center for Sustainable Business, stated that Drawdown Georgia uses these climate solutions as a roadmap to bring together businesses, universities, NGOs, and policymakers.
- Drawdown Georgia takes inspiration from Project Drawdown, localizing climate solutions that are cost-competitive and market-ready for Georgia.
- The initiative has grown into a movement including many partners and funders working to advance climate solutions in Georgia.
- Yamaha U.S. Marine Business Unit is based in Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The unit markets and sells marine outboard motors ranging from 2.5 to 450 horsepower.
- The unit also markets and sells fiberglass, jet-drive sport boats ranging from 19 to 27 feet, and personal watercraft.
- The unit includes manufacturing divisions of Yamaha Marine Systems Co., Inc., including Kracor of Milwaukee (rotational molding), Bennett Marine of Deerfield Beach, Florida (trim tabs), and Yamaha Marine Precision Propellers of Indianapolis (stainless steel propellers).
- Yamaha Marine Group is a division of Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A., based in Cypress, California.
