# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [HSBMA](https://www.hsbma.com/news/blog-post-title-four-8p4l5)  ·  `page_000635`

## Facts

- Subject to execution of relevant agreements and HMG and SK On's final board decisions, the new facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411.
- The project is aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor stated he is very pleased with the decision of Hyundai Motor Group and SK On and thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property.
- Commissioner Taylor noted that together they pursued an extraordinary economic development project that will have regional benefit for job seekers in the area and establish Bartow in Georgia's EV ecosystem in a significant way.
- Commissioner Taylor highlighted Bartow's strong history and talent base built upon advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation, stating the battery partnership should thrive in this environment.
- Commissioner Taylor expressed looking forward to welcoming HMG and SK in meaningful ways in the community and enjoying a long-term partnership together.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with Georgia EMC, the Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as major drivers for Georgia's automotive renaissance and as the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state, respectively.
- Commissioner Wilson explained that supporting cooperation and partnerships across the growing EV ecosystem is creating a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Commissioner Wilson expressed excitement for the jobs of the future this will create for Bartow County and northwest Georgia and gratitude for the support of community and utility partners.
- Georgia's prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate have made it an attractive location for a diverse array of rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Building on assets that make the automotive industry successful, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in the state since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May its plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Bryan County facility in October with Governor Kemp, other state leaders, and local and federal officials.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise that has created a value chain based on mobility, steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service, with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- SK On is a global leading electric vehicle battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider launched as an independent company in 2021 after SK Innovation split off its battery business.
- SK On aims to become a world leader in the clean energy industry by leveraging its global production base and R&D capabilities, as well as production and quality management know-how.
- SK On is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, and has a worldwide presence with battery plants currently operating or in construction across the United States, Europe, and Asia.
