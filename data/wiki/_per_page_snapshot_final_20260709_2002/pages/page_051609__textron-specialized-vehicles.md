# Textron Specialized Vehicles

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Everything You Need to Know About Texron's August Expansion](https://www.golfcartsforsale.com/blog/textron-specialized-vehicles-announces-expansion-augusta/)  ·  `page_051609`

## Facts

- On February 19, Textron Specialized Vehicles announced a deal to purchase a new manufacturing plant and 235 acres of land from Procter & Gamble Co.
- The purchased facility is located where Mike Padgett Highway meets Marvin Griffin Road in the southern portion of Augusta, Georgia.
- This location is in the same city where Textron Specialized Vehicles is headquartered and EZGO golf buggies are manufactured.
- The new facility comprises manufacturing and administrative space totaling 600,000 square feet.
- The facility will be used to augment current Augusta operations, including the production of EZGO golf buggies.
- New offices and manufacturing processes will be installed in the plant, located approximately one mile from the current headquarters.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles has been headquartered in Augusta since Textron Inc. purchased the original manufacturer of EZGO golf buggies in 1954.
- The company manufactures more than 90 models of golf carts, low-speed vehicles (LSVs), neighborhood electric vehicles (NEVs), and utility task vehicles (UTVs) for consumer, commercial, and industrial uses.
- Other brands under Textron Specialized Vehicles include Cushman, Bad Boy, Douglas, and TUG, most of which are manufactured or assembled in Augusta.
- Kevin Holleran serves as the president of Textron Specialized Vehicles.
- The expansion will enable rapid increases in manufacturing throughput and flexibility to meet growing customer demands from individual consumers to commercial enterprises.
- The total purchase amount for the expansion is more than $40 million.
- The Augusta Economic Development Authority issued $20 million in revenue bonds backed by a payment agreement with the company to fund the purchase and capital improvements.
- Walter Sprouse serves as the executive director of the Augusta Economic Development Authority.
- Walter Sprouse stated that Textron Specialized Vehicles’ decision to expand is proof of Georgia’s great infrastructure for manufacturing and that the authority was excited to assist with creating new jobs in Augusta.
- The State of Georgia provided a $600,000 project-development grant and $1.6 million in job-training services through the Georgia Quick Start program.
- In exchange for state assistance, Textron Specialized Vehicles has five years to create 400 new jobs and complete its $40 million investment.
- The company currently employs approximately 1,000 Georgia residents.
- The company's workforce has steadily grown by 13 percent each year.
- Nathan Deal serves as the Governor of Georgia.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that the expansion reinforces the advantages of Georgia’s business resources for a leading company looking to expand operations, noting their commitment to job creation and economic development is evident in the number of companies who’ve expanded or relocated there.
- Adela Kelley is a senior project manager representing the Global Commerce division at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Region 7.
- Chris Carr is a Commissioner at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Region 7.
- Adela Kelley and Chris Carr are major contributors to the partnership between Georgia and Textron Specialized Vehicles.
- Existing industry accounted for 75 percent of total investment to the state and 63 percent of total jobs created among the 329 projects located in Georgia.
- Chris Carr stated that existing industry accounted for 75 percent of total investment to the state and 63 percent of total jobs created, and expressed a desire to work with local partners in Augusta to continue supporting future growth.
- Kevin Holleran stated that the city of Augusta, surrounding region, and state of Georgia have been valuable partners for Textron Specialized Vehicles since its earliest days.
