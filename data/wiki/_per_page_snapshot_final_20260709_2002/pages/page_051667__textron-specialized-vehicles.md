# Textron Specialized Vehicles

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2018-12-07

**Source:** [UPDATE: Textron Specialized Vehicles planning to layoff 400, close one manufacturing facility](https://www.wrdw.com/content/news/Textron-plans-to-layoff-400-workers-nationwide-502180411.html)  ·  `page_051667`

## Facts

- Textron, the owner of EZ-GO, plans to lay off 400 workers in its Textron Specialized Vehicles division nationwide.
- The layoffs are expected to happen fast, by the end of this month.
- Textron operates two manufacturing plants in Augusta, Georgia.
- In January of 2018, Textron laid off 60 employees locally in Augusta.
- A local representative told News 12 in January 2018 that the company did not anticipate any additional job cuts at that time.
- The company filed a new document with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) stating it was not getting the expected return after acquiring Arctic Cat.
- The SEC filing outlines restructuring changes including cutting 400 jobs, closing several branches, and closing one manufacturing facility.
- This restructuring plan is expected to be mostly complete by the end of 2018.
- Textron previously invested $40 million in Augusta when it purchased an old Proctor and Gamble plant on the corner of Marvin Griffin Road and Mike Padgett Highway in 2016.
- The 2016 Augusta expansion brought 400 jobs to the site.
- The SEC filing states the company expects to pay out up to $85 million in closing costs, including at least $10 million in severance.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles maintains locations all over the world.
- Local representatives stated they cannot comment beyond what is included in the SEC filing.
- The company told News 12: "Unfortunately, I can’t offer any comment beyond what is included in the SEC filing at this time. We will share more info when we can."
- It remains unclear whether any jobs in the Augusta area will be affected or if the Augusta plants themselves are in jeopardy.
