# DENSO Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-25

**Source:** [Auto Supplier DENSO Will Expand Tennessee Presence - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/auto-supplier-denso-will-expand-tennessee-presence/)  ·  `page_053843`

## Facts

- DENSO announced an expansion of its manufacturing operations in Athens, Tennessee.
- The expansion includes 54,560 square feet of new production space and renovations to existing areas.
- DENSO will add 200 jobs at its Athens location.
- The company will invest $100 million through the project.
- This investment cements DENSO's status as the largest employer in McMinn County.
- DENSO first established Tennessee operations in 1988 in Maryville before expanding to Athens in 1997.
- The Athens site began operations in 1997 to produce components for vehicle ignition, exhaust, and gasoline delivery systems.
- The expansion increases the Athens site's total footprint to 879,000 square feet.
- The location currently employs 1,444 employees.
- The expanded facility will enable production of a wider range of DENSO products, starting with heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems.
- DENSO's HVAC products improve passenger comfort and can enhance the efficiency of traditional, hybrid, and electric vehicles.
- Since 2020, the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development (TNECD) has supported over 40 economic development projects in Southeast Tennessee.
- These TNECD-supported projects have resulted in approximately 5,700 job commitments and more than $2.6 billion in capital investments.
- Governor Bill Lee stated that DENSO is one of Tennessee's largest employers and thanked the company for creating opportunity over nearly three decades.
- TNECD Commissioner Stuart C. McWhorter noted that fostering partnerships with international companies like DENSO is critical to recruitment efforts and called the company a cornerstone in McMinn County.
- Marty Deschenes, Leader of DENSO North America South Sub-Region Manufacturing and President of the Athens Facility, stated the expansion opens another chapter for career opportunities and greener mobility across North America.
- John Bradley, TVA senior vice president of Economic Development, congratulated DENSO on its decision to expand operations in Athens.
- McMinn County Mayor John Gentry highlighted that the investment will create hundreds of quality jobs and continue the county's longstanding partnership with DENSO.
- Athens Mayor Steven Sherlin noted the expansion will boost the local economy and strengthen the workforce.
- State Senator Adam Lowe (R-Calhoun) praised DENSO for providing quality jobs to citizens and enabling significant investment in Athens and across the state.
- State Representative Mark Cochran (R-Englewood) stated the expansion will bring hundreds of new high-quality jobs to the region and enhance its reputation as a manufacturing powerhouse.
- DENSO is globally headquartered in Kariya, Japan, and is a $47.2 billion leading mobility supplier.
- The company invests in around 180 facilities worldwide to produce electrification, powertrain, thermal, and mobility electronics products.
- DENSO has 162,000 global employees and spent around 7.7 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development in the fiscal year ending March 31, 2024.
- In North America, DENSO is headquartered in Southfield, Michigan, and employs over 27,000 engineers, researchers, and skilled workers across nearly 50 sites in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- In the United States alone, DENSO employs over 17,500 employees across 14 states and the District of Columbia at 41 sites.
- In the fiscal year ending March 31, 2024, DENSO in North America generated $11.7 billion in consolidated sales.
