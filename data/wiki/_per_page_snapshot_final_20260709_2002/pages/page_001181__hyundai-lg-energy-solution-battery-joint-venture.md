# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG to Put $2B More Into Georgia Battery Joint Venture  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/hyundai-lg-to-put-2b-more-into-georgia-battery-joint-venture/)  ·  `page_001181`

## Facts

- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution are set to invest $2 billion more into their joint venture battery plant near Savannah.
- The additional investment will create 400 jobs above the companies' initial pledge.
- In May, the companies stated the battery cell facility located on the site of the Hyundai Metaplant in Ellabell would require an investment of $4.3 billion.
- State officials clarified that the $4.3 billion figure was not new capital but part of a previously announced $5.5 billion total for the battery facility and adjacent EV factory.
- On Thursday, the companies announced an additional $2 billion investment in the battery facility, bringing the full project total to $7.59 billion.
- With the additional investment, the Metaplant is expected to employ 8,500 people across the battery and EV assembly facilities.
- This employment figure represents an increase from the 8,100 jobs initially promised.
- The incremental investment is located in Bryan County.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Co. and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated the investment reflects a commitment to create a sustainable future powered by American workers.
- It remains unclear how the new capital will be deployed, specifically whether it will accelerate production timelines or expand factory capacity ahead of future growth.
- According to Governor Brian Kemp's office, the factory will initially produce 30 GWh of battery cells annually.
- This annual capacity matches the figure announced when the partnership was publicized earlier in the year.
- The facility's output is initially pegged to power 300,000 EVs.
- This production capacity matches the initial capacity planned for the Hyundai Metaplant.
- The battery cells produced by the 50-50 joint venture will be sent to Hyundai Mobis.
- Hyundai Mobis is located on the 2,900-acre Metaplant site.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble the cells into modules before integration into the EV assembly process.
- The project adds to numerous South Korean investments worth billions of dollars and thousands of jobs in Georgia.
- Affiliated suppliers have announced over $2 billion in investments for nearby factories intended to supply the company's first dedicated U.S. EV plant.
- Hyundai is ramping up electric model production at its existing Alabama factory.
- Kia is ramping up electric model production at its existing Georgia factory.
- Kia is investing $200 million in West Point for EV9 production.
- The EV9 is the first all-electric seven-seater SUV introduced in the U.S.
- Kia's EV9 production is targeted to be completed by the end of 2024.
- Hyundai started manufacturing the electrified Genesis GV70 in Montgomery, Alabama earlier this year.
- The U.S. began subsidizing domestic EV production through purchase incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act passed last August.
- Due to their Korean origin, none of Hyundai Motor Group's EVs currently qualify for the subsidies, except when leased.
- Company leaders and Korean diplomats have visited Georgia over the last year to lobby for qualification.
- Hyundai received a $1.8 billion incentive package for its $5.5 billion plant.
- It is unclear if the additional $2 billion commitment requires a separate incentive package.
