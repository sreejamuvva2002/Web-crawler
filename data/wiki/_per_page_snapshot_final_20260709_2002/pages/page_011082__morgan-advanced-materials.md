# Morgan Advanced Materials

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2016-04-01

**Source:** [Thermal blanket has lithium-ion fires covered - Air Cargo News](https://www.aircargonews.net/thermal-blanket-has-lithium-ion-fires-covered/15539.article)  ·  `page_011082`

## Facts

- Morgan Advanced Materials has launched a thermal bag to counter the fire risk from overheating Lithium-Ion batteries due to thermal runaway.
- The FireMaster battery bags went through an extensive period of testing in conjunction with Germanwings, part of the Lufthansa Group.
- The FireMaster bag measures 500 mm x 500 mm.
- The bag comprises two of Morgan’s specialist thermal materials to create a sealable, water-resistant compartment capable of containing the heat spread in the event of a primary-lithium or lithium-ion battery fire.
- The US Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has recorded an average of six incidents every year for the last 25 years involving batteries carried as baggage or cargo.
- An increasing proportion of these FAA-recorded incidents are caused by lithium batteries.
- Studies have proven that lithium battery fires cannot be adequately suppressed by halon fire extinguishing methods.
- Both primary lithium and lithium-ion batteries are categorised as Class 9 hazardous materials.
- Both the FAA and the International Civil Aviation Organisation (ICAO) have restricted the transportation of lithium-based batteries as cargo.
- The most recent ruling restricting lithium battery transport took effect from April 1, 2016.
- Thermal runaway occurs when a lithium battery overheats past its point of thermal stability (150°C/302°F), resulting in fire, explosion, a pressure wave, or all three.
- Thomas Schwarz is the sales director for Morgan’s thermal ceramics business.
- The transportation of lithium-based batteries as cargo has been regulated by aviation authorities for more than a decade.
- No limitations have been placed on the transportation of these products within the main aircraft cabin.
- Thermal runaway, although a rare occurrence, can have a catastrophic effect on an aircraft in flight.
- Passenger airlines have been proactive in trying to find a solution to this manageable danger.
- The bag benefits from Morgan’s FireMaster Marine Plus technology, which was originally designed for use in the oil & gas sector.
- FireMaster Marine Plus provides thermal protection from uncontrolled fire for sustained periods of time.
