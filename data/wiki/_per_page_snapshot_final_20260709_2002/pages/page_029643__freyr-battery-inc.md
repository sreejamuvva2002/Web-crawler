# FREYR Battery, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-01

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Closes Transformative Acquisition of Trina Solar’s U.S. Manufacturing Assets](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241224327270/en/FREYR-Battery-Closes-Transformative-Acquisition-of-Trina-Solars-U.S.-Manufacturing-Assets)  ·  `page_029643`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery announced the closing of its acquisition of Trina Solar’s U.S. solar manufacturing assets.
- The transaction closed in accordance with a previously communicated timeline.
- FREYR acquired Trina Solar’s 5 GW solar module manufacturing facility in Wilmer, Texas.
- The Wilmer, Texas facility commenced production on November 1, 2024.
- The facility is expected to ramp up to full production by H2 2025.
- 30% of estimated production volumes at the Wilmer facility are backed by firm offtake contracts with U.S. customers.
- The transaction creates a commercial and operating platform to establish a leading integrated U.S.-owned and operated solar and battery storage company with a pathway for value enhancing growth.
- The transaction leverages Trina Solar’s global leadership in the solar and renewable energy industries, established U.S. commercial presence, global supply chains, advantaged technology, and a strong track record of manufacturing and project execution for U.S. customers.
- Total consideration to Trina Solar at closing comprised $100 million of cash, $50 million repayment of an intercompany loan, $150 million loan note, 9.9% of FREYR outstanding common stock, and an $80 million convertible loan note that would convert into an additional 11.5% of FREYR outstanding common stock after certain conditions are satisfied.
- In addition to the consideration, FREYR acquired $235 million in indebtedness in connection with the Wilmer, Texas facility.
- FREYR reiterates initial 2025 EBITDA guidance of $75 - $125 million.
- FREYR expects to exit 2025 at full-year run rate EBITDA of $175 - $225 million.
- Integrated solar module/solar cell production annual run rate EBITDA is expected to be $650 - $700 million.
- Ramp up activities at the Wilmer, TX solar module plant continue as scheduled.
- Line 1 of the Wilmer facility has been completed.
- Commissioning on Line 2 commenced in November 2024.
- FREYR expects the seven-line Wilmer facility to reach full production in H2 2025.
- FREYR intends to submit transaction documentation in Q1 2025 to secure U.S. regulatory consents from relevant organizations, including CFIUS.
- FREYR received $50 million from Encompass Capital Advisors LLC (“Encompass”) in exchange for the issuance of preferred stock in connection with this closing.
- FREYR may receive an additional $50 million from Encompass upon proceeding to start of construction on a solar cell manufacturing facility.
- FREYR is proceeding with its site selection process for a planned U.S. solar cell facility.
- Start of construction for the planned U.S. solar cell facility is anticipated in Q2 2025.
- FREYR is progressing with the implementation of a multi-phase strategic plan to establish a vertically integrated U.S. solar manufacturing footprint.
- Site selection for the planned 5 GW U.S. solar cell manufacturing plant is underway.
- The company is evaluating and pursuing debt and equity solutions to fund construction of the solar cell facility.
- FREYR is still targeting a start of construction in Q2 2025 for the solar cell facility.
- Anticipated first solar cell production at the new facility is H2 2026.
- The creation of a U.S.-owned and operated company providing a turnkey solar technology solution is expected to solve a bottleneck for developers.
- The project is expected to create up to 1,800 direct jobs.
- The project is expected to satisfy local content requirements for U.S. solar projects.
- The project is expected to competitively differentiate FREYR.
- Daniel Barcelo, FREYR’s Chairman of the Board and CEO, stated that the closing marks the start of a new chapter as they execute their strategic plan to build a U.S.-based leader in the solar and storage markets.
- Barcelo highlighted key objectives to create shareholder value and enhance competitive position in 2025, including the planned start of construction of the solar cell manufacturing facility and other emerging project development opportunities for FREYR and Trina to mutually pursue.
- Total consideration to Trina Solar consists of $100 million cash, $50 million intercompany loan repayment, a $150 million loan note, 9.9% of FREYR outstanding common stock, and an $80 million convertible loan note converting to an additional 11.5% of FREYR outstanding common stock after certain conditions are satisfied.
- FREYR secured a $100 million commitment for the issuance of preferred stock to certain funds/accounts managed by Encompass.
- $50 million in preferred stock has been issued to Encompass-managed funds/accounts in connection with this closing.
- $14.8 million was allocated for a private placement of 7.0% of FREYR outstanding common stock to Ms. Chunyan Wu, a co-founder and significant shareholder of Trina Solar, subject to certain conditions.
- The transaction funds will be used for general operational and working capital purposes.
- Santander served as financial advisor to the transaction.
- Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom (UK) LLP served as legal advisor to the transaction.
- Arnold & Porter, Ernst & Young, Clean Energy Associates, and Rystad Energy served as advisors to FREYR in support of the transaction.
- Dorsey & Whitney LLP served as U.S. legal advisor to the transaction.
- CICC served as financial advisor to Trina Solar.
- Deloitte served as tax advisor to Trina Solar.
- FREYR is a clean energy solutions provider building an integrated U.S. supply chain for solar and batteries.
- In November 2024, FREYR announced the transformative transaction positioning it as one of the leading solar manufacturing companies in the U.S. with a complementary solar and battery storage strategy.
- FREYR is based in the U.S. with plans to expand its operations in America.
