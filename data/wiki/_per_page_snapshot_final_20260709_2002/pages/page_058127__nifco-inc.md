# Nifco Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-03-31

**Source:** [Nifco Inc. Business Report FY ended Mar. 2016 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_476_hl2015)  ·  `page_058127`

## Facts

- Overall sales for FY ended Mar. 31, 2016 were 265,683 million JPY, representing a 17.9% year-over-year increase.
- Operating income for FY ended Mar. 31, 2016 was 27,574 million JPY, up 31.5% from the previous fiscal year.
- Ordinary income for FY ended Mar. 31, 2016 was 26,374 million JPY, a 27.9% increase year-over-year.
- Profit for the year attributable to owners of the parent reached 17,742 million JPY in FY ended Mar. 31, 2016, up 37.5% year-over-year.
- Composite plastic molded products sales totaled 239,185 million JPY for FY ended Mar. 31, 2016, an 18.5% increase.
- Operating income for composite plastic molded products was 29,591 million JPY, up 29.4% year-over-year.
- Nifco's mid-term business plan (Nifco Innovation Plan 2020) targets overall sales of 330.0 billion yen and operating income of 38.0 billion yen by FY ending Mar. 2021.
- The company plans to invest JPY 3,300 million for R&D activities during the fiscal year ending March 2017.
- Overall capital expenditure for FY ended Mar. 31, 2016 was 21,488 million JPY.
- Nifco Inc.'s German subsidiary, Nifco KTW GmbH, established a new company in Georgia, USA named Nifco KTW America Corporation.
- The new Georgia-based subsidiary will manufacture and sell plastic parts for automobiles.
- The facility is capitalized at USD 200 million (approximately JPY 2.4 billion).
- Capitalization funding is provided 90% by Nifco KTW and 10% by Nifco America.
- The new company will supply its products to a German automaker's North American facility.
- Planned capital investment for the new Georgia plant is JPY 3,088 million, with an investment timeline from May 2015 to May 2016.
- Volkswagen AG nominated Nifco KTS GmbH as one of 55 strategic partners across 61 fields of competence under its "Volkswagen Future Automotive Supply Tracks" (FAST) initiative.
- Nifco KTS GmbH was specifically designated for kinematic components.
- The nomination was announced via a press release on May 23, 2016.
- Nifco acquired German automotive plastic parts supplier KTW GmbH & Co. KG and its group companies to convert them into a fully owned subsidiary.
- The KTW Group manufactures and sells automotive plastic parts such as instrument panels and door trims, with its sole plant located in Bavaria.
- Prior to acquisition, sales to BMW accounted for more than half of KTW's total revenue.
- Nifco aims to enhance its European business by expanding relationships with German automakers using its global sales network.
- The company consolidated its automotive resin parts manufacturing subsidiaries in Germany under its fully owned subsidiary, Nifco Germany GmbH, effective December 31, 2014.
- R&D activities are conducted mainly at the Nifco Technology Development Centre, with consolidated Yokosuka and Nagoya functions relocated to a building next to the Yokosuka Research Park in FY ending March 2015.
- A testing facility and explosion-proof testing facility near the home office and R&D center were scheduled for completion during FY ending March 2017 to verify powertrain and fuel-system product capabilities.
- The company is developing energy-saving automotive products that switch base materials from metal to plastic to save weight and improve fuel efficiency.
- Nifco finished developing new fuel-system parts compliant with U.S. evaporative hydrocarbon emission regulations.
- The company developed related products for collision-reduction brake parts and obstacle-detection sensors for safety applications.
- Nifco spent JPY 6,876 million in capital investments for FY ended Mar. 31, 2016 on establishing a new headquarters office in Yokosuka, the Yamagata Plant, die manufacturing facilities, and fuel-system product equipment.
- Nifco America Corporation spent JPY 1,273 million to install facilities for fuel-system products and forming machines.
- Nifco (HK Ltd.) spent JPY 2,301 million mainly on constructing a new production plant.
- Nifco (Yancheng) Co., Ltd. in China spent JPY 1,736 million mainly on die facilities and forming machines.
- A total of JPY 6,285 million was spent in the Asian region for facilities and equipment during FY ended Mar. 31, 2016.
