# SK Battery

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-09

**Source:** [Kemp Makes Final Appeal to Biden to Save Korean Battery Plant as Deadline Looms - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/kemp-makes-final-appeal-to-biden-to-save-korean-battery-plant-as-deadline-looms/)  ·  `page_050112`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp made one last appeal to President Joe Biden to save the Korean-owned battery factory that has threatened to vacate a $2.6 billion investment in the state without presidential action.
- President Biden has until Sunday to overturn an International Trade Commission ruling barring SK Battery from importing the parts and materials needed for production at its Commerce, Ga., plant.
- The ITC ruling included carveouts to serve existing orders from Volkswagen and Ford.
- SK Battery stated that without President Biden’s intervention, the plant would no longer be economical and would be forced to shut down before beginning earnest production due to the import ban.
- At stake are 2,600 jobs in the first phase alone.
- SK Battery revealed it would ramp up to more than 6,000 jobs on $5 billion of total investment, double what it had previously announced.
- Gov. Kemp stated that a decision in support of Georgia jobs would make up for the economic blow from the loss of a Major League Baseball game, which he blamed on a 'disinformation campaign' by President Biden over new Georgia voting laws.
- Kemp said: 'However, the president now has yet another decision before him that will make or break a $2.6 billion investment in our state. The jobs of at least 2,600 Georgians depend on President Biden’s upcoming decision on the ITC ruling, and I sincerely hope he will use his authority to do the right thing.'
- The ITC separately ruled that SK had not infringed on LG Energy Solution’s patents, a decision seen as giving SK more leverage in the rivals' ongoing legal dispute.
- Both SK Battery and LG Energy Solution had previously stated they were open to a settlement in the initial case.
- Lawyers for SK pointed out the ruling was decided based on the idea that the company 'spoliated evidence' by destroying documents, rather than on the intellectual property merits underlying the dispute.
- If President Biden decides not to act, SK could file a lawsuit in federal court to get the import ban removed, or the companies could reach a settlement that would nullify it.
- LG Energy Solution has already filed an infringement suit in U.S. district court.
- Former acting U.S. Attorney General Sally Yates, now working in Atlanta for King & Spalding, stated the district court is the appropriate venue for the lawsuit.
- Yates, hired by SK to review the ITC ruling, agreed that destruction of evidence is a serious charge but said the trade commission only had an import ban as punishment, which hurts third parties and harms U.S. environmental goals.
- Yates stated: 'The district court can consider both the trade secret and spoliation claims, and, if LG can prove its case, mete out a proportional remedy that awards LG damages and sanctions SK for spoliation. In contrast to the ITC exclusion order, this accountability would not undermine critical climate initiatives, kill thousands of clean-energy jobs, set back the Georgia economy, give China a leg-up in the EV supply chain, or create USMCA compliance problems for U.S. auto companies. The district court is the most appropriate forum to resolve these issues.'
