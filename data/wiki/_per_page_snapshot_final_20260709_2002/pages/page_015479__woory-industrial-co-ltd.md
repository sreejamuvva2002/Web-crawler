# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Chron - Georgia: Gov. Kemp: Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County - 10229667](https://georgiachron.com/ganews/georgia-gov-kemp-woory-to-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rampd-facility-in-laurens-county-georgiachron-10229667)  ·  `page_015479`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd., an automotive parts manufacturer, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The new facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Woory Industrial is helping Georgia become the "e-mobility capital of the nation."
- Governor Kemp noted that the emerging industry brings historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities across Georgia due to the state's highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.
- Woory Industrial Company (Woory) was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory is currently concentrating on developing new products for electric vehicles (EVs) and hydrogen-fueled cars while maintaining its traditional product lines.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the company is facing a revolution in the automobile industry for the first time in 100 years and views the U.S. EV market as the key market among all automotive markets.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that over the past 10 years, Woory has grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation and views Georgia as an opportunity to advance as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- Woory's new facility, located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin, will be the company's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Electric vehicles lack an internal heating source, requiring dedicated heaters to heat the cabin and maintain battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that fostering quality and sustainable growth is a goal of the City of Dublin.
- Mayor Kight noted that Woory's commitment to repurposing an existing manufacturing building promotes infill development and creates new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, expressed excitement about welcoming Woory to the community.
- Dr. Williams highlighted Dublin and Laurens County's rapidly growing automotive sector, citing its location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce, and noted partnerships with area education systems and training providers will support Woory's talent pipeline.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects like Woory's first U.S. home in Dublin are a priority for bringing e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia and creating opportunities in rural communities.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with prepared communities and congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia.
- Georgia has positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry over the past century.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of EV industry job creation opportunities has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory began almost 35 years ago operating a small factory producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea, and has grown into an international enterprise with production plants worldwide employing over 1,300 people.
- Woory's current business focuses on developing and producing HVAC auto components for all vehicle types, including EVs, internal combustion engines, eco-friendly cars, and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo, and currently supplies the HV heater system used to heat EV cabins.
