# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-17

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Aspen Aerogels to Open Advanced Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-02-17/gov-kemp-aspen-aerogels-open-advanced-manufacturing-facility-bulloch)  ·  `page_000099`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels plans to construct an advanced manufacturing facility at Bulloch County’s Southern Gateway Commerce Park.
- Aspen intends to invest a minimum of $325 million in the new site near Statesboro.
- The project will create more than 250 advanced manufacturing jobs.
- Production at the new Georgia site is expected to begin in late 2023.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project and highlighted Georgia's growing electric mobility sector.
- The planned facility, combined with Aspen’s original East Providence, Rhode Island plant, is designed to triple Aspen’s nameplate aerogel capacity.
- The expansion supports Aspen’s thermal barrier expansion plans in the fast-growing electric vehicle market.
- Aspen aims to capitalize on increased demand for its Aerogel Technology Platform™ as EV OEMs adopt PyroThin® aerogel thermal barrier solutions.
- Don Young, President and CEO of Aspen, noted the company is pleased to be in Georgia for siting, development, and construction of the PyroThin materials plant.
- The City of Statesboro and Bulloch County region offer access to efficient ports, a strong available workforce, and secure low-cost utilities and critical raw materials.
- Aspen’s PyroThin materials provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems.
- Aspen plans to build a 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility situated on a 90-acre parcel in southeast Georgia.
- Hiring for personnel will begin in the second half of 2022 across skilled manufacturing operators, advanced process engineers, quality and maintenance technicians, and professional support roles.
- Billy Allen, Vice Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Aspen to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community.
- Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College are expected to provide critical workforce support to Aspen.
- Assistant Director Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division on this project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement about welcoming Aspen’s innovative solutions for the electric vehicle and energy storage markets to Georgia.
- Southern Gateway Commerce Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park.
- Aspen’s carbon aerogel program seeks to increase the performance of lithium-ion battery cells to enable EV manufacturers to extend driving range and reduce costs.
- Aspen manufactures its products at its East Providence, R.I. facilities and is headquartered in Northborough, MA.
