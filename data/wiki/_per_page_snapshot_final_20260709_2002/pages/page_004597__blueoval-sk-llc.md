# BlueOval SK LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Announces $9.63 Billion Loan to BlueOval SK to Further Expand US Manufacturing of Electric Vehicle Batteries | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/articles/doe-announces-963-billion-loan-blueoval-sk-further-expand-us-manufacturing-electric)  ·  `page_004597`

## Facts

- U.S. Department of Energy’s Loan Programs Office (LPO) announced the closing of a direct loan of up to $9.63 billion to BlueOval SK LLC (BOSK).
- The loan will finance the construction of up to three manufacturing plants to produce batteries for Ford Motor Company’s future Ford and Lincoln electric vehicles.
- One plant is located in Tennessee and two plants are located in Kentucky.
- Together, the three facilities would enable more than 120 gigawatt hours of U.S. battery production annually.
- The construction phase created more than 5,000 construction jobs as the plants were being built.
- The facilities will create up to 7,500 BOSK operations jobs.
- This announcement represents the largest loan granted through DOE’s Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) Program.
- BOSK is a joint venture between Ford Motor Company and SK On, a world-leading Korean EV battery manufacturer.
- BOSK was created to supply Ford with batteries for its growing EV product line.
- The announcement reinforces President Biden’s efforts to onshore and re-shore domestic manufacturing to ensure American businesses remain global leaders in future technologies.
- Expanding domestic battery production is critical to reducing America’s reliance on adversarial foreign nations like China, meeting climate commitments, and achieving the goal of making half of all new vehicles sold in 2030 zero-emissions vehicles.
- LPO borrowers are required to develop and implement a comprehensive Community Benefits Plan (CBP).
- As part of its CBP, BlueOval SK partnered with Tennessee College of Applied Technology through the state of Tennessee and Elizabethtown Community and Technical College in Kentucky to construct new community and technical colleges.
- These new colleges will train community members for quality jobs at the BOSK facilities.
- The announcement is one of several recent LPO loans under the ATVM Loan Program, demonstrating leadership in building a strong domestic EV supply chain.
- Through ATVM, LPO provides loans to projects related to fuel-efficient vehicles and eligible component manufacturing in the United States.
- Recent ATVM announcements have spanned critical mineral and battery supply chains, involving mineral processing, battery manufacturing, and battery recycling.
- LPO has been working with BOSK to finalize its loan since June 2023.
- Across all LPO programs, DOE attracted 212 applications for projects totaling over $324.3 billion in requested loans and loan guarantees as of November 2024.
