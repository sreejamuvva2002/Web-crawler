# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-18

**Source:** [Qcells North America Completes Dalton Factory Expansion - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-north-america-completes-dalton-factory-expansion/)  ·  `page_001589`

## Facts

- Qcells announced the successful completion of its Dalton, Georgia, factory expansion on Oct. 18, 2023.
- Qcells added 2 gigawatts (GW) of solar capacity to Dalton, bringing the full factory’s output to more than 5.1 GW.
- The Qcells Dalton factory is the largest manufacturing plant of its kind in the Western Hemisphere.
- It is the first solar panel plant expansion since the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- This addition marks the first phase of Qcells’ historic $2.5 billion investment announced in January 2023.
- The completed Dalton expansion created 510 additional solar factory jobs.
- The expansion will assemble two new solar products: the Q.TRON G2 residential solar panel and a bifacial panel for the commercial and utility markets.
- Qcells’ Dalton factory will manufacture nearly 30,000 solar panels per day.
- Beyond Dalton, the investment includes the development of a fully-integrated solar supply chain factory in Cartersville, Georgia.
- The Cartersville factory will manufacture solar ingots, wafers, cells and finished panels.
- By 2024, between the Dalton and Cartersville facilities, Qcells anticipates its solar production capacity to reach 8.4 GW a year.
- This capacity is enough to power 1.3 million homes annually with clean energy.
- Justin Lee, CEO of Qcells, stated that completing this factory marks the third expansion in Dalton and the beginning of building a fully integrated solar supply chain in America.
- Justin Lee credited the Inflation Reduction Act and Georgia’s economic development team for making these plans possible.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Qcells chose Georgia because of unrivaled assets and a competitive package put together in 2019.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted the milestone is a testament to the Dalton community's hard work and the state-level relationship with Qcells.
- U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff stated he wrote and passed the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act with this goal in mind.
- U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff thanked Qcells for continued investment in Georgia.
- U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff noted Georgia is emerging as the advanced energy capital of the nation due to federal infrastructure and manufacturing policies benefiting it more than any other state.
- Senator Reverend Warnock stated Georgians are seeing real-time benefits of the IRA and its clean energy provisions through Qcells' expansion.
- Senator Reverend Warnock noted over 500 solar manufacturing jobs were added in the process.
- Senator Reverend Warnock expressed a desire to continue partnering with Qcells as Georgia becomes the premier destination for solar manufacturing.
- Qcells opened its first factory in Georgia in 2019 and hired 750 people to manufacture 1.7 GW of solar.
- This initial investment was made possible in part by Section 201 tariffs imposed on solar cells.
- Last year, Qcells announced a plan to add 1.4 GW to its manufacturing output and hire 535 more people.
- The completed third expansion and investments across the full solar value chain follow the passage of the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act within the IRA.
- Both new products (Q.TRON G2 and bifacial panels) will likely achieve an ecolabel known as EPEAT to help customers identify sustainably made products.
- Upon completed construction, Qcells’ production in Georgia could avoid more than 12 million metric tons of CO2 equivalents per year.
- By 2024, the Dalton facility alone will employ nearly 1,800 people.
- By 2024, combined Dalton and Cartersville facilities will employ nearly 4,000 people and produce nearly 46,000 solar panels per day.
- Qcells is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea (Global Executive HQ) and Thalheim, Germany (Technology & Innovation HQ).
- Qcells operates diverse international manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, China, and South Korea.
- Qcells provides renewable electricity retail services to utility, commercial, governmental, and residential markets globally.
