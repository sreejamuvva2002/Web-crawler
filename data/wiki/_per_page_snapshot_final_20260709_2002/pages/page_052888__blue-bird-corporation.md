# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation is a leading manufacturer of school buses.
- The company was founded by Albert Luce, Sr. and has spent the last century defining the modern yellow school bus.
- The company has built more than 620,000 school buses over its lifetime.
- About 180,000 of Blue Bird's manufactured school buses are still in operation today.
- Blue Bird is the only remaining U.S.-owned and operated school bus manufacturer.
- The company's headquarters is located in Macon, Georgia.
- Blue Bird operates a manufacturing facility in Fort Valley, Georgia.
- In 2016, Blue Bird became the first to market with electronic stability control for school buses to prevent rollovers and loss of control.
- In 2025, Blue Bird made driver airbags standard across its school bus lineup.
- In 2025, Blue Bird became the first to market with three-point seatbelts for school buses.
- Blue Bird school buses feature high-resolution front and rear cameras.
- Blue Bird school buses are equipped with LED headlights for improved visibility in early-morning and evening conditions.
- Blue Bird school buses include illuminated signage and stop arms.
- Blue Bird school buses utilize high-intensity exterior LED lighting to increase visibility to nearby drivers.
- Blue Bird is developing low- and zero-emission vehicles to reduce environmental impact.
- The company produced its first electric school bus in 1994.
- The company produced its first propane-powered school bus in 2008.
- More than 25,000 propane and electric school buses have been sold by Blue Bird to date.
- Collectively, Blue Bird's low- and zero-emission buses reduce tens of thousands of metric tons of carbon dioxide annually.
