# Hyundai Mega Plant, Georgia

## Facts

- A federal immigration raid occurred at a Hyundai mega plant in Georgia on Thursday.
- The raid resulted in the arrests of about 475 suspects.
- Most of the detained suspects were Korean nationals.
- The raid took place about 20 miles outside of Savannah, Georgia.
- The operation was part of a long-term investigation and one of many notable worksite operations amid the Trump administration’s immigration crackdown.
- Hyundai officials stated they do not believe any of their direct employees were arrested but said they would comply with all laws and regulations.
- LG Energy Solution, which also plays a role in the plant, stated it will work with authorities.
- The EV manufacturing part of the facility is not believed to have been impacted by the raid.
- The battery factory under construction was the focus of the multi-agency effort.
- Former President Joe Biden previously touted the project as a win for American manufacturing.
- Both Democratic and Republican politicians have hailed the project as a win for American manufacturing.
- Scientist Matt Van Swol posted on X criticizing Democrats regarding the project's political reception.
- Maik Peters, CEO of Pitco Engineering, posted that American companies with legal workers are cut out of mega projects because they are too expensive.
- The South Korean government expressed concerns about the operation targeting Hyundai's Georgia facility.
- Writer James Surowiecki criticized the Trump administration for arresting South Koreans temporarily in the U.S. to help Hyundai set up the factory, despite Lutnick and Trump bragging about getting South Korea to invest billions in the U.S.
- Rep. Nikema Williams, D-Ga., described the raid as an "act of cruelty and injustice" by the Trump administration.
- Rep. Williams reported that U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents have also been wrongfully detained at the site.
- Rep. Williams criticized the administration for instilling fear and chaos within marginalized communities and demanded they follow due process and constitutional rights.
- Rep. Buddy Carter, R-Ga., issued a statement blaming Democrats for enabling illegal immigration issues in the U.S. for far too long.
- Rep. Carter applauded the Trump Administration and Operation Take Back America for taking bold action to put American workers first and protect communities from illegal immigration at the facility.
- Rep. Carter stated that no one can take jobs from hardworking Americans and give them to illegal immigrants under Donald Trump’s watch.
