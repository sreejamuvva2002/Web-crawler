# SK Innovation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK Battery - Plateau Excavation](https://plateauexcavation.com/projects/sk-battery/)  ·  `page_000546`

## Facts

- This project represents one of the largest single investments in a job-creating initiative in Georgia’s history and the largest in more than a decade.
- The project is projected to employ nearly 2,000 people by 2025.
- Plateau led site development in Phase I construction efforts for the new SK Innovation battery manufacturing plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- The facility is located on 283 acres along I-85 about 65 miles northeast of downtown Atlanta inside the Commerce industrial complex.
- The 2.4 million-square-foot, $1.67 billion facility is SK Innovation’s first EV battery factory in the U.S.
- Work began in spring 2019 with Phase 1 project completion scheduled for spring 2020.
- Plateau’s execution and service during the small enabling project at the beginning of this development opened up the remaining phases for the entire site.
- Phase 2 began construction in 2020.
- Phase 2 consisted of a 1.2 million-square-foot facility located along Phase 1 of the project and included site and utility work, construction of medium voltage banks and cross-country piping racks.
- The site development included a unique utility system with different types of effluent produced by the plant, requiring three separate sewer systems.
- Plateau constructed an onsite substation directly for Georgia Power.
- Some of the project challenges included the close proximity of the jobsite to the active facility as well as an accelerated timeline with substantial completion scheduled for December 2021.
- SK, which makes lithium-ion batteries for hybrid and fully electric automobiles, builds batteries for multiple vehicle manufacturers.
- SK Innovation will invest a total of $2.6B to complete construction of its first and second electric battery plants (Volkswagen and Ford), creating ~2,600 jobs by the end of 2023.
