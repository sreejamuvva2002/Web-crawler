# Hyundai Metaplant Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [ICE Detains 475 Workers at Hyundai's Georgia Metaplant - TFLcar](https://tflcar.com/2025/09/ice-detains-nearly-500-workers-at-hyundais-georgia-metaplant/)  ·  `page_001142`

## Facts

- ICE conducted an immigration raid on Hyundai’s Metaplant in Georgia on a Friday.
- The raid is part of a series of high-profile federal government actions over the past few years regarding Hyundai's labor practices.
- One of Hyundai's suppliers was involved in a child labor scandal in late 2022.
- DHS's ICE carried out a large-scale raid on Hyundai’s new 2,900-acre Metaplant site in Ellabell, Georgia, just outside Savannah.
- CNN notes a statement from the Homeland Security Investigations special agent in charge calling this action “the largest single-site enforcement operation” in the agency’s history.
- Federal officials apprehended approximately 475 people during the sweep of the plant.
- Most of the detained individuals are South Korean nationals.
- ICE spokesperson Lindsay Williams said the raid largely focused on Hyundai’s new battery plant, which is under construction as part of a partnership with LG (officially called “HL-GA Battery Company”).
- The agency states the raid occurred “part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.”
- HSI agent Steven Shrank told media that today’s action was part of a “multi-month criminal investigation where we have developed evidence and conducted interviews, gathered documents and presented that evidence to the court in ode to obtain a judicial search warrant.”
- Hyundai spokesperson Michael Stewart told media that the automaker is cooperating fully with agents, and that those detained were not direct employees of the company.
- Direct quote from Stewart: “As of today, it is our understanding that none of those detained is directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.”
- Stewart added: “We prioritize the safety and well-being of everyone working at the site and comply with all laws and regulations wherever we operate.”
- LG has not made an official comment on the raid.
- ICE did not yet comment on whether the 475 individuals were contractors, subcontractors, or actually worked for either partner involved in the Metaplant’s battery site construction.
- Hyundai stressed that the ICE operation did not impact its manufacturing plant’s production.
- The Metaplant started building Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 SUVs in March, as part of Hyundai’s $7.6 billion manufacturing footprint in Georgia.
- The South Korean government expressed “concern and regret” over the ICE operation affecting its nationals.
- South Korea’s foreign minister, Lee Jaewoong, said it is dispatching Washington, DC-based diplomats at the embassy to Atlanta, with plans to form an on-site response team.
- Lee Jaewoong stated: “The business activities of our investors and the rights of our nationals must not be unjustly infringed in the process of US law enforcement.”
- HSI agent Shrank said most of the 475 detainees were taken to a detention center in Folkston, Georgia.
- The Folkston detention center is close to the Florida state border and 2 hours south of Ellabell.
