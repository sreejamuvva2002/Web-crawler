# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Panasonic Automotive Systems與VicOne攜手 推動新世代車用座艙系統資安創新 | iThome](https://www.ithome.com.tw/pr/170442)  ·  `page_047999`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems (headquarters: Yokohama, Kanagawa, Japan; President: Masamasa Nagatoyo) and VicOne (headquarters: Shinjuku, Tokyo, Japan; CEO: Cheng Yi-li) announced expanded support for cybersecurity innovation in container environments for next-generation in-vehicle cockpit systems and completed technical validation.
- In October 2024, Panasonic Automotive Systems released VERZEUSE® for Virtualization Extensions Type-3 attack detection and protection technology.
- The innovation integrates VicOne's automotive cybersecurity software xCarbon to further expand its functionality.
- The expanded VERZEUSE® for Virtualization Extensions Type-3 can effectively monitor communications between containers on hardware development boards running Linux-based in-vehicle system software AGL (Automotive Grade Linux).
- The technology demonstrates application potential for future in-vehicle cockpit systems.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems will exhibit this technology innovation at the 31st ITS World Congress in Atlanta, USA, from August 24 to 28, 2025.
- VERZEUSE® primarily monitors communications between containers: (1) software areas like infotainment systems connected to external networks that are vulnerable to attacks in the virtual vehicle control unit; and (2) software areas like dashboard containers in the virtual vehicle control unit that execute basic functions like vehicle control and software updates.
- The monitoring function is installed in an isolated container, allowing it to monitor communications between the above containers from a secure area, detect and block suspicious communications, protect core vehicle functions from cyberattacks, and improve overall vehicle security and cybersecurity levels.
- The system meets the cybersecurity requirements of next-generation cockpit systems in virtualized environments.
- VicOne's xCarbon is vehicle security software for intrusion detection and defense against cyberattacks.
- By linking xCarbon to the VERZEUSE® expansion interface, it monitors inter-container communications to protect next-gen cockpit systems from attackers exploiting vulnerabilities or communication errors.
- The system can also identify high-risk communications and route them to xCarbon analysis for more efficient security monitoring.
- With rapid development of Software Defined Vehicle (SDV) technology and increasing connected vehicles, cybersecurity risks like cyberattacks on vehicles have risen.
- The UN issued automotive cybersecurity regulation UN-R155 in January 2021.
- In Japan, the UN-R155 regulation was implemented on some vehicles starting July 2022 and will be expanded to all vehicles starting May 2026.
- Under the regulation, vehicles lacking sufficient cybersecurity measures will not meet safety standards and will be banned from sale.
- Security has become an increasingly important focus for automakers and related companies.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems and VicOne will continue to collaborate to advance cybersecurity for next-generation in-vehicle cockpit systems.
- VicOne is a subsidiary of Trend Micro (headquarters: Shinjuku, Tokyo, Japan; CEO: Chen Yihua).
- VERZEUSE is a trademark registered by Panasonic Automotive Systems or its affiliates in the US.
- AGL is an in-vehicle operating system for next-generation cockpit systems.
- The technology references 'ST-CSP-18: Definition Document of In-Vehicle Security Function Requirements Using Software Isolation Technology Ver.1.01' by JASPAR (Japan Automotive Software Platform and Architecture Alliance), 2023.
- SDV stands for Software Defined Vehicle.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. (PAS) was established on April 1, 2022, as an operating company responsible for in-vehicle system business under the Panasonic Group's operating company system.
- As of December 2, 2024, PAS is managed by a fund owned 80% by Apollo Global Management, Inc. subsidiary and 20% by Panasonic Holdings Co., Ltd.
- PAS headquarters are in Japan with subsidiaries in eight countries globally.
- As a Tier 1 automotive component supplier, PAS provides advanced proprietary technology like infotainment systems to Japanese and overseas automakers.
- PAS aims to realize the corporate vision 'Joy in Motion' by meeting global customer expectations with technology close to people's needs.
