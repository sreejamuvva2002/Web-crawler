# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-19

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group Sign MoU for Future Mobility Collaboration - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2023/09/19/georgia-tech-and-hyundai-motor-group-sign-mou-for-future-mobility-collaboration/)  ·  `page_009333`

## Facts

- The partnership between Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group will focus on sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, and workforce development.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing more than $12 billion in EV manufacturing and battery production in Georgia.
- Hyundai’s economic impact in Georgia will be tens of billions of dollars in the years to come and will help create nearly 40,000 jobs.
- The collaboration builds upon Hyundai Motor Group’s long history in Georgia.
- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding on September 19, 2023, to collaborate on research and applications to support the future of sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, workforce development, smart cities, and other areas of cooperation.
- The group is investing $12.6 billion in new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- This includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site in Bryan County, Georgia that will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- This also includes a $5 billion battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Georgia that will employ approximately 3,500 people.
- The close proximity of these facilities to Georgia Tech was an important consideration in choosing the locations.
- The Kia Georgia assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009 and was established under the leadership of Honorary Chairman Mong-Koo Chung and Executive Chair Euisun Chung.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue was Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia plant opening and also attended today’s ceremony.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals that Hyundai Motor’s automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2021 alone.
- According to the study, Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that Hyundai is a global brand synonymous with quality, innovation, and technology advancement, and expressed gratitude for the transformative investments in the state.
- President Cabrera noted that the Hyundai brand will feature prominently on campus and looks forward to working with Hyundai leaders to develop exceptional leaders and new ideas shaping the automotive industry and future mobility.
- Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, said Georgia is like a second home to the company and emphasized their mission-driven commitment to a sustainable future.
- Chang stated that the partnership with Georgia Tech will last for decades and was one of the reasons Hyundai chose Georgia for its EV investments.
- José Muñoz, President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, said Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Muñoz noted that proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the many reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for its new EV manufacturing facility.
- Muñoz envisioned zero emissions hydrogen-powered vehicles on campus, advanced air mobility shuttles to Hartsfield International Airport, and hands-free autonomous vehicles on I-75 and I-85 as part of the collaboration's potential impact.
- The partnership will include opportunities for student professional development and cooperative work programs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia stands at the forefront of an unprecedented transformation in the mobility sector toward sustainable transportation alternatives.
- Wilson highlighted that Georgia’s strategic focus on building a sustainable mobility ecosystem continues to add to its industry-leading success and brings future jobs to Georgians statewide.
- The partnership includes joint research and development programs focused on sustainable mobility, battery technology, and expanding the hydrogen economy.
- Georgia Tech and Hyundai will create technical training and leadership development programming for Hyundai employees.
- The collaboration will initiate engagement activities to stimulate interest in STEM degrees among students.
- Hyundai will actively participate in career recruitment events at Georgia Tech and create learning programs for both undergraduate and graduate students.
- Hyundai’s partnership includes field-naming recognition at Bobby Dodd Stadium, which is now known as Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field.
- The partnership will provide new revenue for Georgia Tech Athletics to support student-athletes and teams athletically and academically.
- The collaboration provides a vital pathway for Georgia Tech to expand its stature as a leading research entity in automotive electrification through world-class hydrogen research.
- The partnership offers Georgia Tech an opportunity to contribute more broadly to Georgia’s commitment as a hub for electric vehicle production.
