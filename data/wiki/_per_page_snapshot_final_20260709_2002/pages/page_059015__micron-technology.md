# Micron Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-05

**Source:** [US semiconductor giant begins $9.3-billion chip plant expansion in Japan](https://interestingengineering.com/innovation/us-giant-breaks-ground-on-chip-plant)  ·  `page_059015`

## Facts

- Micron Technology has broken ground on the expansion of its manufacturing facility in Hiroshima Prefecture.
- The project represents a $9.3 billion investment that will significantly increase production of advanced memory chips.
- The Idaho-based company plans to manufacture next-generation products, including high-bandwidth memory.
- High-bandwidth memory is a key component used in AI processors such as those powering Nvidia’s systems.
- Commercial shipments from the expanded facility are expected to begin around the summer of 2028.
- Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry has committed up to $3.1 billion in subsidies to support the project.
- The expansion forms part of a broader global push by leading memory chip manufacturers to expand capacity amid rapidly rising demand fueled by AI, high-performance computing, and advanced data center infrastructure.
- Micron is expanding its manufacturing footprint in the United States with two leading-edge fabrication plants in Boise, Idaho.
- In January, the company held a groundbreaking ceremony for a $100 billion semiconductor manufacturing campus outside Syracuse, New York.
- The Syracuse campus is part of Micron’s long-term strategy to increase domestic DRAM production and strengthen the US memory chip supply chain.
- Major memory manufacturers including South Korea’s SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics are also investing heavily in new production facilities to meet surging demand from artificial intelligence applications.
- According to Micron President and CEO Sanjay Mehrotra, the company’s first high-bandwidth memory production wafer was manufactured at its Hiroshima facility.
- Mehrotra noted that the project demonstrates the strength of combining American innovation with Japanese manufacturing expertise, arguing that the partnership delivers world-class semiconductor technology rather than a compromise between the two.
- The Hiroshima expansion is expected to strengthen Micron’s ability to manufacture next-generation memory chips with higher power efficiency and faster data transfer speeds for AI systems and autonomous vehicles.
- Japan’s Industry Minister Ryosei Akazawa described the government’s support for Micron, now the country’s only domestic producer of DRAM memory chips, as having “invaluable worth”.
- Akazawa added that Japan is prepared to provide similar support to other international semiconductor companies looking to establish manufacturing operations in the country.
- The Japanese government emphasized its commitment to strengthening its chip industry and attracting further investment.
- Japan has significantly expanded its support for the semiconductor and artificial intelligence industries since 2021, committing tens of billions of dollars to strengthen a sector it considers critical to national and economic security.
- Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi last month unveiled a long-term roadmap aimed at mobilizing approximately $630 billion in combined public and private investment in semiconductors and AI through March 2041.
- The government has not specified how much of the $630 billion total will come from public funding.
- Kota Nosaka, representative director of Micron Japan, pointed out that the Hiroshima facility’s competitive advantage lies in its ability to rapidly deliver high-performance memory products to customers.
- The production of next-generation chips at the Hiroshima site plays a central role in Micron’s long-term global strategy.
