# Thermal Ceramics

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-15

**Source:** [Thermal Ceramics confirms upcoming closure | KVOE](https://kvoe.com/2025/01/15/thermal-ceramics-confirms-upcoming-closure/)  ·  `page_010986`

## Facts

- Local management isn’t mentioning an official date, but it is confirming the closure of Emporia’s Thermal Ceramics plant.
- The Emporia plant has had the Thermal Ceramics brand since the 1980s.
- The plant is closing this year as its parent company, Morgan Advanced Materials, consolidates local operations with a plant in Augusta, Georgia.
- The closure impacts 35 employees.
- Impacted employees have the option of moving to the consolidated facility or taking severance packages.
- Separation package negotiations involve conversations with Boilermakers Local 83 based in Kansas City, Missouri, and discussions have not finished as of late Wednesday afternoon.
- Thermal Ceramics makes chemical components for furnaces.
- Parent company Morgan Advanced Materials creates thermal products for insulation, heat shields, so-called fired refractory shapes and other high-temperature settings involving aerospace, automotive and marine environments.
- Morgan Advanced Materials is headquartered in the United Kingdom.
- Morgan Advanced Materials has 17 manufacturing and/or sales locations in the United States, along with nearly 70 other locations worldwide.
- Thermal Ceramics becomes the latest production business with a local connection to close its operations, following the Tyson Foods announcement in December and closure announcements from Holiday Resort and Bling and Buckle since early December.
