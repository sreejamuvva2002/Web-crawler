# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Establish Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture in the U.S.](https://www.hyundai.news/eu/articles/press-releases/hmg-and-lg-energy-solution-establish-battery-cell-manufacturing-joint-venture.html)  ·  `page_001252`

## Facts

- The new battery cell joint venture will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia, adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which is currently under construction.
- Total investment for the joint venture will be over USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion), with Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution each taking a 50 percent stake.
- Construction on the joint venture facility is scheduled to start in the second half of 2023.
- Battery production at the joint venture is aimed to start at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- The facility will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh, capable of supporting the production of 300,000 EVs annually.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant and supply them to the Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility aims to create a stable regional supply of batteries and allow the Group to respond quickly to soaring EV demand in the U.S. market.
- With this joint venture, LG Energy Solution now has seven battery plants currently operating or under construction in the U.S., where it is concentrating most of its resources to expand production capacity.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution signed a memorandum of understanding to produce EV batteries in the U.S. during a signing ceremony at LGES’s headquarters in Seoul on May 26.
- The May 26 signing ceremony was attended by Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, and Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution.
- Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry through establishing this new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- LG Energy Solution commits to advancing product competitiveness and global operational expertise to drive the EV transition in America and expedite the clean energy transition in the U.S.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have a long-standing partnership in electrification, having previously supplied EV batteries for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6 dedicated EV.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was the Group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution started construction of a battery cell joint venture in Indonesia, which is set to start production in the first half of 2024.
- This facility marks the second battery manufacturing joint venture for Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution as they aim to continue strengthening their ties in electrification efforts.
