# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia’s Connectivity Advantages](https://bxjmag.com/georgias-connectivity-advantages/)  ·  `page_045281`

## Facts

- Tom Croteau serves as deputy commissioner of global commerce for the Georgia Economic Development Department.
- Georgia has been ranked as the best state in the country to do business over the last few years.
- The state offers low unionization and low cost structure advantages, alongside Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- About 90 percent of Hartsfield-Jackson airport is located in Clayton County.
- Courtney Pogue serves as director of economic development for the Clayton County Office of Economic Development.
- The Atlanta Aerotropolis Alliance organizes industrial and retail development around the airport through cooperation of municipalities, economic development entities, and businesses.
- Artie Jones III serves as director of economic development for College Park.
- The western entrance to Hartsfield-Jackson airport is located in College Park.
- Logistics and distribution interest in the aerotropolis includes companies like DHL and FedEx, as well as perishable goods and medicines.
- Fort Gillem in Forest Park features the Gillem Logistics Center where Kroger is building a 1.2-million-square-foot distribution center.
- Meriwether County, located southwest of Atlanta on I-85, has a growing industrial park.
- Jane Fryer serves as executive director of the Meriwether County Industrial Development Authority.
- Mando America Corp. is the largest employer in Meriwether County's industrial park, operating two brand new facilities and preparing to build a third facility in late 2015.
- Mando serves area automotive manufacturers including Kia.
- Rachel Rowell serves as president and CEO of the Development Authority of Polk County.
- Polk County recently completed a 100,000-square-foot spec building in Cedartown, Ga., with room for expansion.
- Polk County possesses two dual-rail served capable sites.
- Polk County's three municipalities collaborate on Blueprint Polk 2020, which includes a College and Career Academy.
- The Port Tax Credit Bonus is available to qualifying Georgia companies that increase imports or exports through a Georgia port by at least 10 percent.
- Georgia’s Retraining Tax Credit equals up to half of direct training expenses of up to $500 per full-time employee per training program for qualifying businesses retraining employees.
- The Investment Tax Credit ranges from 1 percent to 8 percent of qualified capital investments of at least $50,000 for qualifying manufacturing or telecommunications support companies upgrading or expanding.
- Georgia’s Mega Project Tax Credit is available to qualifying large businesses that employ at least 1,800 net new employees.
- Financial technology (FinTech), information security, and health information technology are thriving industries in Georgia.
- About 70 percent of transactions processed in the U.S. come through Atlanta.
- About 90 percent of the FinTech industry is based in Atlanta.
- The American Transaction Processing Coalition was formed in Atlanta.
- Worldpay announced 595 jobs earlier this year, and Fiserv announced another net 500 jobs for the metro Atlanta area.
- Georgia is the leading state in the nation with cybersecurity, generating more than 25 percent of the world market share from companies in Georgia, which hosts more than 115 information security companies.
- Information security is prominent at Fort Gordon in Augusta, where the federal government has enhanced their cybersecurity presence.
- Unisys Corp. announced 700 jobs in Augusta this year.
- In Georgia, 225 health information technology companies employ approximately 30,000 people.
- Google is investing $300 million into infrastructure and data centers in Georgia.
- AT&T operates a call center in Polk County employing over 250 people.
- Automotive development is a particularly hot topic for industrial growth in Georgia.
- Jefferson Southern, a Tier I supplier to Honda, is located in Polk County.
- Meriwether County's industrial park hosts mostly automotive suppliers, including manufacturers of medical devices, auto bodies, and ambulances, due to proximity to Kia.
- Polk County is home to Tip Top Poultry Inc. and Carlton Farms, an agro tourism facility.
- College Park currently has 32 hotels with one under construction, making hospitality a key industry.
- Legacy Movie Ranch will be the first movie ranch in Georgia, located in Meriwether County.
- Atlanta is experiencing net in-migration and has a young demographic due to residential rebirth in the city core.
- Georgia Quick Start is available statewide, enabling the university system to meet with employers to develop curricula and provide training for new employees.
- Clayton State University and Atlanta Technical College are key workforce training players in Clayton County.
- West Georgia Technical College is located in Greenville and works on workforce training including high school dual enrollment programs.
- Industries partner with Georgia Tech and Georgia Northwestern Technical College to create tailored training programs.
- Polk County’s College and Career Academy trains students and teachers in skills needed by local employers.
- The Port of Savannah ranks No. 4 in terms of volume of containers and cargo and is growing fast.
- Atlanta airport is connected to Savannah by about three hours of interstate driving, providing a strength for distribution and logistics companies.
- Clayton County Water Authority can produce up to 42 million gallons of potable water per day and has sustainable practices that attract food processors and heavy water-use companies.
- College Park owns about 350 acres of land adjacent to the airport that can be developed for anything excluding residential.
- Hartsfield-Jackson airport is within a two-hour flight of 80 percent of the U.S. population.
- The airport land will be designated as a tax allocation district (TAD).
- Meriwether County is on I-85, 42 miles from the Atlanta airport, close enough to attract suppliers to Kia Motors.
- Their industrial park is located about 20 miles from the Kia site.
- Polk County operates two active industrial parks: Rockmart 101 (with dual-rail-capable options) and Cedartown North Business Park (home to a 100,000 square foot spec building).
- Georgia offers single sales factor apportionment when calculating taxable corporate income.
- Community incentive programs can include 100 percent Freeport tax exemption, no impact fees, grant assistance, property tax abatements, and collaborative government assistance to meet speed-to-market requirements.
- Floyd Polk Medical Center recently opened a 25-bed critical care facility with a helipad in Polk County.
- The Roosevelt Warm Springs Institute is a medical facility in Meriwether County for severe injuries and wounded warriors.
- Tourism is a large industry in Meriwether County, featuring covered bridges, the Flint River for rafting and canoeing, and Franklin Roosevelt’s Little White House.
- College Park is located 12 minutes from downtown Atlanta, adjacent to the airport, and along the MARTA light rail line.
- The Atlanta BeltLine is a repurposed railroad turned into about 22 miles of walkways with growing businesses.
