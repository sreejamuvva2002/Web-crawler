# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG Forge Ahead in the EV Race with a $4.3 Billion Battery Plant in Georgia](https://www.reportlinker.com/article/4292)  ·  `page_001242`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced a joint venture to build an EV battery plant in Georgia.
- The joint venture represents a $4.3 billion investment.
- The plant is set to be located in Bryan County near Savannah, Georgia.
- The facility represents one of the largest foreign investments in the U.S. battery manufacturing sector.
- This venture is Hyundai and LG's most ambitious project to date, building on their previous collaborations.
- The construction of the plant is anticipated to create thousands of jobs, driving economic growth in the Savannah area and Georgia.
- Senator Jon Ossoff highlighted that the project is a direct result of manufacturing incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The plant is positioned as a step towards establishing Georgia as a key player in the green technology and renewable energy sectors.
- The expansion of EV battery production capabilities accelerates the transition to sustainable transportation options and reduces reliance on fossil fuels.
- The manufacturing process and sourcing of materials like lithium raise concerns about the environmental footprint of battery production.
- The plant will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh.
- The facility's capacity is capable of supporting the production of 300,000 EVs.
- The plant's location in Georgia is adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America signifies a strategic move to integrate battery production with vehicle assembly, reducing logistics costs and minimizing transportation environmental impact.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution are breaking ground on the new venture.
