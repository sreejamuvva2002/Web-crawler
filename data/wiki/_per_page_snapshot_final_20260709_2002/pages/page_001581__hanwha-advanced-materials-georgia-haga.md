# Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia (HAGA)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [North American Clean Energy - Qcells’ Supplier Expands Supply Chain Investment in Georgia](https://www.nacleanenergy.com/solar/qcells-supplier-expands-supply-chain-investment-in-georgia)  ·  `page_001581`

## Facts

- Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia (HAGA) will build a new advanced materials manufacturing facility in Bartow County, Georgia.
- HAGA will supply Qcells with Encapsulant film, which is used to encapsulate solar cells and ensure long-term panel durability.
- The project represents a $147 million investment.
- The investment is expected to create more than 160 new jobs.
- This investment is critical to supporting Qcells plans to develop a fully integrated solar supply chain in America.
- HAGA will be the only company in the United States manufacturing solar EVA.
- Inhwan Kim, CEO of Hanwha Advanced Materials, stated that the products they make are an important piece of the clean energy supply chain puzzle and they are excited to meet this need.
- Inhwan Kim said building cutting-edge advanced materials in Georgia will create new careers in solar and help bring more affordable, reliable clean energy to customers across the country.
- Justin Lee, CEO of Qcells, stated that Qcells is doubling down on building a complete, domestic solar supply chain and this recent investment is critical to making that happen.
- Justin Lee said working with Hanwha Advanced Materials will allow customers to confidently know that the solar they buy from them was made right here in America.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is leading the nation in attracting next generation jobs.
- Governor Kemp noted that since welcoming Qcells to the state in 2018, more than 4,000 related jobs have been announced for Georgians.
- Governor Kemp expressed pride that Hanwha Advanced Materials is adding to that growing number as it becomes a valued member of the Bartow County community.
- Senator Jon Ossoff stated that his solar manufacturing law continues to deliver jobs, investment, and wealth for Georgians.
- Senator Ossoff called the project a continuation of the largest clean energy manufacturing investment in American history.
- U.S. Senator Reverend Warnock stated he is thrilled that Georgians are reaping the economic benefits of the Inflation Reduction Act that has helped pave the way for advanced manufacturing companies like QCells to invest and expand in the state.
- Senator Warnock said he looks forward to continuing to partner with QCells as the state becomes the premier destination for 21st century jobs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hanwha Advanced Materials provides key components for the solar supply chain in Georgia, supporting the state's commitment to making energy solutions more affordable to businesses and consumers.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that over the past five years, clean-tech jobs have been one of the fastest growing manufacturing sectors in Georgia.
- Commissioner Wilson expressed excitement that Hanwha Advanced Materials will add another crucial part of the solar supply chain to Georgia's already robust ecosystem.
- Mayor of Cartersville Matt Santini stated that the community is thrilled to be adding another Hanwha affiliate to their growing family of industries.
- Mayor Santini said their investment in bringing quality jobs to the community is greatly appreciated.
- Mayor Santini noted that the mix of industries in Cartersville and Bartow County is further diversified by adding them to the local mix, and one of their goals from the beginning was to recruit world-class companies to the area.
- Mayor Santini stated this helps accomplish the goal of recruiting world-class companies and notes it is another clean energy company coming to the community, illustrating the growth and importance of that market.
- This announcement comes only two months after Qcells revealed its plans to invest an historic $2.5 billion into building ingot, wafers, and cells as well as expand solar panel manufacturing in Georgia.
- Qcells also recently announced plans to buy polysilicon from REC Silicon, which is using clean energy to power its facility in Moses Lake, Washington.
- Soon, Qcells products, from polysilicon to panel, will be sustainably made in America.
- This investment comes after years of partnership with leaders in Georgia and following the passage of the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act (SEMA) within the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The demand for American-made solar panels is increasing rapidly driven by efforts to ensure energy independence, lower energy costs, and create new careers in energy.
- Qcells’ domestic manufacturing expansion will fulfill the growing need for these clean energy solutions and strengthen Georgia’s position as a global leader in solar.
