# Bosch

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-26

**Source:** [Bosch to acquire chipmaker to meet SiC chip demand for EVs](https://electrek.co/2023/04/26/bosch-to-acquire-chipmaker-to-meet-sic-chip-demand-for-evs/)  ·  `page_018027`

## Facts

- Bosch announced it is acquiring US chipmaker TSI Semiconductor.
- Bosch is investing $1.5 billion to produce silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductors and enable the adoption of EVs.
- Bosch has been transforming its business as the auto industry shifts to sustainable, zero-emission EVs.
- Bosch has invested heavily in introducing new products such as electric powertrains, charging solutions, and electric drives to meet the growing demand for EVs.
- Mike Mansuetti is President of Bosch in North America.
- Mansuetti explained last year that the auto industry is “rapidly evolving” toward electric vehicles.
- In October, Bosch revealed a new $250 million investment to extend its Charleston, South Carolina, campus by roughly 75,000 square feet to build and assemble electric motors.
- Mansuetti added, “Local production helps advance our customer’s regional electrification strategies and further supports the market demand for electrification.”
- Bosch is expanding its North American manufacturing capabilities with plans to acquire Roseville, California-based chipmaker TSI Semiconductors.
- Bosch will invest $1.5 billion in the Roseville site to convert and prepare the facility for the auto industry’s future.
- Starting in 2026, Bosch will produce the first chips on 200-millimeter wafers based on silicon carbide (SiC) material at the new site.
- Bosch has been producing SiC chips since 2021 at its Reutlingen location in Germany.
- Bosch expects to have an average of 25 of its chips in every new EV by 2025.
- SiC chips enable greater range and more efficient recharging, losing up to 50% less energy.
- The full scope of the project is made possible and will rely heavily on federal funding opportunities according to Bosch.
- The Biden administration is investing heavily to promote North American manufacturing and the adoption of sustainable transportation and clean energy in the US.
- The Inflation Reduction Act, passed last August, provides incentives and rebates to purchase EVs (up to $7,500 for new and $4,000 for used) and other clean energy alternatives (example: 30% solar tax credit).
- The Chips and Science Act, signed into law last year, invests in US chip-making capabilities to strengthen the domestic supply chain and enable clean energy adoption.
- Vice President Kamala Harris provided a statement regarding the Bosch acquisition and investment.
- The $1.5 billion investment will bring down costs, strengthen the electric vehicle supply chain, help rebuild American manufacturing, and create economic opportunity for working families of California.
- It will put more electric vehicles on the road, a priority Harris worked for since serving in the US Senate.
- All of this was made possible by the Administration’s Invest in America agenda.
- Harris stated that House Republicans are working to undo progress and want to defund American manufacturing and ship jobs overseas.
- President Biden and Vice President Harris will never let that happen.
- According to a recent Financial Times analysis, over $200 billion in private investments in the US has been announced in the eight months since the Chips and Science Act and Inflation Reduction Act were signed.
- The Biden administration is aiming for a 50% electric vehicle sales share by 2030.
