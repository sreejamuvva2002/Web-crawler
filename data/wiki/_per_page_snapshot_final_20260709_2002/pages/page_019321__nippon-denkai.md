# Nippon Denkai

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Japanese firm shifts copper foil plans from SC to Georgia](https://wgxa.tv/news/local/japanese-firm-shifts-copper-foil-plans-from-sc-to-georgia)  ·  `page_019321`

## Facts

- A Japanese company announced plans on Wednesday for a $150 million plant to make copper foil for electric vehicle batteries in Augusta, Georgia.
- The company shifted the plant location from a previously announced site near an existing facility in Camden, South Carolina.
- Nippon Denkai's American subsidiary plans to hire more than 100 people for the Georgia site.
- The company could eventually triple production at the Georgia site from an initial 9,500 metric tons (10,500 tons) to 28,500 metric tons (31,500 tons).
- Tripling production would raise total investment to $430 million and create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- Denkai America spokesperson Michael Coll stated, “Our goal is to triple the capacity in Augusta; market conditions will determine timeline.”
- The company raised its initial investment announcement by $10 million compared to what was announced in December.
- The company told Japanese investors the 115-acre (47-hectare) Augusta site offers room for expansion.
- The Augusta site can host an “advanced” equipment layout compared to the South Carolina location.
- The Augusta site provides a “key cost competitive edge in electricity price.”
- State and local officials will provide the company with what could be more than $76 million in tax breaks and incentives.
- Nippon Denkai plans to start construction in September.
- The company hopes to begin shipping samples to manufacturers by the summer of 2024.
- The company identified a major opportunity to supply copper foil for electric vehicle battery makers due to many new U.S. battery plants and few domestic copper foil plants.
- The company stated that manufacturers will seek domestic sources to avoid supply chain problems, including risks of politically driven supply disruptions.
- The company projects an annual 21.4% growth in copper foil demand through 2035.
- Nippon Denkai stated that tripling production in Augusta will enable it to pursue projected market growth.
- Combined with its 30-year-old Camden plant, the company's total American production capacity would exceed 35,000 metric tons.
- In June, Denkai announced a $14 million expansion at its 80-worker Camden plant, also tied to the electric battery and vehicle market.
- Augusta-Richmond County will provide property tax breaks valued at a projected $68.7 million over 25 years.
- The company will pay $30 million in taxes under the agreement, according to the Augusta Economic Development Authority.
- The company receives free land valued at $2.25 million, according to Augusta Economic Development Authority president Cal Wray.
- Georgia officials will spend an undisclosed amount of money to train workers for the facility.
- Nippon Denkai can claim various state tax breaks, including an income tax credit allowing an annual deduction of $4,000 per job from state income taxes, up to $5 million over five years.
- The income tax credit requires workers to earn at least $28,000 a year.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson called the announcement part of a “generational opportunity” in electric vehicles for the state.
- Wilson cited the $5 billion Rivian Automotive plant east of Atlanta and the $5.5 billion Hyundai Motor Group plant near Savannah as examples of the state's EV focus.
- Cal Wray noted that a $347 million, 125-job copper smelter announced by Aurubis AG in Augusta is another EV-driven development.
