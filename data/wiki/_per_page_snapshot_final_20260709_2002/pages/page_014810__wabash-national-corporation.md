# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-03

**Source:** [Wabash National Corp (WNC) Q4 2020 Earnings Call Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2021/02/03/wabash-national-corp-wnc-q4-2020-earnings-call-tra/)  ·  `page_014810`

## Facts

- Developed Molded Structure Composite (MSC) technology as a revolutionary new material with lighter weight and improved thermal properties for the refrigerated van market.
- In 2020, worked with a major grocery company that piloted an MSC truck body designed specifically for home delivery of groceries.
- Positions composite expertise as a competitive differentiator within the electric chassis space.
- Lighter weight composite materials for truck bodies and trailers directly impact vehicle range and payload in the EV space.
- Completed an agreement to manufacture Gruau refrigerated inserts for the Ford Transit within the United States.
- Gruau inserts provide 30% to 50% thermal efficiency compared to standard refrigerated body construction, reducing spoilage and improving food safety.
- Developing Tech Net technology to reduce carbon impact from product use and create opportunities to reduce natural resource consumption in manufacturing processes.
- Overall backlog ended Q4 2020 at approximately $1.5 billion, up sequentially by approximately $500 million from the end of Q3.
- Successfully hired approximately 600 new employees across the business during Q4 2020.
- Q4 2020 hiring activity added about 15% to the total workforce.
- Expects to add another 900 employees during the first half of 2021.
- Total hiring from September 30 to March 31 expected to be roughly 1,500 hourly employees.
- Initiated full year 2021 revenue outlook at just under $2 billion ($1.9 billion to $2 billion).
- Expects 2021 earnings per share of approximately $0.75 at the midpoint.
- Q4 2020 consolidated revenue was $404 million with consolidated new trailer shipments of approximately 10,600 units.
- Q4 2020 operating income was $10 million with an operating margin of 2.5%.
- Commercial Trailer Products (CTP) segment generated $283 million in revenue and $23.3 million non-GAAP adjusted operating income in Q4 2020.
- Average selling price for new trailers within CTP was about $27,000 in Q4 2020.
- Diversified Products Group (DPG) generated $75 million of revenue and $3.3 million non-GAAP adjusted operating income in Q4 2020.
- Sold a niche Tank Trailer business at the end of Q4 responsible for approximately $20 million during 2020, which manufactured aluminum tank trailers geared toward the Pacific Northwest.
- Final Mile Products (FMP) generated $52 million of revenue with an operating loss of $4.5 million in Q4 2020.
- FMP EBITDA was a loss of only $600,000 in Q4 2020.
- Expects FMP to begin showing positive EBITDA in the first half of 2021 and be solidly EBITDA profitable full year 2021.
- Generated $104 million of free cash flow during 2020.
- Closed the sale of its Columbus, Ohio branch location during 2020.
- Closed the sale of the Beall brand of Tank Trailers during 2020.
- Utilized $11.2 million to pay down debt, $8.7 million to repurchase shares, and invested $6.4 million in capital projects during Q4 2020.
- Expects 2021 capital spending to rebound between $35 million and $40 million.
- Long-term target is achieving a consolidated operating margin of 8% over the next two to three years.
- CTP expected to exit 2021 at near optimal levels of margin performance going into 2022, while FMP will continue stepping up margins in 2022 and 2023.
- Key executives on the call include Brent L. Yeagy (President and CEO), Mike Pettit (CFO), and Ryan Reed (Director of Investor Relations).
