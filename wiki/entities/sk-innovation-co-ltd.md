# SK Innovation Co., Ltd.

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Commerce, Georgia, Jackson County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

South Korean corporation SK Innovation is constructing a series of lithium-ion battery factories in Commerce, Georgia, totaling about 2 million square feet, to supply electric vehicle batteries for the U.S. market.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The Georgia factories are a major domestic source of EV battery cells, supporting U.S. automakers and reducing reliance on imported batteries.

## Key facts

- SK Innovation’s three planned facilities in Commerce will total about 2 million square feet of battery manufacturing space.
- The plant is intended to serve the Western Hemisphere, handling demand from Mexico, the Americas and Canada.
- SK Innovation plans to invest more than $2 billion in the Georgia factories; state and local tax incentives amount to at least $300 million.
- Senator Raphael Warnock warned that shutting down the plant would eliminate 2,600 clean-energy jobs short term and up to 10,000 long term.
- Governor Brian Kemp urged President Biden to overrule the ITC decision, calling the factories a "critical opportunity" for Georgia.
- The ITC ruling includes a carve-out allowing SK to continue supplying Ford and Volkswagen under existing contracts.
- Mayor Clark Hill highlighted community contributions from SK, including donations to local libraries and $400,000 for pandemic response.
- Analyst Sam Abuelsamid cautioned that overturning the ITC ruling could stifle U.S. battery innovation and slow EV adoption.
- The Georgia plant is expected to have a capacity to produce batteries for 300,000 electric vehicles annually after its second phase.
- SK Innovation plans to create 2,600 jobs in Georgia by 2024.
- If the ITC ban had stood, SK Innovation would have been barred from supplying batteries for the Ford F-150 Electric and Volkswagen’s Tennessee-built electric cars for several years.
- LG Chem is investing $4.5 billion in U.S. battery production over the next four years.
- The legal dispute centers on alleged misappropriation of trade secrets during hiring for factories in the United States and Hungary.
- Two factories on a common site in Commerce, Georgia will build batteries for 300,000 electric cars annually once full capacity is reached.
- SK Innovation plans to create 1,000 jobs by 2021 and over 2,600 jobs by 2024 at the Georgia site.
- The project would make Georgia one of the largest hubs of EV battery manufacturing in the world and is described as one of the largest economic projects in Georgia's history.
- Exterior construction of the first factory has been completed and groundbreaking for the second factory has occurred.
- Legal dispute with rival Korean battery maker LG Chem over alleged trade-secret violations is ongoing.
- Volkswagen will depend on the two factories for battery supply to its Tennessee assembly plant for the ID.4 crossover and another electric model starting in 2022.
- Ford indicated it would use SK Innovation battery cells from Georgia in its F-150 Electric pickup truck.
- Kia currently uses SK Innovation cells in models such as the Niro EV; these cells were at the center of the dispute with LG Chem.
- Hyundai, Kia's parent company, announced a partnership with SK Innovation to explore the entire battery ecosystem, including leasing models and second-life applications.
- SK Innovation plans to build a sprawling battery factory in northeast Georgia that could employ 2,000 workers.
- LG Chem has filed a federal lawsuit and trade complaint accusing SK Innovation of stealing its lithium-ion battery technology.
- The complaints request a Delaware judge and the U.S. International Trade Commission block SK Innovation from importing lithium-ion batteries and related manufacturing equipment into the United States.
- SK Innovation plans to invest 1.98 trillion South Korean won (US$1.01 billion) to establish an EV battery production base in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The plant will be built on a 278-acre site and is expected to have an annual production capacity of 9.8 GWh.
- Georgia will provide administrative support, including education, training, and employment programmes for the project.
- The investment aims to avoid a potential 25% US auto tariff threatened by the Trump White House.
- Construction is expected to start in early 2019 with mass supply anticipated from 2022.
- Governor Nathan Deal said the investment will create more than 2,000 jobs and highlights Georgia’s reputation as a top destination for international automotive-supply businesses.
- "SK Innovation has been actively seeking a production base to secure competitiveness and increase orders in the battery business, a major global market," said chief executive Kim Joon.
- The United States has fallen behind China and the EU in lithium-ion battery production, with projected 486 GWh/year capacity by 2030 versus 2,681 GWh/year for China.
- Benchmark Mineral Intelligence estimates that American plants could deliver 486 GWh/y by 2030, highlighting the scale of the challenge.
- "[US president Joe] Biden’s plan has certainly created very bullish sentiment for building a domestic lithium-ion economy in the US," said Simon Moores.
- Georgia employs about 130,000 people in the automotive sector, prompting state officials to seek involvement across the entire EV supply chain.
- "We’re probably going to see more change in this industry over the next 20 years than we’ve experienced in the previous 100 years," said Pat Wilson of Georgia’s Economic Development department.
- Battery cell unit costs have fallen 87 % over the past decade, while energy density has roughly doubled since 2014.
- Lithium raw material supply may become constrained by the end of the decade, with demand projected to outstrip supply by 60 % according to Benchmark Mineral Intelligence.
- GM is collaborating with Controlled Thermal Resources (CTR) to secure local low-cost lithium using a closed-loop extraction process that reduces CO₂ emissions.
- "The US is one of the biggest EV battery markets in terms of its size, with the potential that it may grow at the fastest rate among major markets," said an LG Energy Solution spokesperson.
- Ford’s Hau Thai-Tang noted that by 2025 Ford will have enough North American volume to justify building its own battery plant.
- The planned plant would have an initial annual capacity of 9.8 GWh, eventually ramping up to 105 GWh - five times Tesla’s Gigafactory 1 in Nevada.
- SK Innovation currently supplies batteries to global automakers Daimler and Hyundai Motor.
- Strategic location: nearby assembly plants for Hyundai, Mercedes-Benz, Honda (Alabama), Mazda, Toyota (Alabama) and Volkswagen (Tennessee).
- There is currently no mine-to-battery supply chain in North America; the plant will need a U.S. lithium source.
- Albemarle’s Silver Peak Mine in Nevada is the only U.S. lithium mine and could be a supplier for the Georgia plant.
- Cypress Development Corp holds a large lithium-claystone deposit that could become an alternative domestic supply for SK Innovation.
- The project would make Georgia home to the United States’ first EV battery cell manufacturing facility.
- Georgia has been recognized as the top state for businesses six times in a row, making it attractive for SK Innovation.
- The EV market is projected to have more than 7-8 million electric vehicles on U.S. roads within five years.
- SK Innovation highlighted the importance of a skilled workforce and a business-friendly environment in choosing Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp called the investment "historic" and said it will provide economic opportunity for thousands of Georgia families.
- The Sun Belt region, including South Carolina, Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia, hosts a high concentration of automakers such as Volkswagen, Daimler, Hyundai, Volvo, Kia, and BMW.
- SK Innovation demonstrated its commitment to the community by donating to the Empower College and Career Center.
