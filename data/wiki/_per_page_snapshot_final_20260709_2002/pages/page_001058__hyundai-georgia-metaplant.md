# Hyundai Georgia Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Responds To Massive ICE Raid On Georgia EV Metaplant (Updated)](https://insideevs.com/news/771406/hyundai-ice-raid-metaplant-statement/)  ·  `page_001058`

## Facts

- Hyundai's Savannah, Georgia Metaplant produces the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 electric SUVs.
- The facility was raided by law enforcement officers, including U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE).
- Approximately 475 people were detained during the raid, with most being South Korean nationals who were not legally in the country.
- Hyundai stated that the detainments will not impact vehicle production at the factory.
- The Metaplant spans 16 million square feet and represents the largest-ever Homeland Security enforcement operation at a single location.
- Detained workers appear to have been employed by LG Energy Solution, which co-owns the plant with Hyundai and operates a nearby battery facility, rather than directly by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai officials confirmed that none of the detained individuals are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai emphasized its commitment to full compliance with all laws, including employment verification requirements and immigration regulations, and expects the same from partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- A Hyundai spokesperson stated that vehicle assembly at the plant will not be affected by the arrests.
- The company is reviewing its processes to ensure all parties working on its projects maintain high standards of legal compliance, including thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- The raid occurs as the Trump administration increases immigration-related detentions, arrests, and deportations nationwide.
- A New York Times report indicated that attorneys for at least two detained suspects stated they were in the U.S. on a visa waiver program allowing stays of 90 days or less for business.
- Reports indicate Korean companies frequently bring their own technicians to set up new operations due to specialized training, but U.S. visa laws lack proper mechanisms to sponsor trained workers to run factories.
- ICE officials stated the raid was part of an active, ongoing criminal investigation and that arrested individuals were found working illegally in violation of visa terms or statuses.
- ICE noted that people on short-term or recreational visas are not authorized to work in the U.S., and a Mexican green card holder was arrested after being deemed removable due to multiple criminal convictions.
- The Metaplant is Hyundai's newest and most important factory, representing the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- The project plans to create 14,000 manufacturing jobs and has been praised by both Democrats and Republicans.
- Hyundai announced this year that the plant can also manufacture hybrid cars amid concerns over electric vehicle demand following the expiration of federal EV tax credits later this month.
- Hyundai officials stated that the safety and well-being of all workers at its sites is a top priority and highlighted the importance of robust oversight throughout its supply chain and contractor network.
- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution reported that subsidiaries of several Korean companies, including Kia and Hyundai, have faced lawsuits over immigration-related labor practices in recent years.
- Labor advocates note that manufacturing companies are increasingly using third-party labor contractors to insulate themselves from liability regarding immigration or labor law violations within their facilities.
- A Homeland Security Investigations official confirmed that various contractor and subcontractor companies are also under investigation.
- In 2020, SK Battery had several dozen Korean workers arrested over alleged visa issues.
