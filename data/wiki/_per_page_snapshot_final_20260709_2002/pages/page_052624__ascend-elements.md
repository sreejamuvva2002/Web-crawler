# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-20

**Source:** [Ground broken, potential praised for $1B EV battery plant in Hopkinsville](https://hoptownchronicle.org/ground-broken-potential-praised-for-1b-ascend-elements-plant-in-hopkinsville/)  ·  `page_052624`

## Facts

- Local and state economic development officials gathered on a Thursday in a former cornfield for the groundbreaking ceremony of the $1 billion Ascend Elements facility.
- The plant will produce lithium-ion materials for electric vehicle batteries at Commerce Park II, which features CSX rail access on John Rivers Road.
- Gov. Andy Beshear stated the facility will be Ascend Element’s largest U.S. facility and the largest economic development project in Christian County and Western Kentucky.
- The privately held Massachusetts company initially announced a $310 million project that would employ 250 workers, with future expansion potentially reaching a $1 billion investment and 400 or more employees.
- On Wednesday prior to the ceremony, the U.S. Department of Energy approved a $480 million grant for the Hopkinsville facility, elevating the project's total planned investment to $1 billion.
- CEO Michael O’Kronley stated the federal grant is part of an initiative under President Biden’s leadership funded through the $1.2 trillion Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act to incentivize domestic production of critical battery materials and reduce U.S. reliance on foreign-made batteries, most of which currently come from China.
- The company utilizes a patented process called Hyrdo-to-Cathode that relies on recycled lithium-ion battery materials.
- The 450,000-square-foot facility will be named Apex 1 and will produce enough material for up to 250,000 batteries a year.
- Hopkinsville was selected from six site finalists in the eastern U.S., with access to CSX rail cited as a key advantage.
- O’Kronley credited Mayor Wendell Lynch, Judge-Executive Steve Tribble, and Carter Hendricks (executive director of the South Western Kentucky Economic Development Council) for their recruitment efforts after about a year of working with local and state officials.
- Ascend Elements will partner with the local school system and Hopkinsville Community College to train potential employees.
- The company estimates the region will experience $4.4 billion in economic impact from the plant's development.
- John Crenshaw, chairman of the Hopkinsville Industrial Foundation, highlighted workforce benefits and thanked the federal government for its greener planet policy goal, expressing hope that the project will be environmentally beneficial.
- The industrial foundation secured land options from the Garnett farm family to develop Commerce Park II.
- The site was previously considered in October 2021 for a 500,000-square-foot beef slaughter facility by Wisconsin-based American Foods Group, which withdrew after local opposition over environmental concerns.
- Beshear asserted that the Ascend Elements plant will not generate any toxic waste.
- Approximately 20 people, including Beshear and O’Kronley, participated in the groundbreaking by tossing dirt into the air to mark the start of Apex 1.
- Ascend Elements has projected the plant will be in operation in 2024.
