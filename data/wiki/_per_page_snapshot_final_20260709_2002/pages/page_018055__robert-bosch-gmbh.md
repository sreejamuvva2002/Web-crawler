# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-17

**Source:** [Bosch auto supplier in Germany: No end in sight to job losses - World Socialist Web Site](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2025/02/17/70fe-f17.html)  ·  `page_018055`

## Facts

- The auto division of Robert Bosch GmbH is the world’s largest auto supplier.
- The company also manufactures power tools, household appliances and products and components for energy and building technology.
- Following massive job cuts and various other cost-cutting programmes over the past two years, the Bosch company employed just under 420,000 workers in 60 countries worldwide at the end of 2024.
- Last year alone, 11,500 employees lost their jobs worldwide.
- This included 4,400 of the company’s 129,000-strong workforce in Germany.
- The preliminary business result for 2024 suggests sales of 90.5 billion euros.
- This corresponds to a maximum profit of 3.5 billion euros and a return of 3.5 percent.
- In 2023, Bosch profits stood at 4.6 billion euros.
- The return in 2023 was 5 percent.
- Bosch executive Stefan Hartung expects 2025 to be a challenging year.
- Hartung announced “painful decisions” for the workforce but not for company owners.
- The company plans to at least double its profits from 2024 by 2026, according to Hartung.
- Hartung stated there will be further savings programmes because of another major shift in the auto manufacturing complex by 2030.
- This structural change is expected to continue over the next five or six years.
- Hartung refused to disclose the number of redundancies the company is planning.
- In March 2024, Hartung announced the wiping out of 3,500 jobs in Germany.
- He stated there would be no end to the current attacks on jobs and wages.
- At the end of 2024, Bosch announced plans to cut a further 12,230 jobs worldwide by 2026.
- This includes 7,000 cuts at its German plants.
- Hartung emphasized that job cuts could only be achieved in close cooperation with the IG Metall union.
- Bosch intends to follow a similar path to Volkswagen regarding workforce reductions.
- At VW, IG Metall’s alliance with the company is leading to the destruction of at least 35,000 jobs.
- This includes a reduction in real wages of up to 20 percent.
- It will also lead to plant closures sooner or later.
- Bosch is cutting wages and salaries as well as jobs.
- The working hours of more than 10,000 employees are being forcibly reduced to 35 hours.
- These reductions are without wage compensation.
- Most of those affected currently work 38 to 40 hours a week.
- They may lose up to 15 percent of their gross salary due to the reduction.
- Bosch Mobility works council chairman Frank Sell is also deputy chairman of the Supervisory Board of Robert Bosch GmbH.
- Sell complained that the works council was “only aware of the planned course of action in advance in hints and has now been presented with a fait accompli.”
- Less than two years ago, in July 2023, Sell declared employees at Mobility sites could look forward to job security until 2027.
- Sell stated works councils will be involved in strategic and economic planning at an early stage regarding future products and target plans beyond 2027.
- Bosch was founded by Robert Bosch in 1886.
- In 1913, the Bosch plant in Stuttgart was temporarily closed due to a strike for better wages and working conditions.
- Hartung instructed works councils in each individual division to negotiate separately with board members and managers.
- He also instructed works councils at each plant to negotiate separately.
- This is intended to prevent a joint response from approximately 130,000 workers in Germany and almost 420,000 workers worldwide.
- IG Metall is postponing its nationwide day of protest until 15 March.
- Demonstrations for the protest have been registered in Hanover, Stuttgart, Cologne, Frankfurt and Leipzig.
- The postponement occurs after the federal election.
