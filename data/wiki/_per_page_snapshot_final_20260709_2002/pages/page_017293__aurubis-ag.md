## Facts

- Aurubis AG is Europe's largest copper producer.
- Aurubis is making a transformative $800 million investment to establish a state-of-the-art copper recycling facility in Richmond, Georgia.
- The facility is scheduled to commence operations in 2025 after a four-year construction period.
- This will be the first large-scale copper recycling plant in the region.
- The facility has an annual processing capacity of 180,000 metric tons of complex copper scrap.
- The facility will produce 70,000 tons of refined copper annually.
- The U.S. exported 960,000 tons of copper scrap in 2024.
- 41% of the 2024 U.S. copper scrap exports were shipped to China.
- Aurubis is positioning itself to capitalize on demand drivers like data center expansion and AI infrastructure, which require rapid copper component turnover every 3–5 years.
- Primary mining accounts for approximately 60% of global copper production.
- Georgia's location taps into the southeastern U.S.'s robust automotive and telecommunications sectors, which generate substantial copper scrap volumes.
- CEO Toralf Haag emphasized the facility's role in closing the loop for North American manufacturers.
- The facility enables manufacturers to reintegrate recycled copper into production cycles without dependency on international exporters.
- The plant processes complex copper scrap, including mixed-material waste from electronics and industrial equipment that traditional recyclers often deem uneconomical.
- Aurubis can recover copper with 99.9% purity, matching the quality of newly mined metal.
- The facility simultaneously extracts precious metals like gold and silver from byproducts.
- With a 70,000-ton annual output, the facility could meet nearly 5% of U.S. refined copper demand.
- This production capacity could reduce import reliance by an estimated 15% over the next decade.
- Over 80% of U.S. copper scrap is historically processed by small-scale regional operators lacking the capacity to handle complex materials.
- Aurubis introduces vertically integrated operations that streamline collection, sorting, and refining.
