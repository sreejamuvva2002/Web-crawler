# Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Chron - Gov. Kemp: Seohan Auto Georgia Announces 180 New Jobs in Liberty County - 10222875](https://georgiachron.com/ganews/gov-kemp-seohan-auto-georgia-announces-180-new-jobs-in-liberty-county-georgiachron-10222875)  ·  `page_059394`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation, a Tier-1 automotive parts supplier for Hyundai Motor Group America, will be investing more than $72 million in a new manufacturing facility in Liberty County.
- The investment is expected to create at least 180 new jobs for the region.
- Governor Kemp stated that the Hyundai Metaplant is and will continue to be an economic driver for the entire region, creating more jobs for hardworking Georgians in communities like Liberty County.
- Governor Kemp added that they are proud to welcome Seohan as the latest supplier in a growing list to invest in Georgia and look forward to the continued growth of this fast emerging sector.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation is based in South Korea and has production facilities around the world, including multiple facilities in the United States.
- Jung Kee Koo, CEO of Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation stated that sustained growth of the EV market over the past few years suggests accelerated changes to the automotive market are inevitable.
- Jung Kee Koo also stated that they believe Georgia will be the center of the EV industry and a new frontier for Seohan's future with limitless opportunities and potential.
- Jung Kee Koo expressed confidence that their experience and technological capabilities would contribute to the prosperity of the local community.
- The new facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2024.
- The facility will be located at Dunwoody Court Site in Tradeport East Business Center, a GRAD Certified site.
- The facility will produce H/shafts, axles, and brake systems for OEMs including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Allen Brown, Chairman of Liberty County Development Authority commented on negotiations with Seohan Auto Georgia, describing them as responsible, thorough, and progressive organizations that are striving to advance innovative technology.
- GDEcD Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented GDEcD's Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project involved collaboration alongside the Liberty County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson commented that the ongoing transition to e-mobility and sustainable technology creates a window of opportunity for companies and states that remain on the forefront.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility earlier this year in Bryan County.
- The Hyundai Metaplant project was predicted to bring approximately $1 billion worth of investments from offsite suppliers around the state.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion worth of investments as well as 27,800 jobs across Georgia.
