# Suzuki Motor Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2018-06-26

**Source:** [Suzuki Nears Completion of New Motorcycle Manufacturing Plant in Japan - Cycle News](https://www.cyclenews.com/2018/06/article/suzuki-nears-completion-of-new-motorcycle-manufacturing-plant-in-japan/)  ·  `page_010939`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor of America, Inc. (SMAI) announced the creation of a new manufacturing plant in Hamamatsu, Japan, home to its parent company, Suzuki Motor Corp.
- The new facility combines engineering, development, engine production, and vehicle assembly into one location to increase efficiency in motorcycle production and delivery.
- The five-year consolidation plan for the new facility was originally announced in 2014 and is near completion as of June 2018.
- Previously, Suzuki motorcycles were developed and manufactured across three separate locations in Japan: product engineering and development at the Ryuyo facility, engine production at the Takatsuka plant, and motorcycle assembly at the Toyokawa plant.
- The consolidated operations are located in the new Hamamatsu Plant in the Miyakoda district.
- The new facility occupies a nearly 700,000 square foot building on a site covering more than 40 acres.
- The facility will produce the majority of Suzuki two-wheeled products for U.S. distribution.
- The site location was chosen in part due to its geographically desirable location and proximity to Suzuki headquarters.
- Kerry Graeber, Vice President MC/ATV Sales and Marketing for SMAI, stated that the consolidation underlines Suzuki’s commitment to engineering and manufacturing motorcycles at the forefront of performance and innovation.
- Suzuki operates an All-Terrain Vehicle assembly plant in Rome, Georgia.
- The Rome, Georgia facility has been active since 2001.
- Suzuki Manufacturing of America (SMAC) exclusively builds utility-focused ATVs for worldwide distribution at the Rome, Georgia site.
- Suzuki launched all-new versions of its KingQuad 750 and KingQuad 500 in May.
