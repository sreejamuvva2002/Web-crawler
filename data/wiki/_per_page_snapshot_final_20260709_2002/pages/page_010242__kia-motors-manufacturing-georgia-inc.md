# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2012-02-29

**Source:** [KIA MOTORS MANUFACTURING GEORGIA PRODUCES 500,000TH VEHICLE](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/3939/kia-motors-manufacturing-georgia-produces-500000th-vehicle)  ·  `page_010242`

## Facts

- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG) celebrated production of its 500,000th vehicle on Feb. 29, 2012, at its automobile manufacturing facility in West Point, Ga.
- KMMG began mass production of the Kia Sorento CUV on Nov. 16, 2009.
- The Kia Sorento CUV quickly became the brand's best-selling product in the U.S.
- KMMG began production of the Kia Optima mid-size sedan on Sept. 2, 2011.
- The Kia Optima mid-size sedan led the brand's U.S. sales in December 2011 and January.
- Since opening, KMMG has added two additional shifts of production.
- The plant has hired more than 3,000 team members to date.
- In 2011, KMMG completed a $100 million expansion.
- The expansion pushed the plant's total capacity to 360,000 units per year.
- Byung Mo Ahn, Group President and CEO for Kia Motors America and KMMG, stated that building 500,000 vehicles in just over two years is a milestone for their first U.S. manufacturing plant.
- KMMG achieved 17 consecutive months of record sales.
- Vehicle number 500,000 produced was a snow white pearl Optima SX.
- The vehicle will be allocated to one of Kia Motors America's (KMA) more than 755 dealerships nationwide.
- KMMG is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Motors Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- KMMG is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- KMMG produces the 2012 Sorento CUV and the 2012 Optima mid-size sedan, two of the brand's top-selling models in 2011.
- Beginning in 2012, the plant has the ability to build 360,000 vehicles annually from U.S. and globally-sourced parts.
