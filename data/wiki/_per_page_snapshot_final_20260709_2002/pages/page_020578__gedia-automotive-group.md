# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor Kemp visits GEDIA in Attendorn: A sign of successful transatlantic cooperation](https://www.gedia.com/en-us/news/news-detail/governor-kemp-visits-gedia-in-attendorn-a-sign-of-successful-transatlantic-cooperation)  ·  `page_020578`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp of Georgia visited the GEDIA Automotive Group in Attendorn, Germany, last Friday with a high-ranking delegation.
- The visiting delegation was led by Commissioner Pat Wilson and included representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The visit underscored the close and successful partnership between GEDIA and the U.S. state of Georgia.
- GEDIA is a leading automotive supplier with approximately 4,700 employees worldwide.
- GEDIA has operated a state-of-the-art thermoforming plant in Dalton, Georgia since 2021.
- GEDIA employs approximately 700 people across North America.
- Approximately 65 of GEDIA's North American employees work at the Dalton site.
- GEDIA maintained close cooperation with local Georgia government officials even before construction began on its Dalton plant, which was a key factor in the site's smooth development.
- The purpose of the Attendorn visit was to further strengthen relations and emphasize the importance of transatlantic cooperation.
- The delegation received a comprehensive tour of GEDIA's Attendorn thermoforming plant, which is comparable to the Dalton site in many respects.
- Plant employees conducted the tour and explained individual processes to the delegation.
- Governor Kemp asked specific questions about the manufacturing processes and gained insight into practical workflows alongside the employees.
- Construction of the Dalton plant began during the Covid-19 pandemic, facing uncertainties, material shortages, and travel restrictions.
- GEDIA overcame a major cyber attack shortly before completing the Dalton plant construction.
- Despite these challenges, GEDIA completed the Dalton plant on schedule without delays.
- The successful completion illustrates the company's determination and professionalism in achieving excellence under adverse circumstances.
- Helmut Hinkel, CEO of the GEDIA Group, stated: “The political environment is changing rapidly, and to remain successful, we need to orient ourselves well and strengthen the right networks.”
- The visit concluded with an evening reception at Schnellenberg Castle where participants discussed the future development of GEDIA in the USA.
- Governor Kemp stated: “The relationship between GEDIA and Georgia is a prime example of a successful international partnership.”
- Helmut Hinkel stated: “We are proud of the trust and support we receive from the Georgia government. This is the basis for our success and future growth.”
- GEDIA has the opportunity to triple production capacity in Dalton on its already developed site.
- GEDIA expressed optimism about its future following discussions that highlighted Georgia's commitment and business-friendly environment.
- Governor Kemp emphasized his openness and determination to attract new investors to Georgia and actively support companies.
- GEDIA praised the comprehensive support received from local government throughout the entire investment and development phase.
- The visit underscored Georgia's position as a preferred location for international companies due to its clear focus on economic development, growth, and innovation.
- Both GEDIA and Governor Kemp expressed confidence in a long-term and successful cooperation.
