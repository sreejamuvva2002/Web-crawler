# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-11

**Source:** [Novelis to build $365 million automotive recycling center](https://www.recyclingproductnews.com/article/37956/novelis-to-build-dollar365-million-recycling-centre-to-help-reduce-carbon-emissions-by-one-million-tons-per-year)  ·  `page_047406`

## Facts

- Novelis will invest $365 million to build a highly advanced recycling centre for automotive in North America.
- The facility will have an annual casting capacity of 240 kt of sheet ingot.
- The company expects the facility to reduce its carbon emissions by more than one million tons each year.
- The new recycling facility will be built adjacent to Novelis' existing automotive finishing plant in Guthrie, Kentucky.
- The centre will enable Novelis to grow its closed-loop-recycling programs with more automotive customers in North America.
- Through closed-loop recycling, the company takes back aluminum remaining after automotive parts are stamped from sheets and remakes it into the same product for new vehicle production.
- The centre will also have the capability to process aluminum from vehicles at the end of their lifecycle.
- Using recycled aluminum as input material requires only 5 percent of the energy used to make primary aluminum.
- This process avoids 95 percent of the carbon emissions associated with production.
- The new recycling centre will be equipped with advanced shredding and sorting technology.
- The facility will include energy-efficient innovations to support Novelis' sustainability goal to reduce energy intensity by 10 percent by 2026.
- Groundbreaking for the recycling centre is scheduled for early 2022.
- Commissioning of the facility is expected in 2024.
- When fully operational, the expansion will add approximately 140 new jobs in Guthrie.
- Novelis held the grand opening of its automotive finishing plant in Guthrie in October.
- The existing automotive finishing plant currently employs 150 people.
- Employment at the existing automotive finishing plant is expected to grow to 190 over the next two years.
- Tom Boney, Executive Vice President and President of Novelis North America, stated that the investment will help Novelis achieve its sustainability goals and support the carbon-reduction targets of automotive customers.
- Tom Boney noted that the Commonwealth of Kentucky has been a great business partner with a strong, highly skilled workforce to meet the growing need for high-strength, low-carbon aluminum.
