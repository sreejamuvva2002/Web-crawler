# UCB, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [UCB TO INVEST $2 BILLION IN GEORGIA, ESTABLISH FIRST U.S. MA](https://bxjmag.com/ucb-to-invest-2-billion-in-georgia-establish-first-u-s-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_055732`

## Facts

- Global biopharmaceutical giant UCB, Inc. is planning a significant investment of $2 billion in Georgia to establish its first U.S. pharmaceutical biologics manufacturing facility.
- The investment will generate 330 new jobs over the next several years at the Rowen Foundation’s state-of-the-art, 2,000-acre science and learning campus in Gwinnett County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment plan for Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that discussions with UCB leadership in Belgium highlighted the Peach State as the right partner for their visionary U.S. plans benefiting patients and Georgians.
- Governor Kemp described the announcement as a significant milestone for the life sciences industry, one of the largest investments in state history, and a move establishing both the Rowen facility and Georgia as a true hub of innovation.
- UCB is a global biopharmaceutical company based in Belgium.
- UCB’s North American headquarters are located in Smyrna and currently support more than 400 jobs.
- The company’s expertise spans neurology and immunology.
- UCB CEO Jean-Christophe Tellier stated the decision reflects confidence in long-term growth and a deep-rooted commitment to the United States.
- Tellier noted that by investing in Georgia, where UCB's U.S. headquarters have been based for more than three decades, the company is strengthening biologics manufacturing capabilities and supporting its innovation pipeline.
- Tellier highlighted that Georgia offers outstanding talent, a strong manufacturing tradition, and an ecosystem designed for sustainable, long-term success.
- The project is expected to generate approximately $5 billion in total economic impact, reflecting broader value for the region and communities.
- UCB’s new manufacturing footprint will be located at Rowen, serving as an anchor tenant for a 2,000-acre planned community in metro Atlanta designed to foster collaboration, knowledge sharing, and innovation.
- The cutting-edge campus will use a digital-first approach by leveraging AI, robotics, and automation while prioritizing efficiency in the use of natural resources.
