# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-22

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Applauds US Investments in Energy](https://cleantechnica.com/2022/08/22/stryten-energy-applauds-investments-in-energy-security-u-s-manufacturers-with-passage-of-inflation-reduction-act/)  ·  `page_050455`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company provides advanced vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) technology that delivers medium and long-duration energy storage (LDES).
- VRFB technology helps ensure grid stability and facilitates increased utilization of solar and wind for businesses and consumers across the U.S.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) includes vital Investment Tax Credits (ITC) for domestically sourced and manufactured standalone clean energy storage intended to lower costs, increase energy efficiency, and strengthen energy security.
- Tim Vargo, Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, welcomed the legislation’s long-term, standalone energy storage investment tax credits and its ten-year runway.
- Vargo stated that these credits will help customers incorporate medium and long-duration storage such as VRFB batteries into their operations more economically than before.
- Vargo noted that leveraging domestic VFRB technology and other long-term energy storage solutions will enable reliable access to clean power and help the U.S. achieve energy security as it transitions to a clean energy economy.
- Scott Childers, Vice President of Stryten Energy’s Essential Power Division, stated that Stryten’s patented flow battery technology is uniquely positioned to provide safe, long-term, reliable on-demand energy needed to build a new economy powered by clean energy.
- Childers added that the Inflation Reduction Act is expected to accelerate demand for domestically sourced energy storage, especially for commercial applications such as charging stations, manufacturing plants, and businesses with 24/7 energy requirements.
- Stryten’s VRFB technology combines multiple innovations including domestically sourced vanadium electrolyte that is easily recycled.
- The VRFB technology features increased power density and reduced battery downtime.
- The technology offers the potential of limitless cycle life with proper maintenance.
- The batteries have high-temperature ratings and are thermally safe.
- The system is scalable to meet varying energy storage capacity needs.
- Stryten’s VRFB technology is ideal for applications that require more than four hours of storage capacity to deploy clean energy on demand from renewable sources such as solar and wind energy.
- Target applications for the VRFB technology include utility-scale storage, microgrids, and military bases.
- Stryten Energy provides energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- The company partners with some of the world’s most recognized companies to meet the growing demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
- Stryten powers applications ranging from submarines to subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- The company's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Stryten is an industry leader backed by more than a century of expertise.
