## Facts

- California-based automaker Rivian announced Thursday it amended its multibillion-dollar federal construction loan, a deal that will reduce its borrowing by $2 billion.
- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Dominance Financing agreed to reduce a previously awarded $6.6 billion loan to $4.5 billion.
- The amended loan allows for the first phase of Rivian’s future plant an hour east of Atlanta to have an annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles, a 50% increase from prior plans.
- CEO RJ Scaringe said in a Georgia press release: "We're committed to working with the U.S. government."
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated: "I want to make this truck as appealing as possible to customers of several critical drivers of global sales profit."
- The EV maker says the change will allow the company to expand the scope of its Georgia factory’s first phase.
- Rivian announced the amended loan agreement via a news release.
