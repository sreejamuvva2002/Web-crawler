# LG Energy Solution

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-29

**Source:** [Exclusive: LG Energy Solution Resumes Business Trips to Georgia](https://www.chosun.com/english/industry-en/2025/10/29/JGSFVAVFSJGCFG26XACLXAHQK4/)  ·  `page_056214`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution (LGES) has resumed business trips to the U.S. state of Georgia for the first time since the "Georgia detention incident."
- This comes over 50 days after 317 South Korean workers were arrested and detained by U.S. immigration authorities at a local factory on September 4.
- According to industry sources on the 28th, LGES dispatched around 30 personnel to the site of its battery joint venture factory with Hyundai Motor.
- Most of the team consisted of subcontractor employees, with only a few from LG headquarters.
- While LGES has continued sending personnel to the U.S. post-incident, this marks the first time it has sent workers to the Georgia site.
- The resumption follows efforts by U.S. officials, including the Deputy Secretary of State, to express regret over the incident.
- Georgia’s governor pledged to resolve visa issues during a recent visit to South Korea.
- President Donald Trump addressed the detention incident on the 27th while en route to Tokyo, Japan, from Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, where the ASEAN Summit was held.
- He stated, "I opposed sending them [the South Korean workers] back."
- He emphasized, "We want them [South Korea] to bring in experts, and they will come and teach our people."
- He also highlighted preparations for a new visa system to facilitate the entry of skilled workers into the U.S.
- The personnel traveling to the Georgia factory this time are reported to hold B-1 visas, not ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization).
- Workers previously detained and since reinstated to frontline roles were excluded from the trip, reportedly due to considerations for their psychological stability and health.
