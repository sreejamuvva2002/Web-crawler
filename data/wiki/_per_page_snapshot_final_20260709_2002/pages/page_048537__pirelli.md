# Pirelli

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-18

**Source:** [Where Are Pirelli Tires Made? - All American Made](https://www.allamericanmade.com/where-are-pirelli-tires-made/)  ·  `page_048537`

## Facts

- Pirelli’s only U.S. manufacturing plant is located in Rome, Georgia.
- The Rome, Georgia facility produces high-performance P Zero and Scorpion tires for the premium segment.
- The Rome, Georgia plant employs approximately 1,000 workers.
- The Rome, Georgia plant initially employed over 250 workers when it opened on August 17, 2002.
- The choice of Rome, Georgia was driven by the availability of skilled labor and flight connections to Atlanta rather than Italian heritage.
- Pirelli has acknowledged the coincidence of the city’s name matching its own.
- The Rome plant is designated one of Pirelli’s “Cyber” factories, utilizing Industry 4.0 automation and data analytics throughout production.
- The Rome, Georgia plant produces approximately 400,000 tires per year, representing about one percent of Pirelli’s total annual output.
- Pirelli’s U.S. production is concentrated in the SUV, truck, and ultra-high-performance segments.
- Pirelli ranks as the fifth-largest tire manufacturer in the world by revenue.
- Pirelli has a presence in over 160 countries and operates over 21 plants globally.
- Pirelli is Italian in origin but majority-owned by ChemChina, a Chinese state enterprise that acquired a controlling stake starting in 2015 (initially 26.2%, later over 50.1%).
- ChemChina reduced its position to approximately 45 to 46.7 percent to signal a market-friendly posture in Europe.
- Pirelli’s premium tire division remains listed on the Milan stock exchange, with ChemChina holding approximately 37 percent of shares.
- Chinese ownership had little operational impact on day-to-day management, and the Rome, Georgia plant is not Chinese-operated.
- Pirelli serves as the exclusive tire supplier for Formula One racing since 2011 and has sponsored races since 1907.
- Pirelli tires are OEM fitments for Aston Martin, Porsche, Bentley, Ferrari, Lamborghini, Maserati, Pagani, and McLaren, with 100 percent supply agreements on several marques.
- Every tire sold in the United States carries a DOT code on the sidewall where the first two characters after “DOT” identify the manufacturing plant.
- Pirelli tires made at the Rome, Georgia plant carry the same quality standards as those made in Europe.
- Competitors with U.S. manufacturing include Goodyear (seven U.S. facilities), Cooper Tire (Findlay, Ohio and Texarkana, Arkansas), and Michelin (South Carolina, North Carolina, and Alabama).
- Pirelli produces roughly 500,000 tires per month in Mexico alone, far exceeding its U.S. output.
- Pirelli has had a production facility in China since 2005, with a second factory opening in November 2007, producing roughly 700,000 tires per year there.
- Before 2022, Pirelli operated two manufacturing plants in Russia (Voronezh and Kirov) employing approximately 3,200 workers combined, with $224 million invested between 2012 and 2015.
