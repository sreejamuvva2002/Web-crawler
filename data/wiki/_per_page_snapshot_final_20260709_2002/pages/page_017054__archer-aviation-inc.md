## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. will report first-quarter results on Monday, May 11, 2026, with a webcast kicking off at 2:00 p.m. Pacific time.
- Archer shares traded at $6.57, a 9-cent gain, with volume topping 30 million shares.
- The company’s market cap is near $4.33 billion.
- Investors are focusing on certification milestones and cash burn rather than immediate revenue.
- The UAE’s General Civil Aviation Authority advanced Archer’s Midnight aircraft into the Restricted Type Certificate program.
- The Midnight is an eVTOL (electric vertical takeoff and landing) aircraft that rises like a helicopter, then shifts to fixed-wing flight.
- A restricted certificate grants limited operational approval ahead of full commercial sign-off.
- CEO Adam Goldstein described the UAE decision as “a major step” for electric air taxis in the country.
- Eng. Aqeel Al Zarooni, assistant director general for aviation safety affairs at the GCAA, said regulators are focused on safely bringing new aviation technology into UAE airspace.
- Archer partnered with Abu Dhabi Aviation to lay out plans for Midnight’s entry into service in Abu Dhabi.
- The partnership scope stretches across certification, operations, maintenance, pilot training, airspace management, vertiport setup, security, and oversight.
- In March, the Federal Aviation Administration signed off on all of Midnight’s Means of Compliance.
- With this milestone, Archer says it can now wrap up its last certification plans and push ahead toward Type Inspection Authorization for official testing linked to certification.
- Executives have repeatedly flagged 2026 as the year the Midnight aircraft could edge into passenger service in the U.S. and UAE.
- The U.S. Department of Transportation and FAA picked eight projects for the eVTOL Integration Pilot Program in March.
- Archer, Joby Aviation, and BETA Technologies were named among those involved in multiple efforts across New York/New Jersey, Texas, and Florida.
- FAA Deputy Administrator Chris Rocheleau expects "valuable operational experience" from the program, with an eye on safe advanced air mobility.
- Archer remains grouped with Joby and BETA under regulators, despite differences in their aircraft and business strategy.
- Shares of the publicly traded eVTOL firms moved higher after the pilot program announcement.
- Archer wrapped up 2025 holding roughly $1.96 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments.
- Archer logged a net loss of $618.2 million for 2025.
- Archer flagged an adjusted EBITDA loss for the first quarter in the range of $160 million to $180 million.
- MarketBeat put Wall Street’s first-quarter estimates at a 25-cent loss per share on $1.66 million in revenue.
- Archer has flagged that timing for its certification, U.S. and UAE rollouts, and first passenger flights remain forward-looking and might not play out as planned.
