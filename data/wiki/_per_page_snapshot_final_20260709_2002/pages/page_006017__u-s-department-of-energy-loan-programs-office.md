# U.S. Department of Energy Loan Programs Office

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-05

**Source:** [Sector Spotlight: Advanced Vehicle Components | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/articles/sector-spotlight-advanced-vehicle-components)  ·  `page_006017`

## Facts

- The DOE Loan Programs Office (LPO) is supporting U.S. advanced vehicle component projects.
- Automakers’ commitment to increasing average efficiency has led to noticeable year-over-year declines in CO2e emissions from on-road transportation over the past two decades.
- Nascent growth in light-duty market share for electric vehicles began leading improvements in average vehicle efficiency as incremental internal combustion engine improvements slowed.
- Advanced component innovations now drive increases in average efficiency, often offering enhanced vehicle performance with less mass or material.
- Efficiency improvements could reduce EV’s average electricity consumption per mile by half over the next 30 years.
- The basic EV powertrain includes the battery pack, traction motor, full-vehicle wiring, power electronics, and thermal management systems.
- These components contain subcomponents that rely on superior electrical properties of constituent materials processed from raw materials identified as critical by the Biden-Harris Administration.
- Novel manufacturing techniques enhance mechanical properties for select subcomponents like battery separators or assembled cells.
- The private sector is developing capacity for these components and the rest of the battery supply chain in the United States, per MESC’s September 2024 grant announcements.
- Power electronic innovations are particularly impactful for EVs, which are estimated to contain twice as many semiconductors by weight as conventional vehicles.
- Clean fuel alternatives like hydrogen, biomass-derived fuels, and sustainable petrochemical replacements will be necessary to decarbonize medium- and heavy-duty vehicles and non-road sectors.
- Inflation Reduction Act tax credits are incentivizing advanced automotive manufacturing and clean fuel production to onshore to the United States.
- Domestic content bonuses across many of these credits make onshoring similarly irresistible for advanced component manufacturers.
- LPO’s Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) Loan Program is the most popular program for manufacturers of advanced vehicle components.
- ATVM can support manufacturing facilities for qualifying components for on-road light-duty, medium-duty, and heavy-duty vehicles that meet specific emission reduction standards.
- ATVM also supports non-road transportation like trains, maritime vessels, aircraft, and hyperloop technology that meet zero or low emission thresholds.
- The Title 17 Clean Energy Finance Program may support advanced vehicle component projects but has additional requirements and higher costs compared to ATVM.
- Applicants typically choose other programs over ATVM when expecting project output will be used primarily in non-vehicle applications.
- As of the end of June 2024, financing requested from LPO for Advanced Vehicle and Component projects totaled $16.8 billion.
- In October 2024, LPO announced a conditional commitment to Aspen Aerogels Georgia, LLC to help finance construction of a facility in Register, Georgia to manufacture PyroThin® for use in EV batteries.
- PyroThin® aerogel thermal barriers are ultra-thin and lightweight, designed to slow or prevent rare events where a battery cell overheats and triggers neighboring cells.
- Aspen currently fills orders for contracted offtakers at its existing manufacturing plant in Rhode Island and plans to build new capacity as demand increases.
- PyroThin® offers a unique combination of thermal management, mechanical performance, and fire protection properties to help EV battery manufacturers reach safety goals without sacrificing performance.
- In the event a battery cell fails, PyroThin® protects neighboring cells from being thermally triggered to avoid significant fires.
- Demand for aerogel thermal barriers is expected to increase significantly in the United States and Europe as more stringent thermal mitigation and prevention standards are implemented.
- In April 2024, LPO closed a $362 million loan to CelLink Corporation to help finance construction of a domestic manufacturing facility in Georgetown, Texas.
- The CelLink facility will produce flexible circuit wiring harnesses that relay information and carry electricity throughout vehicles.
- Once fully operational, the CelLink facility is expected to produce enough wiring harnesses to support the manufacture of approximately 2.7 million EVs per year.
- At its existing California facility, CelLink has produced wiring harnesses for more than a million vehicles already on the road.
- CelLink’s domestically produced “flex harnesses” are lighter, smaller, quicker to produce, and expected to be less expensive than conventional wiring harnesses.
- The flat design of CelLink’s flex harnesses can reduce vehicle weight, provide better heat dissipation for improved propulsion system efficiency, and lower vehicle manufacturing costs.
- CelLink’s technology has applications across numerous industries including aviation and aerospace, where most wire harness production for the U.S. market currently occurs in countries with low labor costs due to complex manual processes.
- In November 2024, LPO announced the closing of a $1.2 billion loan to ENTEK Lithium Separators LLC.
- The ENTEK loan will substantially finance a new facility in Terre Haute, Indiana to manufacture lithium-ion battery separators, the membrane sandwiched between the anode and cathode of a battery.
- The separators will be used primarily in EVs, strengthening the U.S. lithium-ion battery cells supply chain.
- The project will make a significant contribution to growth in domestic battery separator capacity and help U.S. EV manufacturers satisfy battery component sourcing requirements under the 30D Clean Vehicle Credit.
- ENTEK will be able to customize battery separators to accommodate numerous EV battery designs as well as all existing lithium-ion EV battery chemistries.
- In October 2024, LPO announced the closing of a $544 million loan to SK Siltron CSS, LLC to expand American manufacturing of high-quality silicon carbide (SiC) wafers for EV power electronics in Bay City, Michigan.
- The SK Siltron facility is projected to be among the top-five manufacturers of SiC wafers globally.
- SiC semiconductors are designed for high-voltage use and are critical components of EV drivetrains, inverters, and electrical distribution systems like onboard chargers and DC-to-DC converters.
- SiC semiconductors allow for higher efficiency and voltage, which can mean faster charging times and up to 10% longer range compared with traditional silicon semiconductors.
- High-quality SiC wafers are currently under-supplied, and demand is expected to rise with EV sales which saw unprecedented progress in 2023.
- In December 2024, LPO announced the closing of a $9.63 billion loan to Blue Oval SK LLC to construct three manufacturing plants—one located in Tennessee and two in Kentucky.
- Blue Oval SK is a joint venture between Ford Motor Company and SK On, a global leading Korean EV battery manufacturer.
- The Blue Oval SK project will help build a more resilient domestic supply chain to meet strong growing consumer demand for EVs.
- The project will enable more than 120 gigawatt hours of U.S. battery production annually and displace more than 455 million gallons of gasoline per year for the lifetime of the vehicles powered by these batteries.
- In December 2024, LPO announced the closing of a $7.54 billion loan to StarPlus Energy LLC.
- The StarPlus loan will help finance up to two lithium-ion battery cell and module manufacturing plants in Kokomo, Indiana.
- The output from the new StarPlus facilities will be sold to Stellantis for use in electric vehicle models that will be sold in North America.
- At full capacity, the StarPlus project will produce about 67 GWh of batteries, enough to supply approximately 670,000 vehicles annually.
- DOE estimates the EVs produced using StarPlus batteries will displace the usage of 260.3 million gallons of petroleum per year.
- In December 2022, DOE announced the closing of a $2.5 billion loan to Ultium Cells LLC to help finance construction of new lithium-ion battery cell manufacturing facilities in Ohio, Tennessee, and Michigan.
- Ultium Cells is a joint venture between General Motors and LG Energy Solutions.
- Ultium’s large format, pouch-type cells use a state-of-the-art chemistry to deliver more range at a lower cost.
- Cells can be arranged in different combinations to provide clean, reliable energy for pickups, SUVs, family vehicles, luxury vehicles, and commercial vehicles.
- Ultium Cells plans to use this technology in coordination with GM’s work to eliminate 100% of tailpipe emissions from its new U.S. light-duty vehicles by 2035.
- In November 2024, LPO announced the closing of a $475 million loan to Li-Cycle U.S. Inc.
- The Li-Cycle loan will help finance construction of a first-of-its-kind lithium-ion battery resource recovery facility in Rochester, New York.
- Li-Cycle will aggregate source material and process it into “black mass” at three “Spokes” across North America in Rochester, New York; Gilbert, Arizona; and Tuscaloosa, Alabama.
- The black mass will be sent to the Rochester “Hub” facility for additional processing.
- Li-Cycle expects its Rochester “Hub” facility to be a significant source of recycled battery-grade lithium carbonate in North America.
- The facility’s output is expected to support the battery needs of approximately 180,000 EVs annually at peak operations.
- This quantity of EVs would reduce gasoline use by up to 71 million gallons per year, equivalent to more than 633,000 metric tons of CO2 emissions annually.
- In October 2024, LPO announced a conditional commitment to EVgo Swift Borrower LLC for a loan guarantee of up to $1.05 billion to expand public EV charging infrastructure.
- If finalized, the loan guarantee will support EVgo’s deployment of approximately 7,500 charging ports at roughly 1,100 charging stations across the United States.
- First deployments will include high-power 350kW fast chargers capable of charging two EVs simultaneously.
- EVgo plans to continue installing power sharing equipment over a 5-year deployment timeline.
- EVgo’s public charging services will support equitable EV growth and ownership, particularly in metropolitan areas expected to experience the fastest growth.
- People who live in multi-family housing may not have access to home charging and would benefit most from fast charging.
- EVgo’s chargers are compatible with all new EVs capable of fast charging and can reach 80% charge from empty in as little as 20 minutes.
