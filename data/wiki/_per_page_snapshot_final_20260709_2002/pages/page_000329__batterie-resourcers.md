# Batterie Resourcers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-11

**Source:** [Recycling-Startup Batterie Resourcers to build recycling plant in Georgia - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2022/01/11/recycling-startup-batterie-resourcers-to-build-recycling-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_000329`

## Facts

- Batterie Resourcers is a US battery recycling startup building a commercial lithium-ion battery recycling plant in Covington, Georgia.
- The company is supported by Jaguar Land Rover among other investors.
- The Covington plant is scheduled to begin operations as early as August 2022.
- The facility will be set up in an existing hall and have a processing capacity of 30,000 tonnes.
- Battery Resourcers is investing $43 million (around €38 million) into the project.
- The project will create 150 jobs in Covington, Georgia.
- Battery Resourcers states the site is strategically located near several EV production centers and lithium-ion gigafactories.
- Covington is connected to Atlanta, approximately 50 kilometers away, via Interstate 20.
- SK Innovation is building a battery factory in Georgia to supply Volkswagen, Ford, and other customers.
- Nearby automotive manufacturing facilities include Volkswagen in Chattanooga, Mercedes-Benz in Tuscaloosa (producing EQE SUV and EQS SUV), and BMW in Spartanburg (producing PHEV models).
- The Volvo plant in Ridgeville, South Carolina (producing the electric XC90 successor and Polestar 3) remains accessible from the site.
- Michael O’Kronley, CEO and director of Battery Resourcers, states that automotive OEMs currently lack responsible and cost-effective disposal options for discarded batteries and scrap.
- The company claims its next-generation technology and U.S. location provide a sustainable solution that minimizes mining needs and returns battery-grade materials to the lithium-ion supply chain.
- Battery Resourcers has long-term plans to open additional recycling facilities in North America, Europe, and Asia.
- The global network aims to process up to 150,000 tonnes of lithium-ion material annually.
- Battery Resourcers is considering opening an additional facility to produce cathode materials in 2023.
- The facility's current recycling processes will primarily recover cathode materials from NMC, NCA, and LFP batteries.
- Jaguar Land Rover participated in a $20 million funding round for Battery Resourcers in April 2021.
