# Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Toyota Industries Corporation Expands Georgia Presence, Creating 250 New Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-toyota-industries-corporation-expands-georgia-32470)  ·  `page_012356`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc., (TIESNA) will create more than 250 new jobs while expanding the Toyota Industries Corporation campus in Pendergrass with a new manufacturing facility valued at $69 million in investment.
- Japanese Consul General Mio Maeda and local representatives joined TIESNA for the groundbreaking ceremony this afternoon.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has distinguished itself as a leader in the automotive industry and is the best state for business, pledging continued work with state and local partners to bring opportunity to communities across Georgia.
- TIESNA is a subsidiary of Toyota Industries, which is headquartered in Japan and develops and manufactures automobile-related products including car air-conditioning compressors and car electronics components for worldwide carmakers.
- The Toyota Industries Group has operated in Pendergrass since 2004, starting with TD Automotive Compressor Georgia, LLC (TACG).
- TACG was followed by Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America, Co. (TICA) in 2012.
- These existing companies currently support more than 400 jobs in Jackson County.
- TIESNA’s new facility will be located at the 1700 Valentine Industrial Parkway campus in Pendergrass.
- The new facility will produce DC-to-DC converters, which convert high voltage to low voltage to supply power to auxiliary equipment.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Jackson County Commission Chair Tom Crow welcomed TIESNA as the third Toyota Industries related company to Jackson County and praised TACG and TICA as outstanding corporate citizens, pledging community support for its success.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, highlighted the expansion as part of Georgia’s growing automaking sector and noted that significant investments from Toyota Industries and other automotive suppliers are delivering career opportunities for Georgians.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Kristen Miller represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Power, the Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Toyota Industries has been part of the fabric of Jackson County since 2004 and emphasized that maintaining 50 years of representation in Japan allows direct communication with corporate HQs including Toyota Industries’.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
