# Hyundai Metaplant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Ellabell, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Hyundai Metaplant in Ellabell, Bryan County is a $7.5 billion electric-vehicle manufacturing complex that will begin producing EVs in the fourth quarter of 2024. The plant will initially have capacity for 300,000 vehicles per year (expandable to 500,000) and includes a battery-pack joint venture with LG Energy Systems.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Primary EV vehicle assembly plant in Georgia; will produce the first electric SUVs built in the state and host a battery-pack joint venture.

## Key facts

- The Hyundai Metaplant will have an initial capacity of 300,000 vehicles per year and can expand to 500,000 if demand dictates.
- At full employment the plant will host approximately 8,5 00 Hyundai Motor Group employees.
- Georgia’s logistics network-including rail, airport and port facilities-is cited as critical for EV manufacturing growth.
- Since 2018, e-mobility supply-chain projects in Georgia have created about 32,200 new jobs and $27 billion in investment.
- Hyundai chose Georgia primarily for its geographic position, logistics infrastructure, and site suitability rather than tax incentives.
- The state’s Quick Start program provides free, customized workforce training to qualified businesses and has more than 50 years of automotive-manufacturing experience.
- Georgia Quick Start’s new EV training center will serve Hyundai and affiliate companies such as Mobis, Glovis and Transys.
- Multiple Tier-1 suppliers-including Hwashin, Seoyon E-Hwa, DAS Corp., Doowon Climate Control America, Anovion Technologies and Woory Industrial-are establishing new facilities in Georgia to support EV production.
- The $7.59 billion Metaplant is the largest economic development project in Georgia state history.
- Facility spans 16 million square feet and can produce up to 500,000 electric vehicles per year (revised from 300,000).
- Projected employment of 8,500 workers at full build-out, including 2,600 building automobiles.
- Robots of various sizes stamp, weld, and assemble autos; four-legged dog-like robots inspect the work.
- Self-propelled dollies equipped with GPS and motion sensors move EVs around the shop floor.
- Employees are called “Meta Pros,” monitoring equipment or performing tasks too tactile for machines.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced a nearly $8 billion deal to build the Hyundai EV plant, promising more than 8,000 well-paying jobs.
- A federal raid at the Hyundai Metaplant detained approximately 475 foreign workers, about 300 of whom were Korean, as part of an investigation into illegal labor practices.
- The 2,300-acre site hosts a complete supply chain of Hyundai affiliates and a battery joint venture with LG Energy Solution.
- Companies on the site must together invest $5.545 billion and hire at least 8,500 workers by 2031 to qualify for $2 billion in state tax credits and abatements.
- Hyundai told The Current that about 1,200 people were employed across the site as of mid-March, not counting Korean workers.
- More than a dozen suppliers have set up operations in surrounding counties making components such as brakes and bumpers.
- HMGMA stated it was not responsible for actions of affiliates, suppliers, or subcontractors cited in workplace safety violations.
- Hyundai announced it would bring a senior executive to supervise the firms amid the ongoing federal investigation.
- The plant employs 1,340 Meta Pros with an average salary of $58,100, which is 25% higher than the Bryan County average.
- Hyundai’s annual local payroll has reached $497 million.
- Facility spans 11 buildings and 697,000 m² (7.5 million ft²) of space.
- Production capacity is up to 500,000 Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis vehicles per year.
- Solar-roof installations provide up to 5% of the plant’s electricity demand.
- A fleet of 21 hydrogen-fuel-cell trucks from Xcient delivers parts with zero tailpipe emissions.
- Approximately 850 robots unload parts; about 300 AGVs move materials without tracks.
- The welding shop houses 475 industrial robots, part of roughly 850 robots in the plant.
- Sled-like AGVs autonomously transport finished vehicles to parking areas.
- Boston Dynamics’ Spots quadruped robots inspect welds, and Atlas humanoid robots are planned for deployment.
- Hyundai aims for 100% renewable energy use at the Metaplant.
- Future employment goal of 8,500 on-site jobs plus an additional 7,000 indirect jobs for local suppliers.
- HL-GA battery company will produce nickel-cobalt-magnesium cells starting next year as part of a $4 billion joint venture with LG Energy Solutions.
- A second $5 billion battery plant is planned in partnership with SK On.
- Hyundai’s total U.S. investment is projected to reach $21 billion by 2028.
- The Metaplant uses up to 850 robots and about 300 automated guided vehicles (AGVs) for near-fully automated production.
- Factory may eventually employ 8,500 direct workers and 7,000 satellite workers, with an annual capacity of 500,000 cars-more than Tesla’s Texas Gigafactory but less than its Shanghai plant.
- "Meta Pros" earn an average $58,100 per year, 35% higher than the Bryan County average.
- Solar roofs generate up to 5 percent of the plant’s electricity; a fleet of 21 hydrogen fuel-cell trucks (Xcient) provides zero-emission parts logistics.
- Goal to obtain 100 percent renewable energy for the Metaplant by 2030.
- Robots perform welding, door installation, body-in-white assembly, and collaborative tasks alongside human workers.
- Spot, a quadruped robot from Hyundai-owned Boston Dynamics, inspects welds; Atlas, a humanoid robot, is also being trained for factory work.
- The site comprises 11 low-roofed buildings covering 70 hectares (697,000 m²).
- $2.1 billion in state subsidies make the Metaplant the largest public development project in Georgia’s history.
- Hyundai describes the Metaplant as North America’s most automated car factory and its most advanced full-scale facility among Hyundai Motor Co.’s 12 global manufacturing sites.
- During a summer shift, only 1,340 humans were needed to keep two Ioniq models moving continuously on the line.
- The welding shop houses about 500 industrial robots; AGVs deliver parts just-in-time without human intervention.
- Robots both big and small stamp, weld and assemble autos on the production line.
- EVs are moved about the shop floor on self-propelled dollies that use GPS and motion sensors for navigation.
- Four-legged robots resembling medium-sized dogs inspect work throughout the facility.
- Employees are referred to as “Meta Pros” and handle tasks requiring tactile sensitivity.
- The plant is promoted as a “factory of the future,” showcasing cutting-edge manufacturing technology.
- The Metaplant is designed to turn out 300,000 electric cars a year, with a target of 500,000 as expansion proceeds.
- It integrates stamping, body, paint, assembly, and battery production on or beside one campus.
- Hyundai's Georgia investment totals $12.6 billion, the largest in the state’s history.
- The project sits at the center of labor and immigration questions after enforcement action stalled the battery plant schedule.
- The IONIQ 9 three-row SUV is already coming off the line, with Kia and Genesis models joining in 2026.
- Funding source for the Metaplant is private.
