# LG Energy Solution - Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-08

**Source:** [LG instructs employees to suspend U.S. business trips following ICE raid at Georgia EV battery plant | Shacknews](https://www.shacknews.com/article/145851/lg-hyundai-battery-plant-ice-raid-south-korea)  ·  `page_001243`

## Facts

- US ICE authorities arrested around 475 people during a raid at an in-construction battery plant jointly operated by Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution in Georgia.
- Just under 300 of those detained were South Korean nationals who were aiding in the construction.
- Following the incident, LG instructed its employees to suspend all business trips to the US.
- LG directed employees already in the US to either remain in their accommodations or return to South Korea immediately.
- LG Energy Solution and South Korean Foreign Minister Cho Hyun responded to the raid, with Minister Cho calling an emergency meeting in Seoul.
- South Korean officials have been in contact with US authorities to push for the release and safe return of detained South Korean nationals.
- A plane has been chartered to fly the detained South Korean citizens home.
- LG issued an official statement confirming the suspension of further business trips to the US and warning workers to leave or stay secure in their lodgings.
- The incident is expected to continue damaging US and foreign relations regarding trade and economics.
- The raid has stoked anti-immigration and anti-business sentiments, compounding pressures from Trump's tariffs and leaving many in South Korea outraged.
- South Korea has invested billions into American industries, including a $26 billion investment promised by Hyundai from 2025 to 2028.
- South Korean Foreign Minister Cho Hyun stated that Korea is 'deeply concerned and feel a heavy sense of responsibility over the arrests of our nationals.'
- Minister Cho Hyun warned that the incident may negatively affect business between the US and South Korea in the short term.
- In a press conference this week, Minister Cho Hyun stated that if the completion of the Hyundai factory is delayed, the United States will suffer significant losses.
- Construction of the joint Hyundai and LG battery plant in Georgia is currently on pause due to the raid.
