# Hitachi Astemo Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-30

**Source:** [page_055871](https://en.acnnewswire.com/press-release/english/69162/hitachi-damping-force-adjustment-absorber-adopted-by-toyota-for-new-land-cruiser)  ·  `page_055871`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Co., Ltd. announced that its damping force adjustment absorber co-developed with Aisin Corporation was adopted by Toyota Motor Corporation for the new Land Cruiser launched in June.
- The jointly developed suspension adjusts damping force using separate mechanisms for extension and compression, enabling more precise control of damping forces than shock absorbers with a single mechanism.
- The improved damping force control results in a higher level of maneuverability and a more comfortable driving experience for Toyota's new Land Cruiser.
- By incorporating the damping force control valve inside the shock absorber instead of on the outside where it is conventionally installed, the structure can be simplified.
- Compared to conventional products, volume has been reduced by about 20%.
- Weight has been reduced by about 30% compared to conventional products.
- The reduced volume makes the component much easier to mount on the vehicle.
- Hitachi Astemo is a joint venture between Hitachi, Ltd. and Honda Motor Co.
- Hitachi Astemo develops, manufactures, sells, and services automotive and transportation components, as well as industrial machinery and systems.
- Hitachi Astemo is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
