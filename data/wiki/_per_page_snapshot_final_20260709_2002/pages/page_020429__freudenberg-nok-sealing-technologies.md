# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-19

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK expands with new laboratory in Findlay - Fluid Power World](https://www.fluidpowerworld.com/freudenberg-nok-expands-with-new-laboratory-in-findlay/)  ·  `page_020429`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies will increase its testing capabilities with the addition of a new materials laboratory at its Findlay, Ohio manufacturing plant.
- The new laboratory is part of an overall plant expansion taking place in 2015 and will provide the company’s Fluid Power Division with new material characterization, failure analysis and mechanical property test capabilities.
- The laboratory will consolidate and expand an existing engineering test laboratory at the facility.
- Dr. Theodore G. Duclos, president of Freudenberg-NOK, stated that research and development are core values at the company and the laboratory investment supports its mission, customers and markets.
- The Findlay expansion, announced last month, will add 43,000 square feet to the existing factory.
- The new lab will account for approximately 2,000 sq. ft of the total expansion.
- The laboratory represents an investment of approximately $450,000 in equipment and building expenses.
- The entire project represents an investment of approximately $8.6 million in construction and equipment upgrades.
- The expansion will create 25 new jobs at the Findlay plant.
- The project is expected to be complete by winter 2015.
- The company officially celebrated the expansion with a groundbreaking ceremony attended by executives, elected officials, employees, and media.
- The Findlay facility produces approximately 700 plastic parts for customers including vehicle manufacturers and tier one and two suppliers using state-of-the-art manufacturing processes.
- The new laboratory will perform analysis and testing of parts the plant is making from plastics.
- These parts include thrust washers, seals, and other components used in the automotive industry.
- The company is installing a new engineering test rig in the lab to measure torque loss.
- Data from the test rig will help Freudenberg-NOK further improve custom engineered sealing solutions that are reducing CO2 emissions and improving fuel economy.
- The 25 new jobs created will include chemist, chemical engineer, and quality technician positions.
- The company worked with the state of Ohio, the Findlay-Hancock County Economic Development authority, Jobs Ohio, and the Regional Growth Partnership to secure workforce incentives.
- The expansion will help the facility increase its compression and injection molding product production.
- The facility's compression and injection molding products include seal rings, thrust washers, pistons, and assemblies.
- The plant currently manufactures 185 million seals and related components annually.
- The company anticipates the new addition will increase production by approximately 25 percent a year.
