## Facts

- Eaton Corporation is a power management company that works with customers on efficiency, safety, and sustainability.
- The company was founded by Joseph O. Eaton, Henning O. Taube, and Viggo V. Torbenson as the Torbenson Gear and Axle Co. in 1911.
- The Torbenson Axle Company was incorporated in 1916, and a year later its largest customer, Republic Motor Truck Company, bought out the company.
- In 1923, Eaton bought the company back from Republic, changing the name to Eaton Axle and Spring Company.
- Eaton's diversified operations include electrical products, systems and services for power transmission, distribution, and control, lighting products, wiring products, hydraulics for industrial and mobile equipment, pneumatic systems for commercial and military use, and powertrain systems.
- Eaton is publicly traded on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol ETN and is a member of the S&P 500.
- The company ranks #194 in the Fortune 500.
- Eaton has over 100,000 employees.
- Eaton reported $22.23 billion in revenue in 2013.
- Eaton's corporate headquarters is located at 2000 Apollo Dr, Cleveland, OH 44142-4102.
- The CEO of Eaton Corporation is Alexander M. Cutler.
