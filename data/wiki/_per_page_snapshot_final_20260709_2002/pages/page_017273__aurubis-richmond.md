# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-17

**Source:** [Press release: Aurubis AG starts construction of a € 300 million (approx. $ 320 ... | Presseportal](https://www.presseportal.de/en/pm/109932/5251124)  ·  `page_017273`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG broke ground on June 17, 2022, for the United States' first large secondary smelter for multimetal recycling.
- Total investment in Augusta, Richmond County in Georgia is estimated at €300 million (approx. $320 million).
- The plant is expected to be fully operational by mid-2024.
- Aurubis Richmond will create over 120 new jobs directly.
- Once fully on-line, the facility will have the capacity to process about 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- The materials processed include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other recycling materials containing metals.
- Aurubis is a leading provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated the company has doubled down on commitments to use its technology and experience to become a forerunner for recycling valuable materials containing copper, nickel, tin, and other industrial and precious metals locally in the US.
- The US currently produces about 6 million tons of recycling materials that contain valuable metals.
- Aurubis aims to take advantage of this potential and ensure these materials get reused as critical input materials for many industries in the US.
- Roland Harings noted that current supply cannot meet demand for metals such as copper, and the US is lacking recycling capacities.
- The Augusta facility will reduce waste and direct key metals to industry including renewables such as solar and wind, along with electric vehicle and battery manufacturers.
- Copper is 100-percent recyclable without any loss in performance.
- Secondary smelter recycling at an industrial scale requires significant long-term investment.
- Aurubis' state-of-the-art environmental technology meets or exceeds the highest State of Georgia and federal government standards.
- Aurubis Richmond will have a very low impact on air quality, estimated at less than 0.2% share of dust emissions in Richmond county based on the actual emissions at the Aurubis recycling site in Germany.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated this is the largest single investment made by a German company in Georgia history.
- The facility is expected to become a major player in the North American supply chain and a significant employer in greater Augusta, GA.
- Aurubis' metals are crucial enablers to promote global market trends such as renewable energy, digitalization and electric vehicles.
