# LINDE + WIEMANN GmbH KG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [LINDE + WIEMANN to create 200 new jobs, will invest more than $35M in Hart County - Georgia Ports Authority](https://gaports.com/blog/linde-wiemann-to-create-200-new-jobs-will-invest-more-than-35m-in-hart-county/)  ·  `page_057173`

## Facts

- German-based LINDE + WIEMANN GmbH KG will establish a U.S.-based assembly operation with associated national sales and distribution activities in Hart County, Georgia.
- The company's first facility will initially employ approximately 200 people.
- The project represents a capital investment of more than $35 million.
- Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal stated that the decision to locate in Hart County sends a strong message about Georgia's business-friendly climate.
- Gov. Nathan Deal noted that the announcement indicates Northeast Georgia as a hub for manufacturing products vital to major automotive industry leaders worldwide.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr stated they worked with local partners in Hart County to build a strong environment for companies like LINDE + WIEMANN.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr said the state will continue working hard to attract top-tier business in the future.
- LINDE + WIEMANN is a privately-held, mid-sized German company specializing in the manufacturing and assembly of engineered steel systems mounted in motor vehicles.
- The company's products include instrument panel assemblies, front end and door structure assemblies, and structural body-in-white assemblies.
- LINDE + WIEMANN will locate on a 29-acre site in the Gateway II Industrial Park in Hart County, Georgia.
- The company will construct a 60,000 square foot facility and a 3,500 square foot head office building in the initial phases of the project.
- From the proposed facility, LINDE + WIEMANN will supply customers including BMW in Greer, South Carolina, and Daimler in Tuscaloosa, Alabama.
- CEO Ulrich Schoof stated that LINDE + WIEMANN performed an extensive site search over several months to identify the right location.
- As a Tier One supplier to BMW and other automotive OEMs, LINDE + WIEMANN's quality products depend on the quality of the local workforce.
- Hart County offered LINDE + WIEMANN access to qualified labor, attractive logistics, reasonable operating costs, quality of life, room for expansion, and supportive local leadership.
- The company was delighted with the support from the State of Georgia and welcomed the assistance of Georgia's German-speaking project manager.
- LINDE + WIEMANN will develop the Hart County site as a premier hub for its North American business.
- LINDE + WIEMANN expects to create jobs and wealth in the Georgia Piedmont region as it grows its business.
- The new U.S.-based assembly and stamping operation will put LINDE + WIEMANN in a strategic position to expand its client base in the North American market.
- The company presently has production operations in Germany, Spain, the Czech Republic, South Africa, Turkey, and China.
- Hart County Board of Commission Chairman Jimmy Carey welcomed LINDE + WIEMANN to the community and expressed hope for a long, mutually beneficial relationship.
- Chairman Jimmy Carey stated that decisions by world-class advanced manufacturing operations to locate in Hart County reinforce local efforts to provide an educated, skilled workforce and continue long-term development.
- Hart County Industrial Building Authority Chairman Bill Leard welcomed LINDE + WIEMANN and highlighted the excellent career opportunities for area citizens.
- GDEcD Director of International Investment Nico Wijnberg represented the Global Commerce division throughout the project.
- The project involved collaboration with the Hart County Industrial Building Authority, the Hart County Board of Commissioners, and Kevin Lovelace of Georgia Power.
