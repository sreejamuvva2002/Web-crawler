# Dinex Aftermarket US

## Facts

- Dinex designs and manufactures complete exhaust and aftertreatment systems for all EPA emission standards, from EPA 10 through EPA 21.
- Heavy-duty trucks and buses in North America have been equipped with advanced emission technologies since the introduction of EPA 2007 standards.
- New EPA phases up to EPA 2021 have further tightened regulations, transforming exhaust systems into sophisticated emissions control units.
- Today's systems rely on a combination of filters, catalysts, sensors, and DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid) systems to significantly reduce harmful pollutants.
- When operating properly, the exhaust from an EPA 21-compliant vehicle can be cleaner than the surrounding air in many urban environments.
- Keeping the system functioning at peak performance requires regular maintenance, as early signs of trouble often point to underlying engine issues before they escalate.
- Dinex offers a complete range of high-quality aftermarket parts to support all components of EPA 10, 13, 17, and 21 emission systems.
- The company empowers customers with technical training, expert knowledge, and troubleshooting support for advanced emissions systems.
- Dinex blends its global OE expertise and continuous innovation with the agility and competitive mindset of the aftermarket.
- EPA 2004 regulations were the last set of standards that heavy-duty trucks could meet through engine optimization alone.
- With the introduction of EPA 2007, Diesel Particulate Filters (DPFs) became necessary to capture fine particulate matter (PM).
- By EPA 2010, most OEMs adopted Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems to meet strict new NOx limits.
- Since then, the cost and complexity of emission systems have grown significantly while their environmental impact has been extraordinary.
- Toxic nitrogen oxides (NOx) have been reduced by over 95%, and particulate emissions by more than 99% compared to levels from 30 years ago.
- Dinex's 2025 School Bus catalog covers emission systems for the most popular EPA10-21 standards, containing 40 pages with 21 systems and hundreds of quality, competitive spare parts.
- The Diesel Oxidation Catalyst (DOC) burns a wide range of pollutant gases in the exhaust stream and supports the Particulate Filter by helping it burn off trapped particles and soot.
- A failed DOC can lead to significantly more frequent active regenerations, often caused by EGR coolant leaks or insufficient air supply within the system.
- The Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) catalyst is where nitrogen oxides (NOx) react with DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid) and are reduced to harmless components.
- The complete SCR system includes pumps, injectors, and sensors, and unfavorable operating conditions or poor maintenance of the DEF system can damage the SCR substrate.
- New EPA standards coming in 2027 will target near-zero emissions across all driving conditions, tighten control over regulated pollutants, and capture ultrafine particles as small as 10 nanometers.
- Dinex is working closely with leading global OEMs to develop the next generation of emission technologies for future readiness.
