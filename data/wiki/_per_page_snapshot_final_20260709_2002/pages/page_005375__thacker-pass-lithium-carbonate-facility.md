# Thacker Pass Lithium Carbonate Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [THACKER PASS | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/thacker-pass)  ·  `page_005375`

## Facts

- In October 2024, the U.S. Department of Energy’s Loan Programs Office announced a $2.26 billion loan to Lithium Americas Corp’s subsidiary, Lithium Nevada Corp., to finance construction of lithium carbonate manufacturing facilities at Thacker Pass in Humboldt County, Nevada.
- The loan includes $1.97 billion of principal and $289.7 million of capitalized interest.
- The project is located next to a mine site containing the largest confirmed lithium resource in North America.
- Once fully operational, the facility will produce approximately 40,000 tonnes per year of battery-grade lithium carbonate for lithium-ion batteries.
- The announcement reinforces the Biden-Harris Administration’s whole-of-government approach to strengthening America's critical materials supply chain for clean transportation and national energy security.
- The project is expected to create approximately 1,800 jobs during construction.
- The project will support 360 permanent jobs in operations.
- The project supports efforts to expand and secure reliable, sustainable domestic supply chains for critical materials to reach clean energy and climate goals.
- The project aims to reduce reliance on economic competitors like China.
- Lithium carbonate from Thacker Pass could support battery production for up to 800,000 electric vehicles annually.
- The produced lithium carbonate could avoid the consumption of 317 million gallons of gasoline per year.
- The project will benefit in part from union labor under President Biden’s Investing in America agenda.
- The engineering, procurement, and construction management contractor entered into a National Construction Agreement (Project Labor Agreement) with North America’s Building Trades Unions for construction.
- The Project Labor Agreement aims to minimize construction risk, ensure skilled labor availability, address remote location challenges, and prioritize employment of local and regional skilled craft workers.
- The agreement prioritizes the employment of Native Americans and residents of under-represented communities.
- The loan was issued through the Advanced Technology Vehicle Manufacturing (ATVM) Loan Program as a direct loan.
- The project statistics were last updated in October 2024.
- The loan amount represents the approximate amount approved at closing, including principal and any capitalized interest.
