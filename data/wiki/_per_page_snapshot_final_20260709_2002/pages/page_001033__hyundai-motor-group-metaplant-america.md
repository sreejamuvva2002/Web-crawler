# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-02

**Source:** [Georgia’s Hyundai Metaplant begins producing third car - Bryan County News](https://www.bryancountynews.com/news/georgias-hyundai-metaplant-begins-producing-third-car/)  ·  `page_001033`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp drove the new hybrid vehicle off the production line on Tuesday.
- The Kia Sportage Turbo-Hybrid is the first Kia and first hybrid electric vehicle to be produced at the Ellabell Metaplant.
- It is the third vehicle manufactured at the site since it began production in 2022, joining HMGMA’s electric Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- While the Sportage Hybrid has been produced in Korea, this marks the first time it is being manufactured in the U.S.
- Kia North America and America President and CEO Seungkyu (Sean) Yoon, Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess, and about 200 HMGMA employees known as Meta Pros joined Gov. Kemp to celebrate the vehicle reveal.
- The hybrid Kia Sportage debuted by traveling to the stage on an Autonomous Mobile Robot (AMR), a machine used throughout HMGMA’s production processes.
- Two yellow “robot dogs” greeted guests at the event.
- Gov. Brian Kemp called the reveal a historic moment.
- Kemp stated that mutual success is the direct result of a proven partnership approach creating long-lasting relationships between Kia America, HMG’s Metaplant, the local community, and hardworking Georgians.
- Kemp expressed looking forward to the new vehicle joining the Georgia-grown lineup and helping support jobs for communities for years to come.
- The new vehicle’s production marks the first step in HMGMA’s pivot to producing hybrid vehicles.
- Chris Susock, chief manufacturing officer for Hyundai Motor North America, stated the plant’s original intention was to manufacture all-electric vehicles.
- There has been a sharp decline in U.S. electric vehicle demand, especially following the expiration of federal tax credits outlined in U.S. President Donald Trump’s “big beautiful bill.”
- Midway through construction, officials decided to shift focus on making the plant adaptable to hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) production to balance market shifts.
- Susock emphasized that the pivot focuses on flexibility in automation and technology to switch powertrain mix patterns on a dime and adapt to the market very quickly.
- Last month, HMGMA shipped about 6,000 units outside the facility.
- This shipment volume represents about 50% of the plant’s capacity.
- The plant is ramped up to reach full capacity of about 580,000 units by 2028.
- Susock stated that product planning will now rely on forecasts, and HMGMA has the capability and technology to build requested models quickly.
- Future builds could include more Kias, different HEVs, or other models depending on demand.
- Yoon noted that Kia is seeing higher demand for hybrids, especially SUVs including the Sportage, Carnival, and Telluride.
- Tony Heo, President and CEO of HMGMA, stated that employees received extensive training to produce the new vehicle.
- Many team members traveled to Kia facilities in West Point, Georgia, and South Korea to learn best practices for Sportage Hybrid production.
- Both HMGMA and Kia’s plant in West Point, GA, emphasize the creation of high-quality jobs and economic opportunities for their local communities and across the state.
- Yoon stated that the Metaplant in Savannah is a clear testament to confidence in Georgia’s future as an automotive powerhouse.
- Yoon expressed great confidence that the Metaplant will continue strong momentum with the Sportage Hybrid quickly becoming one of Kia’s highest volume models.
