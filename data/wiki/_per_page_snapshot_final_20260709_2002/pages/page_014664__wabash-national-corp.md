# Wabash National Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-15

**Source:** [Wabash National buys truck body manufacturer Supreme](https://www.themiddlemarket.com/news/wabash-national-buys-truck-body-manufacturer-supreme)  ·  `page_014664`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. is acquiring Supreme Industries Inc. (NYSE: STS), a supplier of commercial vehicles and related components, for approximately $364 million.
- The deal is intended to advance Wabash National into the final mile, or the last leg, of the delivery process.
- Supreme was founded in 1974 and is a U.S. manufacturer of light and medium-duty truck bodies.
- Supreme is based in Goshen, Indiana, and operates seven facilities throughout the United States.
- Supreme is one of the largest U.S. truck body suppliers, with reported sales of $299 million in 2016.
- Supreme serves a range of customers including national rental fleets, national and regional leasing companies, truck dealers, and fleet operators.
- Wabash National CEO Dick Giromini stated: “This is a great opportunity for both companies to combine our strengths to provide an enhanced customer experience within the growing final mile delivery space.”
- Wabash National is based in Lafayette, Indiana, and manufactures semi-trailer and liquid transportation systems.
- Wabash National sells its products across North America.
- With Supreme, Wabash National will accelerate organic growth and offer a broader conventional product offering.
- Wabash National has been closely monitoring the transportation landscape as e-commerce growth continues to change the logistics model.
- Other recent truck and auto-related deals include Federal Signal Corp.’s agreement to buy Truck Bodies and Equipment International, Accuride Corp.’s purchase of parts manufacturer KIC LLC, Monroe Truck Equipment Inc.’s acquisition of equipment maker Towmaster Inc., and W.W. Williams Co. LLC’s deal to buy parts provider Auto Safety House LLC.
- Baird is serving as financial adviser to Supreme.
- Haynes and Boone LLP is acting as legal counsel for the transaction.
