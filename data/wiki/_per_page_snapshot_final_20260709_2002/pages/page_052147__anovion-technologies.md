# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-28

**Source:** [New manufacturing facility breaks ground in Bainbridge](https://www.wctv.tv/2023/06/28/new-manufacturing-facility-breaks-ground-bainbridge/)  ·  `page_052147`

## Facts

- An $800 million project with Anovion Technologies will be on a 1.5 million square foot factory.
- The facility will create over 400 jobs for residents of Decatur County.
- Specifically, 412 jobs will be created with the groundbreaking.
- Anovion is an American-owned, climate-tech-driven company that broke ground in Bainbridge, Georgia.
- The project aims to push Georgia to be the number one state for the path of e-mobility.
- Georgia Senator Jon Ossoff stated that manufacturing incentives and infrastructure policy are bringing jobs and investments to the state of Georgia.
- U.S. Congressman Sanford Bishop said the project will contribute to American energy independence, lower costs for families and businesses, and create jobs in Southwest Georgia.
- Anovion is an upstream supplier to battery makers that produce lithium-ion batteries for vehicles.
- The company manufactures materials that go into those lithium-ion batteries.
- Those batteries are used in everyday items like cellphones, cars, and medical devices like pacemakers.
- Anovion is bringing the capability of making synthetic graphite back to the United States.
- Most synthetic graphite production currently happens in Asia and China.
- Chairman and Lead Investor Chip Dunn stated that Bainbridge's location provides access to ports like Savannah or Panama City (Florida), or possibly the Bainbridge port itself.
- The facility requires significant power, and Georgia Power is developing big new infrastructure for them.
- Anovion's average wage is $63,000 a year.
- Executive Director of the Developmental Authority Rick McCaskill identified keeping young people in Bainbridge after graduation as a main local problem.
- The new jobs are hoped to serve as an incentive to keep residents in the city.
- The technical and high-quality jobs will cover roles from warehouse to office, to manufacturing to supervision.
- Anovion plans to provide scholarships for deserving students from the area to attend Southern Regional Technical College.
- President of Southern Regional Technical College Jim Glass commented on the educational doors opening for local students.
- City Manager of Bainbridge Chris Harvey agreed that graduates will have jobs to come home to after high school, technical college, or college education.
- Bainbridge is considered an ideal location for the facility due to its great location and potential to help reduce a carbon footprint while increasing Georgia's clean air manufacturing capacity.
- State leaders describe this as one of the fastest-growing projects in Georgia.
- The grand opening is planned for 2025.
