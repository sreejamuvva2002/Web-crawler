# Mercedes-Benz USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-01-14

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz to Create 800 Jobs in Atlanta | Atlanta Daily World](https://atlantadailyworld.com/2015/01/14/mercedes-benz-to-create-800-jobs-in-atlanta/)  ·  `page_046405`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz USA announced plans to make Atlanta its new corporate home.
- Mercedes-Benz USA is going to add approximately 800 jobs to the Atlanta employment landscape.
- The luxury automotive manufacturer is going to invest $74 million into its new U.S. headquarters according to Gov. Nathan Deal.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Mercedes-Benz USA’s decision to move to Georgia is simply a home run for the state due to its international name recognition and unmatched reputation for quality.
- Gov. Nathan Deal expressed anticipation in building upon this new partnership and adding Mercedes-Benz to the state’s already impressive automotive sector.
- Gov. Nathan Deal cited Georgia's top-ranked economic environment that has made it the No. 1 state for business three times over.
- Mercedes-Benz USA is currently headquartered in Montvale, N.J.
- Mercedes-Benz USA is responsible for the distribution, marketing and customer service for all Mercedes-Benz products in the United States.
- Mercedes-Benz USA president and CEO Stephen Cannon stated that Atlanta is a premier city which provides the perfect foundation to write the next chapter of their success story here in the U.S.
- Stephen Cannon noted that Atlanta offers employees strong quality of life, terrific schools and wonderful cultural and recreational activities.
- The automotive industry, from headquarter operations to major automotive suppliers’ manufacturing operations, is an important part of Georgia’s economy.
- Georgia’s automotive sector has experienced a 149 percent increase in investment during FY 2014.
- Georgia’s automotive sector has experienced an 11 percent increase in jobs during FY 2014.
- The state’s automotive industry exports grew 9.2 percent between 2012 and 2013.
- Atlanta has become a leading city for auto technology, with the GM Innovation Center, Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center and the AT&T Drive Studio calling Georgia home.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Chris Carr called welcoming one of the world’s most recognizable global brands to Georgia an incredible victory for the state.
- Chris Carr credited the success to continued collaboration with economic development partners including the Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Power, Development Authority of Fulton County, City of Sandy Springs, MARTA, the Georgia Department of Transportation, the Georgia Department of Driver Services and the Georgia Council for the Arts.
