# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-20

## Facts

- The Tucson sold in the U.S. is manufactured in Alabama.
- Hybrid and plug-in hybrid versions of the Tucson will be available.
- Both electrified variants pair a 180-hp, turbocharged 1.6-liter engine with an electric motor and a six-speed automatic transmission with all-wheel drive.
- The standard hybrid uses a 59-hp electric motor, while the plug-in hybrid upsizes to a 90-hp motor.
- The standard hybrid produces 226 combined horsepower, while the PHEV generates 261 combined horsepower.
- Hyundai promises an all-electric range of up to 32 miles for the PHEV.
- The PHEV will qualify for a federal tax credit of $6,587 based on battery capacity.
- The 2022 Tucson is expected to go on sale in June.
- Pricing starts at $26,135 for the base SE FWD model and ranges up to $37,285 for the Limited AWD.
- Hybrid model pricing starts at $30,235 and goes up to $38,535 for the Limited trim.
- Based on worldwide sales, the Tucson is Hyundai's best-selling vehicle.
