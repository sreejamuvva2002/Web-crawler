# Battery Belt

**Type:** Event  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Southern United States, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The article describes a rapidly expanding electric-vehicle manufacturing corridor across the Southern United States-dubbed the “Battery Belt”-driven by massive federal and state incentives, new battery and vehicle production facilities, and emerging labor-organizing efforts.

## EV supply-chain relevance

High - the Battery Belt encompasses multiple EV battery and vehicle production projects, many of which are located in Georgia or receive Georgia incentives.

- **Toyota Battery mega-site** — 7 million-square-foot complex with 14 production lines (10 EV batteries, 4 hybrid) slated to begin production in 2025 and fully operational by 2030; employs about 5,100 workers.
- **Hyundai EV and battery production complex** — $7.9 billion investment; up to 8,500 workers; receives up to $2.1 billion in state tax breaks.
- **BlueOval City** — $5.6 billion joint-venture (Ford & SK On) to produce EVs and batteries; 6,000 workers.
- **BlueOval SK Battery Park** — $5.8 billion project to supply batteries to BlueOval City; 5,000 workers.
- **Scout Motors plant** — $2 billion investment; operational in 2026; 4,000 workers; produces 200,000 vehicles per year.
- **BMW plant** — 11,000-worker facility that will soon receive batteries from nearby AESC plant.
- **AESC battery production facility (second)** — $3.1 billion total investment for two facilities; second plant announced in April.
- **Lithium mining project** — $90 million Department of Defense grant to resume mining at former open-pit lithium mine; expected output for 1.2 million EVs per year.
- **Lithium refining facility** — $1.3 billion facility to refine lithium for the Kings Mountain project.

## Key facts

- The Battery Belt region is experiencing a manufacturing boom across the EV supply chain, from charging stations to mineral refining and battery production.
- Toyota’s mega-site will host 14 production lines-10 for electric-vehicle batteries and four for hybrid vehicles-and employ about 5,100 workers when fully operational.
- Georgia’s incentive package includes up to $2.1 billion in tax breaks for Hyundai’s EV and battery complex outside Savannah.
- Federal incentives under the Inflation Reduction Act provide grants and tax credits that have spurred billions of dollars in EV-related investments.
- Since the IRA’s passage, roughly half of $110 billion in new EV manufacturing capital has been directed to the Southern United States.
- S&P Global Market Intelligence estimates that as many as two-thirds of future EV jobs could be located in the South.
- Tennessee’s BlueOval City joint venture between Ford and SK On will be among the largest auto manufacturing facilities in U.S. history.
- Southern Workers Assembly has launched a program to help workers secure jobs in strategic sectors and organize around them.
- Low union density, lower wages, and stringent anti-labor laws are cited as key reasons manufacturers are gravitating toward Southern states.
- Georgia’s 2024 law forbids companies receiving state incentives from recognizing unions except through secret-ballot elections.
