# Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-17

**Source:** [Hitachi Automotive Systems America to Expand in Walton County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2019-05-17/hitachi-automotive-systems-america-expand-walton-county)  ·  `page_002616`

## Facts

- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. is a Tier 1 supplier of world-class products to the global automotive market.
- The company announced plans to create 100 jobs through an expansion at its existing facility in Monroe, Georgia.
- The planned investment is at least $100 million, with potential up to $330 million.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp highlighted that Hitachi has been a partner to the Walton County community by investing in employees and creating jobs for more than twenty years.
- Governor Kemp expressed confidence that the investment will positively impact the local community and encourage continued success in Georgia’s strong automotive industry.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. is a group company of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd., which is headquartered in Tokyo.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. manufactures and markets engine powertrain systems, electric powertrain systems, and integrated vehicle control systems for major automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEM) worldwide.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. President and CEO Paul Carroll stated the company has been operating in Georgia for over twenty-two years.
- The Georgia factory supplies advanced technology products for major automotive OEMs including Ford Motor Company, General Motors, Nissan, Subaru, and Honda.
- Paul Carroll noted a great partnership with stakeholders, especially the State of Georgia, Walton County, and the City of Monroe.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. opened its first facility in Georgia in 1997.
- The company currently employs 900 Georgians.
- The expansion is intended to increase production capacity in response to the growth of the automotive industry.
- Walton County Commission Chairman Kevin Little stated that Hitachi has become Walton County's largest employer and provides quality jobs and investment.
- City of Monroe Mayor John Howard praised Hitachi for continuing to invest in high technology and top-quality jobs for the community.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Sr. Project Manager Tammy Caudell represented the Global Commerce Division on the project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Walton County, Walton EMC, and the Georgia Department of Community Affairs for this expansion.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies like Hitachi create jobs, take an active role in the community, and have driven success and interest from Japanese companies in Georgia.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. is a wholly owned subsidiary of Hitachi, Ltd., headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- The parent company develops, manufactures, sells, and services automotive components, transportation related components, industrial machines and systems.
