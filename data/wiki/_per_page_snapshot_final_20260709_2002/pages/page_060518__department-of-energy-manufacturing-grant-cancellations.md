# Department of Energy Manufacturing Grant Cancellations

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Trump DOE confirms it’s canceling over $700M in manufacturing grants - battery material companies include Ascend Elements and Anovion - Batteries News](https://batteriesnews.com/trump-doe-confirms-its-canceling-over-700m-in-manufacturing-grants-battery-material-companies-include-ascend-elements-and-anovion/)  ·  `page_060518`

## Facts

- The Department of Energy confirmed it is canceling $720 million worth of manufacturing awards.
- The grant cuts affect companies that manufacture battery materials, recycle lithium-ion batteries, and produce super-insulating windows.
- All grant money was authorized by Congress as part of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law passed in 2021.
- The bulk of the grant money was awarded in 2023 and 2024.
- Energy Secretary Chris Wright has been reviewing contracts made during the Biden administration.
- DOE spokesperson Ben Dietderich stated that the canceled projects “missed milestones” and “did not adequately advance the nation’s energy needs.”
- Ascend Elements was awarded $316 million in October 2022 toward a $1 billion facility in Kentucky to refine recycling technology for manufacturing waste and end-of-life batteries into lithium-ion battery materials.
- Federal government records show that $206 million has already been disbursed to Ascend Elements.
- Ascend Elements stated it is proceeding with its plans using alternative funding sources to cover any shortfall from the grant cancellation.
- Anovion was awarded $117 million to reshore technology for producing synthetic graphite for lithium-ion battery anodes.
- According to Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, Chinese suppliers control 75% of the synthetic graphite supply chain and produce 97% of all synthetic graphite anodes.
- Anovion’s plant is slated to be built in Alabama, with only $13.8 million disbursed to date according to a federal database.
- LuxWall was awarded $31.7 million in November 2023 by the DOE to build a factory on the site of an old coal plant near Detroit for manufacturing building-insulating windows.
- Only $1 million has been sent to LuxWall according to government records, though the company opened the first phase of its factory in August 2024.
- The canceled grants were intended to help startups transition from technology development to commercial deployment by bridging the “valley of death” financing gap.
- Government grants like these were designed to encourage private investors to contribute capital, with successful first-of-a-kind factories serving as templates to bolster the country’s manufacturing base.
