# WIKA USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [26 Children of WIKA Employees Receive the 2019 Wiegand Scholarship](https://blog.wika.us/wika-insight/ursula-wiegand-scholarships-2019/?doing_wp_cron=1707609854.6380980014801025390625)  ·  `page_015164`

## Facts

- WIKA USA established the Ursula Wiegand Scholarship Fund in 2002 to recognize the accomplishments of its employees’ children.
- The scholarship is awarded annually to high academic achievers and community leaders who are or will be enrolled in full-time undergraduate studies.
- In 2019, 26 exemplary students received the Ursula Wiegand Scholarship.
- Professional development, training, and ongoing education for its entire workforce are key values at WIKA USA.
- The scholarship is merit-based and extends WIKA USA’s commitment to learning to employees’ families.
- Former WIKA President Michael Gerster established the Ursula Wiegand Scholarship Fund in 2002.
- Recipients are selected based on their academic record and community involvement, requiring supporting documentation that includes a personal statement and teacher recommendations.
- The scholarship is named after Ursula Wiegand, one of WIKA’s founders and the first woman to receive the Business Owner of the Year award in Germany in 1984.
- Each year, WIKA USA provides a one-time award ranging from $500 to $1,000 per person to high-achieving students.
- Applicants must be children of WIKA USA employees and enrolled or planning to enroll in a full-time undergraduate program at an accredited two-year, four-year, or technical postsecondary institution.
- The application process begins in the spring, with winners announced in the summer.
- The award is intended to help students cover costs for tuition, books, lab fees, or other educational expenses.
- Since its inception 17 years ago, more than 300 students have received the Ursula Wiegand Scholarship.
- Employees’ children are permitted to apply each year of their undergraduate studies, and several have received the scholarship multiple times.
- No WIKA USA employees participate in selecting winners; an independent organization handles all application and selection phases.
- The 2019 recipients represented 18 different technical schools, community colleges, colleges, and universities.
- Recipients’ fields of study ranged from business and online marketing to cybersecurity and materials science engineering.
- Twelve scholarship winners, along with their families and WIKA management, attended a July 24 awards luncheon.
- Brent Shadix serves as Executive Vice President Human Resources at WIKA USA.
- Brent Shadix described the scholarship as an investment in future leaders for both the company and society, aiming to support tomorrow’s workforce across all industries.
- The program also recognizes WIKA employees at its Lawrenceville (Georgia) and Pasadena (Texas) locations who have raised outstanding children.
- Brent Shadix noted that the path to educational success is long, arduous, and expensive, and expressed WIKA USA’s honor in supporting this journey.
