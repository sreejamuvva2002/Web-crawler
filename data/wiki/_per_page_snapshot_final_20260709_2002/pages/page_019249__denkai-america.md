# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Copper Foil Manufacturer locates facility, North American Headquarters, to Augusta-Richmond County adding 250 new jobs | Sponsored | wfxg.com](https://www.wfxg.com/sponsored/were-hiring/copper-foil-manufacturer-locates-facility-north-american-headquarters-to-augusta-richmond-county-adding-250-new/article_32053c9a-7669-5630-8e7c-67726a4b03ff.html)  ·  `page_019249`

## Facts

- Denkai America will move its North American headquarters and a new manufacturing facility to Augusta-Richmond County, Georgia.
- Denkai America is the only North American manufacturer of high-quality electrodeposited copper foil used in printed electronics.
- The initial investment will be $150 million to establish an ED copper foil production facility focused on electric vehicle battery foil.
- The project aims for a total investment of $430 million across three phases.
- The new facility will be located on a 115-acre site at the Augusta Corporate Park.
- The move is expected to add 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Construction is expected to begin in September 2022 as part of the three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- Denkai America's materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board applications for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's copper foil is now also used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Operating for more than 60 years, Denkai has team members worldwide with operations in Japan and North America.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project, stating Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America.
- Governor Kemp emphasized Georgia's focus on bringing quality jobs to highly-trained Georgians and its top-ranking pro-business environment.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., Denkai Group President and CEO, stated the investment highlights their commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s President and CEO, said the company is thrilled to expand its manufacturing presence into Augusta due to the skilled workforce needed for growth.
- The company will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, welcomed Denkai America to the Augusta Corporate Park and highlighted the creation of high-skilled jobs.
- Barton Lowrey, Global Commerce Director of Corporate Solutions and Cyber Security at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, represented the state on this competitive project in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- The Technical College System of Georgia’s Georgia Quick Start program has provided specialized workforce training tailored to business needs for more than 40 years.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem and highlighted Georgia's commitment to harnessing electric mobility opportunities for automotive industry workers.
- The investment serves as evidence of Georgia’s commitment to building lasting international relationships and driving economic partnerships across the state, including recruiting the wider electric mobility value chain.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it attractive for industries focused on a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $12.6 billion in investments and more than 17,800 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific requirements prior to a prospective business visit.
