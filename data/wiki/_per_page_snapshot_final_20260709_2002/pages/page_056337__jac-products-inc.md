# JAC Products Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JAC Products Inc. ⋆ Business View Brasil](https://www.businessviewbrasil.com/jac-products-inc/)  ·  `page_056337`

## Facts

- JAC Products Inc. was founded in 1967.
- The company is a leading designer and manufacturer of automotive roof racks and cargo management systems.
- JAC supplies its products to the world’s top car manufacturers.
- Customers include Ford, Toyota, Honda, General Motors, Subaru, Renault, Hyundai, Chrysler, Kia Motors, Nissan, and Peugeot.
- The company has offices around the world and manufacturing facilities in Michigan, Georgia, and Portugal.
- JAC is a pacesetter with extruded aluminum designs.
- Engineering and manufacturing capabilities include plastic injection molding and steel rollform for base components.
- Jeff Cavalier serves as Engineering Manager for JAC Products.
- Cavalier is part of the manufacturing staff at JAC’s Franklin, Georgia facility.
- The Franklin, Georgia facility was originally set up as an aluminum extrusion plant in the mid-1960s.
- JAC took over the Franklin facility 20 years ago to help supply aluminum-based products to the automotive industry.
- Extrusion lines at the Franklin facility produce millions of pounds per year of 6XXX series aluminum for their products.
- JAC has approximately 1500 employees in North America.
- Competitors include the WKW Automotive Group, headquartered in Germany; DURA Automotive, with more than 21,000 employees in 15 countries; and the MINTH Group from China.
- Cavalier states that compared to competitors, JAC is relatively small but turns products around faster for engineering and delivers better products due to its technologies.
- Shaping and forming roof rack rails requires complex stack-up and tolerances defined by tenths and hundredths of a millimeter.
- Manufacturing practices are challenged by multiple areas of responsibility, making the effect of deliverables very important.
- Customer satisfaction is prioritized through quality parts delivered on time from new products to continuous improvement activities.
- Cargo Management Systems for the trucking industry include side rails, header bars, bed dividers, load bars, lockable storage bins, and other storage options.
- Cavalier considers JAC’s innovations with racking systems for work trucks to be top-notch and world-class.
- The company is broadening its expertise on complex step rails.
- JAC supports the aluminum substructure within the automotive industry as automakers switch from steel to aluminum to make cars lighter.
- This shift requires JAC to make changes to the aluminum alloys provided to car manufacturers, who are also moving rapidly to robotic welding of parts rather than bolting them together.
- JAC’s development group in Michigan stays closely involved with car manufacturers’ engineers.
- Product Development constantly tries to understand market movement two, five, and ten years out to keep the company in conversations with OEMs.
- Technology integration extends into cameras and LED lighting systems that can be incorporated into roof racks for 360-degree vehicle views and security systems.
- Coatings and sealings on JAC’s products must withstand years of harsh car wash detergents and rough weather, requiring extensive testing of various finishes.
- Cavalier notes that proximity to manufacturers in Michigan and the southeast, where more auto plants are being built, is helpful for meeting delivery needs.
- The Franklin location helps minimize freight costs for customers.
- Future plans include expanding territorial spread and product lines, growing diversity of products for OEMs, maintaining worldwide relationships, tapping into China, Central and South America markets, and diversifying beyond roof racks.
- At a glance, JAC Products Inc. is headquartered in Saline, Michigan, and manufactures roof racks and cargo management systems.
