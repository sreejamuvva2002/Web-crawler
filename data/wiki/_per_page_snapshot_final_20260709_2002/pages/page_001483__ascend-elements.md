# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-18

**Source:** [Ascend Elements’ EV battery recycling facility receives certification - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/ascend-elements-ev-battery-recycling-facility-receives-certification/)  ·  `page_001483`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a Westborough, Massachusetts-based provider of sustainable, closed-loop battery material solutions, has been certified to the ISO 9001:2015 standard for quality management systems at its electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The certification was issued by DQS Inc., an independent certification body.
- Andrew Aberdale, Ascend Elements Chief Financial Officer and Chief Operating Officer, stated that the ISO certification signals a commitment to quality, customer service, and rigorous safety procedures.
- The Covington facility is one of the largest EV battery recycling facilities in North America.
- The facility has an annual processing capacity of 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and scrap.
- The site occupies 154,000 square feet in Newton County.
- Operational capabilities include battery discharge and disassembly, battery shredding, lithium extraction, and analytical testing.
- From recycled EV batteries, the facility commercially produces lithium carbonate, nickel-manganese-cobalt precursor (pCAM), and cathode active materials (CAM).
