# DOE OCED eXCHANGE Teaming Partners Directory

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DOE: OCED eXCHANGE: Teaming Partners](https://oced-exchange.energy.gov/TeamingPartners.aspx?foaid=94d41c37-fe4e-47d7-b06c-d3ce6e62d560)  ·  `page_003398`

## Facts

- ABB Inc is a large business focused on carbon capture, storage, and transport, with over 105,000 employees globally.
- ABB offers smart sensing devices and solutions across chemical, oil & gas, water, power generation, cement, pulp & paper, marine, hydrogen, and battery industries through its Measurement & Analytical division.
- ABB's US footprint includes a manufacturing facility and R&D site in Bartlesville, OK, and a Corporate Research team in Raleigh, NC.
- ABB is interested in partnering on point source carbon capture technology and CO2 transport and storage (Topics 1, 2 & 3 of DE-FOA-0003473).
- Imperial College London operates a pilot plant equipped with over 250 ABB instruments and sensors to advance carbon capture technology research.
- The Emissions Capture Company Group, LTD (ECCO) is a UK-based large business commercializing technologies that capture industrial emissions and waste into valuable chemicals, with US operations in Texas and South Carolina.
- ECCO is interested in developing technologies at laboratory, pilot, and full scales based on economic viability from selling chemicals and biomass.
- ECCO's current focus areas include waste-to-products from natural gas refining (CO2 conversion, H2S pyrolysis, Methane pyrolysis), ammonia synthesis from air and water, brewery waste to organic fertilizer conversion, and AI/ML in technology development.
- ECCO has capabilities for process architecture design through detailed engineering and project execution.
- ECCO's current scaled project in Texas captures waste CO2 and converts it into baking soda, with numerous proposed sites in rural Energy Communities.
- Energy Systems Network is a non-profit focused on grid industrial energy efficiency via smart manufacturing tools.
- Its Energy Insights program helps manufacturers understand energy consumption using IIOT sensors and standard smart manufacturing SCADA systems.
- The State of Indiana provides funding for the program, which includes an Energy Monitoring and Smart Manufacturing Starter Kit, a base project for data visualization and analysis, system integrator services, and a 4-part, 12-hour training course.
- Texas A&M University Professor Sheng-Jen Hsieh directs the Rockwell Automation Laboratory and researches industrial control, cyber-physical systems, machine learning, Industry 4.0, optical/infrared imaging, and automation/robotics education.
- Dr. Hsieh has been awarded over $6.0M in research funding, resulting in over 200 publications, and serves as an Associate Editor for Industrial Robot and Editorial Board member for Assembly Automation and Sensor Review.
- University of Minnesota Research Program Manager Matthew Aro's team has expertise in manufacturing carbon-sequestering biochar at lab- and pilot-scales and analyzing it for water treatment, concrete/building materials, steel production reducing agent, and pollution remediation.
- The University of Minnesota team modifies biochar to impart new properties, analyzes performance of new materials incorporating biochar, and conducts community demonstrations of new biochar-containing materials.
- Southern Methodist University Professor Saeed Salehi holds the Herman Brown Endowed Chair of Engineering and researches carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) related to cement and concrete.
- Dr. Salehi's CCUS expertise includes CO2 capture technologies, carbonation in concrete, sustainable cement alternatives, CO2 mineralization, life cycle assessment, policy/regulation, and pilot/commercial scale implementation.
- Dr. Salehi has served as PI/Co-PI on R&D projects totaling over $10 million funded by the DOE, Department of the Interior, and energy industry companies.
- World Resources Institute Senior Manager Dr. Ankita Gangotra's team conducts analysis and policy development to accelerate decarbonization in chemicals, refining, cement, and steel sectors.
- WRI published first-of-a-kind analysis on using Low Carbon Fuels, Feedstocks and Energy Sources (LCFFES) to defossilize chemicals and evaluated clean hydrogen capacity needs for fuel/feedstock switching.
- WRI's current work focuses on developing data disclosure and demand-side policy recommendations to incentivize low-emission chemicals development.
- WRI co-convened the Industrial Innovation Initiative (i3) over four years, convening industrial companies, NGOs, and labor organizations to develop consensus-based industrial decarbonization policies.
- WRI participates in the World Economic Forum industrial clusters project and collaborates on hydrogen demand convenings, alongside involvement with the Bipartisan Policy Center and American Petroleum Institute roundtables.
- Rocky Mountain Institute (RMI) is a nonprofit founded in 1982 focused on catalyzing market-based change to align critical geographies with a 1.5°C future.
- RMI works to cut greenhouse gas emissions at least 50% by 2030 through decarbonizing energy production and use across power, transportation, buildings, and industry sectors.
- RMI's Cement Initiative supports sector decarbonization via research, policy measures, industry coalitions, and public commitments to the 1.5°C target, with past work including technoeconomic analysis for CEMEX.
- The RMI team has prior experience on federally and philanthropically funded projects amounting to over $560M, covering business cases, community benefits, and technical components.
- RMI's expertise includes electricity generation, hydrogen, transportation, steel, market adoption, SCMs, alternative heat sources, CCUS, and alternative binders.
- Potential collaboration areas with RMI include technoeconomic analysis, facilitating demand aggregation, developing supply chains, and supporting policy design.
- Industrial Microbes is a small business building new microbes for sustainable chemical production to accelerate decarbonization in the chemical industry.
- The company specializes in fermentation and downstream processes that compete with petrochemicals on price, focusing on decarbonizing building-block chemicals like 3HP for acrylic acid production.
- Industrial Microbes partners with chemical producers to co-develop and scale bio-manufacturing processes, leveraging microbial engineering expertise to bring sustainable products to market.
- Metals Innovation Initiative (MI2) is a Kentucky-based nonprofit providing collaborative, industry-led executive leadership to attract advanced research, sustainability, commercialization, and talent development in Kentucky's metals industry.
- Kentucky's metals industry includes more than 250 facilities employing over 36,000 people statewide.
- MI2 was formed in 2022 by eight Kentucky metals companies (Kobe Aluminum Automotive Products, Logan Aluminum, North American Stainless, Novelis, Nucor, River Metals Recycling, Tri-Arrows Aluminum, and Wieland) with support from the Kentucky Governor and Cabinet for Economic Development.
- MI2 is administered through the Kentucky Science and Technology Corporation and originated from a whitepaper by AccelerateKY based on MIT Regional Entrepreneurship Acceleration Program principles.
- Stevens Institute of Technology Associate Professor Weina Meng holds an NSF CAREER award and researches sustainable construction materials using industrial by-products like slag, fly ash, waste concrete, and biomass in cement/concrete applications.
- Dr. Meng's work aims to mitigate environmental impact while enhancing material performance through CO2 sequestration, waste utilization, rapid surface modification technologies for solid wastes, and multiscale experimentation with computational modeling and machine learning.
- PHNX Materials, INC is a small business refining 2.5B tons of US landfilled coal ash into valuable end-products like ASTM-grade ash, titanium, aluminum, and rare-earth elements.
- PHNX Materials completed a pre-seed round raising $2.45M to produce high-quality supplementary cementitious materials (SCMs) from landfilled coal fly-ash.
- The company has developed technology to strip major impurities from landfilled CFA and formed partnerships with 5 utilities providing landfilled CFA for processing.
- PHNX Materials is interested in decarbonizing cement and producing high-quality metal feedstocks.
- University of Miami Assistant Professor Sivakumar Ramanathan specializes in low carbon concrete materials, including SCMs, SCM reactivity, recycled concrete aggregate, CO2 sequestration in concrete, durability, cellulose nanomaterials, and biochar.
- Auburn University Professor Jeyhoon's group studies transport phenomena in energy-intensive processing industries (metals, cement/concrete, chemicals), including mathematical/physical modeling of tundish liquid aluminum flows, continuous steel casters, thermophysical properties, and latent heat thermal energy storage systems from 250-1500 oF.
- Dr. Jeyhoon's grant sponsors include the Alabama Space Grant Consortium, ALCOA, California Energy Commission, DOE NREL, NSF, ONR, NASA, and US Forest Service.
- Ozinga is a 5th generation American family-owned construction material business founded in 1928, operating over 1000 mixer trucks from 100 plants across the Midwest (IL, IN, WI, MI) and Florida.
- Ozinga is a leader in developing low-carbon construction materials, including converting transport fuel to renewable natural gas and growing alternative SCMs to reduce carbon impact.
- ONB Engineering offers techno-economic analysis, lifecycle assessment, and market readiness validation for carbon management and industrial decarbonization technologies using advanced custom modeling tools.
- ONB Engineering specializes in technical feasibility assessment, reactor/process level modeling, R&D assessment, comprehensive techno-economic/LCA assessments, and engineering package planning (FEL 1-3).
- The company utilizes commercial computational tools like Aspen Plus, DWSIM, Barracuda, and Matlab, and its engineers hold PE, PMP, and Six Sigma certifications.
- University of Miami Associate Professor Prannoy Suraneni is a worldwide expert on concrete decarbonization, SCMs, and cements, with expertise in mechanochemical activation, thermal activation, and CO2 uptake for novel cement manufacturing.
- Dr. Suraneni's lab has tested over 1000 different materials for SCM feasibility and developed numerous test methods to rapidly measure SCM reactivity linked to strength and durability.
- The University of Miami Civil and Architectural Engineering Department has five other faculty members working on concrete decarbonization, with expertise spanning molecular dynamics to large-scale testing and life-cycle analysis.
- AggrePlex LLC produces PozzoDyne™, an Activated Ground Glass Pozzolan made from diverted landfill waste glass that serves as an OPC alternative and mitigates Alkali-Silica Reaction.
- PozzoDyne™ is derived from finely ground waste glass (<20 microns), reduces carbon emissions by 92% per ton compared to traditional cement, and lowers concrete global warming potential by up to 20%.
- AggrePlex plans to establish 100,000-ton/year AGGP facilities in Florida, California, Georgia, Texas, Pennsylvania, and Illinois, processing post-consumer container glass and expanding to windshield, solar panel, and fiberglass glass.
- AggrePlex leadership includes CEO Kevin Crutchfield, President Anthony Cialone, EVP James Burnham, CTO Josef Fischer, Director of Business Development Herb Northrop, Director of Plant Operations David Petroni, and Director of LEAN Manufacturing & Quality Production Koren Wah.
- The company aims to increase glass recovery rates from 25% to 75%, create high-paying technical jobs, and support sustainability throughout the United States.
- Lakril Technologies Corporation is a small business focused on industrial decarbonization through petrochemical disruption using bio-based sugars as the backbone for high-volume chemicals.
