# Hyundai Transys

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-03

**Source:** [Hyundai Plans $240m Transmission Plant – City Business News](https://citybusinessnews.com/hyundai-plans-240m-transmission-plant/)  ·  `page_009588`

## Facts

- Hyundai Transys, a South Korean auto parts supplier, will invest more than $240 million to build a transmission factory in western Georgia.
- The new plant plans to hire 678 workers.
- The factory is located in West Point, adjoining an existing Hyundai Transys factory and a Kia Motors Corp factory.
- Both facilities are part of Hyundai Motor Group, the Korean auto conglomerate that sells cars under the Kia and Hyundai brands.
- The new plant will build eight-speed transmissions.
- The facility will be 620,000 square feet (58,000-square meters).
- The plant will be part of the Kia campus just off Interstate 85 in West Point near the state line with Alabama.
- Hyundai Transys was created by a merger of two Hyundai affiliates last year.
- Hyundai Transys also makes axles and vehicle seats.
- The company has operations in South Korea, the United States, and seven other countries.
- The investment will create 165 additional jobs.
- Once the new plant is complete, Hyundai Transys's total employees in Georgia will rise above 1,700.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated he was thrilled about the long-term investment and the new jobs being created.
- Many supplier plants are clustered near the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and the Kia plant in West Point, with others scattered around and between the two plants.
- According to county financial documents, Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers had more than 4,300 employees in Georgia’s Troup County in 2018.
- This is the largest industrial announcement by value of investment made so far this year in Georgia.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung stated that years of capital investment, employment, training support, and unmatched opportunities made Georgia a natural choice.
- Final incentives for Hyundai Transys have not been agreed on yet, according to Marie Hodge Gordon, spokesperson for the Georgia Economic Development Department.
- The company will qualify for a state income tax credit allowing it to deduct $3,500 per job from its Georgia income taxes for five years.
- If Hyundai Transys does not incur $2.37 million in state income taxes each year, it can use the credit to receive cash payments redirected from money withheld from employee paychecks for state income taxes, due to its location in a less-developed area.
- Workers must make at least $28,000 a year for Hyundai Transys to qualify for tax credits.
- The state will pay to train Hyundai Transys workers.
- The state could contribute money to pay for infrastructure.
- Hyundai will automatically qualify for tax breaks that will save it millions in sales and use taxes on machinery.
- Local officials could abate property taxes.
- West Point’s 2018 financial statements show that Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers were receiving $2.85 million in property tax breaks from the city alone.
