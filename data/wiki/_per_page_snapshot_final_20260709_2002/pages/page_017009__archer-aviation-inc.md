# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-31

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Showcases Midnight Aircraft In Atlanta As It Nears Completion Of Its High-Volume Manufacturing Facility In Covington, Georgia | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/archer-aviation-showcases-midnight-aircraft-atlanta-it-nears-completion-its-high)  ·  `page_017009`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is constructing a nearly 400,000 square foot high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- Construction of the Covington facility is on track to be completed by the end of 2024.
- The first build phase, inclusive of grading and foundation work, was completed earlier this year.
- Phase two, involving the erection of exterior walls and roof, is nearing completion.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- The manufacturing facility is located adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Once complete, the facility will support production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer is planning to mass-produce the Midnight aircraft in partnership with Stellantis.
- Archer recently showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport.
- The showcase event included guests from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- Adam Goldstein serves as CEO and founder of Archer Aviation.
- CEO Adam Goldstein stated that thanks to the hard work of the Archer and Stellantis teams, they are tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year and will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year, aiming to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- Midnight is an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to travel at speeds up to 150 mph.
- It is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The aircraft operates at 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes.
- Midnight features redundant systems targeting safety levels similar to commercial airliners.
- The aircraft is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
