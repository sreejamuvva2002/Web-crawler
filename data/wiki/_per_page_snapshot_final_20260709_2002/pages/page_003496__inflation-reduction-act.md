# Inflation Reduction Act

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-23

**Source:** [U.S. Department of the Treasury Releases New Data on Georgia Consumer Energy Savings Under Inflation Reduction Act | U.S. Department of the Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2524)  ·  `page_003496`

## Facts

- In 2023, more than 82,000 Georgia families saved more than $148 million on clean energy and energy efficiency investments.
- The U.S. Department of the Treasury released new data from the IRS and analysis by the Office of Economic Policy showing that more than 82,000 Georgia families benefitted from more than $148 million in tax credits to lower the costs of clean energy and energy efficiency upgrades to their homes during 2023.
- Today’s announcement marks the first public release of data from 2023 tax filings showing the benefit of the Inflation Reduction Act’s clean energy tax incentives for consumers.
- Nationwide, the number of families benefiting from expanded credits for investments that lower their utility bills increased almost one third compared to tax year 2021, prior to passage of the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The aggregate value of the credits increased by almost two thirds compared to tax year 2021.
- Recent research from U.S. national labs and analysis from Treasury’s Office of Economic Policy shows that after consumers adopt these upgrades, they can save hundreds or thousands of dollars per year on their utility bills, depending on the upgrades made to their homes.
- Households installing residential solar have saved a median of $2,230 annually.
- Households that install efficient heat pumps and improve building efficiency are expected to save up to $600, $1,200, or $3,100 per year, based on the type of heating and cooling system that is being replaced.
- U.S. Secretary of the Treasury Janet L. Yellen stated the Inflation Reduction Act is advancing the goal of lowering costs for American families by making home energy upgrades more affordable and cutting monthly utility bills.
- Senator Reverend Warnock stated that investments in clean energy will help Georgia families save money and that he fought to pass the IRA to ensure historic tax credits for Georgia.
- Representative Nikema Williams (GA-05) stated the IRA is the largest ever federal investment to combat the climate crisis and is already paying dividends for Georgians by lowering costs for hardworking families.
- Georgia families have claimed more than $97 million in credits for residential clean energy investments on 2023 tax returns filed and processed through May 23, 2024.
- The residential clean energy credit helps families afford investments in solar electricity generation, solar water heating, and battery storage, among others.
- Families have also claimed more than $51 million for energy efficient home improvements—which include heat pumps, efficient air conditioners, insulation, windows, and doors—during the same time span.
- These estimates are expected to increase as additional returns are filed and processed.
- Nationwide, more than 750,000 families claimed investments in residential solar electricity.
- Nationwide, more than 250,000 families claimed investments in electric or natural gas heat pumps.
- Nationwide, more than 100,000 families claimed investments in heat pump water heaters.
- Nationwide, nearly 700,000 families claimed investments in insulation and air sealing.
- The Inflation Reduction Act provides incentives for consumers to lower their energy bills and shield themselves from future spikes in fossil energy prices.
- The IRA extended and expanded the Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit, providing a total credit of up to $1,200 annually for investments like installing insulation or efficient windows and doors.
- There is a special credit of as much as $2,000 for electric heat pumps that provide super-efficient heating and cooling.
- The IRA provides a $150 credit for a home energy audit conducted by an inspector to help determine the best home improvement options.
- The IRA extended the Residential Clean Energy Credit, ensuring that households will be able to continue receiving a tax credit to cover up to 30% of the costs of installing, among others, rooftop solar and battery storage through 2034.
