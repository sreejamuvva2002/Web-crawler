# Amara Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-05

**Source:** [Amara Industries Acquires Injection Molder Thomson Plastics | Design 2 Part Magazine](https://www.d2pmagazine.com/2026/02/05/amara-industries-acquires-injection-molder-thomson-plastics/)  ·  `page_011252`

## Facts

- Amara Industries, a parent company to the Gracious Living group of companies, recently acquired Thomson Plastics.
- Thomson Plastics is a large tonnage injection molding company in the Southeastern United States.
- The acquisition enhances Amara Industries’ manufacturing footprint, automation capabilities, and technical depth.
- The acquisition strengthens Amara Industries' position as a key player in the North American injection molding industry.
- Thomson Plastics currently operates from locations in Georgia and North Carolina.
- Thomson Plastics is an ISO 9001:2015 and IATF 16949:2016 certified manufacturer of custom plastic injection molded and assembly products.
- Thomson Plastics' injection molding machines have clamping force capacities ranging up to 3,700 tons.
- Thirty percent of Thomson Plastics' injection molding machine fleet exceeds 1,000 tons in capacity.
- Thomson Plastics’ technical portfolio includes injection molding, gas-assisted molding, over-molding, and insert molding.
- Thomson Plastics offers in-mold labeling, robotic part picking, pad printing, and heat transfer decorating.
- The unified company is expected to help meet rising demand for high-performance molded products in the automotive, consumer goods industrial, and commercial markets.
- Thomson Plastics' approach aligns closely with Amara Industries’ long-term vision for operational excellence and innovation.
- Vince Orlando, co-president at Amara Industries, stated that the addition of Thomson Plastics represents a strategic milestone for the organization.
- Vince Orlando noted that advanced molding capabilities, technical expertise, and a culture of innovation will enhance the company's ability to serve customers with scale, speed, and precision across North America.
- Amara Industries specializes in injection molding, assembly, profile extrusion, decorating, and material compounding.
- Amara Industries' end-to-end capabilities span design, product development, engineering, and materials science.
- The acquisition expands Amara Industries' footprint into the Southeastern United States.
- The expansion is expected to provide customers with faster delivery, greater flexibility, and improved supply chain efficiency.
