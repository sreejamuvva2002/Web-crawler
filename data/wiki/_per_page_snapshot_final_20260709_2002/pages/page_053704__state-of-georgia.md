# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The State of Georgia: What Sets it Apart | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/state-georgia-what-sets-it-apart-9734)  ·  `page_053704`

## Facts

- Georgia was named America’s Best State for Business by CNBC.
- The automotive industry in Georgia generated a significant increase in jobs and investment (35 percent/149 percent).
- Foreign direct investment increased by 27 percent in the 2014 fiscal year.
- Georgia’s workforce is more diverse, educated, and younger than the national average, making it ideal for companies seeking to flourish and grow.
- Georgia Tech is ranked No. 7 on the 2013 U.S. News and World Report college rankings, and the University of Georgia is ranked No. 20.
- Georgia universities and colleges award more than 78,000 degrees and certificates each year to prepare graduates with skills for company needs.
- Emory University ranks in the top 20 U.S. undergraduate schools, while the Georgia Institute of Technology and University of Georgia rank in the top 30.
- The Technical College System of Georgia comprises 23 colleges that graduated more than 34,000 students last year, with almost 90 percent either employed or continuing their education.
- Georgia Quick Start, a specialized workforce training program, is ranked No. 1 in the nation.
- Quick Start helped create and retain more than 12,000 jobs through 133 training programs in the 2013 fiscal year.
- For over 40 years, Quick Start has delivered customized training via classroom, mobile labs, or on the plant floor in areas like quality control, FDA regulatory compliance, and OSHA standards for advanced manufacturing, biotechnology, and pharmaceutical sectors.
- In March, a groundbreaking was held for the Georgia Bioscience Training Center, a state-sponsored facility solely dedicated to preparing bioscience and biomanufacturing talent, operated by Quick Start with state-of-the-art labs and equipment to meet training needs for Baxter and future biotech companies.
- Seventeen of Georgia’s Fortune 500 companies can access 80 percent of the U.S. market using Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- Georgia’s transportation network includes 5,000 miles of rail (the most extensive in the Southeast) and the largest intermodal facility on the East Coast, connecting to 80 percent of the U.S. market in less than two days.
- Georgia’s deepwater ports host more than 40 shipping service lines, provide access to 20 percent of the nation’s population, with 44 percent within easy reach of inland connectivity infrastructure.
- More than 250 distribution centers in Georgia utilize the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Georgia’s logistics network boasts more than 11,000 logistics providers employing more than one million logistics-connected employees.
- Logistics and distribution industries in Georgia experienced a 152 percent increase in investment in the 2014 fiscal year.
- The state has 70 facilities with temperature-controlled and frozen storage capabilities to meet cold chain supply demands.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) employs a team of economic developers who live and work in communities across the state to attract new jobs and investment.
- The GRAD (Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development) Program has provided advanced due diligence for industrial development for eight projects across the state and continues developing market-ready sites.
- GDEcD maintains international trade representatives in 11 markets covering 37 countries, with new representatives announced for Mexico and Colombia this year.
- In January 2014, Governor Nathan Deal announced the High Demand Career Initiative to unite leadership from GDEcD, the University System of Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia, and key business leaders to address workforce gaps.
- Leading global companies in Georgia such as KIA, Southwire, and Decostar Industries are working with community colleges and high schools to create apprenticeships and study programs for talent training and recruitment.
