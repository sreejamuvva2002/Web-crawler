# Milliken & Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-21

**Source:** [Milliken releases 2024 Sustainability Report - NAUMD, Network Association of Uniform Manufacturers & Distributors, a global network](https://naumd.com/milliken-releases-2024-sustainability-report/)  ·  `page_046692`

## Facts

- Milliken & Company released its seventh annual sustainability report disclosing performance against its 2025 Sustainability Goals and 2030 net-zero targets.
- Halsey Cook, president and CEO at Milliken, stated that sustainability is at the core of Milliken’s business strategy and highlighted progress made with associates, customers, and partners.
- Milliken reduced lost-time incidents in 2024 by focusing on back-to-basics priorities.
- Milliken achieved its goal of 100,000 volunteer hours a full year ahead of schedule and exceeded its original 2025 volunteer goal by an additional 31,000 hours.
- In 2024, Milliken evaluated 100% of new products using sustainability assessments, up from a 22% baseline reported in 2018.
- Milliken became the first textile manufacturer to offer non-PFAS materials for all three layers of firefighter turnout gear.
- Milliken’s N/XT Life™ program and partnership with The Recycling Partnership’s Center for Sustainable Behavior and Impact demonstrated support of circular economy concepts.
- Milliken surpassed its 2025 renewable energy goal in 2022 and continues to explore solutions to expand use of renewable energy to reduce emissions.
- Milliken’s continued focus on thermal drying biosolids from wastewater treatment and the elimination of coal ash from steam generation contributed to waste reduction.
- In 2024, Milliken optimized wastewater treatment processes and targeted various waste streams across its manufacturing locations, leading to a decrease in waste intensity compared to its 2018 baseline.
- Milliken’s 2024 scope 1 and 2 emissions were reduced 42% from its 2018 baseline, nearing the company’s 2030 target of a 50% reduction.
- Since 2018, Milliken has invested more than $35 million in coal elimination with cogeneration, energy efficiency, increased renewable electricity, and modernizing manufacturing operations.
- Milliken’s efforts to reduce scope 3 emissions include supplier engagement, improving product sustainability data, and sourcing lower-carbon materials.
- Milliken reaffirmed its commitment to the U.N. Global Compact, pledging to uphold the Ten Principles that focus on human rights, labor standards, environmental protection, and anti-corruption.
- Kasel Knight, SVP, chief legal officer and head of sustainability at Milliken, stated the 2024 Sustainability Report reflects progress and demonstrates commitment to driving positive change.
