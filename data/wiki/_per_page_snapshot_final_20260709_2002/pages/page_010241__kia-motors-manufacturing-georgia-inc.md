# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-11-05

**Source:** [News Archives - Page 6 of 18 - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/category/news/page/6/)  ·  `page_010241`

## Facts

- On Nov. 5, 2015, over 500 local fifth graders joined more than 40 KMMG team members at the Convention Center at AmericasMart in Atlanta for the largest national showcase yet for the SAE Foundation’s A World in Motion® (AWIM) program.
- Students from Troup County participated in the AWIM program showcase hosted by KMMG.
- On Sept. 29, 2015, KMMG team members celebrated the start of production of the all-new 2016 Kia Optima with a ceremony inside the plant’s General Assembly shop.
- The 2016 Kia Optima is another in a growing line of successful launches for the KMMG plant.
- On Aug. 14, 2015, KMMG provided $1.5 million in combined funding to the City of West Point ($900,000) and THINC College and Career Academy ($600,000).
- KMMG considers the City of West Point and THINC College and Career Academy vital partners.
- On July 10, 2015, KMMG provided $25,000 to the Troup County Board of Tax Assessors as part of the plant’s $400,000 overall commitment to the board.
- The funds provided to the Troup County Board of Tax Assessors will be used for additional manpower at the board’s discretion.
- On May 20, 2015, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia was named winner of the fifth annual SAE Foundation Gordon Millar Award.
- Randy Jackson, KMMG’s Senior Vice President of Human Resources and Administration, accepted the award on behalf of the plant.
- The SAE Foundation Gordon Millar Award is funded by AVL.
- On April 23, 2015, KMMG awarded $1.2 million to the SAE Foundation in support of its efforts to advance science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) education through SAE International’s award-winning A World In Motion® (AWIM) program and ensure the technical competency of the future talent pool.
- On Dec. 23, 2014, KMMG team members donated more than 500 toys to the U.S. Marine Corps League in LaGrange.
- On Dec. 22, 2014, KMMG made contributions totaling $10,000 to Troup County Schools, Harris County Schools, Lanett City Schools, Springwood School and LaGrange Academy to support robotics programs in area schools.
- On Nov. 18, 2014, KMMG presented its third $300,000 contribution ($900,000 total) to the SAE Foundation that will be used to continue impacting science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) education for Troup County Schools in 2015.
- On Nov. 17, 2014, KMMG team members celebrated the fifth anniversary of the plant’s official start of mass production.
- The first U.S.-assembled Kia vehicle – a white 2011 model year Sorento CUV – rolled off KMMG’s assembly line on Nov. 16, 2009.
