# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-30

**Source:** [Wabash Announces First Quarter 2025 Results | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/wabash-announces-first-quarter-2025-results-2025-04-30)  ·  `page_014671`

## Facts

- Quarterly revenue was $381 million, lower than the prior quarterly outlook range due to weaker Transportation Solutions demand.
- Parts & Services generated positive revenue growth both sequentially and year-over-year.
- GAAP operating income was $315 million, while Non-GAAP adjusted operating loss was $27.4 million.
- Results exclude the impact of a $342 million gain in connection with a reduced legal verdict.
- Quarterly GAAP EPS was $5.36, or $(0.58) on a Non-GAAP adjusted basis.
- Weaker demand fill led to inflated direct labor costs during the quarter, which have now been right-sized.
- Total backlog stood at $1.2 billion ending Q1 2025.
- Tariff-related uncertainty is causing customers to delay equipment investment decisions.
- The company reduced its 2025 revenue outlook to $1.8 billion and its Non-GAAP adjusted EPS outlook to $(0.60), excluding the impact of the legal verdict gain.
- Net sales for the first quarter of 2025 were $380.9 million, reflecting a 26.1% decrease compared to the same quarter of the previous year.
- The company generated consolidated gross profit of $19.0 million, equivalent to 5.0% of sales.
- GAAP operating profit amounted to $314.6 million as the company recognized a $342 million gain in connection with the reduction of a legal verdict.
- Non-GAAP adjusted operating loss was $27.4 million for the quarter, representing (7.2)% of sales.
- As of March 31, 2025, total company backlog stood at approximately $1.2 billion, a sequential increase of 5% from year-end 2024 and a decrease of 32% compared to the first quarter of 2024.
- New order activity remained modest during the first quarter.
- Brent Yeagy serves as president and chief executive officer.
- Wabash's manufacturing footprint and supply base are both heavily levered to the United States, positioning the company to avoid direct impact from tariffs.
- Second-order tariff effects have been meaningful in the short-term as customers have reduced capital expenditure plans until their own customers' have greater clarity.
- Demand in 2025 is currently projected to undercut replacement levels, resulting in an aging of the fleet which will require catch-up in coming years.
- The administration's activities to leverage a revitalization of U.S. manufacturing could be meaningfully positive for trucking and specifically trailer demand longer term.
- Wabash designs, manufactures, and services dry freight and refrigerated trailers, flatbed trailers, tank trailers, dry and refrigerated truck bodies, structural composite panels and products, trailer aerodynamic solutions, and specialty food grade processing equipment.
- Transportation Solutions generated net sales of $346.8 million in the first quarter, a decrease of 26.3% compared to the same quarter of the previous year.
- Operating loss for the Transportation Solutions segment amounted to $9.8 million, representing (2.8)% of sales.
- Parts & Services net sales for the first quarter were $52.0 million, an increase of 5.5% compared to the prior year quarter.
- Operating income for the Parts & Services segment amounted to $6.9 million, or 13.3% of sales.
- Wabash is headquartered in Lafayette, Indiana.
- Wabash will discuss its first quarter 2025 results during a quarterly investor conference call on Wednesday, April 30, 2025, beginning at 12:00 p.m. EDT.
