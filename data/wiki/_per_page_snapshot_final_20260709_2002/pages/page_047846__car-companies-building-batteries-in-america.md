# Car Companies Building Batteries in America

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Which Car Companies Are Building Batteries in America | U.S. News](https://cars.usnews.com/cars-trucks/features/which-car-companies-are-building-batteries-in-america)  ·  `page_047846`

## Facts

- President Biden signed the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), which includes new credits for purchases of clean energy vehicles and additional credits to automakers for producing batteries in America.
- BMW broke ground on a new battery assembly plant in Woodruff, South Carolina named 'Plant Woodruff' in June of this year.
- Plant Woodruff will produce next-generation batteries for use in the German brand's electric vehicles built nearby in Spartanburg, South Carolina.
- More than 300 jobs will be added with this new plant.
- Construction of the facility is part of a $1.7 billion investment BMW announced last year, with $700 million going towards Plant Woodruff and the additional $1 billion earmarked to prepare the company's Spartanburg facility for the production of fully-electric BMWs.
- Ford is investing $3.5 billion to build a lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) battery plant in Marshall, Michigan called BlueOval Battery Park Michigan.
- The new facility will employ 2,500 people when battery production starts in 2026.
- Ford has also partnered with SK On to build battery production facilities in Tennessee and Kentucky under the name BlueOval SK.
- Two plants are under construction in Glendale, Kentucky, which will ultimately produce more than 80 gigawatt hours of lithium-ion batteries, with production expected to start in 2025.
- GM has built or is planning at least four battery production facilities in America.
- Three battery-production facilities are the result of a joint venture between GM and LG Energy called Ultium Cells.
- The first is a 2.8-million-square-foot factory in Warren, OH, that is already up and running, producing batteries for the Hummer EV and Cadillac Lyriq.
- A similar plant will be built in Spring Hill, Tennessee, where the Lyriq is made.
- A third plant will be located in Lansing, Michigan.
- GM has also formed a new partnership with Samsung SDI to build a fourth facility – a $3 billion plant will be built in St. Joseph County, Indiana, creating 1,700 new jobs when it begins production in 2026.
- Honda has also formed a joint venture with LG Energy Solutions to produce EV batteries at a new plant near Jeffersonville, Ohio.
- Groundbreaking at the 2 million square foot facility took place earlier this year after the two companies committed to investing up to $4.4 billion towards this venture.
- The new battery factory is slated to be complete by the end of 2024 and is expected to create 2,200 jobs.
- Honda anticipates the new facility will be able to deliver up to 40 GWh of batteries annually with the start of mass production by the end of 2025.
- The lithium-ion batteries built in this facility will be used exclusively by Honda in new electric vehicles built in North America.
- The Hyundai Motor Group, including the Hyundai, Genesis and Kia brands, has formed a joint venture to build EV batteries with South Korean battery maker SK On.
- With a total investment of $5 billion, the two companies are building a new production facility in Bartow County, Georgia.
- This new plant is expected to go online by the second-half of 2025.
- The plant will eventually produce 35 GWh of batteries annually, enough to power 300,000 EVs.
- The new plant will be conveniently located near the Hyundai Group U.S. production facilities in Alabama and Georgia and will support the South Korean brand's plans for EV production in America.
- California-based Lucid is presently sourcing its batteries from Panasonic with the lithium-ion cells produced in Japan.
- Panasonic has announced plans to build a new facility in De Soto, Kansas expected to be one of the largest in the world, supplying batteries to Lucid and other car brands.
- Mercedes-Benz builds the all-new EQE SUV and EQS SUV at its production facility in Tuscaloosa, Alabama.
- To support the battery requirements of these new electric models, Mercedes-Benz has built a new battery production facility in nearby Bibb County, Alabama.
- Ola Källenius, Chairman of the Board of Management of Mercedes-Benz Group AG, stated that opening their new battery plant in Alabama is a major milestone on their way to going all-electric and emphasized the importance of local cell sourcing and recycling strategy in the US market.
- Nissan built a battery production facility next to its current vehicle assembly plant in Smyrna, Tennessee shortly after launching the Leaf in 2010.
- With plans to greatly expand its EV offering with a goal of 19 new EVs by 2030, Nissan is looking to build a second battery plant, likely near its Canton, Mississippi facility.
- This facility is expected to build new Nissan and Infiniti EVs starting in 2025.
- Stellantis has formed a joint venture with Samsung SDI to build an electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Kokomo, Indiana.
- With a $2.5 billion investment, the new plant will bring 1,400 new jobs to the Kokomo area when it comes online in 2025.
- Initial production is expected to deliver 23 GWh of batteries annually, with a plan to increase the output to 33 GWh as demand for Stellantis electric vehicles increases.
- Tesla partnered with Panasonic around 20 years ago to supply Tesla automobiles with lithium-ion batteries.
- In 2014 Tesla and Panasonic began construction of the Gigafactory in Nevada, and now that factory provides the high-voltage battery packs for use in Tesla automobiles.
- Panasonic recently announced plans to invest around $10 billion to establish two new battery production facilities in the U.S., one of which will be a $4 billion facility in Kansas.
- Earlier this year, Toyota announced an additional $2.1 billion investment in its new battery production facility located in Liberty, North Carolina, bringing the total investment to $5.9 billion.
- This new battery facility is still under construction, with production expected to begin in 2025.
- The plant will have six battery production lines – four for hybrids and two for battery-electric vehicles.
