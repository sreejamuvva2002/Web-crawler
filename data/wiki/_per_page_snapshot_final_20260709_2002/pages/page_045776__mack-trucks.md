# Mack Trucks

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-09

**Source:** [Mack Trucks invests $14.5 million to expand Roanoke County facility](https://www.wdbj7.com/2024/02/09/mack-truck-spends-145-million-expand-roanoke-facility/)  ·  `page_045776`

## Facts

- Swedish-based Volvo Group invested $14.5 million to expand its facility in Roanoke County, Virginia.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced the investment on Friday.
- Mack Trucks plans to expand its production facility by 72,000 square feet.
- The expansion will create 51 new jobs.
- Mack Trucks plans to increase production for its medium-duty trucks.
- Mack Trucks plans to increase production for new electric truck lines.
- A groundbreaking ceremony was held for the facility expansion.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin stated that companies like Mack Trucks help with economic development advances.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin said, “This is about technology, and manufacturing, enabling productivity, and products that are really beyond anything we could have dreamed of five or 10 years ago.”
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership collaborated with Roanoke County and the Roanoke Regional Partnership to secure a $255,000 grant.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin approved the $255,000 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund.
- The grant funding will be used for employee training through the Virginia Jobs Investment Program.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin stated that Mack Trucks’ expansion further strengthens Virginia’s manufacturing industry ecosystem, which is a core focus of the Commonwealth’s economic development strategy.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin noted that Mack Trucks’ initial investment in a new Roanoke County operation four years ago has yielded this second major investment.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin described the project as another vote of confidence in Virginia by a global industry leader.
- Construction for the expanded operation is scheduled to begin in April.
