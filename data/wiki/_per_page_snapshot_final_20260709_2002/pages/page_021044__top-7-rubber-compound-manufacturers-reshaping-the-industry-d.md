## Facts

- Rubber products are prevalently used across construction, footwear, and automotive.
- Business Research Insights estimates the market for rubber compound to reach USD 9,545.6 million by 2026.
- Phoenix Compounding Technology GmbH is a Germany-based company incorporated in 2001 and headquartered in Hamburg.
- Phoenix Compounding Technology GmbH deals in synthetic rubbers, rubber compounds, testing systems, and inquiry compounds.
- The company claims it has more than 2,000 formulas for rubber compounds and uses state-of-the-art production methods and quality controls.
- It reportedly manufactures products on 27 mixing lines in Germany, China, Hungary, Mexico, and the U.S.
- Cooper Standard was founded in 1960 and is a leading materials science and manufacturing expert headquartered in Michigan, U.S.
- The company employs around 25,000 people globally and functions in 21 countries.
- Cooper Standard develops components, systems, and materials for a host of diverse markets.
- Cooper Standard has manufacturing locations in Surgoinsville, Tennessee; New Lexington, Ohio; and Ontario, Canada, among 107 manufacturing locations globally and 32 other manufacturing locations in North America.
- American Phoenix Inc. is a privately-held company established in 1992 that serves customers in the U.S.
- American Phoenix offers rubber mixing services, including a supply of runner compounds, and provides pre-weighted chemical packaging services for the rubber sector.
- The company has more than 400 employees with seven companies in the American Phoenix corporate family.
- HEXPOL Compounding is a Sweden-based company reportedly established in 1893 and is one of the leading suppliers in manufacturing and developing high-quality advanced polymer compounds.
- In July 2019, Hexpol announced buying Preferred Compounding in a USD 232 million deal.
- Preferred Compounding has six factories, one in Mexico and five in the U.S.
- TSRC was established in 1973 in Kaohsiung, Taiwan, by founder Mr. Glyn T.H. Ing.
- The Taiwan-headquartered company claims to be a leader in synthetic rubber and Styrenic Block Copolymer (SBC).
- TSRC is bolstering its business to boost commercial teams and production bases in the U.S., India, Vietnam, Thailand, and China (Nantong in Jiangsu and Song-Jiang in Shanghai).
- Dongguan New Orient Technology was founded in 2004 and manufactures and distributes plastic and rubber products.
- The China-based company produces high-temperature silicone rubbers, sealed silicone rubbers, mixing silicone rubbers, and accessory materials.
- The company’s annual production capacity of raw rubber is around 30,000 tons.
- Shin-Etsu Chemical is a chemical manufacturer headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, established in 1926.
- Shin-Etsu Chemical has manufacturing bases in 19 countries with 93 overseas sales bases.
- The company has upped investments in the Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), energy conservation, and renewable energy.
- HNBR, a hydrogenated version of NBR (nitrile) rubber, is sought-after in industrial, automotive, food, pharma, oil & gas industries, and medical applications.
- Major upsides for HNBR include high mechanical strength, tremendous media resistance, enhanced wear resistance, and tremendous heat & oxidation stability.
