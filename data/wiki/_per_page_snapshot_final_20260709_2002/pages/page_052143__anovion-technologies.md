# Anovion Technologies

**Source:** [Southwest Georgia: A Region on Radar - Development Authority of Bainbridge and Decatur County](https://www.bainbridgedecaturga.com/southwest-georgia-a-region-on-radar/)  ·  `page_052143`

## Facts

- Agriculture has defined and dominated Southwest Georgia’s economic landscape for generations.
- In 2021, food and fiber production and related processing industries directly represented a value of nearly $14.7 billion to Georgia’s economy, according to a report by the University of Georgia’s Center for Agriculture and Economic Development.
- The report cites data from the 2021 Georgia Farm Gate Value Report, which states these commodities represent $73.2 billion in output to Georgia’s economy.
- These agricultural commodities supported 340,837 jobs in industries in the agricultural supply chain in 2021 beyond their farm gate value.
- Southwest Georgia counties hold a lock on some of the top nationally ranked commodities in total production.
- The state ranks No. 1 in total peanut and pecan production.
- The state ranks No. 2 in cotton lint, cotton seed, and watermelon production.
- The state ranks No. 3 in blueberries and cantaloupe production.
- In 2021, more than half of the nation’s peanuts were grown in Southwest Georgia.
- Cotton ranks first in production value in Georgia and second in the nation.
- Anovion Technologies, an electric vehicle (EV) battery materials manufacturer, announced it will invest $800 million and create 400 jobs in Decatur County.
- The new manufacturing facility being built at the county-owned Downrange Industrial Park will be 1.5 million square feet.
- The facility will produce high-quality synthetic graphite anode, a material critical in the EV battery supply chain.
- Rick McCaskill, head of the Development Authority of Bainbridge and Decatur County, stated that $50 million was put into the park’s infrastructure as a major commitment.
- Anovion selected the Southwest Georgia location because of its proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources for power.
- The company also cited short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, a skilled workforce, and a business-friendly environment as selection factors.
- Anovion was looking at 38 states before choosing Bainbridge.
- Decatur County is a member of Locate South Georgia, a regional economic development entity comprised of 22 counties, many in Southwest Georgia.
- Locate South Georgia offers an annual bus tour covering a portion of the more than 10,000-square-mile region it serves to provide project managers a firsthand look at available sites and buildings.
- In November 2022, the Locate South Georgia tour included Decatur County.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development project manager saw the site during the tour and thought it might be a fit for the Anovion project.
- The request for proposal (RFP) was quickly sent and accepted after the site visit.
- Decatur County became Georgia’s newest member of Georgia’s EV battery ecosystem following the announcement.
- Before the Anovion announcement, Locate South Georgia touted $324.5 million in new investment and 575 jobs created in 2022 among its members.
- Grant Buckley, executive director of the Cordele-Crisp County Industrial Development Council and chair of Locate South Georgia, noted that the region may be starting to see EV-related spin-offs.
- Buckley reported hearing inquiries from Tier 2 or Tier 3 suppliers related to the EV industry among his colleagues.
- McCaskill emphasized that regional marketing efforts by Locate South Georgia and a mindset that projects help all counties have been a real economic development benefit.
