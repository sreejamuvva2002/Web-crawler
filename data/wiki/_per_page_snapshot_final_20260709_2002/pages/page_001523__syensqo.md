# Syensqo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [$850 million investment for manufacturing plant announced - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/850-million-investment-for-manufacturing-plant-announced/)  ·  `page_001523`

## Facts

- Construction has started on a new, battery-grade manufacturing facility in Augusta.
- Syensqo is building an EV manufacturing facility beside their existing plant, formerly known as Solvay Polymers.
- Cal Wray, President of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, stated that EV is one component but the project adds manufacturing and advanced manufacturing while supplying the EV sector.
- The material produced will be used by 456 other industries.
- The project represents growth of the local advanced manufacturing hub, providing value-added or higher manufacturing that pays higher wages.
- The facility is expected to create 100 full-time local manufacturing jobs that will require skilled labor.
- The facility’s products will support Georgia’s growing electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing ecosystem by providing material for more than five million EV batteries per year at full capacity.
- The project will create hundreds of jobs throughout the value chain.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is developing an e-mobility supply chain that will position the state as a leader in this industry for years to come.
- Gov. Brian Kemp expressed excitement for Syensqo to become a valued part of the ecosystem and the Augusta-Richmond County community.
- The total investment for the project is $850 million.
- The employment and financial impact will be felt across the seven-county region of Richmond, Columbia, Lincoln, Burke, and McDuffie counties in Georgia, and Aiken and Edgefield counties in South Carolina.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson attended the groundbreaking event.
- The City of Augusta and the Augusta Economic Development Authority have worked to invest millions of dollars in tax savings on this project.
- The facility will supply polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF).
- PVDF is used as a lithium-ion binder and separator coating in electric vehicle batteries.
- The Augusta plant will be the largest PVDF producer in North America.
- Mike Finelli, Chief Technology & Innovation Officer and Chief North America Officer at Syensqo, thanked the Georgia Department of Economic Development for their support in expanding the company's footprint in Augusta.
- The expansion enables the creation of new jobs, reinforces the local economy, and underscores the strategic importance of Augusta as a key player in the EV supply chain along the burgeoning Battery Belt.
- The project further enhances the Augusta area’s notoriety on the world stage as an attractive place to build a facility.
- Syensqo, formerly known as Solvay, is a worldwide known name.
- This will be the largest facility of its type in the U.S.
- Key suppliers in the EV industry from major producers of autos and batteries will know where Augusta is because they are likely getting the product from Syensqo.
- Syensqo is based in Belgium.
- The company’s U.S. headquarters is in Alpharetta.
- Along with the manufacturing facility in Augusta, the company supports 800 jobs in Georgia.
