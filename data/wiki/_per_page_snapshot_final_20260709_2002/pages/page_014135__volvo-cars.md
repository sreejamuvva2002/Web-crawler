# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-17

**Source:** [Volvo discontinues sales of the EX30 in the US - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2026/03/17/volvo-discontinues-sales-of-the-ex30-in-the-us/)  ·  `page_014135`

## Facts

- Volvo discontinues sales of the EX30 in the US after the 2026 model year.
- The EX30 was unveiled in June 2023 with an originally planned US market launch for summer 2024.
- The original US launch was postponed after the Biden administration imposed a 100 percent tariff on electric vehicles manufactured in China, which remained in place under the Trump administration.
- The EX30 was initially produced exclusively at Volvo’s Zhangjiakou plant in China.
- Volvo delayed most EX30 deliveries to the US until production launched in Ghent, Belgium in April 2025.
- Prior to the Belgian production launch, only a few units of the top-tier Twin Motor Performance model were shipped from China to the US.
- Since production started in Belgium, Volvo has served the US market alongside European countries.
- US dealers were informed that the EX30 will be withdrawn from the US market after the 2026 model year, including both standard and Cross Country versions.
- All existing orders for the EX30 will still be fulfilled.
- Production for the US market will cease after the summer of 2026.
- US dealers had until Friday, 20 March 2026, to place any final orders for the EX30.
- The EX30 was available in the US starting at $40,345 (including delivery charges), while in Germany it started at €38,490.
- Volvo Cars explained the decision as part of a “thorough evaluation of our business and operational strategies.”
- The decision is described as a “direct response to shifting market conditions and financial factors.”
- Key factors include the expiry of the US tax credit of $7,500 on 30 September 2025.
- Relaxed emissions regulations for internal combustion vehicles introduced by the Trump administration also contributed to the decision.
- When Volvo decided to produce the EX30 for the US market in Belgium, the applicable tariff rate was 2.5 percent.
- The US imposed an additional 25 percent tariff just as production and deliveries began from Belgium.
- The tariff rate was reduced to 15 percent on 1 August 2025 but remains six times higher than the original calculation.
- The discontinuation of EX30 sales in the US does not affect its availability in Europe, Canada, and Mexico.
- Volvo plans to continue selling the significantly larger EX90, produced in South Carolina, in the US.
- Volvo also plans to sell the upcoming EX60 in the US.
