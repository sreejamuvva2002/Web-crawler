# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-19

**Source:** [As workers train, Hyundai Metaplant salary picture takes shape | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2024/07/19/workers-train-hyundai-metaplant-salary-picture-takes-shape)  ·  `page_001179`

## Facts

- Hyundai Metaplant is a $7.56 billion electric vehicle factory rising from amid the pine forests of rural Bryan County, Georgia.
- More than 1,471 workers have already been hired as the manufacturer pushes to get the first car off the assembly line by December.
- Georgia and southeast coastal counties are subsidizing Hyundai and its suppliers with $2.1 billion in tax breaks, construction costs, discount land, and other perks.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the deal would produce good-paying jobs for hard-working Georgians.
- Hyundai Metaplant, the LG joint-venture battery facility, and five affiliated Hyundai suppliers must together invest $5.545 billion and hire at least 8,500 workers by 2031.
- The hired jobs must have an average annual yearly salary of $58,105 and be retained until 2048.
- Only two of the eight publicly available agreements—Hyundai Metaplant and Hyundai Mobis—promise salaries higher than $58,000.
- Six other companies beholden to state development benefits have average annual salaries less than the suggested retail price of the electric SUVs their workers are building.
- Two workers practicing car seat assembly make $20 per hour on the factory floor applying fabric and foam to steel frames.
- These workers are training to meet production targets for 300,000 electric vehicles a year.
- Their annualized pay equals $41,600, which is roughly two-thirds the price of the 2025 Hyundai IONIQ 5 electric SUV with a suggested retail price of $66,100.
- The headline salary of $58,105 represents an average across all on-site workers plus benefits.
- This pay scale is above average worker salaries in Coastal Georgia counties, which ranged from $30,686 in Bulloch to $46,305 in Effingham according to 2022 U.S. Census Bureau data.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) pays production workers a base salary of $22.40 an hour, or around $46,592 a year.
- Maintenance positions at HMGMA start at $30.70 an hour.
- The other six companies in the Metaplant economic development agreement set their pay independently of HMGMA despite committing together to the $58,105 average salary.
- HMGMA will not gather salary data from on-site affiliates until March 2025 for their first annual report to the state.
- HMGMA spokesperson Bianca Johnson stated the company does not coordinate with affiliates on pay nor plans to.
- Of the 1,471 new workers reported as of June 30, 859 are employed by HMGMA directly and the remaining 616 work for affiliated companies.
- If the seven companies do not produce 8,500 jobs with an average salary of $58,105 by 2031, HMGMA risks losing billions in public aid and could be forced to pay back part of its incentive package.
- Bianca Johnson stated confidence that the average pay of all on-site entities will reach or exceed $58,105 by Dec. 31, 2031, as required by the EDA.
- More than half a dozen other suppliers have promised to create almost 4,500 additional jobs in separate agreements with the state.
- Hyundai Mobis has promised the highest average pay of $61,642 plus benefits a year, or nearly $30 an hour.
- Seohan Auto Georgia will build brake systems and drive shafts at its Liberty County plant and will pay workers an average salary of $31,304 a year plus benefits, equivalent to around $15 an hour.
- The seat-building team saw a surprise raise from $20 to $22 an hour last month before HR announced it was an error that would be deducted from future paychecks.
- The 35-person seat-building team had to arrange repayment plans via a table set up by Hyundai HR in the break room.
- Workers use a makeshift parking lot on the 3,000-acre Metaplant construction site where one worker's windshield was damaged and another suffered a punctured tire from industrial nails.
- The company does not offer a safe parking zone, pay for damages, or provide a shuttle for workers.
- HMGMA spokesperson Johnson stated the Metaplant is an active construction site and they strive to create a safe work environment but cannot prevent all issues.
- Many seat-building workers finished paying back the mistaken raise and their paychecks returned to $20 an hour.
- One worker said HR verbally promised the team will make more when the manufacturing plant officially opens, but is unsure if he will stay that long.

- Joon Georgia employs 630 workers averaging $45,000 a year plus benefits at [$21.65/hr].
- Ecoplastic Corporation employs 456 workers averaging at least $45,000 a year plus benefits at [$21.65/hr].
