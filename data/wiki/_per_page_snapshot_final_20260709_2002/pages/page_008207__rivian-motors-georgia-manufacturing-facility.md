# Rivian Motors Georgia Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- The Rivian Plant in Georgia will be built on 2,000 acres of land.
- The plant will employ around 7,500 workers.
- The facility is more than five times as large as the Rivian plant in Normal, Illinois.
- Company plans to begin producing vehicles at the Georgia site in 2024.
- Production capacity will be 400,000 vehicles per year.
- Opponents cite concerns including groundwater contamination.
- Opponents disapprove of the lucrative public incentives offered by the company.
- Opponents worry that the huge facility will change the bucolic character of the area.
- Opponents point to increased traffic and light pollution as potential problems.
- The plant is a source of contention in Georgia’s Republican primary for governor.
- Republican candidate David Perdue has been critical of Governor Brian Kemp’s acceptance of the plant’s location.
- New plant will include a test track, adventure trail, 144 electric vehicle charging stations, training center, waste and recycling facilities, and outdoor amenity spaces.
- Rivian Motors vice president of public policy stated the company plans to build a battery factory in Georgia.
- Company plans to produce 400,000 electric trucks annually and employ 7,500 people.
- Rivian is building an EV assembly plant in Georgia and offers competitive pay, benefits, stock, and ownership to employees.
- Plant will create spinoff jobs from its suppliers.
- Local officials and residents say the project will create jobs, but concerns remain about traffic, groundwater, and urban sprawl.
- Rivian says the factory will adhere to all local ordinances.
- Opponents fear the factory will destroy the rural landscape and disrupt the ecology.
- Opponents are concerned about the destruction of a 200-year-old house and the displacement of residents.
- Future of the Georgia electric truck industry depends on availability of skilled labor.
- Significant labor shortage in Georgia is a challenge for Rivian, requiring influx of IT specialists, software engineers, and technically skilled assembly line workers.
- Governor Brian Kemp endorsed the Rivian factory, making him a target for opponents.
- Former senator David Perdue is also opposed to the plant.
- Rivian has committed to investing in Georgia’s economy despite criticism over alleged pollution and startup financial losses.
- Company plans to deliver 300,000 vehicles a year.
- Ford F-150 Lightning is currently available for purchase in Georgia at 45 charging stations.
