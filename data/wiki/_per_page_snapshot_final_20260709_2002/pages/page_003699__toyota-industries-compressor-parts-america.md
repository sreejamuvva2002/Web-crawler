# Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2020-10-27

**Source:** [Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America Announces Jackson County Expansion | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2020-10-27/toyota-industries-compressor-parts-america-announces-jackson-county)  ·  `page_003699`

## Facts

- On October 27, 2020, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America (TICA) will expand its Pendergrass, Georgia, production facility to meet growing demand for its onboard power electronics.
- The company will invest $5.5 million in the project.
- The expansion will create at least 27 new jobs in Jackson County over the next 18 months.
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America’s Pendergrass facility currently manufactures air-conditioning compressors for automotive applications.
- The announced expansion will allow a production line of DC-AC inverters.
- The expansion will add 15,000 square feet to the plant.
- This investment is part of the company’s preparations to supply more parts and products to its onboard power electronics market.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp thanked Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America for being a part of Georgia’s $3 billion automotive industry.
- Kemp stated the decision to expand is a testament to the hardworking Georgians of Jackson County and the strength of the automotive industry in the area.
- Kemp noted the announcement speaks to the importance of Georgia's top-notch logistics network, which continues to attract equipment manufacturers from across the world to expand or plant roots in the Peach State.
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America works in concert with the adjacent TD Automotive Compressor Georgia facility, which assembles the parts manufactured at the TICA plant.
- The combined facilities encompass over 1 million square feet of production space.
- The combined facilities represent a total investment of more than $1 billion.
- Kota Otoshi, executive officer for the electronics division of TICA’s parent company Toyota Industries Corporation, stated the company would like to expand its electrical products from this plant and its onboard power electronics to its market in North America.
- Otoshi expressed a desire to be a corporate citizen who can contribute to the economic and social development of Georgia through the production and sale of its onboard power electronics.
- Parent company Toyota Industries Corp. announced the current TICA facility in 2012, which included an initial $350 million investment.
- Toyota Industries Corp. supplies automotive compressors for Toyota Motor Corporation, as well as Chrysler, Ford, General Motors, and others.
- Jackson County Commissioner for District 1 Jim Hix stated Jackson County is the perfect location for the automotive industry.
- GDEcD Project Manager Joseph Huntemann represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project in partnership with the Jackson County Development Authority.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted the company’s decision to add a new line to their Jackson County facility will mean additional jobs for Georgians.
- Wilson cited Georgia's business climate, along with investments in workforce, logistics infrastructure, and quality of life as compelling reasons for continuing to grow there.
- Wilson highlighted Toyota Industries Corporation investing in Georgia and in its rapidly growing electric vehicle ecosystem as a strategic growth sector for the state.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities.
- The state is a national leader in advanced manufacturing, outpacing the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of products including machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
