# Boston Scientific

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-28

**Source:** [Boston Scientific Expands Research Capabilities in Georgia | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/boston-scientific-expands-research-capabilities-georgia-625-million-investment-johns)  ·  `page_005184`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Boston Scientific will construct a new manufacturing and supply chain facility in Johns Creek.
- The $62.5 million investment is expected to bring roughly 340 new jobs to Fulton County over the next seven years.
- Boston Scientific is headquartered in Massachusetts.
- Boston Scientific designs, develops, and manufactures a wide range of medical devices and therapies used in less-invasive procedures across multiple medical specialties.
- Its solutions and technologies are used by healthcare providers in endoscopy, cardiology, peripheral interventions, oncology, urology, and neurology.
- The company has more than 17,000 products that treat approximately 30 million patients around the world each year.
- Brad Sorenson, Executive Vice President of Global Operations at Boston Scientific, stated the new site in Johns Creek will enable continued growth as they fulfill their mission to transform lives.
- In 2016, Boston Scientific purchased Georgia-based EndoChoice.
- Boston Scientific currently employs approximately 300 individuals in Georgia and more than 41,000 individuals globally.
- The new facility will be located at 11350 Johns Creek Parkway in Johns Creek.
- Johns Creek Mayor John Bradberry stated that locating in the Town Center underscores Johns Creek as a leading destination for healthcare innovation and wellness.
- Marty Turpeau, Chairman of the Development Authority of Fulton County, called the announcement a game changer for Fulton County that adds a world-class brand to its life science ecosystem.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, noted that Metro Atlanta has rich assets to support the life sciences industry, including medical schools, universities, a large cluster of life science businesses, and a highly educated workforce.
- EJane Caraway, Director of Life Sciences, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the City of Johns Creek, the Fulton County Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed the expansion and highlighted collaborative relationships between the state’s universities, technical college systems, and companies as inspiring innovation.
- There are more than 2,200 clinical trials recruiting or underway in Georgia.
- From 2014 through 2017, there were 547 patents awarded to applicants from Georgia for drugs and pharmaceuticals.
- In 2021, Georgia exported $3.2 billion in immunological products and $1.9 billion in medical devices and pharmaceuticals.
