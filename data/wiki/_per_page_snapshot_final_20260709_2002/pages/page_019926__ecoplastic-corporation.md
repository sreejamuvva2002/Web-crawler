# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Longtime Kia and HMG Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/609761360/gov-kemp-longtime-kia-and-hmg-supplier-to-build-manufacturing-facility-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_019926`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs and invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project to build on record-breaking jobs and investments coming to Georgia.
- The facility will serve the Hyundai Metaplant from Statesboro, leveraging close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd.
- Ecoplastic is a Korea-based company that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic serves as a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- The company manufactures automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Shang Han is the President and CEO of Ecoplastic Corporation.
- Ecoplastic is establishing a production facility specifically for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- The new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The plant will be capable of injection molding and painting.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers, garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- Ecoplastic expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County Chair Billy Allen welcomed the Hyundai supplier to the community.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at GDEcD's Global Commerce team, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on the project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that Ecoplastic has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai.
- The investment supports the expanding supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on automotive industry assets.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to Hyundai's project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Ecoplastic currently employs more than 770 employees.
- Ecoplastic recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
- Ecoplastic established its first Technical Research Center in 1987.
- Ecoplastic was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
