# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2 more Hyundai suppliers announce manufacturing plants in Georgia](https://www.ajc.com/news/2-more-hyundai-suppliers-announce-manufacturing-plants-in-georgia/WBDV4OACNBCY5EY7JIFXNIK7ME/)  ·  `page_021104`

## Facts

- Two more automobile parts suppliers have joined the wave of companies flocking to Georgia to service Hyundai Motor Group’s future Metaplant near Savannah and the expansion to Kia’s West Point factory.
- Hwashin, a Korean car part and frame maker, announced Tuesday a $176 million facility in Dublin.
- The Hwashin facility is located roughly 100 miles northwest of Hyundai’s future $7.6 billion electric vehicle factory.
- On the same day, Korean car seat and arm rest supplier Hyundai Industrial Co. announced a $24 million project near Newnan.
- The Hyundai Industrial Co. project will provide parts to manufacturing plants for both Hyundai and Kia.
- Robyn Sincere, president of the West Point Chamber of Commerce, stated that while the suppliers still have plenty of work to do before beginning production at their new West Point locations, they are excited about the opportunity to grow jobs in Georgia for Hyundai’s future EV plant.
- The announcement comes as Hyundai Motor Group announced a $1.52 billion investment in its future Savannah EV plant in February 2023.
- George Kim, president of Hyundai Motor America, said, “We are excited to welcome Hwashin and Hyundai Industrial Co. to Georgia.”
