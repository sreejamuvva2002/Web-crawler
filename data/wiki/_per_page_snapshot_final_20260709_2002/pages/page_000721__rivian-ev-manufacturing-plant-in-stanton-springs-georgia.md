# Rivian EV Manufacturing Plant in Stanton Springs, Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Clayco to build Rivian’s new EV manufacturing Plant in Georgia - Construction Front | Commercial Intelligence for Infrastructure & Energy](https://constructionfront.com/2023-12-21-clayco-to-build-rivians-new-ev-manufacturing-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_000721`

## Facts

- Clayco has been selected by Rivian to build an advanced manufacturing plant in Stanton Springs, Georgia.
- Rivian aims to develop an expansive 1,800-acre complex dedicated to sustainable transportation.
- The facility is located in the Stanton Springs area, situated 30 minutes east of metro Atlanta along I-20.
- The site is part of a high-tech and bio-pharma industrial park that includes existing residents Takeda, Facebook’s Newton Data Center, and the Georgia Bioscience Training Center.
- Upon full completion, the plant will have an annual capacity of 400,000 electric vehicle units.
- The facility is positioned at the Stanton Springs North site, approximately 40 minutes from Atlanta across Morgan and Walton Counties.
- The State of Georgia and the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton & Walton Counties collaborated with local partners to prepare the site for Rivian’s establishment.
- Earthwork for enabling works was almost 95% complete as of October 2023.
- Plant construction is expected to commence in early 2024.
- Clayco will work alongside Jacobs, who will serve as Rivian’s engineer of record.
- Skidmore, Owings & Merrill (SOM) will act as the owner’s design architect for the project.
- The project is backed by an Economic Development Agreement inked in 2022 between the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) and the Joint Development Authority (JDA).
- Rivian’s $5 billion Georgia project aims to create 7,500 jobs at Stanton Springs North.
- The average wage for the created jobs is projected to be $56,000.
