# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-18

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels to build $325M plant in Bulloch’s commerce park at I-16 - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/aspen-aerogels-to-build-325m-plant-in-bullochs-commerce-park-at-i-16/)  ·  `page_052651`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels will invest a minimum of $325 million to build a factory in Bulloch County’s Southern Gateway Commerce Park south of Statesboro for production of aerogel thermal insulating material used in electric vehicle batteries.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced the investment on Thursday, February 18, 2022.
- Company officials expect the plant to be in operation by late 2023.
- The plant will create more than 250 advanced manufacturing jobs.
- For that cost or possibly more, the company expects to build and equip a 500,000-square-foot facility.
- The project greatly expands production capacity beyond that of Aspen Aerogels' existing plant in Rhode Island.
- This appears to be the largest announced dollar-value private investment for any manufacturing facility in Bulloch County, Georgia, in its history.
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County (DABC), stated it is the largest single private investment as far as he knows going back nearly 30 years.
- Previous major investments in Bulloch County include a Walmart Distribution Center and plants by Briggs & Stratton, Great Dane Trailers, and Viracon (recently replaced by Cardinal Glass Industries).
- The Briggs & Stratton small engines plant was first announced in 1994, employed nearly 800 people a few years later, and had an initial investment of a little less than $100 million.
- Public investment incentives will come from the state of Georgia, Bulloch County, and the city of Statesboro in exchange for job creation commitments.
- The DABC board met at 3 p.m. on Thursday to approve local contributions to the incentives package.
- The Bulloch County Board of Commissioners and Statesboro City Council held special meetings simultaneously at 4:30 p.m. and unanimously approved agreements for local incentive contributions.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said Aspen Aerogels has put confidence in Georgia, its people, and the growing electric mobility sector, adding an American company to the state's rapidly expanding EV portfolio.
- The DABC is providing a 90-acre site within the commerce park at the Interstate 16 and U.S. Highway 301 interchange free-of-charge to Aspen Aerogels.
- Through a lease arrangement, the company will enjoy a 10-year, 100% abatement of county-government property tax on the factory and site.
- The property tax abatement does not extend to the portion controlled by the Bulloch County Board of Education, so Aspen Aerogels will pay taxes supporting local public schools.
- The city of Statesboro extended its water, sewer, and natural gas systems several miles earlier to serve the commerce park and county Tax Allocation District.
- The city will extend utility pipelines farther within the park to serve Aspen Aerogels.
- The DABC is applying for a federal Employment Incentive Program grant for $750,000 to cover pipeline extension costs.
- If the grant is not successful, the city will be responsible for up to $500,000, with Aspen Aerogels covering the remainder.
- The site is currently outside city limits, but the City Council agreed that if annexed into Statesboro or its fire service district, the city will not impose property tax or fire fees within the 10-year lease term.
- The company will pay standard rates for city utility services after connections are provided.
- TravelCenters of America broke ground in November for a TA Express center, becoming the first business to start construction in the park after over 10 years of infrastructure spending.
- The Aspen Aerogels plant will be the first actual industry in the park and will take up about half of the DABC’s remaining land at the site.
- Board of Commissioners Chairman Roy Thompson said county officials are never eager to grant tax abatements but that they are necessary to secure new industries with higher-paying jobs.
- Aspen Aerogels originally announced plans in 2015 to build a $70 million plant with a little over 100 jobs at the original Gateway Industrial Park for traditional aerogel products used to insulate petroleum industry pipelines.
- Those 2015 plans changed after a dip in oil prices.
- The company shifted to making new aerogel products under the registered trademark PyroThin to serve as thermal barriers in electric vehicle batteries.
- Aspen Aerogels President and CEO Don Young cited access to efficient ports, a strong available workforce, and secure low-cost utilities and critical raw materials as reasons for choosing the location.
- Young stated the company is increasingly being asked to provide broader solutions for improved battery performance and safety centered on PyroThin materials that provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems.
- Aspen Aerogels is publicly traded under symbol ASPN on the New York Stock Exchange.
- The company's corporate headquarters are in Northborough, Massachusetts.
- Aspen Aerogels currently operates a single production facility in East Providence, Rhode Island, which employs about 250 people.
- Multiple online financial sites showed the corporation’s market capitalization as a little over $900 million as of Thursday.
- DABC Vice Chair Billy Allen welcomed the company, citing the region's commitment to innovation in the electric mobility ecosystem and tremendous education resources including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College.
- Marie Gordon, communications director for the Georgia Department of Economic Development, said officials plan to finalize and sign state documents that week and will release details upon signing per state law.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed Aspen Aerogels' innovative solutions for the electric vehicle and energy storage markets and looked forward to Georgia employees collaborating with Aspen’s network of scientists and engineers.
- Aspen plans to begin hiring in the second half of 2022 across skilled manufacturing operators, advanced process engineers, quality and maintenance technicians, and professional support roles.
