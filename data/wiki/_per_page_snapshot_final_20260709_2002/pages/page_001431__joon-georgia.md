# Joon Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: Supplier to hire 630 near Hyundai's EV plant in Georgia - Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/auto/cars/supplier-to-hire-630-near-hyundais-ev-plant-in-georgia/articleshow/95369966.cms)  ·  `page_001431`

## Facts

- An auto parts manufacturer plans to hire 630 workers at a new factory in southeast Georgia to supply Hyundai Motor Group's first U.S. electric vehicle plant that is under construction nearby, according to state officials.
- Joon Georgia will invest $317 million to produce parts in Bulloch County, according to Gov. Brian Kemp's office.
- The supplier facility will be located roughly 30 miles (50 kilometers) west of the southeast Georgia site where Hyundai executives broke ground on the new EV plant two weeks ago.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated the company is "the first of many" expected to come to Georgia to supply the $5.5 billion Hyundai plant in Bryan County.
- Hyundai plans to open its Georgia plant in 2025, producing up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Joon Georgia is a subsidiary of Ajin USA, which supplies parts to other Hyundai plants.
- Joon Georgia already operates a facility in Cusseta, Alabama, near the Georgia line that makes parts for Hyundai's plant in Montgomery, Alabama, as well as for Kia's auto plant in West Point, Georgia.
- The Joon Georgia factory is expected to open near Statesboro in mid-2024, according to Kemp's office.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, called the announcement a "landmark moment as we drive Georgia's automotive industry into the future."
- State and local officials in Georgia lured Hyundai with tax breaks and incentives worth $1.8 billion, making it the state's largest economic development deal.
- Wilson and other Georgia officials have insisted the Hyundai investment is worthwhile.
- In addition to Hyundai hiring 8,100 workers, suppliers are expected to create thousands of additional jobs in the state.
