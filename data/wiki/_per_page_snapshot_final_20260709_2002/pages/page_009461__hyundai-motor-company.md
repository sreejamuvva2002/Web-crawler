# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-09

**Source:** [UAW condemns Hyundai over worker safety after Georgia factory raid | Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/economy/auto-workers-union-condemns-hyundai-working-conditions-wake-federal-raid-georgia-site)  ·  `page_009461`

## Facts

- The United Auto Workers (UAW) labor union condemned Hyundai for what it calls a "disgraceful record on worker safety" following an immigration raid at an electric car battery factory in Georgia.
- Most of the 475 people detained on Sept. 4 under suspicion of being in the United States illegally were employees of subcontractors rather than direct LGES and Hyundai Motor Company employees.
- At least 300 of the detained individuals reportedly hailed from South Korea.
- The UAW alleged that for years, Hyundai, including its joint ventures and suppliers working in its plants, cut corners on industry-standard safety precautions, refused to respect workers’ right to a union, and relied on the exploitation of immigrant labor to build its factories and supply chains.
- The UAW claimed that three workers at Hyundai died on the job in the last two years.
- The UAW called on federal agencies including the Occupational Safety and Health Administration and National Labor Relations Board to increase workplace safety.
- The UAW stated that the militarized federal crackdown on the detained workers further hurts safety at Hyundai, asserting that workers are not the problem but exploitative corporations are.
- The UAW pledged to always stand with all workers, immigrant and native-born alike, against unsafe corporations and militarized attacks on workplaces.
- Hyundai stated in a press release that the safety and well-being of everyone working at its sites, whether directly employed or working for suppliers, is a top priority.
- Hyundai emphasized taking its responsibility as a corporate citizen seriously and noted that incidents like the raid highlight the importance of robust oversight throughout its entire supply chain and contractor network.
- The detainees in Georgia are set to be released and sent back to their home country after South Korea and the U.S. reached an agreement.
