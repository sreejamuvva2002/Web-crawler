# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation: Soaring Ambitions in Urban Air Mobility | FinancialContent](https://www.financialcontent.com/article/predictstreet-2025-9-30-archer-aviation-soaring-ambitions-in-urban-air-mobility)  ·  `page_016942`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. was founded in 2018 by Brett Adcock and Adam Goldstein.
- The company's headquarters are located in San Jose, California, having initially been based in Palo Alto.
- Archer's flagship product is the four-passenger, one-pilot Midnight electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft.
- The Midnight aircraft targets speeds of up to 150 mph and a range optimized for 20 to 50 miles.
- Archer went public on September 17, 2021, through a SPAC merger with Atlas Crest Investment Corp., trading on the NYSE under ticker ACHR.
- The IPO transaction provided approximately $1.1 billion in gross proceeds, including a $600 million PIPE investment.
- United Airlines became a major strategic partner in 2021 by placing a conditional order for 200 Archer electric aircraft.
- In August 2022, United Airlines paid Archer a $10 million deposit for the aircraft order.
- In November 2022, Archer and United Airlines announced plans to launch the first electric air taxi route in the U.S.
- The Midnight production aircraft was officially unveiled on November 17, 2022.
- Stellantis became Archer's exclusive contract manufacturer in January 2023, providing up to $150 million in equity.
- In August 2023, Archer secured a $215 million equity investment from Stellantis, Boeing, and United Airlines.
- The Midnight aircraft received its FAA Special Airworthiness Certificate in August 2023.
- Piloted flight tests for the Midnight began in early 2024.
- In December 2024, Archer launched Archer Defense and partnered with Anduril Industries to develop hybrid-propulsion VTOL aircraft for military applications.
- In August 2025, Archer acquired a patent portfolio and key employees from Overair to strengthen its intellectual property.
- In August 2025, Archer acquired composite manufacturing assets from Mission Critical Composites to bring fabrication in-house.
- The Midnight aircraft completed its longest piloted flight of 55 miles in 31 minutes in August 2025.
- Archer was selected as the Official Air Taxi Provider for the LA28 Olympic Games in May 2025.
- In June 2025, Archer partnered with Jetex for urban air mobility infrastructure development.
- In September 2025, Soracle (a joint venture of Japan Airlines and Sumitomo Corporation) selected the Midnight aircraft for air taxi services in Osaka, Japan.
- Archer announced a $30 million agreement with Ethiopian Airlines in March 2025.
- As of late September 2025, Archer's total liquidity stood at approximately $1.73 billion.
- Archer completed a 400,000-square-foot high-volume manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The company aims to ramp up Midnight production to two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Archer targets producing 650 Midnight aircraft annually by 2030.
- Archer partnered with Palantir to integrate AI-driven aviation software for optimized production and competitive advantage.
- The Midnight features a vectored-thrust lift+cruise design with six tiltrotors, 12 electric motors, and six independent battery packs.
- The aircraft prioritizes quick 10-minute charging capabilities for rapid operational turnarounds.
- Adam Goldstein serves as the CEO and Chairman of the Board of Directors.
- Priya Gupta assumed the role of Acting Chief Financial Officer in July 2025.
- Tom Muniz has served as Chief Technology Officer since March 2024.
- Archer reported a net loss of $206.0 million for Q2 2025, up from $106.9 million in Q2 2024.
- Operating expenses increased by 45.3% year-over-year to $176.1 million in Q2 2025, primarily driven by R&D expenditures.
- Total debt stood at $81.6 million as of June 2025, with a low debt-to-equity ratio of 0.05.
- Cash and cash equivalents were reported at $1.72 billion as of June 30, 2025.
- Archer completed an $816.8 million registered direct offering in June 2025 to bolster its liquidity.
- The company's market capitalization ranged between approximately $5.96 billion and $6.22 billion as of September 30, 2025.
- Joby Aviation held the largest eVTOL commercial passenger market share at 22% as of early 2025, with Archer closely following at 18%.
- The global eVTOL market is projected to grow from approximately $2.14 billion in 2025 to over $170 billion by 2034.
- Industry supply chain vulnerabilities include bottlenecks for electric motors, advanced avionics, lightweight composites, and current battery technology limitations.
- Rising costs for raw materials like lithium and cobalt impact component expenses across the eVTOL sector.
- Infrastructure gaps currently exist for vertiports, charging stations, and maintenance networks in the UAM sector.
- Archer expects to achieve FAA Type Certification for the Midnight aircraft by late 2025.
- Only approximately 15% of final FAA Type Certification stage (Stage 4) compliance verification documents were approved as of early 2025.
- A U.S. executive order in June 2025 created an eVTOL pilot program to accelerate regulatory frameworks.
- Archer actively participates in the White House's eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP).
