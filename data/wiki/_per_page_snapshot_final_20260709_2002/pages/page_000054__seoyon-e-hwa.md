# Seoyon E-HWA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-01

**Source:** [Seoyon E-HWA Joins Growing List of Hyundai Suppliers | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/seoyon-e-hwa-joins-growing-list-hyundai-suppliers-nearly-doubles-georgia-employment)  ·  `page_000054`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Seoyon E-HWA will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA will invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- The announcement was made on February 1, 2023, in Atlanta.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia is working to ensure it becomes the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Seoyon E-HWA was established in 1972 and operates 32 corporations across North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- The company supplies door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen, and more.
- Seoyon’s existing LaGrange location will continue to support 630 full-time jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok stated the company is an automobile manufacturer in countries including Korea, Europe, and India.
- The new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new manufacturing facility will produce automotive interior parts such as door trims, headlining, and tailgate trims.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in October 2024.
- More than two-thirds, or approximately 500, of the announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production personnel.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison stated the location ideally positions Seoyon E-HWA close to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the Port of Savannah, Interstates I-16 and I-95, and more.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch noted that Seoyon is a valued customer using the Port of Savannah to supply its manufacturing facility in West Georgia.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai’s new EV facility is drawing an experienced and dedicated supplier network to the region.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced over $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers for the Hyundai project have already exceeded the $1 billion in investment predicted from other locations connected to the project around the state by more than $600 million.
