## Facts

- Famed American golf-cart manufacturer Club Car has opened a new factory in Augusta, Georgia.
- The facility is located in the unincorporated community of Appling.
- The factory represents an $8 million capital investment.
- The facility will manufacture the brand’s Urban line of small electric commercial vehicles.
- The factory was officially kicked into gear on Thursday, April 18, with a ribbon-cutting ceremony featuring Georgia Governor Brian Kemp.
- Club Car’s Urban XR and Urban LSV models were engineered for versatile duty beyond groundskeeping.
- The company pitches the Urban line as “eco-friendly, crash-tested vehicles that help you get the job done.”
- The most affordable Urban model, the off-road-only XR, has a top speed of 19 mph and an MSRP of just under $27,000.
- The more expensive LSV is road-legal in some states and capable of 25 mph.
- Both versions can carry a payload of up to 2,204 pounds and were developed with industrial spaces and last-mile delivery in mind.
- Either vehicle version can be ordered with a pickup bed or a van box.
- The Appling facility employs 40 people.
- The facility is able to build up to 20 Urban vehicles per day.
- The new factory is a response to increasing US demand for Club Car products.
