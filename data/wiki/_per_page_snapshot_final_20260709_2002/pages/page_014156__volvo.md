# Volvo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-02

**Source:** [Volvo Says We Should Stay Tuned For A Sports Car](https://www.motor1.com/news/650397/volvo-sports-car-under-consideration/)  ·  `page_014156`

## Facts

- Volvo is prioritizing the development of new crossovers to replace existing models while adding new members to its portfolio.
- CEO Jim Rowan mentioned during an interview in November 2022 that electric sedans and wagons could hit the market after the SUV boom.
- Chief operating officer Bjorn Annwall hinted in a new interview with Australian magazine Car Sales that a sports car is not completely out of the question.
- Bjorn Annwall stated: "Every car brand needs a bit of spice that helps kind of push the boundaries of what the car really is, and I think Volvo should have those complimentary car products as well."
- When asked whether the company intends to launch a new sports car, Annwall said: "Stay tuned."
- Exterior design manager Orjan Sterner admitted it's fun to work on a "coupe or a super sports car" but cautioned profits must prevail at the end of the day.
- The company is engineering volume-oriented vehicles that have a solid business case, so SUVs will remain the main focus for a while.
- Volvo was among the first luxury automakers to announce the gradual demise of combustion engines.
- Volvo plans to morph into a purely electric brand in 2030, five years after Jaguar.
- With SUVs remaining the number one priority, it's likely too late in the game for an ICE-powered sports car.
- Past performance vehicles include the P1800 Cyan and the Polestar 1 plug-in hybrid.
- If Volvo green lights another performance machine, logic suggests it will likely be completely electric.
- Car Sales speculates a sports car is unlikely to arrive until near the end of the decade or in the early 2030s.
