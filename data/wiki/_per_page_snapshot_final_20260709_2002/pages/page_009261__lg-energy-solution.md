# LG Energy Solution

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-28

**Source:** [Battery maker LGES offers measured 2025 outlook after slow EV demand drags down Q3 profit - AOL](https://www.aol.com/battery-maker-lges-offers-measured-002710651.html)  ·  `page_009261`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution (LGES) is a South Korean battery maker that supplies Tesla, General Motors, and Hyundai Motor.
- LGES has a conservative view of revenue growth for 2025.
- LGES will significantly reduce capital expenditure in 2025 due to slowing electric vehicle demand.
- LGES booked a 39% drop in third-quarter profit.
- CFO Lee Chang-Sil expects the result of the upcoming U.S. presidential election to have a significant impact on EV market direction.
- Lee Chang-Sil cited continuing macro uncertainty, geopolitical risk, increased battery exports by Chinese rivals, and automaker customer plans to manufacture their own batteries as factors that would intensify competition.
- LGES expects capital expenditure to be significantly reduced in 2025 compared to 2024, with the exception of some essential and necessary investments.
- In April, LGES announced plans to reduce capital expenditure for 2024 due to slowing growth in EVs.
- Earlier this year, LGES stated that its 2024 capital expenditure would be similar to the previous year's 10.9 trillion won.
- Several automakers are scaling down electrification targets due to slowing EV demand caused by a lack of affordable models, slow proliferation of charging points, trade tension, and increased competition from cheaper Chinese rivals.
- A senior LGES executive told Reuters in July that EV demand will likely recover in about 18 months in Europe and two to three years in the United States, depending on climate policies and other regulation.
- Analyst Kang Dong-jin at Hyundai Motor Securities stated that the pace of EV demand growth could be slower if Donald Trump is elected to a second term due to his suggestion to cut EV tax credits.
- LGES reported an operating profit of 448 billion won ($322.84 million) for July-September, which was in line with its earlier forecast but down from 731 billion won in the same period a year earlier.
- Improved demand from some European and North American automakers helped LGES beat the LSEG SmartEstimate of 374 billion won for the quarter.
- LGES stated it would have booked an 18 billion won operating loss in the quarter without a tax credit received under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act.
- LGES revenue fell 16% to 6.9 trillion won.
- LGES share price rose 1.2% after the results, outpacing a 0.9% rise in the benchmark KOSPI.
