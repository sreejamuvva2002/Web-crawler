# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72M in Harris County - Athens CEO](https://athensceo.com/news/2023/08/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-invest-72-million-harris-county/)  ·  `page_019026`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The facility will create more than 140 new jobs.
- The facility will involve $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed excitement that Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia's automotive industry currently employs over 50,000 Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state's automotive industry will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers expand across the state.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017.
- The company specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project located in the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- Min Ho Kwon stated the company looks forward to continuing its partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- Daesol Ausys’ new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant, Chairman of the Harris County Commission, stated that Daesol Ausys’ decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- Robert Grant noted that West Point and Harris County have a strong relationship with Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials.
- Robert Grant stated local officials have been working closely with company officials to secure the new venture in West Point.
- Craig Greenhaw, Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority, extended a welcome to Daesol Ausys as a new corporate citizen.
- Craig Greenhaw stated he looks forward to a long and mutually beneficial partnership with the company.
- Craig Greenhaw noted that West Point and Harris County continue to collaborate effectively on ventures bringing jobs and capital investment to the region.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the GDEcD Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is driven by the local level.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated Harris County and community partners for helping bring Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement over the continuation of growth and opportunities Daesol Ausys will provide to Harris County residents.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered industry practices, collaboration, and innovation, positioning itself as a leader in emerging automotive and mobility technologies.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain.
- Since 2018, Georgia's pursuit of the EV supply chain has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
