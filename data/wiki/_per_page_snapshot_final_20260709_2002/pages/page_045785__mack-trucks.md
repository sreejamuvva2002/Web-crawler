# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-24

## Facts

- President Donald Trump visited Mack Trucks’ Lehigh Valley Operations plant.
- The visit focused on domestic manufacturing, jobs, tariffs, trucking, and industrial production.
- Mack’s Lehigh Valley Operations facility is a 1.7 million-square-foot facility.
- Trump praised Mack’s long history as an American truck brand and highlighted medium- and heavy-duty truck manufacturing.
- The site already has a role in Mack’s zero-emission product strategy.
- Trump criticized blanket electric vehicles or zero-emission mandates before charging infrastructure is mature.
- Trump stated buyers should still be free to purchase EVs if they choose.
- Mack’s Lehigh Valley Operations facility assembles Class 8 trucks for North American and export markets.
- The facility is tied to the Mack LR Electric, a fully electric refuse truck that entered serial production at the Macungie plant in 2021.
- Mack offers the MD Electric, a Class 6 and 7 battery-electric truck designed for medium-duty applications such as urban delivery.
- Electric trucks are part of active product portfolios, manufacturing plans, dealer training programs, charging discussions, and fleet deployment decisions.
- The pace and scale of EV adoption vary by duty cycle, region, and customer use case.
- Trump made domestic manufacturing a central part of his economic platform.
- Trump moved to roll back several clean vehicle policies, including California’s ability to enforce certain zero-emission vehicle and heavy-duty truck rules.
- This policy rollback created a different environment for fleets and manufacturers compared to previous years.
- Electrification in the commercial vehicle industry is moving forward at a slower pace.
- Applications showing practical alignment with battery-electric trucks include refuse, urban delivery, drayage, yard operations, and return-to-base routes.
- Long-haul and heavy regional applications remain difficult due to range, charging infrastructure, payload, vehicle cost, and operational flexibility.
- The industry is focusing on where electric trucks can provide a workable business case today while manufacturers continue building diesel and other powertrain options.
- Trump’s appearance at Mack Trucks underscored the importance of truck manufacturing to the U.S. economy.
- Even without zero-emission mandates, electrification remains part of the commercial vehicle conversation for manufacturers building electric models and fleets evaluating deployments.
