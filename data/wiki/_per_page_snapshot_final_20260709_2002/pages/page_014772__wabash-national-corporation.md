# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-06

**Source:** [Wabash to move entire refrigerated division to Little Falls | News | hometownsource.com](https://www.hometownsource.com/morrison_county_record/news/wabash-to-move-entire-refrigerated-division-to-little-falls/article_692fea8e-148b-11ed-85d2-2bf53b844790.html)  ·  `page_014772`

## Facts

- Wabash National plans to relocate its entire refrigerated trailer operation to its plant in Little Falls.
- The expansion plan includes the addition of 216 jobs in Little Falls.
- The plan includes a $23 million investment in equipment for the facility.
- The plan includes another $12 million in salaries by the end of 2023.
- In April, the city of Little Falls and Morrison County approved a five-year extension to the 10-year tax rebate Wabash received when it first moved to the area in 2017.
- The existing tax rebate amounts are $50,000 per year from the city and $40,000 per year from the county.
- The previous rebate was contingent upon the addition of 140 new jobs and a $14 million investment to the facility.
- Wabash is one of 14 plants owned by the Indiana-based company throughout the U.S.
- Wabash initially considered Georgia for Phase III of its expansion but chose Little Falls due to strong community relationships.
- Wabash requested an additional five years of tax rebates, extending them until 2037 for a total of 20 years.
- By the end of next year (2023), Wabash hopes to be manufacturing 3,000 refrigerated trailers in Little Falls.
- Wabash also plans to manufacture 2,000 smaller trailers in Little Falls by the end of next year.
- The Little Falls Economic Development Authority (EDA) and Morrison County Rural Development Financing Authority (RDFA) voted unanimously to accept Wabash’s proposal and extend the tax rebates through 2037.
- The rebate amounts from each group were based on taxes paid on the facility by Larsen Boats during its last year in the facility.
- Little Falls City Administrator Jon Radermacher stated that after 20 years, Wabash will ultimately receive a $1 million subsidy from the city.
- The EDA levy has the capacity to handle the extension, and it is not a new tax or property tax increase.
- Tax increment financing was not possible because there wouldn't be enough new taxable increment developed by the expansion.
- Under the agreement structure, Wabash will pay its taxes each year and then get reimbursed $50,000 from the city and $40,000 from the county.
- The reimbursement amounts are capped; if taxable property value climbs higher, the entities retain the remainder prior to the agreement's sunset.
- Wabash will receive $450,000 from a Minnesota Investment Fund (MIF) grant tied to job creation numbers.
- The Little Falls City Administrator stated the city is closing in on $2.5 million in its EDA fund between local, state, and federal dollars.
- Morrison County Board of Commissioners heard a presentation from Community Development of Morrison County Executive Director Carol Anderson on the proposal.
- Wabash acquired the facility in 2017 after Larsen closed it.
- Wabash replaced much of the equipment in the facility from when it was owned by Larsen that it thought it would be able to use.
- The workforce and existing equipment were cited as key reasons for Wabash's expansion plans.
- Wabash is discontinuing refrigerated trailer production in Lafayette, Indiana, and moving all refrigerated trailer manufacturing to Little Falls.
- No jobs will be lost at the Lafayette, Indiana plant; they will switch to making dry trailers instead of refrigerated ones.
- The chassis for the trucks manufactured in Little Falls are still being made in Indiana, with only about four shipped to Little Falls at a time.
- Wabash's new vacuum system technology helps achieve a 25% energy savings in its state-of-the-art refrigerated trailers, up from an initial goal of 3%.
- The new Little Falls trailers are completely sealed and lighter than traditional trailers, avoiding the 500-700 extra pounds of water weight gained over six years by regular semi-trailers.
- Wabash plans to meet with local school superintendents and Central Lakes College representatives to customize training and promote programs for specialized jobs.
- The goal of the training initiatives is to keep local students in the area and prepare them for work at Wabash.
- The expansion could create demand for other manufacturers to relocate to the area, particularly suppliers like chassis makers.
- Morrison County Commissioner Mike Wilson expressed enthusiasm about potential equipment support from Wabash for schools.
- Morrison County Board Chair Greg Blaine emphasized the importance of organizational culture and workforce sustainability for the company's future success.
- Local government entities view maintaining the workforce as a shared responsibility to ensure Wabash's success in Little Falls.
- Community Development of Morrison County Executive Director Carol Anderson noted that Wabash's arrival prevented the facility from sitting empty after Larsen's layoff.
