# Bridgestone Bandag

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-03

**Source:** [State chips in for Bridgestone-Bandag renovation](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/state-chips-bridgestone-bandag-renovation-050800535.html)  ·  `page_018221`

## Facts

- State officials will channel a $450,000 "building reuse" grant to subsidize the renovation of Bridgestone Bandag's facility on the west side of Oxford.
- The N.C. Department of Commerce grant followed Bridgestone Bandag's pledge to invest $1.6 million in the renovation of its West Industry Drive facility and create 61 new jobs.
- Bridgestone Bandag is a subsidiary of Bridgestone Corp., the Japanese tire and auto-parts maker.
- Bandag specializes in retreading tires on a mass scale for the trucking industry.
- The company is at least the third to receive state subsidies for relocation, expansion, or renovation in the West Industry Drive area in recent months.
- The N.C. Department of Commerce pledged $5.7 million about a year ago for the development of a packing and cold-storage facility by Meel Corp.
- In April, the Department of Commerce said it would help underwrite an expansion of the CertainTeed shingle plant.
- Bandag's project qualified for aid because Granville County is classified by state officials as a "Tier 2" county.
- Tier 1 counties are the 40 worst-off economically in North Carolina, while Tier 2 counties are the next 40.
- Tier 3 counties comprise the remaining 20 and are mostly urban areas like Wake and Durham counties.
- Granville County officials stated the building-reuse grant aims to bolster projects in Tier 1 and 2 counties.
- Gov. Roy Cooper stated that "investments in rural North Carolina have a positive impact on our state's overall success," and that building-reuse grants help communities like Granville County create good jobs, improve residents' quality of life, and boost the local economy.
