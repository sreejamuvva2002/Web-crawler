# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [(LEAD) Hyundai to build US$5.54 bln EV, battery plant in Georgia | Yonhap News Agency](https://m-en.yna.co.kr/view/AEN20220520010751320)  ·  `page_009317`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group will invest US$5.54 billion to build a dedicated electric vehicle and car battery manufacturing plant in the United States.
- Construction on the 300,000-unit-a-year EV and battery manufacturing plant is planned to start in January 2023.
- Production at the new Georgia plant is scheduled to begin in the first half of 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group selected Georgia due to the state's speed-to-market, workforce, and ability to meet the company's carbon neutrality standards.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- Hyundai Motor Group Chairman Euisun Chung stated that the U.S. holds an important place in the Group's global strategy and expressed excitement to partner with the State of Georgia for electrified mobility and sustainability goals.
- Governor Brian Kemp described Hyundai's investment as the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Non-affiliated suppliers will invest an additional $1 billion in the plant.
- The project is expected to generate at least 8,100 new jobs in Georgia.
- The new EV plant will help Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp. strengthen their lineup by launching all-electric SUVs such as the Hyundai IONIQ 7 and the Kia EV9 in the U.S.
- Hyundai currently operates a manufacturing plant in Alabama, while Kia operates a plant in Georgia.
- Last month, Hyundai Motor announced a $300 million investment in its Alabama plant to produce the Santa Fe gasoline hybrid model and the all-electric GV70 SUV under the Genesis brand later that year.
- Hyundai plans to roll out 17 EV models by 2030, including six Genesis models.
- Kia is scheduled to release 14 EVs by 2027.
- The investment aligns with the Biden administration's drive to attract more EV investment and generate jobs in the industry.
- The U.S. government aims to have half of the vehicles sold in the U.S. be electrified by 2030.
- Hyundai operates seven domestic plants in Korea and 11 overseas plants (four in China, one each in the U.S., Czech Republic, Turkey, Russia, India, Brazil, and Indonesia) with a combined capacity of 5.65 million vehicles.
- Kia operates eight domestic plants and seven overseas ones (three in China, one each in the U.S., Slovakia, Mexico, and India) with an overall capacity of 3.84 million units.
- The announcement coincided with U.S. President Joe Biden's visit to South Korea as part of his first trip to Asia.
