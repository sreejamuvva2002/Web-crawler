# National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-15

**Source:** [EV Charging Station Sneak Attack On Gas Stations](https://cleantechnica.com/2023/02/18/ev-charging-station-sneak-coming-soon-to-a-gas-station-near-you/)  ·  `page_060528`

## Facts

- The White House unveiled the nation’s first ever national EV charging standards earlier this week.
- The new standards fall under the federal government’s National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) program.
- NEVI aims to make recharging an EV battery as simple, seamless and standardized as filling up a gas tank.
- The new standards apply to all federally funded public EV charging stations, including those funded through NEVI.
- NEVI is administered by the Federal Highway Administration, a branch of the US Department of Transportation.
- NEVI is a $5 billion federal funding program established through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- The program provides funding to states to strategically deploy EV charging infrastructure and establish an interconnected network for data collection, access, and reliability.
- The Bipartisan Infrastructure Law passed the House with all Democratic Representatives voting in favor and 13 Republican votes.
- In the Senate, 19 Republican Senators voted in favor and 30 voted against, with the bill passing with all Democrats voting in favor.
- President Biden signed the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law into law on November 15, 2021.
- The new standards cover more than just the charging plug, applying to EV charging from hardware to traffic control, connectivity and mapping apps.
- EV charging stations have already begun appearing at gas stations, particularly those with convenience stores and restrooms.
- The Pilot Company is on board with its network of 500 Pilot and Flying J travel centers in partnership with General Motors and EVgo.
- TravelCenters of America has committed 200 of its Travel Centers and Petro locations to the initiative.
- bp finalized an acquisition deal for TravelCenters of America on February 15, the same day as the NEVI announcement.
- bp has partnered with Hertz in support of NEVI to build EV charging hubs in cities focusing on rideshare vehicles, rental vehicles, taxis and general public charging.
- FreeWire Technologies manufactured a modular EV charging station installed at a Phillips 66 station in Houston.
- FreeWire’s setup integrates energy storage into an all-in-one package that eliminates the need to install new high-voltage lines.
- FreeWire has prepped a NEVI guide to smooth the process for funding applicants and maintains relationships with gas station stakeholders.
- Last September, FreeWire announced a deal with Chevron and Texaco branded stations.
- Phillips 66 had more than 2,500 locations in the US at the time of reporting.
- Chevron and Texaco had about 8,000 locations between them at the time of reporting.
- FreeWire’s modular setup provides quick installation and more flexibility in site selection compared to conventional EV charging stations.
- A White House fact sheet stated that FreeWire announced a new 66,000 square foot global headquarters in Newark, CA, representing a $20 million investment focusing on R&D and manufacturing of ultrafast EV charging equipment.
- The same White House fact sheet noted FreeWire will create more than 200 well-paying engineering and manufacturing jobs in electrification and clean energy across the community.
- State-level transportation agencies in all 50 states are participating in the NEVI program.
- Florida Governor Ron DiSantis’s state is receiving $198 million in NEVI funding over the next five years to install more EV infrastructure, despite his public attacks on corporate ESG investing.
- Texas is set to receive $408 million in NEVI funding for EV driving infrastructure, even as Republican US Senator Ted Cruz and Governor Gregg Abbott criticize “woke capitalism.”
- Virginia Governor Glenn Youngkin turned down Ford’s new LFP battery factory, costing his state thousands of jobs.
- Virginia will receive $100 million in NEVI funding to build out its EV charging station network.
- Florida Governor Ron DiSantis, Texas Senator Ted Cruz, Texas Governor Gregg Abbott, and Virginia Governor Glenn Youngkin are all targeting the Oval Office during the 2024 election cycle.
