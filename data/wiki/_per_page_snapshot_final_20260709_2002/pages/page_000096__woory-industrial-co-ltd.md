# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-07

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-06-07/gov-kemp-woory-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-laurens-county)  ·  `page_000096`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on June 07, 2023 that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the company is helping Georgia on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, said the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- The new facility will be located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin and will be the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, so heaters for EVs are required for heating the cabin and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has led the company to become a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring for positions in personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight said Woory’s commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote more infill development while creating new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted that Dublin and Laurens County has a rapidly growing automotive sector thanks to its geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled and trained workforce.
- The partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to help fill Woory's talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects like Woory are creating jobs and opportunities in rural communities and that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities and sites prepared for development.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, the state has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory started operating a small factory producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea almost 35 years ago.
- Woory has grown into an international enterprise with production plants around the world employing over 1,300 people.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
