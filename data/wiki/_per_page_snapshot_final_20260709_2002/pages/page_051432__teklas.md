# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-11-10

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS opening its first North American Facility and Headquarters in Calhoun, Georgia - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/2020/11/10/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-teklas-to-open-north-american-headquarters-in-georgia-create-120-jobs-in-calhoun/)  ·  `page_051432`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS will invest $6.5 million to open its first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The project will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- TEKLAS is a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts.
- Governor Kemp cited Georgia's highly skilled workforce, top-notch logistics network, and pro-business environment as key factors for TEKLAS's decision.
- TEKLAS was founded in Turkey in 1971.
- The company designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications, including hoses and pipes made from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS utilizes highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes that employ advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will feature assembly lines designed to serve prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Operations at the new facility are scheduled to begin in the spring of 2021.
- TEKLAS is headquartered in Turkey and already has locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, plus sales offices in Germany and France.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the new Calhoun plant will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The new facility and headquarters will occupy a 200,000-square-foot building.
- The site is located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will hire for positions across various skill levels, including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision roles.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded state and local partners for encouraging TEKLAS's decision.
- Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Kathy Johnson commended TEKLAS for its research, advancements in EV manufacturing, and contribution to expanding the local automotive manufacturing base.
- GDEcD Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on the competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The GDEcD's Center of Innovation for Logistics provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that TEKLAS's choice to establish its North American headquarters and first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in Calhoun demonstrates Georgia's success in attracting future jobs.
- Commissioner Wilson stated that Georgia will continue to foster an ecosystem of manufacturers and suppliers on the leading edge of the automotive industry's transition to electric vehicles and e-mobility.
- TEKLAS is a global supplier of automotive fluid circulation systems for major car original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company has over 45 years of experience providing design, development, and production capabilities.
- TEKLAS provides innovative solutions for thermoplastics, rubber, and metal products to its customers.
