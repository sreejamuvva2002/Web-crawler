# Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Competitive Advantages | Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority](https://dlcda.com/business-advantages/competitive-advantages)  ·  `page_056150`

## Facts

- Dublin-Laurens County is known throughout Georgia as one of the most pro-business communities in the state.
- The community has experienced a resurgence of manufacturing and logistics jobs.
- The region has one of the nation’s lowest costs of living, which translates into lower costs for companies.
- Laurens County has the third lowest property tax rate out of 159 counties in Georgia.
- The community provides a 100% exemption on Freeport inventory that includes a company’s inventory and work in process materials.
- Millions of dollars are being invested in the region’s utility infrastructure to meet future demands while maintaining very competitive rates.
- The City of Dublin has over 1.5 million gallons per day of excess permitted water and wastewater capacity with significant pressure.
- The City has one of the most reliable natural gas networks in the country along with significant excess gas capacity.
- Customers in Georgia with over a 900 kilowatts per hour connected load can require electric companies to bid for their service.
- Georgia Power, five Electric Membership Corporations, and the City of Dublin provide electricity and natural gas throughout the region.
- These utility providers are in the midst of developments designed to significantly increase power generation capacity over the next decade.
- Companies in Middle Georgia typically enjoy electric rates priced at 15%-20% below the U.S. national average.
- Laurens County is designated as a Tier One county by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
- Tier One communities receive full state statutory incentives, including a per job state income tax credit equivalent to $4,000 per job over five years.
- Companies creating jobs in Laurens County can apply the $4,000 per job credit against 100% of their State of Georgia corporate income tax liability.
- Any remaining credits, up to $3,500 per job, can be applied to the company’s withholding tax liability and sent as a cash reimbursement to the company.
- Dublin-Laurens County is located halfway between Atlanta and Savannah with direct access to major highways, interstates, two international airports, and the fastest-growing port in North America.
- The strategic location allows businesses to transport goods and materials to 80% of the consumer market in the United States in less than a 2-hour plane ride or a 48-hour truck drive.
- Dublin is governed by a mayor and a 7-person city council, while Laurens County is governed by a 5-person county commission board.
- Matt Hatchett, a small business owner in the community, represents Dublin-Laurens County as a state representative in the Georgia House of Representatives.
- State Senator Larry Walker, III represents the interests of Dublin-Laurens County in the other chamber of the Georgia General Assembly.
- The area offers a low cost/high quality of life, small-town lifestyle, good schools, major healthcare providers, and availability of large industrial job opportunities.
