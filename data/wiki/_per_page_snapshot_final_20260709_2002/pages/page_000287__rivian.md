# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-17

**Source:** [Rivian wins in Georgia, EV maker gets green light for $5B facility](https://electrek.co/2023/07/17/rivian-wins-georgia-ev-maker-green-light-5b-facility/)  ·  `page_000287`

## Facts

- The Georgia Supreme Court declined to hear an appeal contesting the legitimacy of Rivian’s expected property tax breaks for its new $5 billion EV facility in the state.
- Rivian first announced plans for a 2,000-acre, $5 billion electric vehicle plant in Georgia in December 2021.
- Once fully operational, the Georgia facility will be capable of producing up to 400,000 EVs annually.
- Rivian's existing plant in Normal, Illinois, has a maximum production capacity of 150,000 EVs per year.
- Initial plans stated the Georgia facility would be home to Rivian's next generation of products.
- Initial plans called for construction to begin in summer 2021 and for the start of production (SOP) by 2024.
- Rivian has not yet officially broken ground on the Georgia facility.
- Five residents sued the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties and the grading contractor in October over opposition to the facility.
- Seven members of the opposition group Morgan County Land, Sky & Water Preservation previously challenged aspects of the local property tax incentives for Rivian.
- In September, a Morgan County judge rejected roughly $700 million in local property tax breaks for Rivian, which was a central part of an over $1.5 billion incentive package that also included grants, free land, and infrastructure.
- Rivian's incentive package is the second largest in Georgia state history, behind The Hyundai Motor Group's $1.8 billion in benefits for its $5.5 billion, 3,000-acre EV assembly plant in Bryan County.
- In April, a panel of judges ruled in favor of the JDA and the State of Georgia on four of five aspects of the legal controversy over the majority of Rivian's incentive package.
- The April panel declined to validate the bonds underlying the $700 million in local tax breaks Rivian expected to receive.
- In May, the court threatened to halt the factory project and asked Rivian if it wanted to terminate its agreement with the state and JDA if the tax breaks were not included.
- Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe told the Atlanta Journal-Constitution that the company is "committed to this state and this project."
- Georgia's highest court ruled to let stand an appellate court decision favoring the state and JDA, paving the way for reinstating hundreds of millions of dollars in tax savings for Rivian.
- A formal groundbreaking ceremony has not occurred, but the site is currently being graded.
- Production at the Georgia facility is now expected to begin in 2026 and will build Rivian's next-generation R2 series products.
- According to Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson, the Georgia facility is expected to employ 7,500 workers and spur thousands of supplier and related jobs.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the project's scale will attract suppliers, build a thriving community to support local businesses, and benefit the e-mobility ecosystem and dozens of other supply chain industries.
- Rivian delivered 12,640 EVs in the second quarter and produced nearly 14,000, shattering analyst expectations.
- Rivian introduced in-house Enduro drive units and LFP battery packs this past quarter, which the company says will help streamline production.
- Rivian aims to produce a total of 50,000 EVs for the year, which is more than double its 2022 production.
