# Development Authority of Bryan County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Automotive | Development Authority of Bryan County](http://bryancountyga.com/industries/automotive/)  ·  `page_000373`

## Facts

- Bryan County is located along Interstate 16 and Interstate 95.
- Bryan County provides direct access to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Bryan County is an optimal location for Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers serving Hyundai, other Southeastern auto manufacturers, and the broader U.S. automotive market.
- Boston Dynamics’ all-electric Atlas robot, powered by advanced chips, is being tested on real industrial tasks at Hyundai’s Georgia plant.
- Bryan County is situated in the heart of the booming Southeastern automotive corridor where major OEMs and automotive suppliers are investing billions into EV production and advanced manufacturing.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is located in Bryan County.
- Mercedes-Benz Vans is located 115 miles from Bryan County in Charleston, SC.
- Kia Manufacturing Plant is located 180 miles from Bryan County in West Point, GA.
- BMW Manufacturing Facility is located 210 miles from Bryan County in Greenville, SC.
- General Motors is located 460 miles from Bryan County in Spring Hill, TN.
- Ford’s BlueOval City is located 500 miles from Bryan County in Memphis, TN.
- Bryan County provides easy interstate access and rail connectivity for suppliers serving multiple OEMs across the region.
- Interstate 16 provides direct connections to Atlanta and the Southeastern U.S.
- Interstate 95 serves as a major north-south corridor connecting suppliers to the entire East Coast.
- Bryan County is located less than 20 miles from the Port of Savannah, one of the busiest and fastest-growing deepwater ports in the U.S.
- Bryan County is located 80 miles from the Port of Brunswick, which is the largest import/export automotive facility in the country.
- The Ro/Ro terminal at the Port of Brunswick handles thousands of imported and exported vehicles annually.
- Containerized logistics provide efficient transport for parts and components.
- CSX and Norfolk Southern Class I rail service ensures cost-effective transportation for inbound materials and outbound shipments.
- Direct rail connectivity to the Port of Savannah integrates Bryan County into the global automotive supply chain.
- Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport provides cargo and passenger services.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, the busiest airport in the world, is within easy reach.
- Bryan County’s multi-modal logistics network ensures on-time, cost-efficient supply chain operations for just-in-time and just-in-sequence delivery capabilities.
- Hyundai’s $12.6 billion electric vehicle manufacturing facility in Bryan County is one of the largest economic development projects in U.S. history.
- The Hyundai Metaplant involves a $12.6 Billion investment.
- The Hyundai Metaplant will create 8,500 direct jobs and thousands more in supplier support.
- The Hyundai Metaplant will produce 500,000 electric vehicles annually.
- The facility creates massive demand for parts and components.
- The expanding EV ecosystem opens opportunities for battery, electronics, and component manufacturers.
- The Hyundai Metaplant serves as the anchor for an entire supply chain ecosystem.
- Bryan County’s workforce development programs ensure suppliers have access to talent trained in advanced automation, robotics, and precision assembly.
- Regional Industry Support Enterprise (RISE) supports seven initiatives: military, underrepresented, housing, education, transportation, childcare and marketing.
- Savannah Technical College provides industry-specific training in mechatronics, welding, industrial maintenance, and advanced manufacturing.
- Fort Stewart and Hunter Army Airfield produce a steady pipeline of transitioning military personnel with technical skills in logistics, engineering, and operations.
- Georgia Quick Start offers customized workforce training at no cost to qualifying businesses.
- There are more than 500,000 workers available in the Savannah metro area.
- Automotive manufacturing employment in Georgia has grown 20 percent in the past decade.
- There is strong demand for EV and battery technology skills in the region.
- Bryan County offers Job Tax Credits providing up to $5,000 per new job created.
- Bryan County offers Investment Tax Credits to help offset costs for equipment, R&D, and infrastructure upgrades.
- Bryan County offers Fast-Track Permitting to ensure businesses can start production faster.
- Bryan County offers Utility and Infrastructure Grants to help fund site development and facility expansion.
- Bryan County offers industrial parks and shovel-ready sites designed for automotive supply chain operations.
