# Tegeta Bandag

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bandag to build tire retreading factory in Georgia | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2098479/bandag-to-build-tire-retreading-factory-in-georgia)  ·  `page_018206`

## Facts

- Bandag and its owner Bridgestone entered an agreement with Georgian company Tegeta Motors to build a $2 million (€1.7 million) retreading plant in Georgia.
- The facility will be located in Rustavi, near Georgia's capital Tbilisi.
- The Tegeta Bandag factory will have the capacity to produce up to 5,000 retreaded tires annually.
- The unit is set for operation late next year.
- The factory will be equipped with “state-of-the-art Bandag machinery” sourced from Europe and the US.
- Partners expect to begin operations by retreading tires for buses and trucks.
- The partners plan to double the factory's capacity in five years.
- Tegeta Motors will integrate the retreading operations into its existing activities, which include tire services, oil & battery operations, and recycling activities.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze, CEO of Tegeta Motors, stated: “With the launch of the Tegeta Bandag factory, we are advancing fleet solutions, promoting sustainable mobility, and strengthening our presence in the Caucasus region.”
