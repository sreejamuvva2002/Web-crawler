# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-08

**Source:** [Georgia Breaks Economic Development Records for Third Year in a Row](https://ourcommunitymedia.org/us-news/georgia-breaks-economic-development-records-for-third-year-in-a-row/)  ·  `page_017686`

## Facts

- Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1, 2022 to June 30, 2023) total investments in facility expansions and new locations exceeded $24 billion.
- FY23 projects resulted in 38,400 new jobs across 426 projects supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team.
- Excluding the two largest projects in state history (Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group) from previous year's job creation figures, total jobs as a result of FY23 projects exceeded the previous record by more than 2,800 new positions.
- Annual investment totals have increased by 131 percent over the last three years.
- Eighty-two percent of new jobs created and more than $20 billion in investments are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta attracted $3.6 billion in investment during the last fiscal year.
- Expansions account for 71 percent of total projects, while new locations account for 65 percent of new jobs announced across the state.
- Between July 2018 and July 2023, at least 40 e-mobility projects have been announced in Georgia.
- These e-mobility projects commit more than 30,000 jobs and over $25 billion in investment to the state.
- Job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent in FY23 compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Mobility and cleantech projects alone delivered three investments of $1 billion or more and six of the largest projects by job creation.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation connects public and private partners to foster innovation in strategic ecosystems for mobility and cleantech.
- Investments in aerospace increased by more than 1,000 percent since FY21, with an ecosystem that overlaps with e-mobility through companies like Archer.
- Partnerships on speed-to-market and workforce solutions, including the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification and Georgia Quick Start programs, spurred a 112 percent growth in advanced manufacturing investments since FY21.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that innovation drives a sustainable economy by developing new techniques for existing industries and building an emerging ecosystem to support cleantech and mobility of the future.
- International investments were responsible for 47 percent of expected jobs and 65 percent of total investment in FY23, with top sources including South Korea, Finland, Norway, Japan, Switzerland, and the Netherlands.
