# Georgia Logistics and Infrastructure Network

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Expanding Logistics: Georgia's Infrastrucure & Supply Chain - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/03/31/expanding-logistics-georgias-infrastrucure-supply-chain/)  ·  `page_017901`

## Facts

- Georgia’s leaders have made significant investments over the years to expand the state’s logistics network.
- Sandy Lake serves as director of logistics at the Georgia Center of Innovation, part of the state’s Department of Economic Development.
- The state’s logistics industry has experienced record-setting growth for the last five to six years.
- Current logistics activity levels are more than double pre-pandemic levels.
- A 2021 report commissioned by the Center of Innovation and the Selig Center for Economic Growth at the University of Georgia identified over 32,000 logistics establishments in Georgia.
- These logistics establishments employ more than 250,000 people across the state.
- The logistics industry generates an $83.4 billion economic impact in Georgia.
- The industry has generated $2.2 billion in tax revenue for the state and $2.3 billion in tax revenue for local governments.
- Georgia offers unique modal agility across road, rail, air, and ocean logistics compared to other states.
- The Georgia Ports Authority expanded supply chain agility by deepening the Port of Savannah in 2022 to accommodate larger ships.
- The Georgia Ports Authority completed $262 million in improvements to the Port of Brunswick.
- Two inland ports have opened in North Georgia as part of state logistics expansions.
- The Appalachian Regional Port (ARP) is located in Murray County in Northwest Georgia and opened in 2018.
- CSX provides exclusive rail services on a direct, 388-mile route between the ARP and the Port of Savannah’s Garden City Terminal.
- Each roundtrip container moved through the ARP offsets 710 truck miles.
- The ARP helps avoid 12.5 million truck miles per year on Georgia’s highways.
- Carl Campbell serves as executive director of the Murray County Industrial Development Authority.
- A manufacturer in Dalton can transport a container to the ARP, unload it, and return to its plant in less than 20 minutes.
- The ARP has an annual capacity of 75,000 containers.
- There is a plan to double the ARP’s container capacity within 10 years.
- Companies seeking proximity to the inland port have chosen Murray County for operations, including General Electric Appliances, Huali Floors, and Green Galaxy Group.
- The new GE Appliances facility in Murray County represented a $32 million investment.
- The GE Appliances facility created 100 new jobs.
- Murray County now operates a 555-acre, GRAD-certified industrial park to market to companies for investment and job growth.
- A second inland port operated by the Georgia Ports Authority is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Located in Gainesville, the Blue Ridge Connector will link the Port of Savannah to Northeast Georgia through exclusive rail service by Norfolk Southern.
- The Blue Ridge Connector will have an annual lift capacity of 200,000 containers.
- Tim Evans serves as president and CEO of the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce.
- Gainesville and Hall County host a high concentration of advanced manufacturing in pharmaceuticals, food processing, machinery, and automotive industries.
- Many local companies in Hall County import parts and components and export finished goods through the Port of Savannah.
- The Blue Ridge Connector creates a new trade route that replaces a 300-mile truck route with a 350-mile rail route.
- More than 300 companies in Hall County are engaged in manufacturing and processing, representing nearly one-third of the county’s workforce.
- Steve Syfan serves as co-founder and executive vice president of Syfan Logistics.
- Headquartered in Gainesville, Syfan Logistics specializes in transporting goods for the food, automotive, and shipping industries.
- In 2024, Syfan Logistics saw a 16% increase in load count, marking its biggest year in volume in its four-decade history.
- Syfan Logistics achieved its second highest year in revenue in 2024.
- According to TRIP, freight moved by trucks annually in Georgia is predicted to increase 67% by weight and 96% in value by 2050.
- When the Blue Ridge Connector opens in 2026, Syfan Logistics will shift some freight currently transported from deepwater ports in Savannah and Brunswick to be picked up at the new inland port.
- Syfan Logistics is asset-based and employs 100 drivers.
- The Georgia Ports Authority recently moved all roll-on/roll-off (Ro/Ro), breakbulk, and bulk product from Savannah to the Port of Brunswick.
- In January, Wallenius Wilhelmsen cut the ribbon on its new state-of-the-art facility in Brunswick.
- Wallenius Wilhelmsen is an Oslo-based carrier and one of the largest Ro/Ro carriers in the world.
- Wallenius Wilhelmsen currently employs 518 people in Georgia.
- Ryan Moore serves as president and CEO of the Golden Isles Development Authority.
- In 2023, the Golden Isles Development Authority purchased a 1,400-acre property located 7 miles west of the Port of Brunswick to create the Georgia Breakbulk Logistics Park.
- The Georgia Breakbulk Logistics Park is designed to carve out a niche in the bulk and breakbulk business for the port.
- In December 2024, the Golden Isles Development Authority received a $2 million grant from the Georgia Department of Community Affairs to prepare the industrial park for development.
- The grant is part of the OneGeorgia Authority’s Rural Site Development Initiative and will fund entrances and extend water and sewer infrastructure.
- A recent Georgia Department of Transportation report estimates freight movement will nearly double in Georgia in the next 25 years.
- In July 2024, Gov. Brian Kemp announced a $1.5 billion transportation infrastructure investment, including a $500 million freight program for projects improving efficiency, safety, and reliability.
- In October 2024, the U.S. Department of Transportation’s Federal Railroad Administration announced Georgia will receive $65 million for three rail improvement projects as part of a $2.4 billion national grant under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Norfolk Southern reports an average annual investment of $195 million in roadway and infrastructure improvements.
- Over the past five years, industry-supported rail projects across Georgia totaled $1.2 billion.
- These rail projects have supported the creation of more than 900 new jobs.
- The Georgia Logistics Summit, organized by the Center of Innovation, will be held on April 23 at the Cobb Galleria in Atlanta.
- One summit topic will be "Port to Point: Navigating Beyond the Gates."
- Ryan Moore stated that the Georgia Ports Authority is second to none globally in port operations and growth, outpacing every other port in the nation.
