# Volvo Car Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Cars brings production of global best-seller XC60 to its US plant | Volvo Cars Media Intl](https://www.volvocars.com/intl/media/press-releases/B2ED564227CDD9E8/)  ·  `page_013955`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars announced today it will add its best-selling XC60 mid-size SUV to the production line of its US car plant in Ridgeville outside Charleston, South Carolina.
- The state-of-the-art facility also assembles the fully electric flagship EX90 and the Polestar 3 SUV models.
- XC60 production at the US plant is scheduled to start in late 2026.
- In the first six months of 2025, XC60 sales in the US rose by almost 23 per cent.
- Håkan Samuelsson, CEO of Volvo Cars, stated that adding the XC60 will support and create American manufacturing jobs and reinforces the company's long-term commitment to the US market where it has sold over 5 million cars.
- The XC60 recently surpassed the 240 wagon as its all-time best-selling model, with more than 2.7 million XC60s now on global roads – and counting.
- Over 33 per cent of Volvo Cars' sales in the US are the XC60, and 25 per cent of those are plug-in hybrids.
- The XC60 is currently the fourth best-selling luxury plug-in hybrid in the US and serves as a bridge toward electrification for customers.
- Luis Rezende, President of Volvo Cars Americas, expressed gratitude to the state of South Carolina and Governor McMaster for their leadership.
- Volvo Cars broke ground on its first US manufacturing plant in 2015.
- Volvo Cars has invested USD 1.3 billion in the plant over the last decade.
- The body shop and paint shop have been renewed and expanded significantly.
- The plant also has a state-of-the-art battery pack production line.
- XC60 production will include both mild hybrid and plug-in hybrid forms to secure future local jobs at the plant.
- Volvo Cars will continue producing its fully electric, 7-seater flagship EX90 SUV at the South Carolina plant.
- Volvo Cars' production plants are located in Gothenburg, Ghent (Belgium), South Carolina (US), Chengdu, Daqing and Taizhou (China).
- As of December 2024, Volvo Cars employed approximately 42,600 full-time employees.
