# Georgia EV and Battery Supply Chain

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [fDi Intelligence – Your source for foreign direct investment information - fDiIntelligence.com](https://www.fdiintelligence.com/content/93008fa5-fb1b-5103-9a28-0b86d2f1a6c2)  ·  `page_000578`

## Facts

- The US has historically shied away from establishing an industrial policy on lithium-ion battery production but policymakers now support domestic supply chains due to stiff competition from China and the EU.
- Beijing unveiled its ‘Made in China 2025’ strategy in 2015, and Brussels launched its ‘Strategic action plan on batteries’ three years later.
- American plants are projected to deliver 486 gigawatt hours of battery capacity per year (GWh/y) by 2030, compared with China’s 2,681 GWh/y and the EU’s 778 GWh/y according to Benchmark Mineral Intelligence.
- The capacity gap is equivalent to China building one new battery megafactory every week while the US builds one every four months.
- Simon Moores, managing director of Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, states the US has been relegated to a “bystander” in the global battery arms race despite China holding only 9% of world lithium reserves but processing and manufacturing 55% of global lithium chemical compounds domestically.
- The US Department of Energy published a 10-year national strategy in June 2021 to onshore key inputs including extraction processes, electrode manufacturing, and cell manufacturing.
- President Joe Biden’s plan has created bullish sentiment for building a domestic lithium-ion economy in the US with connective thinking from the top of the government regarding supply chains.
- Outside Tesla’s Nevada gigafactory, the greatest inroads toward electrification are being made in Georgia and Tennessee.
- Korean energy company SK Innovation plans to become a top three global EV supplier with its cornerstone $2.6bn investment in two battery plants based in Jackson County, Georgia.
- SK Innovation’s Georgia plants will supply automakers Ford and Volkswagen.
- US car manufacturer General Motors announced in April it will build a second $2.3bn battery plant in Spring Hill, Tennessee as part of a joint venture with Korean battery maker LG Energy Solution.
- The GM facility is set to be 2.8 million square feet and open in late 2023.
- A spokesperson for LG Energy Solution notes the US is one of the biggest EV battery markets by size with potential to grow at the fastest rate among major markets due to the Biden administration’s emphasis on clean energy and the EV battery value chain.
- Georgia has 130,000 people working in the automotive sector, and the transition to EVs is forcing automakers to rethink operations and supply chains.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, states the industry will see more change over the next 20 years than in the previous 100 years and the state wants to be part of the process across the entire supply chain from refining rare minerals to automobile assembly.
- Bob Rolfe, commissioner of the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development, shares this view stating every single company is going all-in on EVs.
- Battery chemistry advancements include Volkswagen seeing lithium-ion battery energy density double since 2014 and the International Energy Agency reporting unit cost per kWh has fallen by 87% in the past decade.
- Vanessa Witte, senior analyst at Wood Mackenzie, notes battery packs are registered as hazardous goods and can be up to a quarter of a vehicle’s weight, rendering them unsuitable for shipping in bulk over long distances.
- The risks of just-in-time supply chain models that sacrifice resilience for efficiency have been highlighted by events such as the pandemic.
- Companies are trying to shore up local supply chains due to international supply chain blind spots.
- Benchmark Mineral Intelligence estimates global annual raw lithium supply of 440,000 tonnes will meet EV market demand of 432,000 tonnes for 2021, but demand will outstrip supply by 60% (2.4 million tonnes to 1.5 million tonnes) by the end of the decade.
- Hau Thai-Tang, Ford’s chief product platform and operations officer, told CNBC in May 2021 that while Ford doesn’t have to scale today to justify a dedicated battery plant, by 2025 volume from the F-150, E-Transit, and another announced BEV will justify it.
- Simon Moores expects consolidation in the space with automakers acquiring battery manufacturers from initial joint ventures, but limiting factors will be lithium compound shortages or lagging consumer demand.
- Federal tax credits to incentivize electric passenger cars were phased out for most automakers at the end of 2019, dramatically raising costs for consumers.
- The US government needs to find the right mix of policies to make EVs affordable and build out domestic supply chain capacity.
