# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Metaplant: Cutting-Edge EV Factory - IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/hyundai-metaplant-georgia)  ·  `page_000280`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant is located in Ellabell, Georgia, on land that was bare fields less than three years ago.
- The facility represents a major investment in the future of EV and automobile manufacturing in America.
- The 2026 Hyundai Ioniq9, a three-row electric SUV tested at the plant, offers up to 530 kilometers (335 miles) of driving range.
- The Ioniq9 features a native NACS connector that allows owners to use Tesla Superchargers with Plug and Charge ease.
- Hyundai invested US $7.6 billion in the Metaplant.
- The inaugural 2025 Ioniq5 rolled off the plant's assembly line in October.
- Ioniq models are fully eligible for $7,500 consumer tax credits for U.S.-built EVs with North American batteries.
- The tax credits are currently on the Trump administration’s chopping block.
- The factory provides Hyundai a bulwark against potential tariffs and positions the automaker ahead of many rivals.
- The plant consists of 11 cavernous buildings spanning 697,000 square meters (7.5 million square feet).
- It is set to become America’s largest dedicated plant for EVs and hybrids with a capacity for 500,000 Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis models per year.
- Tesla’s Texas Gigafactory has a production capacity of 375,000 vehicles.
- Company executives claim the Metaplant is North America’s most heavily automated factory and a showcase for AI and robotic tech.
- Workers, called "Meta Pros," can park in nearly 1,900 spaces beneath solar roofs that provide up to 5 percent of the plant’s electricity.
- Hyundai has a target of obtaining 100 percent of its energy from renewable sources.
- The plant uses hydrogen trucks from Hyundai-owned Xcient, the world’s first commercialized hydrogen fuel-cell semis.
- A fleet of 21 hydrogen trucks hauls parts from area suppliers using 400-kilometer driving ranges with zero tailpipe emissions.
- Most finished vehicles are shipped by rail rather than truck to reduce fossil-fuel emissions and the carbon footprint.
- Approximately 850 robots at the plant unload parts from the hydrogen trucks at the docks.
- About 300 automated guided vehicles (AGVs) move around the factory without tracks, smartly avoiding human workers.
- An AI-based procurement and logistics system uses AGVs to automatically allocate and ferry parts for just-in-time delivery, saving space, time, and money otherwise used for stockpiling.
- Jerry Roach, senior manager of general assembly, states that the automated system delivers the right parts to the right station at the right time without relying on people to make decisions.
- "Bodies in white," the building blocks of modern unibody car chassis, are welded by 475 robots in the welding shop.
- Sled-like AGVs slide beneath electric Hyundais rolling off the line to autonomously grab wheels and ferry finished cars to parking areas without human drivers.
- Spots, robotic quadrupeds designed by Hyundai-owned Boston Dynamics, use 360-degree vision and "athletic intelligence" to detect potential defects on car welds.
- Hyundai plans to deploy Atlas humanoid robots from Boston Dynamics throughout its global factories for non-union roles.
- Atlas robots are being trained to sense environments, avoid collisions, and manipulate/move parts in complex sequences.
- Hyundai executives state that robots will not replace autoworkers despite their advanced capabilities.
- The plant houses about 850 robots in total, including 475 in the welding shop alone.
- An onsite steel stamping plant is so quiet that no ear protection is required for workers.
- Robots at the stamping plant automatically stamp out roofs, fenders, and hoods and store them in soaring racks overhead.
- Jerry Roach notes the Metaplant allowed Hyundai to design an electrified car plant from the ground up, unlike Tesla and Rivian who had to retrofit existing factories in California and Illinois.
- Automation is acknowledged as inevitable for replacing some human workers, but it also frees humans from heavy lifting and repetitive tasks.
- Collaborative robots install bulky doors on the assembly line without physical safety separation, performing the task perfectly and consistently.
- Jerry Roach emphasizes that automation frees people to focus on precision tasks requiring dexterity, vision, intelligence, and skill, stating "I want my people doing craftsmanship."
- The plant currently employs 1,340 Meta Pros with an annual average pay of $58,100.
- This average pay is 25 percent higher than the average in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai's annual local payroll has already reached $497 million.
- The company foresees eventually creating 8,500 jobs on site and another 7,000 indirect jobs for local suppliers and businesses.
- Hyundai currently sources battery cells from Georgia and SK On, with some Ioniq5 batteries imported from Hungary.
- The Metaplant campus includes the HL-GA battery company, a $4 billion joint operation with LG Energy Solutions.
- HL-GA plans to produce nickel-cobalt-magnesium cells beginning next year, with packs assembled on site by Hyundai's Mobis subsidiary.
- Hyundai is on track to open a second $5 billion battery plant in Georgia as a joint operation with SK On.
- These projects are part of Hyundai's planned $21 billion in U.S. investment between now and 2028.
- This planned investment exceeds the $20 billion Hyundai has invested since entering the U.S. market in 1986.
