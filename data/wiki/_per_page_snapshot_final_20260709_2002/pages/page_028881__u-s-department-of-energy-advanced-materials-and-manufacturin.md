# U.S. Department of Energy Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-19

**Source:** [Funding Selections: Platform Technologies for Transformative Battery Manufacturing | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/funding-selections-platform-technologies-transformative-battery-manufacturing)  ·  `page_028881`

## Facts

- On December 19, 2024, the U.S. Department of Energy’s Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO) announced 11 funding selections through its Platform Technologies for Transformative Battery Manufacturing program.
- The total federal funding amount for the 11 selected projects is $25,540,000.
- The program's goal is to create platform materials and technologies for manufacturing sodium-ion batteries, flow batteries, and nanolayered films, along with systems frameworks to leverage smart manufacturing technologies in battery production.
- Selected projects will support flexible, scalable, and highly controllable battery manufacturing processes.
- The 11 projects directly support the growth of domestic battery manufacturing capabilities and the DOE’s Energy Storage Grand Challenge and Long-Duration Storage Shot goals.
- AM Batteries, Inc. received $2,790,000 to develop a novel dry electrode manufacturing process for sodium-ion batteries in Billerica, Massachusetts.
- AM Batteries partners with Unigrid and The Laboratory for Energy Storage and Conversion at The University of Chicago.
- AM Batteries' project will develop solvent-free electrode coating technology to enable advanced sodium-ion chemistry, potentially improving energy density, reducing manufacturing costs and energy use, enabling scalability, and fostering collaboration.
- Argonne National Laboratory received $1,490,000 for a pilot continuous hydrothermal manufacturing process for hard carbon production from domestic petroleum coke feedstocks in Lemont, Illinois.
- Argonne partners with ACT-ion Battery Technologies.
- The hydrothermal process uses various feedstocks to produce fine-tuned high-performance hard carbon via rapid carbonization and condensation at low temperatures, offering a lower-cost alternative with promising performance for a competitive U.S. sodium-ion battery supply chain.
- Clean Republic SODO LLC d/b/a Dakota Lithium Materials received $1,990,000 to manufacture high-performance NaFePO4-based cathode materials in Seattle, Washington & Grand Forks, North Dakota.
- Clean Republic partners with Boise State University and Savannah River National Laboratory.
- The project leverages an eco-friendly pilot-scale lithium iron phosphate process to produce low-cost cathode materials for long-duration sodium-ion batteries, using dry mixing of feedstocks like sodium carbonate and iron phosphate with bio-additives like glucose to decrease energy consumption and eliminate water/solvents.
- Quino Energy, Inc. received $2,590,000 to develop low-cost, large tank form factor redox flow batteries leveraging existing fuel storage infrastructure in San Leandro, California.
- Quino Energy partners with CellCube, Richardson Electronics, South Coast Terminals, and the University of Houston.
- The project will develop aqueous organic quinone redox flow battery (QRFB) technology in large carbon steel tanks to reduce balance of plant costs and installation, gaining acceptance by site owners and insurers.
- Arkema Inc. received $2,140,000 for high performance non-perfluorosulfonic acid membranes for next generation redox flow batteries in King of Prussia, Pennsylvania.
- Arkema partners with Tetramer Technologies, Stryten Energy, & Pacific Northwest National Laboratory.
- The project will build a scalable platform to tailor non-PFSA ion exchange membranes for flow batteries operating under low/neutral pH, manufacturing more conductive, selective, and durable membranes at lower cost than current state-of-the-art.
- University of Akron received $1,590,000 for scalable manufacturing of anion exchange membranes with polymers of intrinsic microporosity from commodity polymers in Akron, Ohio.
- University of Akron partners with Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, Parker Hannifin Corp. & Schaeffler Group USA.
- The project will develop polymers tailored for RFB membranes to enhance ionic conductivity, selectivity, and stability, plus optimize scalable manufacturing processes for large-area membranes efficiently and cost-effectively.
- University of Maryland: College Park received $2,590,000 for microfabricated nanolayers for solid-state microbatteries and ionic capacitors in College Park, Maryland.
- University of Maryland partners with Forge Nano.
- The project aims to strengthen manufacturable battery technology by transferring atomic layer deposition processes from lab-scale to manufacturing scale tools for commercial applications.
- Illinois Institute of Technology and Spraying System Co. received $2,590,000 for novel spray deposition for large-scale production of multilayered dielectric capacitors and rechargeable batteries in Chicago, Illinois and Glendale Heights, Illinois.
- The project will develop a dry electrostatic spray process and prototype machine to fabricate sandwich-structured capacitors and battery electrodes with uniform thickness over large areas.
- High-energy density dielectric capacitors and rechargeable batteries from this project will greatly improve energy harvesting during regenerative braking of electric vehicles.
- Charge CCCV received $2,590,000 for advancing lithium-ion battery electrode manufacturing through machine-learning enabled in-situ quality control in Vestal, New York.
- Charge CCCV partners with FastCap Systems Corp d/b/a Nanoramic Laboratories & Argonne National Laboratory.
- The project will develop a manufacturing-scale smart digital platform to utilize in-line data and make predictions via APIs, using datasets from national lab/industry partners to create models for in-line production decision making.
- This will enable progress in physics-driven simulation at cell/factory levels, improve digital twin capabilities, reduce R&D overheads, and eliminate costly wasted trials.
- American Lithium Energy Corporation received $2,590,000 for innovative non-invasive sensorics for process efficiency, control, and traceability in Carlsbad, California.
- American Lithium Energy partners with Siemens Corporation, ReElement Technologies, & Voltaiq.
- The project will take a holistic approach to smart manufacturing by incorporating continuous in-process improvement loops via sensor integration and data-driven insights across cell/pack buildup and factory floor optimization.
- It will evaluate an ecosystem approach for on-site recycling of manufacturing scrap for zero-waste capability and adopt digital thread/tools for improved battery cell performance metrics.
- Titan Advanced Energy Solutions received $2,590,000 for smart battery manufacturing enabled by real-time high resolution cell morphology monitoring and control in Salem, Massachusetts.
- Titan partners with Harris Hill Automation & Bureau Veritas.
- The project will develop a cell inspection platform using data-driven digital techniques to reduce manufacturing quality costs, focusing on defect detection and cell uniformity to support predictive analytics and integrate into factory data pipelines.
- This intelligent inspection technology will accelerate time to market and improve scalability for U.S. battery cell manufacturers, raising domestic competitiveness.
