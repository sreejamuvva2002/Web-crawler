# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-03

**Source:** [Rivian Automotive: Georgia sets $1.5B in aid for electric vehicle maker Rivian, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/georgia-sets-1-5b-in-aid-for-electric-vehicle-maker-rivian/91278025)  ·  `page_059059`

## Facts

- Georgia sets $1.5 billion in aid for electric vehicle maker Rivian.
- The incentive package would be by far the largest Georgia has ever offered to a company.
- It would be the 15th largest incentive package any American state has ever given a private entity according to Good Jobs First, a group critical of subsidies to private companies.
- The state of Georgia and local governments will give Rivian Automotive $1.5 billion in incentives to build a 7,500-job, $5 billion electric vehicle plant east of Atlanta, according to documents the company and state signed Monday.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson says the size of the package reflects the largest single industrial announcement in Georgia history.
- Rivian pledged to reach full investment and job targets by the end of 2028.
- Jobs at the new plant will pay an average of $56,000 a year plus benefits.
- Pat Wilson stated: "It's absolutely appropriate because they're creating more jobs."
- Rivian is based in Irvine, California.
- Rivian is a startup manufacturer of electric trucks and commercial delivery vans.
- Rivian challenges established automakers like Ford and General Motors as well as electric vehicle leader Tesla.
- The company is already producing vehicles at its facility in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian hopes to break ground on the Georgia plant as early as this summer.
- Rivian plans to begin production in 2024.
- Rivian aims to produce 400,000 vehicles a year in Georgia.
- The Georgia plant site covers 2,000 acres (800 hectares).
- Local residents oppose the development, arguing it will spoil their rural quality of life.
- The site is located between Social Circle and Rutledge.
- The site is approximately 45 miles (70 kilometers) east of downtown Atlanta.
- The site is situated in an area just beyond the frontier of intensive suburban development.
- The state took over planning and zoning for the project after opponents overwhelmed Morgan County officials.
- Residents have voiced concerns about possible well-water contamination, light pollution, and the disruption of wildlife habitats and farmland.
- A site plan released Monday shows Rivian is responding to resident concerns by shifting the plant footprint away from wetlands.
- Rivian agreed to limit light pollution at the site.
- Local governments have agreed to $700 million in property tax breaks for the project.
- Rivian has agreed to make more than $900 million in payments in lieu of taxes over 25 years beginning in 2023.
- The state will spend $200 million to buy the site, grade it, build road improvements including a new Interstate 20 interchange, and extend utilities.
- The state will spend $62.5 million to build a dedicated training center for the project.
- The state will spend a projected $27 million on providing job training.
- The incentive package includes a $200 million income tax credit calculated at $5,250 per job over five years.
- If Rivian does not owe enough state corporate income tax to utilize the full credit, the state will provide personal income taxes collected from workers instead.
- Georgia estimates Rivian will save $175 million from a sales tax exemption on machinery.
- Georgia estimates Rivian will save $105 million from a sales tax exemption on construction materials.
- Kia previously received more than $450 million in incentives for its plant southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia has promised $300 million in incentives to a $2.6 billion, 2,600-worker battery plant being built by SK Innovation in Commerce, Georgia, northeast of Atlanta.
- Rivian currently plans two consumer models: the R1T pickup with a base price of $67,500 and the R1S SUV with a base price of $70,000.
- The R1T truck was recently named Motor Trend's 2022 truck of the year.
- Amazon owns a stake in Rivian.
- Amazon has ordered 100,000 delivery vans from Rivian, launching the company into the commercial vehicle business.
- Rivian raised $11.9 billion through a stock offering on Nov. 10 that took the company public.
- The proceeds from the stock offering will allow Rivian to finance the new Georgia plant.
