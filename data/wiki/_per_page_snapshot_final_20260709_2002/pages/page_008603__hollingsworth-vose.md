# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-03

**Source:** [Hollingsworth & Vose to Expand and Create 25 Jobs in Floyd County - Virginia's New River Valley](https://newrivervalleyva.org/hollingsworth-vose-to-expand-and-create-25-jobs-in-floyd-county/)  ·  `page_008603`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V), a global manufacturer of advanced materials used in filtration, battery, and industrial applications, will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County, Virginia.
- The expansion includes adding over 28,000 square feet to H&V's facility at 365 Christiansburg Pike Northeast to accommodate new production equipment for increased demand.
- Virginia successfully competed with Georgia for this project, which will create 25 new jobs.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced the investment and stated that H&V has generated positive economic impact and job opportunities in Floyd County for more than four decades.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin noted that businesses with a long history of expansion in Virginia offer powerful testimonials on why a Virginia location is a foundation for success.
- Virginia Secretary of Commerce and Trade Caren Merrick stated that H&V has played a role in the success of Virginia’s advanced materials industry over the past 46 years.
- Secretary Caren Merrick thanked H&V for creating high-quality jobs in Floyd County, citing the New River Valley’s strategic location and business-friendly environment.
- H&V CEO Josh Ayer stated that the company has been part of the Floyd, Virginia community since 1976 and considers the facility essential to serving global and domestic customers.
- CEO Josh Ayer explained that Virginia was chosen for the expansion due to its positive business environment and strong support from the Commonwealth of Virginia and Floyd County.
- Floyd County Board of Supervisors Chairman Joe Turman stated that H&V's new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county's 191-year history.
- Delegate Marie March stated that the expansion will provide crucial jobs for local families and enable them to keep working at home.
- Delegate Marie March welcomed the investment, noting that private enterprise ventures reward and benefit the local community.
- Hollingsworth & Vose was incorporated in 1892 and is headquartered in East Walpole, Massachusetts.
- H&V's advanced materials contribute to a cleaner world through products providing clean air, clean liquids, and energy storage.
- The company has been family-owned for seven generations.
- H&V established its first Virginia facility in Floyd County in 1976 and currently employs over 200 workers.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
