# Georgia Electric Vehicle Supply Chain

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-19

**Source:** [After Trump pulls the plug, can Georgia still become the "EV capital of America?"](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/trump-pulls-federal-support-georgia-103507655.html)  ·  `page_001535`

## Facts

- Enacted July 4, Trump's One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) ends federal tax credits for electric vehicles by the end of September and tax breaks for American battery manufacturers in 2030.
- More clean energy projects were cancelled in the first quarter of 2025 than the past two years combined, according to research group Atlas Public Policy.
- Since January, cancellations and downsized clean energy projects have amounted to roughly $14 billion in abandoned investments.
- The abandoned investments include a $1 billion factory planned to produce thermal barriers for batteries in Statesboro.
- Georgia rolled out a red carpet worth billions of dollars in tax breaks for EV manufacturing plants, intended to become Gov. Brian Kemp-proclaimed 'electric vehicle capital of America' by the end of his second term.
- Industry leaders have brushed aside Washington's setbacks and boasted the industry's growth, which they claim is headed towards an independent supply chain that will rely less on federal support.
- Georgia Public Service Commissioner Tim Echols stated: 'The tax credits played a purpose, but that purpose may be over. We may not need a subsidy in the future.'
- Electrification Coalition Policy Manager Skye Golann said at an Aug. 7 press briefing titled 'How EV Investments Are Powering Prosperity in Georgia': 'We must develop an American critical mineral supply chain.'
- The Biden administration's Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) passed in August 2022 provided tax credits, subsidies and incentives for the purchasing and manufacturing of EVs and their charging facilities.
- A 2024 report by the Rhodium Group and MIT found that investments in batteries, fueling equipment, and critical minerals refining and processing have each grown at least three-fold since the IRA.
- In 2022, the U.S. Department of Energy granted $178 million for the construction of Solvay Specialty Polymers' battery-grade polyvinylidene fluoride facility in Augusta.
- Last year, the DOE awarded $80 million to Fort Valley-based Blue Bird for its electric school bus manufacturing and workforce development.
- Cox Automotive Director of Industry Insights Stephanie Streaty noted that EV purchases ticked up in the U.S., spurred by an IRA credit offering a $7,500 discount for a new EV and $4,000 or 30% for a used EV.
- Last year was a record year of 1.3 million EVs sold, according to Streaty.
- The Georgia Clean Energy Roadshow hosted by Echols at Savannah Technical College on Aug. 12 featured over 60 vehicles, including one of the City of Savannah's Nissan Leafs starting at about $30,000.
- When Echols first began the roadshow 15 years ago, only the Tesla Roadster was on display, worth more than $100,000 at the time.
- This year, the average EV transaction was $10,000 more expensive compared to a gas vehicle, according to Cox Automotive data.
- Before passing the OBBBA, Cox Automotive predicted that EVs would make up 10% of all cars sold for 2025, up 2% from last year.
- Cox adjusted its EV sales prediction to 8.5% due to tariffs, interest rates, and other headwinds, according to Streaty.
- Skye Golann stated: 'Taking away the federal tax credits will dampen some of the growth. I certainly think that the One Big Beautiful Bill Act is a setback for EVs.'
- Lithium-ion batteries could make up 40% of the cost of a car, said Streaty.
- China still dominates the battery's mineral processing, admitted Golann.
- The U.S. is home to just 1% of the world's lithium reserves, according to the World Economic Forum.
- On Aug. 6, GM announced plans to co-manufacture six EV models with Hyundai, including an electric van for the North American market.
- A day later, GM inked a deal to import Chinese batteries that will function as a stopgap until it can build its own lithium batteries at lower cost in the U.S., according to the Detroit Free Press.
- About half of Kia's manufacturing for EV and gas vehicles remains overseas, said Kia's Head of Department Corporate Services and Information Technology Ted Arnold.
- Hyundai, a parent company of Kia, is in the process of bringing suppliers locally so they don't have to import, according to Arnold.
- The OBBBA will end the 45X tax credit for U.S. battery manufacturers in five years, a move that could cede growth to China, Mexico, Canada and members of the European Union, according to think tank Energy Innovation.
- In January, Norway-based Freyr Battery called off a battery manufacturing plant in Newnan, stating it in a letter to Coweta County.
- In February, Aspen Aerogels told investors it halted construction of its second plant manufacturing thermal barriers for EV batteries in Statesboro and will instead increase production in China, per Latitude Media.
- In March, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America President José Muñoz announced an expansion focused on hybrid vehicles at its grand opening, a diversion from its expected all-electric operations.
- Streaty noted: 'The IRA 45x reduction credit doesn't sunset until 2030, so I think that's a glimmer of light.'
- Golann stated that domestic and statewide supply chain growth will take some time.
- Dealers are getting aggressive with rebates, deals and discounts as the IRA goes away, according to Streaty.
- Echols noted that manufacturers have already spent money to build assembly lines and factories and will want to put incentives on those cars.
- Streaty stated that EVs eventually need to be able to sell on their own without constant incentives.
