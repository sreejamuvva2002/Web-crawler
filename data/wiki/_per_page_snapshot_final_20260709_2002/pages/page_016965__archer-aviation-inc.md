# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-12

**Source:** [Archer Completes Construction of High-Volume Manufacturing Facility in Georgia | Design 2 Part Magazine](https://www.d2pmagazine.com/2025/02/12/archer-completes-construction-of-high-volume-manufacturing-facility-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_016965`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. recently completed construction of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The approximately 400,000-square-foot aircraft manufacturing facility is referred to as ARC and serves as the planned production site for Archer’s all-electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, Midnight.
- Archer received the certificate of occupancy for the ARC facility and held a commemorative ribbon cutting ceremony in December.
- At the time of writing, Archer was working to complete tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at the facility.
- The facility is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is planning to begin production of its all-electric, vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft at the Covington facility early this year.
- Production is planned to begin in early 2025.
- Archer aims to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of the year.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with auto manufacturer Stellantis.
- The two companies are working to finalize an agreement whereby Stellantis would work with Archer as its exclusive contract manufacturer to mass produce Archer’s Midnight eVTOL aircraft at the facility.
- As part of the partnership, Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, and experienced personnel.
- Archer stated a goal to scale the facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, stated that the completion of the facility testifies to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization.
- Goldstein noted that with construction on ARC now complete, the team is focused on the start of production planned for early next year and emphasized execution and scaling.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
- Arnaud Deboeuf, Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer, stated that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp offered congratulations to Archer, the Covington community, and all the hardworking Georgians who will find meaningful employment at the campus.
- Governor Kemp noted that when they announced Archer’s choice to build this new manufacturing facility in Newton County, it was just the start of an incredible relationship.
- Governor Kemp stated that today’s announcement marks the next step in that journey, reaffirming Georgia’s commitment to working with innovative companies like Archer to create quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
