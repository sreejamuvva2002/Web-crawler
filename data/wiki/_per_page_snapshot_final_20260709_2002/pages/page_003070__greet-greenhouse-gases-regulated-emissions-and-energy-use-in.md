# GREET (Greenhouse gases, Regulated Emissions, and Energy use in Technologies)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [GREET | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/greet)  ·  `page_003070`

## Facts

- The Department of Energy developed the GREET life cycle analysis to assess environmental impacts associated with technologies, fuels, products, and energy systems across various stages of the supply chain.
- For any given energy or transportation system, GREET calculates total energy consumption (non-renewable and renewable), fossil fuel energy use, greenhouse gas emissions, air pollutant emissions, and water consumption.
- The Argonne National Laboratory R&D GREET Model evaluates energy use and emissions output of transportation and energy sector technologies to assess research and development progress and inform RD&D direction.
- R&D GREET is available both as a Microsoft Excel file and as a .NET tool.
- 40BSAF-GREET is the model adopted by the U.S. Department of the Treasury to calculate emissions reduction percentages under the Sustainable Aviation Fuel Credit, § 40B(e)(2) of the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The latest version of 40BSAF-GREET was released in October 2024.
- 45VH2-GREET is the model adopted by the U.S. Department of the Treasury to determine emissions rates for purposes of the Clean Hydrogen Production Tax Credit under Title 26 of the U.S. Code, Section 45V.
- The latest version of 45VH2-GREET was released in December 2025.
- 45ZCF-GREET is the model adopted by the U.S. Department of the Treasury to determine emissions rates under the Clean Fuel Production Credit, Section 45Z of the Internal Revenue Code.
- The latest version of 45ZCF-GREET was released in June 2026.
- CA-GREET4.0 is a modified version of the Argonne National Laboratory GREET1 2022 model designed to reflect California-specific fuel pathways and generate carbon intensities for those pathways.
- The ICAO-GREET version estimates and verifies default core life cycle assessment values of CORSIA-approved safe aviation fuel pathways.
- The GREET suite of models was developed by Argonne National Laboratory in 1994, with the first version released in 1995.
- The development and applications of GREET have been an integral part of DOE’s research, development & demonstration efforts to pursue energy and material technologies for energy efficiency and affordability.
- GREET models are developed for specific use cases aiming to guide decision-making, research and development, and regulations related to the transportation and energy sectors.
- Users seeking a GREET model for regulatory compliance must review guidance specific to that program to ascertain the appropriate version.
