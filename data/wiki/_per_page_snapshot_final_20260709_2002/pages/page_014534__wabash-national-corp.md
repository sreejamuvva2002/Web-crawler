## Facts

- Wabash National Corp reported a 19% sequential backlog increase to $837 million, citing improving spot and contract rates and better freight indicators.
- Management cited ATA for-hire truck tonnage showing the largest year-over-year increase since October 2022 as a sign of recovery toward 2027.
- Wabash National is opening new upfit sites in Chicago, Atlanta, and Phoenix to improve proximity and win rates.
- Management expects incremental revenue per new upfit site of $10 million to $20 million at peak with gross margins approaching approximately 20%.
- Wabash National's Lafayette South plant added dry van capacity, with management estimating approximately 10,000 incremental trailers compared to prior upcycles.
- Management expects relatively muted early hiring needs for the Lafayette South expansion, anticipating a ramp into 2027 due to flexible capacity.
- Wabash National is scaling its SPECT SYNC digital enablement platform to reduce quoting and configuration friction, targeting AI and digital tools to improve selling, tracking, maintenance decisions, and parts and services recurring revenue.
- Key operating metrics including on-time-to-promise, first-time quality, and total recordable incident rates continued to improve.
- Total injuries declined 9% sequentially and 42% year-over-year, with management citing overall injury rate improvements of 7% and 19% versus 2025.
- Wabash National recognized $3 million of previously announced plant idling costs in Q1 as planned, invested approximately $4 million in capital expenditures in Q1, and returned $3.5 million to shareholders via dividends.
- Total liquidity, including cash and available borrowings, was reported at $165 million, with management engaging with banks on an asset-backed loan ahead of September 2026.
- Wabash National guided for Q2 revenue of $380 million to $400 million and adjusted EPS of negative $0.40 to negative $0.60.
- Management reiterated that Q1 was the low point, expects sequential improvement each quarter, and anticipates positive adjusted EBITDA in 2026.
