# Preciball USA, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-06

**Source:** [Preciball USA Invests $17 Million in Screven County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/preciball-usa-invests-17-million-screven-county)  ·  `page_002193`

## Facts

- Preciball USA plans to invest $17.6 million in a new production facility in Screven County.
- The project will create 65 new jobs over the next several years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment on February 06, 2026, describing Preciball USA as one of the small-to-medium-sized manufacturers whose products are critical to entire industries that call Georgia home.
- Preciball USA designs and manufactures precision and industrial balls for industrial use.
- The company’s products are used in applications such as bearings, pumps, valves, swivel joints, and more.
- Preciball USA’s primary offices will remain open in Pooler.
- Don Peak, Executive Vice President of Preciball USA, stated the new high-precision manufacturing facility will complement existing distribution operations and offer fully integrated, end-to-end domestic production.
- The new manufacturing facility will be located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD)-certified Screven County Industrial Park in Sylvania.
- The company will hire for positions including management, engineering, quality technicians, industrial maintenance, and production roles.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2027.
- Speaker Jon Burns stated the generational investment by Preciball USA will provide citizens of Screven County with employment opportunities for years to come.
- Chairman Stephen Milner of the Screven County Development Authority noted the project reflects years of preparation at the Screven County Industrial Park and brings high-quality jobs and long-term economic value.
- Chairman Will Boyd of the Screven County Board of Commissioners stated the investment strengthens the county's tax base, creates quality jobs, and ensures the county can continue to provide essential services while planning responsibly for future growth.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Dorie Bacon represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Screven County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson credited Governor Kemp, the Georgia General Assembly, and collaboration with the Department of Community Affairs for programs like the Rural Site Development Initiative benefiting communities statewide.
- Preciball USA is part of the Precision Ball Group, a global organization rooted in Belgium’s Peltzer & Fils Group, whose origins trace back to the early 18th century.
