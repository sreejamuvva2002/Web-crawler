# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-18

**Source:** [Aurubis AG starts construction of a € 300 million (approx. $ 320 million) multimetal recycling plant in Augusta, Georgia](https://www.aurubis.fi/richmond-aurubis-com/richmond/media/Press-Releases/june-18-22-aurubis-ag-starts-construction-of-a-300-million-approx-320-million-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-augusta-georgia)  ·  `page_017297`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG, a 150-year-old German-based company, broke ground on June 18, 2022, on the United States’ first large secondary smelter for multimetal recycling.
- The new facility, Aurubis Richmond, is located in Augusta, Richmond County, Georgia.
- Total investment in the Augusta project is estimated at €300 million (approx. $320 million).
- This represents the largest single investment made by a German company in Georgia history.
- The plant is expected to be fully operational by mid-2024.
- Once fully online, Aurubis Richmond will have the capacity to process about 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- Processed materials include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other recycling materials containing metals such as copper, nickel, tin, and other industrial and precious metals.
- The facility will create over 120 new direct jobs.
- Aurubis Richmond is expected to become a significant employer in greater Augusta, GA, and a major player in the North American supply chain.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated that the US currently produces about 6 million tons of recycling materials containing valuable metals, which are currently shipped to Asia and Europe or trucked to local landfills.
- Harings added that the company wants to take advantage of this potential to ensure these materials get reused as critical input materials for many US industries.
- Aurubis’ metals are crucial enablers for global market trends such as renewable energy, digitalization, and electric vehicles.
- The facility will reduce waste and direct key metals to industries including solar and wind renewables, along with electric vehicle and battery manufacturers.
- Copper is 100-percent recyclable without any loss in performance, but industrial-scale secondary smelter recycling requires significant long-term investment.
- The plant utilizes state-of-the-art environmental technology that meets or exceeds the highest State of Georgia and federal government standards.
- Aurubis Richmond will have a very low impact on regional air quality, contributing less than 0.2% share of dust emissions in Richmond County (based on actual emissions at Aurubis' recycling site in Germany).
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Aurubis as one of more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia and congratulated state and local economic development teams for bringing the sustainability-focused company to Augusta.
- Kemp noted that the groundbreaking represents what is made possible in Georgia by strong partnerships and world-class business assets.
- The facility demonstrates a gold-standard commitment to sustainable growth and the circular economy.
