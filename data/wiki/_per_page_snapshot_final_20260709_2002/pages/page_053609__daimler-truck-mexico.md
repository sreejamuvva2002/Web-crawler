# Daimler Truck Mexico

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Daimler Truck Mexico Delivers First Electric Freightliner and Unveils Second Charging Station](https://evmagz.com/daimler-truck-mexico-delivers-first-electric-freightliner-and-unveils-second-charging-station/)  ·  `page_053609`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America LLC’s Mexican division delivered the initial two battery electric Freightliner eCascadias to Transportes Esteban.
- The Freightliner eCascadias were manufactured in Portland, Oregon.
- Transportes Esteban is a prominent cargo transportation company with 22 years of industry expertise.
- The electric Class 8 truck is slated for regular operations within Daimler Truck Mexico’s truck manufacturing plant (TMP) in Santiago.
- The Santiago truck manufacturing plant has been designated as the site for Mexico’s second commercial vehicle charging facility specifically tailored for heavy-duty truck electric charging.
- DTNA’s Mexican branch drew insights from the pioneering “Electric Island” at DTNA headquarters in Portland.
- Marcela Barreiro serves as President & CEO of Daimler Truck Mexico.
- Marcela Barreiro highlighted the commencement of operations for the first all-electric Freightliner unit in Mexico and the inauguration of the second Electric Island in Mexico.
- The second “Electric Island” charging station is situated within the Daimler Truck Santiago TMP for use by the Freightliner eCascadia as part of daily logistics operations.
- This investment aligns with a broader project to curtail the plant’s emissions while leveraging DTNA’s advanced technology.
- The second “Electric Island” installation features a Detroit eFill charger with a charging capacity of up to 120 kW.
- The charger is capable of charging 80% of the eCascadia’s battery capacity in 220 minutes.
- The Detroit eFill chargers incorporate intelligent charging management software that optimizes energy consumption and integrates vehicle information, stations, and the charging process through a telematics platform.
- Since its production debut in 2022, the battery-electric Freightliner eCascadia has been distributed to over 50 fleets in North America.
- The Freightliner eCascadia has accumulated more than 3.5 million electric miles in real-world customer applications to date.
- Daimler Truck Mexico is taking a significant stride in the transition to zero-emission mobility with this delivery and future plans.
