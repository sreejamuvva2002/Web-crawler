# Continental Tire the Americas LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SC: Continental Tire the Americas LLC Announces New Plant in Sumter County, to Create 1,700 New Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/south-carolina/news/sc-continental-tire-americas-llc-announces-new-5734)  ·  `page_018808`

## Facts

- Continental Tire the Americas (CTA) announced plans to build a new tire manufacturing plant in Sumter County.
- The new plant project involves a $500 million investment.
- The Sumter County plant is expected to generate 1,700 new jobs in South Carolina over the next 10 years.
- Nikolai Setzer serves as a member of Continental’s Executive Board and head of Continental’s global tire business.
- Growing demand for Continental and General brand passenger and light truck tires in the U.S. drove the investment decision.
- The first phase of production capacity at the new plant will be nearly 5 million tires upon reaching full production levels in 2017.
- A second phase is expected to increase the plant’s total capacity to 8 million units per year by 2021.
- Governor Nikki Haley praised the $500 million investment for creating 1,700 new jobs in South Carolina.
- CTA’s passenger and light truck tire business volume in the Americas region increased nearly 20 percent over the past two years.
- Bobby Hitt serves as Secretary of Commerce for South Carolina.
- The Sumter County announcement marks the fourth recruitment of 500 or more jobs announced in South Carolina this year.
- The new manufacturing facility will be located at a greenfield site off U.S. Highway 521 in Sumter County.
- The announcement was made at a news conference at the Sumter Opera House.
- Jochen Etzel serves as CEO of Continental Tire the Americas.
- Jim Newsome is president and CEO of the South Carolina State Ports Authority.
- Greg A. Thompson is Chairman of the Sumter Development Board.
- The $500 million investment represents the largest industrial investment in Sumter County history.
- Eugene R. Baten serves as Sumter County Council Chairman.
- Joseph McElveen Jr. is Mayor of Sumter.
- The Coordinating Council approved a $31 million grant for the purchase and improvements of the plant site.
- The project was awarded a $4 million federally funded Community Development Block Grant through Sumter County to offset road and public infrastructure improvement costs.
- Continental Tire the Americas is also announcing an investment of more than $4 million at its Lancaster County, South Carolina headquarters.
- The Lancaster County headquarters currently employs nearly 350 people and opened in 2009.
- The Lancaster investment includes a 16,000-square-foot building addition, bringing the total facility size to 91,000 square feet.
- The Lancaster County expansion will create 80 new jobs over the next four years.
- Continental reported sales of €26 billion in 2010.
- Continental supplies brake systems, powertrain and chassis components, instrumentation, infotainment solutions, vehicle electronics, tires, and technical elastomers.
- Continental employs close to 160,000 people across 45 countries.
- The S.C. Department of Commerce won the Gold Shovel Award and Deal of Year Award from Area Development magazine this year.
- The S.C. Department of Commerce has recruited companies including Boeing, Bridgestone, Monster.com, Heinz, ZF Group, BMW, and Google Inc. to South Carolina.
