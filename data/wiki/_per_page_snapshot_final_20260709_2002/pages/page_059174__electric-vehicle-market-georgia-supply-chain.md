# Electric Vehicle Market & Georgia Supply Chain

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-18

**Source:** [Booming EV market complicates the GOP’s message - The Dominion Post](https://www.dominionpost.com/2023/06/18/booming-ev-market-complicates-the-gops-message/)  ·  `page_059174`

## Facts

- Electric-vehicle sales in the U.S. were 325,000 in 2020, accounting for 2.4% of all passenger vehicle sales.
- EV sales are projected to rise in 2024 to almost 2.5 million vehicles, or 17% of all sales, according to BloombergNEF’s Electric Vehicle Outlook report.
- The projected 2024 EV sales volume would exceed the 1.8 million vehicles Toyota Motor Corp. sold in the U.S. in 2022.
- Between August 2022 and May 2023, $70 billion of investments were announced in the EV supply chain.
- Battleground states Georgia, Michigan, Arizona, Nevada, and North Carolina each received billions of dollars in projects during that period.
- Rivian Automotive Inc. is building an EV production facility east of Atlanta in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Co. is building an EV production facility outside of Savannah in Georgia.
- Both the Rivian and Hyundai facilities in Georgia will be capable of producing hundreds of thousands of vehicles annually.
- President Biden is expected to conduct hard-hat tours and ribbon cuttings at these facilities over the next 16 months, touting them as outcomes made possible by the Inflation Reduction Act signed in August 2022.
- The facility developments are expected to help fulfill campaign promises to bring manufacturing jobs back to America.
- Donald Trump stated he would scrap support for electric vehicles on his 'first day in office' should he win the election.
- Florida Governor Ron DeSantis awarded money for electric transit buses and discussed efforts to bolster growing electric vehicle usage.
- Florida has not attracted high-profile EV supply chain investments compared to other states despite DeSantis's efforts.
- Automakers are committing tens of billions of dollars to shift production to EVs regardless of the presidential election outcome.
- China surpassed Japan to become the world’s largest exporter of automobiles in the first quarter.
- China’s BYD Co. is competing with Tesla Inc. to become the most important EV company.
- China already dominates the EV battery supply chain.
