# voestalpine Stamptee

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-08-23

**Source:** [Bartow Gives Land, Incentives for voestalpine | Cartersville, GA Patch](https://patch.com/georgia/cartersville/bartow-gives-land-incentives-for-voestalpine)  ·  `page_013901`

## Facts

- Bartow County agreed to give newcomer voestalpine land and tax breaks according to a memorandum of understanding signed by Commissioner Clarence Brown.
- voestalpine Stamptee, a division of the Austrian-based international metal-working company, agreed to build in Highland 75 near Cartersville an up to 200,000-square-foot factory on 32 acres for certain stamped-metal components, transmission and power train parts.
- Rome News-Tribune reported the facility could be operational by July 2013.
- Commissioner Clarence Brown told the Rome News-Tribune that the site could draw at least two other companies looking to build plants in close proximity to voestalpine's new Bartow County site.
- Bartow County's Joint Development Authority (JDA) agreed to issue bonds to fund construction of the plant, to be repaid by voestalpine with lease payments.
- The JDA agreed to apply for a $275,000 grant on behalf of voestalpine.
- Bartow County will give the company 15 acres of land in industrial park Highland 75, which is valued at $375,000, and the option to purchase up to about 17 adjacent acres for $25,000 per acre.
- The county will foot the bill and help complete permitting, zoning, infrastructure and temporary water, and will waive water and sewer tap fees.
- The JDA agreed to facilitate, assist and coordinate discussions regarding providing a direct rail serve to the Highland 75 Industrial Park and/or rail service via a transfer station.
- A Highland 75 roadway to be built will be named "Voestalpine Parkway."
- voestalpine receives a property tax break consisting of 15 years on real property and 10 years on personal property, both based on an increasing abatement percentage schedule.
