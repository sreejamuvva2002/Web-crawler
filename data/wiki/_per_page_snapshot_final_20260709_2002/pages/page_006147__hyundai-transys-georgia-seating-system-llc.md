# Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-05

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC Expands Operations | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2021/hyundai-transys-georgia-seating-system-llc-expands-manufacturing-operations-georgia)  ·  `page_006147`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC will invest $9 million in expanding their seating manufacturing operations in West Point, Georgia.
- The expansion will create more than 150 jobs in Troup County.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System manufactures seats and seat foam pads for the Hyundai Santa Fe, KIA Sorento, and other models.
- In addition to providing seating structures for automobile manufacturers, the company also supplies seats to leading electric vehicle makers.
- This expansion will take Hyundai TRANSYS’ employment total in Georgia to more than 1,890 jobs.
- The company is continuing to hire for careers in production seat assembly, foam, quality, and logistics.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this competitive project in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is an automotive parts company that produces critical automobile parts, including transmissions, axles, and seats.
- The company is the only company in the world specializing in transmissions.
- Hyundai TRANSYS produces all types of automatic transmissions, manual transmissions, dual clutch transmissions (DCT), and continuously variable transmissions.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities and remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing, outpacing the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of products including machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
- West Point Development Authority Chairman Kevin Patrick stated that the company's automotive and existing industries are the backbone of their economic success.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Hyundai TRANSYS contributes more than $3 billion to Georgia’s economy.
