# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Korean automotive supplier coming to West Point |](https://capitol-beat.org/2023/08/korean-automotive-supplier-coming-to-west-point/)  ·  `page_000593`

## Facts

- A Korean automotive supplier will build a manufacturing facility in West Point to serve the nearby Kia plant and other automakers, announced by Gov. Brian Kemp on Tuesday.
- Daesol Ausys will invest $72 million in the project.
- The project will create more than 140 jobs.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and is excited that Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia’s automotive industry currently employs over 50,000 Georgians, according to Gov. Kemp.
- Gov. Kemp noted that the state’s automotive industry will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose locations across the state for future jobs.
- Daesol Ausys was established in 2017.
- Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing electric vehicle motor and interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys serves as a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Hyundai Motor Group is building a huge EV manufacturing plant west of Savannah.
- The new Daesol Ausys plant will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations at the new Daesol Ausys plant are due to begin late next year.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and look forward to continuing partnerships with the state of Georgia and Harris County.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked on the project in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, city of West Point, Georgia Power, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program.
