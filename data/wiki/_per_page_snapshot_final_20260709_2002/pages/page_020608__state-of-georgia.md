# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-10

**Source:** [Georgia is ready for what’s next - AI Online -](https://ai-online.com/2021/10/georgia-is-ready-for-whats-next/)  ·  `page_020608`

## Facts

- The U.S. state of Georgia is actively assessing the growing needs of the electric vehicle (EV) and mobility sector and building on existing assets.
- Led by Governor Brian Kemp, Georgia believes it is well on its way to completely revamping its supply chain, infrastructure, workforce training, government policies, and overall value proposition to better meet the shifting needs of the EV and mobility sector.
- The U.S. only produces 10% of the world’s lithium-ion batteries.
- EV cars are designed around the battery, and many components needed to make these cars operational are currently being made overseas, creating a need to bring them to the U.S.
- There is a need for new facilities to accommodate battery recycling across the full life-cycle of EVs.
- Drivers need access to recharging stations to make EVs a viable part of their lifestyles.
- Georgia offers more charging stations per capita than anywhere else in the United States, except one state on the West Coast.
- Georgia is actively identifying ways to expand its charging infrastructure to cover both metro and rural regions.
- In 2018, SK Innovation announced plans to build a massive lithium-ion battery plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- SK Innovation has shared plans to bring its total investment to nearly $2.6 billion.
- SK Innovation has already started production at its Georgia facility.
- SK Innovation will supply batteries for VW as well as a new all-electric Ford F150 Lightning.
- The SK Innovation investment has created a ripple effect in the region.
- Other global e-mobility suppliers and leaders like Enchem Ltd., TEKLAS, GEDIA Automotive Group, and Heliox have announced significant, job-stimulating investments in Georgia.
- Governor Kemp has been personally talking to these companies since taking office in 2019 and visiting them overseas in Korea and Germany to ensure Georgia supports their moves toward electrification.
- Georgia is a hotbed for startups and home to a rapidly growing number of innovative companies moving into the electric mobility space.
- There is serious growth in renewable energies in Georgia, including Plug Power, a major provider of hydrogen solutions that broke ground in August on its manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- The Curiosity Lab at Peachtree Corners is a city-owned and operated living laboratory designed specifically to test technology that has graduated from a closed laboratory environment in a safe, real-world setting.
- The Curiosity Lab features a 5G-enabled three-mile autonomous vehicle test track and smart city infrastructure located in a 500-acre technology park where thousands of people and vehicles interact daily.
- The City of Peachtree Corners is serving as a model for other cities with its EV initiatives.
- The city launched a new solar roadway system that produces energy for an EV charging station at city hall.
- The city established a new EV fast-charging plaza capable of serving up to 16 vehicles.
- Georgia and its wide network of partners are financing these programs because they see opportunity in solving EV infrastructure shortfalls that are holding up wider industry growth.
- Georgia has developed a very competitive environment with a powerful and positive business climate.
- Georgia's extensive infrastructure offers easy access to market, including its ports which are already a major player for automotive imports and have expanded inland connectivity to improve material transport efficiencies.
- Georgia's geographic location puts it within hours, or even minutes, to OEMs across the Southeast’s “Automotive Alley.”
- Access to a skilled and educated workforce is a deciding factor for attracting companies to Georgia.
- Over the past year, Georgia’s university system awarded a record number of degrees.
- Georgia’s technical college system allows technical schools to partner with their communities to design certification programs that meet the workforce needs of local companies.
- Georgia was the first state to create a workforce training program custom-designed for qualifying companies.
- The Quick Start workforce training program has been ranked the number one training program in the nation for 14 years.
- In July 2021, Governor Kemp’s administration established the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA), a coalition of government, private industry, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other relevant stakeholders focused on continued growth of the electric mobility ecosystem throughout the state.
- EMIA committees have been formed to set objectives for supply chain, innovation, infrastructure, workforce, and policy recommendations to further enhance the state’s attractiveness to the electric mobility industry.
