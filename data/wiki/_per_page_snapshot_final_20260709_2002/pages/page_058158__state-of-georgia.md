# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2018-09-27

**Source:** [Georgia Named Top State to do Business for Fifth Year in a Row - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/georgia-named-top-state-to-do-business-for-fifth-year-in-a-row/)  ·  `page_058158`

## Facts

- Georgia was named the top state in the country for doing business for the fifth consecutive year by Area Development, a leading corporate site selection and facility planning publication.
- Georgia ranked first among all states for workforce development programs.
- Georgia ranked first among all states for having a cooperative and responsive state government.
- Governor Nathan Deal announced the ranking alongside Area Development Publisher Dennis J. Shea.
- The Stephens County Development Authority has been highly successful in attracting major international corporations to establish operations in the county.
- Stephens County Development Authority Executive Director Tim Martin attributes the county's success to its attractive workforce development program.
- The Stephens County workforce development program operates in conjunction with North Georgia Technical College to produce a job-ready labor force.
- Tim Martin stated that finding employees is the number one consideration for many potential employers.
- The authority utilizes the Currahee Campus and maintains partnerships with existing industry to support workforce needs.
- Tim Martin highlighted the role of the local school system in ensuring the workforce meets employer requirements.
- Companies such as GEM Industries and Nifco KTW America continue to grow and expand their businesses in Stephens County.
- Toccoa-Stephens County has become a favored location for filmmakers, hosting five major movies over several years.
- The Toccoa-Stephens County development authority works closely with national site developers and project managers to secure new businesses for the state.
- The authority maintains close relationships with project managers selling the State of Georgia to ensure they have viable locations to offer.
- The authority actively uses various social media platforms to promote its business climate and opportunities.
- Area Development’s 2018 Top States for Doing Business rankings were based on a survey of site consultants regarding location and facility planning.
- Survey categories included the overall cost of doing business, corporate tax environment, business incentive programs, competitive labor environment, and access to capital and project funding.
- During fiscal year 2018, the Georgia Department of Economic Development Global Commerce Division helped to create more than 27,000 new jobs through a record-breaking 419 expansion or relocation projects.
- The 419 projects in fiscal 2018 amounted to $5.56 billion in investments across Georgia communities.
