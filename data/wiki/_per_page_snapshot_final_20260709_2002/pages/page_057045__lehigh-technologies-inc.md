# Lehigh Technologies Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies expands R&D capabilities | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2080670/lehigh-technologies-expands-r-d-capabilities)  ·  `page_057045`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies Inc., a sustainable materials company, is located in Atlanta, Georgia.
- The company announced the expansion of its state-of-the-art research and development capabilities at its Application and Development Center (ADC).
- Lehigh added Chad Jasiunas as Technical Manager for Functional Materials to its ADC team.
- Jasiunas brings more than a decade of international experience in the development of solution-SBR systems.
- Prior to joining Lehigh, Jasiunas worked at Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. and Synthos S.A., Europe’s largest manufacturer of emulsion rubber polymers.
- Lehigh made significant investments in new mixing and analytical equipment for its ADC.
- The expansion aims to drive further adoption of sustainable micronised rubber powder (MRP) material in green tire applications.
- Alan Barton, CEO of Lehigh Technologies, stated that the global green tire market is expected to grow significantly over the next five years.
- Barton noted that Lehigh has positioned itself to support this growth by establishing best-in-class research and development facilities and a strong technical team.
- Adel Halasa, Ph.D., a Lehigh Advisory Board member, stated that Jasiunas’s background in solution and functional SBR technologies will help Lehigh deliver MRP-based functional systems for Green Tire applications.
- Lehigh invested in advanced silica-silane mixing capabilities, including a new internal mixing unit custom-built to its specifications.
- The new internal mixing unit includes precision speed and temperature control as well as capability for mixing high viscosity silica-silane-SBR compounds.
- Lehigh also acquired a Metravib model DMA+ 1000 dynamic mechanical analysis (DMA) unit.
- The new DMA unit measures predictive parameters critical to green tire performance such as rolling resistance, traction, and handling under a wide range of conditions.
- Tom Rosenmayer, Lehigh’s vice president of technology, stated that having advanced mixing and DMA testing capabilities is critical to the development of functional materials based on MRP.
- Rosenmayer claimed that among its peers, Lehigh is unique in having this capability in-house, enabling more efficient work with customers to develop innovative solutions.
- MRPs lower costs, increase the sustainability profile of end products, and deliver performance without sacrificing the reliability and performance offered by traditional raw materials.
- Lehigh’s customer base already includes five of the top ten tire companies in the world.
- More than 250m tires have been manufactured using its MRP technology.
