# HL-GA Battery Co.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-20

**Source:** [Bryan County HL-GA Battery Company worker killed in forklift accident - WSB-TV Channel 2 - Atlanta](https://www.wsbtv.com/news/local/man-dies-industrial-accident-ev-battery-plant-being-built-near-savannah/BUUJFU575JBJJO5WFKZK7OXNL4/)  ·  `page_000405`

## Facts

- A man died Tuesday morning at an electric vehicle battery plant under construction when a load fell off a forklift.
- The fatal accident occurred at the HL-GA Battery Co. site located at the Bryan County MegaSite in Ellabell, approximately 25 miles from downtown Savannah.
- The plant is scheduled to start producing EV batteries for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis vehicles in early 2026.
- Deputies responded to the site around 11 a.m. on Tuesday.
- As the truck was being unloaded, the load came loose from the forklift and fell.
- The victim was an employee of a subcontractor at the plant site who was struck and killed because he could not get out of the way in time.
- Law enforcement officials had not yet identified the victim as of Tuesday, as authorities were contacting his family.
- Bryan County officials are continuing to investigate the fatal accident, with no foul play suspected.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) has opened an investigation into the Tuesday death.
- OSHA is also investigating another fatal forklift accident at the same site that occurred on March 21.
- HL-GA Battery Co. issued a statement expressing that it is deeply saddened by the death of a subcontractor employee at its Bryan County construction site on May 20, 2025.
- The company extended condolences to the subcontractor’s family, friends, and colleagues.
- HL-GA Battery Co. stated that the health and safety of its larger team is its highest priority and that it is committed to following all Occupational Industrial Safety and Health Policies.
- The company said it is fully supporting a thorough investigation with its subcontractor and relevant authorities to determine the root cause of the incident and help prevent such accidents in the future.
- HL-GA Battery Co. had no further comment as the investigation remains ongoing.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have committed more than $4.3 billion to build the EV battery plant.
