# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-05

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels, Inc. ## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels, Inc. announced a commercial award for its PyroThin® EV Thermal Barrier segment.
- The company received an additional PyroThin vehicle platform award from The Automotive Cells Company ("ACC"), a battery cell joint venture between Stellantis N.V., Saft-TotalEnergies, and Mercedes-Benz.
- Aspen will supply the Stellantis STLA Medium vehicle platform under this award.
- Expected start of production for the Stellantis STLA Medium platform is in 2025.
- The company received a letter from the U.S. Department of Energy ("DOE") Loan Programs Office ("LPO") confirming its pending application for a loan under the DOE LPO's Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing ("ATVM") lending program is substantially complete.
- The DOE invited Aspen to formal due diligence regarding this loan application.
- This loan application is in connection with the construction of Aspen's planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The DOE invitation does not guarantee or imply that Aspen will be invited further in the process, nor that a loan will be issued or terms will align with proposals.
- Aspen continues to engage with the LPO and its advisors regarding the application.
- Don Young, Aspen's President and CEO, stated these developments are meaningful steps in near-term execution to diversify PyroThin's customer base and optimize cost of capital.
- Young noted the European market will drive a meaningful portion of revenues over the next few years as the company maximizes existing assets and commercial arrangements.
- Aspen's aerogel technology enables customers to achieve objectives around resource efficiency, e-mobility, and clean energy.
- PyroThin® products address thermal runaway challenges within the electric vehicle market.
- Aspen Battery Materials is the company's carbon aerogel initiative aimed at increasing lithium-ion battery cell performance to extend EV driving range and reduce EV costs.
- Aspen is headquartered in Northborough, Mass., and manufactures products at its East Providence, R.I. facilities.
- Forward-looking statements indicate expectations regarding an extended construction and commissioning timeframe for the planned second manufacturing plant in Georgia ("Plant II").
- Aspen is managing Plant II construction to align with expected demand from EV customers and may use contract manufacturers to meet Energy Industrial customer demand.
- Risks associated with the project include the inability to complete construction and commissioning Plant II at estimated costs, potential cancellation of EV thermal barrier contracts by customers without penalty, costs exceeding projections, disruption in existing production lines or contract manufacturing, and failure to enforce patents.
