# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-07

**Source:** [First Hyundai Metaplant supplier will build plant in Bulloch - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/first-hyundai-metaplant-supplier-will-build-plant-bulloch/)  ·  `page_001425`

## Facts

- An automotive parts company will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in building a new manufacturing plant in Bulloch County.
- Plans for the facility mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp made the announcement on Monday afternoon about Joon Georgia’s plans.
- Less than two weeks after the groundbreaking of Hyundai Motor Group’s innovative facility, the regional impact is already being seen.
- Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to the Peach State to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of hardworking Georgians.
- The new plant will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park, at the I-16 interchange on U.S. Highway 301.
- Operations are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, expressed honor and pride at welcoming Joon Georgia to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community.
- He believes the site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park and proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America will serve the company well.
- Education resources including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College will provide critical workforce support to help Joon Georgia succeed.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The South Korean company has a plant in Cusseta, Ala., and produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, expressed excitement to be one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- This marks Joon Georgia's first plant in the State of Georgia.
- Joon Georgia, Inc. will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- The company aims to be the best parts supplier for automotive and EV companies like Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, as well as the best employer and corporate citizen giving back to Bulloch County and the City of Statesboro.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- Workforce recruitment for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- By partnering with the Technical College System of Georgia, the program helps assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using the most current techniques and media.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson called Joon Georgia’s announcement a landmark moment for driving Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.
- Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are creating generational opportunities for communities in Georgia.
- Supporting the jobs of tomorrow is a team effort, and regional collaboration ensures those benefits are shared across county lines.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry according to a release from the governor.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
