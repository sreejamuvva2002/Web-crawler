# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Company Statement on Immigration Enforcement Action at Supplier Facility - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2025/09/05/hyundai-motor-company-statement-on-immigration-enforcement-action-at-supplier-facility/)  ·  `page_060738`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company is aware of the immigration enforcement action that took place at the construction site of its supplier, the HL-GA Battery Company in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai is closely monitoring the situation while working to understand the specific circumstances.
- Based on current understanding, none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai is committed to full compliance with all laws and regulations in every market where it operates, including employment verification requirements and immigration laws.
- Hyundai expects the same commitment from all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- The safety and well-being of everyone working at Hyundai sites, whether directly employed or working for suppliers, is a top priority.
- Incidents like this remind Hyundai of the importance of robust oversight throughout its entire supply chain and contractor network.
- Hyundai is reviewing its processes to ensure all parties working on its projects maintain high standards of legal compliance.
- The review includes thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- North America Chief Manufacturing Officer Chris Susock will now assume governance of the entire megasite in Georgia.
- Hyundai will conduct an investigation to ensure all suppliers and their subcontractors comply with all laws and regulations.
- Hyundai has zero tolerance for those who don’t follow the law.
- Hyundai continues to invest in American manufacturing and create thousands of jobs in full accordance with U.S. law.
