# EP4294872A1 Insulated Wires Patent

## Facts

- Magnet wires are increasingly used in electric vehicles for applications such as inverter drive motors, motor starter generators, and transformers.
- The invention provides magnet wire insulation using a thermoplastic polyaryletherketone (PAEK) composition without requiring an adhesive layer.
- Conventional enamel insulation requires successive baking passes that lower adhesive force and can cause solvent blisters or reduced flexibility.
- The new insulation composition eliminates the need for adhesive agents, enamel, or promoters between the conductor and the thermoplastic layer.
- The insulation composition comprises at least one PAEK polymer and 6 to 40 wt.% of talc relative to the total weight.
- The composition must have a melt viscosity of at least 120 Pa s when measured according to ASTM D3835 at 400°C and 1000 s⁻¹.
- Talc content can range from 5.0 to 10.0 wt.% or 10.0 to 26.0 wt.%, with the latter range providing good adhesion to the conductor.
- The composition may use a combination of two PEEK polymers (PEEK1 and PEEK2) that differ in melt viscosity.
- PEEK1 may exhibit a viscosity between 350 and 600 Pa s, while PEEK2 may exhibit a viscosity between 100 and 200 Pa s.
- The weight ratio of PEEK1 to PEEK2 may range from 50/50 to 70/30.
- Commercial PEEK is available as KetaSpire® polyetheretherketone from Solvay Specialty Polymers USA, LLC.
- The composition may optionally include a polyarylethersulfone (PAES) polymer such as polyphenylsulfone (PPSU), polyethersulfone (PES), or polysulfone (PSU).
- PPSU is commercially available as RADEL® from Solvay Specialty Polymers USA, L.L.C.
- PES is commercially available as VERADEL® from Solvay Specialty Polymers USA, L.L.C.
- PSU is commercially available as UDEL® from Solvay Specialty Polymers USA, L.L.C.
- The amount of PAES polymer may range from 1.0 to 45.0 wt.% based on the total weight of PAEK and PAES polymers.
- A preferred composition ratio includes 55.0 to 70.0 wt% PEEK and 45.0 to 30.0 wt% PPSU.
- Talc particles may have a D50 particle size lower than 10.0 μm, preferably between 1.0 and 5.0 μm.
- Particle size distribution analysis can be performed using equipment supplied by Micromeritics Instruments Corporation in Morcross, Georgia, USA.
- Nucleating agents such as boron nitride, graphite, graphene, or mica may be added to speed up crystallization and increase wire coating throughput.
- Optional plastic additives like colorants, UV stabilizers, heat stabilizers, antioxidants, and flame retardants may comprise no more than 50 wt.% of the composition.
- The insulation layer can be applied directly to a conductor via extrusion or after surface treatments like flame treatment, mechanical abrasion, or chemical etching.
- The technology addresses high-temperature adhesion challenges and windability requirements for EV motor outputs that generate increased heat.
- Square or rectangular wire shapes present additional stress challenges on corners and edges compared to round wires.
