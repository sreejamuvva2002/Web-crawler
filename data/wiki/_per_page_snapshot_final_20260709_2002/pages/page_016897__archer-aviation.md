# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-19

**Source:** [Archer Aviation teams up with United Airlines to make air taxis a reality | National Guns](https://nationalgunbroker.com/archer-aviation-teams-up-with-united-airlines-to-make-air-taxis-a-reality/)  ·  `page_016897`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation announced it is developing an air taxi network in New York City in partnership with United Airlines.
- The service will utilize the piloted electric air taxi named Midnight.
- Midnight aircraft are built at the company’s manufacturing facilities in San Jose, California, and Covington, Georgia.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to carry four passengers.
- Passengers will eventually be able to book these flights as an add-on to regular airline tickets or through Archer’s app.
- Initially, the service will target business and first-class travelers flying from nearby airports and will be more expensive.
- The long-term goal is to make the service affordable enough for everyday commuters to use it regularly, potentially replacing car rides to and from the airport.
- Archer Aviation was founded in 2018 and went public three years later.
- The company has a market valuation of $3.94 billion.
- Archer has signed agreements with major airports in the region including John F. Kennedy International Airport, LaGuardia Airport, Newark Airport, Westchester County Airport, Teterboro Airport, and Republic Airport, as well as three city helipads.
- The company previously announced plans to establish air taxi networks in Los Angeles and San Francisco.
- Archer is currently in the final stages of securing approval from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).
- Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated Archer aims to launch commercial operations as early as this year in Abu Dhabi.
- A New York City launch for the service is targeted for 2026, pending regulatory approval which comes with high standards.
- Goldstein said his motivation behind launching the aircraft was to help modernize urban transportation due to significant population growth and outdated transit solutions.
- Goldstein noted that traditional helicopters typically have 200 to 300 single points of failure.
- Archer's all-electric propulsion system features fewer moving parts, no combustion engine, and no gearbox, allowing for added redundancy compared to piston or combustion engines.
- Instead of a single main rotor, the aircraft has 12 separate propellers, enabling it to continue flying even if an engine or propeller fails.
- A digital flight control system helps prevent any command or maneuver that could damage the aircraft.
- Fixed wings allow the aircraft to glide up to 20 miles, providing an added layer of safety not typically found in rotorcraft.
- The FAA has set higher aircraft certification safety standards for Archer's aircraft than for conventional light helicopters and light airplanes.
- Goldstein stated that highly redundant systems enable the aircraft to exceed these higher safety standards.
- Joby Aviation is pursuing a similar business model while simultaneously working in partnership with Delta Air Lines and Uber to develop electric flying taxis.
- Joby plans to launch its operations in Dubai as early as this year, with U.S. operations expected in the next few years pending completion of the FAA certification process.
- Unlike Joby's six-rotor configuration with tilting propellers, Archer employs a 12-rotor design with fixed wings.
- While Joby develops many components in-house, Archer uses traditional aerospace suppliers.
