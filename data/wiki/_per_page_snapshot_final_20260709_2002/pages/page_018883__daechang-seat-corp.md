# Daechang Seat Corp.

## Facts

- A South Korean company that makes automotive seat frames will open a factory in Georgia to supply a new Hyundai Motor Group plant.
- Daechang Seat Corp., based in Gyeongju, South Korea, said Monday that it would invest $72.5 million in a plant in Savannah.
- The company plans to hire more than 500 people for the new Georgia facility.
- It is the latest company to announce a plant to supply Hyundai’s $7.6 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- Hyundai’s plant was announced last year.
- Hyundai’s plant could grow to 8,500 employees.
- Hyundai’s plant could begin producing vehicles as early as 2024.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to invest nearly $2.3 billion.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to hire nearly 5,800 people.
- Daechang Seat opened a factory in Phenix City, Alabama, in 2019.
- The company announced a $23.4 million expansion in Phenix City last year.
- The Phenix City expansion includes building a new building and hiring an additional 300 people.
- The company employs more than 5,000 people in seven countries.
- Jinsuk Lee is the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- Jinsuk Lee stated: “Our primary objective is to drive the growth of DSC through facility expansions, ultimately transforming DSC into a global entity.”
- Workers will make an average wage of more than $18 an hour.
- The average wage does not include bonuses and benefits.
- Angela Hendrix is a spokesperson for the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- The state will pay to train workers.
- Daechang Seat could qualify for $10 million in state income tax credits.
- The tax credits are valued at $4,000 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the tax credits, workers must make at least $31,300 a year.
- Local officials have granted a 15-year property tax reduction.
- The property tax reduction is projected to save the company $5.8 million.
- Local officials are selling land for the plant at a reduced rate.
