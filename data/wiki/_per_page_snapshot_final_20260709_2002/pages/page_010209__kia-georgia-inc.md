# Kia Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-26

**Source:** [Kia Made in USA? Why Kia Is Finally Building The Telluride Hybrid In Georgia](https://autopostglobal.com/car-news-industry/article/99849/)  ·  `page_010209`

## Facts

- Electric vehicle demand shows signs of slowing while global oil prices remain uncertain, causing hybrid vehicles to gain traction as a practical alternative.
- Hyundai Motor Company and Kia are accelerating hybrid production in the United States.
- The move aims to reduce tariff exposure and close the gap with Toyota Motor Corporation, which leads U.S. hybrid sales.
- On Feb. 25, Kia announced the start of production of the 2027 All-New Telluride Hybrid at its Georgia plant.
- The Telluride Hybrid marks the five millionth vehicle produced at Kia Georgia Inc., highlighting the company’s push to expand hybrid output.
- The Telluride was developed for North American consumers and U.S. road conditions, serving as a key model for the brand.
- Last year, the Georgia plant produced 128,858 Telluride units, representing the highest volume among its lineup.
- Kia plans to increase local Telluride sales from 120,000 units to 180,000 units with the addition of the hybrid variant.
- Hyundai Motor Company is also expanding hybrid production across its U.S. operations.
- The group previously produced the Santa Fe Hybrid at its Alabama plant, where 69,151 units were built last year.
- The Santa Fe Hybrid was the third most-produced model at the Alabama facility after the Tucson and gasoline-powered Santa Fe.
- Hybrid production is under review at Hyundai Motor Company Metaplant America, the company’s dedicated EV facility.
- Local production of the Palisade Hybrid, which began sales in the fourth quarter of last year, is being considered at the Metaplant.
- By localizing production of high-demand hybrid models in its largest overseas market, Hyundai Motor Company aims to improve profitability while challenging Toyota’s dominant position.
- Last year, Hyundai and Kia incurred more than 7 trillion won, or about $5.25 billion, in tariff costs.
- Operating profit fell 19.5 percent for Hyundai and 28.3 percent for Kia last year.
- In 2022, Hyundai and Kia sold 331,023 hybrid vehicles in the U.S., up 48.8 percent from a year earlier.
- Most hybrid models, except limited cases such as the Santa Fe Hybrid, are produced in South Korea and exported to the U.S., where they face a 15 percent tariff.
- According to global market research firm Omdia, Toyota sold 1,183,248 eco-friendly vehicles in the U.S. last year.
- Of Toyota's U.S. eco-friendly vehicle sales last year, 1,111,420 units, or 93.9 percent, were hybrids.
- Hyundai Motor Company produced 773,116 vehicles in the U.S. last year.
- Hyundai plans to expand production capacity at Hyundai Motor Company Metaplant America from 300,000 units to 500,000 units.
- Hyundai is targeting a combined U.S. production system of 1.2 million vehicles, including output from its Alabama and Georgia plants.
