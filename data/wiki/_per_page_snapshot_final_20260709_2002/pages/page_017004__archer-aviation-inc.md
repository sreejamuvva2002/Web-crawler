# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [eVTOL facility project critical to unlocking Georgia’s skies](https://canada.constructconnect.com/dcn/news/usa/2023/01/evtol-facility-project-critical-to-unlocking-georgias-skies)  ·  `page_017004`

## Facts

- Construction crews are preparing to break ground for a new $118-million electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft manufacturing facility alongside Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County, outside Atlanta, Ga.
- The facility will consist of three separate buildings across more than 96 sprawling acres.
- Up to 2,300 aircraft per year will be produced at the plant.
- The facility is slated to open in the first half of 2024.
- The facility is critical to Georgia’s efforts to remain a leader in the aerospace industry.
- Georgia has a robust aerospace education pipeline comprising dozens of high schools with training programs, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- Georgia is currently home to more than 800 aerospace companies.
- Santa Clara, Calif.-based Archer Aviation Inc., an aerospace company designing and developing eVTOL aircraft, is building the facility.
- Archer Aviation's vision is to unlock the skies and free everyone to reimagine how they move and spend time.
- The facility will create 1,000 jobs over a 10-year period.
- Adam Goldstein, founder and CEO of Archer, announced the decision to locate the company’s first manufacturing facility in Covington to ramp production for Urban Air Mobility networks across major cities nationwide.
- The aircraft manufacturing facility is part of a long-term master plan for the airport and will utilize one of several large parcels of land set aside for development adjacent to it.
- The plant will be strategically accessible from Georgia’s robust interstate system via I-20.
- Glen Burks, vice-president of manufacturing at Archer, described the facility as a crucial step in bringing eVTOL travel to life and praised Covington as a location and partner for the company's development.
- Construction of the plant is expected to take roughly a year.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that fostering innovation is a state priority and that Georgia’s established aerospace leadership was essential to attracting Archer Aviation to Newton County.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), has spent over 10 years cultivating an ecosystem for innovative companies by building and connecting Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) networks for Advanced Air Mobility commercialization.
- The GDEcD's Global Commerce Industrial Projects team represented the agency on the project in partnership with the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Center of Innovation’s Aerospace industry team, Georgia EMC, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Tech.
- GDEcD commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Archer Aviation is at the forefront of aviation technological evolution and will use innovative eVTOL design to sustainably tackle urban mobility issues.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber president and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick stated that the region's aviation expertise combined with academic, business, civic, and government partner support makes it an ideal location for the investment.
- Lanier Sims, chairman of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, stated that Archer fits the community’s industry mix and committed to ensuring the local workforce is prepared for the jobs created by Archer Aviation.
