# Southern Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Southern Company | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/southern-company)  ·  `page_028358`

## Facts

- Southern Company will build or upgrade an estimated 16.7 GW of energy generation capacity from gas, nuclear, hydropower, storage, and transmission projects across two states, providing direct cost savings to customers.
- In February 2026, EDF closed an unprecedented $26.5 billion in loans to support two wholly owned subsidiaries of Southern Company—Alabama Power and Georgia Power.
- The investment is funded under President Trump’s Working Families Tax Cut.
- The project will lower American energy costs, create thousands of jobs, and increase grid reliability in Georgia and Alabama.
- As of the loan closing, more than 10,000 megawatts (MW) of new generation and upgrades to existing generation are planned to be added across Georgia and Alabama to provide base load power and forecastable, dispatchable capacity for electric customers.
- New natural gas generation projects underway in Georgia include Plants Bowen, Wansley, McIntosh and Yates, among other sites.
- Southern Company is making investments to modernize and upgrade hydroelectric generation facilities from the Coosa River in Alabama, to the mountains of north Georgia to the lakes and rivers of central and western Georgia.
- Following the completion of Vogtle Units 3 and 4—the first new nuclear units constructed in more than 30 years and also supported by previous DOE loans—Southern Company is upgrading Vogtle Units 1 and 2 to enable those units to deliver additional energy for customers for decades to come.
- Southern Company is planning for additional future upgrades at Plant Hatch and Plant Farley.
- The historic investment will expand and enhance a diverse generation mix to deliver over 16 gigawatts (GW) of firm reliable power to the electrical grid.
- The project includes 5 GW of new gas generation, 6 GW in nuclear improved through upgrades and license renewals, hydropower modernization, battery energy storage systems, and over 1,300 miles of transmission and grid enhancement projects.
- The loan will deliver over $7 billion in electricity cost savings to millions of American customers in Georgia and Alabama.
- The loan will create and sustain thousands of jobs across the two states.
- Southern Company is listed as the owner of the project.
- Project locations span various sites across Alabama and Georgia.
- The EDF Tech Sector for this initiative is Utilities.
- Financing is provided through the Energy Dominance Financing Program under a loan structure.
- The total loan amount is $26.5 billion, issued in February 2026.
- U.S. jobs supported are estimated at thousands at the time of closing.
- Customer savings are estimated at $7 billion at the time of closing.
