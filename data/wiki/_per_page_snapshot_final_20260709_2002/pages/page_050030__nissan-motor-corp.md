# Nissan Motor Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-20

**Source:** [Nissan, SK On announce $661M EV battery supply deal | Supply Chain Dive](https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/nissan-sk-on-ev-battery-supply-deal/743021/)  ·  `page_050030`

## Facts

- Nissan Motor Corp. and SK On inked a battery agreement to bolster the automaker’s electric vehicle production in North America.
- Under the $661 million deal, SK On will supply Nissan with roughly 100 GWh of high-nickel batteries from 2028 to 2033.
- Nissan will use the U.S.-made batteries for EV manufacturing at its assembly plant in Canton, Mississippi.
- The deal supports Nissan’s push to grow its U.S. EV manufacturing capacity.
- Nissan is focusing on finding new partnerships to help bring vehicles to market faster.
- Nissan previously invested $500 million toward EV production at the Canton plant.
- In 2024, Nissan announced plans to launch 16 new EVs over the next three years, some of which will be assembled in Canton starting in 2028.
- The partnership marks SK On’s first supply agreement with a Japan-based automaker.
- SK On, a South Korea-based subsidiary of SK Group, has also collaborated with Ford on EV battery material production.
- SK On currently operates two battery plants in the U.S., with four additional plants under construction with partners.
- SK On’s U.S. production capacity is expected to surpass 180 GWh once all of its plants are fully operational.
