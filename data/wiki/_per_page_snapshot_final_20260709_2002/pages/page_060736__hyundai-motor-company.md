# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-03

**Source:** [Hyundai Debuts U.S.-Built 2025 IONIQ 5 Range, Including New Adventure-Ready XRT - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2024/09/03/hyundai-debuts-u-s-built-2025-ioniq-5-range-including-new-adventure-ready-xrt/)  ·  `page_060736`

## Facts

- Hyundai announced the release of the refreshed 2025 IONIQ 5, including a rugged new IONIQ 5 XRT variant.
- The expanded lineup offers more driving range and innovative features than ever.
- The IONIQ 5 will be the first model range manufactured at the brand-new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) facility in Georgia.
- Upgraded 2025 IONIQ 5 models, including the new XRT, arrive at Hyundai dealerships this fall.
- The factory-built 2025 IONIQ 5 XRT brings additional off-road capability and a bolder, more rugged appearance to this Hyundai EV for the first time.
- IONIQ 5 aims to further enhance customer satisfaction by being the first Hyundai model to come equipped with the Tesla North American Charging Standard (NACS) port.
- This port opens 17,000+ chargers on Tesla’s Supercharger network without using an adapter, doubling the size of the DC fast-charging network available to new IONIQ 5 customers.
- 2025 IONIQ 5 models also will be sold with a Combined Charging System (CCS) adapter.
- All-new or refreshed Hyundai EVs will come exclusively with a NACS port beginning in Q4 2024.
- Models produced at HMGMA are expected to be eligible for a $3,750 U.S. tax credit at start of sale.
- In the future, Hyundai anticipates the U.S.-built 2025 IONIQ 5 models to qualify for additional federal tax incentives.
- Leases continue to qualify for the $7,500 credit that Hyundai fully passes onto consumers with more attractive lease offers.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, stated: "IONIQ 5 has been a sales success for us since day one. We’re continuing to give customers more of what they want by expanding the IONIQ 5 lineup with a dedicated off-road-capable XRT model, NACS port, design upgrades and a long list of features developed with customers in mind. The high-performance IONIQ 5 N shows how exciting EVs can be and has been a halo for our entire brand. We’re taking ‘America’s Most Awarded EV Lineup’ from strength to strength."
- Brad Arnold, senior design manager of exteriors, Hyundai Design North America, stated: "We designed this IONIQ 5 XRT to offer more Protection, Connection, and Functionality. These will be hallmarks of our XRT models going forward. The digital camo pattern was created using our IONIQ pixel as the building block. The matte-black 18-inch wheels also display the same IONIQ pixel theme while providing strength and cooling."
- NACS charging port and support for NACS networks including Tesla Superchargers are included.
- Standard Range battery capacity increases from 58.0 to 63.0 kWh.
- Long Range battery capacity increases from 77.4 to 84 kWh.
- AWD Limited trim current range is 260, with a new targeted range of 250+ to 280+ depending on trim / wheel & tire size.
- RWD SE/SEL/Limited current range is 303, with a new targeted range of 310+.
- RWD SE Standard Range current range is 220, with a new targeted range of 240+.
- Exclusive XRT Model Features include a dual motor (AWD only) + 84 kWh battery.
- The XRT features a +23mm suspension lift with unique tuning, bringing ground clearance to seven inches compared to the standard IONIQ 5's 6.1 inches.
- The XRT includes unique model-specific black 18-inch wheels and 235 / 60 R 18 all-terrain tires.
- The XRT features XRT-exclusive unique front and rear fascias, etched digital camouflage cladding, black window surround trim, black side sills, and black Hyundai flying ‘H’ badges on hood and liftgate.
- The XRT includes an HTRAC liftgate badge and IONIQ 5 lettering in black relief, an XRT logo embossed on the rear fascia, black mirror caps, XRT badged all-weather floor mats, H-Tex™ seating surfaces featuring XRT-unique pattern and logo, and a black headliner.
- Exclusive XRT exterior colors are Ultimate Red and Cosmic Blue Pearl.
- The XRT approach angle is 19.8 degrees compared to the non-XRT's 17.5 degrees, and the departure angle is 30.0 degrees compared to the non-XRT's 25.4 degrees.
- Non-XRT exterior changes include restyled front and rear bumpers emphasizing IONIQ’s wide, confident SUV stance, a new rear spoiler, new more aerodynamic wheels, available flush-type roof side rails, and new exterior colors: Atlas White Matte, Ecotronic Gray Matte, Celadon Gray Matte.
- Interior changes include redesigned HVAC controls, a new center console with physical heated front seat controls, a new steering wheel design, and heated rear seats for SEL and above trims.
- New infotainment/convenience technology includes a 12.3-inch audio/video navigation (AVN) system, standard wireless Apple CarPlay® / Android Auto™ integration, Hyundai Digital Key 2 Premium (NFC + Bluetooth Low Energy + UWB) for SEL and above, controller Over-The Air (OTA) update support, Smart Cruise Control 2, and USB Type-C ports (1 USB-C data in front seat, 2 USB-C charging in front and 2 in rear).
- Safety features include Blind-Spot Collision Warning for SE trim / Blind-Spot Collision-Avoidance Assist for SEL trim and above, Forward Attention Warning via In-Cabin Camera/Direct Driver Monitoring System, Parking Distance Warning with forward warning in addition to reverse warning, a rear windshield wiper and washer, Advanced Rear Occupant Alert (radar type) for SEL trim and above, Parking Distance Warning – Side for XRT and Limited, Parking Collision-Avoidance Assist – Reverse/(New)Forward/(New)Side for XRT and Limited, and Remote Smart Parking Assist 2 for XRT and Limited.
- The IONIQ 5’s interior offers improved ergonomics and convenience, with the upper part of the Universal Island featuring physical buttons for frequently used functions including heating and ventilation controls for first-row occupants, a button for the heated steering wheel and parking assist function, and a relocated wireless charging pad to significantly enhance usability.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 features a more powerful next-generation infotainment system with standard wireless Apple CarPlay® / Android Auto™ integration across the range and wireless Over-The-Air (OTA) software updates for all models to ensure major controls remain up to date and clear the way for new features to be added remotely.
- Hyundai engineers introduced new safety and convenience features including the Hands-On Detection (HOD) steering wheel, Lane Following Assist 2, Remote Smart Parking Assist 2 (RSPA 2), and Parking Collision-Avoidance Assist – Reverse / Forward / Side (PCA-R/F/S).
- In response to customer feedback, Hyundai applied features including Hyundai Digital Key 2 Premium and a rear wiper/washer combo.
- The body, front and rear doors, and B-pillar parts have been reinforced to improve side collision protection, and the vehicle features eight airbags including second-row side airbags for enhanced safety.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 XRT is a more capable variant positioned between the upgraded standard model and the high-performance IONIQ 5 N, designed to appeal to customers who want to play in the dirt and have all-electric adventures.
- The IONIQ 5 XRT boasts a more aggressive front and rear design with unique bumpers, stylish side skirts, and dedicated 18-inch aluminum wheels, emphasizing heightened capability in rough terrain made possible by higher ground clearance and suspension tuning.
- Hyundai Motor America offers U.S. consumers a technology-rich lineup of cars, SUVs, and electrified vehicles while supporting Hyundai Motor Company’s Progress for Humanity vision.
- Hyundai has significant operations in the U.S., including its North American headquarters in California, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama assembly plant, the all-new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (in development in Georgia), and several cutting-edge R&D facilities.
- These operations, combined with those of Hyundai’s 835 independent dealers, contribute $20.1 billion annually and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy according to a recent economic impact report.
