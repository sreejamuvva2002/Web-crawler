# Kia Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07

**Source:** [Kia to Invest Over $200 Million in EV9 Production Expansion - Georgia_CEO](https://goldenislesceo.com/news/2023/07/gov-kem-p-kia-invest-over-200-million-ev9-production-expansion/)  ·  `page_010177`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Kia will invest more than $200 million at its West Point plant.
- The investment will create nearly 200 new jobs.
- The investment is to accommodate assembly of the company’s all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- The production line transformation will make the EV9 the fifth model assembled at Kia Georgia.
- The EV9 joins the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan in Kia Georgia's lineup.
- EV9 assembly is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Governor Kemp noted that this project will bring more opportunity to Georgians and help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- The addition of the EV9 will further increase the facility’s output.
- The EV9 is Kia’s first three-row EV SUV.
- The EV9 is the first Kia EV to be assembled in North America.
- The EV9 is the flagship vehicle for Kia’s global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that like Telluride, the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia.
- Sean Yoon stated that the EV9 will be the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market and on the road.
- Kia Georgia is a certified Georgia Made manufacturer.
- Kia Georgia will continue to utilize the services of Georgia Quick Start, the nation’s top-ranked workforce development program.
- Georgia Quick Start operates the Kia Georgia Training Center.
- Quick Start’s comprehensive, customized training services will prepare new employees with the skills required to perform successfully on the new assembly line.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that the Kia Georgia team looks forward to continuing its tradition as the plant begins production of its first EV.
- Stuart Countess noted that partnerships developed statewide with the Governor’s Office, the Department of Economic Development, and Georgia QuickStart have been tremendous.
- Stuart Countess noted that local partnerships with the City of West Point and Troup County have also been tremendous.
- City of West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated that Kia has been an outstanding corporate citizen and community partner for seventeen years.
- Mayor Tramell noted that by providing employment opportunities and fostering economic growth, Kia has helped to improve the livelihoods of many people in West Point.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, the City of West Point, Troup County, Georgia Center of Innovation’s Manufacturing and Energy teams, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that since Georgia recruited Kia in 2006 to create long-lasting opportunities for West Georgia, Kia has exceeded expectations as a partner by giving back to its adopted community.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state of Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Earlier this year, Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as “Kia Day.”
- The recognition commended Kia’s vehicle manufacturing and its impact to the state since making one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia’s history with the 2006 announcement that a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility would be established in West Point.
- From Georgia, Kia ships vehicles to sales markets around the globe, including the United States, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East and Pacific nations.
- Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire supply chain as the emerging electric vehicle market continues to grow.
- This pursuit has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of over 28,400 jobs since 2020.
