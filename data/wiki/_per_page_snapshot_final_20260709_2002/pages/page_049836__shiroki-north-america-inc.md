# Shiroki North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Case Study: Shiroki North America eCatalog | MISUMI USA: Industrial Configurable Components Supply](https://us.misumi-ec.com/maker/misumi/mech/tech/casestudy/shiroki_12122012/)  ·  `page_049836`

## Facts

- Shiroki North America, Inc. is a premier manufacturer of automobile seat tracks and adjusters, window regulators, doorframes and door sash sold to automotive OEMs and their top tier suppliers.
- Key customers include Toyota Engineering and Manufacturing, Toyota Boshoku America, Nissan, Mitsubishi, Subaru-Indiana, Total Interior Systems-America, Johnson Controls, and NHK Spring Co., Ltd.
- The firm's parent company is Shiroki Corporation of Japan, which has been a global leader in automotive manufacturing for half a century.
- Shiroki North America initiated an in-house machine design/build engineering process based on standardization of tooling and die parts used in production jigs for mass manufacture of customer products.
- The company orders fixed and configurable factory automation and motion control components directly from Illinois-based MISUMI USA to achieve its in-house design/build goals.
- Brandon Johnson, Assistant General Manager of Engineering and Maintenance for Shiroki North America, notes that MISUMI components are utilized in virtually all new production jigs the company designs.
- The largest quantity of MISUMI components purchased is used as spare and replacement parts for existing production jigs as part of ongoing quality control and maintenance operations.
- Once engineers design a part to precise dimensions, it is standardized to save time and control costs, with MISUMI assigning a unique product number to facilitate re-ordering.
- Due to MISUMI's very short lead and delivery times, Shiroki North America does not need to maintain an abundance of inventory for spare and replacement parts, which helps hold down costs.
- The company's engineering team uses SolidWorks as the design platform when designing new production jigs for customer products.
- MISUMI's ability to configure components to exacting dimensions reduces the need for time- and labor-intensive fabrication of custom parts, whether in-house or by an outside supplier.
- MISUMI's CAD support offers automatic download of native CAD files, meaning engineers do not have to spend time drawing components or pay an outside supplier to draw them.
- Electrical panels play an integral role in Shiroki North America's products, including window regulators and seat adjustment mechanisms.
- The company uses MISUMI's print catalog and Web ordering system for electrical panel building components such as wire duct, terminals, and overall enclosures.
- Shiroki North America's partnership with MISUMI enabled the company to build a sizable machine development group and manufacture machines in-house rather than outsourcing.
- This initiative led to the largest cost savings in Shiroki North America's history.
- Shiroki North America entered the U.S. market in 1988 to provide a convenient supply source of high quality auto parts and assemblies for U.S.-manufactured vehicles.
- The company currently employs about 500 people.
- Shiroki North America operates manufacturing plants with a total of 238,000 sq ft of space in Dalton, Georgia, and Smithville and Gordonsville, Tennessee.
- The company continues to support operations in Japan, China, and Thailand.
