# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Seoyon E-Hwa, Savannah Tech sign training partnership MOU - Savannah Technical College](https://www.savannahtech.edu/seoyon-e-hwa-savannah-tech-sign-training-partnership-mou/)  ·  `page_001334`

## Facts

- Leadership from Seoyon E-Hwa and Savannah Technical College signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) for a training partnership at STC’s Savannah Campus.
- The training partnership MOU will include credit and non-credit training programs.
- STC President Dr. Kathy Love stated that working closely with advanced manufacturing partners like Seoyon E-Hwa is crucial for ensuring a supply of skilled employees to be successful.
- Seoyon E-Hwa, a global auto parts supplier that manufactures both interior and exterior components, announced in February 2023 that it will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs.
- The company plans to invest more than $96 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- Seoyon E-Hwa President Chung Yun Kim stated that partnering with Savannah Technical College provides a great opportunity to hire skilled employees and foster industrial-academic cooperation.
- The new manufacturing facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new facility will produce automotive interior parts such as door trims, headlining, and tailgate trims.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in October 2024.
- More than two-thirds, or approximately 500, of the announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production personnel.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry by building on the assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Established in 1972, Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 corporations in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- Seoyon E-Hwa supplies door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen, and more.
- Savannah Technical College is a unit of the Technical College System of Georgia and serves as the premier provider of career-ready technical education in Coastal Georgia.
- Savannah Tech offers a comprehensive range of nearly 150 market-driven programs preparing students for high-demand careers including Aviation Technology, Cybersecurity, Health Sciences, Logistics & Supply Chain Management, Automation & Robotics, Information Technology, Culinary Arts, and Public Services.
- With campuses in Chatham, Effingham, and Liberty Counties, Savannah Tech serves more than 13,000 credit and non-credit students with pathways including dual enrollment programs for high school students and specialized support services for military veterans and their families.
- Beyond academics, Savannah Tech is an economic and community development partner collaborating with industry leaders to provide customized training programs, assessment services, and continuing education opportunities.
- Savannah Technical College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges of Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award associate degrees, diplomas and technical certificates of credit.
- Savannah Technical College complies with the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG), which prohibits discrimination based on protected status in all programs and activities.
