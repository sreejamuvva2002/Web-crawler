# Salesforce, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Salesforce, Inc. will be investing $15 million to expand its presence in Georgia.
- The expansion is expected to create over 250 new jobs by the end of 2028.
- The investment will bring economic growth and job opportunities to Fulton County.
- Governor Kemp stated the decision is a direct result of the state's commitment to fostering innovation and economic opportunity.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia's pro-business environment and investments in workforce development as key factors in attracting companies like Salesforce.
- Salesforce is a global leader in AI-powered Customer Relationship Management (CRM) known for cloud-based technology that helps businesses connect with customers.
- The company currently employs over 2,000 people in Georgia.
- Salesforce serves customers across various industries including financial services, healthcare, retail, manufacturing, logistics, and the public sector.
- Tanya Counter serves as Site Lead for Salesforce Atlanta.
- Tanya Counter emphasized the company's confidence in Atlanta's talent and its commitment to creating an inclusive workplace.
- Tanya Counter stated the expansion reflects their dedication to helping customers, employees, and communities thrive.
- Tanya Counter expressed pride in continuing to grow and invest in a city they call home.
- Salesforce Tower Atlanta, located at 950 East Paces Ferry Rd NE, will serve as the hub for this expansion.
- Atlanta Mayor Andre Dickens praised the city's diverse talent pool, global connectivity through Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, and welcoming community.
- Mayor Dickens thanked Salesforce for their continued investment and looked forward to the opportunities this expansion will bring for residents and the local economy.
- Fulton County Board of Commissioners Chairman Robb Pitts welcomed the expansion as a win for the county driven by premier companies' confidence in the community.
- Chairman Pitts expressed pride in welcoming this growth and the opportunities it will bring for residents and the local economy.
- Katie Kirkpatrick serves as President and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber.
- Katie Kirkpatrick highlighted metro Atlanta's deep pool of talented professionals and thriving communities as key drivers for growing companies seeking quality jobs and economic growth.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented by Senior Regional Project Manager Caroline Kinchler, worked closely with Invest Atlanta, Fulton County, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, and Georgia Power to secure the expansion.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated Atlanta and Fulton County on attracting companies like Salesforce.
- Commissioner Wilson emphasized Georgia's support for industries that drive technology and innovation as a means to boost local economies.
- With Salesforce's expansion, Georgia continues to solidify its position as a leading state for business and innovation.
- The investment will contribute to the overall economic growth of Fulton County and the state as a whole.
