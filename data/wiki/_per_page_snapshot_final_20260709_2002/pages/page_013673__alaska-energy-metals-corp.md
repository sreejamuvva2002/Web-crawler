## Facts

- Gregory Beischer serves as CEO of Alaska Energy Metals.
- Gregory Beischer contributed an article to EV World Magazine addressing nickel supply constraints for the electric vehicle market.
- The rapid decarbonization of transportation is straining the global EV supply chain.
- Nickel is an essential component in EV batteries and faces a risk of running short.
- A potential nickel shortage could cause major disruption to the EV industry.
- Batteries are central to electric vehicles and consist of lithium, cobalt, and 64 pounds of nickel.
- Mining remains the only method to extract these essential battery materials.
- The U.S. Department of Energy projects that nickel supply will be critically low by 2025.
- Domestic mining operations must rapidly scale up extraction to meet essential metal demands.
- Nickel miners face ongoing challenges including slow permit approvals and community pushback.
- Limited resource supply combined with heightened demand threatens EV affordability and availability.
- EVs are expected to maintain high price points due to current market conditions.
- The current wait time for purchasing an electric vehicle ranges from two months to three years.
- EV purchase wait times will likely increase further if existing supply chain issues remain unresolved.
- Strained domestic nickel supply has forced a heavy reliance on Asian markets, particularly Indonesia.
- Indonesia currently holds the position of the world's largest supplier of nickel.
- Dependence on Indonesia for nickel creates future supply risks due to the country's political leverage over the resource.
- Indonesian nickel production relies on coal-powered execution, which conflicts with global energy transition objectives.
- Consumer demand for cleaner transportation is driven by concerns over climate change, air quality, high operating costs, and greenhouse gas emissions.
- Securing the future energy supply requires refocusing and reprioritizing domestic nickel mining efforts.
