# Michelin

## Facts

- Michelin tread technologies are designed to deliver maximum traction, longer tire life, and consistent performance across challenging road and weather conditions.
- From advanced tread patterns to innovative siping designs, Michelin tread solutions help fleets improve safety, efficiency, and return on investment.
- The exclusive B² technology from Michelin makes the bead area more robust to increase the service life of tires in mines, quarries, construction, and handling operations.
- Tires with Diamond Tread Pattern technology feature a wide metal bead wire that increases the area in contact with the rim.
- The enhanced strength of the bead area results in fewer rotations on the rim.
- With the Michelin diamond tread pattern, 44%1 of the tread is always in contact with hard surfaces, while with a bar tread pattern, only 29%1 of the tread is always in contact.
- The larger contact area provides exceptional traction because two sections of lug are always in contact with the surface.
- Even pressure distribution across the tire surface helps maintain stability on various terrains, resulting in enhanced robustness and wear resistance.
- Matrix Siping technology features an innovative interlocking 3-dimensional matrix siping with zig-zag walls that lock together when in contact with the road.
- Sipes provide hundreds of biting edges to improve wet and snow traction.
- Interlocking sipes add strength and stability normally associated with solid tread blocks to improve wear for longer-lasting performance.
- Michelin-patented Regenion technology uses 3D metal printing techniques to provide solid grip over the tire’s lifetime in all conditions.
- As the tire wears, new grooves emerge in Regenion designs.
- The compact and rigid tread pattern of Regenion provides higher mileage, more consistent wear, and lower fuel consumption.
- In 2018, two Michelin engineers behind Regenion technology won the European Inventor Award in the industry category from the European Patent Office (EPO).
- New grooves opening as the tread wears provide long-lasting control in all weather conditions throughout the tire's life.
- The rigid tread pattern leads to more consistent wear and longer mileage.
- Compact tread designs lower rolling resistance, helping to reduce fuel consumption.
- Stubble Shield technology includes an anti-stubble strip, oriented lugs that deflect stubble, and rubber components that prolong the life of agricultural tires.
- MICHELIN tires with Stubble Shield technology are identified with a dedicated pictogram.
- Tires with Stubble Shield technology provide extra protection against stubble damage and support tire lifespan.
- Agricultural tires resistant to damage from residual stubble reduce downtime caused by punctures, allowing confident operation on any terrain.
- Michelin’s tread innovations are engineered for traction, durability, and efficiency in every condition.
- Tread patterns determine how the tire grips the road, with Michelin’s Diamond Tread Pattern increasing surface area contact to improve traction on hard surfaces.
- Specialized siping enhances grip in wet or snowy conditions while helping to maintain tire flexibility in cold temperatures.
- Regenion designs feature tread grooves that evolve as the tire wears, maintaining grip over the lifespan for longer-lasting traction and better fuel efficiency without sacrificing mileage.
- Different tread designs are tailored for specific uses: Regenion is ideal for long-haul trucks, Matrix Siping is perfect for mixed climates, Diamond Tread Pattern excels on hard surfaces, and Stubble Shield is designed for agricultural tires.
