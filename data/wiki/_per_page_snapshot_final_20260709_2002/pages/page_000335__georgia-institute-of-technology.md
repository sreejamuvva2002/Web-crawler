# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-24

**Source:** [Georgia Tech reveals iron-chloride cathode for solid-state batteries - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2024/09/24/georgia-tech-reveals-iron-chloride-cathode-for-solid-state-batteries/)  ·  `page_000335`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech researchers developed a new cathode chemistry for solid-state batteries called FeCl3 (iron trichloride).
- The FeCl3 cathode uses abundant materials iron (Fe) and chlorine (Cl), avoiding the use of nickel and cobalt.
- Initial tests showed the FeCl3 cathode performed as well or better than other more expensive cathodes.
- The new cathode demonstrated a higher operational voltage compared to LFP batteries currently flooding the market.
- Lead researcher and associate professor Chen stated the technology could make EVs much cheaper than internal combustion cars.
- Chen noted the cathode provides a promising form of large-scale energy storage that enhances electrical grid resilience.
- The research group claims their cathode would greatly improve the sustainability and supply chain stability of the EV market.
- Researchers estimate the technology may need less than five years before reaching commercial viability in electric vehicles.
- Chen stated the team's immediate goal is to make the materials as perfect as possible in the lab and understand the underlying functioning mechanisms.
- The team expressed openness to opportunities to scale up the technology and push it toward commercial applications.
- The research group's findings were published in the paper “Low-cost iron trichloride cathode for all-solid-state lithium-ion batteries” in Nature Sustainability.
