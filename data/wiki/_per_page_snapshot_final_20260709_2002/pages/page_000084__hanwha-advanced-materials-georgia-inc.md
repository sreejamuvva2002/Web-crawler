# Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-23

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Qcells Supplier to Invest $147 Million in Cartersville Manufacturing Facility | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-03-23/gov-kemp-qcells-supplier-invest-147-million-cartersville-manufacturing)  ·  `page_000084`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on March 23, 2023 that Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia, Inc. (HAGA) will create more than 160 new jobs in the City of Cartersville.
- HAGA will invest an estimated $147 million in a new manufacturing facility to supply the Qcells facility in Cartersville.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is leading the nation in attracting next generation jobs and has announced more than 4,000 related jobs since welcoming Qcells to the state in 2018.
- HAGA is a subsidiary of Hanwha Group.
- HAGA will supply Qcells with encapsulant film used in solar cells to ensure long-term panel durability.
- HAGA will be the only company in the United States manufacturing solar encapsulants.
- Inhwan Kim, CEO of Hanwha Advanced Materials, stated that their products are an important piece of the clean energy supply chain puzzle and building advanced materials in Georgia will create new careers in solar and help bring affordable, reliable clean energy to customers across the country.
- HG Park, President of Qcells North America, stated that Qcells is doubling down on building a complete, domestic solar supply chain and this recent investment is critical to making that happen.
- HG Park noted that working with Hanwha Advanced Materials will allow customers to confidently know that the solar they buy from them was made in America.
- HAGA will construct its new manufacturing facility at Highland 75 Corporate/Industrial Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified site in Cartersville.
- The facility is predicted to come online in Summer 2024.
- The company will be hiring for engineers and line operators.
- Mayor of Cartersville Matt Santini stated that the community is thrilled to add another Hanwha affiliate, appreciating the investment in quality jobs and noting it diversifies the local industrial mix while illustrating growth in the clean energy market.
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor stated that the news testifies to the community's patient planning efforts to jumpstart quality economic development years ago after difficult economic times.
- Steve Taylor noted that HAGA's future jobs will be appreciated by job seekers and the local team that prepared the GRAD-Certified Highland 75 Corporate/Industrial Park.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Bartow-Cartersville Joint Development Authority, Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, Georgia Electric Membership Corporation (Georgia EMC), and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hanwha Advanced Materials provides key components for the solar supply chain in Georgia, supporting the state's commitment to making energy solutions more affordable to businesses and consumers.
- Pat Wilson noted that clean-tech jobs have been one of the fastest growing manufacturing sectors in Georgia over the past five years and HAGA adds another crucial part to Georgia's robust ecosystem.
- Solar photovoltaic is the fastest-growing source of energy in Georgia, which ranks as a Top Ten state for solar energy production.
- The Solar Energy Industries Association ranked Georgia seventh by cumulative solar capacity in 2022.
- Georgia’s energy solutions providers are helping accelerate renewable energy product development by lowering risks, reducing costs, providing access to innovative industry research, and investing in superior infrastructure networks.
- To reduce supply-chain barriers to the growth of the solar industry, companies like Qcells are dedicated to building a comprehensive solar value chain in the U.S.
- In January, Qcells announced it would expand its solar panel production capacity in Georgia to 8.4 gigawatts, with 3.3 gigawatts manufactured at the Bartow County facility by 2024.
- Hanwha Advanced Materials Company is a global leader in the irreplaceable, high-performance, lightweight composite materials market and provides products essential for lightweight composite materials and solar materials.
- Qcells is one of the world’s leading clean energy companies, recognized as a manufacturer of high-performance solar cells and modules, intelligent storage systems, and large-scale renewable energy projects.
- Qcells also provides renewable electricity retail services and packages to end customers worldwide.
- Qcells is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea (Global Executive HQ) and Thalheim, Germany (Technology & Innovation HQ).
- Qcells operates diverse international manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, China, and South Korea.
