# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Aurubis Richmond: First US multimetal recycling plant starts production of strategic metals](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2025/aurubis-richmond-first-usmultimetal-recycling-plant-starts-production-of-strategic-metals)  ·  `page_001290`

## Facts

- Aurubis Richmond is the first US multimetal recycling plant.
- The facility recovers strategic metals including copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals from recycling materials for American energy infrastructure, data centers, AI applications, and the defense industry.
- American industry currently needs about 1.8 million t of copper per year.
- Experts estimate that demand for strategic metals like copper will rise nearly 30% in the next five years.
- The US currently imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Aurubis Richmond operates as the first and most technologically advanced secondary smelter in the US to process complex recycling materials.
- The facility's "First Melt" celebration took place on Wednesday, September 24, 2025, in Augusta, Georgia/US.
- Event participants included US Representative Rick W. Allen for Georgia’s 12th District and German Ambassador to the United States Jens Hanefeld.
- Aurubis AG CEO Dr. Toralf Haag stated that Aurubis Richmond is the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the US, matching its position in Europe.
- Dr. Toralf Haag noted that high availability of recycling material and good local conditions make the American market appealing for continued expansion of strategic raw material production expertise.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated in a video message that Aurubis Richmond is now a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain.
- Governor Kemp described the facility as a testament to American manufacturing, independence, and resiliency.
- The project involves an investment of about $800 million.
- Aurubis is creating more than 240 jobs in Georgia through this facility.
- The plant will process up to 180,000 t of complex recycling material annually, including printed circuit boards, copper cable, and other metal-bearing products.
- An expansion stage for the facility is scheduled to start in 2026.
- Aurubis Richmond recovers critical metals from incoming materials on site, closing the value chain locally.
- The facility will be supplied with recycling materials locally to help keep valuable raw materials in the market.
- US Representative Rick W. Allen emphasized that Aurubis Richmond is a strategic site in Georgia’s 12th District that reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals for national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, described the facility as an important player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
- David Schultheis thanked Georgia policymakers and governmental authorities for their strong support, smooth approval processes, and enabling the company to settle quickly in the state.
- The new plant is equipped with cutting-edge technology that fulfills the latest environmental standards enacted by Georgia state and federal authorities.
- The facility operates with the highest consideration for water, air, and soil quality.
- Aurubis Richmond is designed as a flexible “one-stop shop” that will receive a broad range of metal-bearing recycling materials from US suppliers.
