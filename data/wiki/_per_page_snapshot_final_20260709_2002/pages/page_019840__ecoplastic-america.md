## Facts

- Since May, Ecoplastic America has launched its careers website, hit the ground running with community outreach events, and started training its team in everything from safety and paint to leadership development.
- Kevin Campbell is Ecoplastic America's new Talent Acquisition and Public Affairs Manager who started working for the company this spring.
- Ecoplastic America is hosting open interviews starting Tuesday, June 25, 2024, from 2pm to 6pm at its Statesboro office located at 25 Siebald Street in Downtown Statesboro.
- The Statesboro office is located beside the Judicial Annex, behind the Courthouse.
- Applicants must apply for the 'Production Team Member' job post on the company's website before attending the open interviews.
- Ecoplastic America will continue holding open interviews every Tuesday from 2pm to 6pm at the Statesboro office following June 25, 2024.
- Production Team Members will work hand-in-hand with experienced Production Supervisors to ensure production lines run smoothly and efficiently.
- Sabrina Vasher is Ecoplastic America's Director of Human Resources.
- The company is seeking candidates who can perform administrative tasks, provide manufacturing support, have specialized skills in some areas, and are willing to learn.
- Ecoplastic America is committed to building a team and then training them to perform at their highest potential.
- Ecoplastic America's team attended Georgia Southern University's inaugural CPE Leadership Conference in May.
- The conference featured dynamic discussions on talent retention, diversity, effective communication, boosting morale, and optimizing performance.
- Dr. Brent Stubbs, Senior Manager of Learning & Development at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, presented at the conference.
- Justin Farquhar, Vice President of Workforce Savannah Economic Development Authority, presented at the conference.
- Sabrina Vasher shared her expertise alongside Jamie Calloway, HR Manager for Ajin-Joon of Georgia, in a panel discussion on HR dynamics in Korean-owned Tier 1 Hyundai supplier companies in South Georgia.
- The Ecoplastic America team has completed specialized robotics paint operations training by Sames.
- The team has also completed safety training by Georgia Quick Start.
- Ecoplastic America views its commitment to training team members as part of the company's culture and vision for the future.
- Maurice Williams is Ecoplastic America's Senior Manager of Paint Operations.
- Thomas Kim is the CEO of Ecoplastic America.
- Creating a collaborative, safety-conscious, and engaged team with a positive work culture continues to be a top priority for the company.
- Ecoplastic America will be hiring 500 employees from the area.
- The company is investing more than $205 million in its Bulloch County facility.
- All positions are located in Bulloch County, Georgia.
