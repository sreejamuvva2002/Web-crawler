# Duckyang Industrial Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-20

## Facts

- On May 20, 2021, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Korean automotive manufacturer Duckyang will open its first U.S. facility in Jackson County.
- Duckyang will invest $10 million in opening the new manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, Georgia.
- The facility will be located at 984 Broadway Avenue in Braselton.
- Duckyang’s new facility will be 230,000-square-foot.
- Duckyang is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules to customers around the world including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules are integrated auto parts comprised of more than 130 subcomponents found near the driver’s seat of a vehicle.
- Duckyang is an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA).
- By opening a facility in Georgia, Duckyang will be able to better serve several of its automotive customers within the region.
- Dong-in Son, CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd., stated that entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA will allow cooperation with other automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors that already have a manufacturing base in the U.S.
- Duckyang expects business expansion opportunities to supply not only electric vehicle parts but also interior parts, including cockpit modules, door trim panel modules, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, maintenance, logistics, and warehouse management.
- Town Manager and Clerk of Braselton Jennifer Scott stated that the town looks forward to welcoming Duckyang to their community.
- John Scott, Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, stated that companies like Duckyang are helping Jackson County and Georgia secure a place in the automotive industry's future as it moves toward electric vehicles.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project in partnership with the Jackson County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain and using relationships with SK Battery America and Korean companies like Duckyang to secure local supply of needed parts for their customers.
- During 2020 alone, Georgia facilitated more than $1.3 billion in investment and more than 2,700 jobs from South Korean companies.
- Duckyang Industrial was established in July 1977 and is the first company to develop and produce automobile cockpit modules in Korea.
- Duckyang solidified its position through a strategic alliance with Visteon, an American global automotive supplier that was spun off from Ford in 1999.
- Duckyang plans to expand production capabilities for interior automotive parts in addition to electronics in the future as it continuously promotes research and development on new technologies.
