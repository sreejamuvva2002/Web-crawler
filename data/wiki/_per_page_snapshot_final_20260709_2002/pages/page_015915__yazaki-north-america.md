# Yazaki North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yazaki is Ready to Open Second Plant in San Marcos to Expand Production for Ford](https://www.guatemalaportal.com/yazaki-is-ready-to-open-second-plant-in-san-marcos-to-expand-production-for-ford/)  ·  `page_015915`

## Facts

- Yazaki will expand its operations in Guatemala with a second plant in San Marcos to produce wiring harnesses for Ford F-150 vehicles.
- The new facility is expected to generate up to 3,500 jobs and strengthen Guatemala’s role in the regional automotive supply chain.
- Yazaki North America (YNA) will construct its second plant in the municipality of Pajapita, San Marcos.
- The new site is located about seven kilometers east of YNA's existing facility in Ayutla.
- Initial earthworks for the new facility have already begun.
- The new facility will cover 35,000 square meters.
- The new plant is expected to start operations in September 2027.
- At full capacity, the new plant is expected to generate around 3,500 formal jobs.
- This expansion follows the success of Yazaki’s first plant in Ayutla, which was inaugurated in 2023.
- The first plant already produces wiring harnesses and supplies directly to Ford.
- Kenichi Fujisawa serves as president and CEO of YNA.
- Kenichi Fujisawa stated that "Ford is satisfied and ready for operations to begin at the second plant."
- Formal hiring is expected to start in mid-January next year.
- Government officials highlighted that Yazaki is the first global automotive company manufacturing in Guatemala within the electrical and electronic segment.
- An official stated, "This marks an important step in the evolution of our industrial base and represents progress in the sophistication of our economy."
- The automotive value chain operates through levels of specialization, with Yazaki positioned at Tier 2.
- Japan’s ambassador to Guatemala is Ryosuke Kuwana.
- Ryosuke Kuwana emphasized that the project strengthens Guatemala's integration into North America’s automotive value chain.
- A company executive noted that "The quality of the Guatemalan workforce was key in our decision."
- Authorities reported that in 2025, 80 companies were evaluating investment opportunities in Guatemala.
- Nine companies are already established in Guatemala, including Nextil.
- Most of these established companies are focused on light manufacturing.
- Foreign direct investment reached US$1.6945 billion in 2024.
- Projections for foreign direct investment are US$1.8 billion in 2025.
- Expectations are to exceed US$2 billion by 2026.
- Initiatives such as recent visits from international companies and upcoming Japanese business missions could further boost investment.
