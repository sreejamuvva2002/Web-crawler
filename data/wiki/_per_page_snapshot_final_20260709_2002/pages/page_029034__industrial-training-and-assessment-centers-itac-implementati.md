# Industrial Training and Assessment Centers (ITAC) Implementation Grants

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-12

**Source:** [Industrial Training and Assessment Center Implementation Grant Awards | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/mesc/industrial-research-and-assessment-center-implementation-grant-awards)  ·  `page_029034`

## Facts

- 219 small- and medium-sized manufacturers (SMMs) across 38 states selected to receive a combined total of more than $40 million to implement energy saving improvements.
- The ITAC Implementation Grants are matched by more than $83 million in industry investment.
- The program is funded by the President’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- The ITAC Implementation Grants program provides up to $300,000 per manufacturer to implement recommendations made by DOE and other qualified energy assessments.
- A total of 34 additional energy assessment providers have been qualified as "ITAC-equivalent."
- Projects funded include installing Light-emitting Diode (LED) lighting to optimize energy usage.
- Projects funded include installing auxiliary equipment (e.g., monitors, sensors, level and pressure gauges) to improve day-to-day operation equipment & improve energy efficiency.
- Projects funded involve upgrading existing heating or cooling equipment to optimize energy usage.
- Projects funded include installing onsite power generation equipment (e.g., solar panels, micro-turbines) to generate electricity at the facility/plant.
- Projects funded include installing variable-frequency drives on existing equipment (e.g., such as motors, pumps, compressors) to optimize energy usage.
- Projects funded include installing a capacitor bank to ensure accurate electricity demand and minimize site energy waste.
- Projects funded involve upgrading other industrial grade ancillary equipment (e.g., pipes, fans, blowers, & compressors) to further improve site energy usage.
