# Meta

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Meta announced three new large solar projects in Georgia as part of an agreement with Walton EMC to provide renewable energy equal to 100% of the company's data center usage in Newton County.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Meta's solar projects will supply clean electricity for its data centers and support Georgia Power's Make Ready Program to upgrade electric vehicle charging stations.

## Key facts

- Meta announced three new large solar projects in Georgia to provide renewable energy equivalent to 100% of its data center usage in Newton County.
- Walton EMC will supply the renewable electricity under the agreement with Meta.
- The Georgia Public Service Commission, led by Commissioner Tim Echols, tripled the budget for Georgia Power's Make Ready Program, allocating $53 million over three years to upgrade electric vehicle charging stations.
- House Bill 406 passed the Republican-controlled Georgia House unanimously (161-0) to expand EV-charging opportunities across the state.
- QCells is constructing a $2.31 billion solar panel manufacturing plant in Cartersville that will create 2,000 jobs and could meet 30% of U.S. solar panel demand by 2027; its expansion in Dalton adds 510 additional jobs.
- Senator Jon Ossoff noted broad bipartisan support in Georgia for securing energy independence and affordable clean energy.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Georgia aims to become "the electric mobility capital of America" by the end of his second term.
- The Justice 40 initiative seeks to ensure 40% of federal green-investment benefits reach historically marginalized communities.
