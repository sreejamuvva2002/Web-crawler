## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) officially began production the week of October 1, 2024.
- Production began ahead of its projected schedule, less than two years after breaking ground.
- The first electric vehicle to roll off the assembly line was an IONIQ 5.
- Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia vehicle models will be produced at the Bryan County facility.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County contributed $250,000 to the Well Mitigation Fund.
- Development Authority of Bryan County contributed $250,000 to the Well Mitigation Fund.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority contributed $250,000 to the Well Mitigation Fund.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America contributed $250,000 to the Well Mitigation Fund.
- Total contributions to the Well Mitigation Fund reached $1 million.
- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp celebrated Hyundai Motor Group's historic impact on Georgia on February 29, 2024.
- José Muñoz serves as President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America.
- The HMGMA facility was scheduled to officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- Automotive supplier Kyungshin America Corp. will establish a new distribution facility in Rincon, Effingham County.
- Kyungshin America's new facility will create an estimated 70 new jobs.
- Kyungshin America's investment exceeds $22 million in Effingham County.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority released a Regional Workforce Study for the Savannah region on November 14, 2023.
- The workforce study was conducted by Wadley Donovan Gutshaw Consulting, LLC (WDGC).
- The workforce study aimed to identify labor supply challenges facing Savannah region industrial employers and develop initiatives to minimize exposure to existing and future supply shortfalls.
- The workforce study scope included a market analysis, identifying needs and challenges, and providing a strategic workforce plan.
- Automotive supplier Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center in Chatham County.
- Daechang Seat Savannah specializes in automotive seat frames and is located in close proximity to HMGMA.
- Daechang Seat Savannah will create over 500 jobs.
- DAS Corp., an automotive parts company, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter, Candler County.
- DAS Corp.'s facility will create 300 new jobs and generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
