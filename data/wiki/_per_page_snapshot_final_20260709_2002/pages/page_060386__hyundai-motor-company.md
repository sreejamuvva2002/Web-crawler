# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Plans 3 Battery Factories With Annual Capacity Of 90 GWh In Georgia – Candela Green Energy](https://candela.com.my/hyundai-plans-3-battery-factories-with-annual-capacity-of-90-gwh-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_060386`

## Facts

- Hyundai has broken ground on a $5 billion electric car factory near Savannah, Georgia.
- Hyundai is partnering with SK On and LG Energy Solution to build three new battery factories in Georgia.
- The three new battery factories will have a total annual capacity of 90 GWh.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution are considering building two joint venture battery plants in Georgia near the new Hyundai factory, each with an annual capacity of 35 GWh.
- The new factories aim to help Hyundai and Kia qualify for the US federal electric vehicle tax credit under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The federal EV tax credit program requires that 40% of battery materials and 50% of battery components be sourced within the United States or from countries with a free trade agreement with the USA.
- Under the federal EV tax credit program, cars must be finally assembled in the US to qualify.
- The sourcing percentage requirements for the federal EV tax credit will rise over the next 4 years to 80% by 2027.
- LG Energy Solution is the principal battery supplier for General Motors.
- Hyundai and SK On plan to build a $2 billion battery cell factory in Georgia near the new Hyundai factory.
- The start of production for the Hyundai & SK On factory is planned for the first quarter of 2026.
- The Hyundai & SK On factory will have an annual capacity of 20 GWh.
- SK On will produce pouch-type high-nickel batteries for Hyundai’s new factory near Savannah, its existing factory in Montgomery, Alabama, and a Kia factory in West Point, Georgia.
- SK On already operates two battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia and has secured land for two more factories within the state.
- Hyundai and Kia have been using SK On’s pouch cells for most of their EVs, including the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 6.
- SK On pouch cells will power the IONIQ 7 SUV expected to be released next year.
- SK On pouch cells will power the Genesis GV70 EV, which will be produced in the Alabama plant.
- The Kia EV6 uses pouch cell batteries from SK On.
- The Kia EV9 SUV was introduced in April of 2023 and also uses pouch cell batteries.
- Much of the money made available by the Biden administration to support the electric car market will be spent in states like Georgia, Alabama, and South Carolina.
- Timothy Fox, an analyst at ClearView Energy Partners, told Bloomberg Green that domestic manufacturing in traditional Republican districts may soften partisan splits on renewables as an implicit long-term strategy for Democrats.
- President Biden hopes the IRA and other infrastructure-related laws will form the basis of an industrial policy that revitalizes cities across the country while fighting climate change.
