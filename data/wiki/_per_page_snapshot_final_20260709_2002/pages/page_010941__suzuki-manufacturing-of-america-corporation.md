# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-02

**Source:** [Suzuki Celebrates 20 Years Of ATV Manufacturing In America – MotoAmerica](https://motoamerica.info/2022/05/24/suzuki-celebrates-20-years-of-atv-manufacturing-in-america/)  ·  `page_010941`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor USA, LLC is celebrating 20 years of manufacturing KingQuad ATVs at the Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) facility.
- KingQuad ATVs first rolled off the assembly line in Rome, Georgia in May of 2002.
- KingQuad ATVs have continued to be built exclusively in the United States since production began in 2002.
- Suzuki is the inventor of the 4-wheel ATV.
- The SMAC facility is a multi-million dollar, 35-acre manufacturing site located in Rome, Georgia.
- The facility's location in the center of the biggest ATV market in the world allows access to real-world rider feedback for product refinement and timely delivery of KingQuad ATVs to Suzuki dealers worldwide.
- Suzuki founded its engineering operations in 1909.
- Suzuki led the industry by creating the first ever four-wheel ATV in 1983.
- Kerry Graeber serves as Vice President and Division Manager for MC/ATV Sales and Marketing at Suzuki.
- Kerry Graeber stated that building KingQuad ATVs in America for 20 years represents the commitment, hard work, and dedication of the people who build them in the Rome, Georgia facility.
- The SMAC facility is a key component of the continued success of Suzuki’s global ATV business.
- KingQuad ATVs built at the Rome, Georgia facility are shipped to U.S. Suzuki dealers and nearly 30 countries worldwide.
- KingQuad ATVs are built in the USA using U.S. and imported parts.
