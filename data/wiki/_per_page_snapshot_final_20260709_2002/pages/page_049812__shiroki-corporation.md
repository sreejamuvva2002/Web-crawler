# Shiroki Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Shiroki Corporation Business Report FY2009 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_134_hl2009)  ·  `page_049812`

## Facts

- Shiroki Corporation planned to infuse additional 20 million US dollars (approximately 1.9 billion yen) in Shiroki North America, Inc., its production facility in Smithville, Tennessee, U.S.A.
- The additional fund for Shiroki North America, Inc. will be used as operating capital, funds for paying debt, and costs for restructuring due to a sharp decrease in sales and profit amid the stagnant automotive industry in the U.S.
- Shiroki North America is the Group's largest production facility outside Japan.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC is located in Georgia, USA.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC is installing manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment amount of 238 million JPY.
- The project period for Shiroki-GA, LLC's manufacturing equipment installation is from January 2010 to December 2010.
- Most of the Company's capital investment was devoted to the automotive component business.
- The Company improved facilities in order to improve productivity and reduce cost and prepared the facilities to produce products for new vehicle models.
- The Company has completed consolidation of its production operations in Aichi Prefecture, Japan.
- Manufacturing operations in Aichi Prefecture have been integrated into two plants of Toyokawa Plant, Toyokawa City, and Nagoya Plant, Toyota City.
- By shutting down the former Toyota Plant, the number of Shiroki's production bases in Japan has been reduced from six to five.
- Sales declined 9.3% year-on-year to 111,924 million JPY in FY2009 because the Company's major customers (automakers) reduced their production volumes.
- Operating income improved to 2,680 million JPY as a result of consolidating production sites in Japan, improving manufacturing processes by implementing a one-shift, fixed-working-hour system, and engaging in cost reduction programs.
- R&D expenditure for the automotive components business was 2,154 million JPY in FY2009.
- Recent R&D achievement for the automotive components business includes next-generation power seats (next generation manual seats).
- Recent R&D achievement for the automotive components business includes new wire-type regulators.
- Recent R&D achievement for the automotive components business includes new arm-type regulators.
- Recent R&D achievement for the automotive components business includes new door frames.
- The Company holds a technical partnership (licensing-in) with Keiper GmbH & Co. in Germany for seat recliners from April 1, 1988 to March 31, 2011.
- The Company has a licensing-out partnership with NHK Spring (Thailand) Co., Ltd. in Thailand for seat recliners until December 9, 2010.
- The Company has a licensing-out partnership with Summit Laemchabang Auto Body Co. in Thailand for door frames until December 31, 2010.
- The Company has a licensing-out partnership with Asan Co., Ltd. in Korea for automotive door frames until October 31, 2010.
- The Company plans to increase supplies to Japanese automakers in India through Technico Industries, Ltd., a parts supplier in Gurgaon, Haryana, India, in which it holds equity.
- Shiroki will provide Technico with facilities for the three lines of stamping, sub-assembly and assembly from Japan to start production in 2010.
- Orders were received from Maruti Suzuki India Ltd., New Delhi, for seat recliners and seat adjusters, which will be Technico's first seat parts.
- Shiroki's major client Toyota Motor Corp. has placed parts orders for its newly developed small car, the EFC (Entry Family Car), targeted at emerging countries.
