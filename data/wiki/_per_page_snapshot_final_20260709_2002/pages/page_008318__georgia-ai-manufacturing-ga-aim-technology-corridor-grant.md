# Georgia AI Manufacturing (GA-AIM) Technology Corridor Grant

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-06

**Source:** [$65M Grant to Build AI Manufacturing in Georgia | News Center](https://news.gatech.edu/news/2022/09/06/65m-grant-build-ai-manufacturing-georgia)  ·  `page_008318`

## Facts

- Aaron Stebner and Tom Kurfess are among the leaders of GA-AIM, a $65 million grant that focuses on manufacturing and AI in the state of Georgia.
- Aaron Stebner outlined an aggressive plan for artificial intelligence and manufacturing when he applied for a faculty position in 2019.
- In his cover letter, Stebner promised to establish the Georgia Institute of Technology as a world leader in additive manufacturing of solid materials (ceramics & metals) R&D, especially in the fusion of data sciences and AI to create new, world-leading technologies.
- Stebner originally thought it would take 10-15 years of incremental steps and funding to achieve his goal.
- The project is funded by a $65 million grant from the U.S. Department of Commerce’s Economic Development Administration.
- The grant was announced by President Joe Biden.
- Stebner's plan will begin to become a reality two years after he arrived on campus.
- The initiative covers the entire state of Georgia and all of its manufacturing sectors, ranging from agriculture to airplanes.
- This $65 million grant is the largest of the nine projects within the larger Georgia AI Manufacturing (GA-AIM) technology corridor grant.
- The grant will allow Stebner and Georgia Tech to transform the Advanced Manufacturing Pilot Facility (AMPF) into the Artificial Intelligence Manufacturing Pilot Facility (AI-MPF).
- The 24,000 square-foot facility is located on 14th Street.
- The facility will more than double in size after Georgia Tech and statewide GA-AIM partners were selected as one of 21 Phase II awardees.
- The selection was part of the $1 billion Build Back Better Regional Challenge (BBB) competition.
- The BBB competition is part of the Investing in America’s Communities initiative under the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021.
- AMPF had previously been a shell waiting for funding to fill it out, according to Stebner.
- Aaron Stebner is an associate professor at the George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering and the School of Materials Science and Engineering.
- The transformed facility will be one of the nation’s first manufacturing labs designed for autonomy.
- The goal of the new lab is to help the state and the nation become world AI manufacturing leaders.
