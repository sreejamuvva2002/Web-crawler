# Nippon Denkai

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-22

**Source:** [Japanese company, Nippon Denkai, shifts location for $150 million copper foil plant from SC to Georgia | Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/technology/japanese-company-nippon-denkai-shifts-plans-150-million-copper-foil-plant-sc-georgia)  ·  `page_019246`

## Facts

- A Japanese company announced plans Wednesday for a $150 million plant to make copper foil for electric vehicle batteries in Augusta, Georgia.
- The plant location was shifted from a previously announced site near an existing Camden, South Carolina, facility.
- Nippon Denkai's American subsidiary said it would hire more than 100 people.
- The company could eventually triple production at the Georgia site from an initial 9,500 metric tons (10,500 tons) to 28,500 metric tons (31,500 tons).
- Tripling production would raise investment to $430 million and create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- Denkai America spokesperson Michael Coll said: "Our goal is to triple the capacity in Augusta; market conditions will determine timeline."
- The company raised its initial investment by $10 million from what it had announced in December.
- Nippon Denkai told Japanese investors that the 115-acre (47-hectare) Augusta site is better than the South Carolina location because there is room for expansion.
- The Augusta site can host an "advanced" equipment layout.
- The Augusta site offers a "key cost competitive edge in electricity price."
- State and local officials will give the company what could be more than $76 million in tax breaks and incentives.
- Nippon Denkai said it will start construction in September.
- The company hopes to be shipping samples to manufacturers by the summer of 2024.
- The company sees a big opportunity to make copper foil for electric vehicle battery makers, noting many battery plants are being built in the United States but few copper foil plants are.
- Companies will want domestic sources to avoid supply chain problems including risks of politically driven supply disruptions.
- Nippon Denkai projects an annual 21.4% growth in copper foil demand through 2035.
- The ability to triple production in Augusta will allow the company to pursue that projected growth.
- Combined with the 30-year-old Camden plant, the company would have an American production capacity of more than 35,000 metric tons.
- In June, Denkai announced a smaller $14 million expansion at the 80-worker Camden plant, also related to expansion in the electric battery and vehicle market.
- Augusta-Richmond County will give property tax breaks valued at a projected $68.7 million over 25 years.
- The company will pay $30 million in taxes, according to the Augusta Economic Development Authority.
- The company gets free land valued at $2.25 million, said Cal Wray, the authority's president.
- Georgia officials said they will spend an undisclosed amount of money to train workers.
- Nippon Denkai could claim various state tax breaks, including an income tax credit allowing it to annually deduct $4,000 per job from state income taxes, up to $5 million over five years.
- The income tax credit applies as long as workers make at least $28,000 a year.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson pointed to the announcement as more fruit of the state's focus on what he calls a "generational opportunity" in electric vehicles.
- The state's focus includes the $5 billion Rivian Automotive plant east of Atlanta.
- The state's focus includes the $5.5 billion Hyundai Motor Group plant near Savannah.
- Cal Wray said a $347 million, 125-job copper smelter announced by Aurubis AG in Augusta is another development driven by the growth in electric vehicles.
