# Goodyear Tire & Rubber

## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber is a brand-name company that has shed 71% of its value since January 2022.
- The author more than doubled their position in Goodyear between $6.28 and $6.50 per share in mid-March.
- Goodyear faces short-term challenges from skyrocketing crude oil prices due to the Iran war, which drive up the cost of petroleum-derived rubber.
- Goodyear's 2025 full-year operating results showed declining tire units sold across all three regions (Americas, EMEA, and Asia Pacific) alongside a shrinking operating margin.
- Goodyear blamed a sharp contraction in demand from its Commercial segment in the Americas for the sales decline.
- President Donald Trump's tariffs have negatively impacted Goodyear's results.
- High rubber prices, Iran war uncertainty, and tariffs are expected to weigh on Goodyear's results throughout 2026.
- Goodyear's operational turnaround began in November 2023 with the launch of the "Goodyear Forward" plan.
- The "Goodyear Forward" plan focuses on divestitures of non-core assets, operational cost-savings, and a shift toward higher-margin products.
- Goodyear sold its chemical business to an affiliate of Gemspring Capital Management for $650 million.
- Goodyear sold the Dunlop brand to Sumitomo Rubber Industries for $735 million.
- Goodyear sold its off-the-road tire operations to Yokohama Rubber Company for $905 million.
- Collectively, Goodyear banked around $2.3 billion from divestitures, which it primarily used to lower outstanding debt.
- Goodyear surpassed initial cost-cutting expectations laid out in November 2023.
- Supply chain and purchasing adjustments, paired with plant optimizations, helped Goodyear reach a $1.5 billion run rate cost-saving benefit by the end of 2025.
- Goodyear is leaning into its prime positioning in the higher-margin replacement tire market.
- Consumers are hanging onto their vehicles longer than ever before.
- Electric vehicles wear out tires faster than similar gas vehicles, putting Goodyear in a strong competitive position.
- The company's forward P/E was just 6 when shares were purchased on March 18-19.
- Goodyear stock is currently trading at a 37% discount to its book value.
- The author believes Goodyear's stock is positioned to double within the next five years.
