# Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-09-04

**Source:** [Deal: Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. to create 100 jobs in Newton County - The Covington News](https://www.covnews.com/news/business/deal-nisshinbo-automotive-manufacturing-inc-create-100-jobs-newton-county/)  ·  `page_058243`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. will create 100 jobs and invest $72 million in an expansion of its Covington facility.
- New jobs at the facility will include positions in production and administration.
- NAMI is a leading manufacturer and supplier of environmentally friendly brake pads.
- NAMI has maintained a presence in Newton County for more than two decades.
- Along with NAMI’s business development center in Sterling Heights, Michigan, the Newton County facility produces high-quality friction materials for automotive manufacturers.
- The Newton County facility develops environmentally friendly, copper-free brake pads.
- NAMI CEO Shigeru Matsuki announced the deal together with the Governor’s Office and the Newton County Industrial Development Authority.
- Select Newton Vice President of Economic Development David Bernd noted that more than $70 million in Foreign Direct Investment is being secured for 100 well-paying advanced manufacturing careers.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Project Manager for International Investment Joseph Huntemann represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with Select Newton.
- Japanese-affiliated companies currently operate more than 500 facilities in Georgia that employ approximately 30,000 Georgians.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that NAMI’s success in Georgia is a combination of strong international relationships and a pro-business environment.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Georgia provides the business climate, skilled workforce and thriving automotive sector necessary for manufacturers like NAMI to enjoy long-term growth.
- NAMI CEO Shigeru Matsuki expressed hope to grow in Covington for the next 20 years and beyond.
