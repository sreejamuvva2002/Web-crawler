## Facts

- Lt. Governor Casey Cagle’s Ninth Annual Business and Education Summit was a two-day event that began on Monday in Dalton, Ga and concluded on Tuesday in Calhoun, Ga.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. was named the 2016 Business Partner of the Year at the summit.
- Lt. Governor Cagle stated that voestalpine Automotive is a true leader in strategic public-private partnerships necessary to unleash students’ full potential and provides world-class apprenticeship opportunities for the next generation.
- Lt. Governor Cagle noted that voestalpine’s success is a clear indicator of what public school systems and private sector job creators can achieve when working together to create a dynamic and skilled workforce.
- Sen. Bruce Thompson stated that the organization is at the forefront of workforce development in Georgia and expressed gratitude for opportunities provided to Bartow County students.
- Sen. Bruce Thompson represents Senate District 14, which includes Bartow and Cherokee counties as well as portions of Cobb.
- The summit brought together business, industry, and education leaders to discuss abandoning the one-size-fits-all model in public education and building a workforce to maintain Georgia’s status as the No. 1 state to conduct business.
- School systems are implementing relevant career pathways with public-private partnerships to fill current gaps in Georgia’s workforce.
- voestalpine sought out a partner to develop a skilled workforce prior to locating in Bartow County.
- voestalpine is the first company in Georgia to organize an approved registered apprenticeship program exclusively for high school students, despite many U.S. Department of Labor Apprenticeship Programs being available for adults statewide.
- This apprenticeship program serves as a superior model that can be replicated with other industries in the Bartow community and throughout the state.
- The College and Career Academies initiative partners local school systems with technical colleges, other post-secondary institutions, and local businesses to develop high school and college-level programs training students for quality, in-demand jobs.
- There are currently over 37 College and Career Academies in Georgia due to Lt. Governor Cagle’s initiative.
- Approximately 20,000 students from different College and Career Academies across the state enjoy graduation rates in the high ninetieth percentile and 100 percent post-secondary job placement rates.
