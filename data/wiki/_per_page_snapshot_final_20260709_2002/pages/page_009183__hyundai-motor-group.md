# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group, Georgia Tech Sign MOU on Hydrogen Mobility Development](https://www.gatech.edu/news/2026/04/29/hyundai-motor-group-georgia-tech-sign-mou-hydrogen-mobility-development)  ·  `page_009183`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech announced an expansion of their collaboration to advance hydrogen-powered transportation, deepen applied research and education, and accelerate the use of zero-emissions vehicles in Georgia.
- The partnership brings hydrogen fuel cell vehicles and fueling infrastructure to the Georgia Tech campus.
- This initiative turns Georgia Tech into one of the nation’s most prominent campus-based examples for hydrogen mobility.
- Ken Ramírez, executive vice president and head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group, stated that combining the university’s research with Hyundai’s global experience creates a foundation for real-world solutions to drive the energy transition and inspire future mobility leaders.
- Ken Ramírez is a 1991 Georgia Tech graduate and a member of the Georgia Tech Advisory Board.
- Randy Parker, president and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, donated four NEXO fuel cell SUVs as part of the expanding relationship with Georgia Tech.
- The hydrogen-powered NEXO vehicles will expand the clean mobility footprint on campus while providing real-world experiences with zero-emissions transportation technology.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that expanding work with Hyundai advances hydrogen research, reduces emissions on campus, and strengthens Georgia’s role in the future of clean mobility.
- The partnership includes a hydrogen electrolyzer project to be installed at Georgia Tech’s North Avenue Research Area.
- The installed vehicles and infrastructure will serve as one of the most visible real-world test beds for hydrogen mobility in the U.S. and support campus operations and interdisciplinary research.
- Key research focus areas include engineering (exploring hydrogen-based systems and mobility solutions), sustainability (assessing environmental benefits of hydrogen technologies), energy systems (understanding integration of hydrogen fuel cells into current infrastructure), and public policy (evaluating regulatory and social implications of hydrogen adoption).
- The initiative uses Georgia Tech as a living laboratory for clean transportation technologies, connecting its research enterprise with campus operations.
- Faculty and students will study the real-world performance of hydrogen technology, infrastructure requirements for large-scale deployment, and environmental impacts of hydrogen energy systems.
- Insights from this initiative aim to inform and accelerate the widespread use of hydrogen technology in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The initiative supports Georgia Tech’s strategic plan goal of expanding the use of zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Beyond hydrogen energy, the partnership seeks to advance innovation in autonomous driving, electric vehicle (EV) batteries, charging infrastructure, materials science, and cybersecurity.
- Hyundai’s regional presence in Georgia includes the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and HTWO Logistics, a clean logistics partnership operating Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks near Savannah.
- The collaboration reinforces the link between education, research, and Hyundai’s long-term goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2045.
- Additional announcements regarding the partnership’s research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
