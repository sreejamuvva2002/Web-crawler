# Hyundai EV Lab at Effingham College & Career Academy

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-24

**Source:** [EV Lab Sponsored by HMGMA Opens at Effingham College & Career Academy - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2025/10/24/ecca-ev-lab/)  ·  `page_060741`

## Facts

- Effingham County School District partnered with Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America to unveil the new Hyundai EV Lab at Effingham College and Career Academy on Friday, October 24.
- The state-of-the-art lab provides students with hands-on learning opportunities in electric vehicle technology—an industry that continues to drive innovation and workforce growth in Georgia.
- Todd Wall, ECSD Career, Technical, and Agricultural Education Coordinator, stated the EV Lab is an invaluable addition to the engineering program because it provides true hands-on experience where students assemble, take apart, troubleshoot, and problem-solve in real time.
- HMGMA Chief Administrative Officer Brent Stubbs said Hyundai chose the region not just for its infrastructure or resources, but for its people and community, highlighting that the partnership with Effingham County Schools and ECCA reflects a spirit of working together to build the future and develop talent for mobility in Effingham County.
- The Hyundai EV Lab was designed to bridge the gap between classroom learning and real-world application and allows students to build, operate, and troubleshoot fully functioning electric vehicles.
- Aaron Parker, ECCA Engineering Teacher, noted that the lab offers a true, real-world, hands-on learning experience where students apply skills directly in the workforce, giving them a strong foundation for the future whether they continue at a technical college or university.
- ECSD Superintendent Dr. Yancy Ford commended the collaboration, noting how the lab represents the district’s commitment to connecting education and industry and providing real-world skills that connect directly to the future of Georgia’s workforce.
- Following the ceremony, students demonstrated their work by driving one of their completed vehicles out of the lab to the applause of guests, educators, and community partners.
- Effingham College and Career Academy is located in Rincon and opened in the fall of 2010 as one of Georgia’s first college and career academies.
- ECCA offers district high school students programming options including engineering and technology, STEM, and computer science.
- In 2025, ECCA began offering a manufacturing pathway and welcomed its first 22 students with plans to expand the program in 2026 and beyond.
