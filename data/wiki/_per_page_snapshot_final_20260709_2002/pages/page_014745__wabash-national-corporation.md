# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [Wabash National — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Wabash_National)  ·  `page_014745`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corporation is an American industrial manufacturing company specializing in the design and production of semi-trailers, truck bodies, liquid transportation systems, and other engineered products for the commercial transportation sector.
- Founded in 1985 and headquartered in Lafayette, Indiana, the company employs approximately 6,000 people and reported trailing twelve-month revenue of $1.64 billion as of September 30, 2025.
- Wabash National focuses on integrating physical and digital technologies to deliver end-to-end solutions that enhance supply chain efficiency in transportation, logistics, and infrastructure markets.
- The company holds nearly 300 global patents and pioneered advancements such as aerodynamic components and sustainable refrigeration systems.
- It became the first semi-trailer manufacturer in the region to achieve certifications in both quality management and environmental standards.
- In recent years, Wabash National has expanded its offerings to include electric vehicle solutions and digital platforms for fleet management, aligning with industry shifts toward sustainability and connectivity.
- As of 2025, the company emphasizes lightweight composite technologies that support electric vehicle (EV) solutions by reducing trailer and body weight to extend range and efficiency for battery-electric trucks in commercial fleets.
- In September 2025, Wabash National opened a new parts and service center in Atlanta, Georgia, along with expanding its Fleetco dealer network in the Southeast to strengthen customer access and support.
- DuraPlate composite panels incorporate 15-30% post-consumer resin and are fully recyclable at the end of their lifecycle, minimizing waste in the transportation sector.
- EcoNex Technology is a proprietary molded composite material designed for improved thermal efficiency, reduced weight, and lower emissions in refrigerated trailers.
- In August 2022, the company committed $20 million to upgrade its 53-acre Little Falls, Minnesota facility, adding over 200 jobs by the end of 2023 and establishing it as the primary hub for EcoNex Technology.
- During the early 2020s, Wabash National prioritized domestic sourcing, achieving 95% U.S.-based materials to mitigate volatility from supply chain disruptions and material shortages.
- In February 2025, Wabash acquired TrailerHawk.ai, a provider of advanced cargo security technology, to integrate AI-driven monitoring into its trailers-as-a-service offerings and enhance supply chain security.
- In April 2025, the company partnered with Echo Global Logistics to expand its drop trailer business, improving fleet flexibility for logistics customers.
- Wabash National's dry freight vans feature patented DuraPlate composite panels that reduce overall trailer weight by up to 300 pounds compared to traditional steel panels and offer a service life of 15-20+ years with minimal repairs.
- In December 2024, Wabash announced the 2026 model year DuraPlate Dry Van, incorporating Cell Core technology for enhanced durability and the same 300-pound weight reduction.
- Ongoing R&D explores bio-based resins and energy-harvesting integrations, including collaborations with academic institutions to test composites that recapture vibrational energy for auxiliary power.
- The company holds numerous U.S. patents covering composite floor structures, joint configurations, punching methods for panels, reinforced preforms, and honeycomb sandwich composites.
- Brent L. Yeagy serves as President and Chief Executive Officer of Wabash National as of 2025, having held the position since June 2018.
- In 2025, the company reported Q3 net sales of $382 million (a 17.8% decline year-over-year) and net income of $40 million for the quarter, while revising full-year revenue guidance to $1.5 billion.
- Wabash National's stock (NYSE: WNC) declined over 60% in the year leading to late 2025, closing around $7.80 per share in November 2025.
- The company differentiates itself through proprietary DuraPlate composite panels and lightweight technologies that align with the growing adoption of EVs in transportation and logistics.
