## Facts

- Georgia declared Monday as Hyundai Day at the state Capitol.
- Hyundai is accelerating the timeline for its Bryan County plant.
- HMGMA revealed the official start of production will be in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- The grand opening celebration is targeted for the first quarter of 2025.
- Production was previously announced to begin in the first quarter of 2025.
- José Muñoz, president and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated Georgia is like a second home to Hyundai.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs.
- Hyundai’s investments will bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA.
- HMGMA will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County.
- The Bartow County battery plant will employ 3,500 workers.
- A Center for Automotive Research study expects Hyundai’s investments in Georgia to create nearly 40,000 jobs.
- These investments are expected to create $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated Hyundai Motor Group has been an incredible partner to the state for decades and knew the historic benefits a project like this would bring when talks began.
- The HMGMA project was announced in May 2022.
- Since the initial announcement, Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- This brings the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lt. Gov. Burt Jones stated commitment from stakeholders enabled Hyundai's positive impact and job creation.
- These initiatives support Georgia's position as the number one state in which to do business.
- Partnerships foster a thriving environment to grow Georgia's businesses and workforce.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated Hyundai's investment and expansion is a testament to hard work, dedication, and sound policy from Governor Kemp and the General Assembly.
- The Metaplant will have a generational impact on economic development.
- It will act as a force multiplier for additional investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- Hyundai's announcement to start vehicle production in Q4 of this year shows the project's transformational impact for the state.
- The Metaplant site is nearly 3,000 acres.
- Four building exteriors are complete at the site.
- Outfitting for internal operations is underway.
- "Meta Pros" are HMGMA’s full-time employees.
- Meta Pros are being hired at a rapid pace for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other employment roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75% of that workforce are from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison stated Hyundai's investment in the Savannah region is tremendous.
- Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits.
- Hyundai formed key partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Hyundai committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in HMGMA.
- Hyundai will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75% coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production start, electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100% clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant.
- The park will include trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and other amenities for employee use.
