# Hyundai Steel Company

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Steel - LED | Louisiana Economic Development](https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/hyundai-steel)  ·  `page_002771`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $5.8 billion in a new manufacturing facility in Louisiana.
- This marks Hyundai’s inaugural North American steel facility.
- The project will establish a fully integrated, made-in-America supply chain to support its automotive manufacturing operations across the United States.
- The ultra-low carbon steel production mill will be located in Donaldsonville, Louisiana.
- The site anchors the RiverPlex MegaPark on the Westbank of the Mississippi River in Ascension Parish.
- RiverPlex covers approximately 17,000 acres and is the largest undeveloped tract along the deep-water Mississippi River.
- Hyundai Steel Company (HSC), a member of Hyundai Motor Group, is expected to create more than 1,300 direct new jobs.
- The average salary for these direct jobs will be $95,000.
- Louisiana Economic Development estimates the project will result in about 4,100 indirect new jobs.
- The total potential new jobs in the Capital Region will be 5,400.
- The state-of-the-art electric arc furnace (EAF) steel mill is expected to produce 70% lower emissions than traditional blast furnaces.
- The mill is expected to produce 2.7 million metric tons of steel annually.
- Hyundai Steel Company is partnering with the Port of South Louisiana to build a deep water dock on the Westbank of Ascension Parish to accommodate steel and materials shipments.
- River Parishes Community College and the Louisiana Community and Technical College System (LCTCS) will develop a new local workforce training center to support the project’s workforce needs.
- The training center will be equipped with hands-on skills coaches from LED’s FastStart program and modern learning spaces to provide job-specific instruction and administrative assistance.
- Construction is anticipated to begin in the third quarter of 2026.
- Governor Jeff Landry and Louisiana Economic Development secured the $5.8 billion project.
