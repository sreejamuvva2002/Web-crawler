# Dinex Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Dinex Group to bring 250 jobs to Dublin, Georgia | GaPundit](https://gapundit.com/dinex-group-to-bring-250-jobs-to-dublin-georgia/)  ·  `page_019381`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that the Denmark-based Dinex Group plans to open a manufacturing plant in Dublin-Laurens County.
- The automotive emissions control and exhaust manufacturer will create 250 jobs.
- Dinex Group will invest $15 million in this operation.
- Dinex Group is a leading global manufacturer and distributor of innovatively engineered exhaust and emission control products and solutions for the heavy-duty diesel engine and vehicle industry.
- The Dublin facility will be Dinex's first in the United States.
- The facility will produce exhaust and emission systems for off- and on-road markets for diesel engines in North America.
- Dinex's customers include Caterpillar, Carrier, John Deere, Case New Holland, and Volvo among several others.
- Dinex will develop a full range of products for the truck and bus aftermarket (AEM) in North America.
- Following an extensive multi-state search including more than 50 site visits, Dinex selected Dublin due to logistics, supplier access, operating costs, workforce, and quality of life.
- By the end of September 2012, Dinex will have capabilities to supply quality products 'Made in the USA' to customers across the U.S.
- Dinex will collaborate with Quick Start, Georgia’s workforce training program, to train its workforce on advanced manufacturing applications.
- Quick Start provides tailored and industry-specific training resources free of charge to qualified new, expanding, and existing companies creating new jobs in Georgia.
- The Dinex manufacturing plant will be located in a 60,000-square-foot facility in Dublin.
- The facility expects to expand by more than double within the next five years.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that automotive suppliers are leading the way for the manufacturing rebound in Georgia and credited companies like Dinex.
- Gov. Nathan Deal noted Georgia is uniquely equipped to help manufacturers compete globally.
- Dinex Vice President and CFO Kim Toft Jensen cited Georgia’s pro-business community meeting requirements for logistics, supplier access, operating costs, workforce, and quality of life.
- Dinex President and CEO Torben Dinesen expressed confidence in continued support from the state of Georgia and Laurens County.
- Attracting this manufacturing plant highlights the need for skilled labor in Dublin, Georgia.
- The development underscores the importance of continued development of the Port of Savannah and Georgia’s privately-owned freight railroad network.
- VW division Audi is considering building a North American plant.
- Mercedes-Benz is considering building a North American plant.
- Porsche Cars North America is headquartered in Sandy Springs.
- Porsche will build a new headquarters campus near the airport.
