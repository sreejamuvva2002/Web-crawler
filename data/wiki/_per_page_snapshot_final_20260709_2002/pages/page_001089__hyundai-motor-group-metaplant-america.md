# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-27

**Source:** [Production of Electric Vehicles in Georgia to Begin in First Half of 2025, Officials Say • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/georgia-news/production-of-electric-vehicles-in-georgia-to-begin-in-first-half-of-2025-officials-say/)  ·  `page_001089`

## Facts

- Groundbreaking celebrations were held on a Tuesday for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant (HMGMA).
- Production at the dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery plant facility is expected to begin in the first half of 2025.
- Kia established a production facility in West Point, Georgia, in 2006.
- The Kia West Point facility accounts for nearly 40 percent of all Kia sales in the U.S.
- Kia was the first company to open an auto assembly plant in Georgia.
- Sean Yoon is the president and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America.
- Kia plans to produce EVs in the U.S., building on its success and growth in Georgia.
- Together with its suppliers, Kia Georgia has helped create more than 14,000 jobs.
- The HMGMA project was announced five months prior to the article's publication date.
- HMGMA is projected to create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV batteries and other EV components in the U.S. market through this project.
- Kia Georgia manufactures the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage SUVs as well as the K5 midsize sedan.
- More than 90 percent of the vehicles assembled at Kia Georgia are destined for dealerships across America.
- The Kia West Point factory has provided over $10 million in community support to Georgia since 2009.
- Over $6 million of that support has funded educational programs, including the THINC College & Career Academy in LaGrange and SAE’s “A World in Motion.”
