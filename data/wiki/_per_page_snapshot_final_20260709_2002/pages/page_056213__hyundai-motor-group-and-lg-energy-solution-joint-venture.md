# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy to build $4.3 billion electric vehicle battery factory in the United States - ExBulletin](https://exbulletin.com/politics/2172226/)  ·  `page_056213`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced they would build a $4.3 billion electric vehicle battery plant in the United States.
- South Korea’s Hyundai Motor Group is the world’s third-largest automaker in terms of vehicle sales.
- LG Energy Solution (LGES), headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, is ranked second in the world based on battery sales.
- LGES and Hyundai Motor Group will each own 50% of the joint venture.
- Hyundai Motor Group houses Hyundai Motor, Kia, and auto parts maker Hyundai Mobis Co Ltd.
- LGES supplies car manufacturers such as Tesla Inc and General Motors Co.
- LGES CEO Youngsoo Kwon stated: “Two strong leaders in the automotive and battery industries have partnered, and together we are poised to drive America’s electric vehicle transition.”
- In October 2022, Hyundai opened its $5.4 billion electric vehicle and EV battery plant in Bryan County, 30 minutes west of Savannah, Georgia.
- The company planned to begin commercial production at the new plant by the first half of 2025 with an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Garrison Douglas, spokesman for Republican Gov. Brian Kemp, said the 3,000-job battery plant would be among 8,100 overall jobs.
- The $4.3 billion investment will be part of the previously announced total of $5.4 billion.
- The Hyundai/LG plant is expected to supply batteries for 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- This battery capacity matches the planned initial production of the nearby vehicle assembly plant.
- Hyundai said the Georgia plant could later expand to build 500,000 vehicles a year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “This is exactly what we envisioned when Georgia landed the Hyundai Metaplant in May of last year, and this project is the latest step on Georgia’s path to becoming the nation’s electric vehicle capital.”
- Under the Biden Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), electric vehicle buyers can receive a tax credit of up to $7,500 if the vehicle meets certain criteria.
- To qualify for the IRA tax credit, the vehicle must be assembled in North America and a certain percentage of its battery parts and minerals must be sourced from North America or a US free trade partner.
- Currently, no Hyundai or Kia vehicle is eligible for the IRA tax credit.
- Democratic U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff of Georgia stated: “The IRA’s manufacturing incentives continue to bring jobs and investment to Georgia.”
- Senator Ossoff's goal remains to make Georgia the world leader in peak energy generation.
- Hyundai Motor finalized a $5 billion electric vehicle battery joint venture in Cartersville, Georgia, with SK On in April.
- SK On is a battery unit of SK Innovation Co Ltd.
- This marks Hyundai’s second battery factory.
- Georgia state and local governments have pledged US$1.8 billion in tax relief and other incentives for the project.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, noted that this is the largest subsidy package a US state has ever promised an auto plant.
