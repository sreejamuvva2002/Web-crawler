# Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2014-08-21

**Source:** [Yamaha’s SxS business expecting major growth | Powersports Business](https://powersportsbusiness.com/uncategorized/2014/08/21/yamahas-sxs-business-expecting-major-growth/)  ·  `page_015631`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. plant in Newnan, Ga., is now home to nearly 100 percent of production for both side-by-side and ATV segments.
- A second plant in Newnan, Ga., meant to house side-by-side production specifically, is fully operational.
- The initial Newnan plant serves as the home for ATV production.
- Yamaha Motor Corp., U.S.A. is moving its Cypress, Calif.-based ATV/side-by-side research and development and marketing personnel from California to the Yamaha corporate offices in Kennesaw, Ga.
- The Atlanta-area Kennesaw facility also houses Yamaha’s WaterCraft and Marine divisions.
- Engines for vehicles in both the side-by-side and ATV segments are still manufactured in Japan.
- Yamaha plans to grow its side-by-side business almost tenfold over the next three years.
- Mike Martinez, vice president of Recreation Vehicle Operations at Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A., stated that dealers need to prepare for an expanding business for side-by-side vehicles that are two to three times larger than what they might currently sell.
- Part of the staffing transfer to Georgia is working closer with the accessory group and the factory to support accessory growth.
- Accessories are considered a huge part of the side-by-side puzzle, critical to Yamaha's future success and dealer profitability.
- The Viking VI 3-seat side-by-side has an MSRP of $12,799 for the standard version and $13,799 for EPS models.
- New accessories for the Viking VI include a molded sun top, soft sun top, second battery kit, camo polyester seat cover set, heated seat cover, chain saw mount, and bow case mount.
- Mike Martinez revealed the growth plans during a Viking VI press event at Lost Pines Resort in Austin, Texas.
- 75 dealers who won a Viking sales contest attended the event to get an early look at the Viking VI.
- Approximately 40 Yamaha staffers from Kennesaw, Cypress, and Japan attended the event alongside U.S. regional managers and regional marketing managers.
