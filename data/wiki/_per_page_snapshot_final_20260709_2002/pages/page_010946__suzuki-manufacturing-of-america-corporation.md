# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-24

**Source:** [Suzuki Celebrates 20 Years Of ATV Manufacturing In America - Roadracing World Magazine | Motorcycle Riding, Racing & Tech News](https://www.roadracingworld.com/news/suzuki-celebrates-20-years-of-atv-manufacturing-in-america/)  ·  `page_010946`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor USA, LLC is celebrating 20 years of manufacturing KingQuad ATVs at the SMAC facility in Rome, GA.
- KingQuad ATVs first rolled off the assembly line in Rome, Georgia in May of 2002.
- KingQuad ATVs have continued to be built exclusively in the United States since production began in 2002.
- Suzuki has been building award-winning KingQuad ATVs at the SMAC facility since 2002.
- The SMAC facility is a multi-million dollar, 35-acre manufacturing site.
- The SMAC facility is located in the center of the biggest ATV market in the world.
- The facility's location allows access to real-world rider feedback for product refinement and timely delivery of KingQuad ATVs to Suzuki dealers worldwide.
- Suzuki founded its engineering commitment in 1909.
- In the early 1980s, Suzuki led the industry with the creation of the first ever four-wheel ATV in 1983.
- Kerry Graeber serves as Vice President and Division Manager for MC/ATV Sales and Marketing at Suzuki.
- Kerry Graeber stated that building KingQuad ATVs in America for the past 20 years is an extremely important milestone and a source of pride for Suzuki.
- The performance, toughness, and durability of Suzuki ATVs represent the commitment, hard work, and dedication of the workers who build them at the Rome, Georgia facility, according to Graeber.
- The SMAC facility is a key factor in the continued success of Suzuki’s global ATV business.
- KingQuad ATVs manufactured at the Rome, GA facility are shipped to U.S. Suzuki dealers and nearly 30 countries worldwide.
- KingQuad ATVs are built in the USA using both U.S. and imported parts.
