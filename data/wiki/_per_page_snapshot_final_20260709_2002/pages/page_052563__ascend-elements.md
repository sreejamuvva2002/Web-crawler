# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-23

**Source:** [INTERVIEW WITH ASCEND ELEMENTS – FineEngineering Magazine](https://fineeng.eu/interview-with-ascend-elements/)  ·  `page_052563`

## Facts

- Based in Westborough, Mass., USA, Ascend Elements is a leading provider of sustainable, closed-loop battery material solutions.
- The company recycles used lithium-ion batteries and Gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- Ascend Elements manufactures sustainable cathode precursor (pCAM) and cathode active material (CAM) made from recycled lithium-ion batteries.
- Its Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis technology was developed at Worcester Polytechnic Institute.
- The Hydro-to-Cathode™ process recovers up to 98% of the critical battery materials in spent Li-ion batteries and scrap.
- The process produces new cathode material at a lower cost and with up to 90% lower greenhouse gas emissions than similar cathode made with newly mined metals.
- The technology can customize and engineer cathode active material composition, particle size and distribution, crystallinity, morphology, and porosity to specific customer requirements.
- Products include sustainable pCAM and CAM made to customer specifications.
- High-Nickel Cathode products include NMC 811, NMC 622, NMC 532, and NCMA.
- Raw materials produced include recycled lithium carbonate, recycled nickel sulfate, and black mass.
- Recent federal legislation – the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and the Inflation Reduction Act – are further increasing demand for recycled battery materials.
- Tax credits detailed in the Inflation Reduction Act require use of domestic battery materials and recycled content in new EV batteries.
- Founded in 2015, Ascend Elements recently completed its Series C funding round of $300 million (equity and debt).
- In October 2022, Ascend Elements received two grants from the U.S. Department of Energy totaling $480 million.
- The growth of the lithium-ion battery recycling industry is tied to the growth of the electric vehicle market and grid-scale energy storage market.
- Every new lithium-ion battery manufacturing facility will need a battery recycling facility of similar size.
- The technology plays an important role in the United States’ domestic battery manufacturing and recycling infrastructure plans, as described in the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and Pres. Biden’s recent invocation of the Defense Production Act (DPA).
- Ascend Elements received $480 million from the U.S. Department of Energy to commercialize the Hydro-to-Cathode technology at a one-of-a-kind manufacturing facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- Construction is ongoing for Apex 1, an Ascend Elements battery recycling and sustainable cathode manufacturing facility in Hopkinsville, KY.
- Ascend Elements will celebrate the grand opening of its Base 1 battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia, USA in 2023.
- The company is exploring opportunities to expand into Europe and may announce new projects in 2023.
- Co-founders of Ascend Elements are commercializing technology that transforms used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap into sustainable cathode active material for EV batteries.
- Independent study by the United States Battery Consortium (USABC) found Ascend Elements' sustainable cathode active material performs as well or better than materials made from newly mined metals.
- The patented Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology can produce battery-ready cathode active materials with up to 93% lower greenhouse gas emissions compared to traditional recycling methods.
- The technology enables recycling of any type of lithium-ion battery, including cell phone, laptop, and EV batteries, regardless of chemistry.
- In addition to lithium, nickel, cobalt and graphite, the recycling processes recover plastics, aluminum and copper.
- The company's goal is to recover every last bit of recyclable material and keep 100% of batteries out of landfills.
