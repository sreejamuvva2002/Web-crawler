# Novelis Inc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-05-13

**Source:** [Aleris Automotive Sheet Business: US proposes Novelis to divest automotive body sheet biz in North America, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/us-proposes-novelis-to-divest-automotive-body-sheet-biz-in-north-america/75722763)  ·  `page_047326`

## Facts

- Novelis is a Canadian corporation headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia.
- Novelis offers flat-rolled aluminium products for automotive, beverage can, and specialty segments.
- The US Department of Justice proposed that a federal court order Hindalco-owned Novelis to divest Aleris Corporation's entire aluminum automotive body sheet operations in North America.
- Novelis is a wholly-owned subsidiary of India's Hindalco Industries.
- Novelis acquired Aleris for USD 2.8 billion last month.
- As per a mutually reached settlement, Novelis is required to divest the automotive body sheet business in North America, which is just one plant in Lewisport.
- Assistant Attorney General Makan Delrahim of the Justice Department's Antitrust Division stated that the proposed divestiture preserves competition in the market for aluminum automotive body sheet and protects automakers and American consumers.
- Prior to its acquisition by Novelis, Aleris was a Delaware corporation headquartered in Cleveland, Ohio.
- Aleris offers flat-rolled aluminum products to the automotive, aerospace, building, and construction industries among others.
- In 2018, Aleris' revenues were approximately USD 3.4 billion.
- The proposed final judgment follows the US' March 9 arbitration win.
- Prior to filing its civil antitrust lawsuit to block the merger, the Department's Antitrust Division reached an agreement with Novelis and Aleris to refer the matter to binding arbitration if they were unable to resolve the US' competitive concern.
- Under the arbitration terms, Novelis agreed to divest Aleris' aluminum automotive body sheet operations in North America if the US prevailed in the arbitration.
- The arbitrator ruled in favour of the United States, holding that aluminum automotive body sheet constitutes a relevant antitrust product market.
- Hindalco cemented its position as the world's largest producer of value-added aluminium products with the completion of the USD 2.8 billion acquisition of Aleris by Novelis Inc last month.
- The acquisition positions Hindalco as one of the world's largest aluminium companies, with a global footprint spanning 49 manufacturing facilities in North America, Europe and Asia.
