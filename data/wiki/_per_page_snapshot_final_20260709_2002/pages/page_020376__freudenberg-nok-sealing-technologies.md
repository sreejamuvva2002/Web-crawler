# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK’s community partnerships earn state economic development accolades | White County News, Cleveland, GA](https://www.whitecountynews.net/local/freudenberg-noks-community-partnerships-earn-state-economic-development-accolades)  ·  `page_020376`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies’ commitment to community has been honored in the 2020 Georgia Emerging Automotive Recognition (GEAR) Awards.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development presented the Citizenship Award to the Cleveland plant during a videoconference event on Oct. 14.
- Officials pointed to multiple initiatives Freudenberg-NOK’s culture team has put in place as part of the recognition.
- Freudenberg-NOK engineers assisted students at White County Middle School’s Rocket Day event.
- The company donated funds for a school engineering program in White County.
- Freudenberg-NOK teamed up with White County 4-H to plant trees.
- The company supported the Turkey in a Box Thanksgiving meal program.
- Freudenberg-NOK provided gear to aid first responders during the early stage of the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Scott McMurray, deputy commissioner for global commerce at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated: “It really is evident through the work of your culture teams how much you value your employees, but also how much emphasis you placed on serving the local communities.”
- Several Freudenberg-NOK representatives were on hand for the virtual presentation and credited company-wide support for giving back.
- Plant Manager Wyman Hare said: “We have a great group of employees here that put a focus on other people outside of our actual company, but also out in our community.”
- Building local partnerships for the betterment of others follows guiding principles of the Freudenberg Group, with financial support from the Freudenberg family, according to Rhonda McLean.
- Rhonda McLean, human resources manager for Transmission Driveline Lead Center Oil Seals, Americas, said: “It really is a team effort. It comes from our leadership. It comes from our employees. The plant’s been here 31 years, and they’ve always been so philanthropic.”
- McLean thanked the White County Chamber of Commerce for nominating the company for the award.
- Beth Truelove, president of the White County Chamber of Commerce, told the White County News: “Freudenberg is a fantastic corporate citizen in White County. It was such a pleasure to see them recognized for all of their contributions to our community.”
- Truelove added: “From their dedication to the White County School System as they develop a skilled workforce, to the quality of life that they enhance through support and beautification of our towns, they are the shining example of quality manufacturer that has found a home in White County.”
- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies was one of five companies receiving GEAR Awards.
- Nifco KTW America in Toccoa received Supplier of the Year for employing 200-plus people.
- Sangsin Technology America in McDonough received Supplier of the Year for employing 199 or fewer people.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation in Gainesville was named Transportation Equipment Manufacturer of the Year.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems in Peachtree City received the Automotive Innovation Award.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated: “It’s a pleasure to celebrate each of our 2020 Georgia GEAR Award winners.”
- Wilson noted: “The state’s automotive and mobility industries are major drivers of jobs and innovation, and I congratulate each of our winners on their resilience and thank them for their continued commitment to creating jobs and opportunities for Georgians.”
