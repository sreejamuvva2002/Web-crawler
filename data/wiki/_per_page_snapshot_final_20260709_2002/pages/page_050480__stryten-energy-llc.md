# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Expands U.S. Battery Manufacturing by 10 GW - Battery-Tech Network](https://battery-tech.net/stryten-energy-expands-u-s-battery-manufacturing-by-10-gw/)  ·  `page_050480`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC announced plans to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity by an additional 10 gigawatts, bringing the total to 24 gigawatts.
- The expansion aims to enhance the supply of batteries for military, long-duration energy storage, transportation, and material handling applications.
- The effort involves increasing production across Stryten Energy’s 11 manufacturing and battery component plants in the United States.
- Over 2,500 employees currently work at these facilities.
- The facilities produce batteries used by military and government entities, data centers, grid storage systems, automotive industries, and material handling operations.
- The planned investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits.
- Mike Judd serves as President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy.
- Mike Judd stated that Stryten Energy is committed to growing its domestic energy storage capacity to serve the needs of customers and partners in the United States.
- Mike Judd emphasized that American energy security must rely upon domestic companies manufacturing batteries that keep supply chains running and critical infrastructure supplied with backup power.
- Mike Judd noted that investments to significantly increase gigawatt capacity across their footprint will help support growing energy demand and achieve the nation’s goal of true energy resilience.
- The company is scaling up production of various battery chemistries, including advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries.
- Stryten Energy emphasizes energy security, which entails continuous access to reliable power sources.
- The expansion includes facilities in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas.
- These facilities are expected to create hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs and provide economic benefits to local communities.
- Stryten Energy operates a vertically integrated supply chain with plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin.
- The expansion plan includes increasing capacity for processing recycled plastic from spent batteries.
- Recycled material will be used to produce new battery components for automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- The use of recycled materials contributes to a sustainable circular economy within the U.S. battery industry.
- Stryten Energy recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia to support military and industrial battery needs using its proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Dragonfly Energy of Reno, Nevada, to introduce advanced lithium batteries to automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
- Stryten Energy launched Storion Energy LLC, a subsidiary focused on eliminating barriers for battery manufacturers to source competitively priced electrolytes for vanadium redox flow batteries used in long-duration energy storage applications in the U.S.
