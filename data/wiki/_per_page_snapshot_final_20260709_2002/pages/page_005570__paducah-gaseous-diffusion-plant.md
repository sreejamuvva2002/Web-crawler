# Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Paducah Site | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/pppo/paducah-site)  ·  `page_005570`

## Facts

- The Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant (PGDP) was constructed in 1952 to produce enriched uranium, initially for the nation’s nuclear weapons program and later for nuclear fuel for commercial power plants.
- The plant is owned by the Department of Energy (DOE), which oversees environmental cleanup activities at the site, including environmental remediation, waste management, depleted uranium conversion, and decontamination and decommissioning.
- Commercial enrichment was conducted under lease from 1993 until 2013 when operations ceased and the gaseous diffusion facilities were returned to the DOE Environmental Management (EM) program.
- EM has conducted extensive cleanup activities at the site since the late 1980s and is currently deactivating the returned plant facilities while continuing the aggressive remediation program being managed by its Portsmouth/Paducah Project Office.
- On June 23, 2026, members of the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Environmental Management workforce discussed job opportunities with area teachers and administrators during a tour of the Paducah Site.
- On June 2, 2026, incoming next-generation workforce members at the Paducah Site will undergo new training by longtime employees on the site’s past uranium enrichment operations.
- On May 26, 2026, two universities in Ohio and Kentucky began collaborating to build STEM interest in local high school students and inspire them to pursue careers at the DOE’s Portsmouth and Paducah sites.
- On May 19, 2026, two educators in communities near the DOE’s Portsmouth, Ohio, and Paducah, Kentucky, locations started maximizing resources to cultivate next-generation talent to advance the nation’s energy sector and future redevelopment at the DOE sites.
