## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co is an American multinational tire manufacturing company headquartered in Ohio, the United States.
- Goodyear produces rubber-related chemicals and tires for developing, manufacturing, marketing and distributing automobiles, commercial and light trucks, motorcycles, SUVs, race cars, aeroplanes, farm equipment, heavy earth-moving machinery and aerospace crafts.
- Goodyear has approximately 62,000 employees and 55 facilities in 23 countries globally, including commercial truck service and tire retreading centres.
- Goodyear operates through an extensive portfolio of brands, consisting of Dunlop, Goodyear, Fulda, Falken, Kelly, Mastercraft, Roadmaster, Cooper and Mickey Thompson.
- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co stock (GT.xnas) is traded on the Nasdaq and listed as a Standard and Poor’s 400 (S&P 400) component.
- The company had a $6.62B (USD) market cap as of November 2021 and $12.3B (USD) in 2020 yearly revenue, representing a year-per-year drop of 16.4%.
- On 9 November 2021, Goodyear partnered with the Stark Area Regional Transit Authority of Ohio (SARTA).
- The partnership announced the testing of intelligent tire sensors and prototype tires on SARTA’s fleet of diesel and zero-emission hydrogen fuel cell-powered (HFC) buses and vehicles.
