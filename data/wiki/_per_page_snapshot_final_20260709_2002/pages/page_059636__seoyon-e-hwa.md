# Seoyon E-HWA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier plans $76M Georgia plant, hiring 500 - The Christian Index](https://christianindex.org/stories/hyundai-supplier-plans-76m-georgia-plant-hiring-500,44194)  ·  `page_059636`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA announced it will invest $76 million in a new plant near Savannah to manufacture parts for Hyundai's upcoming plant in nearby Ellabell.
- The company plans to hire more than 500 workers for the new facility.
- The new plant will produce interior and exterior parts, including door trim, headlining, and tailgate trim.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans to begin production at the Savannah plant in October 2024.
- The company already operates a plant with 630 employees in LaGrange, located near Hyundai's plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and Kia's plant in West Point.
- Seoyon E-HWA operates 32 manufacturing units worldwide.
- Seoyon E-HWA is the fourth Hyundai supplier to announce plans for a southeast Georgia plant since Hyundai announced a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in May.
- Hyundai's upcoming plant could grow to employ 8,100 workers and is supposed to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Hyundai Mobis previously announced plans for a $926 million powertrain plant in Richmond Hill that will employ 1,500 people.
- Joon Georgia announced plans for a $317 million plant near Statesboro that will employ 630 people.
- Ecoplastic announced plans for a $205 million plant employing 456 workers in Register.
- Governor Brian Kemp has proclaimed his goal for his second term is to make Georgia the leading state for electric-powered vehicles.
- Trip Tollison, president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, stated the new jobs would be “well paying” but did not disclose specific wage details.
- The company already imports parts through Savannah's port to supply its existing LaGrange plant.
- The state will pay to train workers for the new facility, though the total incentive package from state and local governments was not immediately clear at the time of reporting.
- Seoyon E-HWA could qualify for more than $3.1 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $1,250 per job over five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 annually.
