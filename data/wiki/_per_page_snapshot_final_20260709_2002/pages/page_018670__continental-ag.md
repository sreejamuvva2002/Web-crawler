## Facts

- Continental AG improved its adjusted EBIT margin by 260 basis points year over year, demonstrating resilience in a challenging market.
- The company successfully concluded most of its pricing negotiations in the automotive sector, contributing to improved financial performance.
- Continental AG reduced headcount by over 3,000 employees and is targeting EUR200 million in cost savings for 2024.
- The tires segment showed strong performance with a 3.5% organic sales growth, driven by solid passenger vehicle replacement market sales.
- The company is making strides in R&D efficiency, aiming for a high single-digit R&D to sales percentage by 2027, with a 30 basis point reduction achieved in Q3 2024.
- Continental AG faces persistent weakness in industrial markets, particularly affecting its ContiTech segment.
- The company adjusted its guidance for ContiTech, reducing the sales outlook by EUR400 million and the EBIT margin by 70 basis points.
- The automotive sector continues to be burdened by weak global production volumes and high customer inventory levels.
- Continental AG is dealing with tax investigations by Italian authorities, leading to provisions in the low to mid double-digit million EUR range.
- The company anticipates further raw material and labor cost headwinds in Q4 2024 and into 2025, which could impact profitability.
- 90% of pricing negotiations have been successfully closed with a significant portion being sustainable.
- Uncertainties remain, and further rounds of pricing negotiations will be needed in 2025.
- Continental AG achieved EUR100 million in cost savings year-to-date and is targeting EUR200 million by the end of 2024.
- The EUR400 million savings target is focused on administrative functions, with additional savings expected in 2025.
- A portion of Continental's US tire sales is covered by European production, with additional sourcing from South America.
- The US cannot supply itself exclusively via the domestic market due to insufficient capacity.
- Regarding a braking issue with BMW, vehicles can be delivered with the brakes and the company is clarifying the situation with BMW.
- Continental has reviewed and adjusted the provisions built up in previous quarters related to the BMW braking issue.
- Strong replacement tire business in Europe and APAC is expected to continue, with a focus on increasing premium business in the ultra-high-performance segment.
