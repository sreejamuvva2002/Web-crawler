# Aspen Aerogels (NYSE: ASPN)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [More about Gov. Kemp: Aspen Aerogels to Open Advanced Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/563413541/more-about-gov-kemp-aspen-aerogels-to-open-advanced-manufacturing-facility-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_052647`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels plans to construct an advanced manufacturing facility at Bulloch County’s Southern Gateway Commerce Park near Statesboro, Georgia.
- The company intends to invest a minimum of $325 million in the project.
- Hiring for personnel is expected to begin in the second half of 2022 across skilled manufacturing operators, advanced process engineers, quality and maintenance technicians, and professional support roles.
- The new facility will span 500,000 square feet on a 90-acre parcel in southeast Georgia.
- Aspen plans to create more than 250 advanced manufacturing jobs at the site.
- Production is expected to begin in late 2023.
- When combined with Aspen’s original East Providence, Rhode Island plant, the Georgia facility is designed to triple Aspen’s nameplate aerogel capacity.
- The project supports Aspen’s thermal barrier expansion plans in the fast-growing electric vehicle market.
- Aspen aims to capitalize on increased demand for its Aerogel Technology Platform™ as EV OEMs adopt its PyroThin® aerogel thermal barrier solutions.
- PyroThin materials provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties specifically for EV battery systems.
- The company’s carbon aerogel program seeks to increase the performance of lithium-ion battery cells to help EV manufacturers extend driving range and reduce vehicle costs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project, highlighting Aspen's confidence in Georgia and its growing electric mobility sector.
- Don Young, President and CEO of Aspen, cited efficient ports, a strong available workforce, and secure, low-cost utilities and critical raw materials as key site advantages.
- Billy Allen, Vice Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed the company and emphasized the region's commitment to innovation in the electric mobility ecosystem.
- Assistant Director Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed confidence that Georgia employees will collaborate with Aspen’s network of scientists and engineers to address mobility challenges.
- Southern Gateway Commerce Park holds Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification from the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The region’s education resources, including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College, are expected to provide critical workforce support for Aspen.
- Aspen Aerogels is headquartered in Northborough, Massachusetts, and currently manufactures products at its East Providence, Rhode Island facilities.
