# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow County, Georgia, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A 50/50 joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and battery maker SK On to build a $5 billion EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County, Georgia that will supply battery cells for Hyundai, Kia and Genesis vehicles.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant will produce battery cells to support production of 300,000 all-electric Hyundai, Kia and Genesis vehicles annually in the United States.

## Key facts

- $5 billion joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to build a battery plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- Each partner will hold a 50% equity stake in the investment.
- The facility is expected to produce enough cells to support 300,000 all-electric Hyundai, Kia and Genesis vehicles annually by the second half of 2025.
- Location is strategically within a couple hundred miles of Hyundai’s U.S. operations it will supply (HMMA, Kia Georgia, Metaplant America).
- "We expect the stable supply of EV batteries from SK On will also enable us to contribute to emissions reduction and meet climate goals in the market," said Heung-soo Kim, Hyundai executive vice president.
- Governor Brian Kemp called Hyundai Motor Group and SK On "valued partners and key players" in Georgia’s automotive industry.
- SK Battery America recently exceeded its hiring goal of 2,600 workers for two Georgia facilities that will manufacture batteries for Ford and Volkswagen EVs.
