# Hyundai Motor Company Georgia EV Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A large-scale immigration raid by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement detained 475 workers at Hyundai's $7.6 billion electric-vehicle manufacturing plant in Bryan County, Georgia, the state's largest economic-development project, which also includes an under-construction battery joint venture with LG Energy Solution.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The facility manufactures electric vehicles for the U.S. market and will host a battery-production joint venture that supplies those EVs, making it a core component of Georgia's EV supply chain.

## Key facts

- The raid detained 475 people, most of them South Korean nationals.
- Hyundai's Georgia site is considered the state's largest economic-development project.
- Hyundai Motor Group started manufacturing EVs at the $7.6 billion plant a year ago.
- The plant employs about 1,200 workers in a largely rural area west of Savannah.
- A battery joint venture (HL-GA Battery Co.) between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution is under construction and slated to open next year.
- ICE video showed agents directing workers to line up outside the site before detaining them.
- The operation was described as the "largest single site enforcement operation" in the agency’s two-decade history.
- South Korean officials expressed concern and are sending diplomats to the site.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp said all employers must follow the law after the raid.
- Asian Americans Advancing Justice-Atlanta called the raid "unacceptable" in a joint statement.
- Local business owner Sammie Rentz worries the raid will hurt nearby businesses.
- Resident Tanya Cox questioned whether the plant has brought sufficient jobs to the community.
