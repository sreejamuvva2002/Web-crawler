# Innovation Isles Aerospace Park

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-12

**Source:** [Innovation Isles Aerospace Park Certified ‘Ready for Accelerated Development’ - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/innovation-isles-aerospace-park-certified-ready-for-accelerated-development/)  ·  `page_016724`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), in cooperation with the Golden Isles Development Authority, announced that the Innovation Isles Aerospace Park has earned a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification with “Select” status.
- The site’s new “GRAD Select” status provides an additional seal of approval that makes it especially attractive for future industrial development.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the park is a key addition to the GRAD site portfolio due to its strategic location.
- The park is adjacent to the Brunswick Golden Isles Airport, which offers daily service to and from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The park is located only a short distance to the Port of Brunswick, a national Ro/Ro hub.
- Combined with the speed-to-market advantages of a GRAD site, the park is positioned as a prime location for manufacturers and aerospace service or R&D companies.
- The Innovation Isles Aerospace Park spans 650 acres.
- The site is located off Innovation Drive and Jetport Road in Brunswick.
- The site is less than 30 minutes away from the Port of Brunswick, which is one of two facilities that make Georgia Ports Authority the second busiest in the nation for total Ro/Ro trade.
- The site is just over five miles from Interstate 95, described as a major east coast artery from Miami to the Houlton–Woodstock Border Crossing between Maine and the Canadian province of New Brunswick.
- Innovation Isles Aerospace Park is less than 10 minutes from Brunswick’s central business district.
- Existing road access through the adjacent North Glynn Commerce Park will be expanded to become the primary entrance.
- Golden Isles Development Authority CEO and President Ryan Moore called the GRAD certification a significant milestone that underscores their commitment to economic growth in the Golden Isles.
- Ryan Moore emphasized that workforce strategies must evolve in tandem with business attraction efforts as they market the site for investment.
- In 2020, the Golden Isles Development Authority and the Brunswick–Golden Isles Chamber of Commerce launched a community-wide talent development strategy for Brunswick and the Golden Isles.
- The talent development strategy was facilitated by the Carl Vinson Institute of Government at the University of Georgia.
- Initiatives from these efforts include a teacher externship program that allows middle through high school educators to step out of the classroom and into local industries.
- The teacher externship program equips participants with firsthand exposure to guide students toward fulfilling careers in their community.
- Student industry tours are led by the CEO of the Golden Isles College and Career Academy and the Talent Development Strategy Leader.
- Completing the GRAD certification program is a hands-on way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development in their regions.
- To qualify for GRAD status, available sites must meet the program’s due diligence standards, be reviewed by a third party, and earn final approval from a board of advisors comprised of public and private sector economic development professionals.
- The GRAD Select status indicates that a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Program offers more than 60 industrial certified sites ready for fast-track industrial projects due to extensive prior planning and investment into site readiness.
