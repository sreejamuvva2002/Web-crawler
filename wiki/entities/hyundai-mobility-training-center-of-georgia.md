# Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia

**Type:** Workforce Training  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[workforce-training|Workforce Training]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

An 89,000-square-foot state-of-the-art training facility in Bryan County, Georgia that prepares workers for electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and its affiliates.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Trains the next generation of EV manufacturing professionals and supports workforce development for Hyundai's EV production lines.

## Key facts

- The center is an 89,000-square-foot state-of-the-art facility designed to train EV manufacturing professionals.
- It can train up to 824 individuals at a time with customized immersive education tailored to Hyundai’s advanced EV manufacturing processes.
- Facilities include STEP Line, IMPACT Line, High Voltage Battery and Safety Lab, VR Experience Lab, Automation & Mechatronics Lab, Momentum Room (5,764 sq ft), six flexible classrooms, robotics lab, welding lab, paint lab, and a robotic dog for automation training.
- Operated by Georgia Quick Start, part of the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Hyundai’s project in Georgia will create approximately 8,500 jobs by 2031.
- "Our focus has never wavered from bringing quality jobs to hardworking Georgians…" - Gov. Brian Kemp.
- "The Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia represents our commitment to investing in people…" - José Muñoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company.
- "This center is about giving Georgians the tools to build their own futures - right here at home," - Greg Dozier, Commissioner of TCSG.
- "Our subject matter experts partnered directly with HMGMA to learn the skills its Meta Pros will need…" - Scott McMurray, Deputy Commissioner, Georgia Quick Start.
- The facility is strategically located adjacent to HMGMA in Bryan County and is designed to accommodate future hybrid-vehicle training as HMGMA expands.
