# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-05

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | North America’s Largest Lithium-ion Battery Recycling Facility](https://ascendelements.com/battery-resourcers-to-open-north-americas-largest-lithium-ion-battery-recycling-facility-by-august/)  ·  `page_000685`

## Facts

- Battery Resourcers, a vertically integrated lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials company, announced plans to open a commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Covington, Ga.
- On January 19, 2022, Battery Resourcers changed its name to Ascend Elements.
- The 154,000-square-foot facility is scheduled to be fully operational in August 2022.
- When operational, it will be North America’s largest battery recycling facility.
- The facility will have capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of discarded lithium-ion batteries and scrap per year.
- The processing will return battery-grade lithium, cobalt, and nickel back into the battery supply chain.
- Battery Resourcers is investing $43 million in the facility.
- The facility is located at 9172 Industrial Drive in Covington.
- The project will bring at least 150 jobs to the community.
- The site is strategically located near several EV manufacturing hubs and lithium-ion gigafactories.
- Industry analysts and the federal government agree the United States needs to build a sustainable battery recycling infrastructure, and the facility opening will help meet that demand.
- Michael O’Kronley, CEO and Director of Battery Resourcers, stated that automotive OEMs currently have very few options for responsible and cost-effective disposal of discarded batteries and scrap.
- O’Kronley said the company is providing a sustainable solution to help minimize the need for mining while returning valuable, battery-grade materials back into the lithium-ion supply chain.
- O’Kronley expressed a desire for the battery recycling industry to bring more recycling capacity online in the coming years.
- He stated that the industry needs to match gigafactory production capacity with “gigarecycling” facilities capable of recycling millions of batteries.
- O’Kronley noted that while the Covington facility will be the largest in North America at opening, he encourages development of even larger recycling facilities to prevent batteries from going to landfill.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Battery Resourcers is the latest job creator moving to Georgia due to the state’s leadership position in electric vehicle manufacturing.
- Governor Kemp welcomed Battery Resourcers as they open their state-of-the-art Covington facility and expressed honor that they chose Georgia for this milestone business venture.
- Pat Wilson, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, noted that Battery Resourcers had many options to consider before choosing Georgia.
- Wilson highlighted Georgia’s assets for business growth, including an educated workforce, pro-business climate, high quality of life, and commitment to sustainability.
- The opening of the Covington facility marks the first phase of Battery Resourcers’ strategic expansion.
- Plans are already in motion to open an additional facility for precursor and cathode-active material production in 2023.
- The new facility will use the company’s patented Hydro-to Cathode™ technology.
- Compared to mining and production of new materials, the Hydro-to Cathode recycling process is 93% cleaner at a 59% lower cost.
- A recent study published in the scholarly journal Joule found recycled cathode from the Hydro-to-Cathode process outperforms new cathode materials in terms of cycle life by as much as 53%.
- The company’s long-term plans include opening additional facilities in North America, Europe, and Asia.
- These future facilities aim to process up to 150,000 metric tons of lithium-ion material globally per year.
- Battery Resourcers is based in Worcester, Mass.
- The company operates the world’s most efficient lithium-ion battery recycling process.
- Unlike other battery recycling companies, Battery Resourcers starts with a mixed stream of used lithium-ion batteries or production scrap and ends with the production of finished battery-ready cathode active materials.
- The company is engineering a novel process for graphite recovery and purification to return both cathode and anode active materials back to new battery manufacturers.
- Battery Resourcers was founded in 2015 with a mission of returning 100% of battery active materials back into new batteries.
- The company currently makes EV-grade, finished cathode active materials that meet or exceed performance requirements set by other industry-leading brands.
