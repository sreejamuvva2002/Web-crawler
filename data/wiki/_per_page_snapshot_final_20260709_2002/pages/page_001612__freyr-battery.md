# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-01

**Source:** [Freyr Battery cancels $2.6B Georgia battery factory plans | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/freyr-battery-cancels-georgia-battery-plant-pivot-solar-module-cell/740250/)  ·  `page_001612`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery called off plans to build a $2.57 billion battery cell manufacturing plant in Newnan, Georgia.
- The company stated the facility is no longer part of its strategic goals.
- Freyr notified Coweta County officials of the cancellation via a letter last month.
- The Newnan plant was initially announced in 2022.
- The canceled facility would have created 723 jobs.
- First-phase production for the Newnan plant was projected at approximately 34 gigawatt hours.
- The decision to scrap the Newnan plant occurred a month after Freyr acquired Trina Solar’s 5-gigawatt solar module manufacturing facility in Wilmer, Texas.
- Freyr is pivoting to establish a commercial presence in the U.S. solar market.
- Information regarding the pivot was detailed in a November 2024 presentation by the company.
- Trina Solar originally announced an investment of more than $200 million to establish the Wilmer plant in September 2023.
- Approximately one year later, global banking company Standard Chartered secured a $235 million green loan to finance the development and construction of the Wilmer facility.
- The Wilmer plant spans 1.35 million square feet.
- Upon completion, the Wilmer facility will create up to 1,500 jobs.
- The Wilmer site will source polysilicon from the U.S. and Europe.
- The Wilmer facility will feature seven production lines.
- Production at the Wilmer plant began on November 1, 2024.
- Freyr announced on February 10 that it selected Austin, Texas, as its new global headquarters.
- The Austin headquarters location brings the company closer to the Wilmer site in the Dallas-Fort Worth metropolitan area.
- Freyr is currently developing a separate 5 GW solar cell manufacturing facility in the U.S.
- Freyr plans to announce the location of the new solar cell factory in the coming months.
- Production at the new solar cell facility is expected to begin in the first half of 2026.
- The company expects to spend $850 million on the new solar cell facility.
- The new solar cell facility is expected to create 1,800 jobs.
- Both the Wilmer plant and the new solar cell factory are fully eligible for Inflation Reduction Act production tax credits.
- Freyr plans to apply for the IRA production tax credits.
- The future of these tax credits under President Trump remains unclear due to his vocal opposition to the subsidies.
