# AJIN Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-18

**Source:** [AJIN Georgia grand opening marks major milestone for Bulloch County's economic growth  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/ajin-georgia-grand-opening-marks-major-milestone-for-bulloch-countys-economic-growth-9239447)  ·  `page_000471`

## Facts

- Local and state leaders gathered on Thursday, July 18, 2024 to celebrate the grand opening of AJIN Georgia in Bulloch County.
- AJIN Georgia is the first Tier 1 supplier announced for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The facility will produce interior metal and structural parts.
- Construction of the facility kicked off in early 2023.
- Jamie Calloway serves as the HR General Manager of AJIN Georgia.
- Attendees included HMGMA CEO Onchung Gwon, representatives from MOBIS, state and local officials, the Korean consulate, and academic leaders from Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College.
- Representatives from Ajin USA and Wooshin in Alabama were also in attendance.
- Mr. Jungho Sea serves as the CEO and President of AJIN Georgia.
- Billy Allen is the Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County.
- The facility is located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Bulloch County has successfully attracted three to four Hyundai suppliers.
- Sydne Smith is the COO of the Georgia Department of Economic Development and conveyed congratulations from Governor Brian Kemp and Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- AJIN Georgia will create 630 new jobs.
- State Senator Billy Hickman represents District 4.
- A Georgia Southern University economic impact study highlights the vital role of manufacturing in the local economy.
- State Senator Billy Hickman stated that Ajin Georgia, HMGMA, and other suppliers create career opportunities for local youth to stay or return home after college.
- The Council General of the Republic of Korea congratulated AJIN Georgia on its successful site selection.
- The Council General of the Republic of Korea praised AJIN Georgia's role in establishing an electric vehicle supply chain in the southeastern U.S.
- The event highlighted the economic and cultural partnership between Korea and the U.S.
- The plant cost $317 million.
- The facility spans 82 acres.
- The facility includes hundreds of robots handling most production processes.
- Over 200 of the initial 300 employees have been hired.
- A hiring push is planned for August to fill the remaining positions.
- AJIN Georgia is providing career opportunities for citizens with no production experience.
- The company can train an employee in less than a week to perform most production jobs.
- The grand opening of AJIN Georgia marks a significant milestone for the community and the broader economic landscape.
