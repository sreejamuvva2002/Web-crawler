# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02

**Source:** [200 New Jobs Headed to Emanuel County - Athens CEO](https://athensceo.com/news/2026/02/200-new-jobs-headed-emanuel-county/)  ·  `page_053988`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- The automotive project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971.
- The company supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Dongwon Autopart Technology supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast, including in Hogansville, Georgia, at facilities that will remain operational.
- The company has been in the United States since 2003.
- Construction on Dongwon Autopart Technology’s new manufacturing facility will be located at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will be hiring for positions such as management, system leads, and production personnel.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- This project marks Dongwon Autopart Technology’s second location in Georgia.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers choose strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia’s ports.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, noted this will be the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp emphasized that Georgia’s thriving automotive industry is creating new opportunities for Georgians statewide.
- Representative Butch Parrish, Chairman of the House Rules Committee, highlighted the commitment to bringing high-quality jobs to rural communities.
- Emanuel County Development Authority Chairman Will Donaldson expressed excitement about welcoming Dongwon Autopart Technology to the county.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, stated the company remains committed to excellence and supplying internal and external customers with high-quality products and service.
