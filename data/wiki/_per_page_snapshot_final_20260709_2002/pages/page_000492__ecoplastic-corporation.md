# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Ecoplastic building plant in Bulloch bringing additional 456 new jobs  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/ecoplastic-building-plant-in-bulloch-bringing-additional-456-new-jobs-6506426)  ·  `page_000492`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on January 5, 2023 that Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs and invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic is the second Hyundai supplier to announce plans to build a plant in Bulloch County.
- On November 7, 2022, Joon Georgia announced plans to build a $317 million plant in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Joon plans to employ 630 workers at its new facility.
- In February 2022, Aspen Aerogels announced plans to build a $325 million manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Construction on the Aspen Aerogels plant is visible from Highway 301 and I-16 in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Ecoplastic will serve the Hyundai Metaplant well from Statesboro due to its proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in 1984 as a Korea-based company manufacturing plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- Ecoplastic engages in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Han Shang, President and CEO of Ecoplastic, stated the company is honored to establish a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Ecoplastic aims to contribute to the economic development of Georgia and Bulloch County by increasing local jobs and providing a stable supply of high-quality products to client HMGMA.
- Ecoplastic appreciates the support of the State and County governments for the project.
- Ecoplastic's new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South, just over one mile from I-16.
- The site is on additional land acquired by the Development Authority of Bulloch County and recently rezoned to light industrial by the Bulloch County Commission.
- The new plant will be capable of injection molding and painting.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers, garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- Ecoplastic expects to begin operations at the new facility by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County Chair Billy Allen expressed pleasure that this important Hyundai supplier chose their community and confidence in the location's workforce and quality of life.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Ecoplastic has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and over 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Projects announced to date have already exceeded the predicted $1 billion investment from offsite suppliers.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was originally established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd.
- Ecoplastic established its first Technical Research Center in 1987.
- Ecoplastic was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
- Ecoplastic currently employs more than 770 employees and recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
