# 2022 VTO Annual Merit Review Plenary and Awards

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-21

**Source:** [2022 VTO AMR Plenary and Awards | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/eere/vehicles/articles/2022-vto-amr-plenary-and-awards)  ·  `page_002734`

## Facts

- The 2022 VTO Annual Merit Review plenary session was held on Tuesday, June 21, 2022.
- VTO presents awards annually to individuals from partner institutions for contributions to program efforts and to recognize research, development, demonstration, and deployment achievements in specific areas.
- Amgad Elgowainy and Jarod Kelly from Argonne National Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for leading a Cradle-to-Grave analysis supporting the U.S. DRIVE partnership between DOE and industry partners.
- Jiguang (Jason) Zhang from Pacific Northwest National Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for outstanding technical contributions to VTO’s battery R&D activities.
- Yan (Joann) Zhou and Jim Kuiper from Argonne National Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for distinguished achievements supporting electric vehicle charging.
- Matthew Kramer from Ames Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for years of outstanding skill and leadership in magnetic materials research, planning, and collaboration for electric drive technologies.
- Cummins, Inc. received a Team Award for demonstrating 55% brake thermal efficiency in a heavy-duty diesel engine to contribute to the SuperTruck 2 goal of more than doubling freight efficiency compared to trucks produced a decade earlier.
- Navistar, Inc. received a Team Award for demonstrating 55% brake thermal efficiency in a heavy-duty diesel engine in a test cell to contribute to the SuperTruck 2 goal.
- Clemson University, Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC, University of Delaware, Lanxess, and Proper Group International received a Team Award for Dr. Srikanth Pilla’s expertise in leading carbon fiber thermoplastic composites door R&D to achieve weight savings, performance, and sustainability.
- Brett Aristegui, Trev Hall, Darren Stevenson, David Kirschner, Neil Kirschner, Dan Nardozzi, and Erin Russell-Story from the National Energy Technology Laboratory received a Team Award for dedication to VTO and commitment to the national network of Clean Cities coalitions as Clean Cities Regional Managers.
- Dennis Dees from Argonne National Laboratory received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for decades of research in continuum modeling of high-energy, high power, and long-lasting batteries to support the DOE advanced battery portfolio.
- David Anderson from the U.S. Department of Energy received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for serving as inaugural Program Manager for Energy Efficient Mobility Systems and creating an enduring program.
- Ira Bloom from Argonne National Laboratory received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for support of multiple DOE programs on advanced batteries in areas of accelerated testing, life estimation, and elucidating physical and chemical changes that limit battery life.
- Steve Zimmer from the United States Council for Automotive Research (USCAR) received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for leadership as executive director of USCAR and contributions to U.S. DRIVE partnerships.
