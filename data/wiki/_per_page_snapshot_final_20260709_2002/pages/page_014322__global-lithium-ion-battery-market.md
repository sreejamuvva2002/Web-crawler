## Facts

- The United States Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) of 2022 allocated over $370 billion in clean energy incentives, including substantial production tax credits for domestically manufactured battery cells and modules.
- IRA incentives spurred a wave of gigafactory announcements across the American South and Midwest.
- The United States has seen over $90 billion in announced domestic battery manufacturing investments since 2022.
- Major gigafactory projects coming online or scaling through 2025 and 2026 include Tesla's Gigafactory Nevada expansion, Panasonic's Kansas City facility, Samsung SDI's joint venture with Stellantis in Indiana, and LG Energy Solution's multiple JV facilities.
- Major OEMs including General Motors, Ford, and Stellantis are committing to all-electric lineups and deploying substantial capital toward battery supply chain localization.
- The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) reported that battery storage capacity additions reached 18.3 GW in 2025.
- BESS projects accounted for 18.3 GW of newly added capacity in the U.S. in 2025, with states like California, Texas, and Arizona leading deployments.
- Federal tax credits of up to $7,500 per EV under the IRA continue to stimulate purchase demand in the U.S.
