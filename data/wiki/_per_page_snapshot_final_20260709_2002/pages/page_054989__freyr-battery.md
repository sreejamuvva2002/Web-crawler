# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Announces Plans for US Gigafactory in Georgia - Batteries News](https://batteriesnews.com/freyr-battery-announces-plans-us-gigafactory-georgia/)  ·  `page_054989`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery (NYSE: FREY) announced the selection and purchase of a site in Coweta County, Georgia for its planned Giga America battery plant.
- The company selected the Bridgeport Industrial Park site through a rigorous evaluation process based on key operational, logistical, and financial criteria.
- The site was chosen for its suitability to co-locate modular upstream and downstream projects with the Giga America cell manufacturing facility.
- FREYR is planning to initiate detailed project engineering of Giga America in the coming months.
- The initial phase of Giga America is planned to be a cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- The initial phase will be based on the next-generation of 24M’s U.S.-based SemiSolidTM production platform.
- The initial phase has an initial projected capital cost of $1.7 billion.
- The company is evaluating value accretive upstream and downstream modules as well as additional cell production lines.
- These additional phases are expected to bring total capital investments to more than $2.6 billion through 2029.
- Upon completion of all contemplated construction phases, the Giga America complex is expected to be one of the largest battery cell manufacturing developments globally.
- Tom Einar Jensen serves as FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer.
- Jensen stated that expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy from its inception.
- Jensen noted that with the recent passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, they expect U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV, and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- Brian Kemp serves as Governor of Georgia.
- Kemp stated that job creators and innovators from all over the world bring their operations to Georgia because they know they will have success here.
- Kemp expressed pride in welcoming FREYR to the Peach State as the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunity to communities.
- Kemp referred to Georgia as the No. 1 state for business and looked forward to FREYR's growing impact on Coweta and the surrounding region.
- Bob Blackburn serves as Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman.
- Blackburn stated they are thrilled to welcome FREYR Battery to the Coweta County community.
- Blackburn noted that with the addition of FREYR, Coweta County is quickly becoming a premier destination for top-tier manufacturing industries that support the U.S. economy and beyond.
- Blackburn looked forward to the innovation, opportunities, and success that FREYR will generate.
- In accordance with ambitions to accelerate development of Giga America in parallel with ongoing construction of Giga Arctic in Mo i Rana, Norway, the company is intensifying and broadening its financing efforts.
- The total package of financial incentives for Giga America includes robust assistance collectively provided by The State of Georgia and Coweta County.
- The incentive package also includes expected production tax credits associated with the recently passed Inflation Reduction Act.
- FREYR intends to apply with the U.S. Department of Energy for packages that could include a grant and/or direct loans to assist with the development of Giga America.
- The U.S. energy storage market is already the largest in the world.
- According to Rystad Energy, to satisfy a 1.6-degree scenario, cumulative collective storage demand of 4.5 TWh from the U.S. and Europe will be required through 2030.
- Under the same scenario, Rystad expects annual collective storage demand in the U.S. and Europe to reach 1.4 TWh by 2030.
- This represents a 34-fold increase from projected corresponding annual 2022 demand.
- Jensen stated that FREYR is deeply committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors.
- Jensen added that as they advance their U.S. expansion plan with key stakeholders, they expect to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S.
- These investments aim to enhance energy security and economic activity and meet growing demand for energy storage solutions in the U.S. market.
- The Giga America project will be based on the SemiSolidTM technology of FREYR’s U.S.-based licensing partner, 24M.
- The SemiSolidTM platform enables capital and energy efficient production of lithium-ion batteries at scale.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for Giga America with U.S. Independent Power Producers (IPPs).
- The company’s long-term ambition is to replicate the strategy employed at Giga Arctic to produce next-generation batteries that are among the cleanest anywhere in the world.
- Over the life of the project, FREYR and its partners expect to create more than 720 U.S. jobs for highly skilled workers in Coweta County.
- FREYR has purchased the land for development of Giga America at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site in Coweta County, GA.
- The industrial zoned tract is 368 acres with options secured for further expansion.
- The Giga America site already offers substantial room for additional upstream and downstream development projects to further strengthen an anticipated strong cost position and improve the carbon footprint of the batteries produced.
- The development of Giga America positions FREYR to meet rapidly growing customer demand for Energy Storage Systems (ESS) applications with U.S.-based conditional offtake partners.
- FREYR is seeing very strong interest for increased volume commitments through ongoing customer dialogues.
- FREYR is accelerating its previously planned U.S. expansion based on strong tailwinds in U.S. renewable energy development, an intensifying focus on grid stability initiatives, and recently announced tax incentives associated with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Projected U.S. demand is being augmented further by increased interest from existing and new customer dialogues in the commercial mobility and electric vehicle segments.
- Giga America will leverage 24M’s SemiSolidTM platform, which simplifies the production process of lithium-ion batteries significantly.
- The platform enables highly capital, labor, and energy efficient manufacturing at scale based on U.S. technology originating from MIT.
- FREYR’s project team in the U.S. intends to replicate major elements of the Giga Arctic development by maintaining a continuous technical dialogue with key personnel in Norway.
- The announcement was published on November 11, 2022.
