# Eco Plastic

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Eco-plastic When will the US production base be monetized?](https://en.topdaily.kr/articles/12449)  ·  `page_019858`

## Facts

- Eco Plastic, a company specializing in plastic automotive parts, is experiencing delayed profitability for its U.S. subsidiary established approximately four years ago.
- The U.S. subsidiary has continued to incur financial deficits since its establishment.
- Initial expectations for profitability starting last year were delayed due to external factors including an EV demand slowdown ("chasm") and adjustments to the prime contractor's production system.
- ECOPLASTIC AMERICA reported sales of 191.7 billion won and a net loss of 28.2 billion won in the previous year.
- Sales increased by 1,051.3% (11.5 times) to 16.6 billion won compared to the prior year, but the company continued to incur deficits with a net loss of 21.3 billion won in 2024.
- The U.S. subsidiary, established in 2022, operates an EV parts (bumper) factory in Savannah, Georgia.
- Eco Plastic was selected as a primary bumper supplier for Hyundai Motor Company's North American EV factory, leading to a joint expansion that included establishing a local production base.
- The U.S. EV parts factory underwent reviews in late 2022, broke ground in June 2023, and started full-scale mass production in October 2024.
- A client-tailored production system is in place, but capacity utilization rates have only shown a slow upward trend.
- The Georgia plant's capacity utilization rate reached 55.6% last year.
- In 2024, the factory's first year of production, the utilization rate was 10.8%.
- The low initial utilization rate has hindered the subsidiary from achieving stable profitability.
- At groundbreaking, Eco Plastic anticipated performance growth through long-term overseas supply contracts alongside its domestic business.
- The facility was planned to supply parts to Metaplant America (HMGMA), a dedicated EV factory under construction by Hyundai Motor Group with an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Projected revenue and operating profit for 2025 were set at 313.9 billion won and 6.3 billion won, respectively.
- The 2025 projections were based on an annual production target of 140,000 units.
- For 2030, the company projected revenue of 738.2 billion won and operating profit of 34.8 billion won, based on an annual production of 330,000 units.
- Changes to the prime contractor's production system have disrupted the supplier's original plans.
- HMGMA was originally planned as a dedicated EV factory but announced plans to introduce hybrid models alongside EVs upon its completion last year.
- HMGMA began producing the Ioniq 5 in October 2024 and started mass production of the Ioniq 9 electric SUV in March 2025.
- Starting in June, Kia models will be added to the lineup for a total of three EV types, with plans to mass-produce two hybrid models next year.
- Changes to the production system were not reflected in EcoPlastic's 2023 annual profit and loss forecast for its U.S. subsidiary.
- The timing for the U.S. subsidiary's profitability is expected to be repeatedly delayed until HMGMA's hybrid model production begins.
- Sequential startup of production lines for three EV models is further affecting initial utilization rates for parts suppliers.
- The slow performance improvement of the overseas subsidiary has increased the financial burden on the parent company.
- Eco Plastic holds a 100% stake in its U.S. subsidiary and provides annual additional capital contributions.
- Capital contributions totaled 6.7 billion won in 2022, 12.9 billion won in 2023, 13.4 billion won in 2024, and 34 billion won last year, reaching a cumulative total of 67 billion won.
- The parent company provided loan support during the subsidiary's first year of mass production to help establish the overseas business.
- Loans to the U.S. subsidiary totaled 102.9 billion won in 2024 and stood at 100.4 billion won as of last year.
- The scale of debt guarantees provided by Eco Plastic for its U.S. subsidiary is significant.
- As of last month, Eco Plastic's total outstanding debt guarantee balance was 274.7 billion won, representing 82.5% of its total capital (333 billion won) at the end of 2025.
- The largest portion of these guarantees, 187.5 billion won, is allocated to the U.S. subsidiary.
- An Eco Plastic official stated that HMGMA's fixed capacity and changing production system prevent suppliers from achieving sufficient utilization rates with only two EV models, but they expect the U.S. subsidiary to meet original performance and sales targets once hybrid models are mass-produced next year.
