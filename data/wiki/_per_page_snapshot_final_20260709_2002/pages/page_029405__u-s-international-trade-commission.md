# U.S. International Trade Commission

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-21

**Source:** [Federal Register, Volume 90 Issue 34 (Friday, February 21, 2025)](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2025-02-21/html/2025-02893.htm)  ·  `page_029405`

## Facts

- U.S. International Trade Commission instituted an investigation on February 14, 2025, following a complaint filed on January 13, 2025, under section 337 of the Tariff Act of 1930.
- A supplement to the complaint was filed on February 3, 2025.
- Complainants include Syensqo SA of Belgium, Solvay Specialty Polymers USA LLC of Alpharetta, Georgia, and Solvay Specialty Polymers Italy S.P.A. of Italy.
- The complaint alleges violations of section 337 based upon the importation into the United States, the sale for importation, and the sale within the United States after importation of certain polyvinylidene fluoride resins.
- Alleged infringement involves claims 1-7, 10, and 12 of U.S. Patent No. 8,337,725 (the '725 patent).
- Complainants allege that an industry in the United States exists or is in the process of being established as required by applicable Federal Statute.
- Complainants requested that the Commission institute an investigation and issue a limited exclusion order and a cease and desist order.
- The plain language description of the accused products defining the scope of the investigation is polyvinylidene fluoride polymers comprising acrylates in solid form.
- Respondents named include Inner Mongolia 3F Wanhao Fluorochemical Industry Co. Ltd. (Fengzhen, Inner Mongolia Province, China), Zhejiang Juhua Co. Ltd. (Quzhou City, Zhejiang Province, China), Zhejiang Fluorine Chemical New Material Co. Ltd. (Shangyu Hangzhou Gulf Chemical Industry Zone, Shaoxing, Zhejiang, China), Hubei Fluorine New Materials Co. Ltd. (Qianjiang Economic Development Zone, Hubei Province, China), and Sinochem Lantian Co. Ltd. (Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province, China).
- The Office of Unfair Import Investigations will not participate as a party in this investigation.
- Named respondents must submit responses to the complaint and notice of investigation within 20 days after service by the complainant.
- Failure of a respondent to file a timely response may be deemed a waiver of the right to contest allegations and may result in an initial determination, final determination, and issuance of exclusion or cease and desist orders without further notice.
- The notice was issued on February 14, 2025, by Sharon Bellamy, Supervisory Hearings and Information Officer.
