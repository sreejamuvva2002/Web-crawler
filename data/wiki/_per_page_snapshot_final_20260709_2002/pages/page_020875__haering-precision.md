# Haering Precision

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-13

**Source:** [Haering Precision Terms Innaugural Skills Invitational a Success; Logan Turner Takes Home Win - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/haering-precision-terms-innaugural-skills-invitational-a-success-logan-turner-takes-home-win/)  ·  `page_020875`

## Facts

- Haering Precision recently announced the successful conclusion of its first annual Skills Invitational.
- The event was held in partnership with North Georgia Technical College.
- The Skills Invitational took place on April 24 at North Georgia Tech’s Clarkesville campus.
- Participants were drawn from Franklin, Hart, Stephens, and Union counties.
- The event aimed to ignite interest in machining and metals jobs among high school students.
- It provided a platform for students to explore diverse career opportunities within the manufacturing industry.
- Participants from local high schools teamed up with senior precision machining students from North Georgia Tech in a pro-am style contest.
- The contest featured challenges designed to test technical skills, creativity, and teamwork.
- Students faced hands-on challenges simulating real-world scenarios in precision machining and manufacturing.
- Tasks included interpreting technical drawings and programming CNC machines.
- Judges evaluated teams based on accuracy, efficiency, innovation, and collaborative problem-solving.
- Dustin Chambers serves as the Commercial Plant Manager at Haering Precision.
- Dustin Chambers stated the Skills Invitational was a platform for students to apply knowledge, showcase skills, and explore interest in machining, reaffirming belief in manufacturing's future.
- Winners received prizes including tools and paid internship opportunities at Haering Precision.
- The paid internships provide exposure to real-world manufacturing processes and foster professional growth.
- Franklin County High School senior Logan Turner took home first place in the contest.
- Tim Bennett serves as the Senior Technical Recruiter at Haering Precision.
- Tim Bennett emphasized that the event empowered young talent and encouraged them to explore possibilities within the manufacturing industry.
- Haering Precision and North Georgia Technical College plan to expand opportunities for students to pursue careers in precision machining and manufacturing.
