# Enchem

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** ["Enchem Achieves Over 50% Electrolyte Market Share in North America, Replacing Chinese Materials with Korean Supply" - The Asia Business Daily](https://www.asiae.co.kr/en/article/2025072910141477854)  ·  `page_008007`

## Facts

- Enchem is a secondary battery material and electrolyte company.
- Enchem announced on July 29 that it has expanded its supply to Ultium Cells, the joint venture between LG Energy Solution and General Motors (GM).
- Enchem's electrolyte market share in North America is estimated to exceed 50% this year.
- Enchem established its Georgia plant in 2019.
- Enchem began supplying electrolytes, a core material for batteries, to global customers in 2022.
- Ultium Cells has built its first and second plants in Ohio and Tennessee, respectively.
- Ultium Cells operations commenced in 2022 and last year.
- GM announced in its second-quarter earnings conference that it will begin producing lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries at the Ultium Cells Tennessee plant starting in 2027.
- GM stated it will develop lithium-rich manganese (LMR) batteries through Ultium Cells by 2028.
- GM has formalized its plans to make strategic investments in battery production within the United States and to strengthen the proportion of key materials such as lithium, graphite, and manganese sourced from North America.
- Price competitiveness has emerged as a critical factor in the battery market.
- Ultium Cells is accelerating LFP battery production due to price competitiveness.
- Enchem is increasing its share of electrolyte supply for LFP batteries.
- The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)" aims to exclude Chinese battery and material companies.
- Enchem, as one of the first battery material companies to enter the U.S. market, expects to see increased sales as joint ventures move to expand domestic production.
- Even though Enchem's electrolyte market share in the United States already exceeds 50%, the company continues to further expand its market share.
- An Enchem official stated, "With the passage of OBBBA, the North American supply chain is rapidly being reorganized."
- The same Enchem official added, "As relationships between K-battery companies and North American automaker joint ventures deepen, this will also act as a positive factor for Enchem."
