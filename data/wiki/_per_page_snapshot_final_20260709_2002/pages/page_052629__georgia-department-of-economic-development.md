# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-07

**Source:** [New Georgia Economic Development Records Set for Jobs, Investment in Just Nine Months | Development Authority of Peach County](https://peachcountydevelopment.com/news/article/new-georgia-economic-development-records-set-for-jobs-investment-in-just-ni)  ·  `page_052629`

## Facts

- Through the third quarter of fiscal year 2022 (July 1, 2021 - March 31, 2022), GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 251 project locations.
- These supported projects resulted in more than 35,400 new jobs for the state.
- These supported projects resulted in $12.9 billion in investment for the state.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the Q3 FY2022 economic development records on April 7, 2022.
- The reported job creation represents a 48% increase compared to three-quarters through the previous fiscal year.
- The reported job creation represents a 6% increase compared to the prior year’s overall total.
- Total investment increased by 54% compared to the same time period last year.
- Nearly 78% of both projects and investments were located outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area.
- Over half of the jobs created were located outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area.
- Key industry sectors driving growth include automotive, advanced manufacturing, logistics and distribution, software and technology, and food processing.
- Georgia’s electric vehicle (EV) and sustainability ecosystems have grown tremendously over the past year.
- The state is actively recruiting the entire EV supply chain to ensure an effective, efficient, and affordable environment for EV production.
- Rivian plans to develop a $5 billion campus at Stanton Springs North.
- Rivian plans an electric vehicle service center in Atlanta that will provide training on EVs.
- SK Battery America partnered with Ascend Elements to recycle cell and module lithium-ion battery manufacturing scrap from SK’s Jackson County facility.
- Ascend Elements is an American lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup.
- Ascend Elements announced its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington in January.
- Aurubis will invest $340 million in a ‘first-of-its-kind in the U.S.’ state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- Aurubis is one of the leading recyclers of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials worldwide.
- Aspen Aerogels announced plans to locate a new 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility in Statesboro.
- Aspen Aerogels will produce PyroThin materials that provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems at its new Statesboro facility.
- Aspen Aerogels is an electrification solutions and sustainability leader.
- The No. 1-ranking workforce training program, Georgia Quick Start, helped secure numerous projects across the state.
- Georgia Quick Start has provided specialized training tailored specifically to business needs for more than 40 years.
- Georgia Quick Start is administered through the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Pepsi Co.’s expansion in DeKalb County was secured with help from Georgia Quick Start.
- Remington Firearms’ world-class research and development center in LaGrange was secured with help from Georgia Quick Start.
- Titan Steel Doors’ expansion in Hart County was secured with help from Georgia Quick Start.
- Pepsi Co., Remington Firearms, and Titan Steel Doors projects alone represent 1,082 new jobs and $373 million in investment.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) deepwater and regional ports projects have played a key role in securing diverse projects.
- Serena & Lily Inc.’s new facility is located in Effingham County.
- The GPA has been reporting record-breaking numbers for 19 consecutive months as it expedites plans for long-term growth.
- The Savannah Harbor Expansion Project (SHEP) was recently completed, deepening a 40-mile stretch between the port and the Atlantic Ocean.
- SHEP is a joint state and federal project that will accommodate super-sized freighters traveling from the expanded Panama Canal.
- Inland ports are attracting investments, such as the Appalachian Regional Port driving Roper Corporation’s decision to invest $118 million in expanding its facilities in Walker County.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport serves as a key asset driving Georgia’s growth.
- The state maintains an extensive rail network that drives growth.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation maintains more than 1,200 miles of highway as part of Georgia’s highway and interstate system.
- The highway and interstate system connects companies to 80% of the country in two days or less of driving time.
- Ryder System Inc. selected Henry County for a new facility that will create more than 250 new jobs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that targeting new strategic industries for growth and supporting existing businesses’ expansions are central to the department’s mission.
- GDEcD is the state’s sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating new markets, attracting tourists, and promoting the state for arts and entertainment projects.
- GDEcD plans and mobilizes state resources for economic development.
