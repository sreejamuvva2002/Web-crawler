# West Point Economic Developments

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-08

**Source:** [West Point preparing for huge economic developments](https://www.wtvm.com/2021/04/08/west-point-preparing-huge-economic-developments/)  ·  `page_021412`

## Facts

- Close to 900 new jobs are coming to West Point, Georgia.
- Recent expansions and developments in the city's automotive industry are expected to increase West Point's population.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated that after years of idling following the departure of the textile industry, Kia and its suppliers are moving into West Point, adding jobs, housing, and community growth.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced Monday a $9 million investment at Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System to expand its operations.
- A new $240 million facility is being built at Hyundai TRANSYS.
- Job expansion is occurring at Jin-Tech America, a supplier of interior automotive parts.
- West Point Development Authority director Meghan Duke expressed excitement about the next generation of automobiles being built in West Point.
- Both Hyundai TRANSYS and Jin-Tech America provide parts to West Point’s Kia facility.
- The city is building a new apartment complex near the West Point Coca-Cola facility.
- Many new homes are being constructed in the Villages of Harris Creek neighborhood on Highway 103.
- Johnny's Pizza owner Ben Hamilton expressed excitement about increased foot traffic and investment in West Point businesses.
- Meghan Duke reported downtown growth driven by lunch-hour crowds visiting local businesses due to project momentum.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System is accepting job applications for its expansion.
