# Georgia State Tax Incentives

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-10

**Source:** [The Georgia Guide to State Tax Incentives | Gusto](https://gusto.com/resources/states/georgia/ga-tax-incentives)  ·  `page_015344`

## Facts

- Georgia offers a Job Tax Credit that rewards businesses for creating new jobs, with the potential to eliminate corporate income tax liability and reduce payroll withholding obligations.
- The Job Tax Credit provides between $1,250 and $4,000 per year for five years for every new job created, depending on the business's county tier.
- To qualify for the Job Tax Credit, businesses must be engaged in manufacturing, warehousing/distribution/logistics, software development, fintech, data centers, contact centers, telecommunications, or research and development facilities.
- Qualified jobs under the Job Tax Credit must have a minimum 35-hour workweek, include health insurance benefits, and pay more than the average wage of the county with the lowest average wage in the state ($636 a week).
- Businesses must meet and maintain the minimum number of new jobs created per year to qualify for the Job Tax Credit.
- Georgia offers a Quality Jobs Tax Credit that incentivizes higher wages and robust benefits, starting at a minimum credit of $2,500 for jobs paying at least 110% of the county's average wage.
- The Quality Jobs Tax Credit increases to $5,000 per new job for businesses creating jobs that pay at least 200% of the county's average wage.
- Credits from the Quality Jobs Tax Credit can be applied to state corporate income tax and any excess to business payroll withholding.
- Georgia's Investment Tax Credit rewards qualifying manufacturing and telecommunications businesses investing in growth projects, offering a credit of 1% to 8% of a $100,000 investment depending on county tier.
- The Investment Tax Credit can be applied to 50% of tax liability, and excess credits for companies in designated rural Tier 1 and 2 counties can offset payroll withholding obligations.
- Qualifying for the Investment Tax Credit requires operating a manufacturing or telecommunications business (or corporate facility/support office) in Georgia for at least three years and making a minimum $100,000 investment in a new or existing GA manufacturing/telecommunications company.
- Eligible expenses for the Investment Tax Credit include land acquisition costs, building improvements, machinery and equipment, recycling and pollution control equipment, and defense plant manufacturing.
- The Port Tax Credit Bonus is an add-on credit that offsets up to 50% of corporate income tax liability for businesses increasing imports or exports through Georgia deepwater ports by more than 10% from the previous year's base amount.
- To qualify for the Port Tax Credit Bonus, companies must meet minimum Port Traffic requirements of 75 net tons, five containers, or 10 twenty-foot equivalent units.
- Georgia's Mega Project Tax Credit rewards large companies with a $5,250 credit per job per year within a six-year period if they hire at least 1,800 net new full-time employees and invest a minimum of $450 million or have a payroll of $150 million.
- Mega Project Tax Credit qualifications also require paying an average wage above specified minimums or demonstrating high growth potential.
- The Georgia Child Care Tax Credit offers a credit equal to 100% of the cost if a business buys or builds a state-licensed child care facility for employees, or 75% of direct costs if sponsoring at a state-licensed facility.
- Child Care Tax Credits can be applied to 50% of corporate income tax liability and are well above the federal credit which only offers 25%.
- The Retraining Tax Credit offsets retraining costs for existing employees on new technology and equipment, providing 50% of direct costs (including materials and training wages) up to $500 per employee per approved system, with a maximum of $1,250 per employee per year.
- Georgia's state Research and Development Tax Credit offers 10% of the cost of qualified R&D expenditures, applicable to 50% of net GA corporate income tax and excess payroll withholding.
- Qualified R&D activities include new product development, developing/testing prototypes/dyes/molds, testing manufacturing assembly line or distribution processes, implementing plant equipment/ERP software, and improving legacy software or cloud storage.
- The Life Sciences Manufacturing Tax Credit Bonus provides an additional $1,250 for creating jobs manufacturing pharmaceuticals, medicines, and medical equipment/supplies in Georgia, applicable to jobs created after July 1, 2021.
- The PPE Tax Credit Bonus, available for tax years 2020 through 2024, offers $1,250 per new job creating personal protective equipment or hand sanitizer in Georgia, requiring qualification under the Job Tax Credit.
- The Digital Entertainment/Film Tax Credit provides a 20% credit on qualified production expenditures in Georgia, with a minimum expenditure requirement of $500,000 within the state.
- Companies can receive an additional 10% credit for using the Georgia logo on approved projects and linking to the Georgia Film website, subject to a gross income cap of less than $100 million for interactive entertainment companies.
- Georgia caps the Digital Entertainment Tax Credit at $12.5 million per year on a first-come, first-served basis, with no single company receiving more than $1.5 million in credits.
- The Premium Tax Credit offers insurance businesses between $750 and $3,500 for creating five to 25 net new jobs with at least a 35-hour workweek, reasonable health insurance, and wages above the lowest-paying county's average.
- Georgia does not offer state tax credits for using solar energy or alternative energy, but businesses can apply for the federal Energy-Efficient Commercial Buildings Deduction.
- Georgia provides sales and use tax exemptions for manufacturing companies on machinery/equipment, industrial repairs, raw materials/packaging, manufacturing energy, material handling equipment, pollution control equipment, clean room equipment, and inventory.
- Data centers, high-tech companies, and distribution centers in Georgia can take sales and use tax exemptions on certain purchases, equipment, inventory, and primary material handling equipment.
- Many cities and counties in Georgia offer local tax incentive programs that businesses can apply for by checking their respective county websites.
