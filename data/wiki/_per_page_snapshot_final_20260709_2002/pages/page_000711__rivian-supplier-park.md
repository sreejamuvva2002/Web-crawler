# Rivian Supplier Park

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Supplier Park | Clayco](https://claycorp.com/project/rivian-supplier-park)  ·  `page_000711`

## Facts

- Rivian invested nearly $120 million to develop a 1.2 million square-foot supplier park and warehousing/logistics facility near its existing plant in Normal, Illinois.
- The design-build opportunity was expected to generate several hundred supplier-related jobs and nearly 100 direct Rivian positions.
- The supplier park was a strategic initiative designed to support Rivian’s expanding electric vehicle production by co-locating key suppliers adjacent to the main campus.
- Co-locating suppliers adjacent to the main campus significantly reduced shipping, logistics, and warehousing costs, ultimately streamlining supply chain operations.
- Construction was underway, with completion anticipated in 2026.
- The development included a connector tunnel beneath Highway 150, enabling direct and efficient access between the supplier park and Rivian’s main manufacturing facility.
- Additional infrastructure improvements included a new roundabout at the intersection of College Avenue and Highway 150, along with other road and logistics enhancements identified through a comprehensive traffic study.
- In addition to the primary buildings, the project featured a 1 million square-foot exterior paving area.
- This expansive paved zone accommodated trailer staging and mobile warehousing, providing critical flexibility for inbound and outbound logistics operations.
- Rivian anticipated that several key suppliers would establish operations within the park, improving material supply processes and enhancing production reliability.
- WEG Transformers USA is located in Washington, Missouri.
- The project represented a major economic development initiative contributing to significant local job creation and long-term regional growth through both Rivian and its extended supply chain.
