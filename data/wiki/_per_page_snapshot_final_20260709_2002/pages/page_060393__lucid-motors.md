# Lucid Motors

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2019-12-05

**Source:** [Lucid Motors sees EV facility groundbreaking](https://evertiq.com/news/47361)  ·  `page_060393`

## Facts

- Lucid Motors has officially begun phase-one construction of its EV manufacturing facility in Arizona.
- The facility represents an investment of more than USD 300 million.
- The facility’s “ground-building” ceremony was held Tuesday in Casa Grande, Arizona, with Arizona Governor Doug Ducey, representatives from the Arizona Commerce Authority, and other local officials in attendance.
- The facility is scheduled for completion in late 2020.
- Production of Lucid’s first car, the Lucid Air luxury sedan, is scheduled to follow the facility's completion.
- According to the company website, the Lucid Air will have a 400-mile range.
- The Lucid Air will be capable of accelerating from 0-60 mph in under 2.5 seconds.
- The company has announced the formation of 4,800 direct and indirect jobs by 2029.
- More than USD 700 million in capital expenditures are expected from Lucid over the next approximately five years.
- The project will have an estimated USD 32 million revenue impact on the region over the next 20-year period.
- The project includes new training programs and related curriculum at local community colleges.
- Lucid Motors CEO and CTO Peter Rawlinson stated that the Lucid Air is a cutting-edge electric vehicle designed, engineered, and destined for manufacture entirely in America.
- Peter Rawlinson expressed pride in moving forward on the commitment to manufacturing the Lucid Air in Casa Grande.
- Rawlinson noted that with supportive investors, an outstanding team of designers and engineers, and a product strategy extending well beyond the Air, they expect today to be just the start of a longstanding presence in the city.
- According to the press release, Lucid visited more than 60 sites spanning 13 states during its search for a location.
- The company cited business climate, infrastructure, workforce, and proximity of the automotive supply chain in the Arizona-Sonora region as factors for its final selection.
