# Daimler Truck

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-11

**Source:** [Nearly 7,000 UAW Members at Daimler Truck Vote to Authorize Strike, If Necessary - UAW | United Automobile, Aerospace and Agricultural Implement Workers of America](https://uaw.org/nearly-7000-uaw-members-at-daimler-truck-vote-to-authorize-strike-if-necessary/)  ·  `page_053595`

## Facts

- Nearly 7,000 Daimler Truck North America workers voted by a resounding 96% on Friday, March 8th to authorize a strike if necessary.
- The contract covers 7,000 parts and assembly workers in North Carolina, Georgia, and Tennessee, and expires on April 26th.
- Workers build Freightliner trucks, Western Star trucks, and Thomas Built Buses.
- Workers are facing declining real wages and job security against a backdrop of rising cost of living and massive profits and shareholder payouts by Daimler Truck.
- Daimler amassed nearly $6 billion in 2023.
- The company plans to spend billions of dollars on stock buybacks over the next two years while lavishing millions of dollars on its shareholders.
- Daimler Truck CEO Martin Daum boasted that the company’s profits are “red hot” and claimed that any “leftover money certainly belongs” to the company’s stockholders.
- CEO Martin Daum received over $7.5 million in total compensation last year alone.
- Daimler workers are the latest UAW members standing up to corporate greed.
- Thousands of UAW members have won record contracts in the last year, including autoworkers at the Big Three automakers, and Allison Transmission workers in Indianapolis, IN.
