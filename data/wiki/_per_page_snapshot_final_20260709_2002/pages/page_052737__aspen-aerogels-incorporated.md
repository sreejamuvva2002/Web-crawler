# Aspen Aerogels Incorporated

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-28

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q2 2022 Earnings Call Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2022/07/30/aspen-aerogels-aspn-q2-2022-earnings-call-transcri/)  ·  `page_052737`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels is targeting revenue of $1.6 billion and EBITDA of $400 million as it seeks to fully utilize its second aerogel manufacturing plant.
- The company has existing manufacturing capacity to meet a 2x revenue target of $240 million for 2023.
- Aspen expects to utilize phase 1 of Plant II to meet a 3x revenue target of $720 million for 2025.
- On June 29, 2022, the company announced it would not proceed with concurrent public offerings of common stock and convertible notes due to unfavorable market conditions.
- Koch Strategic Platforms provided a $100 million indication of interest in the June financing efforts.
- Aspen has applied for a $150 million U.S. Department of Energy grant for advanced battery materials as part of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Act to support U.S.-based companies dedicated to the electrification economy.
- The company is exploring other DOE programs focused on battery performance and safety that target American manufacturing to address supply chain resiliency in critical sustainability areas like energy storage.
- Aspen Aerogels is building Plant II, an aerogel manufacturing facility under construction in Georgia.
- Long lead time items for Plant II were purchased starting in Q4 of 2021.
- The first phase of Plant II remains on a projected timeline for completion in late 2023.
- The project faces challenges related to supply chain disruptions and inflation.
- Koch Project Solutions is supporting the team building Plant II.
- Phase 1 of Plant II targets $650 million of annual revenue capacity.
- Upon full utilization, Plant II will bring Aspen's total annual revenue capacity to approximately $900 million.
- At full capacity utilization at this revenue level, Aspen is targeting EBITDA of approximately $225 million per year.
- The auto industry is on track to invest a half a trillion dollars in the next five years to transition to electric vehicles.
- Aspen continues to make investments in its high-volume assembly facility in Mexico alongside Plant II in Georgia.
- Aspen's carbon aerogel initiative within Aspen Battery Materials is preparing to commercialize products.
- Aspen Battery Materials has completed the development phase of its next-generation silicon anode material, which targets lower costs and increased energy density, and is preparing qualifying materials for select automotive and battery OEMs.
- The company expects to become cash flow positive in 2024.
- Q2 2022 revenue was $45.6 million, representing 44% year-over-year growth.
- EV thermal barrier revenue reached $10.8 million in Q2, a 42% quarterly increase.
- PyroThin thermal barrier revenue was nearly $11 million in Q2 compared to less than $7 million for all of 2021.
- Aspen's thermal barrier segment requires an annual revenue run rate of around $120 million to deliver 15% plus gross margins as production ramps up in Mexico.
- The company is moving thermal barrier assembly work from Rhode Island to Mexico and Georgia to improve processes and margins.
- Thermal barrier assembly hourly staff in Rhode Island peaked at 428 in June 2022 before decreasing to 280, while approximately 600 people are in Mexico ready for volumes.
- Plant II will feature a linear process requiring little manual material handling and circular processes for key chemicals like CO2 and ethanol to reduce material costs and environmental impact.
- General Motors and Toyota have awarded Aspen business, with forecasts indicating 2.3 million vehicles in North America and half a million vehicles in Japan respectively.
- The company is actively courting additional OEMs in Europe and Asia, with quotes surpassing the value of current awards with GM and Toyota.
- Seven of the top eight EV OEMs are focused on high energy density, high nickel content chemistries and prismatic or pouch cell form factors, which are compatible with PyroThin cell-to-cell barriers.
- Global transportation requirement updates (GT-R) focusing on thermal propagation warnings became mandatory in China, South Korea, and Japan last year, are being considered for implementation in the U.S. this year, and will come into effect in India next year.
- Aspen's capital expenditures for 2022 are expected to range between $250 million and $300 million.
- Through the end of Q2 2022, Aspen had $62.5 million of capital allocated toward Plant II.
- The company is proactively managing supply chain risks for raw materials such as xylenes, batting, and CO2, with nationwide CO2 shortages and tight labor markets posing near-term risks to throughput.
- Don Young serves as President and CEO, Ricardo Rodriguez serves as CFO, and Laura Guerrant serves as Vice President of Investor Relations and Corporate Communications.
