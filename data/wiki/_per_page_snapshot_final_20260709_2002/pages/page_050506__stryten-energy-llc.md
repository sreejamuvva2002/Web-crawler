# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-01

**Source:** [Stryten Energy to Expand US Manufacturing Capacity to 24 Gigawatts - America | Energetica India Magazine](https://www.energetica-india.net/news/stryten-energy-to-expand-us-manufacturing-capacity-to-24-gigawatts)  ·  `page_050506`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC announced a plan expected to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 Gigawatts.
- The expansion plan was announced on April 01, 2025.
- The plan focuses on increasing production across its 11 US manufacturing and battery component plants.
- More than 2,500 people produce batteries at these facilities for multiple applications.
- Battery applications include military and government, data centers, grid storage, automotive, and material handling.
- The new investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits to accelerate capacity growth while supporting the domestic industrial base.
- Mike Judd, President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated the company is committed to growing its domestic energy storage capacity to serve the needs of customers and partners in the United States.
- Judd emphasized that American energy security relies on domestic companies manufacturing batteries that keep supply chains running and critical infrastructure supplied with backup power.
- The company utilizes a unique suite of domestically manufactured battery solutions using advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- Stryten operates production plants in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas to increase gigawatt production capacity.
- The expansion plans will bring hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs and deliver a positive economic impact to surrounding communities.
- Stryten operates a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its factories and the broader US battery manufacturing industry.
- Plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin ensure a reliable US source for critical materials needed to manufacture batteries.
- The investment plan includes increasing domestic processing capacity of recycled plastic from spent batteries for new battery components used in automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia that supports military and industrial battery needs with its proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Dragonfly Energy, headquartered in Reno, Nevada, to bring advanced lithium batteries to automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
