## Facts

- Georgia Manufacturing Alliance (GMA) re-launched as GMA 2.0 on August 4, 2022, initiating a new membership drive for state manufacturing leaders.
- CEO Jason Moss stated that Georgia manufacturers are positioned well for growth in a post-COVID-19 landscape and that strong relationships created by the organization are helping businesses emerge from shutdowns.
- Since its founding in 2008, GMA has partnered with companies throughout Georgia to strengthen relationships and partnerships between manufacturers and suppliers.
- The August 4 re-launch and membership drive was sponsored by Taylor English in Atlanta.
- The new membership drive aims to bring manufacturing leaders together to strengthen ramp-up efforts as facilities reopen and employees return to the workplace.
- The drive for new members continues through the end of 2022 as more companies ramp up to full-production schedules.
- GMA provides monthly plant tours, educational sessions, tradeshows, and networking opportunities designed to help make profitable business connections for its members.
- Additional resources produced by GMA include the Georgia Manufacturing Directory, Georgia Manufacturing Summit, Georgia Manufacturing Calendar, and Georgia Manufacturing Job Board.
