# Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Hyundai, Kia Supplier to Invest in Manufacturing Facility in Coweta Co., Creating 100 Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-hyundai-kia-supplier-invest-manufacturing-32439)  ·  `page_021101`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. will create 100 new jobs and invest more than $24 million in a new manufacturing facility near Newnan in Coweta County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that partners like Kia and Hyundai are attracting a large network of suppliers, creating jobs and opportunity for Georgians across the state.
- Suppliers have committed to bringing over 5,300 jobs to Georgia, mostly to communities outside of large urban areas, since last year's Hyundai Metaplant announcement.
- Hyundai Industrial was established in 1978 and is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- The supplier specializes in manufacturing car seats, arm rests, and head rests for Hyundai and Kia.
- Mr. Woosuck Kim, Team Leader at HDI America Inc., stated that Hyundai Industrial has more than four decades of experience providing comfortable and safe experiences for drivers and passengers with some of the world’s biggest automotive brands.
- Hyundai Industrial expressed excitement about being part of the e-mobility transition in Georgia and looking forward to working with Coweta County to create mutually beneficial growth for the community and future employees.
- Hyundai Industrial has purchased an existing building located at 83 Amlajack Way in Coweta County.
- The purchased building will be retrofitted to produce arm and head rests for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Industrial will focus on filling manufacturing production roles, with job postings to be made available when positions open.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman John Reidelbach welcomed Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. to Coweta County.
- The new facility will have close proximity to I-85, making transport to nearby automobile plants effortless, and is expected to increase workforce opportunities in the community.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project in partnership with the Coweta County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that with Kia in West Georgia and Hyundai on the eastern coast, suppliers like Hyundai Industrial can locate almost anywhere in the state while remaining central to at least two major manufacturers.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that this project exemplifies Georgia’s partnership approach to economic development by matching a community's assets and plans with a company's aligned goals.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered industry practices, collaboration, and innovation, positioning itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- Since 2020, Georgia has created more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs by pursuing the entire electric vehicle supply chain as the market grows.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021, and suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state.
