## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis will exhibit innovative future mobility technologies at CES 2025.
- Hyundai Mobis is introducing human-centric technologies including holographic windshield displays, human-centric interior lighting, and M.Brain.
- Hyundai Mobis is strengthening sales efforts for mass-produced new technologies covering electrification, electronics, and lighting systems.
- On December 12, 2024, Hyundai Mobis (KRX: 012330) announced its participation in CES 2025.
- CES 2025 will be held at the Las Vegas Convention Center in Las Vegas, USA, from January 7 to 10.
- Hyundai Mobis has participated in CES for ten consecutive years since 2016, focusing on mobility innovation and mass-produced new technologies.
- The exhibition theme is 'Beyond and More,' centered on 'Human Tech' that breaks down boundaries between people and technology to maximize safety and convenience.
- Hyundai Mobis designed its exhibition booth with transparent outer walls and ceiling structures to symbolize seamless connections between people, technology, and visitors.
- Hyundai Mobis will give the first public unveiling of the 'Holographic Windshield Transparent Display' at CES.
- The Holographic Windshield Display is co-developed with German optics company ZEISS.
- Hyundai Mobis will equip the Holographic Windshield Display on Kia’s electric vehicle EV9 for its unveiling.
- The display projects driving information, navigation, and infotainment data across the front windshield using a special optical film to enhance safety and spatial feel.
- As a next-generation display technology not currently in mass production, Hyundai Mobis plans to begin actual mass production as early as 2027.
- Hyundai Mobis will introduce 'Human Centric Interior Lighting' that changes like a chameleon based on user mood and situation.
- The smart lighting system can implement 32 situational patterns including stress/motion sickness reduction, dismounting hazard prevention, door ding avoidance, and UVC sterilization.
- Hyundai Mobis's M.Brain is a brainwave-based driver distraction care system that monitors inattentive states like drowsy driving in real-time.
- If driver attention wanes, M.Brain issues warnings via visual LED lights, tactile vibrating seats, and auditory headrest speakers.
- Hyundai Mobis will operate a 'Private Zone' at CES to strengthen face-to-face networking with global client company representatives.
- The Private Zone will exhibit 16 strategic products across electrification, electronics, chassis, and lighting systems for customized sales activities.
- Hyundai Mobis has strengthened customer-centric sales by participating in major global mobility events like the Paris Motor Show and Auto China.
- At the ‘CEO Investor Day’ held on the 19th of last month, President Lee Gyu-suk stated the aim to expand sales from global automakers to 40% of total core auto component sales by 2033.
- Hyundai Mobis aims to become a global TOP 3 automotive supplier based on its leading technological competitiveness.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to invite approximately 40 international students from undergraduate, master’s, and doctoral programs at U.S. engineering schools including MIT, Stanford, and Georgia Tech to the CES site.
- The company intends to showcase future technological competitiveness to these students, introduce crucial roles, and offer consultation opportunities to secure top global talent.
