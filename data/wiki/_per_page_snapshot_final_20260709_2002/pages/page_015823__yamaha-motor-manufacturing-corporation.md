# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2011-06-14

**Source:** [Yamaha brings all of its ATV production to Georgia | Powersports Business](https://powersportsbusiness.com/features/2011/06/14/yamaha-brings-all-of-its-atv-production-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_015823`

## Facts

- The Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation plant is located in Newnan, Ga., southwest of Atlanta.
- The facility produces four vehicle segments: ATVs, PWCs (WaveRunners), side-by-sides, and golf cars.
- In May, Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S.A. announced that the Georgia factory would take over production from Japan of all Yamaha ATVs.
- The Grizzly 700 and 550 FI (standard and EPS) utility ATVs made the move to the Georgia plant in May.
- The transfer brings all Yamaha 4×4 ATV and side-by-side production to Georgia.
- Additional ATV models will be transferred to the facility in 2012 and 2013.
- A press conference brought together dozens of local and state dignitaries, though Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal was absent due to a scheduling conflict.
- Newnan Mayor Keith Brady proclaimed “Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation Day” in the city.
- Yamaha has invested $250 million into the plant over the last 10 years.
- Two Grizzly lines will now be produced at the Georgia facility, replacing Japanese production.
- A new “Assembled in the U.S.A.” logo was unveiled in front of hundreds of plant workers.
- Henio Arcangeli Jr., president of Yamaha Motorsports Group, stated the initiative is important for Yamaha, its 1,300 dealer partners across the United States, and the people of Georgia.
- Arcangeli noted that transferring production to Newnan will better support dealer partners and customers with high-quality ATVs engineered in the state-of-the-art facility.
- The production transfer of the Grizzly models will take place through 2012.
- The plant should be able to add about 200 new jobs to the facility due to the expansion.
- The expansion will also add jobs to the 125 North American suppliers located nearby.
- The Newnan factory first began making golf cars in 1988.
- WaveRunner PWCs found a home in Newnan in 1989.
- Utility ATVs began production at the facility in 1998.
- Rhino side-by-sides came on board at the plant in 2003.
- YMMC senior vice president Mike Chrzanowski stated that in 2011, the facility started production of two of the biggest, best-selling ATVs in Yamaha’s lineup.
- Chrzanowski claimed nowhere in the world has another factory with the range of capabilities YMMC has to produce four different product lines using an ingenious flexible assembly line.
- Harry Ito, president of Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp., presented Grizzly ATVs to both the Coweta County Sheriff’s Department and the Coweta County Fire Department.
- Ito expressed gratitude to management and employees for their dedicated service ensuring the success of the production transfer.
- Chrzanowski noted that since April 2009, more than 600 man-hours had gone into preparing for the shift in production from five to seven ATV base models.
- Chrzanowski emphasized that the hard-working and dedicated workforce is the most important factor in designing, developing, testing, and producing products.
- As the U.S. economy continues to improve, Yamaha looks forward to increased volume from the new model and the resulting increase in new jobs.
- Yamaha officials pointed to optimized manufacturing capacity, consolidated production management and manufacturing technologies, streamlined supply chain, and speed to market as benefits for dealers.
- Congressman Lynn Westmoreland (R-Ga.) welcomed the expansion during a “Chamber of Commerce” day presentation and provided ideas for other uses of the state’s workforce.
- Westmoreland told Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S.A. president Mr. Toshi Kato and Mr. Ito that he believed they could build pianos if allowed.
