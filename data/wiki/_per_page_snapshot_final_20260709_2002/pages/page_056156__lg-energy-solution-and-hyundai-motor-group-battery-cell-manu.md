# LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group to Establish Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture in the U.S. – LG Energy Solution](https://news.lgensol.com/company-news/press-releases/1780/)  ·  `page_056156`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution (LGES) and Hyundai Motor Group announced an EV battery cell manufacturing joint venture in the U.S. on May 26, 2023.
- The two companies signed a memorandum of understanding to produce EV batteries in the U.S. and accelerate electrification efforts in North America.
- The signing ceremony occurred at LGES headquarters in Seoul with Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, and Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, in attendance.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution stated, “Two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands, and together we are ready to drive the EV transition in America.”
- Youngsoo Kwon added, “By further advancing our product competitiveness and global operational expertise, LG Energy Solution will commit our best efforts to offering the ultimate sustainable energy solutions to our customers.”
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company stated, “Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry. We will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing a new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution, a leading global battery producer and long-time partner.”
- LGES and Hyundai Motor Group will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- The joint venture involves a total investment amount of over USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- The new battery cell manufacturing facility will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The site is adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which was under construction at the time of the announcement.
- The joint venture will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh.
- This capacity will support the production of 300,000 EV units annually.
- Construction for the facility is scheduled to begin in the second half of 2023.
- The joint venture plans to start battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the new plant.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply the assembled battery packs to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The facility aims to create a stable regional battery supply and enable Hyundai Motor Group to respond quickly to soaring U.S. EV demand.
- With this joint venture, LGES will have seven battery plants operating or under construction in the U.S.
- LGES is concentrating most of its resources on expanding U.S. production capacity to expedite the clean energy transition in the country.
- LGES and Hyundai Motor Group have a long-standing electrification partnership, having previously supplied EV batteries for the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6 dedicated EV.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was Hyundai Motor Group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, LGES and Hyundai Motor Group began construction of a battery cell joint venture in Indonesia, scheduled to start production in the first half of 2024.
- This marks the second battery manufacturing joint venture between LGES and Hyundai Motor Group as they aim to strengthen their long-time partnership in electrification efforts.
