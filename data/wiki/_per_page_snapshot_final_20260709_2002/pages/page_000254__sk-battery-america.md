# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-08

**Source:** [South Korean EV battery maker SK lays off 958 workers in Georgia - Los Angeles Times](https://www.latimes.com/environment/story/2026-03-08/south-korean-ev-battery-maker-sk-lays-off-958-workers-in-georgia)  ·  `page_000254`

## Facts

- SK Battery America laid off 958 employees on Friday.
- The layoffs represent about 37% of the Georgia plant's workforce.
- The Georgia plant's total workforce was 2,566 people prior to the reductions.
- The factory is located in Commerce, Ga.
- The plant supplied cells for Ford Motor Co.’s now-canceled electric F-150 Lightning pickup truck.
- The plant also supplies battery cells for Volkswagen AG and Hyundai Motor Co.
- SK Battery America stated it reduced its workforce to align operations with market conditions but remains committed to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- SK is currently pursuing customers in the automotive and stationary storage sectors.
- The layoffs reflect broader struggles in the U.S. EV market as EV sales slow nationally.
- Elimination of federal EV tax credits and waning consumer demand have squeezed profitability for South Korean battery manufacturers.
- SK Innovation stated that the end of the U.S. federal tax credit for EV buyers ate into profitability in the fourth quarter of 2025 despite increased shipments to Europe.
- The company cited President Trump's elimination of purchase subsidies and rollback of fuel economy and emissions standards as factors in the unraveling of the EV transition in key markets such as the U.S.
- SK has a second battery plant in Georgia that will supply Hyundai and is expected to start production in the first half of 2026.
- SK's other plant in Tennessee, formerly part of a joint venture with Ford, isn’t expected to start production until 2028.
- The Tennessee plant could supply both automotive and stationary storage cells.
