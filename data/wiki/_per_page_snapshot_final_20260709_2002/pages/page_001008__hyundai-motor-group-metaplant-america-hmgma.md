# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces New Production Start Date at Metaplant America During Hyundai Day in Georgia | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/hyundai-announces-new-production-start-date-at-metaplant-america-during-hyundai-day)  ·  `page_001008`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) accelerated its official start of production to the fourth quarter of 2024, with a grand opening celebration targeted for the first quarter of 2025.
- The start of production date was previously announced as the first quarter of 2025.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty A. Kemp celebrated Hyundai Motor Group's positive impact on the state alongside company leaders during an event marking Feb. 26, 2024 as Hyundai Day.
- President and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz participated in the celebration.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group's dedication to the state and officially recognizing Feb. 26, 2024, as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display outside the State Capitol included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- A hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck was displayed for use in logistics between Hyundai's manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- José Muñoz stated that Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted Hyundai helps recruit businesses, gives back to communities, and works with world-class colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Oscar Kwon, president and CEO of HMGMA, called the project the largest economic development project in Georgia's history and credited the "Meta Pros" and state/local partners including the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Joint Development Authority.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders, Department of Economic Development Director Pat Wilson, and local officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority attended the celebration.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA, which will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia that will employ 3,500 workers.
- A Center for Automotive Research study projects Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create nearly 40,000 jobs and generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings annually.
- HMGMA continues hiring efforts and has developed educational partnerships with area colleges to create an employment pipeline.
- The Electric Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate (EVP TCC) is being offered at four area technical colleges as part of this collaboration.
- Students who complete the EVP TCC program receive preferred status when applying for positions at HMGMA.
- Hyundai is actively developing a hydrogen mobility value chain at HMGMA, first announced at CES 2024.
- Through the Clean Logistics Project in Georgia, the Metaplant will utilize XCIENT Fuel Cell tractors to manage logistics between the plant and its suppliers.
- The XCIENT is Hyundai's commercialized class 8 6x4 fuel cell electric model with a proven record of real-world application and over 6.25 million accumulated miles globally.
- Hyundai is developing hydrogen refueling infrastructure needed to support the fuel cell activity at HMGMA.
- This hydrogen ecosystem collaboration is anticipated to stimulate economic growth and create job opportunities within Georgia.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated electric vehicle mass-production plant, located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Georgia facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant optimizing order collection, procurement, logistics, and production using AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system aims to create a human-centered work environment where robots assist human workers.
- Hyundai Motor America supports Hyundai's Progress for Humanity vision with significant U.S. operations including its North American headquarters in California, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama assembly plant, HMGMA in development in Georgia, and several R&D facilities.
- Hyundai's combined U.S. operations and 835 independent dealers contribute $20.1 billion annually and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy.
