# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-29

**Source:** [Rural jobs, investment dominate Georgia economic growth - Rough Draft Atlanta](https://roughdraftatlanta.com/2024/08/29/rural-jobs-investment-dominate-georgia-economic-growth/)  ·  `page_010166`

## Facts

- Georgia’s economic development efforts are continuing to feature a rural focus.
- The state Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team reported more than $20.3 billion in investment across Georgia during the last fiscal year.
- The last fiscal year included 429 facility expansions and new locations that created 26,900 new private-sector jobs.
- More than 18,300 of those jobs, representing $12.5 billion in investment, came outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Investments outside the metro Atlanta area accounted for 83% of all facility expansions and new locations during the fiscal year.
- Prioritizing economic growth in rural Georgia has been a priority of Gov. Brian Kemp since he first ran for governor back in 2018.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that this strategy ensures regional opportunities for rural Georgians to take advantage of without having too long a commute.
- Gov. Brian Kemp cited the huge “Metaplant” Hyundai Motor Group is building in Bryan County west of Savannah as a prime example of job opportunities in the pipeline for areas outside metro Atlanta.
- The electric-vehicle manufacturing plant is now under construction.
- The Metaplant site will be within easy reach not only for residents of Savannah but for job seekers living in rural counties west of Bryan County along Interstate 16.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that engineering graduates from Georgia Southern University can stay home and have a great-paying job with great benefits instead of moving to Atlanta.
- Manufacturing accounted for more than 50% of the expansions or new locations that took place across Georgia during fiscal 2024.
- Notable expansions included Kia Georgia’s addition of the EV9 to its lineup in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corp. established a campus in Jackson County.
- StandardAero expanded or established operations in Augusta.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer completed its most recent expansion in Fayette County.
- Hyundai Metaplant suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens counties.
- Gov. Brian Kemp expressed confidence that Georgia's industry will eventually become a major success despite sluggish sales, given the state is all-in on electric vehicles manufacturing.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the Biden administration with the IRA tried to push the market before it was ready.
- Gov. Brian Kemp predicted that once charging stations are built out, there’s going to be a big market for these vehicles.
- The governor and Georgia First Lady Marty Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland during the last fiscal year.
- These missions focused on strengthening existing relationships with key investors including Hyundai and its supplier network.
- Those trips and similar overseas missions the previous year helped land more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that a lot of the ground plowed internationally is starting to pay off.
