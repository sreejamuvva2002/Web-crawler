# Murata Electronics North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Murata Electronics Launches Tight Frequency Tolerance Ceramic Resonators for Automotive Market](https://www.autocentral.com/doc/murata-electronics-launches-tight-frequency-t-0001)  ·  `page_047054`

## Facts

- Murata Electronics launched tight frequency tolerance ceramic resonators for the automotive market.
- The low-cost CSTCR, CSTCE, and CSTCV series are the only ceramic resonators that meet the total frequency tolerance of +/- 0.3%.
- Tight tolerance versions of the existing CSTCR and CSTCV will be available by the end of 2001.
- The newly introduced CSTCE ceramic resonator is one of the world's smallest resonators at 1.3 x 3.2mm.
- The CSTCE covers a frequency range of 8.00MHz to 12.5MHz.
- The CSTCR resonator, introduced late last year, is the world's smallest resonator at 4.5 x 2.0mm for the frequency range of 4.00MHz to 7.99MHz.
- Murata shipped over 28 million continuous non-defective ceramic resonators to Visteon Corporation, a major supplier of integrated automotive systems.
- Keith Seamands, STA-Quality Engineer for Visteon, commended Murata's quality in a letter, stating that the excellent quality indicates everyone at all levels is doing an excellent job.
- Tom Cho, Piezoelectric Product Manager for Murata Electronics North America, stated that the intelligent design and unrivaled quality of their ceramic resonators for CAN-BUS operations embodies their dedication to exceed customer expectations.
- Visteon's praise of Murata's flawless resonators confirms the company's commitment to exceeding customer expectations.
- Murata's ceramic resonators provide cost-cutting benefits by serving as smaller, more robust replacements for expensive quartz crystals.
- Ceramic resonators are commonly found in automotive applications such as airbags, power train, and Anti-lock Brake System (ABS) modules.
- The improved total frequency tolerance capability was designed to fulfill the needs of the expanding automotive electronics business.
- While cell phone and wireless consumer product sales are slowing, the automotive industry is continually upgrading car features that rely heavily on high-tech electronics.
- Murata is already the market leader in ceramic resonators for high reliability applications such as automotive.
- The introduction of tight tolerance resonators allows Murata to sustain its industry-leading position as the market moves toward full adaptation of CAN-BUS in most vehicles.
- Previously, only higher cost quartz crystal resonators were considered for CAN-BUS due to frequency tolerance issues.
- Murata's tight tolerance resonators free design engineers from using higher cost components while achieving desired performance and reliability targets.
- Tight tolerance versions of the CSTCR (4.00 to 7.99MHz), CSTCE (8.00 to 12.50 MHz), and CSTCV (14.70 to 70.00 MHz) are currently available from Murata as samples.
- Murata Electronics North America, Inc. is one of 47 wholly-owned subsidiaries of the worldwide organization of Murata Manufacturing Co., Ltd. of Japan (NIKKEI:6981).
- U.S operations for Murata are headquartered in Smyrna, Georgia.
- Murata has a manufacturing facility in State College, Pennsylvania.
- Murata is a leading patent holder and global manufacturer of ceramic electronic components for the telecommunications, computer/peripheral, automotive, communications, and EDP markets.
