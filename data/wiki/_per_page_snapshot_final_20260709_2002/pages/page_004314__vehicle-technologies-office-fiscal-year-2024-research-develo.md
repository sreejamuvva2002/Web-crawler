# Vehicle Technologies Office Fiscal Year 2024 Research & Development Funding Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-20

**Source:** [Funding Selections: Fiscal Year 2024 Vehicle Technologies Office Research & Development Funding Program | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/funding-selections-fiscal-year-2024-vehicle-technologies-office-research-development)  ·  `page_004314`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced $51.7 million in funding for 19 projects that will advance research and development (R&D) critical to unlock innovative and efficient mobility options that are affordable for all Americans.
- The Fiscal Year 2024 Vehicle Technologies Office Research & Development funding opportunity was originally announced on April 4, 2024.
- Projects selected will support the demonstration and deployment of new and efficient mobility options that are affordable for all Americans, in accordance with strategies and plans detailed in the U.S. National Blueprint for Transportation Decarbonization.
- Next-generation phosphate-based cathodes received funding for 4 projects totaling $17.01 million.
- Advancing the state of the art for sodium-ion batteries received funding for 5 projects totaling $6.42 million.
- Developing concepts for decreasing greenhouse gas emissions from off-road vehicles such as construction, agriculture, mining, and forestry vehicles received funding for 4 projects totaling $7.24 million.
- Developing and deploying vehicle-to-everything (V2X) technologies that can lead to meaningful savings at the vehicle and transportation system level received funding for 2 projects totaling $7 million.
- Developing high-performance, domestically produced electrical steels (E-steels) for use in electrified powertrains received funding for 2 projects totaling $5.6 million.
- Addressing critical cybersecurity needs for smart and secure electric vehicle charging received funding for 2 projects totaling $8.46 million.
- Selection for award negotiations is not a commitment by DOE to issue an award or provide funding.
- DOE and the applicants will first undergo a negotiation and DOE may cancel award negotiations and rescind the selection for any reason.
