# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-11

**Source:** [Freyr Battery moves global headquarters to Austin, Texas](https://www.mysanantonio.com/business/article/freyr-battery-austin-headquarters-20158464.php)  ·  `page_000812`

## Facts

- Norwegian battery maker Freyr Battery announced it will move its global headquarters to Central Texas after canceling plans for a $2.6 billion Georgia factory.
- Austin will be the home of Freyr Battery's new global headquarters, according to a Monday, February 10 news release.
- The company chose Austin "to geographically align the company’s workforce with its operations and strategy to provide solar modules made in the United States."
- This announcement comes just days after Freyr agreed to sell its 368-acre site in Coweta County, Georgia previously designated for a large-scale "Giga America" factory that would have supplied electric batteries for clean energy functions.
- The relocation coincides with the company's acquisition of Trina Solar’s 5-GW solar module factory in Wilmer, which already employs more than 1,000 people across the Wilmer and Dallas communities.
- Freyr Battery develops and manufactures clean batteries to power energy storage, electric vehicles, and marine applications.
- Daniel Barcelo, FREYR’s Chairman of the Board and Chief Executive Officer, stated: "Today marks another important step for FREYR as we execute our strategic plan to establish an integrated U.S. solar + battery storage manufacturing enterprise built on advanced technology."
- Barcelo added that the company's global headquarters will be in Austin, Texas, describing it as a vibrant and fast-growing hub of renewable energy and advanced technology development strategically located close to their operations.
- Austin is located about three hours south of Freyr's manufacturing facility in Wilmer, a suburb of Dallas.
- Barcelo stated the company intends to "bring more than 1,000 new American jobs to the Texas economy" and build its teams in Austin and Wilmer.
- Last week, Freyr confirmed plans to abandon its Georgia lithium-ion battery plant in a letter to the Coweta County Development Authority during a Thursday, February 6 meeting.
- Jason Peace, Frey's senior vice president of business development, cited rising interest rates, falling battery prices, and a change in leadership as contributors to the decision to abandon the Georgia plant.
- In a letter to the Coweta County Development Authority, Freyr stated it would work with the Georgia Department of Community Affairs to repay grants and incentives tied to the project.
- In conjunction with its relocation to Texas, Freyr will sell its Coweta County site to an unnamed buyer for $50 million.
- The sale of the Coweta County site is expected to close on February 15.
- Freyr's estimated net profits from the Coweta County land transaction are expected to be $22.5 million.
- Freyr’s new global headquarters will be located at 1211 E 4th St., Austin, TX 78746.
