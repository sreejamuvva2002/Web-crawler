# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-31

**Source:** [American Manufacturing | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/topics/american-manufacturing)  ·  `page_003327`

## Facts

- The landing page for the U.S. Energy & Employment Report (USEER) was published on December 31, 2025.
- An interactive web resource provides data on how the Inflation Reduction Act and Bipartisan Infrastructure Law are leading to announcements of historic levels of private sector investments in the United States, dated April 16, 2026.
- DOE is making investments through its Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains, Advanced Materials & Manufacturing Technologies Office, and Loan Programs Office.
- DOE is deploying a combination of loans, tax credits, rebates, and grants to shore up manufacturing capabilities through various key sectors.
- DOE provides access to smart manufacturing tools and resources to help small and medium-sized domestic manufacturers secure and improve their operations and efficiency.
- The Domestic Production Act is being employed as a tool to accelerate domestic manufacturing and deployment of key energy technologies, starting with the acceleration of domestic Heat Pump and heat pump component manufacturing nationwide.
- DOE is targeting investments this decade to set the nation on a course to a modernized and upgraded energy system that leads the world.
- DOE is securing and sustaining American clean energy supply chains by diversifying and expanding the supply of critical materials, developing alternatives and substitutes to those materials, and keeping those materials in the supply chain.
- DOE is prioritizing materials and products circularity through re-use, recycling, and re-manufacturing to reduce environmental impacts and secure manufacturing supply chains while uplifting communities and creating high-paying jobs.
- DOE is building the nation’s capabilities and capacity to manufacture current and next generation energy technologies by investing in entrepreneurs and small and medium-sized manufacturers.
- Through government-enabled and private-led funding opportunities, DOE is investing in domestic manufacturing to secure the nation’s energy infrastructure and eliminate supply chain vulnerabilities.
- Every dollar DOE spends on these investments must catalyze many times that amount in private sector investment.
- To help facilitate, expedite, and streamline non-federal funding required by companies, DOE launched the Manufacturing Capital Connector platform to connect companies applying to DOE-administered manufacturing programs, like the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C) Program, to capital providers seeking high-quality projects.
- These investments complement the extraordinary funding in the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) and Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) and enable DOE to more fully unleash the benefits of new programs.
- Nearly 16 million jobs have been created since 2021, with hundreds of thousands of those jobs in clean energy manufacturing.
- Clean energy manufacturing roles include heavy equipment operators, autoworkers, pipe fitters, and linemen.
- DOE uses market, economic, and supply chain security-related technical analysis, collaborating with experts through its Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains Office to identify gaps and growth opportunities across the nation's energy supply chains, from raw materials to processing and manufacturing.
- These analyses inform investment and program implementation.
- DOE is ensuring benefits flow to communities at risk of being left behind, from today’s energy-producing communities facing a major energy transition to communities that have too often borne the downsides of past energy systems without sharing in economic upsides.
- DOE is creating high-quality, accessible, and career-track jobs with fair wages and benefits and the free and fair choice to collectively bargain and join a union.
- DOE is prioritizing domestic manufacturing needs and restoring U.S. manufacturing growth and global competitiveness across a range of technologies to strengthen solutions for an affordable, reliable, resilient, and secure energy system across America.
