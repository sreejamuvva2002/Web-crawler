# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-21

**Source:** [Robert Bosch GmbH: Bosch creates single unit to oversee software, systems development, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/bosch-creates-single-unit-to-oversee-software-systems-development/77086916)  ·  `page_018051`

## Facts

- Bosch is creating a single unit to oversee software and systems development for vehicles.
- The consolidation of the new division will start in early 2021.
- The new division will combine parts of Bosch's automotive electronics, chassis, and powertrain divisions with its car multimedia division.
- The consolidated division will have 17,000 employees in more than 20 countries.
- Bosch is described as the world's largest auto parts supplier.
- Bosch is shifting its emphasis from hardware to software as vehicles transform into mobile devices combining transportation with digital services like e-commerce and infotainment.
- The shift toward vehicle software is a trend driven heavily by electric car maker Tesla Inc.
- Bosch board member Harald Kroeger, a former Daimler AG executive and onetime Tesla board member, is spearheading the new division.
- Harald Kroeger stated: "Supplying software from a single source is our response to the enormous challenge of making cars ever more digitalized."
- Bosch is joining automakers such as Tesla, Volkswagen AG, and General Motors Co., along with large suppliers like Aptiv PLC, in consolidating vehicle functions into a single digital "architecture."
- Similar to what Tesla has been doing for more than five years, the consolidated system will oversee computers, control units, and sensors.
- The centralized architecture can be revised more easily and quickly through wireless over-the-air updates.
- Centralized computers and software in vehicles will link functions such as automated driving, advanced driver assistance, digital dashboards, and Internet connectivity.
- Bosch supplies key components to many of the world's automakers, from Tesla to Daimler.
