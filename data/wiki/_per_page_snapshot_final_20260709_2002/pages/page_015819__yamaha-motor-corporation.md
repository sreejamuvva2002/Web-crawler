# Yamaha Motor Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-11

**Source:** [Yamaha to install solar arrays at Kennesaw, Newnan facilities | Boating Industry](https://boatingindustry.com/top-news/2024/04/11/yamaha-to-install-solar-arrays-at-kennesaw-newnan-facilities/)  ·  `page_015819`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Corporation will install solar arrays at the company’s Southeastern Headquarters in Kennesaw, Ga. and Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America (YMMC) in Newnan, Ga. this summer.
- The initiative supports Yamaha’s goal to reduce emissions produced as a direct result of business activities and achieve carbon neutrality within its manufacturing operations and facilities by 2035.
- Yamaha expects conclusion of the solar installations by the end of 2024.
- Yamaha will work with VeloSolar in Atlanta, Ga., for the installation of the solar array panels.
- With more than a decade of experience designing, installing and maintaining commercial solar installations, VeloSolar is one of Georgia’s largest solar installation companies.
- The Kennesaw solar array will supply about 60 percent of the facility’s electricity needs.
- At the Newnan facility we anticipate avoiding roughly 13,600 tons of carbon dioxide over the life of the array – that’s equivalent to eliminating approximately 14,930,000 pounds of coal emissions.
- These panels are in addition to the already existing solar arrays at YMMC.
- The solar projects in Georgia also align with Yamaha’s participation in the DrawdownGeorgia Business Compact.
- A statewide initiative facilitated by the Ray C. Anderson Center for Sustainable Business at the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech), the Drawdown Georgia Business Compact is a business-focused collaborative initiative focused on galvanizing climate action in Georgia.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President and CEO, Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA, stated that the installation represents a substantial step forward in reducing carbon emissions and that the company will continue to identify emission-reducing opportunities as it moves toward 2035.
