# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-04

**Source:** [Rivian plans Georgia plant 50% larger - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2026/05/04/rivian-plans-georgia-plant-50-larger/)  ·  `page_012735`

## Facts

- Rivian is a California-based company and a partner of Volkswagen.
- Rivian already operates a vehicle plant in Normal, Illinois, producing the electric delivery van EDV, R1 models, and the mid-sized electric SUV R2.
- The Illinois facility's current total capacity is 215,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian has long planned to build a second vehicle plant in Georgia near Atlanta.
- The groundbreaking ceremony for the Georgia plant took place in September.
- Work at the Stanton Springs North site in Georgia has focused solely on site development, infrastructure, and civil engineering, while building construction has not yet begun.
- Rivian revised its plans to increase the Georgia factory's annual capacity to 300,000 vehicles, up from the previously planned 200,000.
- The expansion is funded by a $4.5 billion loan from the US Department of Energy.
- The loan comprises a credit sum of $4.006 billion and $494 million in capitalized interest.
- The DOE loan will only need to be repaid after the construction phase.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated, “We’re very excited to partner with the U.S. Department of Energy to grow our manufacturing footprint in Georgia.”
- Scaringe noted that the R2 dramatically expands their market opportunity and that thousands of dedicated people will soon work in the Georgia plant to scale American manufacturing and ensure US leadership in innovation and technology.
- The new Georgia plant will produce the R2 and the upcoming compact SUV R3.
- The R3 is expected to be more affordable than the R2 and will be exported.
- Rivian plans to manufacture 50,000 robotaxis for its partner and investor Uber at the Georgia plant.
- Production of these models at the Georgia plant is scheduled to begin at the end of 2028.
- In the first quarter of 2026, Rivian produced 10,236 vehicles and made 10,365 customer deliveries.
- Rivian's revenue in Q1 2026 increased by 11% to $1.38 billion.
- Automotive segment revenue declined by 2% to $908 million due to a $100 million drop in regulatory credit sales and a higher proportion of commercial vehicles lowering revenue per unit delivered.
- Vehicle deliveries rose by 20% in Q1 2026.
- The software and services segment grew 49% to $473 million, driven by the development partnership with Volkswagen following successful winter tests of its series-oriented zonal architecture.
- Volkswagen invested an additional $1 billion in Rivian as equity.
- VW's stake in Rivian increased from 12.3% to around 16%, making it likely the largest shareholder ahead of Amazon.
- Rivian reported a Q1 2026 operating loss of $881 million, compared to $655 million in the prior year quarter, citing lower gross margins and higher operating costs.
- Rivian's Q1 2026 net loss was $416 million, a 23% reduction from $541 million in the previous year's quarter.
