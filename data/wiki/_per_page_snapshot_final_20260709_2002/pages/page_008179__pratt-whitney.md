# Pratt & Whitney

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [Pratt & Whitney Invests $200M To Expand Georgia Manufacturing - AirPro News](https://airpronews.com/2026/02/28/pratt-whitney-invests-200m-to-expand-georgia-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_008179`

## Facts

- Pratt & Whitney commits $200 million to add a seventh forging press in Columbus, Georgia.
- The investment increases production for key commercial and military engines.
- Pratt & Whitney is an RTX business.
- An official company press release regarding the investment was issued on February 24, 2026.
- The investment funds the addition of a seventh isothermal forging press at the Columbus Forge facility.
- The expansion aims to increase production of critical engine components for commercial aircraft and military programs.
- The new infrastructure aims to boost output of rotating compressor and turbine disks by 30%.
- These components are essential for Geared Turbofan (GTF) engines used in commercial aviation.
- These components are also essential for the F135 engine that powers the F-35 Lightning II fighter jet.
- The company expects the new forging press to become operational by 2028.
- The Columbus Forge facility plays a central role in Pratt & Whitney’s supply chain.
- The facility produces high-performance parts that must withstand extreme temperatures and stresses.
- Pratt & Whitney President Shane Eddy emphasized the strategic necessity of the expansion.
- Shane Eddy stated: “This latest investment will increase output of critical parts for our growing military and commercial engine programs and underscores our commitment to supporting customers worldwide.”
- The project is expected to initially create 15 new jobs.
- Further hiring is anticipated as operations scale up.
- Pratt & Whitney has invested more than $1 billion in its Columbus operations since 2008.
- The Columbus campus currently employs approximately 2,600 workers.
- RTX reports the facility recently completed a $70 million expansion of its GTF Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul (MRO) center.
- The MRO expansion added 81,000 square feet to the facility.
- The MRO expansion increased annual capacity by more than 25%.
- Pratt & Whitney aims to streamline logistics by locating new manufacturing and aftermarket services on the same campus.
- The expanded capabilities support the growing global fleet of GTF-powered aircraft and F-35 fighters.
