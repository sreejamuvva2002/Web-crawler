# Yamaha Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Co. relocates U.S. headquarters to Georgia after 50 years in California](https://www.walb.com/2026/03/10/yamaha-motor-co-relocates-us-headquarters-georgia-after-50-years-california/)  ·  `page_015801`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Company is relocating its U.S. headquarters to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The company produces motorcycles, personal watercraft, various marine products, ATVs and other motorized products.
- Yamaha currently employs 2,000 Georgians at various plants in Newnan and Kennesaw.
- The company stated it is “undertaking structural reforms aimed at improving the profitability of its U.S. operations in response to cost increases resulting from U.S. tariffs and changes in the market environment.”
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented on the relocation move on Tuesday, March 10.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “After many years of great partnership, we are honored and proud to welcome Yamaha’s American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business. This is another loud and clear testament to what we offer job creators from around the world.”
- The relocation process will start in 2026 and continue into 2028.
- Yamaha first began manufacturing in Georgia in 1986.
- In 1999, the company relocated its marine division to Kennesaw.
- The Kennesaw plant was expanded in 2023.
- In 2019, Yamaha moved its motorsports division to Georgia.
- Yamaha’s U.S. headquarters had previously been based in Cypress, California for 50 years.
- As part of the relocation, the company will sell its fixed assets in California, including land, offices and warehouses.
