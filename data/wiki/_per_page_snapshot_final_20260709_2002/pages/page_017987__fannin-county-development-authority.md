# Fannin County Development Authority

## Facts

- Fannin County is located in the Blue Ridge Mountains on the Tennessee-North Carolina line, 90 miles north of Atlanta, 1 ½ hours from Chattanooga, 2 ½ hours from Asheville, and 90 minutes from Atlanta Airport.
- The county contains three incorporated cities: Blue Ridge, Morganton, and McCaysville (which shares a downtown with Copperhill, Tenn.).
- Fannin County’s Chamber of Commerce & CVB is more than 1,000 members strong and dedicated to welcoming new businesses to the community.
- Chamber President and CEO Christie Gribble states that the county is a destination that many people desire to live and work.
- The local unemployment rate has been hovering between 2.5 pct. and 2.7 pct., which is well below what economists consider "full employment."
- Fannin County has two technical colleges: North Georgia Technical College in Blairsville and Chattahoochee Tech in Jasper.
- Both technical colleges have economic development programs to assist with training employees and develop curriculum for future staffing needs.
- The technical colleges assist with the State’s nationally recognized Quick Start training program, which is provided as an incentive by the State of Georgia.
- Fannin County Industrial Park Street in Blue Ridge, GA, 30513 offers 4.54 acres of vacant land primarily for industrial use, located 1.5 miles from Appalachian Hwy.
- Erick Youngberg serves as the Executive Director of the Fannin County Development Authority.
- Affordable housing is a priority for the county's Development Authority to support a growing local workforce and students who want to remain in the county.
- The Development Authority has been actively pursuing multiple options for meeting affordable housing needs.
