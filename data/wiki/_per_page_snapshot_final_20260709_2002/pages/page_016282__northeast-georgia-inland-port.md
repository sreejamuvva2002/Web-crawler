# Northeast Georgia Inland Port

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2018-12-03

**Source:** [Georgia announces new inland terminal location - Georgia Ports Authority](https://gaports.com/press-releases/georgia-announces-new-inland-terminal-location/)  ·  `page_016282`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal spoke during the announcement for the Georgia Ports Authority’s new inland terminal on Monday, Dec. 3, 2018, in Gainesville, Ga.
- Cargo moved via the Northeast Georgia Inland Port near Gainesville will reduce Atlanta truck traffic by shifting containers to rail.
- The new inland port is set to open in 2021.
- The terminal will be operated by the Georgia Ports Authority and served by Norfolk Southern.
- On Dec. 3, 2018, Gov. Nathan Deal, representatives of the Georgia Ports Authority, the Greater Hall Chamber, and Norfolk Southern Railroad announced plans to make Hall County the site of the GPA’s next inland port.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port will be situated in the heart of the manufacturing and logistics corridor along Interstate 85.
- The region is important for the production of heavy equipment, food, and forest products.
- Besides serving existing port customers, the terminal will act as an economic development tool drawing new investment to Hall and surrounding counties.
- The Gainesville terminal will handle both import and export containers.
- Norfolk Southern will provide service on a direct rail route to and from the Port of Savannah’s Garden City Terminal.
- Jeff Heller is Vice President Intermodal & Automotive at Norfolk Southern.
- Savannah is a rapidly growing gateway for global commerce, and Hall County and the surrounding region in Northeast Georgia are key areas of expansion in the state.
- Manufacturers and distributors around the globe continue to set their sights on this region for development.
- The GPA’s inland port at Gainesville combined with Norfolk Southern’s rail service will provide crucial links in the supply chains of local industries, consumers, and the rest of the world.
- It will serve as a catalyst for new opportunities for industrial development.
- Improved access to rail will increase logistics options and overall efficiency while reducing congestion on Georgia highways.
- Presently, containers moving by truck travel a 600-mile roundtrip to and from the Port of Savannah.
- When the new rail yard opens, drivers will be able to make shorter trips from area manufacturers and distribution facilities to the Northeast Georgia site.
- Griff Lynch is Executive Director at GPA.
- The Gainesville location is part of the Network Georgia initiative, which provides new and existing port customers with additional logistics options.
- The new rail hub will allow importers and exporters to move loaded containers to the coast with greater efficiency.
- It will provide a ready source of empty containers for Georgia exports.
- Jimmy Allgood is GPA Board Chairman.
- The new choice in cargo handling will transform the local rail market and help port customers optimize their supply chains.
- Inland terminals are bringing ports and producers closer together, providing new economic opportunities.
- Georgians will benefit from increased employment options as more companies expand or locate here.
- Georgia Ports already support more than 440,000 jobs across every corner of the state.
- The terminal is located in the Gateway Industrial Centre on GA 365.
- It will be a new 104-acre terminal providing logistics solutions for customers across Northeast Georgia.
- The facility’s services will draw from the 1.5-million population in Hall, Gwinnett, and surrounding counties.
- Tim Evans is vice president of economic development for the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce.
- The new inland port terminal will shorten the supply chain for many manufacturers, processors, and distributors in the region.
- Accessing the container port by rail can save time and money because rail deliveries to and from the Port of Savannah can shorten truck delivery times from approximately seven hours to less than 30 minutes.
- Direct access to the Port of Savannah, the fastest growing container port in the U.S., is an innovation in logistics infrastructure that provides many existing businesses a competitive advantage.
- Auto Metal Direct (AMD), a worldwide distributor of auto body panels and trim for classic cars and trucks, announced its intent to open a new 318,000 square-foot distribution and fulfillment center.
- Construction will soon begin on the new home for AMD in the Gateway Industrial Centre.
- Upon completion, the $15 million development will bring 40 jobs to Hall County.
- Mark Headrick is President of AMD.
- Hundreds of containers each year are received by AMD, so GPA services are essential for the maintenance and growth of their business.
- The proximity of the new inland port was an influencing factor in the eventual location of their building.
- Pat Wilson is Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner.
- The GPA continues to be a fantastic economic development partner and catalyst for growth in the state.
- Ports have positioned Georgia as a global leader in terms of logistics infrastructure.
- Ports have been a critical piece of the puzzle in locating new projects and existing industry expansions across the state.
- AMD expansion is a testament to GPA efforts and will further bolster the strength of the workforce in this community.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port is the second Network Georgia-related announcement this year.
- GPA held a grand opening for the Appalachian Regional Port on Aug. 22 just north of Chatsworth, Ga., on a 42-acre site in Murray County.
- The Hall County inland terminal is scheduled to be complete in 2021.
- At full build-out, it will have the capacity to handle up to 150,000 containers per year.
- Phil Sutton is Vice President Administration at Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation.
- GPA has supported Kubota for many years, helping to find solutions to reduce lead times for both in- and outbound shipments.
- Using the Port of Savannah provides Kubota a great cost alternative to West Coast ports.
- Kubota anticipates several levels of potential cost savings with the new inland port, including reduced costs for chassis and container fees and reduced FTZ administrative fees.
- Kubota expects a reduction in lead times due to greater availability of empty containers and shorter transit times to and from the inland port.
- There is huge potential to significantly reduce reliance on OTR motor carriers and reduce lead time for inbound parts and outbound finished goods.
- Mike Giles is President of the Georgia Poultry Federation.
- For many years, Georgia poultry producers have reached customers all over the world through the Port of Savannah, which currently ranks as the number one export facility for poultry products in the nation.
- Because poultry producers are relatively close to the port, the Georgia poultry industry enjoys a competitive advantage when delivering chicken products to overseas customers efficiently and cost-effectively.
- GPA has invested in infrastructure improving service for poultry shipments through Savannah, and the new inland rail yard continues that investment in future connectivity between the poultry industry and customers worldwide.
- Arrik Williams is Business Development Manager at Tatsumi Intermodal USA.
- The construction of the inland terminal near their facility would be an immediate enhancement for current and potential new operations.
- Tatsumi Intermodal USA’s business model is built around appointing container equipment and negotiating trucking rates between Hall County, Atlanta, Savannah, and Charleston.
- Being able to look to Gainesville for these applications will allow better rate negotiation while adding transloading services for customers, simultaneously reducing tractor trailers on interstates.
- Brandon Loftus is Director of Operations at ZF Gainesville, LLC.
- Continued development of the supply chain for ZF’s business in Gainesville is vital to future growth plans.
- ZF utilizes the port regularly for import and export and supports the Gainesville community and State of Georgia which continually supports industry and manufacturing through infrastructure improvements.
- This activity should help streamline logistics and support ZF’s assembly and manufacturing processes.
- Larry L. Enterline is Chief Executive Officer at FOX Factory.
- FOX Factory factored the favorable business climate into their decision to expand Powered Vehicles Group’s operations in Georgia.
- Benjamin Sippl is Vice President at Mugele Group.
- The terminal will benefit nearby industry and especially local trucking companies.
- Due to trucker availability challenges, a rail facility carrying material from the coast will make it easier for manufacturers to get material delivered to assembly lines on time and at a more economical cost.
- Rail access could make Mugele more attractive by offering consolidation warehouses of goods nearby a port that can be picked, packed, and shipped to the assembly line on JIT basis.
- This reduces exposure to risks during transportation and offers manufacturers flexibility with their own in-house storage capacity.
- Georgia’s deepwater ports and inland barge terminals support more than 439,000 jobs throughout the state annually.
- They contribute $25 billion in income, $106 billion in revenue, and $2.9 billion in state and local taxes to Georgia’s economy.
- The Port of Savannah handled 8.5 percent of U.S. containerized cargo volume and 10 percent of all U.S. containerized exports in FY2017.
