# Seohan Auto Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-11

**Source:** [Hyundai EV plant lures new parts supplier to Savannah area | Columbus Ledger-Enquirer](https://www.ledger-enquirer.com/news/business/article274200895.html)  ·  `page_000392`

## Facts

- The Hyundai electric vehicles plant being built near Savannah in Bryan County has attracted another automotive parts supplier.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the attraction of the new supplier on Tuesday.
- Seohan Auto Georgia will invest more than $72 million in a new manufacturing facility.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in nearby Liberty County.
- The facility will create at least 180 jobs.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the Hyundai Metaplant is and will continue to be an economic driver for the entire region.
- Kemp noted the plant is creating more jobs for hardworking Georgians in communities like Liberty County.
- Kemp welcomed Seohan as the latest supplier in a growing list to invest in Georgia.
- Kemp expressed anticipation for the continued growth of this fast-emerging sector.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is a Tier-1 automotive parts supplier for Hyundai Motor Group America.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is based in South Korea.
- The company has production facilities around the world, including multiple facilities in the United States.
- Jung Kee Koo, Seohan Auto Georgia’s CEO, stated that sustained growth of the EV market over the past few years suggests accelerated changes to the automotive market are inevitable.
- Koo stated that Seohan believes Georgia will be the center of the EV industry.
- Koo noted Georgia will be a new frontier for Seohan’s future with limitless opportunities and potential.
- Seohan’s new facility in Georgia is scheduled to begin production late next year.
- The new manufacturing facility will produce H/shafts, axles, and brake systems.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America broke ground on the Bryan County plant last October.
- The Hyundai plant is projected to provide $5.5 billion in direct economic impact to the coastal region.
- The economic impact of offsite suppliers announced to date has reached nearly $2 billion.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked in partnership with the Liberty County Industrial Development Authority, the Georgia Ports Authority, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program to land the Seohan project.
