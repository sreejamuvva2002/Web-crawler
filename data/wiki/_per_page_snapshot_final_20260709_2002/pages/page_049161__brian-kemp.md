# Brian Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-01

**Source:** [Brian Kemp Announces Record-Breaking Economic Development · Dome Politics](https://domepolitics.com/2025/09/governor-brian-kemp-announces-record-breaking-economic-development-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_049161`

## Facts

- In collaboration with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), Governor Brian Kemp announced steady growth in business recruitment and expansion for Fiscal Year (FY) 2025.
- Between July 1, 2024, and June 30, 2025, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 423 facility expansions and new locations.
- These commitments represent more than $26.3 billion in investment, a new record.
- The commitments include 23,200 new private-sector jobs over the next few years.
- In FY25, 77% of all expansions and new locations occurred in communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta remains one of the Southeast’s top business hubs.
- Companies like TriNet, Duracell, and Mercedes-Benz established or expanded key corporate functions in the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Georgia attracted more than 6,500 new jobs from international companies expanding in the U.S.
- The Republic of Korea, Japan, and Canada were top sources of international investments.
- International companies expanding or locating in Georgia are committed to more than $3 billion in investment.
- 64% of international company investments are for expansions within the state.
- Notable examples include Irving Tissue’s expansion in Macon, GreenBox Systems’ new location in Butts County, and KISS USA’s expansion in Bryan County.
- Kemp attributed these accomplishments to Georgia’s reputation as the ‘No. 1 state’ for business in the country.
- Kemp expects more growth like this in the future.
- Lt. Gov. Burt Jones applauded the record-breaking announcement and expressed optimism regarding the future of Georgia’s workforce.
- Jones stated that Georgia continues to invest in workforce programs to ensure a steady talent pipeline with existing industry partners.
- Workforce program partners include the University System of Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia, College Career Academies, and other stakeholders.
- These investments and initiatives aim to ensure a workforce that is growing and able to meet skillsets for jobs of today and tomorrow.
