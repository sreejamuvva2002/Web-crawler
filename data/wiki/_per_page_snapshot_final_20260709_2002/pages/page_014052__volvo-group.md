# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-25

**Source:** [Volvo Vows To Make Hydrogen Fuel Cell Construction Machines](https://cleantechnica.com/2021/06/25/volvo-vows-to-make-hydrogen-fuel-cell-construction-equipment-a-thing-cleantechnica-interview/)  ·  `page_014052`

## Facts

- Volvo Group is pursuing zero emission heavy duty construction machines and trucks through hydrogen fuel cell technology.
- Its subsidiary Volvo Construction Equipment (Volvo CE) is establishing a new dedicated fuel cell test lab to accelerate R&D.
- Volvo CTO Thomas Bitter stated that hydrogen fuel cells are a key enabler for sustainable solutions in heavier construction machines, supporting the company's Science-Based Targets and ambition to be 100% fossil free by 2040.
- Battery electric powertrains are viewed as ideal for urban construction, shorter distances, and lower loads, while hydrogen fuel cells will complement them for larger machines requiring long haul, long distances, and short refueling stops.
- Volvo emphasizes a life-cycle approach to hydrogen production, prioritizing green hydrogen from renewable energy over natural gas sourced hydrogen to reduce carbon footprint.
- The new fuel cell test lab is located at the Volvo Construction Equipment Technical Center in Eskilstuna, Sweden.
- This facility marks Volvo Group's first dedicated R&D center for fuel cell technology and its first site to test complete fuel cell units.
- Volvo CE positions hydrogen fuel cells as one component of a three-pronged strategy alongside EV batteries and sustainable ICE fuels to achieve carbon neutrality by 2040.
- Cellcentric, a joint venture between Daimler Truck AG and the Volvo Group, will accelerate the development, production, and commercialization of fuel cell systems for long-haul trucking and construction equipment.
- Cellcentric aims to become a leading global manufacturer of fuel cells for mobile and stationary applications, including clean powertrains for commercial vehicles and emergency power for data centers, targeting climate-neutral transportation by 2050.
- Volvo Trucks USA delivered two battery-electric VNR Electric trucks equipped with electric refrigerator units from Advanced Energy Machines to grocery chain Albertsons Companies at its distribution center in Irvine, California.
- The delivery occurred on May 28, 2021, at a LEED-certified Albertsons store in Irvine, California, marking a step toward the company's sustainability goals.
- Albertsons operates 1,400 Class 8 trucks nationwide, all certified under the EPA SmartWay program for high transportation sustainability and efficiency standards.
- The Southern California Volvo Trucks fleet covers 335 stores from the Central Coast to the California-Mexico border.
- Tim Burke, Albertsons vice president of transportation, noted the electric trucks will improve local air quality and provide a quieter working environment for drivers and staff.
