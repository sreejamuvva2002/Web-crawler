# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Governor Deal Announces New Porsche Location | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-governor-deal-announces-new-porsche-location-5145)  ·  `page_048911`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal, Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed, and Detlev von Platen, President and CEO of Porsche Cars North America, announced that the company will build a new headquarters in Georgia.
- The project will create approximately 100 jobs over the next three years.
- The investment totals up to $100 million.
- The complex will be located in the “Aerotropolis Atlanta” development site in Hapeville, adjacent to the International Terminal under construction at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The facility will eventually house a workforce of 400 employees.
- The new headquarters will include modern office facilities, Porsche’s Technical Service and Training Center, and a Customer Experience Center featuring a test track facility.
- Groundbreaking is planned for fall of this year (2013), with the move-in date scheduled for the second half of 2013.
- The headquarters will house PCNA, Porsche Financial Services, Inc., and Porsche Business Services, Inc., relocating them from Chicago.
- Georgia Quick Start, the state’s top-ranked workforce training program, has been offered to design and implement a customized training program for the new and existing workforce.
- Project partners include the Atlanta Development Authority, the Fulton County Development Authority, the city of Hapeville, Georgia Power, Jacoby Development, and Cushman Wakefield.
- The site is the former Hapeville Ford Assembly Plant in South Fulton County.
- PCNA sold over 25,000 cars in 2010, finishing 33 percent ahead of the previous year.
- PCNA will support continued success with a lineup including iconic sports cars, a new generation of Cayenne SUVs, and the Panamera Gran Turismo.
- PCNA was established in 1984 as a wholly owned subsidiary of Stuttgart, Germany-based Porsche AG.
- PCNA currently employs approximately 220 people who provide parts, service, marketing, and training for 196 dealers.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that extending Southern hospitality to the Porsche team adds an incredible new destination to Georgia’s list of must-see places.
- Detlev von Platen said the new American headquarters will make the organization stronger and demonstrates a commitment to remain a vital part of the Atlanta region.
- Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed welcomed the Porsche team to the innovative Aerotropolis complex, calling it a testament to the city's vitality.
- Hapeville Mayor Alan Hallman expressed excitement about the project creating synergy for the entire Aerotropolis and putting South Fulton County on the map.
- Scott Condra, Senior Vice President of Development for Jacoby Development, noted that attracting Porsche demonstrates Atlanta is ready for airport-adjacent development and redevelopment.
- Porsche boasts a racing heritage with some 30,000 motorsport wins to date.
