# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Porsche Breaks Ground on N. American HQ for 400 Employees, 100 New | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/automotive/news/ga-porsche-breaks-ground-n-american-hq-400-7205)  ·  `page_048676`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal, Porsche President and Chief Executive Officer Detlev von Platen, and numerous dignitaries broke ground for Porsche North America’s new headquarters at Aerotropolis Atlanta.
- The 26.4-acre complex will employ 400 workers and create 100 jobs.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Porsche’s new headquarters is a terrific asset to Georgia’s economy and moves the state closer to becoming the No. 1 state in the nation in which to do business.
- Deal expressed confidence that One Porsche Drive will become a renowned address standing for vision, innovation, success, and one of the greatest driving experiences in the world for millions of travelers arriving in the state each year.
- Porsche’s expansion and new location were announced in May 2011.
- The project will consolidate U.S.-based Porsche employees from Porsche Cars North America as well as the company’s financial, business, and consulting arms.
- The site will encompass not only the company’s U.S. headquarters but also the Porsche Technical Training Center and the Porsche Customer and Driving Experience Center.
- The Porsche Customer and Driving Experience Center will feature a 1.6-mile test track and handling circuit.
- The company anticipates beginning operations in the first quarter of 2014.
- Porsche President and Chief Executive Officer Detlev von Platen stated that the world-class facility underscores the company's commitment to customers and dealers in the United States, which remains the single largest market for Porsche vehicles.
- Von Platen noted that today’s ceremony symbolized Porsche putting down permanent roots in Atlanta, a city it has proudly called home since 1998.
- The company chose Aerotropolis Atlanta after conducting a global search for the best location to grow and showcase its brand.
- Proximity to the customer base represented by Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport was a key factor in Porsche’s selection of the Aerotropolis site.
- The Aerotropolis site is located on the location of a former Ford Motor Company plant.
- Aerotropolis is owned and developed by Jacoby Development.
- Partnering organizations assisting with the expansion include the Georgia Department of Economic Development, City of Atlanta, City of Hapeville, InvestAtlanta, Fulton County, and Clayton County.
- Blair Lewis, director of the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Corporate Solutions team, led the project team that assisted Porsche with its location selection.
- Chris Cummiskey, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that effective collaborations help attract and keep company headquarters in Georgia.
- Cummiskey noted that besides workforce and logistics capabilities, the ability of community and state partners to team up and meet companies’ needs makes Georgia and Atlanta a mecca for corporate headquarters.
- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (PCNA) is based in Atlanta and serves as the exclusive U.S. importer of Porsche sports cars, the Cayenne SUV, and Panamera sports sedan.
- PCNA was established in 1984 and is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Porsche AG, which is headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany.
- PCNA employs approximately 220 people who provide parts, service, marketing, and training for 192 dealers.
- The company works to provide Porsche customers with a best-in-class experience that aligns with the brand’s 63-year history and leadership in vehicle performance, safety, and efficiency.
- Porsche boasts a racing heritage that includes some 30,000 motorsport wins to date.
