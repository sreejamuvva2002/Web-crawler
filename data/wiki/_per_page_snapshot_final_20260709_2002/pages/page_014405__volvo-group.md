# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Volvo Group expects North America weakness through 2026 amid freight slump - SRN News](https://srnnews.com/truckmaker-volvo-meets-operating-profit-forecast-amid-weaker-americas-demand/)  ·  `page_014405`

## Facts

- Swedish truckmaker Volvo expects trucking demand in North America to remain under pressure into next year.
- Weak freight activity and U.S. President Donald Trump’s tariffs weigh on the North American market.
- The Gothenburg-based group also makes construction equipment and engines.
- Volvo lowered its forecast for the North American truck market while keeping its European view unchanged.
- Volvo expects heavy truck deliveries of around 265,000 in North America this year, 10,000 fewer than previously envisioned.
- Volvo expects to deliver some 290,000 vehicles in Europe.
- The company’s shares fell 8% by 0935 GMT, touching their lowest price in six months.
- The North American long-haul freight market remains in recession, with lower freight volumes and prices.
- Customers are holding back orders amid uncertainty over new emissions rules and tariffs.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt stated transport buyers are in a wait-and-see mode as uncertainty clouds market conditions.
- Europe truck demand is largely replacement-driven, supported by stable fleet utilisation.
- Equity analysts from Jefferies see a potential pickup later in 2026 due to normalized inventory levels and back-end-loaded demand.
- Volvo’s adjusted operating profit fell around 17% to 11.7 billion Swedish crowns ($1.2 billion) in the third quarter, matching the average forecast in an LSEG poll of analysts.
- Trump’s move to slap a 25% tariff on imported heavy trucks has deepened trade uncertainty across North America.
- The USMCA trade agreement still shields most manufacturers from fresh duties.
- Volvo Group is best positioned among European truckmakers to weather trade barriers because it builds all trucks destined for the U.S. market domestically, along with most of its components.
- Volvo runs 16 manufacturing and remanufacturing sites across the U.S., Canada and Mexico.
- Volvo makes vehicles under brands such as Mack Trucks and Renault as well as its own name.
