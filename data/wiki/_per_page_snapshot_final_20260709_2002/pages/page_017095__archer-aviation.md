# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-22

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Selects Georgia Site for Manufacturing Facility | Aviation Maintenance Magazine](https://avm-mag.com/archer-aviation-selects-georgia-site-for-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_017095`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation announced plans to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The facility site is located adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The location is 40 minutes east of Atlanta.
- Archer plans to initially build a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site.
- The initial facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility can be expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential manufacturing sites across the United States before selecting Georgia.
- Key site selection considerations included local labor market talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer will receive an incentive package that includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Archer anticipates that financing for the project will be provided by Synovus.
- Synovus is the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer's CEO, stated that after many months of discussion and evaluation, they selected Georgia for their manufacturing facility.
- Adam Goldstein noted that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Adam Goldstein stated that their eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs.
- Adam Goldstein expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp, Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, Frank Turner, their teams, and his colleagues at Archer for pioneering innovative aviation technologies.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia has made nurturing innovation a priority while working with businesses to define the future.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Governor Kemp highlighted that Archer can take advantage of Georgia's signature workforce training program, Quick Start, to help fill over 1,000 long-term jobs in Covington.
