# Hapag-Lloyd

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2021-12

**Source:** [Multinational Liner Shipping Company Hapag-Lloyd Selects Georgia for North American Headquarters - Valdosta CEO](https://valdostaceo.com/news/2021/12/multinational-liner-shipping-company-hapag-lloyd-selects-georgia-north-american-headquarters/)  ·  `page_012269`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development announced on Tuesday, December 14th, 2021, that German multinational liner shipping company Hapag-Lloyd will invest $18 million in its new North American headquarters.
- The new North American headquarters will be located in Dunwoody, Georgia.
- The investment and new headquarters will create an additional 250 jobs in the metro Atlanta region.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that logistics is an innate part of Georgia’s economic prowess, with the state’s economy connected to the movement of goods and people via sea, rail, air, and road.
- Hapag-Lloyd is based in Hamburg, Germany, and operates a fleet of over 250 modern container ships with a total transport capacity of 1.8 million TEUs.
- Hapag-Lloyd currently employs more than 500 Georgians across the state.
- Once the North American headquarters project is complete, Hapag-Lloyd will employ more than 750 people in Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson met with company leadership in Hamburg during a November economic development mission to Germany.
- Uffe Ostergaard, President of Hapag-Lloyd’s North American region, stated the new headquarters will bring the team together under one roof to better serve customers and expand career pathways for staff.
- The company will open its new 125,000-square-foot headquarters at Three Ravinia in Dunwoody by summer 2022.
- Hapag-Lloyd will be hiring for careers in finance, human resources, IT services, and more.
- Dunwoody Mayor Lynn Deutsch welcomed the company to Dunwoody and appreciated the longstanding partnership with the State of Georgia and DeKalb County as they continue to grow the Dunwoody Perimeter Market.
- Dorian DeBarr, President of Decide DeKalb, stated that DeKalb has a skilled workforce, is easily accessible to major highways and transportation hubs, and is a business-friendly community for those looking to settle or expand.
- GDEcD’s Director of Corporate Solutions and Cybersecurity in Global Commerce Kristi Brigman represented the Department on this project.
- The project was completed in partnership with GDEcD’s Europe office, Decide DeKalb, the Dunwoody Development Authority, and Georgia Power.
