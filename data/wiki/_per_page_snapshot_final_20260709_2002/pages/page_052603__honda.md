# Honda

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Honda EV production begins to take form with new battery deal](https://www.teslarati.com/honda-ascend-elements-ev-production-us-new-battery-deal/)  ·  `page_052603`

## Facts

- Honda has partnered with Ascend Elements to recycle lithium-ion batteries and recuperate the elements into new cells.
- Honda is working towards selling its first electric vehicle, the Honda Prologue, in 2024.
- Honda's EV production and manufacturing capabilities are taking form, including battery and drivetrain production facilities and battery cell manufacturing plants.
- The new battery recycling and recuperation deal is with a recycler in Massachusetts.
- Honda has been working with Ascend Elements to recycle lithium-ion batteries since 2021.
- Honda is hoping to start using the materials the recycler takes from used batteries.
- Honda is focused on using recycled lithium, nickel, and cobalt in new battery cells.
- The deal was announced today to help Honda achieve a perfectly circular supply chain and achieve “zero environmental impact” by 2050.
- Honda did not specify the amount of material it would be receiving from the recycler.
- Honda did not specify when it would be able to start using the recycled materials.
- Honda has established a series of battery production deals, the first with LG Energy Solutions.
- Honda and LG Energy Solutions are launching a new production facility in Ohio.
- Honda's second supply deal is with GS Yuasa.
- The second supply deal will likely focus on meeting demand in Asia.
- Honda continues to develop its electric drivetrain capabilities at its production facilities scattered throughout America.
- Honda currently produces electric drivetrains for use in hybrid applications.
- Honda is working with various manufacturers to develop new electric vehicles.
- The Honda Prologue comes from Honda’s partnership with GM.
- Honda and GM have stated they will likely continue to cooperate “to produce affordable EVs.”
- Honda is working with Sony in its Afeela joint venture.
- The Afeela joint venture is set to bring its first EV to market in 2026.
- Honda’s motorcycle division plans to introduce three new electric motorcycles to the United States and Europe.
- Honda’s motorcycle division plans to introduce a larger number of smaller models and scooters to Asia.
