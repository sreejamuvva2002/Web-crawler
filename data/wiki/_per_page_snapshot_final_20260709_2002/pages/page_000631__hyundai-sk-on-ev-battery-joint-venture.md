# Hyundai-SK On EV Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai and SK On Choose Bartow County for Their EV Battery Plant - autoevolution](https://www.autoevolution.com/news/hyundai-and-sk-on-chose-bartow-county-for-their-ev-battery-plant-214069.html)  ·  `page_000631`

## Facts

- The Inflation Reduction Act is starting to move things in the EV and battery markets in the US.
- Primarily Korean companies were caught off-guard by the IRA announcement and are now in a dire situation.
- Hyundai and Kia do not currently make electric vehicles in the US and will not for a while.
- The Kia EV9 will be the first US-made EV from Hyundai, coming in 2024 from the West Point, Georgia factory that also builds the Kia Telluride.
- Hyundai Motor Group intends to expand US production of electric vehicles under the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Hyundai Motor Group chose SK On as its battery cell supplier.
- Hyundai and SK On have established a joint venture to supply battery cells for local US production.
- The Hyundai-SK On joint venture plans to invest $5 billion in a local US battery production facility.
- Battery cells from the Hyundai-SK On joint venture will be assembled into battery packs by Hyundai Mobis and supplied to the Group's production facilities.
- Hyundai and SK On have chosen Bartow County, Georgia as the location for their US battery manufacturing facility.
- The Bartow County facility is located close to Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The annual production capacity of the new facility is initially targeted at 35 GWh.
- The 35 GWh capacity is sufficient to support the production of 300,000 electric vehicles.
- Battery-cell production at the Bartow County facility is scheduled to start in the second half of 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group intends to expand its US footprint with a new EV production facility in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The $5.5 billion EV manufacturing plant in Bryan County will begin production in 2025.
- The Bryan County plant will begin production just in time to use batteries produced at the new Bartow County factory.
- Hyundai's partnership with SK On is not exclusive.
- Hyundai is in talks with LG Energy to establish two additional battery plants in the US, enough to power one million electric vehicles.
- The battery cells supplied by SK On are of the pouch type.
- Hyundai already uses this pouch cell chemistry for the Ioniq 5, Ioniq 6, and Kia EV6.
- Hyundai will utilize the same SK On battery cells in the upcoming Ioniq 7 SUV, Genesis GV70, and Kia EV9.
- With both battery and vehicle production in the US, Hyundai Motor Group's EVs will qualify for the IRA tax credit.
- The IRA tax credit is considered a significant incentive that can help sales tremendously.
