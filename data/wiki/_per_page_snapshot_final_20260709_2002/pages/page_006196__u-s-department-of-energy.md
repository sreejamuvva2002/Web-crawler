# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Technical Targets for Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cell Components | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/fuels/doe-technical-targets-polymer-electrolyte-membrane-fuel-cell-components)  ·  `page_006196`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) published technical targets for polymer electrolyte membrane (PEM) fuel cell components, including membrane electrode assemblies (MEAs), membranes, electrocatalysts, and bipolar plates.
- These targets were developed with input from the U.S. DRIVE Partnership, which includes automotive and energy companies, specifically the Fuel Cell Technical Team.
- The guideline component targets are designed to assist component developers in evaluating progress without testing full systems.
- Additional information on these targets is available in the Fuel Cells section of the Fuel Cell Technologies Office's Multi-Year Research, Development, and Demonstration Plan.
- For MEAs, the 2020 cost target is $14/kWnet (down from $17/kWnet in 2015), projected to high-volume production of 500,000 80-kWnet systems per year.
- The 2020 MEA durability with cycling target is 5,000 hours (up from 2,500 hours in 2015).
- The 2020 MEA start-up/shutdown durability target is 5,000 cycles.
- The 2020 MEA performance at 0.8 V target is 300 mA/cm2 (up from 240 mA/cm2 in 2015).
- The 2020 MEA performance at rated power (150 kPaabs) target is 1,000 mW/cm2 (up from 810 mW/cm2 in 2015).
- The 2020 MEA robustness targets for cold operation, hot operation, and cold transient are all set at 0.7.
- For membranes, the 2020 maximum oxygen cross-over target is 2 mA/cm2 (down from 2.4 mA/cm2 in 2015).
- The 2020 maximum hydrogen cross-over target for membranes is 2 mA/cm2 (up from 1.1 mA/cm2 in 2015).
- The 2020 membrane area specific proton resistance target at maximum operating temperature and water partial pressures from 40–80 kPa is 0.02 ohm cm2.
- The 2020 membrane area specific proton resistance target at 80°C and water partial pressures from 25–45 kPa is 0.02 ohm cm2.
- The 2020 membrane area specific proton resistance target at 30°C and water partial pressures up to 4 kPa is 0.03 ohm cm2.
- The 2020 membrane area specific proton resistance target at -20°C is 0.2 ohm cm2.
- The 2020 membrane maximum operating temperature target is 120°C.
- The 2020 membrane minimum electrical resistance target is 1,000 ohm cm2 (down from >5,600 ohm cm2 in 2015).
- For membranes, the 2020 cost target is $20/m2 (up from $17/m2 in 2015), projected to high-volume production of 500,000 80 kW systems per year.
- The 2020 membrane mechanical durability target is 20,000 cycles until >15 mA/cm2 H2 crossover (down from 23,000 in 2015).
- The 2020 membrane chemical durability target is >500 hours until >15 mA/cm2 crossover or >20% loss in OCV (up from 742 hours in 2015).
- The 2020 combined chemical/mechanical membrane durability target is 20,000 cycles.
- For electrocatalysts, the 2020 platinum group metal total content target is 0.125 g/kW (down from 0.16 in 2015).
- The 2020 PGM total loading target for electrocatalysts is 0.125 mg PGM/cm2 electrode area (down from 0.13 in 2015).
- The 2020 electrocatalyst mass activity target is 0.44 A/mg PGM @ 900 mVIR-free (down from >0.5 in 2015).
- The 2020 loss in initial catalytic activity target for electrocatalysts is <40% mass activity loss (down from 66% in 2015).
- The 2020 loss in performance at 0.8 A/cm2 target for electrocatalysts is <30 mV (down from 13 mV in 2015).
- The 2020 electrocatalyst support stability target is <40% mass activity loss (down from 41% in 2015).
- The 2020 loss in performance at 1.5 A/cm2 target for electrocatalysts is <30 mV (down from 65 mV in 2015).
- The 2020 PGM-free catalyst activity target is >0.044 A/cm2 @ 0.9 VIR-free (up from 0.016 in 2015).
- For bipolar plates, the 2020 cost target is $3/kWnet (down from $7/kWnet in 2015), projected to high-volume production of 500,000 80 kW systems per year.
- The 2020 bipolar plate weight target is 0.4 kg/kWnet (up from <0.4 in 2015).
- The 2020 bipolar plate H2 permeation coefficient target is <1.3x10-14 Std cm3/(sec cm2Pa) @ 80°C, 3 atm, 100% RH.
- The 2020 bipolar plate corrosion (anode) target is <1 µA/cm2 and no active peak.
- The 2020 bipolar plate corrosion (cathode) target is <1 µA/cm2 (down from <0.1 in 2015).
- The 2020 bipolar plate electrical conductivity target is >100 S/cm.
- The 2020 bipolar plate areal specific resistance target is <0.01 ohm cm2 (up from 0.006 in 2015).
- The 2020 bipolar plate flexural strength target is >25 MPa (down from >34 MPa for carbon plate in 2015).
- The 2020 bipolar plate forming elongation target is 40% (up from 20–40% in 2015).
