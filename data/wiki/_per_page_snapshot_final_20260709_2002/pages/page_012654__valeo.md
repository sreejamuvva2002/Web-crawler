# Valeo

**Source:** [Valeo Breaks Ground on $225 Million High-Tech Manufacturing Facility in McAllen, Texas](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/901696254/valeo-breaks-ground-on-225-million-high-tech-manufacturing-facility-in-mcallen-texas)  ·  `page_012654`

## Facts

- Valeo breaks ground on a $225 million high-tech manufacturing facility in McAllen, Texas.
- The project will produce next-generation vehicle computing systems for General Motors.
- The facility will create up to 500 high-wage jobs in South Texas.
- The new facility represents a major expansion of Valeo’s North American footprint.
- The investment is aligned with the company’s Elevate 2028 plan, which focuses on accelerating innovation in electrification, advanced driver assistance systems, and software-defined vehicle architecture.
- The McAllen plant will manufacture central compute systems described by the company as the “brain of the vehicle,” enabling safer, more connected, and more intelligent mobility.
- Jeffrey Shay, Group President of Valeo North America, stated that the investment reflects a commitment to innovation and supporting customers in the transition toward software-defined vehicles.
- Jeffrey Shay noted that McAllen offers the workforce, community, and readiness needed to deliver advanced technology at scale.
- The project marks one of the largest industrial investments in the history of Hidalgo County.
- Local leaders emphasized that the project was secured through coordinated execution, infrastructure readiness, and a business-friendly environment.
- Ike Tawil, McAllen City Manager, called Valeo’s investment transformative for the community and the largest single industrial investment in Hidalgo County history.
- The facility is being developed at the Nearshoring Industrial Park in McAllen by CiL Capital.
- CiL Capital is led by President and CEO Joaquin Spamer.
- The site was designed as a shovel-ready, build-to-suit environment capable of supporting advanced manufacturing requirements, including cleanroom production, specialized power infrastructure, and high-speed data connectivity.
- Joaquin Spamer stated that the park was built to respond to the nearshoring movement and deliver at the speed of business.
- The McAllen Economic Development Corporation worked closely with Valeo and partners to align workforce, infrastructure, and operational requirements.
- Ralph Garcia, Chief Operating Officer of the McAllen Economic Development Corporation, called the project the largest industrial opportunity the region has ever seen.
- McAllen Mayor Javier Villalobos noted the broader economic impact, highlighting the evolution of the local economy from agriculture and services to advanced manufacturing.
- The facility is expected to strengthen North American automotive supply chains through its proximity to Mexico’s manufacturing base and integration into cross-border logistics networks.
- Valeo already maintains a significant presence in the United States with multiple production sites and research and development centers, operating globally in nearly 30 countries.
- The project will support workforce development through partnerships with regional institutions, including the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley and South Texas College.
- These partnerships will create pathways for students into high-skill careers in advanced manufacturing and automotive technology.
- Ralph Garcia later served as Chief Executive Officer of the McAllen Economic Development Corporation and emphasized that McAllen responded quickly to a highly competitive U.S. project, positioning the Rio Grande Valley as a serious contender for advanced manufacturing and nearshoring opportunities.
