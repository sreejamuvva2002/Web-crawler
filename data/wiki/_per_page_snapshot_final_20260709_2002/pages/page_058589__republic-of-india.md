# Republic of India

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-23

**Source:** [India’s selective opening to Chinese power firms reflects pragmatic adjustment to energy needs, but more open approach needed: expert - Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202607/1365078.shtml)  ·  `page_058589`

## Facts

- India has reportedly allowed four Chinese power equipment manufacturers with factories in the country to participate in government tenders for critical power projects.
- The four companies are TBEA Energy, Nanjing Electric India, New Northeast Electric India and Taikai Electric (India).
- The exemption allowing these companies to participate in tenders is valid for two years and should not be treated as a precedent for other companies.
- India's power ministry sought the exemption in January for entities with manufacturing units in India involved in critical power projects.
- The exemption comes as India accelerates the expansion of its transmission network to support rising electricity demand and renewable energy additions.
- Previously, amid bilateral tension, New Delhi required Chinese bidders to register with a government panel and secure political and security clearances before competing for any state contract.
- In January, India was examining broader relaxations on Chinese bidders for government contracts, amid easing of border tensions.
- The latest exemption does not suggest that India has abandoned its broader restrictions on Chinese companies.
- In March, India would offer faster approval for investments involving minority Chinese stakes across a wider range of sectors, including electronics, batteries, rare-earth magnets and processing.
- The faster investment approval applies provided Chinese ownership remains below 10 percent and majority control stays with Indian residents.
- Security concerns related to Chinese investments remain and proposals will continue to undergo scrutiny.
- Excluding Chinese supply chains could increase costs, delay delivery and reduce the efficiency of infrastructure expansion against the backdrop of India's rising infrastructure and energy needs.
- India's coal-fired power output in June rose to its highest level since November 2023, as rising electricity demand kept pressure on the country's power supply system.
- In the first five months of this year, bilateral trade jumped more than 20 percent year-on-year, showing that economic complementarity remains strong despite lingering political frictions.
- Chinese Minister of Commerce Wang Wentao met Indian Minister of Commerce and Industry Piyush Goyal on the sidelines of the WTO's 14th Ministerial Conference in Yaoundé, Cameroon.
- China expressed willingness to work with India to make economic and trade cooperation a "ballast stone" in bilateral relations and welcomed India's participation in events such as the "Shared Market: Export to China" series to expand its exports to China.
- In April, India's PHD Chamber of Commerce and Industry (PHDCCI) renewable energy and EV delegation visited Shanghai and held B2B meetings with Chinese companies.
- Stronger India-China partnerships in renewable energy and EV sectors could help accelerate innovation and reduce technology costs.
