# Bridgestone Bandag

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Abilene, Texas: A Manufacturing Powerhouse Backed by Robust Education Resources | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/texas/abilene-texas-manufacturing-powerhouse-backed-32315)  ·  `page_018303`

## Facts

- Last year, the Development Corporation of Abilene (DCOA) announced a $60 million expansion of Bridgestone Bandag, a Bridgestone Americas Tire Operations (BATO) division.
- Bridgestone Bandag is a global leader of medium and heavy-duty truck tires for North America’s original equipment and replacement markets.
- The company has been an integral part of the Abilene community for more than half a century.
- The expansion adds 50,000 square feet to the existing 200,000-square-foot facility.
- The project is expected to create 25 new jobs and retain 179 full-time employees.
- The expansion will contribute approximately $21 million in taxes to the City of Abilene, Taylor County, and Abilene Independent School District.
- Misty Mayo, President and Chief Executive Officer of the Development Corporation of Abilene, stated that having a company with a long history of manufacturing in Abilene make an additional commitment is a true highlight.
- The DCOA serves as the economic development driver of Abilene, with a mission to attract and sustain industries that support job creation and foster strong business.
- Over the next 10 years, the total estimated direct and indirect economic impact of the expansion is $1.81 billion.
- The $60 million expansion marks the fifth largest project in the DCOA’s history.
- Sam Vinson, DCOA Board Chair, noted that Bridgestone Bandag is a well-established company whose expansion brings immense value to the community.
- Chris Daniel, Executive Director of Bandag Manufacturing at Bridgestone Americas, spoke at the groundbreaking ceremony regarding the decision to grow in Abilene.
- Daniel stated the growth is driven by growing demand for products and the team’s commitment to delivering innovation and superior quality for customers globally.
- The company plays a vital role in the transformation of mobility by building greener retreads that help maximize fuel efficiency for fleets.
- The expansion involves developing an automated rubber mixing and production operation to meet burgeoning demand in the retread tire industry.
- The investment aligns with Bridgestone’s E8 Commitment, a program to achieve carbon neutrality in mobility by 2050.
- The E8 Commitment encompasses eight core values beginning with “E,” including Energy, Ecology, and Efficiency.
- Since its inception in 1957, Bandag has diverted approximately 300 million tires away from landfills.
- The retreading process significantly reduces material emissions, water consumption, and energy use associated with producing new tires.
- The Bandag retreading process has conserved approximately four billion gallons of oil.
- Abilene is located in Taylor County, Texas, directly on I-20, just two and a half hours from the Dallas/Fort Worth metro area.
- Abilene’s metropolitan statistical area covers a three-county region with a population of 179,308.
- The region offers reliable infrastructure, a pro-business environment conducive to economic growth, and progressive business incentives.
- The project taps into Abilene’s skilled manufacturing labor force, bolstered by education initiatives from four universities, one technical college, one community college, and high school training opportunities.
- The DCOA operates a program called NEXTUniversity (NEXTU) that prepares and trains students for degree programs through local independent school districts.
- NEXTU assists young professionals in pursuing careers such as welding, industrial systems, aircraft maintenance, and nursing.
- Through high school and beyond, NEXTU students receive scholarship dollars covering technical classes, books, supplies, and tools.
- With career coach guidance, NEXTU students obtain industry-based certifications and career connections.
- More than 13,000 students are actively being educated in Abilene through the program.
- Primary manufacturing is a focus at local institutions like Texas State Technical College (TSTC) and Cisco College.
