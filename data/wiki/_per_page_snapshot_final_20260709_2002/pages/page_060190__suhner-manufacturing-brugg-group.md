# Suhner Manufacturing / Brugg Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2010-05-17

**Source:** [Brugg Honored](https://wireropeexchange.com/brugg-honored/)  ·  `page_060190`

## Facts

- Suhner Manufacturing, Brugg Cables, and Brugg Wire Rope are located off U.S. 27 south in Rome, Georgia, and ship products worldwide.
- All three companies are under the ownership of Otto Suhner.
- The three companies were honored by Georgia Northwestern Technical College for their nomination in the state Manufacturer of the Year Award competition.
- Pete McDonald, vice president for economic development at GNTC, noted that manufacturing remains the foundation of the American economy and praised the machinery humming at the Suhner facility on Suhner Drive off U.S. 27 South.
- Suhner manufactures flexible shafts used in the power movement of automotive components like car seats and sunroofs.
- Suhner Vice President Guido Broder stated that the company's primary products evolved from speedometer cables to a broader repertoire due to technological changes.
- Suhner relocated to Rome after a Georgia Department of Economic Development Red Carpet Tour more than 34 years prior to the article's publication.
- The company was founded in 1854 by Gottlieb Suhner in Herisau, Switzerland.
- Suhner was the first Swiss company to open a manufacturing plant in Georgia in 1976.
- Suhner currently employs close to 150 people.
- Plant manager Lee Coleman reported that Suhner produces close to 30 million feet of flexible cable annually, amounting to more than 14 million cable shafts of varying sizes.
- The company adapts its systems to meet manufacturer demands for cable systems wrapped in various coatings and fitted with custom connections.
- Suhner's engineering division develops shafts that allow continuous operation across several high-speed ranges.
- Suhner supplies flexible shaft products to automotive industry companies including Johnson Controls, Webasto, and CRH.
- Suhner also supplies the lawn and garden industry, with customers such as Echo, Stihl, and MTD.
- Otto Suhner acquired controlling interest in the Brugg group of companies in 1991.
- Brugg Cables was brought to Rome by Otto Suhner 12 years prior to the article's publication.
- Brugg Wire Rope followed to Rome in 2002.
- Kevin Heling serves as president-general manager of Brugg Wire Rope, located at 1501 Parrish Drive.
- Brugg Wire Rope is an exclusive manufacturer of cables used in the elevator industry.
- Leading North American customers for Brugg Wire Rope include Otis and Schindler elevator companies.
- Brugg Wire Rope recently completed a 14,000 square foot warehouse and engineering addition to its Rome plant.
- Heling indicated there is sufficient land at the facility to accommodate future growth for the Brugg group.
- Brugg Wire Rope employs approximately 25 people and produces more than 12 million feet of elevator cable annually.
- The company's output is primarily sold to stock service companies, where it is cut to length.
- Brugg Wire Rope recently installed a new high-tech tubular stranding machine manufactured in China, with Chinese personnel assisting in Rome for several weeks during installation.
- Heling cited efficiency as a key competitive advantage for the company.
- The manufacturing process involves winding nineteen wires into one strand, then winding eight strands around a sisal core rope.
- Virtually all wire used by Brugg Wire Rope is imported from the Czech Republic.
- The facility receives 10 to 14 heavy 20-foot freight containers monthly via trucking companies.
- In addition to rope systems, Brugg Wire Rope produces new flat belt elevator lift systems.
- These belts are manufactured in Switzerland and China, then imported to Rome for custom cutting and logistics before customer shipment.
- Brugg Cables is located at 25 Anderson Road in Rome and is the newest member of the Swiss family there.
- Karl Zimmermann serves as plant manager for Brugg Cables.
- Brugg Cables manufactures optical ground wire cable used to transmit data at super high speeds across the country.
- The company was founded by Gottleib Suhner in 1896 and is now the largest within the Brugg Group.
- The highly automated Brugg Cables plant employs only 10 to 15 full-time workers, with employees sometimes shared between facilities based on demand.
- Heling emphasized that the Georgia Work Ready program focuses on proper training for hard work.
- Heling stated the company is working harder and smarter.
- Suhner and Brugg were among the first companies in Rome to adopt the Georgia Work Ready program.
- Broder noted that hiring personnel ready for jobs is a priority due to the Work Ready program's influence.
