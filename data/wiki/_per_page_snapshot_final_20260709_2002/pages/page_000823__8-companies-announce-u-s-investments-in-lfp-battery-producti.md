# 8 Companies Announce U.S. Investments in LFP Battery Production

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-25

**Source:** [$84.1 Billion LFP Battery Project Lands in the U.S - MANLY](https://manlybattery.com/84-1-billion-lfp-battery-project-lands-in-the-u-s/?srsltid=AfmBOooYGnlfv4zavdzPKQaW8NfTs0vWAI-gmacRcd1-QuUpqxxdqxiS)  ·  `page_000823`

## Facts

- Chinese power battery companies have secured six spots in the global installation TOP10 list, with a market share exceeding 60%.
- From January to July, lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries significantly led ternary batteries in production and installation volumes, both with percentages exceeding 60%, reaching as high as 68.1% of total installation volume.
- New energy vehicles equipped with LFP batteries are now spread globally, with BYD and Tesla being the most notable among global new energy vehicle giants.
- Car companies from Japan and South Korea, such as Toyota and Hyundai, have begun using LFP batteries.
- Overseas markets are accelerating adoption of LFP battery technologies, with U.S. interest and investment rising due to the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Eight companies announced plans to build LFP battery and material factories in the U.S., with six specifying investment amounts totaling over 841 billion RMB.
- Tesla and Rivian are considering similar investments, with Tesla leaning towards collaborations with Ford and CATL.
- BYD Executive Vice President Li Ke revealed in December that the company is looking to establish a dedicated battery production base in the U.S.
- On February 14, Ford and CATL jointly announced the construction of a lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery factory in Marshall, Michigan.
- CATL will offer construction and operational services and license its battery technology for the Ford project.
- The Ford-CATL factory investment is projected at $3.5 billion (equivalent to ¥255.2 billion RMB).
- The Ford-CATL factory has an initial design capacity of about 35GWh per year.
- The Ford-CATL factory will supply battery packs for approximately 400,000 Ford electric vehicles.
- Ford is considering expanding the Marshall, Michigan factory's capacity.
- On March 24, LG Energy Solution officially declared its intention to invest ₩7.2 trillion in a large battery manufacturing complex in Arizona with a total capacity of 43.3GWh.
- LG Energy Solution's Arizona investment includes ₩4.2 trillion for a cylindrical battery factory in North America with an average annual capacity of 27GWh, with production beginning in 2025.
- LG Energy Solution will use ₩3 trillion (equivalent to ¥163.22 billion RMB) to establish a battery production plant specifically for ESS (Energy Storage Systems).
- The LG Energy Solution ESS plant will be the world's first ESS-only production factory upon completion.
- LG Energy Solution is developing a cobalt-free LFP battery to outperform CATL by 20% in energy density and aims to supply Tesla.
- During an April 28 media event, Guoxuan High-tech Deputy General Manager Chen Ruilin revealed plans to establish two U.S. production bases, one for batteries and the other for battery materials.
- Reports from last October indicated Gotion, a subsidiary of Guoxuan, received approval to build a battery materials factory near Big Rapids, Michigan with an estimated investment of $2.364 billion.
- In June, Gotion received federal approval to continue its Michigan construction.
- The location for Guoxuan's battery factory will be determined soon.
- In June 2022, Guoxuan announced a 60GWh production line equipment tender that included locations in the U.S.
- On December 6, 2022, American Battery Factory (ABF) announced its plan to construct an LFP super-factory in Tucson, Arizona with an estimated investment of $1.2 billion (¥87.49 billion RMB).
- In November 2022, Norwegian battery startup FREYR Battery, listed in New York, announced the purchase of land in Coweta County, Georgia to build the Giga America battery factory.
- The FREYR Battery Giga America factory has a phase one capacity of about 34GWh and an initial capital estimate of $1.7 billion.
- In October 2022, OUR NEXT ENERGY (ONE) revealed a $1.6 billion investment plan (¥116.67 billion RMB) to build the ONE Circle LFP battery factory in Van Buren Township, Michigan.
- Production at the ONE Circle LFP battery factory is set to start in 2024, aiming for a 20 GWh annual capacity by 2027.
- In October 2022, specialty mineral company ICL Group announced a $400 million investment plan (¥29.17 billion RMB) to establish an LFP cathode active material factory in St. Louis, starting operations in 2024.
- U.S. battery tech developer Kore Power plans to set up the KOREPlex factory in Arizona, producing both EV batteries and storage systems using NMC and LFP technologies.
- Investment and capacity details for the Kore Power KOREPlex factory remain undisclosed.
