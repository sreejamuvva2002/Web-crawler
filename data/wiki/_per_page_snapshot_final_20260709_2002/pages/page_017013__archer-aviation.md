# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-15

**Source:** [Electric Air Taxi Company Archer Aviation to Invest $118M in Georgia Factory | Auto News - News18](https://www.news18.com/news/auto/electric-air-taxi-company-archer-aviation-to-invest-118m-georgia-factory-6386497.html)  ·  `page_017013`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation, a California company based in Santa Clara, will invest $118 million to construct a plant near Atlanta, Georgia.
- The company expects to receive a roughly $40 million incentive package from Georgia and local governments for the project.
- The incentive package includes the donation of 96 acres (39 hectares) of land at Covington Municipal Airport.
- The plant will eventually hire up to 1,000 people.
- Archer's plan involves a battery-powered vertical takeoff and landing craft with six propellers that hold four passengers and a pilot.
- The aircraft's propellers pivot to allow it to take off and land like a helicopter and fly like a plane.
- United Airlines put down a $10 million deposit to buy 100 aircraft from Archer for a total of $1 billion.
- United Airlines plans to fly the craft from downtown Manhattan to Newark Liberty International Airport in New Jersey, cutting travel time to 10 minutes.
- After building a prototype weighing 3,330 pounds (1,510 kilograms), Archer plans to unveil a production model dubbed "Midnight" on Wednesday.
- The company aims for the aircraft to enter service in 2025.
- The U.S. Air Force is evaluating Archer's aircraft for possible use.
- Archer is seeking Federal Aviation Administration certification and says it could receive it in 2024.
- Archer wants to produce 650 aircraft per year at the Georgia site, possibly scaling up to 2,300 per year.
- Automotive firm Stellantis, owner of Dodge, Chrysler, Jeep, and Ram, is providing manufacturing and engineering help.
- The incentive package includes a cash grant from the state and other tax incentives, including a property tax break.
- A job tax credit could be worth up to $15 million to Archer over five years, contingent on employees making at least $31,300.
- The company chose the Georgia location for locally available workers, the ability to conduct test flights, affordable construction costs, and good highway, rail, and air connections.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that their eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute and that the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Archer expects to borrow money from Georgia-based Synovus Financial Corp for the plant's construction.
- Construction is expected to begin and conclude in 2024.
- The company went public last year, raising about $850 million.
- Archer lost $91 million in the quarter that ended Sept. 30, reporting $600 million in cash on hand.
- Georgia economic development officials have been focusing on recruiting electric car companies, landing major plants from Rivian Automotive and Hyundai Motor Group last year.
- Norwegian battery startup Freyr announced a $2.6 billion plant in Georgia on the same day as Archer's announcement.
- A constructed electric battery plant owned by South Korean firm SK Innovation already exists in Georgia.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson said economic recruiters focused on aerospace had worked with Archer, noting that technology and innovation continue to drive change across strategic industries.
