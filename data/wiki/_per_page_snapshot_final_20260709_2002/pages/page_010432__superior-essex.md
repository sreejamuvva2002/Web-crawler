# Superior Essex

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Superior Essex - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superior_Essex)  ·  `page_010432`

## Facts

- Superior Essex is headquartered in Atlanta, United States.
- Key people include Daniel Choi serving as CEO.
- The company's products include Magnet Wire and Communication Cables.
- Revenue reached $2.99 billion in 2007.
- The company employs 3,800 people globally, with over 3,000 employees across 11 countries.
- The parent company is LS I&D, a Korean entity.
- Essex Solutions manufactures magnet wire for the automotive industry as well as energy, industrial, commercial, and residential sectors.
- Superior Essex Communications was founded in 1998 and manufactures and supplies communications cable in North America.
- Superior Essex Communications is active in developing power over Ethernet applications, intelligent buildings, smart cities, and 5G Fiber infrastructure as part of the Cisco Digital Ceiling ecosystem.
- In 2021, Superior Essex Communications was appointed a corporate board member of the Continental Automated Buildings Association (CABA).
- Essex Brownell operates as a distributor of lead wire, motors, drives, electrical and electronic insulation, tapes, repair parts, and shop and test equipment.
- Essex Brownell operates 16 facilities in the United States, Mexico, and Canada.
- Essex Wire Corporation was founded in 1930 as an original equipment manufacturer and operated 44 plants in North America by 1965 with sales of $355 million.
- Superior Cable came into existence in 1954 and had sales passing $500 million in 1997.
- In April 1999, Superior Essex was formed by a merger of Essex Wire Corporation and Superior Cable.
- In 2008, the Korean LS Group acquired Superior Essex for roughly $900 million to extend its reach into the North American and European market.
- Superior Essex's PowerWise cable won a 2018 Top Product of the Year Award for its 4PPoE innovation.
- Superior Essex was the first cable manufacturer awarded the International Living Future Institute's Living Product Challenge certification in September 2017 for its Category 6+ low smoke, halogen free (LSHF) cable.
- Manufacturing facilities are located across the Americas (United States, Canada, Mexico), Europe (France, Germany, Italy, Serbia, United Kingdom), and Asia (China, Malaysia, Japan).
