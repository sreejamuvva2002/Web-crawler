# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-17

**Source:** [Rivian breaks ground on Georgia EV plant - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2025/09/17/rivian-breaks-ground-on-georgia-ev-plant/)  ·  `page_059034`

## Facts

- Rivian has officially launched its new manufacturing site in Georgia.
- The facility is located near Social Circle.
- The project represents a multi-billion-dollar investment.
- The site is expected to create 7,500 permanent jobs by 2030.
- The build-out phase will create 2,000 construction jobs.
- Customer vehicle production is scheduled to begin in 2028.
- The site will be dedicated to Rivian’s next generation of all-electric models, including the midsize, five-seater R2 SUV and the R3 crossover.
- The company plans to develop the plant in two phases.
- Each phase is designed to deliver 200,000 units of annual capacity.
- The total planned annual capacity is up to 400,000 vehicles.
- The site covers nearly 2,000 acres.
- The facility will use modern construction techniques and advanced environmental management.
- Plans include recreational trails for employees and customers, including a dedicated Rivian experience trail.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated: “We are cementing Rivian’s future at our Georgia plant, helping ensure America maintains its technology leadership and excellence in automobile manufacturing.”
- Scaringe added: “Our Georgia facility will support our global expansion and provide the scale necessary to get millions of future drivers in our incredible all-electric vehicles, both in the United States and overseas.”
- An analysis by IMPLAN, cited by Rivian, estimates the investment will generate nearly 8,000 indirect jobs.
- The total number of positions supported by the project is more than 15,000.
- The IMPLAN report estimates over $1 billion in annual labour income will be created for suppliers, vendors, and small businesses in the region.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated: “Today is another milestone in bringing quality, good-paying jobs to Georgians in this part of the state.”
- Kemp added: “With today’s groundbreaking, this innovative company is further delivering on its commitment to the people of Georgia, and the thousands of hardworking Americans who will work at this facility will see generational benefits for their families.”
- Jerry Silvio, Chairman of the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton & Walton Counties, stated: “The Rivian project is exactly the kind of opportunity the JDA envisioned 25 years ago: high-tech, good-paying jobs in the communities we call home.”
- Silvio added: “With Rivian here, our region continues to be a hub for innovation and high-tech manufacturing.”
- At the beginning of the year, Rivian secured a $6.6 bn loan from the US Department of Energy (DOE) to set up the new production facility in Georgia.
- The DOE funds will be paid out in two tranches.
- Excluding capitalised interest, Rivian will receive around 3.4 billion dollars in the first phase and 2.6 billion in the second.
- Construction of a previously planned new plant in Stanton Springs North was suspended in 2024.
- The company recorded high losses in 2023 and again in the first quarter of last year.
- Rivian previously relocated production of the upcoming R2 to its operating factory in Normal, Illinois.
- Once the Georgia site is operational, both the midsize SUV (R2) and the midsize crossover R3 will roll off the production line in Georgia.
