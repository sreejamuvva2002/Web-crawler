# General Motors Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GM Banking $200M on Powertrain Engineering | American Machinist](https://www.americanmachinist.com/news/article/21897961/gm-banking-200m-on-powertrain-engineering)  ·  `page_020630`

## Facts

- General Motors Co. plans to invest $200 million to expand operations at its Global Powertrain Engineering headquarters in Pontiac, Mich.
- The investment will consolidate functions from four other operations into the Pontiac location.
- The capital investments are part of GM's previously announced plan to invest $1.5 billion in North American operations.
- At the Pontiac site, GM will build a 138,000-sq.-ft. testing center available in the second half of 2014.
- Sam Winegarden, GM vice president of Global Engine Engineering, stated that the moves will help the Powertrain team work more effectively to develop powertrain technologies for the world's best vehicles.
- In 2008, GM consolidated seven Powertrain Engineering operations at Pontiac from various Michigan locations.
- The upcoming consolidation will reduce GM's total footprint by 640,000 sq. ft. and eliminate three leased facilities.
- The leased operations being eliminated are located in Wixom, Mich., Castleton, Ind., and Torrance, Calif.
- A fourth powertrain operation, GM R&D’s Propulsion Systems Research lab in Warren, Mich., will relocate to the Pontiac campus.
- GM previously announced that its hydrogen fuel cell operation would move from Honeoye Falls, N.Y., to Pontiac, with the shift scheduled for this year.
- With the new testing center, GM’s Powertrain Development Center in Pontiac will total 450,000 sq.-ft.
- GM predicts the consolidated facility will bring engine technologies to market faster and less expensively by reducing development time.
- Engineers at the new facility will test engines for durability, reliability, and quality under extreme conditions, including cold ambient temperatures, high RPMs, and repetitive starting and stopping.
- Expanding and consolidating powertrain operations at Pontiac will reduce development time for GM's "next-generation advanced propulsion technologies."
- GM’s Performance Build Center engine plant in Wixom will relocate early in 2014 to Bowling Green, Ky., where GM assembles Chevrolet Corvettes.
- The Wixom Advanced Engineering Lab, including electric motor engineering development and performance engineering, will relocate to Pontiac by mid-2015.
- The Torrance Advanced Technology Center’s work on electric motor and power electronics engineering development will shift to Pontiac by the end of 2014.
- Heavy-duty transmission, power electronics, hybrid and battery electric drive unit development work done at Castleton will relocate to Pontiac by mid-2014.
- GM R&D’s Propulsion Systems Research Lab will relocate to Pontiac during the second half of 2015.
