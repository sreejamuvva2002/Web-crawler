# United States Federal Electric Vehicle Tax Credit Program

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2018-01-19

**Source:** [The Electric Car Thread | Page 396 | Ars OpenForum](https://arstechnica.com/civis/threads/the-electric-car-thread.1418263/page-396)  ·  `page_050138`

## Facts

- The new-for-2023 tax incentive requires the battery to be manufactured in the US and approximately 50% of all battery materials to be domestic.
- A criticism of the new-for-2023 tax incentive was that no cars currently met the criteria for battery manufacturing and domestic material sourcing.
- The federal EV tax credit is $7,500, structured as $3,750 plus $3,750.
- Starting January 1st, the manufacturer sales cap for the federal tax credit goes away.
- Illinois offers a $4,000 incentive on EVs.
- The EV itself must be assembled domestically, not necessarily the battery pack.
- Battery materials can qualify if they come from countries with free trade agreements (FTAs) with the US.
- KORUS (Korea-US FTA) means SK batteries are acceptable for material sourcing requirements.
- For 2023 unlimited cap credits, at least 50% of battery pack components must be sourced from the US, ramping up by 10% each year.
- Battery pack components do not receive the same free trade agreement language as materials to spur domestic production.
- The Bolt may become eligible for a $3,750 battery materials credit next year depending on sourcing, IRS final rules, and GM certification.
- LG is likely sourcing its battery pack components mainly from SK, which probably disqualifies it from the component credit half.
- Panasonic and Tesla produce battery pack components like cells and shells in Nevada.
- The critical minerals requirement mandates that a specific percentage of the value of applicable critical minerals contained in the battery must be extracted or processed in the US, an FTA country, or recycled in North America.
- For vehicles placed in service after proposed guidance is issued but before January 1, 2024, the applicable critical minerals percentage is 40 percent.
- The battery components requirement mandates that a specific percentage of the value of components must be manufactured or assembled in North America.
- GM is prioritizing battery replacements in customer cars over new vehicle sales.
- Many 2022 EUVs have been sitting on dealer lots for a year waiting for battery replacements.
- Dealer markups and market adjustments are driven by supply and demand, with some dealers adding thousands to MSRP.
- Australia is starting to introduce incentives for EV buyers, but currently lacks incentives for manufacturers to sell them there.
- Australia does not have fleet emissions standards or a cut-off date for ICE sales, which may delay adoption by 5-10 years compared to the rest of the world.
- Used EV imports to Africa are currently small because only about 10% of EVs are old enough to enter the used import market, but this will accelerate as the EV market grows.
