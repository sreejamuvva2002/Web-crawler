# SK Battery America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-14

**Source:** [$31M Settlement for Improper Disposal of Waste Leading to Fire | Jurimatic by Exlitem | Jury Verdicts & Settlements | Exlitem](https://jurimatic.com/31-million-settlement-reached-for-devastating-fire-caused-by-improper-disposal-of-batteries)  ·  `page_049956`

## Facts

- SK Battery America is an electric vehicle battery manufacturer with a factory in north Georgia.
- A fire destroyed Metro Site Inc.'s material recovery facility in Commerce, Georgia, on July 14, 2023.
- The blaze started after lithium-ion battery scraps exploded at the facility.
- Metro Site Inc., a waste and recycling company not authorized to process batteries, filed a trespass lawsuit against SK Battery America in Jackson County State Court in Georgia (Case number: 23SV271).
- Investigators traced the lithium-ion batteries found in the debris back to SK Battery America, a subsidiary of South Korea’s SK Group.
- Post-fire inspections revealed over 600 burnt and charred SK batteries at the site.
- Expert findings confirmed the shipped batteries underwent thermal runaway, causing the fire.
- SK Battery America had sent defective lithium-ion batteries to Metro Site at least twelve times despite knowing they had caused previous fires there.
- The Plaintiff alleged that SK Battery America knew Metro Site was not licensed or equipped to handle hazardous materials like lithium-ion batteries.
- SK Battery America was accused of destroying key evidence during litigation by deleting video footage from inside its plant, which the court ruled constituted evidence spoliation.
- Following a December 22, 2022 incident where battery cells delivered by Roll-Off Systems caused a fire at Metro Site, SK Battery America updated its policies and provided additional employee training to prevent future occurrences.
- Jinsu Kim issued revised instructions to the company’s logistics and module teams to prevent further battery cell deliveries to Metro Site.
- After implementing the updated policies, no incidents occurred between December 2022 and July 14, 2023, during which Roll-Off Systems transported approximately 2,700 loads of waste from SK Battery America to Metro Site without issue.
- The defense argued that SK Battery America took previous incidents seriously and implemented internal measures, including policy revisions and employee training.
- Metro Site Inc. and SK Battery America reached a $31 million settlement less than two weeks before the scheduled trial.
- Plaintiff attorney Jackson McDonald stated the settlement was driven by a demand for a public disclosure clause to highlight SK Battery’s alleged misconduct and deter similar actions by corporations.
- The agreement avoided a second spoliation hearing and ended one of several legal disputes surrounding the fire.
- McDonald predicted increased litigation against manufacturers related to lithium-ion batteries and electric vehicle technology, emphasizing the significant risks posed by emerging industries.
- An SK Battery spokesperson confirmed the settlement and highlighted procedural improvements to prevent similar incidents.
- The company maintained that it had not intentionally sent hazardous materials to Metro Site or destroyed evidence, expressing commitment to community safety and a desire to move beyond the dispute.
- Plaintiff attorneys planned to introduce evidence of recurring fires at the SK Battery America plant based on an open records search of 911 service calls revealing numerous internal factory fires.
