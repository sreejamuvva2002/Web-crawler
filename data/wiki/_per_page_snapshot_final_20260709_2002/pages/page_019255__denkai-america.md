# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07

**Source:** [Copper Foil Manufacturer Denkai America to Locate New Manufacturing Facility, North American Headquarters in Richmond County - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/news/2022/07/copper-foil-manufacturer-denkai-america-locate-new-manufacturing-facility-north-american-headquarters-richmond-county-1/)  ·  `page_019255`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Denkai America will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American Headquarters in Richmond County.
- Denkai America is the only North American manufacturer of high-quality electrodeposited (ED) copper foil used in printed electronics.
- The company’s initial investment will be $150 million to establish an ED copper foil production facility.
- The facility will focus on electric vehicle (EV) battery foil.
- The project will take place on a 115-acre site.
- The company has a goal to invest a total of $430 million over the next five years.
- The project aims to create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America, further strengthening the state's status as a leader in the emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Governor Kemp emphasized that the state has been laser-focused on bringing quality jobs to highly-trained Georgians across the state.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board (PCB) applications, primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's materials are now also used in the lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Denkai has been operating for more than 60 years.
- The company has team members worldwide with operations in Japan and North America.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., Denkai Group President and CEO, stated that the investment highlights their commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Heday Nakajima expressed gratitude to the teams in Richmond County and the State of Georgia for making the project possible.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s President and CEO, stated that they are thrilled to expand their manufacturing presence into Augusta due to its skilled and talented workforce.
- Construction is expected to begin in September 2022 as the first part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- The Development Authority of Augusta, Georgia, welcomed Denkai America and its North American Headquarters to the Augusta Corporate Park.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, stated that Denkai America will be a great corporate citizen and bring high-skilled jobs to Augusta and the Augusta Corporate Park.
- Steven Kendrick thanked the Augusta Commission and the Augusta Utilities Department for their support and hard work in making the project happen.
- Global Commerce Director of Corporate Solutions and Cyber Security Barton Lowrey represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is the Technical College System of Georgia’s nationally ranked workforce training program.
- Georgia Quick Start has provided specialized training tailored specifically to business needs for more than 40 years.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that as electric mobility adoption becomes widespread, the State of Georgia is committed to ensuring Georgians in the automotive industry have access to future jobs at home.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that the investment demonstrates Georgia's commitment to building lasting international relationships and driving economic partnerships, including recruiting the wider electric mobility value chain.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it an attractive location for rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $12.6 billion in investments to Georgia since 2020.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than 17,800 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park.
- To earn a GRAD program certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Completing the GRAD certification program with the Georgia Department of Economic Development is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development.
