# Georgia’s Incentives Portfolio

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia’s Incentives | Georgia](https://georgia.org/retraining-tax-credit)  ·  `page_002198`

## Facts

- Georgia’s incentives portfolio fosters economic growth by offering tax credits and exemptions that reduce costs for companies establishing new locations or expanding current ones.
- The corporate income tax rate dropped to 4.99% in 2026.
- Georgia was the first Southeastern state to adopt single-factor apportionment.
- Companies can tap into Job Tax Credits by creating new jobs in qualifying industries.
- In certain locations, Job Tax Credits can reduce payroll-withholding obligations to generate cash flow for businesses.
- Quality Jobs Tax Credits are earned for new jobs that pay 110% or more of the county’s average wage.
- Georgia exempts sales and use taxes on manufacturing-integral items including machinery, repair parts, molds, dies, tooling, raw materials, packaging, and supplies.
- Energy essential to manufacturing operations is exempt from the State’s 4% sales and use tax.
- Additional local exemptions for manufacturing energy vary by jurisdiction from 1% to 3%.
- Distribution centers may qualify for a sales and use tax exemption on equipment used for handling, moving, or storing goods if they invest at least $5 million in expanding or establishing a new facility.
- Most counties exempt local property tax for manufacturers’ goods held for 12 months or less, including goods to be shipped within a year.
- Companies must apply for the freeport exemption each year.
- There is no state property tax on inventory or any real or personal property in Georgia.
