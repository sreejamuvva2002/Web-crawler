# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-16

**Source:** [YKK AP America Makes $125M Residential Investment](https://www.dwmmag.com/2022/08/16/ykk-ap-america-makes-125m-residential-investment/)  ·  `page_016187`

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. is investing up to $125 million to expand its residential door and window offerings with a new manufacturing facility in Macon, Ga.
- President Oliver Stepe says the operational goal is to double the company’s capacity while setting a forward course for the decades to come.
- Macon has been the headquarters of YKK AP’s residential products business since 2007 and will be home to the approximately 400,000-square-foot residential door and window manufacturing facility set to break ground in October 2022.
- Strategic planning for the project has been in the works for two years with final approvals coming down the line only three months ago.
- Oliver Stepe says leveraging the desire and interest to do it is that they are trying to scale the business, noting they still have room to grow in the residential window business.
- The company's currently-leased facilities were out of room and capacity, making the expansion a decision between continuing to grow or staying static.
- Rising interest rates and a potential slowdown in housing starts are not deterrents for YKK AP because the project has been in the works for years.
- Oliver Stepe notes that historically, the company's largest investments have occurred at times of financial challenges.
- YKK AP acquired 50 acres in Macon on which the new facility will be constructed, leaving plenty of room for future growth.
- While the operational goal is to double capacity in the residential market, the acquired acreage will allow for current capacity to be quadrupled in the future.
- Oliver Stepe emphasizes that companies focusing on a disciplined approach in the market and with employees are the ones that will grow through economic cycles.
- The new facility will utilize advanced sustainable manufacturing and enhanced work environments for employees.
- Many of YKK AP's factories already use internally-designed and engineered machinery, which will continue at the new location.
- The investment will see the introduction of a $20 million highly automated production line.
- Glass sorting and additional automation are in the works for the new facility.
- A good portion of existing machinery will be transferred to the new location once completed.
- Employee-focused improvements include increased ambient lighting, outdoor space, 100% climate control, bright-colored walls, and multiple outdoor seating areas.
- YKK AP will add more than 100 new jobs with the expansion, plus the 250 Macon employees set to transition to the new location.
- Oliver Stepe says the company wants people to feel happy at work, focusing on comfortable and pleasing environments.
- The new residential development will not take away from YKK AP's commercial offerings, as production, customers, and markets for the two areas remain in separate lanes.
- JE Dunn Construction will serve as the construction manager for the project.
- SSOE Group will serve as architects and engineers.
- Scott Bodden, project director at JE Dunn Construction, says they look forward to collaborating with SSOE Group to support YKK AP’s growth and create jobs.
- Scott Bodden notes the facility will be built on innovation, beginning with innovation in project delivery.
- The facility is scheduled to be operational by Q1 of 2024.
