# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch is banking on innovations, partnerships, and acquisitions – cost reduction remains in focus - Bosch Media Service US](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/us/en/press-release-23680.html)  ·  `page_018033`

## Facts

- The Bosch Group increased its sales and earnings in 2023 and is successfully implementing its growth strategy despite a difficult environment.
- Stefan Hartung, chairman of the board of management of Robert Bosch GmbH, stated that the company achieved its financial targets in 2023 and strengthened its market position from semiconductors to integrated building systems.
- The company aims for long-term average annual growth of between 6 and 8 percent with a margin of at least 7 percent and wants to rank among the top three suppliers in its key markets globally.
- In 2023, Bosch generated sales of $99 billion (91.6 billion euros), an increase of 3.8 percent or an exchange rate-adjusted 8.0 percent.
- Earnings from operations before interest and taxes (EBIT) amounted to $5.2 billion (4.8 billion euros, up from 3.8 billion euros in 2022).
- The EBIT margin from operations was 5.3 percent, which is higher than expected but lower than the long-term target margin of at least 7 percent required by 2026.
- CFO Markus Forschner noted that a successful final sprint at the end of 2023 contributed to meeting expectations, but the 2024 business year will be at least as challenging as 2023.
- Bosch expects global economic growth of only 2.3 percent in 2024, along with stagnating vehicle production and continued weakness in the industrial technology market.
- In North America, sales to third parties increased 6 percent year-over-year to $16.5 billion (15.2 billion euros), while total net sales from the region increased 8 percent to $17.9 billion.
- The Mobility business sector in North America increased sales by 15 percent to $10.4 billion, while Industrial Technology increased 33 percent to $1.6 billion.
- Energy and Building Technology sales in North America were up nearly 8 percent to $1.3 billion, and the Consumer Goods business sector was down nearly 6 percent to $3.3 billion in 2023.
- Mike Mansuetti, president of Bosch in North America, highlighted that more than 42,000 associates in the United States, Mexico and Canada contributed to the company's global success.
- In 2023, Bosch invested more than $778 million (719 million euros) in capital expenditures in North America, a more than 50 percent increase over 2022.
- Since the beginning of 2022, Bosch has announced more than $3 billion dollars worth of investments in North America across its portfolio.
- In the Mobility business sector, Bosch announced and closed an acquisition of a wafer fab in Roseville, Calif., intending to invest $1.5 billion at the site to produce silicon carbide chips by 2026.
- In its Industrial business sector, Bosch closed the acquisition of U.S.-based specialist hydraulic company HydraForce in 2023.
- In its Energy and Building Technologies business sector, Bosch announced and closed the acquisition of Vancouver, Canada-based Paladin Technologies Inc.
- In its Consumer Goods business sector, the company announced a $130 million investment to support the manufacturing of power tools accessories in Lincolnton, N.C.
- In its core mobility business, Bosch is launching some 30 production projects for electric vehicles this year alone.
- Stefan Hartung estimates that 70 percent of all new cars in Europe will likely be purely electric by 2030, and 40 to 50 percent in China and North America.
- For heavy vehicles covering long distances, plug-in hybrids and range extenders will remain in demand for some time.
- The Mobility business sector expects further stimulus from vehicle dynamics technology, growing by 10 percent annually with new and redundant braking systems tailored to electrified and automated driving.
- Bosch is committed to a vehicle motion management (VMM) system solution that coordinates brakes, steering, powertrain, and dampers, equipping more than 20 major-brand test vehicles with VMM variants for recent winter tests.
- Hartung stated the company will put its first VMM order into volume production this year and expects to achieve sales worth hundreds of millions for the VMM solution by 2030.
- Bosch reaffirmed that sales with hydrogen technology could reach nearly $5.5 billion globally by 2030, though major growth in Europe or North America is not expected until the next decade.
- The market for hydrogen engines is expected to be worth around $1 billion by 2030, with Bosch's injection technology on the road in India this year and working on five production orders from truck manufacturers.
- Bosch expects global installed capacity for hydrogen electrolysis to reach a good 170 gigawatts by 2030, with its electrolysis stack on course for market entry next year.
- Bosch's heat pump business grew by almost 50 percent in 2023 despite Europe stagnating, and the company announced an IDS Ultra cold climate heat pump system at CES 2024 designed for colder climate zones such as the Northern U.S. that met all requirements of the DOE Cold Climate Challenge.
- Research and development expenditure remained stable at $7.9 billion (8 percent ratio), while capital expenditure reached a new high of $5.9 billion.
- Upfront investments totaled nearly $13 billion in 2023, with CFO Forschner noting close attention to cost-effectiveness and project adjustments.
- At the end of 2023, Bosch employed 429,416 people worldwide, an increase of some 8,078 from the previous year, with the strongest regional growth in the Americas.
