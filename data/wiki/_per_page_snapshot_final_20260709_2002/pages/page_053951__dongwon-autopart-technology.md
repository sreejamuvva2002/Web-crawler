# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-10

**Source:** [Dongwon Autopart Technology to Invest $30M in Georgia, Creating 200 Jobs - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/dongwon-autopart-technology-to-invest-30m-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_053951`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Dongwon Autopart Technology will invest $30 million to establish a new automotive components manufacturing facility in Emanuel County.
- The project will bring an anticipated 200 new jobs to the region over the next several years.
- The project further strengthens Georgia’s position as a national leader in automotive manufacturing.
- The project highlights continued supplier growth across the state’s rural communities.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia’s thriving automotive industry is creating new opportunities for Georgians statewide, no matter their zip code.
- Governor Kemp expressed excitement for the project to bring new jobs to Emanuel County and see its impact on the community for years to come.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was founded in 1971.
- The company supplies key components including door frames, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company currently supports more than 500 direct jobs across the Southeast.
- Dongwon has an existing facility in Hogansville, Georgia, which will continue operating.
- Dongwon has maintained a U.S. presence since 2003.
- The company continues to scale alongside growth in the automotive and electric vehicle supply chain.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, stated pride in being part of Georgia’s growing automotive industry.
- Hollstein noted appreciation for Emanuel County’s warm welcome and commitment to delivering high-quality products and service to customers.
- The new production facility will be located at the Highway 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- Construction is expected to move forward soon.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- Planned positions include management roles, system leads, and production personnel.
- The investment represents the largest new economic development project in Emanuel County in more than three decades.
- Local and state leaders emphasized the project’s importance to rural job creation and regional economic growth.
- Representative Butch Parrish, Chairman of the House Rules Committee, stated that the Georgia House of Representatives is committed to bringing high-quality jobs to rural communities.
- Parrish noted that Emanuel County’s collaboration with state partners is delivering meaningful opportunities for the region.
- Chairman Will Donaldson of the Emanuel County Development Authority noted the project reflects long-term economic development efforts.
- Donaldson expressed pleasure and excitement at welcoming Dongwon Autopart Technology to Emanuel County, noting it is rewarding to see investment in economic development generate results for the community.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, added that the project will be transformational for the county.
- Warnock stated it will be the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Warnock mentioned enjoying working with Dongwon’s leadership to secure their new state-of-the-art facility in the 297 Industrial Park.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) supported the project through its Global Commerce team.
- GDEcD partnered with the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start on the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the automotive industry is expanding along the I-16 corridor as suppliers seek strategic access to OEMs and Georgia’s ports.
- Wilson noted Dongwon’s second Georgia location underscores the strength of the state's automotive sector, which has been creating jobs statewide for more than a century.
- Dongwon’s investment reinforces Georgia’s competitiveness in automotive manufacturing, particularly in supplier recruitment.
- The state’s infrastructure, workforce training programs, and logistics connectivity continue to draw global manufacturers.
- As electric vehicle and advanced automotive production grows across the Southeast, projects like this position Georgia and communities like Emanuel County to capture future supply chain investment.
