# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-29

**Source:** [Volvo Group cuts North America forecast by 10K trucks](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/volvo-group-cuts-north-america-114200133.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_014274`

## Facts

- Volvo Group has lowered its 2025 sales outlook in North America to 265,000 trucks.
- The reduction amounts to a cut of 10,000 units from the previous forecast.
- The company cites new tariffs and weak demand as reasons for the downward revision.
- The revised outlook was reported during the company's Q3 earnings this month.
- President and CEO Martin Lundstedt stated that customers are in "wait-and-see mode" due to high uncertainty from current market conditions.
- Lundstedt noted during the Oct. 17 earnings call that turmoil from uncertainty in North America is temporarily burdensome but manageable short-term.
- Lundstedt emphasized that the company has built a strong platform for the future in North America.
- Volvo Group faces mounting challenges in one of its largest markets due to trade tensions and a freight slowdown.
- A new 25% tariff on imports of heavy-duty and medium-duty trucks and their associated parts begins Nov. 1.
- The company incurred 500 million SEK in tariff-related costs in Q3 alone, according to CFO Mats Backman.
- Lundstedt stated that Volvo Group has a "100% U.S. footprint for North American trucks."
- The company is preparing for potential tariff agreements between the U.S. and Mexico.
- Lundstedt said the company anticipates a starting point of around 10,000 units with the ability to move north if needed.
- The company has a "structural undercapacity in North America for normal markets and also for upmarkets."
- Lundstedt stated that adding capacity for North and South American markets makes Volvo and Mack necessary.
- In the U.S., Volvo Trucks operates an assembly plant in Virginia.
- The company maintains additional manufacturing facilities in Maryland and Virginia.
- Volvo Trucks operates seven parts distribution centers in the U.S.
- Sister brand Mack Truck shares Volvo's powertrain assembly facility in Maryland.
- Mack Truck also has its own plant in Pennsylvania.
- The Pennsylvania plant recently began manufacturing the Mack Pioneer, the brand's first long-haul truck in decades.
- It is unlikely Volvo will expand production capacity due to the ongoing freight recession and weak demand.
- The company expects new tariffs to further slow orders into 2026.
- In Q3, Volvo Group's total truck orders increased by 10% year-over-year.
- Deliveries fell by 20% year-over-year in Q3.
- Sales of heavy-duty trucks during the first nine months of 2025 were down 10% compared to the same period last year.
