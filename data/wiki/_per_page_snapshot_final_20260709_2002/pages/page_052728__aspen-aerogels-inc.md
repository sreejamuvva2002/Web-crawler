# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-25

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/25/aspen-aerogels-aspn-q4-2025-earnings-transcript/)  ·  `page_052728`

## Facts

- U.S. EV demand reset lower as GM reduced EV production rates.
- GM followed suit with a ramp down of its EV production rates beginning in Q4 2025.
- GM has maintained its full line of EV nameplates and has stated that it remains dedicated to its long-term EV success, including in the Cadillac division where EV sales represented nearly 30% of total sales over the year 2025.
- European EV pipeline projected $220 million linked to 2027 launches and over $450 million for 2028, across passenger and commercial vehicle platforms.
- Seven European design wins secured for the Thermal Barrier segment, including Volvo Car, with additional awards expected in the year.
- European EV programs incorporate battery cells from a diversified global supply base, including European, Korean, Japanese, and leading Chinese manufacturers.
- Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) segment development is targeting both utility-scale external systems and modular rack-level indoor applications.
- Revenue from the new BESS segment is anticipated to begin in fiscal 2026.
- Fire safety is identified as the most critical application for the company's aerogel technology in battery storage projects.
- Domestic capacity in the U.S. provides policy advantages for domestic BESS projects.
- The company is evaluating opportunities to enter the defense market, particularly for EV-related devices and battery storage applications.
- A strategic review has been initiated with external advisers to assess growth strategy, capital allocation, and value creation options from a position of financial strength.
- Annual fixed cash cost base was structurally reduced by approximately $75 million.
- Adjusted EBITDA breakeven revenue target declines from approximately $330 million in fiscal 2024 to $175 million in fiscal 2027.
- Cash and cash equivalents totaled $158.6 million at the end of fiscal year 2025.
- Expected 2026 debt obligations include approximately $35 million in scheduled debt payments, including $24 million in term loan principal amortization.
- Energy Industrial segment revenue is expected to grow by 20% in fiscal 2026, driven by subsea, LNG, and pent-up maintenance demand.
- The company aims to scale the Energy Industrial business into a $200 million high-margin segment without incremental capital investment.
- Market share in the subsea and LNG segment is extremely high; the lack of 2025 growth was due to a lack of available projects rather than lost bids.
- A new North Sea pipe-in-pipe subsea project award won earlier in the year is expected to be delivered in Q3 2026.
- LNG activity is positioned to roughly double versus 2025 in both project count and revenue contribution for 2026.
- Pent-up demand for maintenance exists among refinery and petrochemical end users who minimized turnaround activities over the past year.
- The company is adding customer-facing sales and technical service teams globally to support Energy Industrial growth.
- GM production is expected to increase as downtime subsides, with EV volumes normalizing through 2026.
- European OEM programs are expected to contribute approximately $10 million to $15 million of revenue in 2026.
- Q1 2026 total revenue guidance is between $35 million and $40 million, with adjusted EBITDA between negative $13 million and negative $10 million.
- Annualized production rate is expected to be approximately 72,000 units exiting Q4.
- The company amended its MidCap credit agreement in December to enhance covenant flexibility and maintain a substantial liquidity cushion.
- Net cash position is expected to expand from approximately $50 million today to over $70 million by the end of 2026.
- Building materials and retrofit opportunities are being explored in Europe, targeting thermal efficiency regulations for existing buildings.
- The company operates a capital-light, flexible manufacturing model that eliminates the need for new plant construction and scales using existing assets and swing capacity.
