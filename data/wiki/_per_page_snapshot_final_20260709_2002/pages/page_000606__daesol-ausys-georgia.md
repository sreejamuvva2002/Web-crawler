# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Another Korean auto parts manufacturer to open facility in Georgia, governor announces – WSB-TV Channel 2 - Atlanta](https://www.wsbtv.com/news/local/another-korean-auto-parts-manufacturer-open-facility-georgia-governor-announces/ALVN2FDSZBHKXI2DBICE4XILMQ/)  ·  `page_000606`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will build a new manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia will invest $72 million in Harris County.
- The project will create more than 140 jobs.
- West Point is also home to Kia and Hyundai assembly sites.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia, Hyundai, and General Motors.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and expressed excitement that Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that Georgia’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for future jobs.
- The newly announced West Point plant will focus on producing luggage boards and covers.
- Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing interior components and accessories for electric vehicles.
- Automotive industry job creation in Georgia rose 324% in the 2023 fiscal year compared to 2021, attributed to the state’s business climate and pursuit of the entire EV supply chain.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and look forward to continuing the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- The Daesol facility in West Point is expected to begin operations in December 2024.
- The company will hire for job openings in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- In recent months, multiple Korean manufacturing companies with ties to the automotive industry have announced developments in Georgia, including EV parts manufacturer NVH Korea and automotive supplier Hanon Systems.
- Woory Industrial announced the opening of an EV parts manufacturing and research facility in Georgia in June.
