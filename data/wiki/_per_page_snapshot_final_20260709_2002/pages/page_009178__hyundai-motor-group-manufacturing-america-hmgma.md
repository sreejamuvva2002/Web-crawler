# Hyundai Motor Group Manufacturing America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Commences IONIQ 9 Production at Georgia Facility | EV.com](https://ev.com/news/hyundai-motor-group-commences-ioniq-9-production-at-georgia-facility)  ·  `page_009178`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group has commenced production of the IONIQ 9 at its advanced Georgia manufacturing facility.
- The three-row IONIQ 9 serves as Hyundai's new flagship electric SUV for North America.
- It is the second model produced at the HMGMA plant, following the IONIQ 5.
- The vehicle is built on Hyundai’s specialized Electric Global Modular Platform (E-GMP).
- Standout features include a sweeping panoramic display and what Hyundai describes as its most advanced driver-assistance systems yet.
- Early reports suggest the combination of long range and quick charging could make it particularly appealing to families making the switch to electric.
- Hyundai is investing billions into the Georgia facility to make it one of its key EV manufacturing hubs equipped with the latest equipment.
- Local officials have noted the plant’s progress represents one of the largest industrial developments in recent Georgia history.
- The facility is providing good-paying factory jobs that require technical skills, delivering real economic benefits to the local area.
- Job numbers are expected to grow as Hyundai continues to add more production lines at the site.
- Manufacturing the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 in Georgia allows Hyundai to sidestep import tariffs that would otherwise drive up vehicle prices.
- Buyers of these domestically produced vehicles may qualify for $7,500 federal EV credits and state-level incentives applicable only to U.S.-made vehicles.
- The plant is gearing up for higher production volumes, with insiders expecting more EV models to roll off the lines in the coming years.
- Local production helps Hyundai stay competitive on both price and availability as EV demand grows, albeit slower than some predicted.
