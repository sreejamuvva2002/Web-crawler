# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2009-06-09

**Source:** [Jackson stresses jobs coming to the area - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/jackson-stresses-jobs-coming-to-the-area/)  ·  `page_010052`

## Facts

- Randy Jackson serves as the director of human resources for Kia Georgia.
- Jackson spoke at the William J. Griggs Recreation Center in LaGrange concerning Kia’s application process and needed skills.
- The event was sponsored by the Troup County Coalition, where residents brought questions to Jackson.
- In January 2008, layoffs and position cuts led a total of 43,013 people to fill out applications at Kia's website in a 30-day period.
- Kia hired 90 employees on June 9, 2009.
- Kia officials planned to bring in about 80 or 90 new hires every two weeks for a couple of months.
- The June 9 hiring brought Kia's total headcount to a little short of 700 employees.
- Kia officials are planning on hiring 2,500 people for 2,500 jobs at the plant.
- Kia suppliers are expected to add another 7,500 jobs to the area.
- Jackson estimated more than 30,000 jobs coming to the area through broader economic development, including construction jobs, hotel workers, and restaurant staff.
- An additional 10,000 jobs are being created specifically through Kia and its suppliers.
- Jackson noted that the supplier base is just now beginning to hire for the projected 7,500 supplier jobs.
- Kia wants local people who are ready for hard work, self-disciplined regarding punctuality, team players, able to sustain the physicality of the job, and possess a high school diploma or equivalent.
- Arthur Williams and Paul Grimes serve as managers of team relations for Kia Georgia and have more than 40 years of collective experience in car manufacturing.
- Many of the production and maintenance employees hired so far live in Troup and Chambers counties, as well as Muscogee and Harris counties in Georgia, and Lee County in Alabama.
- Maintenance and production employees will be trained, taught, and coached by experienced automotive manufacturers and salaried workers from other carmakers.
- Kia plans to fund future non-profit organizations that will be established when the plant enters production.
- Kia is interested in forming agreements with local colleges like Auburn University and the Georgia Institute of Technology to allow co-op students to work at the plant for classroom hours and experience.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG) is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Motors Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- The KMMG facility is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- The plant was scheduled to begin production in the last quarter of 2009.
- At full capacity, the plant will produce 300,000 vehicles annually and employ approximately 2,500 team members.
