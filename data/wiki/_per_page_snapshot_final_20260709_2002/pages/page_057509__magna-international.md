# Magna International

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Magna International constructing fourth manufacturing plant in South Carolina - Who's On The Move](https://whosonthemove.com/magna-international-constructing-fourth-manufacturing-plant-in-south-carolina-174740/)  ·  `page_057509`

## Facts

- Magna International is constructing a new seat manufacturing plant in Spartanburg County, South Carolina.
- The facility represents the company's fourth manufacturing plant in South Carolina.
- Magna International is projected to invest $29 million in the facility.
- The project will create 480 new jobs over the next five years.
- Magna International is headquartered in Aurora, Ontario, Canada.
- The company operates facilities across the globe and employs approximately 155,000 people worldwide.
- Magna's product capabilities include producing automobile body, chassis, exterior, seating, powertrain, electronic, vision, closure, and roof systems and modules, as well as contract manufacturing.
- President of Magna Seating Mike Bisson stated the project marks a new chapter in the ongoing collaboration between Magna and BMW.
- Mike Bisson noted that this is Magna's first seating business award with BMW.
- The new plant is located in the Tyger River Industrial Park on Highway 290.
- The facility will be 230,000 square feet.
- The seating plant is expected to be operational in June 2017.
- The facility will feature dedicated assembly and sequencing lines to supply seats to BMW’s nearby assembly plant.
- Magna currently has three manufacturing locations in South Carolina.
- Magna currently employs approximately 1,450 people in the state of South Carolina.
- The Coordinating Council for Economic Development has approved job development credits related to this project.
- The S.C. Department of Commerce is South Carolina’s leading economic development agency.
- The S.C. Department of Commerce works to recruit new businesses and help existing businesses grow in the state.
- The S.C. Department of Commerce has recruited world-class companies to South Carolina including BMW, Boeing, Continental, Giti Tire, LPL Financial Holdings, Mercedes-Benz Vans, Toray, and Volvo Cars.
