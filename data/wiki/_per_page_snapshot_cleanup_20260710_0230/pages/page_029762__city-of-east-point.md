# City of East Point

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Economic Development - City of East Point, Georgia](https://eastpointga.gov/economic-development/)  ·  `page_029762`

## Facts

- East Point’s Economic Development Department serves as a resource for business recruitment, retention and expansion, assisting with site selection, potential incentives (including job tax credits), and attractive financing products.
- The city is located Inside the Perimeter (ITP) nestled between major interstates, with close proximity to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and downtown Atlanta.
- East Point has a Qualified Federal Opportunity Zone under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, allowing investors to defer federal taxes by reinvesting capital gains in designated low-income communities.
- As a Tier 3 County, East Point qualifies businesses to apply for Tier 3 level job tax credits offering $1750 per eligible job with a minimum of 15 jobs created.
- Several areas in the city are identified as HUB Zones, providing federal preferential consideration for set-aside contracts and a 10% price evaluation preference in open contracts.
- The Department of Economic Development partnered with Public Works to be awarded a nearly $5 million LCI grant for transportation, pedestrian, and cycle-related improvements, with additional phases approved and underway in planning and construction.
- The city operates its own Power Company through MEAG/Electric Cities of Georgia, offering extremely competitive utility rates.
- East Point maintains its own private Water Reservoir (Sweet Water Creek), providing abundant water for industrial, commercial, and residential needs independent of Atlanta’s supply.
- The East Point Corridors Redevelopment Plan and Tax Allocation Bond District was approved in 2006 to stimulate investment in the commercial core and historic neighborhoods along Main Street, Cleveland Avenue, and Washington Road.
- The Camp Creek / I-285 TAD was approved in 2001 to support redevelopment efforts.
- The Camp Creek Business Center contains 2.9 million square feet of industrial and office space with capacity for an additional 2.0 million square feet.
- The Camp Creek Business Center has created more than 1,500 jobs and is positioned near a labor pool of more than 1.2 million people aged 20 to 64 within a 30-minute radius.
- Duke Realty provides acreage to support up to 2.0 million square feet of new industrial or office space at the Camp Creek Business Center.
- The Camp Creek Shopping District features 1.2 million square feet of retail space, with Camp Creek Marketplace at 98% occupancy and lease rates over $25 per square foot.
- Lawrence Avenue Industrial Park encompasses over 200 acres and is adjacent to Norfolk Southern Railroad’s industry yard with daily switching service.
- William C. Meredith Company has operated a manufacturing plant in Lawrence Avenue Industrial Park since 1921, producing Pentachlorophenol treated poles for electric utilities and municipalities.
- Oakleigh Industrial Park hosts PPG’s architectural coatings plant, which began East Point operations in 1952 and manufactures latex and oil-based paints for residential, commercial, protective/marine, automotive refinish, and industrial markets.
- Southmeadow Business Park includes tenants such as Porsche, the Georgia Department of Revenue, Medical Depot, and Martin Brower.
- McDonald Development Company owns 150 acres at Southmeadow Business Park available for development to accommodate individual users up to 1 million square feet.
- Willingham Industrial Park supports automotive repair shops, warehousing facilities, manufacturing, and recycling operations along Central Avenue with access to I-85.
- The city recently adopted its first Small Business Incentives Program offering permit and license fee waivers plus tax and utility discounts for businesses with 9 employees or less.
- The East Point Business and Industrial Development Authority (EPBIDA) issues tax-exempt and taxable industrial development bonds to provide state and local ad valorem tax advantages through structured sale/leaseback transactions.
- EPBIDA board members are appointed by seated City Council members for four-year terms, with the board meeting on the first Thursday of each month at 6:30 pm.
- The city is updating its Economic Development Strategic Plan (EDSP) to align strategies over the next 10 years with resident and business community needs.
- A Transit Oriented Development (TOD) Plan completed in fall 2012 outlines catalyst redevelopment sites near East Point and Lakewood/Ft. McPherson MARTA stations along US 29.
