# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Freyr Battery poised to scale up clean and cost-competitive battery production](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20220929VL204/battery+green-energy.html?chid=13)  ·  `page_055021`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery is a Norway-based manufacturer establishing the first battery supply chain in the country with contributions from Asian partners.
- The company aims to leverage cheap energy prices and optimized production processes to make cost-competitive products.
- According to an International Energy Agency report, demand for EV batteries will surge from 340GWh to 3500GWh by 2030.
- Freyr Battery was established in 2018.
- The company has been listed on the New York Stock Exchange since July 2021.
- Freyr is constructing a production base in northern Norway.
- The project's first phase includes a customer qualification plant (CQP) and two gigafactories, Giga Arctic, costing around US$1.7 billion.
- The CQP will host one industrial-scale production line instead of a pilot.
- Production at the CQP is scheduled to start in the first quarter of 2023.
- CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated the CQP will produce sample cells, test raw materials, and train operators to help achieve high production in the gigafactories later.
- Giga Arctic will have eight production lines with an annual capacity of 29GWh.
- Operations at Giga Arctic will start in the first half of 2024.
- To secure material supply, Freyr partnered with Aleees of Taiwan to build an LFP cathode plant in the Nordic region.
- The joint cathode plant will be the largest outside China, with an initial capacity of 10,000 tonnes per year by 2024.
- CEO Tom Einar Jensen emphasized that scale is essential for driving down production costs and staying competitive.
- Freyr will leverage economical renewable energy, noting that 100% of industrial electricity onshore in Norway comes from hydro or other green power sources.
- The company claims it probably pays the lowest power prices in the world compared with other battery makers.
- Producing batteries with green energy can cut down the carbon footprint by 25 to 30%.
- If cathode materials are also made with renewable energy, it would reduce more than 50% of carbon emissions during the whole battery production process compared to many China-based manufacturers.
- Freyr has licensed the 24M SemiSolid™ production platform from MIT startup 24M Technologies.
- The platform enables an optimized production process with smaller and simpler facilities while making much larger and thicker electrodes.
- The platform requires less than half of the operators that a conventional battery plant needs and allows for three times more batteries per employee.
- CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated that half the CapEx, 20 to 25% lower OpEx, and a localized supply chain enable them to be very competitive while having the cleanest battery solutions on the planet.
- Freyr formed a joint venture with Koch Strategic Platforms to build a gigafactory in the US.
- The joint venture duo has looked into more than 150 sites in the country and narrowed the list down to five.
- Freyr will also expand to Finland in the City of Vaasa.
- The company cited Finland's abundant renewable energy, minerals and metals, and workforce competency as advantages for its planned battery factory.
- The startup recently opened a technology resources campus and business unit in Japan.
- The new Japanese office enables Freyr to access battery engineering proficiency, next-generation solutions, and local partnerships to scale up rapidly.
- Freyr has secured initial customer agreements with companies focusing on energy storage systems (ESS).
- On August 30, the company announced a battery cell sales agreement with Nidec Corporation of Japan.
- The technology is particularly suitable for ESS, but the company has also received significant interest from EV and commercial mobility segments, with two times the contract volume for ESS.
- Freyr aims to reach 50GWh of annual production capacity by 2025, 100GWh by 2028, and 200GWh by 2030.
- The company is considering building anode manufacturing facilities besides producing cathode materials with Aleees.
- Freyr plans to diversify its technology platform beyond 24M to offer a broader set of battery technologies.
- CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated the company has no restrictions on who they partner with and welcomes any companies that see them as a relevant partner.
