# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-14

**Source:** [SungEel's Stephens County facility's groundbreaking set for March 2023 - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/sungeels-stephens-county-facilitys-groundbreaking-set-for-march-2023/)  ·  `page_050840`

## Facts

- SungEel’s groundbreaking for its first U.S. recycling facility is scheduled for March 2023.
- Stephens County Development Authority President and CEO Brittany Ivey stated the South Korea-based company anticipates breaking ground in March with project completion by the end of 2023.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced in August 2022 that lithium-ion battery recycler and raw materials provider SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Stephens County.
- The company is expected to invest more than $37 million in the new facility.
- The project is expected to create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- SungEel utilizes over 10 years of patented technology specializing in complete recycling via a 100 percent full circulatory system.
- The recycling system processes end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap with a metal recovery rate exceeding 95 percent.
- Recovered metals from the process include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia’s new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park in Toccoa.
- The site is certified under the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop sites ready for fast-track development.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will hire technicians, operators, administrative staff, and sales personnel.
