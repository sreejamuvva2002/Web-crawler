# Ajin Georgia

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[automotive-components|Automotive Components]]  ·  **Location:** Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, Bulloch County, Bulloch County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

AJIN Georgia is a Tier 1 automotive supplier that opened a state-of-the-art plant in Bulloch County to produce interior metal and structural parts for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA). The $317 million facility creates 630 new jobs and marks a major milestone for the region’s electric-vehicle supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

First Tier 1 supplier announced for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Georgia, supporting EV interior and structural component production.

## Key facts

- AJIN Georgia is the first Tier 1 supplier announced for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Georgia.
- The $317 million plant spans 82 acres and utilizes hundreds of robots for interior metal and structural part production.
- The facility will create 630 new jobs, with over 200 of the initial 300 positions already filled at opening.
- AJIN Georgia can train an employee with no prior production experience in less than a week to perform most production jobs.
- State officials highlighted Georgia’s decade-long status as the top state for business and its growing EV supply chain ecosystem.
- The Council General of the Republic of Korea praised AJIN Georgia’s role in establishing an electric-vehicle supply chain in the southeastern United States.
- The Ajin Georgia facility is roughly the size of 15 football fields.
- It employs regional workers and supplies vehicle parts for Hyundai’s upcoming EV plant.
- Construction progressed from a pad of dirt to an operational plant in under 14 months.
- The plant is located six miles outside Register, GA, along Interstate 16.
