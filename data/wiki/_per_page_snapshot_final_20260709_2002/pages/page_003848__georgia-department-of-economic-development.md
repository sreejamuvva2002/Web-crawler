# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-10

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Georgia Shatters Investment and Job Records in FY22 | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-08-10/gov-kemp-georgia-shatters-investment-and-job-records-fy22)  ·  `page_003848`

## Facts

- Georgia achieved another record year for economic development investments and job creation during fiscal year 2022.
- FY22 investments and job creation were 94 percent and 53 percent above prior economic development records, respectively.
- Investments in expansions and new locations totaled more than $21.2 billion between July 1, 2021, and June 30, 2022.
- 51,132 jobs were created in the state through 358 projects supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team during FY22.
- Excluding Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group, Georgia's FY22 jobs and investments totaled 35,532 jobs and $10.7 billion.
- Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group were two of 12 automotive projects that created a total of nearly 16,000 new jobs across the state.
- Eighty-five percent of FY22 investments and more than 30,000 new jobs came from outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- Companies choosing to expand in Georgia accounted for 74 percent of total projects, while new locations created 33,846 jobs across the state.
- Advanced manufacturing added more than 10,000 new jobs in the state during FY22.
- Both advanced manufacturing and automotive industries include manufacturers and suppliers for the electric vehicle (EV) industry.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) development includes EV battery manufacturing along with affiliate suppliers on-site.
- Component recyclers Aurubis and Ascend Elements have joined HMGMA as part of Georgia’s efforts to build a sustainable, reliable supply chain for its electric mobility ecosystem.
- Lt. Governor Geoff Duncan stated that Georgia is working towards its shared goal to be the technology capital of the East Coast and continues to attract critical investments necessary to achieve that goal.
- Speaker David Ralston credited strong public-private partnerships, lowering the tax burden on businesses and families, and incentivizing workforce development and investment for creating the nation’s best environment for business.
- Access to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport (the busiest and most efficient airport in the world), the Port of Savannah (the single largest and fastest-growing container terminal in America), and a robust network of highways and rail lines support Georgia's logistics and distribution industry.
- Foreign direct investment (FDI) accounted for over $8 billion in investments created in fiscal year 2022.
- The top five foreign direct investment sources were South Korea, Germany, Japan, France, and the Netherlands.
- Korea-based projects represented the highest job creation and investment from a single country, with Germany producing the second most jobs and investments after Korea.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson led economic development missions to the United Kingdom, South Korea, and Germany in FY22.
- Meetings were held with economic development drivers like Hyundai Motor Group and SK On during these missions.
- Discussions regarding new opportunities for development occurred with the German state of North Rhine-Westphalia and the Liverpool City Region Freeport.
- GDEcD offers 'Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development' (GRAD) certification to recognize pre-qualified industrial sites where due diligence is completed prior to a prospect visit.
- Through GRAD, Georgia has certified more than 60 industrial sites as ready for fast-track industrial projects.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program provides specialized training tailored specifically to business needs and has developed this workforce training capability for 55 years.
- Since Governor Kemp took office, more than 119,963 new jobs and over $42.9 billion in investment have been announced in communities across the state.
- Seventy-seven percent of state economic development projects, 81 percent of investments, and 57 percent of jobs since Kemp took office have gone to communities outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area.
