# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Taxes & State of Georgia Incentives | Fairburn, GA](https://www.fairburn.com/175/Taxes-State-of-Georgia-Incentives)  ·  `page_015346`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia attracts successful companies due to a pro-business environment, a talented workforce, world-class infrastructure, and unparalleled access to the world market.
- Georgia offers a performance-based package of business assistance designed to foster success for companies and the Georgia communities they call home.
- Strategic industries such as distribution, technology, manufacturing, telecom, processing companies, and their headquarters qualify for Georgia's Job Tax Credit.
- Depending on the community's tier, companies must create between five and 25 net new jobs per year to qualify for the Jobs Tax Credit.
- For each year (up to five years) the jobs are maintained, qualified companies can claim a tax credit with a value of $750 to $3,500 per job, per year.
- Companies may claim a tax credit equal to 10% of their increased qualified research and development expense over gross receipts under Georgia's R&D Tax Credits program.
- To qualify for the R&D tax credit, Georgia taxpayers must qualify for a research credit under the IRS code.
- Emerging companies can apply for the R&D tax credit to offset their payroll withholding once all other tax liabilities have been exhausted for their first five years.
- Georgia allows small businesses making capital investments of less than $410,000 to write off up to $102,000 of these expenses in the current year under Small Business Tax Relief.
- For capital investments greater than $410,000, the Small Business Tax Relief write-off is reduced dollar for dollar.
- Qualified equipment purchases are exempt from sales tax when the equipment is used in the manufacturing process under Georgia's Sales & Use Tax Exemption.
- Under Georgia's local option Freeport law, 100% of qualified raw materials, work-in-process, and finished goods are exempt from property tax under the Inventory Tax Exemption.
- Quick Start is Georgia's nationally ranked employee training program that provides customized training for new employees in skill-based jobs at no cost to qualifying companies.
- The Quick Start training program is given to the company for its future use.
- Quick Start provides training space, instructors, and all needed materials related to the program.
- Quick Start has the potential to save companies millions of dollars in training costs.
- Georgia Work Ready Hiring Assistance Program helps companies hire the best people and boost their bottom line.
- The Georgia Work Ready program is available at no cost for companies meeting minimum hiring requirements.
- The Georgia Work Ready program is accessible through the state's network of technical colleges.
- Georgia companies can implement Work Ready in two ways: through job profiling and Work Ready Certificates.
- Work Ready job profiles identify the job tasks and skill levels necessary to be successful in any job.
- Companies match those job profiles to employees' Work Ready Certificates, which measure core skills, to ensure the right person is placed in the right job every time.
