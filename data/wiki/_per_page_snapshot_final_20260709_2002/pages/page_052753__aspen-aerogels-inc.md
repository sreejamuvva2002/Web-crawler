# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels announced a commercial award for its PyroThin® EV Thermal Barrier segment.
- The company received an additional PyroThin vehicle platform award from The Automotive Cells Company (ACC).
- ACC is a battery cell joint venture between Stellantis N.V., Saft-TotalEnergies, and Mercedes-Benz.
- Aspen will supply the Stellantis STLA Medium vehicle platform with an expected start of production in 2025.
- Stellantis is one of the world's leading automakers with brands including Jeep, Ram, Fiat, Peugeot, Citroën, DS Automobiles, Chrysler, Dodge, Opel, Vauxhall, Alfa Romeo, Lancia, Maserati, Abarth, Free2move, and Leasys.
- Aspen received a letter from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Loan Programs Office confirming its pending loan application under the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) program is substantially complete.
- The DOE invited Aspen to formal due diligence for the loan application.
- The loan application is in connection with the construction of Aspen's planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The DOE invitation does not guarantee or imply that Aspen will be invited further forward in the process.
- Aspen continues to engage with the LPO and its advisors regarding the loan.
- DOE's invitation into due diligence and term sheet negotiation is not an assurance that a loan will be issued or that terms will align with applicant proposals.
- Don Young, Aspen's President and CEO, stated these developments are meaningful steps in near-term execution to diversify the customer base for PyroThin and optimize cost of capital.
- The European market will drive a meaningful portion of Aspen's revenues over the next few years as it maximizes existing assets and commercial arrangements.
- Aspen's aerogel technology enables customers and partners to achieve objectives around resource efficiency, e-mobility, and clean energy.
- PyroThin products enable solutions to thermal runaway challenges within the electric vehicle market.
- Aspen Battery Materials is the company's carbon aerogel initiative aimed at increasing lithium-ion battery cell performance to extend EV driving range and reduce EV costs.
- Aspen manufactures its products at its East Providence, R.I. facilities.
- Aspen is headquartered in Northborough, Mass.
- Aspen expects extended construction and commissioning timeframes for its planned second manufacturing plant in Georgia (Plant II).
- Aspen plans to manage the construction of Plant II to align with expectations of demand from EV customers.
- Aspen may use contract manufacturers to meet demand from Energy Industrial customers.
- EV thermal barrier customers have the right to cancel contracts with Aspen at any time and without penalty.
- Aspen faces risks regarding costs, expenses, or investments incurred in excess of projections used to develop pricing under contracts with EV thermal barrier customers.
- Aspen faces risks regarding its inability to create customer or market opportunities for PyroThin or other battery performance and safety products.
- Disruption or inability to achieve expected capacity levels could occur at any of the three existing production lines in East Providence, RI, the Mexico assembly facility, or at any contract manufacturer.
