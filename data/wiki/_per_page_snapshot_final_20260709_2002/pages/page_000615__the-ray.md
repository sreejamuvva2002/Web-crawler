# The Ray

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2020-06-15

**Source:** [New Solar-Powered EV Charging Station on I-85 in Georgia Delivers 300% More Power | Ray C. Anderson Foundation](https://www.raycandersonfoundation.org/articles/new-solar-powered-ev-charging-station-in-georgia-delivers-300-percent-more-power)  ·  `page_000615`

## Facts

- The Ray, Donovan Energy, and ABB partnered to upgrade the solar-powered electric vehicle charging station located at the visitor information center off Interstate 85 in West Point, Georgia.
- The upgraded charging station was commissioned on June 3, 2020.
- Charging at this location is free.
- The Ray is a nonprofit organization and an 18-mile living transportation lab located along the I-85 corridor between LaGrange, Georgia, and the Georgia/Alabama state line.
- The original station was a 50 kW unit installed in 2015 by The Ray in partnership with Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia.
- That original station was the Southeast’s first solar-powered EV charging station, and prior to its installation, travel between the state capitols of Georgia and Alabama was not possible for the majority of electric vehicles.
- The new charger is an ABB 175 kW Terra HP, a modular, high-powered charging system designed to minimize charging time.
- John Picard, Vice President of The Ray, stated the initiative aims to demonstrate what’s next and showcase the electrification of transportation by showing how easy it is to store energy and rapidly recharge.
- Georgia DOT Commissioner Russell R. McMurry, P.E., noted pride in collaborating with The Ray on drive-over tire pressure checks, visitor center solar panels, the upgraded EV charging station, and testing emerging technologies like all-weather striping for machine-operated vehicles along I-85 to bring sustainable innovations to transportation.
- Donovan Energy served as the technology partner for this electrification project, recommended and procured the ABB modular system, and is a clean energy project development and finance company based in Cincinnati that has delivered over 400 energy efficiency and building retrofit projects since 2016.
- The ABB Terra HP is future-proofed as a modular system, allowing a second 175 kW power unit to be added to scale up to 350 kW for the next generation of EVs.
- Jeff Martin, Chief Operating Officer of Donovan Energy, stated the flexible architecture of the ABB Terra HP high power solution was an ideal fit for The Ray's upgrade goals.
- Bob Stojanovic, Director of ABB’s EV Infrastructure business in North America, stated ABB is committed to deploying technology for societal advancement efficiently and sustainably, and is privileged to deploy DC fast charging technology fitting The Ray's vision.
- Harriet Langford, President of The Ray, stated the debut of high-powered, 175kW fast charging on the interstate highlights dedication to transportation electrification and demonstrates how The Ray can evolve and upgrade previously piloted technologies.
- Tim Echols of the state's Public Service Commission stated that having EV chargers in convenient locations like rest stops is the next frontier for electric vehicle charging, with The Ray, Georgia DOT, and City of West Point leading the way from theory to reality.
- The solar-powered EV (PV4EV) charging station is grid-connected to ensure operability day or night and all year long.
- Clean energy generated by twelve-panel Solar LIFT adds renewable resources to the local grid that supplies power to the ABB Terra HP, ensuring clean cars are fueled by clean, renewable energy on The Ray on I-85.
- The Ray is a proving ground for evolving transportation ideas and technologies, named after Ray C. Anderson (1934-2011), a Georgia native and industry captain recognized as a leader in green business who challenged Atlanta-based Interface, Inc. to pursue zero environmental footprint.
- The Ray is chaired by Ray’s daughter Harriet Langford.
- Donovan Energy approaches energy in new ways to help customers reduce use and save money, offering solutions for procurement, PACE finance, LED/HVAC/envelope solutions, and renewable energy production across education, commercial real estate, healthcare, municipal, manufacturing, nonprofit, and parking sectors.
- Georgia DOT plans, constructs, and maintains state and federal highways, is involved in bridge, waterway, public transit, rail, aviation, bike and pedestrian programs, helps local governments maintain roads, has nearly 4,000 employees, and focuses on innovation, safety, sustainability, and mobility.
- ABB (NYSE: ABB) is a global engineering company energizing society and industry transformation, connecting software to electrification, robotics, automation, and motion portfolios, with over 130 years of history and 144,000 employees in over 100 countries.
