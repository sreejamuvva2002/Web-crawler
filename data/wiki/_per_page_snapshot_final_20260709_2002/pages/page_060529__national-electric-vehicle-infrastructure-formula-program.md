# National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Formula Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The NEVI Funding Saga: The Need, the Freeze, and the Challenge - Southern Alliance for Clean Energy (SACE)](https://cleanenergy.org/news/the-nevi-funding-saga-the-need-the-freeze-and-the-challenge/)  ·  `page_060529`

## Facts

- On May 22, Southern Alliance for Clean Energy joined a coalition of environmental, consumer, and community organizations to challenge the Trump administration’s unlawful and indefinite freeze of billions in federal funding for the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program.
- In 2022, Congress allocated $5 billion for the program through the bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA).
- The program was designed to build electric vehicle charging stations every 50 miles on major corridors across all 50 states.
- In February, the Federal Highway Administration (FHWA), acting on behalf of the Trump administration, halted the funds from flowing with the issuance of a memorandum.
- The Southeast’s portion of the frozen funding totals over $679 million.
- The NEVI program was designed to increase access to high-speed charging infrastructure along the interstate system and build confidence among American drivers that electric vehicles can function reliably for long-distance travel.
- Currently, the Southeast lacks access to charging stations, with only 0.40 charging stations per 1,000 people.
- This lack of accessible charging keeps consumers from having the confidence to purchase and drive electric vehicles.
- Southeast states have significantly underinvested in charging station deployment as a region, so formula funds would guarantee investment and increased access for EV drivers.
- The Southeast has long stretches of highway in rural areas currently without access to highway charging stations, and NEVI would increase investments and economic opportunities to these rural areas similar to the Rural Electrification Administration's impact on electrical power.
- Many electric vehicles that the NEVI network would support are built in the Southeast, which is home to 31% of the national total labor force that manufactures EVs.
- The region relies heavily on tourists and snowbirds who require access to fast charging while driving long distances, and the funding would provide up to 80% of the funding to install charging to meet this need.
- Funds need to be restored so infrastructure that supports healthier, lower-cost transportation is available to every American, especially Southerners in rural communities without access to reliable high-speed charging.
- Stephen Smith, Executive Director of the Southern Alliance for Clean Energy, stated that suspending the NEVI Program puts hard-won progress at risk and leaves Southern communities without the resources to lead in the transition to clean energy.
- The Government Accountability Office (GAO) found that the February Federal Highway Administration memo directing the freeze violates the Impoundment Control Act.
- The White House subsequently ordered the Department of Transportation to ignore the GAO ruling.
- On June 25, a federal judge in Washington issued a preliminary ruling ordering the Trump administration to restore NEVI Formula Program funding, unlocking about $1 billion in federal funds while the case moves forward.
- The court order did not take effect until July 2 to provide the federal government an opportunity to appeal, which they did not.
- The ruling restores funding for 14 of the 17 Plaintiff States.
- Minnesota, Vermont, and the District of Columbia were excluded from relief because the judge found they did not provide sufficient evidence on how the freeze harmed them.
- While the ruling is not a final decision, it provides forward momentum for states getting EV charging projects moving forward.
- SACE and allies are still awaiting a ruling on the motion to intervene and will continue seeking nationwide restoration of NEVI funds.
- Electrify the South is a Southern Alliance for Clean Energy program that leverages research, advocacy, and outreach to promote renewable energy and accelerate the equitable transition to electric transportation throughout the Southeast.
