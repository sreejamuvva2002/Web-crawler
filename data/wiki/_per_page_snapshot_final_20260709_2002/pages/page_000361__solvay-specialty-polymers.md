## Facts

- A Belgian company is expanding its operations in Augusta by building a new electric vehicle battery component factory.
- Solvay Specialty Polymers announced Thursday it will build a plant next to its existing Augusta factory.
- The new plant will manufacture EV-grade binders and separator coatings.
- Sen Raphael Warnock announced the project in a press release.
- The parts produced by the factory are critical components within the lithium-ion batteries that power EVs.
- Warnock credited a $178 million federal grant awarded to Solvay for the new factory.
- Warnock is a Democrat in the middle of a re-election campaign against Republican Herschel Walker.
- In a prior release, Warnock stated he was proud to fight for and help secure the $178 million needed for the new facility.
- Warnock pledged to continue working closely with businesses looking to start or expand in Georgia to secure critical investments that will help create local, good-paying jobs.
