# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis to Open State-of-the-Art Recycling and Copper Smelting Facility in Augusta, GA - Gain](https://businessscrap.com/aurubis-to-open-state-of-the-art-recycling-and-copper-smelting-facility-in-augusta-ga/)  ·  `page_017327`

## Facts

- Aurubis will invest $340 million in a state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- The new facility will be the very first of its kind in the U.S.
- The facility will create 125 jobs in the Augusta area.
- Governor Kemp stated that this marks the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Aurubis plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics from the Augusta facility.
- The facility will be built on 150 acres at Augusta Corporate Park, which is a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- Operations are expected to begin by the first half of 2024.
- The company plans to begin hiring for positions as soon as summer 2022.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated that the investment sets a clear example of sustainable growth and will make the company a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S.
- Aurubis’ commitment to sustainable development and raw material security will play a role in building Georgia’s electric mobility value chain.
- The project underscores the strengths of Georgia’s ports system, highly-skilled workforce, and pro-business environment, according to Governor Kemp.
- Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
