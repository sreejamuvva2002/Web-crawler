# Hyundai-SK On Battery Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow Centre, Highway 411, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A new Hyundai electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility in partnership with SK On will be built at the Bartow Centre industrial site in Bartow County, creating thousands of jobs and billions of dollars of investment for Georgia's EV supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturing that will help supply automotive manufacturers across the United States with lithium-ion cells for electric vehicles.

## Key facts

- The new Hyundai electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility will bring billions of dollars of investment and thousands of new jobs to Georgia.
- Officials estimate the facility will invest approximately $4 to $5 billion into Bartow County.
- The plant is expected to create more than 3,500 new jobs.
- Located at the former Bartow Centre industrial site on Highway 411.
- Operations are expected to begin in 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp highlighted Hyundai Motor Group and SK On as valued partners key to Georgia's automotive industry.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor said the project will have regional benefit for job seekers and establish Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem.
- SK On currently employs over 2,000 workers at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce, GA.
- The construction of the plant is being investigated by FOX 5 I-Team for alleged worker safety issues.
