# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-10

**Source:** [Down 23%, Is It Finally Time to Buy Archer Aviation Stock? | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/05/10/down-23-is-it-finally-time-to-buy-archer-aviation/)  ·  `page_016905`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is pioneering electric vertical takeoff and landing vehicles (eVTOLs) with a market cap of just $4.8 billion.
- Morgan Stanley analysts believe the eVTOL industry could be worth $1.5 trillion by 2040.
- The company plans to launch its own air taxi service alongside manufacturing, utilizing vertical integration to expand revenue opportunities and unlock economies of scale.
- Archer Aviation teamed up with global automotive giant Stellantis to take advantage of expertise in large-scale production and supply chains.
- Archer Aviation and Stellantis created a 400,000-square-foot facility (called ARC) in Georgia.
- The ARC facility aims to produce 650 units of Archer Aviation's flagship Midnight eVTOL aircraft annually by 2030.
- Fourth-quarter sales were just $300,000 (up from zero in the prior year period), mainly coming from early-stage partnership agreements instead of recurring operating revenue.
- The Midnight eVTOL has not secured approvals from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) necessary for commercial use.
- Operating losses reached almost $234.4 million as the company continues to pour cash into research and development to meet government testing requirements.
- Archer Aviation holds just over $1 billion in cash and equivalents on its balance sheet.
- The company will likely rely on continued equity dilution (issuing and selling more stock shares) to fund operations.
- Management believes the company can deliver piloted flights later this year and provide bona fide air taxi services during the 2028 Olympic Games.
- FAA approval timelines are outside management's direct control because government agencies are not known for rushing.
