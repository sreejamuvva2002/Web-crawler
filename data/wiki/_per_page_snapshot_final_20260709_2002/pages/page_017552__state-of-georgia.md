# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-30

**Source:** [Georgia Budget Trends Primer for State Fiscal Year 2026 - Georgia Budget and Policy Institute](https://gbpi.org/georgia-budget-trends-primer-for-state-fiscal-year-2026/)  ·  `page_017552`

## Facts

- Gov. Kemp signed a $37.8 billion state budget for FY 2026, representing a 4.5% year-over-year increase from the prior year.
- Georgia offers tax breaks that will cost the state more than $11.1 billion in forgone revenue collections in FY 2026, with some delivering outsized gains to manufacturing, film, and insurance industries.
- Senate Bill 366, enacted during the 2024 legislative session, allows General Assembly tax-writing committee chairs to request economic analyses of tax expenditures, permitting at least 12 per year.
- House Bill 111, signed by the governor in April, retroactively reduced Georgia's flat corporate and personal income tax rates to 5.19% as of January 2025.
- The state's flat income tax rate is scheduled to drop to 5.09% in January 2026 if overall revenue growth meets the required 3% benchmark.
- At the close of 2024, Georgia’s Revenue Shortfall Reserve stood at $5.5 billion, equivalent to 15% of prior year revenues.
