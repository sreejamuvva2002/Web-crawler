# Right to Work Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [What Businesses are Coming to Right to Work Georgia? - NRTWC](https://nrtwc.org/what-businesses-are-coming-to-right-to-work-georgia-3/)  ·  `page_001720`

## Facts

- Three new businesses are coming soon to Right to Work Georgia: Brightmark, Duckyang, and IC Biomedical.
- Each one will be adding a new location somewhere within the state of Georgia.
- Brightmark will be locating in Macon-Bibb with a $680 million investment.
- Duckyang will be creating 285 new jobs with their new location in Braselton.
- IC Biomedical is adding a new facility in Cartersville.
- Altogether, these businesses will create an economic boost.
- Brightmark is investing $680 million soon in Right to Work Macon-Bibb County, Georgia.
- Brightmark’s advanced recycling facility will be the world’s largest and will utilize proven plastics renewal technology.
- The project aims to advance a mission of reimagining waste while supporting economic development in the region.
- Georgia’s pro-business environment and support for private-sector innovation continues to pave the way toward a more sustainable future.
- A quote expresses thanks to Brightmark for investing in Georgia and the hardworking Georgians of Macon.
- Duckyang is investing $10 million soon toward its first US manufacturing location in Right to Work Braselton, Georgia.
- Duckyang’s investment will create 285 new jobs.
- In Georgia, the focus is on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain.
- Strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies, such as Duckyang, are helping grow the electric mobility supply chain.
- The project helps bring suppliers to the state in order to secure a local supply of needed parts.
- Partners in Jackson County supported these projects.
- The automotive industry continues to move toward electric.
- Duckyang is helping Jackson County and the State of Georgia secure a place in the industry’s future.
- IC Biomedical is investing millions toward a new manufacturing facility in Right to Work Cartersville, Georgia.
- IC Biomedical’s investment will create over 80 new jobs.
- Georgia was chosen for IC Biomedical due to a highly trained workforce, transport links, and state infrastructure committed to the life science sector.
- IC Biomedical joins metro Atlanta’s growing life sciences community with assets like Emory School of Medicine, Morehouse School of Medicine, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Georgia Tech, and many businesses engaged in research, development, manufacturing, and distribution of life science related products and supplies.
