# Hyundai-SK On Georgia EV Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-25

**Source:** [Hyundai and SK On plan $5B Georgia EV battery plant | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/Hyundai-SK-On-plan-5B-Georgia-EV-battery-joint-venture/648576/)  ·  `page_000458`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and battery maker SK On finalized plans for a $5 billion EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The boards of Hyundai Motor Company, Kia Corporation and Hyundai Mobis approved the joint venture.
- Hyundai and its battery partner will each take a 50% stake in the investment to supply the automaker’s U.S. production facilities.
- The facility is expected to start making battery cells by the second half of 2025.
- The battery cells will support production of 300,000 all-electric Hyundai, Kia and Genesis vehicles yearly.
- Hyundai is working to secure a stable supply of batteries as it builds out its EV production capabilities in the U.S.
- The automaker signed a memorandum of understanding for an EV production facility with SK On in November.
- This signing occurred the month after Hyundai broke ground in Savannah, Georgia on its first dedicated EV campus in the U.S.
- Heung-soo Kim, the automaker’s head of corporate future growth planning and EV divisions, said in November that the MOU will allow Hyundai to accelerate production in the North American EV market.
- Heung-soo Kim, who is also executive vice president, stated that the stable supply of EV batteries from SK On will enable Hyundai to contribute to emissions reduction and meet climate goals in the market.
- The Bartow County facility is strategically located within a couple hundred miles of the Hyundai operations it is intended to supply.
- The supplied Hyundai operations include Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, Kia Georgia and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp called Hyundai Motor Group and SK On "valued partners and key players in our state’s ever-growing automotive industry" in a Dec. 8 statement.
- The partners selected Bartow County for the facility on Dec. 8.
- SK On subsidiary SK Battery America recently exceeded its hiring goal of 2,600 workers for two Georgia facilities.
- These two Georgia facilities will manufacture batteries for Ford and Volkswagen’s EVs.
