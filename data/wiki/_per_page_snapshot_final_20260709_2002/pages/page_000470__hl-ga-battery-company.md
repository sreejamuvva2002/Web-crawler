# HL-GA Battery Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-04

**Source:** [Federal agents raid Hyundai supplier at Bryan Co. megasite - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/federal-agents-raid-hyundai-supplier-at-bryan-co-megasite/)  ·  `page_000470`

## Facts

- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Homeland Security Investigation (HSI) officers came onto the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America megasite Thursday to serve a criminal warrant at one of the plant’s suppliers being built at the site.
- ICE and Homeland Security agents arrived at the HL-GA Battery Company construction site in Ellabell, which sits next to Hyundai’s electric-vehicle producing Metaplant.
- LG Energy Solutions is the parent of HL-GA Battery and is partnering with Hyundai to build the battery plant at the site.
- Officials announced they had a warrant and ordered construction to stop immediately.
- Mary Beth Kennedy, senior public relations specialist for HL-GA Battery Company, issued a statement at 3 p.m.
- HL-GA Battery Company stated it is cooperating fully with appropriate authorities regarding activity at its construction site.
- HL-GA Battery Company paused construction to assist law enforcement's work.
- HL-GA Battery Company does not have further details on the situation at the time of the statement.
- A Hyundai spokesman stated the company is cooperating with law enforcement and committed to abiding by all labor and immigration regulations.
- Multiple buses and other law enforcement vehicles were seen at the megasite, but no information about people being detained has been released.
- ICE and Homeland Security officials have not released any details regarding the raid.
- Georgia State Patrol confirmed they were assisting federal agencies in serving the warrant.
- Georgia State Patrol assisted with traffic and air control around the Bryan County plant off Interstate 16.
