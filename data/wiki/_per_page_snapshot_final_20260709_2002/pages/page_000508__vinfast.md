# VinFast

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-26

**Source:** [North Carolina sues EV maker VinFast over unbuilt factory | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/north-carolina-sues-ev-maker-vinfast-unbuilt-factory-chatham-county/821097/)  ·  `page_000508`

## Facts

- North Carolina is suing automaker VinFast after the Vietnam-based company allegedly failed to meet obligations related to the construction of a proposed electric vehicle and battery manufacturing plant in Chatham County.
- Attorney General Jeff Jackson filed the lawsuit on Thursday.
- The lawsuit alleges that because VinFast “breached its agreements with the state,” North Carolina is exercising its contractual right to acquire the 1,977-acre site and protect taxpayer money tied to the project.
- VinFast said it does not expect the facility to be operational until 2028.
- The state argued that a 2028 operational date is two years past the agreed upon construction deadline.
- North Carolina awarded a job development investment grant for the project.
- North Carolina appropriated up to $450 million for site development and infrastructure improvements in 2022.
- Four years ago, VinFast, an all-electric vehicle manufacturer, agreed to invest more than $3 billion in a manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- The original agreement included plans to create 7,500 jobs.
- At the time of the announcement, the project was considered the largest economic development announcement in North Carolina history.
- The state said there has been “zero progress” on the project since the initial agreement.
- The state argued that VinFast had “defaulted” on its agreements.
- Attorney General Jeff Jackson stated: “Vinfast agreed to build a factory and create jobs for North Carolinians — it didn’t do either.”
- Jackson added: “When North Carolina makes a deal, we build in protection for taxpayers. VinFast broke the deal, so we’re using that protection to find a project for this site that will create jobs.”
- According to the state’s complaint, VinFast failed to begin “vertical construction” of its buildings and structures as of January 2024.
- VinFast has not provided evidence that the project could be completed on a delayed timeline.
- VinFast had requested an extension until July 1, 2028.
- VinFast cited slowdowns in the EV market affecting the project and its ability to secure funding as reasons for the extension request.
- The state declined VinFast’s request for an extension.
- The state argued that VinFast won’t be able to meet its job creation commitments this year.
- VinFast failed to file its 2025 annual report.
- VinFast failed to respond to state requests for a project update.
- VinFast ceased operations on the site in December 2024, according to the complaint.
- The complaint states: “VinFast has failed to take any concrete action that shows it can and will fulfill its obligations.”
- The complaint notes: “Under the terms of the agreements, the State has a right and a duty to protect taxpayer dollars and ensure a prime industrial site is used for its highest and best purpose.”
- In January, North Carolina’s Justice Department notified VinFast that it had defaulted on its agreements.
- VinFast argued that it had met its construction deadlines.
- VinFast plans to open the facility by 2028.
