# Honda Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-12

**Source:** [Honda Announces Losses Associated with Reassessment of Automobile Electrification Strategy; Revision to Forecast for Consolidated Financial Results; and Future Direction | Honda Global Corporate Website](https://global.honda/en/newsroom/news/2026/c260312eng.html)  ·  `page_008923`

## Facts

- Honda announced the cancellation of development and market launch of three EV models that had been planned for production in North America: the Honda 0 SUV, Honda 0 Saloon, and Acura RSX.
- The decision was made as part of a reassessment of Honda's automobile electrification strategy due to various factors including recent changes in the business environment.
- Honda now expects to record losses in its consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ending March 2026, resulting in the revision of previously announced forecasts for the consolidated financial results for the fiscal year.
- U.S. EV market expansion has slowed down due to several factors including the easing of fossil fuel regulations and revisions to EV incentives.
- Changes in U.S. tariff policies have unfavorably impacted the profitability of Honda's gasoline and hybrid vehicle business.
- Honda expects to record write-off and impairment losses on tangible and intangible assets that were intended to be used for the production of the three cancelled EV models, as well as losses related to additional expenses resulting from the cancellation of their development and sales.
- In consideration of intensified competition in China, Honda reassessed the recoverability of investments accounted for using the equity method in China and now expects to incur an impairment loss on those investments.
- For the consolidated financial results for the current fiscal year, Honda expects operating expenses of 820 billion yen to 1.12 trillion yen.
- Honda expects a share of loss from investments accounted for using the equity method of 110 billion yen to 150 billion yen for the current fiscal year.
- Honda expects special losses of 340 billion to 570 billion yen in its non-consolidated financial results for the same fiscal year.
- These amounts are preliminary estimates as of March 12, 2026, and will be finalized in the consolidated and non-consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026.
- Combined with losses to be recorded in the current fiscal year, total losses associated with the reassessment of the automobile electrification strategy are expected to be a maximum of 2.5 trillion yen.
- Additional expenses or losses may be incurred in connection with the above-mentioned reassessment in the next fiscal year or later.
- Honda adopted a dividend on equity (DOE) ratio as its shareholder redistribution indicator to achieve more stable and continuous dividends despite the revision to consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026.
- Despite the revised forecasts, Honda made no revision to the forecast for the dividend per share for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026.
- In light of the recent slowdown in EV market growth in the U.S., Honda will reassess its resource allocations and further strengthen its hybrid models.
- Honda plans to enhance its model lineup and cost competitiveness in India due to expected market expansion.
- In other countries in Asia, Honda will strive to enhance competitiveness by introducing next-generation hybrid models and reassessing the allocation of its resources.
- Honda will establish a fixed-cost structure appropriate for its scale and implement future EV introductions flexibly from a long-term perspective while monitoring the balance between profitability and market trends.
- Honda is planning to announce details of the reestablishment of its mid- to long-term strategy for its automobile business at a press conference in May 2026.
- Revised forecasts for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026 show sale revenue unchanged at 21,100 billion yen compared to previously announced forecasts.
- Operating profit revised forecast is -570.0 to -270.0 billion yen, down from a previously announced forecast of 550.0 billion yen.
- Profit before income taxes revised forecast is -650.0 to -310.0 billion yen, down from 620.0 billion yen.
- Profit for the year attributable to owners of the parent revised forecast is -630.0 to 360.0 billion yen, down from 360.0 billion yen.
- Share of profit (loss) of investments accounted for using the equity method revised forecast is -690.0 to -420.0 billion yen, down from 300.0 billion yen.
- Earnings per share attributable to owners of the parent revised forecast is -172.62 to -105.07 yen, down from 75.05 yen.
- Previous year results for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2025 showed sale revenue of 21,688.767 billion yen and operating profit of 1,213.486 billion yen.
- Some Honda Executive Officers will voluntarily return a part of their monthly executive compensation for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2027 due to the revised forecasts and losses.
- The President and Representative Executive Officer, and Vice President and Representative Executive Officer will return 30% of monthly compensation for three months.
- Executive Council members and Managing Executive Officers involved in automobile operations will return 20% of monthly compensation for three months.
- Only executives who held relevant responsibility during the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026 are included in the compensation return, excluding executives scheduled to retire as of that date.
- The President and Representative Executive Officer, and the Executive Vice President and Representative Executive Officer will forfeit their short-term performance-linked compensation (STI) for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026.
- Annual compensation of the Representative Executive Officers will be reduced by approximately 25% to 30% from the standard level.
