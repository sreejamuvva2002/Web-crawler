# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-16

**Source:** [EV Thermal Barrier Firm to Halt $325M Plant in Statesboro - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/ev-thermal-barrier-firm-to-halt-325m-plant-in-statesboro/)  ·  `page_000820`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels is abandoning a $325 million plant to make thermal barriers in Statesboro.
- It becomes the latest battery or component supplier to vacate a major Georgia project amid uneven demand for electric vehicles.
- Chief Financial Officer Ricardo Rodriguez stated the company is meeting long-term demands by investing to maximize capacity at its aerogel facility in Rhode Island.
- The company will supplement capacity by leveraging an external manufacturing model that worked well in 2024 for its energy industrial products.
- Aerogel sheets are used between battery cells to prevent what Aspen calls 'thermal runaway,' when lithium-ion batteries reach a critically high temperature and potentially burst into flames.
- Aspen is working with outside manufacturing partners, initially in China in the near term.
- The company enacted job and spending cuts designed to save $35 million per year.
- These moves are intended to shore up its balance sheet and accelerate its path to profitability.
- The 250 jobs promised when the Massachusetts-based company announced its location in Bulloch County in 2022 will never materialize.
- The company will assess options to derive value from assets in Statesboro, including relocating some equipment to upgrade and expand its existing plant, a path requiring minimal capital.
- This news comes a week after Freyr Battery, a Norwegian investor, also pulled the plug on a $2.6 billion Georgia plant in Coweta County.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County will look to activate clawback measures to recoup taxpayer funds spent on the 90-acre site.
- The plant was set to be located in what is now Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, the former Southern Gateway Commerce Park.
- This park is now home to two Korean suppliers to the Hyundai Meta Plant in nearby Bryan County: Ajin Georgia and SECO Ecoplastic.
- Combined, these two investments total more than $500 million.
- Aspen was tentatively approved in October for a massive loan by the Biden administration’s Department of Energy to support the Georgia plant.
- Aspen opted to forgo the debt to reduce external obligations even as it scrambled to ramp up production overall.
- CFO Ricardo Rodriguez noted it feels great not to take on an incremental $671 million of debt on the balance sheet while still fulfilling expected demand.
- In October, Aspen executives viewed low-cost capital as vital for a plant poised to receive an outpouring of $1 billion over multiple phases reaching 500,000 square feet.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp has decried the picking of 'winners and losers' by the government in the EV space, even as federal support has proven critical for investors in the state.
- Kemp's office previously incorporated Aspen into roundups about record investment hauls.
- In the coming quarter, Aspen projects a possible 'low point' for its EV thermal barrier business.
- General Motors, whose Ultium platform counts Aspen as an exclusive supplier, is clearing out finished-vehicle inventories built up in 2024.
- Longer-term, the company is optimistic, having landed deals with six OEMs including GM, Toyota and Valmet, a Porsche supplier.
- It was set to supply Audi and Scania through a deal with Northvolt, the now-bankrupt Swedish battery producer.
- Aspen is looking into 'plan B' options to fulfill that demand.
- President, CEO and Chairman Don Young stated they are confident electric vehicles will be the choice of a growing number of car buyers and that energy and power generation will be important macroeconomic drivers where they have an important role to play.
- Aspen swung to a narrow profit on $452.7 million in annual revenue, a 90 percent increase from the previous year.
- As early as 2023, the company had decided to delay construction on the plant in Register, 60 miles west of Savannah, 'pre-empting a reset in EV demand expectations.'
- The company was experimenting with contract manufacturers during the delay.
- When outside suppliers proved sufficient enough to support bids for new contracts, it was a 'no brainer' to avoid the large capital outlay.
- CFO Ricardo Rodriguez said they can more modularly build up the supply source and would not have gotten awards last year and this year if customers were not OK with that model.
- Aspen’s PyroThin thermal barrier recently won a Volvo Truck contract that the company expects to add $45 million in revenue per year when it becomes active.
