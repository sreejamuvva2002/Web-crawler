# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [YKK AP America Introduces Autonomous GTP System at Georgia Window Facility](https://www.usglassmag.com/ykk-ap-america-introduces-autonomous-gtp-system-at-georgia-window-facility/)  ·  `page_016067`

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. installed a new goods-to-person (GTP) automation system at its residential window plant in Macon, Georgia.
- The system was installed at the 400,000-square-foot purpose-built facility’s shipping department to further reduce the manual movement of heavy windows.
- Dennis Howard serves as senior manager of transportation and logistics, and Daymon Loyd serves as director of residential manufacturing.
- According to YKK AP officials, the GTP system improves operations and quality and reduces time to load by 39%.
- The GTP system uses autonomous robots to move finished products from the end of the line to material storage units and into the GTP field, a fenced area that houses final products.
- Daymon Loyd stated: 'Overall, our company is making strategic investments in automation to reduce risk and improve safety.'
- Loyd added: 'Specifically, in residential, our goal was to reduce material handling to improve quality. The shipping department is where most material handling is done, so it made sense to start there.'
- When the Macon facility opened in early 2024, YKK AP aimed for zero injuries, zero backorders and 100% on-time delivery.
- The facility currently achieves 97% on-time delivery rates, and to hit 100%, YKK AP needed to invest in the GTP system to close the gap.
- Loyd says the facility handles 80% of its products with GTP; the other 20% are too large for the material storage units.
- Howard says integrating the system with YKK AP’s IT infrastructure was the most significant deployment challenge.
- Howard notes: 'Additionally, training for the interface of the system (not the end user) served as a challenge, and we had to have an aggressive training schedule.'
- The bulk of the training involved troubleshooting technical issues as they arise because that’s one thing you can expect with any new technology.
- Howard and Loyd say that while YKK AP did not have to retrain employees, it still had to train them to use the specific system.
- Fortunately, the process was seamless thanks to its user-friendly interface.
- Howard says the GTP system is vital for companies seeking to improve safety via automation, but they must be open to change.
- Loyd notes: 'This was our residential manufacturing team’s first foray into GTP technology, and we had to remain open and flexible to changing the original plans to accommodate the technology correctly.'
- He adds that much of this capital equipment was on the manufacturing floor before it was in action, and it took time and trial and error to deploy.
- Now that it is, it’s been great for the team to have witnessed the project from start to finish, and now see it operating at its full potential.
