## Facts

- Constellium Automotive USA in White is laying off the majority of its workforce.
- A notice regarding the layoffs was filed with the Georgia Department of Labor.
- The Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice was filed on June 1, 2026.
- The company is laying off 57 of its 99 employees at its plant.
- The plant is located at 1 Constellium Drive in White, Georgia.
- The company stated that changing business needs require a mass layoff.
- The notice was submitted to the state Office of Workforce Development.
- The estimated first date of separation for impacted workers is August 1, 2026.
- More than sixty days notification was given to impacted workers on June 1, 2026.
- The WARN notice classifies the layoffs as a permanent layoff/reduction in force.
- Impacted positions include operator, material handler, maintenance tech, quality manager, EHS specialist, operations supervisor, and shift supervisor.
- The White plant originally opened in May 2017.
- The facility spans 135,000 square feet.
- The plant manufactures advanced aluminum automotive structural components and crash management systems.
- The facility supplies automakers in the Southeastern U.S.
- Plant operations include bending, machining, laser cutting, CMT welding, heat treatment, and quality control processes such as CMM, laser scanning, and tensile testing.
- Constellium’s headquarters is located in Paris, France.
- The company maintains corporate offices in Baltimore, Maryland, and Zurich, Switzerland.
- Constellium operates manufacturing sites across Europe, North America, and China.
- The company produces products for aerospace and transportation, packaging and automotive rolled products, and automotive structures and industry.
