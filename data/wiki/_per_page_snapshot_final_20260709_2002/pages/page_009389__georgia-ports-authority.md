# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-28

**Source:** [Georgia Ports Highlights Site Selection Opportunities — Savannah Traffic Club](https://www.savannahtrafficclub.org/news/georgia-ports-highlights-site-selection-opportunities)  ·  `page_009389`

## Facts

- For automakers and their suppliers, other manufacturers and logistics operations, Georgia’s ports are a key factor in the decision to bring jobs to the Peach State.
- GPA President and CEO Griff Lynch stated that GPA's mission is to drive success for the entire state, not just Savannah or Atlanta.
- GPA and GDEcD announced 350 projects from potential port users in Fiscal Year 2023.
- These announcements will bring more than 33,000 manufacturing and logistics jobs to the state.
- These announcements will bring $21 billion in investment to 96 counties across Georgia.
- GPA, GDEcD, and Georgia Department of Transportation are working to foster responsible growth delivering good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- Georgia Ports is investing $29 million in an overpass that will route trucks leaving Ocean Terminal in Savannah directly onto U.S. 17, avoiding community streets.
- GDEcD's Global Commerce division states Georgia’s growing electric vehicle ecosystem has touched every corner of the state.
- This EV ecosystem growth is led by announcements associated with Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Metaplant America locations have spread across more than 10 counties in the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated companies, especially in e-mobility, are prioritizing speed-to-market solutions.
- Investing in site development and obtaining “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” certification are ways communities can prepare for new opportunities.
- Anovion Technologies is a synthetic graphite manufacturer creating 400 jobs for Southwest Georgia in Decatur County.
- Anovion Executive Chairman Chip Dunn described the state’s recruitment effort that swayed the company’s decision to locate in rural Georgia.
- The recruitment effort included meetings with Gov. Brian Kemp and other factors like GPA’s inland port system, and access to waterway and land transport.
- GPA’s inland port system includes the Appalachian Regional Port in Crandall, Ga.
- The inland port system also includes the Blue Ridge Connector rail yard now under construction near Gainesville, Ga.
- The inland port system supports the movement of large, heavy products from centrally located communities to the state’s deepwater ports.
- In the Savannah area alone, industrial property inventory totals more than 124 million square feet.
- More than 14 million square feet of industrial property in the Savannah area was under construction at the close of Calendar Year 2023.
- Stephen Ezelle, a broker for Cushman & Wakefield, stated in a fourth-quarter report that the Savannah regional economy continues to experience expansion and strong absorption in the industrial sector despite a slowing U.S. economy.
- Ezelle stated the outlook for Savannah’s industrial market is favorable due to continued growth at Georgia Ports with conservative projections to eclipse 8 million TEUs of throughput by 2030.
- Operational efficiency offered by GPA and the shift in import cargo from the West Coast and NY/NJ are key drivers in the growth of Savannah’s industrial market, according to Ezelle.
- The report indicates Savannah is expected to continue balanced expansion thanks to the Hyundai announcement and GPA’s growing national market share.
- Ezelle predicts continued absorption of well-located light industrial warehouse space.
- Savannah’s industrial sector ended 2023 with an overall vacancy rate of 7 percent.
- GPA Director of economic and industrial development Stacy Watson stated being within 30 miles of the port is great, but not required to be competitive in drayage, lease rates, and cost of doing business.
- Watson stated forty or 50 miles away from Savannah is an easy drive, attributed to the port's location upriver of the Savannah population center and GDOT's efforts making truck routes less congested.
- Direct truck routes from Port of Savannah connect to Interstates 95 and 16.
- On-terminal service from two Class I railroads expedite cargo moving to and from the marine terminal.
- Watson noted available properties in Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh counties, and points west offer interstate and rail links to the port and inland markets.
- GPA's Stacy Watson cited Augusta as an example, noting a majority of manufacturers there are port users.
- Cal Wray, president of Development Authority of Augusta, Ga., stated the region is ripe for growth.
- Wray stated Augusta-Richmond County provides an ideal location for port users due to available, affordable industrial land, a key base for manufacturers with over 350 already calling Augusta home, and easy rail/highway connections to the port.
- Wray noted Augusta has a large industrial base with recent announcements including Aurubis Richmond (metal smelting, 250 jobs, $600 million investment).
- Recent announcement includes Denkai America (copper film producer, 250 jobs, $440 million investment).
- Recent announcement includes Purecycle Technologies (recycling plastic into like-new pellets for reuse, 100 jobs, $400 million investment).
- A stand-out industry ecosystem in Augusta is electric vehicle manufacturing, with EZGo Textron and Club Car and their related suppliers in Richmond and Columbia counties.
- Metals and chemical manufacturing are two other important industries in Augusta.
- Wray stated GPA supports efficient operation of existing industries and local economic development efforts.
- Wray stated GPA is a key partner in ongoing recruitment of new industry and a great resource to existing manufacturers who have invested billions in the region, many announcing significant further investments in the last five years.
