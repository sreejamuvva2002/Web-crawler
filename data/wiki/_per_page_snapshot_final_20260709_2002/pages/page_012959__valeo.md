# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-25

**Source:** [Valeo gives cautious 2022 outlook amid global uncertainties | Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/next/2022/02/25/valeo-outlook)  ·  `page_012959`

## Facts

- French car parts maker Valeo expected to report a lower core profit margin in 2022 amid geopolitical tensions and inflationary pressures, after missing its target in 2021.
- RBC analyst Tom Narayan noted that Valeo's H2/21 results were consistent with the Jan 26 pre-announce, but 2022 guidance was well below consensus expectations.
- Analyst Tom Narayan stated this cautious outlook aligns with Renault, Michelin, Faurecia, and Stellantis regarding the global chip shortage.
- Valeo shares dropped 10% in midday trade, heading for its worst day since March 2020.
- Semiconductor suppliers, car parts producers, and automakers expect some improvement in component shortages this year as chipmakers boost production capacity.
- Valeo specializes in the design, production, and sale of components and services for the automotive sector.
- Valeo forecast its annual core profit (EBITDA) margin for 2022 to be between 11.8% and 12.3%, compared to 13.4% in the previous year.
- Valeo projected 2022 sales between 19.2 billion and 20.0 billion euros, representing an 11-16% growth from the prior year.
- The outlook excludes possible effects from the Ukraine-Russia crisis but includes a negative impact from integrating Valeo Siemens eAutomotive.
- CEO Christophe Perillat stated the direct impact of the Ukraine-Russia crisis should be minimal, noting the company has no sales in Ukraine and less than 1% of total sales in Russia.
- Valeo plans to increase original equipment sales by around 13% in the medium term to reach a revenue of 27.5 billion euros ($30.73 billion) in 2025.
- The company is betting on recovery in car production volumes for its growth strategy.
- Valeo expects further revenue growth to 40 billion euros by 2030.
