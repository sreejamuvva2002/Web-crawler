# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-28

## Facts

- US EV startup Rivian announced it is going to make major cuts to its workforce, claiming inflation and a drive for profitability.
- The company employs around 14,000 employees.
- Cutting about six percent of jobs as announced would affect about 840 workers.
- The news was broken by Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe in an email to employees.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated that over the last six months, inflation reached record highs, interest rates rapidly rose, and commodity prices continued to climb, contributing to global capital markets tightening.
- Rivian is financially well positioned, but its strategy must support sustainable growth as it ramps toward profitability.
- The company needs to continue growing and scaling without additional financing in the current macro environment.
- To achieve this, Rivian has simplified its product roadmap and focused on where it is most impactful to deploy capital.
- Rivian recently fired its Head of Production.
- Rivian simultaneously announced intentions to restructure production lines.
- The company has struggled with ramping up production for some time.
- Rivian went quite deep into the red in 2021.
- Just days before quarterly numbers, Rivian raised prices and then immediately backtracked.
- Billionaire George Soros invested in the company in February.
- Rivian recently celebrated a milestone as the first units of the electric delivery fleet ordered by Amazon hit the road.
- It will cost more time and money before the ramp-up of the electric pickup truck R1T is complete.
- The first of the vehicles rolled off the production line last September.
- Tesla also announced intentions to cut 10% of its workforce to save on ramping up production.
- Tesla CEO Elon Musk expressed a "very bad feeling" regarding the global economy.
- Both Rivian and Tesla have an electric pickup in their lineup, although Tesla has not actually released theirs.
- Tesla has an ongoing lawsuit against Rivian for stealing employees and allegedly proprietary technology.
- Rivian's severance packages include 14 weeks of regular pay and health coverage.
- Severance packages include Rivian-paid COBRA benefits.
- Severance packages include continuation of mental health and fertility benefits through the end of the year.
- Severance packages include job placement assistance.
- Severance packages include this quarter’s equity vesting.
