## Facts

- Fox Factory moved from California to Gainesville, Ga., in 2018.
- The company opened a new $50 million facility in Gainesville, Georgia.
- Georgia Quick Start provided a customized training program to prepare the team with both theoretical knowledge and operational skills.
- Fox Factory manufactures high-performance shock absorbers and racing suspension products.
- The company needed to expand its talent space and physical footprint to continue on its growth trajectory.
- Joe Cobain serves as the general manager at the Gainesville plant.
- During site selection, Fox Factory looked at numerous other states before choosing Georgia.
- Quick Start enhanced its existing CNC (Computer Numerical Control) Machining program to include a high-level math course that included geometry and trigonometry.
- High-quality technical training is critical for Fox due to the precision required to manufacture its products.
- Fox Factory is a global leader in the design and manufacturing of premium products that deliver championship-level performance for specialty sports and on- and off-road vehicles.
- Lanier Technical College is listed as a training partner or resource associated with the project.
