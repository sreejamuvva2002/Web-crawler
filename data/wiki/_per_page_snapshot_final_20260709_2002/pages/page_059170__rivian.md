# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-04

**Source:** [Rivian builds nearly 14,000 EVs in Q2 - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2023/07/04/rivian-builds-nearly-14000-evs-in-q2/)  ·  `page_059170`

## Facts

- Rivian produced 13,992 electric vehicles from April to June (Q2).
- Q2 production was 4,597 more than in the first quarter.
- Rivian increased deliveries from 7,946 to 12,640 vehicles in Q2.
- Production increased by 33 percent and deliveries increased by 37 percent compared to the previous quarter.
- In Q2 2022, 4,401 Rivian EVs were built at the Normal plant in Illinois.
- In Q2 2022, 4,467 Rivian EVs were delivered.
- Rivian stated that the new figures remain in line with the company’s expectations.
- Rivian is on track to reach its forecast of 50,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian will announce its Q2 financial results on August 8.
- To meet the 50,000 vehicle annual target, Rivian must produce an average of 12,500 cars per quarter.
- Rivian built 9,395 vehicles in the first quarter, which was below the 12,500 quarterly average needed for the annual target.
- The annual target should be well within reach as a young carmaker is expected to increase its production rate during the year.
- In early March, Rivian announced a recall of over 12,700 units of the R1S and R1T due to a passenger airbag deployment issue.
- The recalled units covered all production until September 15, 2022.
- In mid-April, Rivian recalled 5,030 units of the R1S electric SUV.
- The reversing light on the recalled R1S vehicles did not comply with specifications and had to be replaced.
- Rivian lost billions of dollars in 2022 and must cut costs.
- Reports indicated further job cuts at the beginning of February.
- Rivian plans to install the Tesla charging port (NACS) in its future electric cars starting from 2025.
- The R1 model series and the upcoming R2 platform will feature the NACS charging system from 2025.
- Until 2025, Rivian vehicles can use the Tesla Supercharger network with an adapter that the company plans to provide starting in 2024.
- When Rivian vehicles have a Tesla charging port from 2025, an adapter will be offered to enable charging at CCS stations.
