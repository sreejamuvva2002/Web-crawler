# Seoyon E-Hwa Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-01

**Source:** [Over 700 auto parts manufacturing jobs coming to Georgia | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/over-700-auto-parts-manufacturing-jobs-coming-to-south-georgia-kemp-says)  ·  `page_001327`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Seoyon E-Hwa, a global auto parts supplier, would bring almost $76 million and more than 740 new jobs to Chatham County through a new manufacturing facility.
- The move would nearly double Georgia employment for the company.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: "When I announced the largest economic development project in Georgia history last year, I promised it was just the start in bringing even more jobs and opportunity to hardworking Georgians."
- Governor Brian Kemp added: "As we work to ensure our state is the e-mobility capital of the nation, projects like this will continue to choose the No. 1 state for business and benefit communities in just about every zip code of Georgia."
- The new facility is slated to open at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The facility will produce automotive interior parts as early as October 2024.
- This location marks Georgia's second Seoyon facility.
- The company's first location in LaGrange is expected to continue supporting 630 full time jobs there.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated: "With its second Georgia facility, Seoyon is committing to nearly doubling the number of jobs it supports in the state and extending its partnership with our business community."
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Hyundai’s new EV facility is drawing an experienced and dedicated supplier network to the region, adding to the state’s industry expertise and shaping a larger skilled workforce.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement to work with partners across the state and near Savannah to create future jobs.
- More than two-thirds of the new jobs represent permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production personnel.
- Interested applicants can learn more about Seoyon E-HWA by visiting their career page.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Co., Ltd. is a Korean automotive parts company established under the name Hanil E-Hwa Co., Ltd. in 1972.
- The company specializes in supplying interior parts like door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers and other parts for car companies including Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen, and others.
- Seoyon operates 32 corporations across North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- On July 2, 2014, the company split and began a spin-off new establishment under its current name.
- In Gov. Kemp's 2023 State of the State Address, he pledged to focus on job growth specifically in manufacturing electric vehicles.
- The governor announced plans to bring two giant electric vehicle (EV) assembly plants to Georgia.
- Officials hope to build on $23 billion in announced projects to make Georgia the center of electrified transportation.
- The governor's office reported that more than 35 EV-related projects have announced over $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia since 2020.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America broke ground in Bryan County.
- The $5.5 billion manufacturing plant is the first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing plant for the country.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project have already exceeded the $1 billion in investment predicted from other locations around the state by more than $600 million.
- In September 2022, Georgia’s EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan was approved after the Biden Administration announced that $5 billion would be spent putting charging stations on major highways.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation announced on July 21, 2022 that U.S. 441 from Cornelia in northeast Georgia to Dublin in east central Georgia is the strip of highway where the first set of charging will go.
- U.S. 82 from Brunswick in coastal Georgia to Albany in southeast Georgia is designated as the second highway for charging infrastructure.
