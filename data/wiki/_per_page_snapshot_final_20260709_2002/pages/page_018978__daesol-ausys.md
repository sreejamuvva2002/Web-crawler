# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Korean Auto Supplier to Invest $72M in Harris County - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-auto-supplier-to-invest-72m-in-harris-county/)  ·  `page_018978`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys, based in Incheon just south of Seoul, is set to make luggage boards and covers in a $72 million Harris County plant that will employ at least 140 workers.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project in a news release on Tuesday.
- Daesol Ausys is a supplier to Kia, Hyundai and General Motors.
- Sister company Daehan Solution invested $37 million to put a plant in West Point in 2008, promising 300 jobs.
- According to the Associated Press, Daehan Solution currently employs 350 workers and also has a factory near the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Ala.
- Daesol Material Georgia LLC set up a $35 million factory to make fiber padding in the Northwest Harris Business Park in 2017 and now employs 50 people.
- Daesol Ausys will land in the same business park as Daesol Material Georgia LLC and plans to launch production in December 2024.
- It is unclear from the release whether the investment is specifically related to the production of the Kia EV9, the electric SUV the auto maker has said will necessitate a $200 million upgrade to its plant.
- On its website, Daesol Ausys lists only floor mats, package trays, sun visors, dash insulators and other standard interior parts in its catalogue.
- The release notes that the company makes “motor and electric vehicle interior parts.”
