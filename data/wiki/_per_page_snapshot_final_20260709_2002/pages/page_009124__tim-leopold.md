# Tim Leopold

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2024 Rising Star: Tim Leopold - Automotive News](https://www.autonews.com/awards/2024-rising-star-tim-leopold)  ·  `page_009124`

## Facts

- Tim Leopold is 44 years old.
- He is leading the conversion of Honda’s Marysville Auto Plant from production of five- to six-speed automatic transmissions.
- The auto industry is undergoing a massive transformation as it mobilizes to electrify its gasoline-powered fleets, and Leopold is at the center of that shift at Honda Development and Manufacturing of America.
- Honda is building an electric vehicle and battery hub in Ohio.
- Marysville Auto Plant is Honda’s oldest plant in North America.
- Marysville will be the first of Honda’s existing factories to build EVs.
- The first vehicle planned at Marysville is a compact crossover from Acura in late 2025 that will use the automaker’s in-house EV architecture.
- The team is reconfiguring lines to “greatly reduce” consumption of resources that the plant requires to operate as part of Honda’s goal to reach 100 percent carbon neutrality by 2050.
- Tim Leopold stated: “We’re redefining not only our company, but how we operate within our company, and to be in a big role at our flagship plant in Marysville only comes once in a career.”
- The team is working around real-time assembly of the Honda Accord, plus the Acura TLX and Integra, which are all exclusively built at Marysville.
- Tim Leopold stated: “The goal is to make the conversion without having to completely shut down and retool the plant, which is a significant challenge.”
- After completing two separate internship programs with Honda while studying at Ohio University, he was offered a full-time position with the automaker before even graduating.
- For about a decade, he was part of an “expert support team” for innovation projects throughout North America.
- Tim Leopold stated: “I’ve always been a firm believer that I learn through every interaction — both good and bad. Each of those projects, whether they were successful or not, it helped groom me to who I’ve become.”
- In 2010, he went to Honda’s transmission plant in Tallapoosa, Ga., to lead the conversion of assembly from five-speed to six-speed transmissions.
- The Georgia facility’s smaller size exposed him to many facets of the manufacturing business, and his role eventually evolved from leading the initial project to overseeing the whole manufacturing operation.
- Tim Leopold stated: “It was a huge growth opportunity for me, and a great experience.”
- In 2017, under his leadership, the plant moved from assembly of six-speed transmissions to 10-speeds.
- Unlike the six-speed transmission, it was not just a “transfer of technology” from Japan.
- Tim Leopold stated: “This was the first opportunity to design not only the product, but the equipment, the quality and the market approach, and to pull all of the manufacturing experts together on one team and launch it from North America.”
- Tim Leopold stated: “I’ve had the privilege of being a part of so many different projects of varying scales, some smaller and some extremely large, like with Marysville. But I’ve found value in each one of them.”
