# AJIN Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-07

**Source:** [Statesboro's AJIN Georgia presents $40,000 to local organizations  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/community/statesboros-ajin-georgia-presents-40000-to-local-organizations-7940361)  ·  `page_051864`

## Facts

- AJIN Georgia presented $40,000 in total support to 12 local non-profit, education, and public safety agencies at a luncheon.
- The donations were presented by Jamie Calloway, AJIN General Manager of Human Resources.
- Calloway stated the gifts reflect the generosity of Jung-Ho Sea, AJIN CEO and President, and his commitment to being a good corporate citizen.
- AJIN had not yet manufactured any parts at the time of the donation but was already giving back to the community.
- Earlier in the year, Jung-Ho Sea donated high-tech, Korean-made refrigerators to local agencies and Korean War Veterans.
- Calloway noted that Sea has a special connection to Korean War Veterans due to their role in enabling him to start his companies.
- Senior leadership attending the luncheon included Taechul Kim (COO), Jaeik Jang (CFO), Daewoong Kim (Sr. mgr/Finance), Seunghwan Kim (GM/Project), Junhyeon Sim (Sr. Specialist/Project), Chunwoo Shin (Sr. Mgr./Ajin USA HR), and Jaewook Jeong (Sr. Mgr./HR).
- AJIN Georgia was the first Hyundai Tier 1 supplier to announce plans to build a plant in Bulloch County in November 2022.
- The facility is located on I-16 near Hwy. 301 South in the Bruce Yawn Industrial Park.
- The plant represents a $278 million investment and spans 853,000 square feet.
- AJIN Georgia will manufacture a variety of car parts at the facility.
- Production is planned to begin by October 2024.
- Initial employment will be just under 300 employees, with a target of 600 employees as production demand increases.
- Local dignitaries in attendance included Bulloch County Commissioner Chair Roy Thompson, Commissioners Jappy Stringer and Ray Mosley, Bulloch County Commission Chair and Bulloch County Development Authority Vice Chair Curt Deal, and Bulloch County Development Authority Chair Billy Allen.
- The 12 recipient organizations were the Bulloch County Boys & Girls Club, Bulloch County Parks and Recreation Department, Statesboro Kiwanis Club, Statesboro Safe Haven, Statesboro Village Builders, Bulloch County Emergency Medical Services, Bulloch County Fire Department, Bulloch County Sheriff’s Office, Bulloch County Schools, Evans County Schools, Metter College and Career Academy, and Ogeechee Technical College.
