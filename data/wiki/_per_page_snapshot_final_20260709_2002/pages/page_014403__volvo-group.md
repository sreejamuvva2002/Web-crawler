# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-17

**Source:** [Volvo Group expects North America weakness through 2026 amid freight slump – Wall Street Observer](https://wallstreetobserver.com/volvo-group-expects-north/)  ·  `page_014403`

## Facts

- Swedish truckmaker Volvo expects trucking demand in North America to remain under pressure into next year.
- Weak freight activity and U.S. President Donald Trump’s tariffs are weighing on the North American truck market.
- The Gothenburg-based Volvo Group, which also manufactures construction equipment and engines, has lowered its forecast for the North American truck market while keeping its European view unchanged.
- Volvo Group expects heavy truck deliveries of around 265,000 in North America this year, which is 10,000 fewer than previously envisioned.
- Volvo Group expects to deliver some 290,000 vehicles in Europe.
- Volvo Group's shares fell 8% by 0935 GMT, touching their lowest price in six months.
- Volvo stated that the North American long-haul freight market remains in recession with lower freight volumes and prices.
- Customers are holding back orders amid uncertainty over new emissions rules and tariffs.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt told Reuters that transport buyers are in a wait-and-see mode as uncertainty clouds market conditions.
- Volvo expects European truck demand to hold up better than North America's because it is largely replacement-driven and supported by stable fleet utilization.
- Equity analysts from Jefferies see a potential pickup later in 2026, as normalized inventory levels and back-end-loaded demand could support a gradual recovery.
- Volvo Group's adjusted operating profit fell around 17% to 11.7 billion Swedish crowns ($1.2 billion) in the third quarter.
- The third-quarter adjusted operating profit matched the average forecast in an LSEG poll of analysts.
- President Trump's move to slap a 25% tariff on imported heavy trucks has deepened trade uncertainty across North America.
- The USMCA trade agreement still shields most manufacturers from fresh duties despite the new tariffs.
- Volvo Group is best positioned among European truckmakers to weather the trade barriers because it builds all trucks destined for the U.S. market domestically, along with most of its components.
- Higher costs on imported parts may still affect Volvo Group's operations.
- Volvo Group runs 16 manufacturing and remanufacturing sites across the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- Volvo Group makes vehicles under brands such as Mack Trucks and Renault as well as its own name.
