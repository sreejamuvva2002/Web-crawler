# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Auto Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/auto-supplier-to-build-manufacturing-facility-in-bulloch-county/)  ·  `page_001455`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs and invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Ecoplastic to the state's growing ecosystem of manufacturers, logistics professionals, and suppliers.
- The new facility in Statesboro will serve the Hyundai Metaplant and benefit from close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Established in 1984, Ecoplastic Corporation is a Korea-based manufacturer of plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- The company manufactures automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Han Shang, President and CEO of Ecoplastic, stated the company is honored to establish a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic aims to contribute to the economic development of Georgia and Bulloch County by increasing local jobs and supplying high-quality products to Hyundai Motor Group Manufacturing Alabama (HMGMA).
- The company expressed appreciation for the support of state and county governments.
- The new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The plant will be capable of injection molding and painting to produce exterior parts, bumpers, garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- The company expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- Hiring will include positions in HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County Chair Billy Allen welcomed Ecoplastic to the county.
- Local officials expressed confidence that Bulloch County's location, workforce, and quality of life will support the supplier's success.
- GDEcD Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the state's Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted Ecoplastic's decades of experience supplying Kia and Hyundai.
- GDEcD is building a supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County to provide well-paying, high-tech manufacturing jobs across the region.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced over $21 billion in investment and over 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Announced projects to date have already exceeded the predicted $1 billion supplier investment amount.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd.
- The company established its first Technical Research Center in 1987.
- It was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
- As a Tier-1 supplier to Hyundai and Kia, Ecoplastic manufactures automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, console, trims, and plastic molds.
- Ecoplastic currently employs more than 770 people.
- The company recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
