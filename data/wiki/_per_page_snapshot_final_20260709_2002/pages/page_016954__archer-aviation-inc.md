# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-10

**Source:** [Archer Aviation - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archer_Aviation)  ·  `page_016954`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. is a publicly traded company headquartered in San Jose, California.
- The company is developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft.
- Its eVTOL aircraft are designed to allow airline operators to transport people in and around cities via an air taxi service.
- The aircraft have a claimed range of up to 100 miles (160km).
- The aircraft have a claimed speed of up to 150 miles per hour (240km/h).
- United Airlines is Archer's first major corporate partner.
- United Airlines ordered two hundred Archer electric aircraft.
- Maker, Archer's full-size demonstrator aircraft, was unveiled on June 10, 2021, at an event in Los Angeles, California.
- Maker is a fully electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft with 12 electric propellers.
- Maker features six tilt-props (each with five blades) for forward and VTOL flight.
- Maker features six aft propellers that are stationary (each with two blades) for VTOL-only flight.
- The aircraft is powered by six independent battery packs.
- In November 2021, Archer moved Maker from its headquarters to its flight test facility to start initial test flights.
- Maker received its airworthiness certificate to start flight test operations from the FAA in December 2021.
- Archer completed its first flight in December 2021.
- Archer's aerial ridesharing service, also referred to as Urban Air Mobility (UAM), has been pushed back one year to 2025.
- The UAM service is planned to begin operations in Miami, Florida and Los Angeles, California.
- Archer is working with Urban Movement Labs and the Los Angeles Department of Transportation to help build necessary infrastructure and service routes.
- Archer is working with the City of Miami on similar plans.
- Midnight is Archer's planned production aircraft.
- The Midnight aircraft was unveiled on November 17, 2022, at an event in Palo Alto, California.
- Midnight is a piloted, four-passenger aircraft designed to perform rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time between flights.
- Midnight has 12 propellers: six tilt props in the front of the wing for forward and VTOL flight and six aft propellers that are stationary for VTOL flight only.
- Midnight is designed to travel at up to 150mph (240km/h) with a maximum range of 100 miles (161km).
- The aircraft is powered by six independent battery packs.
- On June 12, 2024, Midnight completed its first transition flight.
- Archer flew over 400 test flights in 2024.
- The Midnight aircraft's first test flight outside the United States took place in July 2025 at Al Bateen Executive Airport in Abu Dhabi.
- The flight evaluated performance under local environmental conditions as part of preparations for regional certification.
- In February 2026, Archer Aviation announced plans to integrate Starlink satellite connectivity into its Midnight electric air taxi aircraft.
- The system is intended to provide reliable communication between the aircraft and ground operations while supporting future urban air mobility services.
- Archer was founded on October 16, 2018, by Adam Goldstein and Brett Adcock to develop electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft.
- The company was originally started by Goldstein and Adcock and privately funded.
- Marc Lore, a Walmart executive, supported its launch.
- In 2022, Brett Adcock departed from both the leadership team and the board.
- Adam Goldstein is currently the sole CEO and Chairman of the Board of Directors of the company.
- Initially, Archer worked on developing aircraft with the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering at the University of Florida.
- Goldstein and Adcock are alumni of the University of Florida.
- Archer operates a research lab on the University of Florida's campus in Gainesville, Florida, which was funded by Goldstein and Adcock.
- In August 2022, United Airlines paid Archer a $10 million deposit for 100 electric flying taxis.
- On November 10, 2022, Archer and United Airlines announced plans for the first electric air taxi route in the US.
- The initial route is between Newark Liberty International Airport and the Downtown Manhattan Heliport.
- Archer became an IPO (ACHR) on September 20, 2021.
- On November 17, 2022, Archer unveiled details of its production vehicle dubbed "Midnight".
- The aircraft is designed to carry passengers on short trips of around 20 miles (32km) between airports and downtown city centers.
- In November 2022, Archer unveiled its Midnight production aircraft, an electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft that can carry four passengers and a pilot.
- It is designed to be optimized for back-to-back 20-mile trips with a payload of over 1,000 pounds.
- Midnight is the evolution of Archer's previous aircraft, Maker.
- Midnight is expected to be certified by the FAA in late 2024.
- Archer announced its Maker prototype achieved full transition from vertical to horizontal flight on November 29, 2022.
- This milestone validated flight control systems and aircraft architecture applicable to the Midnight production aircraft.
- In January 2023, Archer announced a partnership with Stellantis, a multinational automobile manufacturer.
- Stellantis would provide up to $150 million in equity capital to support Archer's growth.
- The partnership includes collaboration on the development and production of Archer's eVTOL aircraft for urban air mobility.
- The partnership solidifies Stellantis as Archer's exclusive contract manufacturer for mass production of its eVTOL aircraft.
- In March 2023, Archer and United Airlines announced plans for an electric air taxi route between O'Hare International Airport and Vertiport Chicago on the city's near west side.
- On June 5, 2024, Archer received its Part 135 Air Carrier and Operator Certificate from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).
- In July 2024, Archer and Southwest Airlines established an agreement to develop operational concepts for air taxi networks.
- In February 2025, Archer Aviation received its Part 141 certification from the FAA, enabling the company to establish its pilot training academy.
- In June 2025, at the Paris Air Show, Archer Aviation and Dubai-based Jetex signed an agreement to collaborate on developing eVTOL facilities.
- The collaboration starts in the United Arab Emirates at the Jetex Abu Dhabi private terminal.
- Jetex will provide access to its network of private terminals to support the launch of Archer’s Midnight air taxi services in the region.
- In July 2025, Archer Aviation's Midnight eVTOL aircraft conducted its first test flight outside the United States at Al Bateen Executive Airport in Abu Dhabi.
- The flight tested performance under UAE-specific conditions including high heat, humidity, and dust.
- The flight marked a major milestone in advancing Archer's planned urban air mobility operations in the region.
- It was supported by the Smart and Autonomous Systems Council (SASC) and observed by senior officials from the UAE General Civil Aviation Authority and related aviation authorities.
