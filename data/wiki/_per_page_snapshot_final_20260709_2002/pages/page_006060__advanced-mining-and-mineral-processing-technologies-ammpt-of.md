# Advanced Mining and Mineral Processing Technologies (AMMPT) Office

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Advanced Mining and Mineral Production Technologies Office | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/mining/advanced-mining-and-mineral-production-technologies-office)  ·  `page_006060`

## Facts

- The mission of the Advanced Mining and Mineral Processing Technologies (AMMPT) Office is to expand American mining and mineral production by developing and deploying advanced technologies.
- AMMPT funds research and development (R&D) for mineral resource characterization, extraction, and extractive metallurgical processing.
- AMMPT also funds advanced R&D collaborations, such as METALLIC and HERMES, in coordination with the U.S. National Laboratories.
- These initiatives address high-priority challenges related to minerals production, including characterization and prospecting, mining and extraction, and production and processing.
- AMMPT focuses on identifying new resources through faster, more precise resource characterization, AI-driven decision-making and prioritization, and improved data collection and resource estimation.
- AMMPT focuses on increasing technical recoverability by advancing conventional and unconventional extraction technologies and making previously inaccessible resources mineable.
- AMMPT aims to strengthen the mineral production ecosystem through market and workforce development, interagency and international collaboration, and tools and data to support resilient supply chains.
- AMMPT advances critical materials across industry sectors including energy production (hydrocarbon & nuclear fuels), electricity generation, energy storage, electronics (data centers, power transmission, quantum), and advanced mobility (land, sea, air).
- The office is part of the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Energy under Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation.
