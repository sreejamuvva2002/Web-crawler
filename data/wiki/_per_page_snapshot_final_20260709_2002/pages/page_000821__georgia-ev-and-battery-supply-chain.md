# Georgia EV and Battery Supply Chain

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-02

**Source:** [Charged Up - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/charged-up/)  ·  `page_000821`

## Facts

- Georgia is accelerating growth in electric vehicle (EV) parts and power manufacturing on the ground alongside EV aircraft development in the skies.
- Archer Aviation, a California-based company developing electric-powered vertical takeoff and landing aircraft, announced on Nov. 14 that it will manufacture its air EVs next to Covington Municipal Airport.
- Plant construction and production at the Archer Aviation facility are scheduled to start in 2024.
- Archer plans to invest $118 million over 10 years for the facility.
- The facility will initially build up to 650 four-passenger aircraft per year.
- Archer eventually plans to employ 1,000 people at the facility.
- The Archer Aviation facility could expand to manufacture 2,300 airplanes annually.
- Archer’s aircraft take off and land like helicopters but fly as airplanes within a metro area.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that creating urban air mobility networks across major cities nationwide requires ramping production of their aircraft.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein noted that the new facility represents a huge step toward meeting production challenges.
- Delta Air Lines invested in Archer competitor Joby in October to help launch an air taxi service to and from airports.
- Joby expects to start commercial service for its air taxis in 2025.
- Two auto parts suppliers announced plants that will supply Hyundai’s $5.5-billion Metaplant America EV facility.
- Hyundai’s Metaplant America EV facility broke ground in Bryan County on Oct. 25.
- Joon Georgia expects to invest $317 million and employ 630 people at a facility outside Statesboro, with operations beginning in mid-2024.
- Joon Georgia is part of a South Korean conglomerate through Ajin USA, which manufactures body parts for Hyundai in Alabama.
- Hyundai Mobilis, the parts supplier wing of Hyundai Motor Group, plans to spend $926 million and employ 1,500 people building EV power electric systems and integrated charging control units in Richmond Hill.
- The Hyundai Mobilis plant will supply the nearby Metaplant America facility.
- The Hyundai Mobilis plant will also support Hyundai’s Alabama operations and Kia’s West Point factory, which is located near an existing Mobilis site.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson called Joon’s announcement “a landmark moment as we drive Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.”
- Luxembourg-based FREYR Battery announced on Nov. 11 that it will spend $1.7 billion to build its Giga America lithium-ion battery plant in Coweta County.
- The FREYR Battery investment is projected to grow to $2.6 billion with more than 720 jobs by 2029 if a planned second phase proceeds.
- FREYR Battery manufactures batteries for EVs but focuses heavily on electric storage to allow EV charging with clean energy during periods without solar or wind power.
