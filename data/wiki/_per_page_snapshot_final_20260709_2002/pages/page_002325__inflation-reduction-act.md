# Inflation Reduction Act

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-16

**Source:** [Memorandum from Deputy Secretary Wally Adeyemo - Inflation Reduction Act – Year 1 | U.S. Department of the Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/featured-stories/memorandum-from-deputy-secretary-wally-adeyemo-inflation-reduction-act-year-1)  ·  `page_002325`

## Facts

- Deputy Secretary Wally Adeyemo issued a memorandum dated August 16, 2023, to interested parties regarding the Inflation Reduction Act’s first year.
- Congress passed and the President signed the Inflation Reduction Act one year prior to the memo's date.
- The law is described as the single most significant legislation to combat climate change in American history and one of the largest investments into the American economy in a generation.
- Over the past year, companies have announced nearly 200 new projects totaling over $110 billion of investment in building America’s clean energy economy.
- Treasury analysis indicates that announced investments in clean energy, electric vehicles, and batteries are concentrated in communities with lower wages, lower college graduation rates, and lower employment rates.
- China has historically spent over $400 billion per year on industrial policy, largely through direct subsidies to firms and below-market credit to state-owned enterprises.
- The Inflation Reduction Act uses modest incentives to promote private investment and will unlock $3 trillion of investment in America for renewable energy technology.
- Progress is measured across three key metrics: creating good-paying jobs (especially in disadvantaged communities), strengthening energy security/resilience, and tackling climate change.
- A central tenet of the law is spurring job creation across the United States, aligning with Secretary Yellen’s modern supply-side economics framework.
- Over the past twelve months, investments have started translating to American jobs, detailed in a 70-page list covering tens of thousands of jobs from Colorado Springs to Minneapolis and Albuquerque to Toledo.
- The annual economic opportunity for workers in the wind and solar industries will grow by $220 billion per year in wages by 2035.
- Clean energy investments could support over 1.5 million clean energy jobs in the United States by 2030.
- Events of the past year, including climate emergencies, supply chain snarls, and Russia’s weaponization of exports, demonstrated the acute need for energy security investment.
- The Inflation Reduction Act accelerates development and deployment of clean energy, including key supply chains, to build a resilient energy sector.
- Estimates suggest the law can bring down costs of certain emerging technologies by as much as 25 percent and position America as the global leader in these areas.
- Analysts expect twice as much wind, solar, and battery storage deployment over the next seven years compared to the base case without the law.
- A typical American household could save around $200 per year on electric bills due to the law's impact.
- Households taking advantage of electric vehicle incentives can save thousands per year on energy and fuel costs, with EV drivers seeing fuel costs drop by about 60 percent.
- The President’s 2030 emissions reduction goal is within reach, with independent studies suggesting the law will drive significant emissions reductions.
- Lower costs of emerging climate technologies will lead to global deployment, eventually reducing hundreds of millions of tons of emissions around the world each year.
- Increased clean energy deployment and reduced particle pollution will avoid thousands of premature deaths and asthma attacks.
- The incentives are part of a broader whole-of-government approach that includes the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and electric grid infrastructure upgrades.
- Federal policies run in tandem with state, local, and private-sector action and market forces.
- In its first year, the Treasury Department focused on achieving three goals: creating good-paying jobs, strengthening energy security, and tackling the climate crisis.
- Treasury efforts spanned the Office of Tax Policy, Climate Hub, Inflation Reduction Act Program Office, and the Office of the Deputy Assistant Secretary for Management and Budget.
- Treasury is working closely with the IRS to modernize customer service and technology to ensure clean energy tax incentives are delivered accurately and seamlessly.
- Treasury will complete the first phase of implementation in the coming weeks, having provided information or proposed guidance for Investment and Production Tax Credits’ cross-cutting bonus provisions and new credit monetization provisions.
- Plans for phase two of implementation will be announced in September.
- On the day of the law’s enactment, Treasury issued guidance on the electric vehicle tax credit and worked to ensure consumers could easily find a list of eligible vehicles online.
- Since enactment, Treasury and the IRS have issued 38 pieces of guidance on clean energy provisions while engaging with hundreds of stakeholders and reviewing nearly 5,000 comments.
- On August 10, 2023, Maxeon Solar Technologies Ltd. announced plans to spend $1.2 billion to build the biggest US factory for polysilicon solar panels in Albuquerque, New Mexico.
- Construction on the Maxeon plant is set to start in the first quarter of 2024 with production expected to begin in 2025.
- The Maxeon facility will produce up to 3 gigawatts of solar cells and panels a year, doubling the company’s manufacturing capacity.
- One gigawatt of solar capacity is enough to power about 173,000 American homes.
- On August 7, 2023, solar parts manufacturer Unimacts announced it would triple operations in Las Vegas, Nevada, having hired 80 people and opened a facility making metal torque tubes at the start of 2023 thanks to the IRA.
- CEO Matt Arnold stated that the metal tubes allow solar panels to rotate during the day, boosting efficiency by about 10 percent.
- Unimats can make enough torque tubes in a year at its Las Vegas facility to support two gigawatts worth of solar panels and is looking to expand capacity to support six gigawatts while employing over 200 people.
- On August 2, 2023, Governor Bill Lee and state economic development leaders announced that Hanon Systems will invest nearly $170 million to establish a new manufacturing presence in Huntingdon Park, Loudon County, Tennessee, creating 600 new jobs.
- Hanon Systems is a leading supplier of thermal and energy management solutions for the automotive industry, employs more than 22,000 people across 21 countries, and is headquartered in South Korea.
- On August 2, 2023, XCharge North America announced a 3,500 square foot manufacturing facility in Kyle, Texas, aimed at R&D, distribution, and services for central EV charging solutions, with plans to expand to approximately 20,000 square feet.
- The XCharge facility aims to enhance local EV accessibility, as only 20 public-access Level-2 and DC Fast charging stations currently serve a population exceeding 68,000 within San Marcos city limits and surrounding counties.
- On July 24, 2023, Switzerland-based Meyer Burger announced plans to open a high-performance solar cell manufacturing facility in Colorado Springs in 2024, creating more than 350 jobs with an average annual wage of nearly $78,000.
- Production at the Meyer Burger plant is planned to start during the second half of 2024 and will be its first Western Hemisphere site to manufacture chips and wafers for cutting-edge solar technology.
- On July 24, 2023, Canadian solar panel maker Heliene announced plans to spend about $145 million on a new facility in the Minneapolis-St. Paul area with an annual capacity of about one gigawatt of modules and 1.5 gigawatts of cells.
- On July 24, 2023, Stellantis and Samsung SDI announced a signed memorandum of understanding to build a second EV battery plant in the United States under their existing StarPlus Energy joint venture, expected to begin production in 2027 with an initial annual capacity of 34 gigawatt hours.
- On June 26, 2023, Tennessee Governor Bill Lee and state officials announced that Enchem America Inc. will invest $152.5 million to establish electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville, Tennessee, creating 190 new jobs in Haywood County.
- The Enchem facility will be located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park, operate similarly to its U.S. headquarters in Commerce, Georgia, and manufacture electrolytes for EV batteries and other EV battery-related services.
- On June 23, 2023, automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea announced plans to establish a new facility in Locust Grove, Georgia, creating over 160 new jobs and bringing $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- The new NVH Korea facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles in the United States for manufacturers such as Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- On June 22, 2023, the Colorado Office of Economic Development and International Trade announced that VSK Energy Inc. plans to invest $250 million to develop a solar photovoltaic module manufacturing facility in Brighton, Colorado, bringing as many as 900 new jobs.
- VSK Energy Inc. is a U.S.-based joint venture between several international solar manufacturers and plans to move into the 76 Commerce Center along Interstate 76 in 2024.
