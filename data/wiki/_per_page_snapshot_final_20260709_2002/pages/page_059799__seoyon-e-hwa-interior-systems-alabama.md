# Seoyon E HWA Interior Systems Alabama

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-18

**Source:** [Selma-Dallas County EDA announces expansion at Seoyon E HWA ...](https://www.selmatimesjournal.com/2023/05/18/selma-dallas-eda-announces-expansion-at-seoyon-e-hwa-interior/)  ·  `page_059799`

## Facts

- Seoyon E HWA Interior Systems Alabama will add 60 jobs and $4.2 million to Selma and Dallas County.
- The expansion was announced by Selma-Dallas County Economic Development Authority Executive Director Wayne Vardaman at a press conference on Thursday at the Selma-Dallas County Public Library.
- Seoyon E HWA Interior Systems Alabama is a Tier I Hyundai supplier located in Craig Industrial Park that manufactures several kinds of plastic interior parts for Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama.
- The expansion is in conjunction with HMMA's upcoming production of the updated Santafe MX5A, which will be manufactured later this year at the HMMA Hope Hull Assembly Plant.
- Selma-Dallas County EDA Executive Director Wayne Vardaman stated he has worked with the company for a very long time, called it a very good company, and noted it took a lot of hard work to get them here.
- Selma Mayor James Perkins Jr. was in office when he helped the EDA successfully bring Seoyon E HWA to Selma in 2005.
- Selma Mayor James Perkins Jr. expressed excitement about having their expansion and bringing more jobs to Selma.
- State Rep. Prince Chestnut said he is grateful to have Seoyon's business in the Queen City.
- The plant currently employs 439 employees.
- State Rep. Prince Chestnut stated they are really appreciative of Seoyon investing in their community and see the potential for more growth.
- Craig Field Airport and Industrial Authority Executive Director Jim Corrigan said it is great to see Seoyon have a great relationship with Selma and Dallas County.
- Jim Corrigan noted that for 18 years, they have been bringing jobs to Selma.
- Dallas County Commissioner Curtis Williams attended the press conference and said he is glad to see more business in the area.
- Dallas County Commissioner Curtis Williams stated it feels great to have Seoyon expand and bring more jobs, and expressed gratitude.
