# Adient plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-17

**Source:** [SEC-Show](https://otp.tools.investis.com/clients/us/adient1/SEC/sec-show.aspx?Type=page&FilingId=17067227-63064-105360&CIK=0001670541&Index=11100)  ·  `page_016700`

## Facts

- Adient plc is a global leader in the automotive seating supply industry with leading market positions in the Americas, Europe and China.
- Adient maintains longstanding relationships with the largest global automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Adient's proprietary technologies extend into virtually every area of automotive seating solutions, including complete seating systems, frames, mechanisms, foam, head restraints, armrests and trim covers.
- Adient operates more than 200 wholly- and majority-owned manufacturing or assembly facilities, with operations in 29 countries.
- Adient has partially-owned affiliates in China, Asia, Europe and North America.
- Adient's business model focuses on developing and maintaining long-term customer relationships to grow with leading global OEMs.
- Adient works closely with customers as vehicle platforms are developed, creating close ties with key decision makers at OEM customers.
- Adient has more than 70,000 employees operating in more than 200 manufacturing and assembly plants in 29 countries worldwide.
- Adient intends to maintain high capacity utilization and increase efficiency through continued use of standardized manufacturing processes.
- Adient employs a Sales & Operational Planning (S&OP) process to ensure appropriate service levels, minimal inventory and optimal factory utilization.
- Adient works with OEMs to develop complete seating solutions to meet consumer expectations for performance, safety and comfort.
- Adient's customer teams lead the new business acquisition process to ensure alignment with product, process and manufacturing strategies.
- Management expects to increase investment in innovation for product and process technologies.
- Adient utilizes a global Core Product Portfolio (CPP) strategy for part and design reuse across all product applications.
- Through the CPP strategy, Adient provides high quality products with market competitive cost and mass (low weight to improve fuel economy).
- Adient's worldwide engineering network includes ten core development centers located in Plymouth (USA), Trencin (Slovakia), Burscheid (Germany), Yokohama (Japan), Solingen (Germany), Chongqing (China), Kaiserslautern (Germany), Ceska Lipa (Czech Republic), Ansan (South Korea) and Pune (India).
- Adient leverages low cost country development centers in India, China, Czech Republic and Slovakia.
- Adient is a leading supplier of "just-in-time" seating in China, operating through wholly owned entities and 6 joint ventures with 36 manufacturing locations in 22 cities.
- Adient has relationships with global OEM customers to expand business with regional customers growing globally and new automotive market entrants.
- Adient is able to leverage its position as the market leader in Europe, North America and China to grow in other markets such as Southeast Asia.
- Adient designs and manufactures a full range of seating systems and components for passenger cars, commercial vehicles and light trucks, including vans, pick-up trucks and sport/crossover utility vehicles.
- Adient is a supplier to all global OEMs and has longstanding relationships with premier automotive manufacturers including BMW, Mercedes-Benz Group, Ford Motor Company, General Motors Company, Honda Motor Company, Hyundai Motor Company, Jaguar Land Rover, Kia Corporation, Mazda Motor Corporation, Mitsubishi Motor Corporation, Nissan Motor Corporation, Renault Group, Stellantis N.V., Suzuki Motor Corporation, Toyota Motor Corporation, Volkswagen Group and Volvo Car Group.
- Adient supplies most growing regional OEMs such as Beijing Automotive Group CO., Ltd., Changan Automobile (Group) Co., Ltd., FAW Group Corporation, Proton Holdings Berhad, Ashok Leyland, Tata Motors Limited and Zhejiang Geely Holding Group Co., Ltd.
- Adient also supplies newer auto manufacturers such as NIO and Xpeng Motors.
- Adient has more than 6 joint venture partnerships with key OEMs including Guangzhou Automobile Group Co., Ltd., Beijing Automobile Group Co., Ltd. and FAW Group Corporation.
- The Automotive Seating industry provides OEMs with complete seats on a "just-in-time" or "in-sequence" basis, assembled to specific order and delivered directly to an automotive assembly line.
- Adient is a global leader in complete seat assembly and one of the largest in all major seating components, operating plants that produce seating foam, metal structures, seat covers and seat mechanisms.
- Demand for automotive parts in the OEM market is generally a function of new vehicle production driven by macro-economic factors such as credit availability, interest rates, fuel prices, consumer confidence, employment and other trends.
- Most OEMs have adopted global vehicle platforms to increase standardization, reduce per unit cost and increase capital efficiency and profitability.
- Three seating sourcing patterns exist among OEMs: core seat structures, component sourcing, and engineering "in-sourcing".
- As a supplier with global scale and strong design, engineering and lean manufacturing capabilities, Adient is positioned to accommodate core seat structures, component sourcing, and engineering in-sourcing patterns.
- New safety and environmental regulations and rapid customer preference changes are requiring suppliers to respond faster with new designs and product innovations.
- Electric vehicles (EVs) continue to be a focus in the global automotive industry driven by product offerings from legacy manufacturers and new entrants.
- The rollout of EV platforms varies across regions with factors such as pricing, affordability, government incentives, infrastructure and overall consumer acceptance influencing adoption pace.
- While seating systems are not largely impacted by the shift to EVs, key attributes of seat design are evolving as the market pivots toward EVs.
- This movement provides Adient opportunities to provide value added solutions through its Evolution of Seating Systems Sustainability (ES3) and capture market share through new entrants based on its existing leading market position.
- As the global automotive industry incorporates Advanced Driver Assist Systems (ADAS) and Automated Driving Systems (ADS), Adient is poised to capitalize on greater seating content accompanying these innovations.
- Adient has developed an interiors concept for autonomous driving addressing major seating and interior trends expected to drive the future automotive industry.
- Adient will continue to partner with OEMs and other customers in the development of ADAS and ADS concepts.
- Adient faces competition from traditional seating suppliers, component specialists, and competitors partnered with an OEM through ownership or interlocking business relationships.
- Independent principal competitors include Lear Corporation, Toyota Boshoku Corporation, Forvia SE and Magna International Inc.
- Raw materials used by Adient include steel, aluminum, polyurethane chemicals, fabrics, leather, vinyl and polypropylene.
- The automotive industry experienced significant volatility in commodity prices continuing into fiscal 2023, which may continue as demand increases and/or supply remains constrained.
- Price volatility has resulted in an overall increase of input costs for Adient that may not be, or may only be partially, offset through customer negotiations.
- During fiscal 2024, commodity prices and availability could fluctuate throughout the year and significantly affect Adient's results of operations.
- Adient embeds sustainability into its company by integrating ESG principles into product development initiatives, manufacturing processes, procurement practices and corporate governance activities.
- In fiscal year 2021, Adient set a goal of reducing its scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions 75% by 2030 (with 2019 as the base year).
- In 2023, Adient expanded its goal with a longer term target of achieving carbon neutrality at its manufacturing locations for scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.
- Recent sustainability examples include replacing company pool vehicles with EVs as leases end, recovering waste heat from air compressors, adding occupant sensors to plant floors, optimizing foam mold temperature machines, replacing cardboard boxes with reusable packaging, and collecting rainwater for sanitary applications.
- Adient has set a goal to attribute 100% of the electricity needed worldwide to renewable sources by 2035.
- Several of Adient’s facilities already generate renewable electricity on-site via solar panel installations, and more than 60 Adient locations now consume electricity from a renewable source.
- Sustainability has been part of product development for more than three decades, with vehicle electrification emphasizing the need for lighter, slimmer seating products made of environmentally friendly materials.
- Adient developed Soft Back Panel and Soft Side Valance components that integrate up to 70% recycled polyethylene terephthalate (PET) while improving knee clearance and reducing seat weight by as much as 2 kg.
- Adient developed an innovative, EV-ready concept seat called the Pure Essential that combines comfort with a sleek appearance while reducing cost, complexity, weight and carbon footprint.
- The Pure Essential seat promotes product circularity by using just two primary eco-friendly material types: green steel for the seat structure and recyclable polyester for comfort features and trim covers.
- Adient is working with suppliers to reduce scope 3 (value chain) emissions 35% by 2030 (with 2019 as the base year).
- In fiscal year 2024, Adient plans to expand the use of its proprietary Product Carbon Footprint Tool to impact designer, engineer and customer decisions.
- Adient updated its Ethics Policy in 2023 to address evolving laws and regulations including those regarding ESG.
- In 2023, Adient initiated ESG-related due diligence questionnaires for top suppliers to promote transparency in supply chain human rights policies and practices.
- Adient supports women-, minority- and veteran-owned businesses by spending more than $1 billion with diverse suppliers every year.
- Adient's Executive Vice President, Chief Legal and Human Resources Officer, and Corporate Secretary oversees global talent processes to attract, develop and retain employees.
- As of September 30, 2023, Adient's workforce composition consists of approximately 45% working in the Americas, 42% in EMEA and 13% in Asia-Pacific.
- Approximately 41% of the global workforce is female and 49% of employees in the U.S. have identified themselves as an ethnic minority.
- Adient maintains a health and safety management system certified to the ISO 45001 Occupational Health and Safety standard.
- Globally, 100% of Adient’s facilities are internally audited and compliant, and 60% are also third-party audited and certified.
- Adient President and CEO Doug Del Grosso signed the "CEO Action for Diversity & Inclusion" pledge.
- Adient is a member of the Center for Automotive Diversity, Inclusion and Advancement (CADIA).
- In fiscal 2022, Adient published a Human Rights Policy Statement and a Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DE&I) Commitment Statement.
