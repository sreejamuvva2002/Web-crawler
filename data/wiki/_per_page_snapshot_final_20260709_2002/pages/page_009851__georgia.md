## Facts

- Immigrants make up nearly a fifth of Georgia’s labor force (17%), quadrupling the share from three decades ago.
- With nearly 975,000 workers, Georgia has America’s seventh-largest immigrant labor force.
- Immigrants in Georgia’s workforce contribute an estimated $55 billion to the Peach State’s economy annually in personal income.
- Immigrant spending power makes up more than 1 in 10 of all spending power dollars in the state.
- Lawmakers in Atlanta should prioritize pro-immigrant policies that support families, and that further expand immigrants’ ability to contribute to the state's economy.
