# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-17

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q4 2021 Earnings Call Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2022/02/18/aspen-aerogels-aspn-q4-2021-earnings-call-transcri/)  ·  `page_052738`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels is leveraging its aerogel technology platform to become a fast-growing supplier of critical parts and thermal barriers to EV OEMs.
- The company is experiencing rapid adoption of its PyroThin thermal barriers in the electric vehicle market to address thermal runaway and fire safety in lithium-ion battery packs.
- Aspen's value proposition for PyroThin is highest in NMC and mixed silicon graphite anode chemistry applications, particularly for pouch or prismatic cell form factors.
- The company has started supplying production parts for PyroThin thermal barriers in the fourth quarter of 2021.
- Aspen Aerogels generated PyroThin revenue from 10 customers in 2021 and is currently providing prototype materials to potential longer-term customers as they develop battery platforms.
- The company has disclosed GM and Toyota as customers in its presentation.
- Aspen Aerogels plans to build Plant 2, a new silica aerogel manufacturing facility, in Statesboro, Georgia, near the port city of Savannah.
- Plant 2 is positioned central to a growing automotive and battery ecosystem in the Southeast U.S.
- The first phase of Plant 2 will have approximately $650 million of revenue capacity with a startup date in late 2023.
- Upon Phase 1 startup, Aspen's combined plants will have approximately $900 million of revenue capacity, sufficient to service industrial business and approximately 2.2 million electric vehicles.
- The company projects gross margins of approximately 35% and EBITDA margins of approximately 25% at full capacity utilization for the two-plant operation.
- Aspen plans to construct necessary infrastructure during Phase 1 to support a second phase that will add another $700 million of revenue capacity in approximately 15 months from kickoff.
- Upon completion of both phases, Plant 2 and Plant 1 will have an overall revenue capacity of approximately $1.6 billion.
- The full Plant 2 project is projected to require capital of approximately $575 million for Phase 1 with full infrastructure and approximately $125 million for Phase 2.
- Capex per dollar of revenue for the full Plant 2 project is less than $0.55, which is lower than the prior projection of $0.65 per dollar of revenue capacity.
- Aspen Aerogels expects to incur between $250 million and $275 million of capital expenditures in 2022, principally for the Plant 2 project.
- The company reiterated a target to double revenue from 2021 to 2023 and upgraded its target to triple revenue from 2023 to 2025 to approximately $720 million.
- Aspen Aerogels received a recent $150 million investment from Koch Strategic Platforms (KSP), comprised of convertible notes and common equity, following a $75 million KSP equity investment in June 2021.
- The company is leveraging resources at Koch Industries for the planning, engineering, and execution of the Plant 2 capital project to de-risk scaling its business.
- Aspen Aerogels projects content per vehicle (CPV) for PyroThin to settle in the range of $300 to $325, with PyroThin content making up around 60% of that total.
- The company is investing in carbon aerogel battery materials, specifically focusing on silicon-rich anode materials, and plans to share key performance and cost metrics in 2022.
- Ricardo Rodriguez, Aspen's chief strategy officer with a deep automotive and tier 1 background, will assume the role of CFO upon John Fairbanks' retirement on April 1, 2022.
- Aspen Aerogels ended 2021 with more than $75 million in cash and received a $150 million investment from KSP to provide a financial foundation for raising additional capital over the next year.
- The company's 2022 revenue outlook is set between $145 million and $155 million, representing growth of between 19% and 27% compared to 2021.
- Aspen Aerogels estimates that awarded business from two North American and Asia-based automotive OEMs represents 100% of the 2023 PyroThin thermal barrier revenue target.
- The company anticipates converting highly active prototyping and quoting work into additional program awards for battery platforms from other automotive OEMs.
- Aspen Aerogels plans to publish its first ESG report this spring, followed by a more in-depth report recapping 2022 progress and defining future ESG positioning.
