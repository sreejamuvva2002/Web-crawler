# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2012-11-28

**Source:** [Kia Motors to invest $1.6 billion in West Point auto assembly plant | Columbus Ledger-Enquirer](https://www.ledger-enquirer.com/news/local/article29282200.html)  ·  `page_010001`

## Facts

- Kia Motors plans to invest $1.6 billion on new machinery, tools, equipment and other production-enhancing technology over the next 16 years at its West Point, Ga. auto assembly plant.
- The investment aims to "satisfy demand" for Kia's vehicles in the U.S.
- West Point Mayor Drew Ferguson stated the investment gives Kia long-term stability and the ability to grow its supplier network in the area, which is expected to drive new job creation.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia operates as the South Korean automaker's only U.S. assembly plant.
- The investment will improve the surrounding area while keeping Kia on the cutting edge of the auto industry.
- Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal stated Kia has an exceptional track record of growth in the state and that its economic impact goes far beyond the 10,000-plus jobs created by the company and its suppliers.
- The deal took nine months to negotiate between Kia, the city of West Point, the West Point Development Authority, the Troup County Industrial Development Authority, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- The financial package includes $1 billion in bonds to be issued by the West Point Development Authority.
- An additional $600 million will be provided by the Troup County Industrial Development Authority.
- The city of West Point will receive $800,000 annually for the first five years and about $181,000 for years 6 through 16.
- The city's development authority will receive $650,000 earmarked for economic development.
- Mayor Ferguson noted the $181,000 annual payment offsets public safety and service costs, while a $4 million front-end payment assists with community development goals like better housing, education, and commercial/retail development.
- Troup County will receive $450,000 on top of a $3 million grant to seed a new career academy in advanced manufacturing that is currently in planning stages with the county's school system.
- Troup County Commission Chairman Ricky Wolfe stated plans to complete community assessment and curriculum definition by August of the following year, aligning it with the local school system.
- In exchange for cash payments to the city and county, Kia will receive 100 percent tax abatements on any capital improvements over the 16-year period.
- The county school board will abate 50 percent of taxes for the first eight years and 25 percent for the final eight years.
- Chairman Wolfe stated the $1.6 billion investment surpasses Kia's original $1.1 billion investment to construct the plant.
- The mother facility currently employs just over 3,000 people.
- Twenty percent of Kia workers live in Troup County, and 15 to 16 percent live in Muscogee County.
- More than 450 Kia workers commute to West Point from Columbus.
- Chairman Wolfe highlighted that Kia has consistently blown through sales and profit numbers almost every month, providing a way to competitively expand and gain market share.
- Prior to Kia's arrival near Interstate 85 on the Georgia-Alabama border, West Point's textile industry had collapsed, leaving downtown quiet and declining.
- Kia announced plans in 2006 to build a manufacturing plant on 2,200 acres in West Point and Troup County, located about 45 minutes north of Columbus.
- Production at the plant launched in November 2009.
- U.S. sales of the Sorento crossover utility vehicle and the Optima four-door sedan have fueled Kia's rapid hiring and production growth.
- The West Point plant also manufactures the Hyundai Santa Fe sport utility.
- In February of the current year, Kia assembled its 500,000th vehicle at the facility.
- The plant has a manufacturing capacity for 360,000 vehicles each year.
- Kia Motors spokeswoman Corinne Hodges confirmed there are no current plans to expand the size of the assembly plant.
- Mayor Ferguson noted that Kia's growth has injected confidence into local businesses and helped land nearly 40 new downtown businesses and Point University in the community of about 3,600 people.
- Ferguson stated the city now has a strategic plan and funding stream to execute its core city redevelopment plan, expressing excitement over Kia's long-term presence.
