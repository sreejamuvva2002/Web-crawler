# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-07

**Source:** [Another Hyundai supplier to expand near Georgia EV campus | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/sewon-america-second-georgia-facility/643777/)  ·  `page_000947`

## Facts

- Auto parts manufacturer Sewon America is investing over $300 million toward a second facility in Georgia.
- The investment was announced via a Feb. 21 press release.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located in Rincon, Georgia.
- The facility will produce EV body parts for original equipment manufacturers.
- One of the OEM customers is the Hyundai Metaplant near Savannah.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment in Georgia.
- This supplier investment commitment is at least $800 million higher than initial expectations.
- Sewon’s investment is expected to create 740 jobs.
- Sewon is based in South Korea.
- Sewon already operates one facility in LaGrange, Georgia.
- The second Rincon facility will be Rincon’s largest private investment.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that on his first international trip as governor to Korea, he congratulated Chairman Kim on the company’s then-recent expansion and discussed continuing to work together.
- Gov. Kemp added that those discussions led to the announcement as Sewon America continues to grow its Georgia operations.
- In November, Joon Georgia became the first supplier to announce operations for the Hyundai Metaplant campus.
- Joon Georgia plans to invest $317 million in a production facility in Bulloch County.
- Later that same month, Hyundai Mobis became the second supplier to invest in the area.
- Hyundai Mobis announced it would spend $926 million on an EV Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- In January, Ecoplastic Corporation announced it is investing approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in southeast Georgia.
- Ecoplastic Corporation’s facility will supply components to the incoming electric vehicle campus.
- Last month (February 2023), Seoyon E-Hwa announced it will invest almost $76 million toward its second manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is estimated to create 8,100 jobs in Georgia’s coastal region.
- Gov. Brian Kemp called the Hyundai Metaplant the “largest economic development project in state history.”
- Hyundai began recruiting to fill the Metaplant jobs in November.
- The recruitment information came from a Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority press release.
