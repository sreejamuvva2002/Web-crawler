# Georgia $1.5 Billion Transportation Infrastructure Investment

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-18

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp announces details for $1.5 billion investment in transportation infrastructure in Ga.](https://www.wtoc.com/2024/07/18/gov-kemp-announces-details-15-billion-investment-transportation-infrastructure-ga/)  ·  `page_013339`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced a new $1.5 billion transportation infrastructure investment for the state on Thursday.
- The funding will advance transportation projects reviewed by the State Transportation Board during its monthly meeting and serves as a down payment for future expansion as Georgia experiences record job and population growth.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that unprecedented job creation has been welcomed to all regions of the Peach State over the last few years, and this funding will ensure the infrastructure network meets the needs of that growth.
- The investment was first announced during the 2024 Legislative Session.
- The investment will more than double the state’s funding to cities and counties for local transportation infrastructure.
- $250 million is allocated for Local Roads Funding, which more than doubles State funding for every city and county for local transportation investments.
- $593 million is allocated for Capital Construction, increasing GDOT’s capital construction program by 58 percent to help manage cost increases due to inflation.
- The Capital Construction funding will expedite projects in GDOT’s existing pipeline of projects.
- The Capital Construction funding will help accelerate 24 projects by a total of 43 years.
- $50 million is allocated for Capital Maintenance, increasing GDOT’s capital maintenance budget by 32 percent to resurface state highways around the state.
- $98 million is allocated for Airport Aid, advancing improvements at various general aviation airports.
- $500 million is allocated for a new Freight Program that includes 18 projects devoted to freight-carrying infrastructure.
- The Freight Program aims to improve efficiency, safety, and reliability for the transportation of goods across Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that Georgia is the No. 1 state to build and grow a business and emphasized that investing surplus budget funds in existing and future transportation infrastructure is critical to ensure businesses can expand and goods and services can be transported.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated that Georgia has witnessed unprecedented growth and development in every corner of the state over the last several years.
- Speaker Jon Burns noted that this historic growth presents a significant opportunity to strengthen and secure roads, bridges, and economic development centers facing increased demand for the foreseeable future.
