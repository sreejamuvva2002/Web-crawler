# Toyota Motor Manufacturing Czech Republic

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Toyota announces new BEV to be produced in Europe](https://mobilityground.com/global-news/japan/toyota/toyota-announces-new-bev-to-be-produced-in-europe/)  ·  `page_021165`

## Facts

- Petr Fiala (Czech Prime Minister), Lukáš Vlček (Minister of Industry and Trade), and Yoshihiro Nakata (President of Toyota Motor Europe) announced the production of a new battery electric vehicle at Toyota Motor Manufacturing Czech Republic.
- The new BEV will be the first produced in one of Toyota’s European plants.
- TMMCZ’s plant in Kolin will be expanded from 152,000 to 173,000 square metres.
- The expansion will accommodate production of the new vehicle and its battery, along with new paint and welding shops.
- Toyota will fund the construction of the new BEV manufacturing activity through new capital expenditures of approximately €680 million.
- The Czech Government will provide an investment of up to €64 million towards a dedicated facility for battery assembly.
- This announcement supports Toyota’s multi-pathway strategy aimed at achieving carbon neutral operations in Europe by 2040.
- Petr Fiala stated that the Czech Republic secured Toyota as a significant foreign investor despite interest from other countries.
- The investment will expand current production in Kolín and represents an important step toward a higher technological level while keeping car manufacturing in the country.
- The automotive industry accounts for around 10% of the Czech Republic's GDP.
- The project will create 245 new job opportunities in the region.
- Lukáš Vlček attributed the success to negotiations in Europe and Japan, citing a stable business environment, strong infrastructure, and skilled workforce as key factors.
- The government supported the investment with €64 million, with estimated benefits for the state reaching €250 million.
- Yoshihiro Nakata stated that the new BEV production demonstrates dedication to sustainable mobility and innovation and will enhance Toyota's presence in the European market.
- Toyota currently produces Aygo X and Yaris Hybrid at TMMCZ at a capacity of approximately 220,000 vehicles per year.
- The introduction of BEV production increases the potential of the plant's multi-pathway production capacity.
- The project will lead to incremental direct employment with similar benefits for the supply chain, as the majority of parts volume will be sourced in the Czech Republic.
- TMMCZ has maintained a manufacturing presence in the Czech Republic for over two decades and has produced over 4.5 million vehicles across that period.
- Toyota first started producing Aygo at the plant in 2002 when it operated as a joint venture.
- Toyota acquired full ownership of the plant in 2021.
- TMMCZ is one of the largest employers in the Central Bohemia region, with a workforce of around 3,200 members.
- Approximately 65% of TMMCZ's supply parts are sourced in the Czech Republic.
- TMMCZ hosts Toyota’s first logistics European mega hub, established in 2024 to supply vehicles to Toyota and Lexus dealerships across six Central European markets including Germany, Austria, Poland, Slovakia, Hungary, and the Czech Republic.
- Historical investments at TMMCZ from 2002-2005 for starting the new plant and production totaled €1,069 million.
- Historical investments at TMMCZ from 2020-2024 for plant capacity extension for Yaris and Aygo X TNGA production and other investments totaled €251 million.
- Total historical investments at TMMCZ from 2002-2024 amount to €1,320 million.
