# Suzuki Manufacturing of American Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2002-05-31

**Source:** [Suzuki Becomes a ``Made-in-America'' Manufacturer with Opening of
    Georgia ATV Plant](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2002/05/31/141262.html)  ·  `page_010879`

## Facts

- The facility is located in Rome, Georgia, approximately 60 miles northwest of Atlanta.
- The plant spans 108,350 square feet.
- A $30 million joint investment was made in the Floyd County, Ga. business community by Brea, Calif.-based ASMC and SMC of Japan to build the facility.
- Up to 150 team members will work at the plant at full production.
- 85 percent of the workforce is expected to come from within a 30-mile radius.
- Suzuki officials anticipate purchasing more than 50 percent of production parts from local suppliers annually.
- The facility produces Suzuki's new 400cc Eiger and 500cc Vinson ATV models.
- The United States will be the destination for the majority of Suzuki ATVs produced at the plant.
- Distributors in Canada, Europe, Australia, and New Zealand will receive the remainder of production.
- Onsite manufacturing processes include parts receiving, welding, painting, assembly, packing, storing, shipping, and quality control.
- Quality control is closely linked to Suzuki's Japan-based design engineering department.
- The plant features an advanced conveyor assembly line with robotic subassembly of ATV frames.
- Frames undergo electro-coated treatment and powder-coat paint application.
- Continuous, in-process quality-control inspection is conducted on the production line.
- Onsite support functions include purchasing, finance, human resources, information technology, production control, and engineering.
- Production technicians utilize Kaizen continuous improvement activities inherited from Suzuki's manufacturing plants in Japan.
- The facility incorporates both Japanese and American manufacturing philosophies.
- SMAC director Phil Wofford described the facility as one of the most technically advanced plants in the U.S.
- Georgia Gov. Roy Barnes helped dedicate the new facility and christen the company's first-ever domestically made vehicle, a 2003 Suzuki Eiger 400 ATV.
- SMC executive vice president Sokichi Nakano joined Gov. Barnes for the plant-christening ceremonies.
- SMC senior managing director Shunichi Wakuda attended the ceremony.
- SMC managing director Tsuneo Kobayashi attended the ceremony.
- ASMC president and CEO Rick Suzuki attended the ceremony.
- The plant's opening occurred on May 31, 2002.
- ASMC markets more than 65 products through a U.S. dealer network totaling more than 1,700 dealerships in 49 states.
