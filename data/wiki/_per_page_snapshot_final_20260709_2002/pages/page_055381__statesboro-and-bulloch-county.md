# Statesboro and Bulloch County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2016-03-01

**Source:** [Statesboro | Bulloch County: History Meets High-Tech - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2016/03/01/statesboro-bulloch-county-history-meets-high-tech/)  ·  `page_055381`

## Facts

- Georgia Southern University’s City Campus in Statesboro will house a FabLab business incubator for engineering entrepreneurs featuring state-of-the-art equipment such as 3-D printers, scanners, and CNC machines.
- The GSU City Campus serves as headquarters for the College of Business Administration Business Innovation Group, which promotes regional entrepreneurship, innovation, and economic development.
- Georgia Southern University currently enrolls approximately 20,000 students and employs nearly 2,000 faculty and staffers.
- Georgia Southern University’s local economic impact in Bulloch County was $846 million as of the 2014-2015 fiscal year.
- Ogeechee Technical College collaborates with local officials on job training and economic development initiatives.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County provided land in an industrial park for a $12.6-million Natural Resources Building to house Ogeechee Technical College programs including agribusiness, fish and wildlife management, and forensic science.
- East Georgia State College’s Statesboro campus enrolls more than 1,400 students.
- Great Dane Trailers opened in Bulloch County in 2012, prompting two vendors to locate there.
- Thermo King, a refrigeration-unit manufacturer, and Carrier Transicold, affiliated with the Carrier cooling brand, established testing facilities in Bulloch County for units installed on Great Dane products.
- Boston-based Aspen Aerogels announced in November 2015 it will invest $70 million in a Bulloch County plant that will employ more than 100 workers.
- Aspen Aerogels manufactures energy infrastructure insulation for various energy technology industries.
- Architectural glass fabricator Viracon expanded its Bulloch County operations in 2014, adding 125 jobs through a $2-million investment.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County maintains approximately 150 acres remaining in the rail-served GRAD Gateway Industrial Park.
- The development authority is emphasizing a 200-acre tract at the intersection of U.S. 301 and Interstate 16, which is served by water, sewer, and natural gas lines and has a tax allocation district overlay.
- Bulloch County’s I-16/U.S. 301 industrial site offers a direct connection to the Port of Savannah, located approximately 45 minutes away.
- Statesboro Mayor Jan Moore oversaw the approval of a tax allocation district overlay to incentivize redevelopment for the Blue Mile project.
- The Blue Mile is a grassroots revitalization effort focused on the mile-long South Main Street corridor stretching from Georgia Southern University’s main entrance to the county courthouse.
- The South Main Street Revitalization Committee, led by Keely NeSmith Fennell and Darron Burnette, oversees the Blue Mile project which gained momentum in 2012.
- New construction within the Blue Mile corridor must meet uniform design guidelines established for the area.
- The Downtown Statesboro Development Authority is investing in residential redevelopment through its Homes for Heroes initiative to rehabilitate abandoned houses for public safety workers.
- Statesboro was named one of 15 semi-finalists and the only Georgia community in America’s Best Communities competition sponsored by Frontier Communications, Dish Network, CoBank, and The Weather Channel.
- Eight finalists in the America’s Best Communities competition will each receive $100,000 to launch projects, with the winning community announced in April 2017.
- Statesboro implemented Right Start meetings through its planning and development office to provide potential business operators with coordinated views from engineering, fire, licensing, planning, and water/sewer departments.
- The city partnered with Birmingham-based Retail Strategies to create a Retail Recruitment Strategic Plan to identify appropriate businesses for its retail mix.
- A Krispy Kreme doughnut shop was recently permitted in Statesboro as part of ongoing retail development driven by the Georgia Southern University student population.
- The multi-jurisdictional Statesboro-Bulloch County Crime Suppression Team was formed in January 2014 through a city and county task force.
- Georgia Southern University contributed two campus police officers to the crime suppression team, covering all associated costs for officers and vehicles after Board of Regents approval.
- Interim Georgia Southern University President Jean Bartels actively supports economic development partnerships between the university, city, and county.
- South Georgia Tormenta FC was announced in fall 2015 as a new professional league soccer franchise that will join the Premier Development League in May 2016.
- The team will play seven home games on a 14-match schedule for 2016, with its season opening May 13 and first home game May 21 at Georgia Southern University.
- Darin Van Tassel, co-owner and president of South Georgia Tormenta FC, projects the team will generate a $5.5-million impact on the local economy.
