# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-01

**Source:** [Suzuki Reorganizes Business – Drag Bike News](https://www.cycledrag.com/suzuki-reorganizes-business)  ·  `page_010834`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) became a 100% subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC (SMO) effective March 1, 2024.
- SMAC was founded in 2001 and is based in Rome, Georgia.
- SMAC serves as Suzuki’s All-Terrain Vehicle (ATV) manufacturing facility.
- For over two decades, all Suzuki adult KingQuad ATVs have been manufactured at the Rome, Georgia location.
- Prior to the reorganization, SMAC stock was owned by Suzuki Motor Corporation (SMC) (20%) and Suzuki Motor of America, Inc. (SMAI) (80%).
- SMAI is owned by SMC and acts as the holding company for Suzuki companies operating in the United States.
- The organizational change consolidates ATV sales and production arms to strengthen the overall ATV business in the United States and globally.
- A primary objective of the move is to strengthen the supply chain and purchasing policies for locally sourced parts.
- SMO aims to improve inventory management for both the company and its dealer body by more accurately linking production to sales demand.
- Improved inventory management is expected to protect sales margins, flooring costs, and profitability for both groups.
- The reorganization enables better, more direct communication between SMO and SMAC, improving staff interaction.
- A collaborative environment will be created between the production and sales groups of Suzuki ATVs.
- The change fosters better connection between the Rome- and Brea-based teams.
- Masami Haga is the President of SMO and commented on the new structure.
- Haga stated that expanding commitment to the growth of the entire powersports business is a primary goal of the change.
- The evolution will build on relationships with Suzuki dealers and SMAC vendor partners.
- Leaders expect improved responsiveness to the ATV market by linking production to sales demand, noting ATV industry sales are generally steady throughout the calendar year with demand typically increasing in fall months.
- SMAC becoming a subsidiary of SMO sets the stage for new Suzuki ATV offerings and enhancements to existing products in the future.
- Suzuki’s United States distribution headquarters are in Brea, California, where it relocated in 1981 after first coming to the United States in 1963.
- Suzuki is credited with offering the first four-wheel ATV in 1982 with the introduction of the Suzuki LT125.
- Suzuki’s current flagship ATV is the KingQuad 750AXi.
