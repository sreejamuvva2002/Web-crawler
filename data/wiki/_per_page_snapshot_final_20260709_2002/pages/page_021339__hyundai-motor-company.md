## Facts

- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz sought to distance his company from an Immigration and Customs Enforcement raid earlier this month at the company’s battery factory in Georgia.
- The immigration raid resulted in the arrest of hundreds of South Korean workers.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz clarified that the immigration raid had targeted a facility operated by LG Energy Solutions.
- The factory is operated by LG Energy Solutions, and most of the detained workers were employed by suppliers for that company, not Hyundai.
- Muñoz made these remarks during a briefing with reporters on Thursday after the automaker’s CEO Investor Day event in New York City.
- Muñoz stated he could not believe what he saw because he would have known beforehand if it involved his facility.
- Upon reviewing details, Muñoz realized the raid targeted LG’s battery plant, not Hyundai’s facility.
- Muñoz noted that all media photos of the raid showed Hyundai’s Metaplant facility rather than the neighboring LG factory.
- The plant’s construction was delayed two to three months due to labor shortages.
- The September 4th raid resulted in the arrest of 475 workers, most of whom were from South Korea.
- The battery plant is jointly operated by Hyundai and LG Energy Solutions.
- President Donald Trump announced he will allow foreign companies to temporarily bring in “people of expertise” to train American workers.
- Critics warn that the raid may have chilled foreign investment in the US, particularly targeting automakers working on EVs.
- Labor experts warn that the ICE raid in Georgia could chill foreign investment in the US and target particular industries, especially automakers working on EVs.
- Muñoz defended the foreign workers who were in Georgia to help build the factory, arguing that the expertise to build these specialized factories does not exist in the US.
- Muñoz called for the creation of a new type of visa for highly skilled workers who help develop enormous projects to ensure future raids do not happen.
- Muñoz stated that both the South Korean and US governments are working actively to ensure situations like this do not happen again.
