# Georgia Electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (EVTOL) Program

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-27

**Source:** [Lawmakers Huddle: Georgia on the verge of 'transportation revolution' | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2025/06/27/lawmakers-huddle-georgia-on-the-verge-of-transportation-revolution)  ·  `page_001638`

## Facts

- Beginning July 1, a new law gives the Georgia Department of Transportation authority to regulate electric vertical takeoff and landing, or EVTOL, aircraft.
- Walmart will deliver products by drones from several northern arc Walmarts at the end of this year, with expansion statewide in 2026.
- Tesla has kicked off their robo-taxis.
- Waymo in Atlanta has kicked off autonomous vehicle operations.
- Through the rest of 2025 and the rest of 2026, Georgia will see an explosion of autonomous vehicles hitting its streets.
- EVTOL aircraft will initially move around 'lily pads' (formally called vertiports).
- Alpharetta and Roswell have stepped up with plans in action to go along the 400 corridor.
- Vertiports do not need a runway like Hartsfield, PDK, or Charlie Brown airports.
- A vertiport will be about 43,000 square feet (about 1 acre) with four landing facilities.
- All four landing facilities will have power generation from Georgia Power or one of the state's EMCs to charge EVTOL aircraft in 15 minutes.
- Operators will be able to use any vertiport to create competition and a price structure along the lines of an Uber XL or an Uber Black.
- Rep. Todd Jones hopes the first flight will kick off the World Cup here in Atlanta in July of 2026.
- Republican Rep. Todd Jones of South Forsyth chairs the House Committee on Technology and Infrastructure Innovation.
