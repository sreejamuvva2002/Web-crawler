# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Designing with a focus on human states: an experience-driven approach to expanding the possibilities of mobility | Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.](https://automotive.panasonic.com/en/studio/story/04)  ·  `page_047799`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems believes time spent in a car should be more than just moving from one place to another, aiming to redefine the mobility experience with a focus on people and their lifestyles.
- The company created the “WELL Cabin” project and initiatives with the “WELL Cabin GranLuxe” to explore the future of cabin spaces.
- The WELL Cabin GranLuxe is a concept vehicle based on the Toyota HiAce featuring a 55-inch large transparent OLED display, a proprietary entertainment system, and a refined living space.
- The WELL Cabin GranLuxe was exhibited at Tokyo Auto Salon 2025, attracting significant attention.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems' core business is OEM, working with car manufacturers to deliver products that exceed their expectations.
- The company shifted its focus from B-to-B business to considering end users by examining people's actions and lifestyles beyond traditional mobility assumptions.
- Research identified a clear need for “the desire to rest while on the move,” particularly a short 30-minute power nap for businesspeople, which inspired cabin environment exploration.
- The company previously developed the “Luxe” concept before the WELL Cabin GranLuxe to help passengers switch moods or build anticipation for their next experience.
- The team utilized “Interpretation” methods, often used by tour guides, to introduce cultural or natural value inside the vehicle as entertainment during travel.
- Product planning involved setting up personas and interviewing users to envision ideal in-cabin environments tailored to specific cases.
- Designs were inspired by executives using short naps or meditation between business meetings to reset their minds, leading to spaces that allow relaxation while supporting productivity.
- The cabin experience was designed to share information with vegan tourists about sushi without seafood or explain Japanese cultural connections to enhance the journey beyond simply learning information.
- Designer Kanji Muto focused on shaping “human states” such as mental calmness or clarity by combining elements like light, sound, and scent.
- The original “Luxe” concept was designed for executives with an emphasis on a relaxing environment for small groups.
- The WELL Cabin GranLuxe broadened its focus to include tourists, inbound travelers, and affluent customers while drawing inspiration from Japanese cultural concepts of “hare” (special occasions) and “ke” (everyday things).
- The exterior design motif was inspired by a cruise ship sailing across the open sea to convey elegance, relaxation, and luxury.
- Panasonic collaborated with Italdesign, a renowned automotive design studio in Italy, to ensure the vehicle's exterior would leave a lasting impression on international audiences.
- The company envisions advanced autonomous driving reducing commuting time to zero, making cabin spaces chosen for desired experiences increasingly important as remote work reduces unwanted travel.
- Panasonic believes that in the era of autonomous driving, cars will sense people inside, read their state of mind and body, and actively respond, making the design of human states more important than ever.
- Satoshi Tsuchiya joined the company in 2006, worked on advanced development of displays and video LSIs as well as IVI and CID systems, has been involved in product planning since 2019, and is currently the project manager for the “WELL Cabin” project in the Cabin UX Business Development Office.
- Yusuke Shiotani joined the company in 2025, is engaged in co-creation activities with car OEMs and product planning for “WELL Cabin”, holds a Travel Service Supervisor qualification, and handles planning and customer engagement for inbound-focused business.
- Kanji Muto joined the company in 1993, is engaged in interior design development in the Cabin UX Business Development Office, and is currently responsible for the design development of “WELL Cabin”.
