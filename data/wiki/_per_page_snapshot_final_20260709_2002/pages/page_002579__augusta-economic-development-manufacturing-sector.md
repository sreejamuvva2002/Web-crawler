# Augusta Economic Development & Manufacturing Sector

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-23

**Source:** [Three Investments Spring to Life in Augusta | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/three-investments-spring-life-augusta)  ·  `page_002579`

## Facts

- In April, Syensqo began construction on a new battery-grade polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) manufacturing facility in Augusta that may well become the largest PVDF production facility in North America.
- Syensqo was born from a demerger of Solvay.
- Chief Technology and Innovation Officer and Chief North America Officer Mike Finelli explained how the decision underscores “the strategic importance of Augusta as a key player in the EV supply chain along the burgeoning Battery Belt.”
- Syensqo leaders reinforced that local and state partnerships were deciding factors in choosing Augusta to support the company’s future.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson thanked Syensqo in return, highlighting their more than two decades in the community as “a premier, quality employer.”
- The Augusta Economic Development Authority echoed that sentiment, sharing, “Syensqo fits the community’s goal of creating well-paying, skilled manufacturing jobs and shows once again that Augusta-Richmond County is the right place for manufacturing to not only locate but to grow and expand.”
- StandardAero has been operating in Augusta since 1974.
- The company services approximately 425 aircraft and 500 turbine aircraft engines each year from their current facility.
- Projects for a new hangar and engine shop officially kicked off in April 2024.
- Vice President and General Manager of StandardAero’s Augusta facility Chris Bodine stated, “The new facility will add over 60% of new space to our existing footprint.”
- The current facility was built in the 1970s when aircraft were a lot smaller.
- The new facility will allow support for additional super mid-size to large cabin aircraft for airframe and avionics while significantly expanding the engine shop.
- President of StandardAero Business Aviation Tony Brancato added that the expansion will allow work on larger Dassault Falcon, Gulfstream, and Bombardier aircraft and meet rapidly growing MRO needs of HTF7000 series engine operators.
- The new facility will be adjacent to the Augusta Regional Airport, one of eight regional commercial airports in Georgia that play a key role in supporting the state’s soaring aerospace industry.
- Aerospace and defense products totaled $11.1 billion in exports in 2023, making them the largest Georgia export by dollar value and landing Georgia as a top five state for overall aerospace exports in the U.S.
- The state’s logistics infrastructure includes Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, deepwater ports in Savannah and Brunswick, and thousands of miles of rail and highway systems to help companies move products to global markets.
- GF Casting Solutions AG has plans for a state-of-the-art, LEED-certified high-pressure die casting facility in Augusta targeting the U.S. e-mobility market.
- President Carlos Vasto explained that building the facility is a very long-term investment that will complete their footprint to become truly global in the e-mobility market.
- The GF Casting Solutions facility is estimated to begin operations in 2027.
- It will produce cast aluminum parts with a special focus on large structural components for the automotive industry.
- The facility will be built at the Augusta Corporate Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) site.
- GRAD-certified sites are one of the speed-to-market solutions that Georgia offers.
- GF Casting Solutions will work with Georgia Quick Start to develop its future workforce.
- At the time of the announcement, Vasto shared, “The location is ideal for addressing the thriving automotive industry in the region.”
- Over the last few years, Georgia has asserted itself as a core piece of the rapidly developing “Battery Belt” and e-mobility industry in the Southeast.
- Georgia possesses an abundance of development-ready sites.
- Georgia has a skilled workforce, including cybersecurity talent nurtured at the Georgia Cyber Center.
- Georgia has robust logistics infrastructure.
- Georgia has a reliable, low-cost energy grid supported by long term investments such as Plant Vogtle in Waynesboro, near Augusta.
- The state offers attractive incentives such as a low corporate tax rate, workforce training programs, and a high quality of life.
- Communities in every corner of the state benefit from the state’s business-friendly environment.
- Augusta was featured on the 2024 International VIP Tour, which gave members of Georgia’s Consular Corps a first-hand look into factors that make Georgia unique.
- The tour highlighted how five featured manufacturers built their success in east Georgia.
