# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-16

**Source:** [Customer Satisfaction: The Core of Suzuki’s New Operations - Adrenaline Culture of Speed](https://livingwithgravity.com/customer-satisfaction-the-core-of-suzukis-new-operations/)  ·  `page_010869`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor USA rolled out its 500,000th ATV from the Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) plant in Rome, Georgia.
- The SMAC facility opened in 2002 and has produced ATVs for the US and Canadian markets as well as exporting to 25 other countries worldwide.
- Before SMAC opened, Suzuki manufactured its North American ATVs in Japan.
- Masami Haga serves as President of SMO and SMAC.
- Masami Haga stated that “North America represents the largest ATV market in the world, and it’s important that these products are built here by skilled American workers.”
- Suzuki collaborates with US-based suppliers for parts and components to embed itself in the local economy.
- The 500,000th ATV produced at the facility is the KingQuad 750 AXi flagship model.
- Suzuki touts the KingQuad range as the “inventor of the four-wheel ATV” with two decades of continuous production.
- Suzuki introduced its first four-wheeled ATV, the LT125, in 1982.
- Suzuki's current ATV lineup includes models for kids as well as 400cc and 750cc KingQuads.
- Suzuki operates an extensive dealer network of over 700 dealers across North America.
- Suzuki plans to display the 500,000th ATV at its headquarters rather than sell it.
- The KingQuad 750 AXi features a powerful engine, rugged design, independent suspension, and electronic power steering.
- Skilled American workers at the SMAC facility ensure each ATV meets Suzuki's high quality and performance standards.
- Local production in Georgia allows Suzuki to respond faster to market demands and customer feedback.
- Working with US-based suppliers reduces lead times and improves overall production efficiency.
- The SMAC facility follows stringent environmental standards, including reducing waste and optimizing energy use.
- Suzuki's ATVs are designed for various off-roading activities including trail riding, hunting, and farming.
- Suzuki continues to explore new technologies and innovations to enhance ATV performance and durability.
