# Kia Georgia West Point Assembly Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Makes History with 2025 EV9 SUV Assembly | EV.com](https://ev.com/news/kia-georgia-makes-history-with-2025-ev9-suv-assembly&blogId=2680)  ·  `page_001078`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia has started production at its West Point assembly plant in Georgia for the 2025 Kia EV9 electric vehicle (EV) SUV.
- The addition of the 2025 EV9 marks a historical milestone as the first EV assembled in the West Point assembly plant.
- Governor of Georgia Brian Kemp, James Watson (Kia Georgia’s Vice President of Production), and SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon (President and CEO for Kia North America and Kia America) attended the production celebration.
- Governor Brian Kemp praised the milestone, stating, “I want to congratulate the Kia Georgia team members on today’s achievement and thank them for choosing the Peach State yet again as we work to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.”
- Prior to the EV9 addition, the Kia Georgia West Point assembly plant only assembled the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage models.
- The transition to adding the EV9 to Kia Georgia’s assembly roster was supported by an investment of over $200 million.
- The over $200 million investment led to the creation of around 200 new jobs at the plant.
- Production of the new EV9 in Georgia is aimed to be eligible for the $7,500 EV tax credit by leveraging local production incentives.
- The Kia EV9 has won the 2024 World Car of the Year and 2024 World Electric Car awards at the New York International Auto Show.
- The Kia EV9 also won Best of the Best at the Red Dot Award: Product Design 2024.
- SeungKyu Yoon, President and CEO for Kia North America and Kia America, stated, “Having achieved prestigious accolades such as World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year, EV9 will be in excellent hands with the team members at Kia Georgia and their track record for assembling award-winning, world-renowned products speaks for itself.”
- The company anticipates the 2025 Kia EV9 will exceed the 50,000-unit milestone within its first year of launch in the U.S. market due to high demand in global EV markets.
