# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Electric Vertical Aircraft Manufacturing Company Archer to Create 1,000 Jobs in Covington | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-electric-vertical-aircraft-manufacturing-31083)  ·  `page_016742`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Archer Aviation Inc., an aerospace company advancing sustainable urban air mobility, will create 1,000 jobs and invest $118 million over 10 years in a new state-of-the-art electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) manufacturing facility located alongside the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has made fostering innovation a priority as they work with companies to bring the jobs of the future to the No. 1 state for business.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Archer Aviation Inc. is based in Santa Clara, CA, and is currently developing and manufacturing eVTOL aircraft for use in Urban Air Mobility networks.
- Archer Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein announced the company's decision to locate its first manufacturing facility in Covington after many months of discussion and evaluation.
- Adam Goldstein stated that Archer's goal of creating Urban Air Mobility networks across major cities nationwide requires ramping up aircraft production, and the new facility represents a huge step toward meeting this challenge.
- Archer Vice President of Manufacturing Glen Burks described the new manufacturing facility as a crucial next step in bringing their vision for eVTOL travel to life.
- Glen Burks expressed excitement over Covington as a location and partner, noting they look forward to breaking ground in the coming months.
- Archer plans to construct a new aircraft manufacturing facility adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County, which was established in 1963 as part of a long-term airport master plan.
- The site is strategically accessible by Georgia’s robust interstate system via I-20, and large adjacent parcels of land were set aside for future development.
- The new Archer facility will use one of these adjacent parcels.
- Archer’s new facility, located at the Covington Municipal Airport, will consist of three buildings across more than 96 acres.
- Construction is expected to begin in December 2022 and follow a phased buildout of eVTOL aircraft production, with eventual capacity able to produce up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Hiring is expected to begin in late 2023, and the facility is slated to open in the first half of 2024.
- Covington Mayor Steve Horton expressed excitement for Archer Aviation to join the city's business community.
- Mayor Steve Horton noted that the Covington Municipal Airport is a point of pride for the city and that Archer's presence within the airport plan is another distinction, continuing Covington's trend on the cutting edge for quality jobs and new technology.
- Newton County Industrial Development Authority Chairman Lanier Sims expressed excitement for Archer to join the community.
- Lanier Sims stated that Archer fits perfectly with Newton County's current industry mix, will be an incredible asset to the Covington airport, and emphasized the Development Authority's commitment to ensuring the workforce is prepared for outstanding jobs from Archer Aviation.
- Lanier Sims expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development for their leadership in keeping Georgia the number one state for business.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick welcomed Archer Aviation to metro Atlanta, citing community strength to help ensure the success of their new facility.
- Katie Kirkpatrick highlighted Metro Atlanta’s expertise in the aviation industry coupled with support from academic, business, civic and government partners as making the region the perfect place to invest in a new facility.
- The Global Commerce Industrial Projects team represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project in partnership with the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, the Georgia Center of Innovation’s Aerospace industry team, Georgia EMC, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Tech.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that technology and innovation continue to drive change across strategic industries, and Archer Aviation is on the forefront of a technological evolution in the aviation industry using an innovative, state-of-the art eVTOL aircraft design to sustainably tackle mobility issues in urban centers.
- Pat Wilson expressed thrill over Archer's decision to invest in Georgia and highlighted the Center of Innovation’s specialized Aerospace team's involvement in fostering Georgia’s aerospace and defense industries.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline, with a dozen high schools that offer training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has been cultivating an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer Aviation for more than 10 years by building and connecting Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) networks for the commercialization of Advanced Air Mobility and UAS Systems.
