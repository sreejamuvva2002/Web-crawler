# Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-05

**Source:** [Yamaha announces major US headquarters relocation](https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2026/03/05/yamaha-us-headquarters-relocation/89000261007/)  ·  `page_015716`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. announced plans to relocate the headquarters of Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A. from Cypress, California, to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The transition is expected to take place in phases from late 2026 through 2028.
- As part of the plan, Yamaha will sell its 25.1-acre Cypress campus, which has served as its U.S. home base for nearly five decades.
- The company described the decision as part of broader structural reforms designed to centralize operations and make more efficient use of corporate assets.
- Yamaha purchased the Cypress property in 1978 and opened its offices there in 1979.
- The Southern California campus has long housed corporate leadership and financial services operations.
- Much of Yamaha's U.S. business has already shifted to Georgia over the years.
- The company's Marine division moved to Kennesaw in 1999, followed by its Motorsports division in 2019.
- The relocation of remaining corporate functions will consolidate key operations in one state.
- Yamaha plans to use a sale and leaseback arrangement during the transition.
- Under that structure, the company would sell the California property while temporarily leasing it back to maintain operations as teams gradually move east.
