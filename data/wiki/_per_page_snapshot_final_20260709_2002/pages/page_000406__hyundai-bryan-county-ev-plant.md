# Hyundai Bryan County EV Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-26

**Source:** [EVs, embraced by drivers, automakers, to be built in Bryan County GA](https://ninerswire.usatoday.com/story/business/automotive/2022/07/26/savannah-georgia-ev-electric-cars-hyundai-plant/10068727002/)  ·  `page_000406`

## Facts

- Savannah will be an EV epicenter thanks to a Hyundai assembly plant slated to open in 2025.
- Bryan County will be the epicenter for EV production as Hyundai is set to build its first EV plant in the U.S. by 2025.
- Georgia Ports Authority terminals in Savannah and Brunswick will move vehicles from the facility around the world.
- Hyundai's local facility is the biggest economic development project in state history.
- The facility is part of a larger, industry-wide movement away from fossil fuel-powered cars and trucks and toward electric vehicles.
- Jae Hoon Chang, Hyundai Motor’s president and CEO, stated during the Bryan County facility announcement that “This high-tech EV plant represents the future of our business.”
- Competitors aggressively pushing into the EV market alongside Hyundai include Toyota, Ford, and BMW.
- Toyota will have 70 models to choose from in the coming years and introduced the all-new 2023 bZ4X EV at $42,000.
- Nissan announced the Nissan Ambition 2030 campaign to make “40% of U.S. vehicle sales fully electric, with even more to be electrified” by 2030.
- Nissan has sold more than 600,000 LEAFs globally and more than 173,000 in the U.S., with its newest EV, the Ariya, joining the lineup in fall 2022.
- Average EV price points are $56,500 compared to $47,000 for gas-powered models.
- The Nissan LEAF is priced at $27,800, approximately $4,000 less than the gas-powered Nissan Altima.
- The Hyundai Kona EV and Hyundai Kona are priced the same at around $34,000.
- President Joe Biden signed an executive order in December 2021 to “reach net zero emissions economy-wide by no later than 2050.”
- The Obama administration's Climate Action Plan previously aimed to reduce carbon dioxide emissions by 32% from 2005 levels by 2030.
- Gas prices hit all-time highs above $5 a gallon in some U.S. markets in June, driving consumer interest in EVs as an economic alternative.
- Automakers are prioritizing sleek exterior designs and modern aesthetics for EVs to address early criticism of compact and boxy styling.
- General Motors’ Cadillac LYRIQ features a 33-inch cabin display and is positioned with futuristic exterior styling.
- Ford's F-150 Lightning was developed based on prospective buyer feedback requesting a modern look for the EV pickup truck version.
- Hyundai's IONIQ model has been noted by Edmunds.com for its eye-catching exterior styling.
