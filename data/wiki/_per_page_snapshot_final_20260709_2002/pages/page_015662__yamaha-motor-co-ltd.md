# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03

**Source:** [Yamaha to move headquarters to Georgia](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2026/03/yamaha-to-move-headquarters-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_015662`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. is moving its U.S. headquarters from California to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The relocation follows roughly 50 years of operation in California.
- The decision followed a meeting between Governor Brian P. Kemp and Yamaha executives in October of last year at the Southeast U.S./Japan (SEUS-Japan) annual gathering in Tokyo.
- The relocation of the U.S. headquarters will begin this year and is expected to continue through 2028.
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. manufactures ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, other motorized products, motorcycles, marine products, power products, and robotics.
- The company currently employs over 2,300 Georgians across its existing operations in the state.
- The Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America factory in Newnan is a 1.3-million-square-foot facility that opened in 1986 and currently employs roughly 2,000 Georgians.
- Yamaha relocated its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The company expanded its Kennesaw marine facility with a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- In 2019, Yamaha moved its motorsports operations to Georgia to benefit from close proximity to existing facilities.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed the announcement and stated, “After many years of great partnership, we are honored and proud to welcome Yamaha’s American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business.”
- Governor Kemp added that the move is a testament to what Georgia offers job creators and told other California-based companies looking for a better home, “we’ll give you plenty of reasons to keep Georgia on your mind.”
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. was founded in 1955 and maintains its global headquarters in Iwata, Shizuoka, Japan.
