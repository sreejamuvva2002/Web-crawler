# SK Innovation

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK Innovation battery project targets US automotive market - Best Magazine](https://www.bestmag.co.uk/sk-innovation-battery-project-targets-us-automotive-market/)  ·  `page_000576`

## Facts

- SK Innovation plans to invest 1.98 trillion South Korean won (US$1.01 billion) to establish an electric vehicle (EV) batteries production base in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The project aims to establish SK Innovation in the US, which is described as “the largest battleground of global automakers”.
- The plant will be built on a 278-acre site in Commerce City.
- The facility is expected to have an annual production capacity of 9.8 gigawatt-hours.
- The announcement confirms speculation of such a project reported earlier this year.
- The state of Georgia will provide administrative support, including education, training, and employment programmes.
- SK Innovation is part of the South Korean conglomerate, the SK Group.
- SK Innovation will establish SK Battery America (working title) as a subsidiary to handle its US investments, both current and future.
- Establishing the subsidiary should enable the SK Group to avoid a potential 25% US auto tariff that the Trump White House has threatened to impose.
- The likelihood of the tariff was made more likely by General Motors’ recent decision to idle five factories in North America.
- Chief executive Kim Joon stated that SK Innovation has been actively seeking a production base to secure competitiveness and increase orders in the battery business, a major global market.
- Kim Joon added that with their business at the hub of the global auto industry, they will grow as a top player in the battery business.
- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal commented that SK Innovation’s decision to make this significant investment and create more than 2,000 jobs in Jackson County reflects Georgia’s reputation as a top destination for international businesses, especially those in the automotive supply sector.
- The project is expected to create more than 2,000 jobs in Jackson County.
- Construction is expected to start on the plant in early 2019.
- Mass supply from the facility is expected from 2022.
