# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-17

**Source:** [Georgia town of 157 lands $671M Energy Department loan for manufacturer | Just The News](https://justthenews.com/nation/states/center-square/georgia-town-157-lands-671m-energy-department-loan-manufacturer)  ·  `page_052757`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy is giving conditional approval to Aspen Aerogels Georgia for a possible $671 million loan for a new manufacturing facility in Register.
- The company will make aerogel thermal barriers, which are protective layers for electric vehicle batteries.
- The plant will initially create 550 construction jobs and add 225 permanent, full-time jobs.
- Operating at full capacity, the Georgia factory is expected to supply thermal barriers for over 2 million vehicle batteries annually.
- The incremental fuel savings is equivalent to removing over 5,000 internal combustion engine vehicles from the road each year.
- The facility enables a reduction of over 8,000 tons of carbon dioxide equivalent annually.
- The project will significantly expand EV thermal barrier manufacturing capacity in North America, helping to reinforce the domestic EV supply chain while delivering new economic opportunities for workers in Georgia.
- The loan would be part of the Advances Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Program, which supports manufacturing of advanced technology vehicles, qualifying components, and materials that improve fuel economy.
- Aspen currently has multi-year contracts with General Motors, Toyota, Scania, Automotive Cells Company, and Audi.
- Register is located in the southeastern corner of Georgia.
- The addition of the manufacturing plant is part of the President’s Justice40 Initiative, which targets what the administration defines as disadvantaged communities.
- The Aspen Aerogels facility will be located in a disadvantaged community identified by the Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool, which faces one of the highest rates of expected agricultural losses and barriers to transportation in the country.
- Aspen is seeking to work with local and federal partners on public electric transportation solutions to help its employees commute to the facility.
- The loan is contingent on the department and the company meeting certain conditions.
- The project will boost the Biden administration's goals for electric vehicle adoption.
