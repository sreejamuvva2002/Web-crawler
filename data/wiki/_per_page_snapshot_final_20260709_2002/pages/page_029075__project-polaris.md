# Project Polaris

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Polaris | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/polaris)  ·  `page_029075`

## Facts

- In January 2025, the U.S. Department of Energy’s Loan Programs Office announced the closing of a $15 billion loan guarantee to Pacific Gas & Electric Company (PG&E).
- The loan guarantee for PG&E’s Project Polaris will support a portfolio of projects to expand hydropower generation and battery storage.
- The portfolio includes upgrading transmission capacity through reconductoring and grid enhancing technologies.
- The portfolio will enable virtual power plants throughout PG&E’s service area in Northern and Central California.
- LPO’s financing comes at a lower interest rate than traditional capital market financing, helping reduce upward pressure on electricity costs for PG&E’s 16 million customers.
- At full deployment, PG&E’s investments are expected to support thousands of on-going construction and operations jobs.
- PG&E plans to expand outreach programs to deliver community benefits in partnership with local governments, Native American Tribes, community-based organizations, and low-and-middle income customers.
- PG&E has committed to locate many projects in disadvantaged communities, as identified by the Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool.
- Project Polaris is owned by PG&E.
- The loan program is 1706.
- The guaranteed loan amount is $15 billion.
- The issuance date was January 2025.
- The project supports more than 1,000 permanent U.S. jobs.
