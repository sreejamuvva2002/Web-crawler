# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** ['This project is monumental': Hyundai breaks ground on EV battery plant in Bryan County](https://www.yahoo.com/news/project-monumental-hyundai-breaks-ground-150032149.html)  ·  `page_009186`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on Metaplant America, an electric vehicle and battery plant under construction in Bryan County.
- The automaker is investing $5.5 billion in its first EV plant in the United States.
- Dozens of Hyundai officials, including Executive Chair Euisun Chung, traveled to Savannah for the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Site preparation began on the 11-building, 16 million-square-foot facility this summer, and much of the 3,000-acre site has already been cleared and graded.
- Hyundai projects a 2025 opening for the plant.
- According to Hyundai's North American Division President José Muñoz, the plant will be the "foundation of Hyundai Motor Group's future in North America" as the automaker aims to "lead the future of electrified mobility."
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, U.S. Sens. Raphael Warnock and Jon Ossoff, and U.S. Rep. Buddy Carter attended the ceremony alongside dozens of other state and local dignitaries.
- U.S. Rep. Buddy Carter is noted as a Republican not backing Trump's pledge to scrap the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated, "At Hyundai Motor Group, our corporate mission is 'Progress for humanity.' Looking around I know we picked the right site, the right partners and the right state for progress."
- Chung added, "Together we will make the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America the envy of the industry."
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp said, "Even in Georgia, where we seem to make history every day, this project is monumental."
- Kemp also noted, "We're always working toward the future, and this project is significant."
- The groundbreaking occurred five months after Hyundai officials initially announced the facility.
- Trip Tollison, president of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, called the deal "God's handiwork."
- Tollison noted the agreement took just several months to complete, whereas such deals typically take one and a half to two years.
- Tollison stated the event marked a new chapter of progress that made the plant's completion feel "more real than ever."
- Tollison remarked, "It’s a ceremonial symbolic day that captures all of our hard work."
- Tollison added, "We have a ton of work to do but I think local officials are happy with the progress."
- In anticipation of the plant, neighboring Effingham County halted future developments to prepare for growth.
- Hyundai has not revealed which specific vehicle models will be produced at the Savannah plant.
- The automaker currently produces two EV models and has announced plans for eight additional models before the end of the decade.
- Savannah-area economic development officials stated Hyundai is expected to pay wages "very competitive with others in the advanced manufacturing market."
- The local average annual salary in the advanced manufacturing industry is $58,105, excluding benefits.
- Gov. Brian Kemp emphasized Georgia's strong workforce and the state's commitment to bringing quality jobs.
- Kemp stated, "The auto industry will see more change in the next ten years than in the last 100. Thanks to Hyundai, that future is going to be made right here."
- Savannah Mayor Van Johnson expressed concern about where the plant's workforce will come from and wants to partner with local colleges to ensure positions are filled.
- Johnson said, "I want to make sure we have the workforce to take advantage of these opportunities."
- Johnson noted, "8,100 jobs could from inside or outside our communities. I’m asking what skills are needed now for these jobs because we can’t wait until 2025 to get started on that."
- Jose Munoz, president and CEO of Hyundai North America, pledged Bryan County residents' full commitment to providing resources.
- Munoz added, "Here we are five months later, ready to break ground and start construction. This is going to be a massive operation. We are excted to be your neighbor and partner."
- Tae-yong Cho, ambassador of the Republic of Korea to the U.S., stated that both countries are a "perfect duo to do business together."
- Cho said, "It’s great to be in Georgia."
- Cho added, "This is a great occasion for the U.S. and Korea. Today the Korean auto industry embarks on a new journey of climate consciousness. Georgia is a state that embodies the spirit of economics."
