# North Carolina Electric Vehicle Industry

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-18

**Source:** [Introducing North Carolina's Electric Vehicle Industry  | Burlington, NC - Official Website](https://www.burlingtonnc.gov/2426/Introducing-North-Carolinas-Electric-Veh)  ·  `page_000133`

## Facts

- Since December 2021, North Carolina has announced two major projects in the electric vehicle industry.
- Toyota, a Japanese automotive company, will construct a battery manufacturing facility at the Greensboro-Randolph megasite.
- VinFast, a Vietnamese electric vehicle company, will bring its first US manufacturing plant to Moncure in Chatham County.
- Toyota announced an automotive battery plant on an 1,800-acre megasite in the Greensboro-Randolph area in December of 2021.
- The $1.29 billion Toyota project is expected to be online by 2025.
- The Toyota battery plant will create 1,750 jobs.
- The battery plant will initially have four production lines in operation to produce 200,000 batteries per year.
- The Toyota battery plant has the capability to expand to six production lines.
- Within 20 years, economic growth from the Toyota plant is expected to increase by $9.5 billion in North Carolina.
- The Toyota battery plant project has received around $439 million in state and local incentives for its development.
- The Toyota battery plant location is just 30 miles southwest of Burlington.
- The Toyota site offers easy access to the highway, railroad, airport, and seaports.
- Quality schools to train employees in related manufacturing industries are located within Randolph County and its surrounding counties.
- There is strong speculation that Toyota will soon announce an even larger expansion at the new battery plant.
- The 2022-23 North Carolina Budget, passed by state lawmakers and signed into law in mid-July, contained an appropriation of $225 million to “the megasite industrial park in Randolph County.”
- No company was specifically named for the $225 million megasite appropriation.
- The state budget agreement would trigger if a mystery company added an additional 2,125 jobs with capital investment between $1.7 and $2.1 billion.
- Most of the $225 million appropriation would be for site work, roadwork, and wetlands mitigation, with some available for job creation.
- In March 2022, North Carolina welcomed its first automobile manufacturing facility within the state: VinFast.
- VinFast chose Moncure in Chatham County to construct a new automobile manufacturing facility.
- The VinFast project will bring 7,500 jobs to the area over the course of five years.
- The 2,100-acre Triangle Innovation Point megasite will host the VinFast project and supplier industries.
- The goal for the VinFast facility is to begin operation in July of 2024.
- VinFast expects initial vehicle output capacity to be around 150,000 annually.
- VinFast manufacturing will focus on two models of VinFast SUVs in addition to producing batteries for electric vehicles and electric buses.
- The arrival of VinFast marks the largest North Carolina economic development project in history.
- By 2026, VinFast plans to invest $4 billion in both the company and the county.
- Within 30 years, the VinFast facility is expected to grow the North Carolina economy by $71.6 billion.
- The North Carolina Department of Commerce has approved $854 million in state incentives for the VinFast project.
- The VinFast project also received $400 million in local incentives.
- Electric vehicles help to improve fuel economy, lower fuel costs, and reduce emissions.
- Using a battery electric vehicle (BEV) or plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) means energy use does not rely on petroleum consumption but rather electricity.
- Electricity sources for EVs can extend to outputs such as solar energy, coal, nuclear energy, wind energy, and hydropower.
- The manufacturing work from these projects will bring in higher paying and higher skilled jobs to the area.
- Higher paying jobs increase average spending within the community, which can support local businesses and supply additional funding for community events.
- Although the EV projects are locating in Chatham County and Randolph County, surrounding communities like Burlington are likely to experience economic benefits from related manufacturing industries moving close to the automotive plants.
- This will introduce indirect job creation to surrounding areas, producing a much wider economic impact than just the EV projects themselves.
- VinFast does not have an established presence in the United States.
- North Carolina has never had an automobile facility before this project.
- Both the state and VinFast have something to prove and are hoping to demonstrate success.
- Electric vehicles are relatively new within the automotive industry, so the newness of the technology presents risks.
- There is concern with existing supply chain issues exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic and other contributors, which could lead to stutters in the workforce needed and the supply chain at hand.
- The introduction of such a novel industry is high risk but can lead to high reward.
- North Carolina is taking a high-risk, high-reward perspective on VinFast and Toyota after seeing the success of automotive manufacturing in other states and the increasing need for alternative forms of energy consumption.
- The location of these two manufacturing facilities is just south of Burlington.
- Alamance County shares county lines with Chatham County, where VinFast will relocate.
- Alamance County also shares a border with Randolph County, the location of the Toyota plant.
- Projects on the scale of VinFast and Toyota, with new job creation estimates nearing 10,000, will most certainly have significant regional impacts in future supplier site development and the regional workforce.
- There is a significant opportunity for the relocation of not only people who will work for VinFast and Toyota, but also the relocation of suppliers, which can create numerous jobs within Burlington.
- Many comparisons are drawn to the BMW facility in South Carolina.
- BMW pledged to employ 2,000 people within 8 years of its announcement in 1992.
- Thirty years later, BMW employs more than 11,000 people and has invested $12 billion in the facility.
- Many manufacturing operations of suppliers flocked to South Carolina after the establishment of BMW.
- For every one BMW employee, around 2.5 to 3.5 indirect jobs were created by suppliers.
- There was a noticeable increase of international companies to South Carolina following the BMW facility.
- Before the BMW facility was built, there were 34 international companies present in South Carolina.
- Twenty years later, there are more than 130 international companies present in South Carolina.
- Should VinFast and Toyota grow successfully in the next few years, North Carolina could see stark changes in its industrial scenes at the heart of the state.
- An essential aspect of manufacturing work is the presence of accessible education and training.
- Alamance Community College offers programs in fields such as mechatronics, industrial systems, welding, and more.
- Elon University will be a promising asset, especially given its construction of the Innovation Quad to house the Engineering and Physics programs.
- Once VinFast and Toyota begin the hiring process, they will look for people in these educational programs.
- Burlington is a growing, attractive area for workers to reside in with an easy commute to work at those locations.
- Burlington offers quality education, available areas for manufacturing, and a thriving community to encourage workers to want to live in.
- North Carolina has not had a strong presence in the automotive industry until recently but is eager to start onto a road of success.
