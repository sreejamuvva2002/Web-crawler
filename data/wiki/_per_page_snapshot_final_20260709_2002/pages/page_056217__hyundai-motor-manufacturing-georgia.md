# Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai rushes construction on $7.6B EV factory due to IRA incentives](https://www.teslarati.com/hyundai-georgia-plan-ira-ev-tax-incentives/)  ·  `page_056217`

## Facts

- Hyundai is reportedly rushing the construction of its $7.6 billion electric vehicle and battery production facility in Georgia due to EV tax incentives tied to the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- The IRA rewards domestic EV and battery production, prompting foreign automakers to establish roots in North America or any country with a free trade agreement with the United States.
- Hyundai stated that the IRA might be unfair to automakers that import electric vehicles and batteries from outside North America.
- The IRA provides up to $7,500 in EV tax credits to vehicles and batteries made in North America.
- Jose Munoz, Hyundai’s President and Global Chief Operating Officer, shared sales and lease figures after signing a partnership with Georgia Tech.
- The Hyundai group sold or leased the second-most electric vehicles in the United States during the first half of 2023, behind only Tesla.
- Hyundai still receives some benefits from the IRA since it provides tax credits to leased electric vehicles regardless of the country that produced them.
- The South Korean company admits that the IRA is pushing it to set up production lines in North America faster.
- Jose Munoz stated, “What we decided is to double down,” and noted the company is trying to accelerate the project as much as possible.
- Hyundai is confident that the original project date of January 2025 would be pulled ahead, possibly by three months or more.
- Hyundai initially aims to assemble 300,000 electric vehicles annually at the Georgia plant.
- Hyundai has partnered with South Korean battery supplier LG Energy Solution (LGES) to produce cells at the Georgia plant, qualifying its EVs for more tax incentives under the IRA.
- LGES and Hyundai increased their investment in the Georgia plant by $2 billion to increase its battery cell production capacity.
- Jose Munoz stated, “We would like to ensure that the sourcing of the batteries is 100% USA to comply with the IRA.”
- The increased investment will also ensure Hyundai and LG use “the best possible technology” to make batteries.
