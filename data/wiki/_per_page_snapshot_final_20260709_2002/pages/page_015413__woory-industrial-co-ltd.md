# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County - Valdosta CEO](https://valdostaceo.com/news/2023/06/woory-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-laurens-county/)  ·  `page_015413`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd., an automotive parts manufacturer, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Woory Industrial is the latest job creator helping the state become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that the emerging industry continues to bring historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities across the state thanks to a highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed confidence that Woory will have a long and beneficial partnership with Dublin-Laurens County.
- Woory Industrial Company, known as Woory, was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory maintains its traditional product lines while concentrating on developing new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the automobile industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years and that the transition to EVs is the future.
- Jungwoo Kim identified the U.S. EV market as the key market among all automotive markets.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Jungwoo Kim stated that Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- Jungwoo Kim expressed confidence that Woory's experience and technology will contribute to community prosperity and appreciated the partnership of the State of Georgia and the City of Dublin.
- Woory's new facility is located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin.
- The facility will be Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Electric vehicles lack a heating source, so their heaters are required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that fostering quality and sustainable growth is a goal of the City of Dublin.
- Mayor Joshua Kight noted that Woory's commitment to placing an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote infill development and creates new career paths.
- Mayor Joshua Kight welcomed Woory to the Dublin community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, expressed excitement about welcoming Woory to the Dublin-Laurens County community.
- Dr. Fred Williams stated that Dublin and Laurens County have a rapidly growing automotive sector due to their geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled and trained workforce.
- Dr. Fred Williams noted that partnerships with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory's talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects like Woory's first U.S. home in Dublin, Georgia are why the state prioritizes being on the cutting-edge of this transformational manufacturing opportunity to create jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with prepared communities and sites, and congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged partnership and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- The state has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs since 2020.
