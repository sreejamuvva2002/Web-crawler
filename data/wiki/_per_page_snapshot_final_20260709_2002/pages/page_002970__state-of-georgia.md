# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-29

**Source:** [Gov Kemp: Georgia Job Creation Remains Strong | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2024-08-29/gov-kemp-georgia-job-creation-remains-strong)  ·  `page_002970`

## Facts

- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024 (Fiscal Year 2024), GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations.
- These FY2024 expansions and new locations resulted in more than $20.3 billion in investment statewide.
- The FY2024 projects committed 26,900 new private sector jobs across Georgia.
- 83% of the FY2024 expansions and new locations are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Rural Georgia expansions represent more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that his administration is committed to prioritizing growth in rural Georgia and investing in education, workforce development, and infrastructure.
- Metro Atlanta attracted nearly $7.8 billion in investments as a logistics and business hub during FY2024.
- Statewide, new locations account for $12.8 billion in investments, while expansions are responsible for 9,800 new jobs.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted the necessity of workforce pipeline development through the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns noted tax cuts, regulatory relief, and historic investments in education, workforce development, and infrastructure as key drivers of economic success.
- Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland during FY2024.
- These missions focused on strengthening relationships with Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network while promoting Georgia as a premier business destination.
- In the prior year, Governor and First Lady Kemp led similar missions to Israel, France, and Switzerland.
- Georgia landed more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market during FY2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson cited Georgia's strong partnerships and diverse industry landscape as major advantages for businesses.
- The state celebrated over 50 years of continuous business and trade representation in Europe and Japan, which remain top sources of foreign direct investment.
- The coming fiscal year will mark 40 years of continuous representation in Korea, a partnership that earned GDEcD the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- Markets where Georgia maintains business representation accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Kristi Brigman highlighted state achievements across mobility and technology sectors during a decade of excellence.
- Manufacturing, including aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup at its facility in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- StandardAero operates facilities in Richmond County.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer announced a recent expansion in Fayette County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens Counties.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support Qcells’ operations.
- Investments in food processing and agribusiness totaled more than $1.3 billion in FY2024.
- Japanese probiotic beverage company Yakult located in Bartow County to support East Coast operations.
- Korean bakery CJ Foodville located in Hall County to support East Coast operations.
- Walmart will source milk from local dairy farmers at a future dairy processing facility in Lowndes County.
- Technology and corporate hubs are creating at least 4,400 new jobs as a result of FY2024 wins.
- Cargill Corporation is establishing a new office hub in the City of Atlanta.
- U.S. Soccer’s future headquarters and national training center will be located in Fayette County.
- Georgia ranked No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia the No. 1 state for EV investments.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
