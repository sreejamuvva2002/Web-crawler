# Freyr

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Freyr's Georgia battery factory will focus on energy storage](https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1137818_freyr-s-georgia-battery-factory-will-focus-on-energy-storage)  ·  `page_000817`

## Facts

- Norwegian battery firm Freyr confirmed plans for a Georgia factory that will supply batteries for energy storage.
- The factory is dubbed "Giga America."
- The factory will be located on a 368-acre tract in Coweta County on the southwestern edge of the Atlanta metropolitan area.
- Production will be stepped up in phases.
- The first phase targets 34 gigawatt-hours from a $1.7 billion investment.
- Freyr did not confirm when the first phase would be completed.
- A second phase will add more cell production lines.
- The second phase will bring total investment to $2.6 billion by 2029.
- Freyr expects an incentive package from the State of Georgia and Coweta County.
- Freyr expects tax credits under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The company is applying with the U.S. Department of Energy for a federal incentive package.
- The potential federal package could include a grant and/or direct loans.
- The factory will use Boston-based 24M's SemiSolid manufacturing process.
- The SemiSolid process is billed as boosting efficiency in the manufacture of lithium-ion cells.
- Technicians from 24M will collaborate with Freyr's Norway-based staff.
- Freyr considers the U.S. energy storage market to be the world's largest.
- Citing Rystad Energy, Freyr expects combined U.S. and European energy storage demand to reach 1.4 terawatt-hours by 2030.
- This expected demand represents a roughly 34-fold increase from 2022.
- Raw material prices have kept battery prices inflated despite growing interest in battery-based energy storage from companies, utilities, and residences.
- Battery prices have steadily decreased over the past decade.
- A recent raw material price surge is expected to delay further affordability gains until 2024 at the earliest.
- The battery plant is part of a cluster of new manufacturing projects in Georgia.
- Rivian's Georgia plant is scheduled to start vehicle production in 2024.
- Rivian's smaller R2 model is due to debut at the Georgia plant later.
- An upcoming Hyundai/Kia "Metaplant" in Georgia is expected to reach full capacity around 2025.
- The Hyundai/Kia Metaplant will produce up to 500,000 EVs at full capacity.
- SK Innovation's Georgia plants will produce batteries for up to 300,000 EVs annually.
- SK Innovation's Georgia plants will supply batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning.
