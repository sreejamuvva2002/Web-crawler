## Facts

- Volvo Trucks North America has started production of its new regional hauler, the all-new Volvo VNR, at its New River Valley assembly plant in Dublin, Virginia.
- Production began less than a year after Volvo unveiled the new regional-haul VNR.
- The all-new Volvo VNR was unveiled in March 2025, with order books opening six months later.
- The all-new Volvo VNR is 90% new compared to the legacy model.
- Peter Voorhoeve, president of Volvo Trucks North America, stated the VNR is purpose-built for navigating congested city streets, distribution centers, and urban routes where visibility, precision, and agility are critical.
- The first units rolled off the assembly line in February 2026.
- First units will be delivered to Clayton, a national single-family homebuilder.
- The all-new Volvo VNR is assembled at Volvo Trucks’ New River Valley Plant, where all Volvo trucks for the North American market are built.
- The Virginia facility is Volvo’s largest manufacturing site globally and plays a central role in its U.S. operations.
- Volvo recently invested $400 million in major upgrades at the plant.
- Upgrades include a new 350,000-square-foot facility for cab welding along with upgraded paint and material flow systems.

- It incorporates safety innovations such as integrated side curtain airbags.
