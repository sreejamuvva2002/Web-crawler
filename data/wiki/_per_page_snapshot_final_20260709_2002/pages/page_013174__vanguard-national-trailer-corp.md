# Vanguard National Trailer Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-09-03

**Source:** [Top of Georgia | Success Stories](https://topofgeorgia.us/success-stories)  ·  `page_013174`

## Facts

- Vanguard National Trailer Corp. is a leading semi-trailer manufacturer based in Monon, Ind.
- The company invested about $30 million in a new manufacturing facility in Dade County.
- Production started at the Trenton, Ga., plant in August 2016 with 70 employees.
- Once operating at full capacity, the new location is expected to create 400 jobs.
- Vanguard National Trailer Corp. broke ground on a nearly 100-acre site in the Dade County Industrial Park on September 3, 2015.
- The company built a new 356,000 square-foot facility over the course of a year.
- Vanguard manufactures dry freight and refrigerated semi-trailer vans for the North American transportation industry.
- The newly created jobs include both office and manufacturing positions.
- Vanguard National Trailer took advantage of Georgia’s Quick Start program, the nation's No. 1 workforce training program.
- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal welcomed Vanguard to Dade County, stating: 'We are excited to welcome Vanguard National Trailer Corp. to Dade County. Their decision to establish a manufacturing and assembly plant in Georgia is a testament to our economic development assets that are critical in generating job opportunities for Georgians. I am confident that Vanguard Trailer Corp. will benefit from opening a new facility in our state and further prove why Georgia is a top destination for job creators.'
- Georgia Attorney General Chris Carr, then Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated: 'We are excited that Vanguard Trailer Corp. is locating to Georgia and taking advantage of one of the state’s greatest economic development assets in Quick Start. I am confident that Vanguard will be successful for many years to come and will find Georgia to be an excellent place in which to do business.'
- GDEcD Director of Logistics, Energy, Agribusiness & Food Processing Brittany Holtzclaw supported the company on behalf of Georgia in partnership with the Dade County Industrial Development Authority, the Northwest Georgia Joint Development Authority, Georgia Power, and the Georgia Department of Transportation.
