# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-14

**Source:** [Archer Aviation, Stellantis begin construction of air taxi manufacturing plant | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/archer-aviation-118m-covington-georgia/644760/)  ·  `page_016891`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation broke ground on its facility in Covington, Georgia, where it will produce its flagship electric air taxis in partnership with Stellantis.
- The facility will span 350,000-square-feet and produce up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The facility has room to expand and support production of up to 2,300 aircraft annually.
- The air taxis, known as Midnight, are expected to be used by United Airlines in 2025.
- Adam Goldstein is Archer’s co-founder and CEO.
- Goldstein said the Covington facility will be the “industry’s first high volume eVTOL manufacturing facility.”
- Archer and Stellantis selected Covington for its site after a comprehensive survey of potential markets across the U.S.
- The companies considered “the availability and diversity of talent in the local labor market, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs and logistics” when selecting the site.
- Construction will be completed in two phases.
- In the first phase, the facility will span 350,000 square feet and produce 650 aircraft yearly.
- The second phase will build out an additional 500,000 to 800,000 square feet.
- The expanded facility will have the ability to produce up to 2,300 aircraft yearly.
- Goldstein stated that production will be balanced against many other factors to ensure vehicles coming off the line are immediately put into service.
- Stellantis will provide up to $150 million in equity capital subject to Archer’s ability to achieve certain business milestones.
- The capital can be accessed “if and when we need it,” with the goal being to minimize dilution.
- The total state incentive for the Covington project is 33% of the planned investment.
- Archer will invest $118 million over 10 years, according to the press release.
- Archer has built another facility near its headquarters in San Jose, California.
- The San Jose facility will come online in April and will produce the company’s first 6 to 10 planes for testing.
