# Georgia Clean Vehicle Related Tax Credits

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2015-07-01

**Source:** [Clean Vehicle Related Tax Credits | Environmental Protection Division](https://epd.georgia.gov/forms-permits/air-protection-branch-forms-permits/clean-vehicle-related-tax-credits)  ·  `page_028623`

## Facts

- Historic Low Emission/Zero Emission Vehicle (LEV/ZEV) and Medium Duty/Heavy Duty Vehicle (MDV/HDV) Tax Credits in Georgia were discontinued effective July 1, 2015.
- Tax credits for vehicles converted to alternative fuels (Converted Vehicle) and Electric Vehicle Chargers are still available.
- The tax credit allowed for electric vehicle chargers is 10% of the cost of the charger or $2,500, whichever is less.
- The electric vehicle charger tax credit is available only to businesses; individual homes do not qualify.
- A qualified charger must be rated greater than 130 volts and designed to charge on-road vehicles.
- The charger must be installed in Georgia and remain in Georgia for 5 consecutive years.
- The tax credit allowed for converted vehicles is 10% of the cost of conversion, not to exceed $2,500 per converted vehicle.
- The converted vehicle must be fueled solely by an alternative fuel; a vehicle capable of operating on an alternative fuel and gasoline or diesel does not qualify.
- Natural gas (compressed or liquefied) qualifies as an alternative fuel for the converted vehicle tax credit.
- Battery electric qualifies as an alternative fuel, but hybrids operating on gasoline or diesel do not qualify.
- Propane qualifies as an alternative fuel for the converted vehicle tax credit.
- Hydrogen fuel cell qualifies as an alternative fuel for the converted vehicle tax credit.
- The Georgia Environmental Protection Division (EPD) is responsible for certifying electric vehicle chargers and converted vehicles that meet the requirements.
- The Georgia Department of Revenue is responsible for administering the electric vehicle charger and converted vehicle tax credits.
- EPD certification is required to apply for the applicable tax credit.
- Applicants must download and read EPD’s instructions before applying.
- Applicants must download either the Electric Vehicle Charger Certification Form or the Converted Vehicle Certificate Form.
- Applicants must complete the certification form in accordance with EPD’s instructions and submit the signed form and required documentation to EPD electronically or by US mail.
- EPD will review the completed certification form and supporting documents, and if all requirements are met, EPD will sign the form and send it back.
- Patrick Brand leads the Certification Program at Georgia EPD for charger or converted vehicle certification inquiries.
- The Georgia Department of Revenue handles inquiries on how to obtain the tax credit after receiving an approved certification form.
