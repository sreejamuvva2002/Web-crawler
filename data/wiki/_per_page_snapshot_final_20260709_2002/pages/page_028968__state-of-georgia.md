# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-11

**Source:** [Governor and First Lady Kemp Reinforce Economic Partnerships in Korea | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/governor-and-first-lady-kemp-reinforce-economic-partnerships-korea)  ·  `page_028968`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp announced on June 11, 2024 that they are leading a delegation of state officials on an international mission to the Republic of Korea to reinforce strategic economic development partnerships.
- The mission will focus on meetings with existing industries operating or under construction in Georgia, including Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, CJ Foodville, LG Group, SK Group, and Hanwha Qcells.
- The state delegation consists of President Pro Tem John Kennedy, Representative Soo Hong, and Representative Lehman Franklin.
- The delegation includes representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), Georgia Environmental Protection Division, and Georgia Ports Authority.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that meeting with industry leadership at their headquarters provides a unique opportunity to learn about their perspective on industry shifts and position Georgia at the forefront of new opportunities.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985.
- Korea’s Consular Office with jurisdiction across the Southeast, Puerto Rico, and U.S. Virgin Islands has been located in Georgia since 1976.
- Korea has been one of the top sources of Foreign Direct Investment and international job creation in Georgia for the last three years.
- In fiscal year 2023 alone, Korean companies announced more than $10 billion in investments across the state.
- In fiscal year 2023 alone, Korean companies announced the creation of 12,605 jobs across the state.
- Total trade between the State of Georgia and Korea totaled over $15.8 billion in calendar year 2023.
- Trade with Korea accounted for nearly 10 percent of the state’s total trade in calendar year 2023.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: 'The Republic of Korea was the very first country I visited when I became governor for a good reason – because of the incredible partners located there,' and noted that Korean partnerships are bringing historic investments and jobs to communities across the state, calling Georgia the No. 1 state for business.
