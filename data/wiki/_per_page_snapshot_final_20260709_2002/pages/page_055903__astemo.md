# Astemo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Japanese automotive group Astemo investing £100m and creating 200 jobs in Greater Manchester | Business Live](https://www.business-live.co.uk/manufacturing/japanese-automotive-group-astemo-investing-32065797?int_source=nba)  ·  `page_055903`

## Facts

- Japanese automotive group Astemo is investing £100m in its local factory in Horwich, Greater Manchester.
- The investment will create a new production line to manufacture inverters for electric vehicles following a “significant” contract win.
- Astemo will be the only company in the UK with that specific EV inverter production capability.
- The project is expected to create around 220 new high-skilled manufacturing jobs.
- Greater Manchester mayor Andy Burnham and Manchester City Council leader Bev Craig visited Astemo’s Tokyo base last month during a trade mission to Japan.
- During the visit, they met with Astemo executive officer Mathieu Devillard.
- Andy Burnham stated the investment puts Greater Manchester at the forefront of the electric vehicle revolution and creates high-quality, long-term jobs in advanced manufacturing.
- Burnham noted that international engagement has made Greater Manchester the number one UK destination for foreign direct investment outside London.
- Bolton Council leader Coun Nick Peel said the project will create hundreds of jobs in Horwich and ensure a new production line to manufacture inverters for electric vehicles.
- Business and Trade Secretary Jonathan Reynolds stated Astemo is investing over £100 million into its UK site, creating and safeguarding more than 200 jobs.
- Reynolds emphasized the government is working hand in hand with investors to build a globally competitive zero-emission vehicle supply chain in the UK.
- Joe Manning, managing director at inward investment agency MIDAS, confirmed over £100m in confirmed investment supports 220 jobs and noted MIDAS has worked closely with Astemo for several years.
- Ahead of the Japan trade mission, Japanese vinegar manufacturer Mizkan announced it was investing £17m in its Middleton base.
- Mizkan will be the headline sponsor for the 50th annual Japan Week cultural festival in Manchester this September.
- Cllr Bev Craig stated Greater Manchester's economy is worth approximately £80 billion annually and provides a foundation for Japanese companies to establish and expand UK operations.
