# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Daechang Seat Corp. USA to Create 500 Jobs in Chatham County - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county/)  ·  `page_018888`

## Facts

- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The new facility will be located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The investment will create over 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that another HMGMA supplier coming to Georgia highlights the impact projects like the Hyundai Metaplant have beyond their initial investment.
- To date, suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project reach at least nine counties.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed excitement about adding Daechang Seat Corp. USA to Georgia's growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people in seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee is the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- Jinsuk Lee expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah for DSC's role in creating improved opportunities and employment prospects within Chatham County and the Savannah area.
- Jinsuk Lee stated DSC's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform DSC into a global entity.
- DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- DSC hopes to collaborate with even more projects in the future.
- DSC’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will be hiring for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- Hiring will occur through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- Griff Lynch is the Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO.
- Griff Lynch noted that DSC is only minutes from the Port of Savannah, ideally located to take advantage of Georgia’s world-class connectivity.
- The Georgia Ports Authority aims to provide the best global gateway for U.S. supply chains and welcomed DSC as a job creator in Georgia’s automaking sector.
- Trip Tollison is the Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO.
- Trip Tollison announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will be the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The project brings 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment to Chatham County.
- Trip Tollison described Daechang Seat Corp. USA as a well-established global company and expressed anticipation for their growth and community integration.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- Alyce Thornhill represented GDEcD on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson is the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Pat Wilson stated that countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry and welcomed DSC in Savannah.
- Pat Wilson highlighted Savannah as an established global gateway and noted Chatham County has leveraged that advantage to create a bustling community.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain.
- Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DSC has recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
- DSC anticipates greater opportunities for growth with the completion of new manufacturing facilities, providing larger installation spaces.
