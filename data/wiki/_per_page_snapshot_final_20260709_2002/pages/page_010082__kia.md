# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04

**Source:** [Kia Confirms First Midsize Pickup For America | Carscoops](https://www.carscoops.com/2025/04/kia-working-on-a-midsize-pickup-for-the-us-will-be-different-from-the-tasman/)  ·  `page_010082`

## Facts

- Kia confirms it is developing a separate midsize pickup truck for the US market.
- The new truck will target the midsize pickup segment.
- There are rumors that the new truck could be an electric vehicle (EV).
- More details about the new truck will be announced at Kia’s Investor Day on April 9.
- Kia CEO Ho Sung Song confirmed the company is planning to enter the North American midsize pickup segment with a new model.
- The new model will be entirely separate from the Tasman, which is slated for other global markets.
- Kia CEO Ho Sung Song shared the update on Thursday during the ongoing Seoul Mobility Show in Korea.
- Ho Sung Song stated: “We’re going to have more details next week about the pickup for the U.S. But in general terms … we are studying now.”
- A formal announcement is expected during Kia’s Investor Day, scheduled for April 9.
- Kia introduced a rugged “Weekender” version of the Tasman at the Seoul Mobility Show.
- Ho Sung Song told Autonews: “We don’t develop that vehicle [the Tasman] for the American market.”
- Ho Sung Song added: “But in the meantime, we are now seriously studying how to get into the pickup truck market in the United States through a different model that is not the Tasman.”
- No details were offered on what kind of powertrain the future US-bound pickup might use.
- Sources have previously suggested the future pickup could be an electric model.
- Industry rumors point to a debut window for the new pickup sometime between 2026 and 2029.
- The new pickup may potentially launch alongside a Hyundai-branded sibling.
- Both Kia and Hyundai models are expected to be assembled in Georgia at Hyundai’s Metaplant America facility.
- Building the trucks in Georgia is intended to sidestep ongoing trade tensions and avoid the 25 percent tariff on imported trucks known as the Chicken Tax.
- Another option reportedly still in play is a deal with GM that would let Kia and Hyundai offer their own internal combustion versions of the Chevrolet Colorado and GMC Canyon.
- Both strategies (building in Georgia vs. partnering with GM) could be on the table.
- The current US midsize pickup scene is dominated by internal combustion vehicles like the Toyota Tacoma, Chevy Colorado, GMC Canyon, Nissan Frontier, Ford Ranger, and Jeep Gladiator.
- Fully electric trucks like the Ford F-150 Lightning, Rivian R1T, and Tesla Cybertruck tend to sit in a larger category, leaving a potential opening for Kia to introduce a more compact, EV-friendly alternative.
- The Tasman will launch in markets like South Korea, Australia, Africa, and the Middle East with traditional gas and diesel powertrains.
- It is unclear whether Kia’s US-bound pickup will borrow design elements or utility features from the Tasman.
- Kia is gearing up to expand its electric light commercial vehicle lineup, starting with the PV5 van.
- The PV7 and PV9 are set to follow the PV5.
- CEO Ho Sung Song framed Kia’s expansion into new segments, including LCVs and pickups, as “an opportunity” for the brand, “not a risk.”
