# Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-21

**Source:** [News – Page 3 – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/news/page/3/)  ·  `page_029869`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Sewon America, a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co., will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- The Savannah JDA was recognized as Business Facilities magazine’s Bronze Deal of the Year Award recipient on Jan. 12, 2023, for the Hyundai Motor Group announcement.
- Micron Technologies received the Gold award and Chevron Phillips Chemical received the Silver award in the same category.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Seoyon E-HWA, a global auto parts supplier that manufactures both interior and exterior components, will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA will invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- Hyundai Motor America kicked off the Progress Project for the second year in honor of Black History Month.
- The Progress Project by Hyundai provides financial assistance to black-owned small businesses in a region, with this year's focus on Savannah, Georgia.
- Savannah is home to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, a new dedicated EV and battery manufacturing plant currently being built in Bryan County.
- The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (Savannah JDA) announced the appointment of Maria Whitfield, a veteran human resources executive, to the position of Director of Workforce.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Ecoplastic Corporation, an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 456 new jobs.
- Ecoplastic Corporation will invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The Savannah JDA tapped Wadley Donovan Gutshaw Consulting, LLC (WDGC) to conduct a Regional Workforce Study and Comprehensive Strategic Plan for the Savannah region.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis, one of the world’s largest automotive suppliers, plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The Hyundai Mobis plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- Plans for the Joon Georgia facility mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, LLC is working alongside the Georgia Department of Labor to secure its workforce.
- Some 8,000 plus job openings will be open over the next several years at the Hyundai plant.
- More than a dozen positions are available now at the Hyundai plant, including Global Trade Manager, Procurement Management Assistant Manager, and Treasury Manager.
