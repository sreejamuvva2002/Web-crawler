# Hyundai EV Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-21

**Source:** [Not just an ugly industrial site: Hyundai officials unveil site design for EV factory - AOL](https://www.aol.com/sports/not-just-ugly-industrial-hyundai-091555289.html)  ·  `page_021182`

## Facts

- Hyundai is constructing an EV manufacturing facility on a Bryan County megasite along Interstate 16.
- Bomee Oh, senior associate for the design division of Haeahn Architecture, presented details on Hyundai's sustainability commitment at a Savannah Joint Development Authority meeting.
- The plant design features solar panels covering the employee parking area to honor environmental sustainability.
- The site will include displayed artwork, walking trails, recreation fields, a pond, and a greenspace larger than Forsyth Park.
- Hyundai officials declined to provide artist renderings of the designs for public view at the time of the meeting.
- Commissioner Chairman Carter Infinger stated excitement for the public to see the design, noting it addresses fears about an industrial appearance.
- Suppliers relocating to the region are bringing more than 5,000 jobs and have invested $2.093 billion.
- There are currently 10 suppliers in the region with more expected to arrive.
- In April, Gulfstream announced plans to add 1,600 jobs and invest $150 million in capital investment to expand its facilities.
- Maria Whitfield, director of workforce for the Savannah JDA, noted that some organizations are discussing enhanced incentives like quarterly bonuses, onsite childcare, and large referral bonuses to attract talent.
- Whitfield is collaborating with regional workforce development professionals to create action plans preparing the local labor force for manufacturing roles.
- The Savannah JDA is working with local youth to break manufacturing career stigmas and conducting industry tours.
- Workforce development professionals are helping identify candidates who may not pursue traditional university pathways but want to enter the local hiring market directly.
- The Savannah JDA hired Wadley Donovan Gutshaw Consulting, LLC to conduct a workforce study and draft a comprehensive plan to address regional labor supply challenges.
- An electronic survey was distributed to regional companies, and roundtable interviews were held with staffing agencies, the military, transportation, and higher education sectors.
- Trent Thompson, vice president of infrastructure for Savannah-based engineering firm Thomas and Hutton, stated the new water and sewer system will transform regional infrastructure beyond just the megasite.
- The water and sewer infrastructure project costs $343 million and will support new developments along the I-16 corridor from New Hampstead to Bulloch County.
- Blue pipes visible along I-16 are 18-inch force mains forming part of the phase two sewer connection between the megasite and Savannah.
- The entire water and sewer system is scheduled for completion in 2025.
- A wastewater treatment plant across I-16 from the megasite is nearing completion and features a membrane bioreactor technology.
- Thompson described the effluent from the membrane bioreactor as very high quality and reusable for applications like irrigating the EV factory's greenspaces.
- The plan includes sending reclaimed water back to Hyundai to irrigate its park area and landscape across the entire site.
