# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis fires up $850 million Richmond plant](https://theaugustapress.com/aurubis-fires-up-850-million-richmond-plant/)  ·  `page_017278`

## Facts

- Aurubis Richmond poured its first melt this week, marking the launch of full-scale operations at the company’s $850 million Augusta facility.
- The milestone positions the plant to chip away at America’s chronic copper shortage while supplying other critical metals.
- David Schultheis, Aurubis president and managing director, stated that the U.S. currently imports about 1 million tons of copper each year.
- The facility expects to fill about 10% of that copper import gap.
- Aurubis Richmond now employs more than 200 workers.
- The company expects to reach 240 employees by mid-2026.
- Construction currently draws about 500 people to the site daily, in addition to jobs tied to suppliers and contractors.
- The company has logged 2 million man-hours during construction.
- Construction activities included installing 3,500 metric tons of steel.
- Construction activities also included laying 25 miles of pipe.
- Phase 2, approved in late 2022, will double intake capacity to more than 180,000 tons of recycling material.
- Phase 2’s first melt is set for February 2026 and will add the “metallurgical part,” specifically the furnaces.
- Aurubis’ multi-metal production includes copper, nickel, tin, and zinc.
- These metals are vital to data centers, electrification, energy production, and electric vehicles.
- Employee Kristian Hughes of Beech Island expressed pleasure with the mission of processing copper and various other metals at a better rate.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag called the plant “an industrial powerhouse” producing essential metals.
- Haag stated that the U.S. can now reassert ownership of essential metals like copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals, which are the backbone of advanced manufacturing.
- Without these metals, the U.S. would lose global competition, security, and competitiveness, according to Haag.
- Advisory board Chairman Prof. Dr. Fritz Vahrenholt said the company once considered expanding into China but decided against it due to mismatched interests.
- Vahrenholt cited America’s advantages as secure, competitively priced energy and fewer bureaucratic hurdles compared to Germany.
- He noted Germany “punishes us with high CO2 taxes” and made the mistake of phasing out nuclear energy.
- The company is delighted with President Trump’s plans to quadruple U.S. nuclear capacity by 2050 and his opposition to carbon dioxide taxes.
- Congressman Rick Allen praised the project, linking it to winning the economic war with China by matching and beating China in the race to energy.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp sent a video message thanking Aurubis and local officials for welcoming the project.
- German Ambassador to the U.S. Jens Hanefeld noted that Aurubis Richmond complements Germany’s two existing copper smelters, compared with China’s 65.
- Hanefeld’s trip to Augusta was his first outside Washington, D.C., since his appointment.
- Hanefeld stated the company is delighted to be part of creating joint resilience and views it as an investment in a common security partnership.
