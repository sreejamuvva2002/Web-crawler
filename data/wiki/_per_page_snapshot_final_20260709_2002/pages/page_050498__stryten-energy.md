# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-17

**Source:** [24GWh！Stryten Energy Expands US Capacity - EnergyTrend](https://www.energytrend.com/news/20250417-49269.html)  ·  `page_050498`

## Facts

- U.S.-based Stryten Energy announced the expansion of its battery production capacity to 24GWh across the U.S.
- The strategic layout covers key areas such as military and grid energy storage.
- Stryten Energy intends to comprehensively upgrade the production capacity of its 11 battery assembly plants located in Arkansas, Georgia, and other places.
- The company will lay out multi-technology routes including lead-acid, lithium-ion, and all-vanadium liquid current batteries.
- The technology routes aim to satisfy the differentiated needs of the military in terms of high-reliability and long-duration energy storage on the grid.
- Stryten Energy is investing in recycled plastic technology for used batteries and using recycled materials for new battery production.
- A new lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia has gone into production.
- The new Georgia plant utilizes patented lithium module technology to focus on the military and industrial battery market.
- Stryten Energy's capacity expansion plans align with U.S. government policy supporting local supply chains and clean energy.
- Ravi Manghani, Senior Director of Strategic Sourcing at Anza Renewables, stated that local sourcing is still the least risky option for the U.S. energy storage industry at this stage due to multiple challenges.
- There has been an acceleration in local energy storage manufacturing, with some manufacturers focusing on energy storage while traditional electric vehicle market companies shift to energy storage as EV growth rates fall short of expectations.
- Growing demand for energy storage and favorable market conditions are causing battery manufacturers to aggressively expand their customer base, directly contributing to planning announcements for more local manufacturing facilities.
- Through technology partnerships and vertical integration, Stryten Energy aims to gradually eliminate its dependence on key materials to support the autonomous development of the U.S. energy storage industry.
