# Battery Resourcers (now Ascend Elements)

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-recycling|Battery Recycling]]  ·  **Location:** Covington, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Battery Resourcers, a vertically integrated lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered-materials company (renamed Ascend Elements in January 2022), announced plans to open a 154,000-square-foot commercial-scale recycling facility in Covington, GA. When fully operational in August 2022 the plant will be North America’s largest with capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of discarded lithium-ion batteries and scrap per year, returning battery-grade lithium, cobalt and nickel back into the EV supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The facility will recycle used lithium-ion batteries and return critical materials (lithium, cobalt, nickel) to battery manufacturers, supporting the growing electric-vehicle market.

## Key facts

- Facility size is 154,000 square feet.
- Processing capacity of 30,000 metric tons per year of discarded lithium-ion batteries and scrap.
- Returns battery-grade lithium, cobalt and nickel back into the EV supply chain.
- $43 million investment allocated to build the Covington plant.
- At least 150 jobs will be created for the local community.
- Location is strategically near several EV manufacturing hubs and lithium-ion gigafactories.
- Battery Resourcers claims its recycling process starts with a mixed stream of used batteries and ends with finished cathode active materials.
- Hydro-to-Cathode™ process is reported to be 93% cleaner and 59% lower cost than traditional mining and production.
- Study in the journal Joule found recycled cathode from Hydro-to-Cathode outperforms new cathode material by up to 53% in cycle life.
- Company is based in Worcester, Massachusetts and operates the world’s most efficient lithium-ion battery recycling process.
- CEO Michael O’Kronley emphasized providing a sustainable solution that minimizes mining needs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp called the project the latest job creator attracted by Georgia’s leadership in EV manufacturing.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted Georgia’s educated workforce, pro-business climate, high quality of life, and commitment to sustainability as reasons for choosing the state.
