# Trenton Pressing

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing completed a $12 million expansion in Dade County, Georgia.
- The expansion added 100,000 square feet of manufacturing and assembly operations to the company's new Plant 10.
- A ribbon-cutting ceremony for the completed facility was attended by over 200 people.
- Trenton Pressing is a Tier-One automotive supplier.
- The company provides metal stamping, welding processes, complex assemblies, turnkey contract manufacturing solutions, and warehousing and shipping services.
- Premium segment clients include BMW, Club Car, and ZF.
- Upon completion of the expansion, Trenton Pressing employs over 200 people in Dade County.
- Hans Pfunder serves as chief operating officer.
- Hans Pfunder stated that the facility allows the company to put even more products in the market thanks to the local talent force in Dade County, Georgia.
- CEO Mike Pfunder expressed his intention to stay in America.
- Mike Pfunder indicated expectations for future growth, noting the facility is named Plant 10 because they expect to someday open Plants 9, 8, 7, and so on.
- Georgia's automotive manufacturing sector boasts 164,000 production workers according to SelectGeorgia.
- Georgia has 194 auto-related facilities across the state according to SelectGeorgia.
- Evan Stone serves as executive director of economic development in Dade County.
- Evan Stone stated that retaining and growing the existing industrial base is a high priority for local economic development and that more jobs create opportunities for local talent to make a good wage.
- Ted Rumley serves as Dade County executive.
- Ted Rumley said the expansion proves Dade County remains a good investment due to its skilled workforce.
- Alex Case serves as mayor of Trenton.
- Alex Case praised the company's $12 million investment in a new facility and new jobs as demonstrating confidence in the local economy and workforce.
- Jeff Mullis serves as president and CEO of the Northwest Georgia Joint Development Agency.
- Jeff Mullis described the investment as proof that Northwest Georgia is a premier place to develop and grow industry, especially in the automotive supply sector.
- State Representative Mike Cameron praised Georgia's business-friendly climate and investment in workforce training.
- Mike Cameron noted that the Southeast United States has become a magnet for automotive brands and suppliers over the last two decades with Georgia leading by example.
