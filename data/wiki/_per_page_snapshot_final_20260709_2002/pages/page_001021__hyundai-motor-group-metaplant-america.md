# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai’s New Hydrogen Fuel Trucks Energize Georgia Facility](https://truckdrivernews.com/hyundais-new-hydrogen-fuel-trucks-energize-georgia-facility/)  ·  `page_001021`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) has started using hydrogen-powered trucks at its electric vehicle (EV) facility in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The Hyundai XCIENT fuel-cell trucks will transport parts from suppliers to the plant.
- This deployment is part of Hyundai’s efforts to create a more sustainable supply chain and cut carbon emissions.
- HMGMA is starting with 21 XCIENT trucks for daily deliveries of parts.
- At first, the trucks will move parts to a nearby consolidation center.
- Later, they will work with a larger network of suppliers.
- These trucks make up about one-third of the truck fleet managed by Glovis America at HMGMA.
- Oscar Kwon serves as the CEO of HMGMA.
- Oscar Kwon stated: “Our Hyundai XCIENT hydrogen fuel-cell trucks offer a cleaner alternative to traditional freight methods and support our goal of zero emissions in our operations.”
- This project is part of a larger plan to use hydrogen in logistics.
- Hyundai introduced its HTWO brand earlier this year to focus on hydrogen production, storage, and transportation.
- HTWO stands for “Hydrogen” and “Humanity” and reflects Hyundai’s goal to make hydrogen energy a key part of its business.
- HMGMA will set up a mobile hydrogen refueling station at the Georgia facility.
- The company plans to build a permanent station for hydrogen production and refueling in the future.
- Earlier this year, Hyundai shared its vision for hydrogen energy at the CES 2024 event in Las Vegas.
- At CES 2024, the company highlighted how hydrogen can reduce greenhouse gas emissions and improve the way energy is stored and used.
- Hyundai has committed to being carbon neutral by 2050.
- Hyundai plans to use 100% renewable energy at all overseas factories by 2045.
- By 2035, Hyundai aims to use 3 million tonnes of hydrogen per year across its operations.
- The company believes hydrogen is an essential tool for creating a cleaner future.
- The hydrogen trucks in Georgia are part of Hyundai’s broader U.S. efforts, which include the NorCAL ZERO initiative in California that uses hydrogen trucks to help reduce emissions at ports.
- In Georgia, Hyundai’s hydrogen trucks will make the logistics process at the EV plant cleaner and more efficient.
- These efforts also create jobs and promote the use of clean technology in the region.
