# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced Thursday that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On chose a location in Bartow County for a new facility to help supply automotive manufacturers' plants across the country.
- Officials say the new facility will be one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia history.
- The project will invest approximately $4 to $5 billion into Bartow County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia's ever-growing automotive industry.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted his administration has focused on bringing jobs and opportunity to overlooked communities and is proud the companies are investing further in the No. 1 state for business.
- Officials estimate the facility will create more than 3,500 new jobs.
- The facility will be located at Bartow Centre, an industrial site on Highway 411.
- The company facility is expected to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision and thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property.
- Steve Taylor stated the project will have regional benefit for job seekers and establishes Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem in a significant way.
- SK On is the lithium-ion battery branch of SK Innovations.
- SK On currently employs over 2,000 people at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The construction of the plant has faced controversy, with the FOX 5 I-Team investigating allegations of worker safety issues at the plant's construction site.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced earlier this year plans to invest over $5.5 billion into an electric vehicle manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- The Bryan County investment led to multiple parts suppliers announcing new facilities to supply that plant.
