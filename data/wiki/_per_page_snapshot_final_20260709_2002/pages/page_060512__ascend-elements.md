# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [Announces major order from Ascend Elements USA](https://www.socialpost.news/announces-major-order-from-ascend-elements-usa/)  ·  `page_060512`

## Facts

- American company Ascend Elements has secured a major order worth USD 5 billion in the US to provide cathode precursor materials (pCAM) for battery manufacturing.
- Beginning in Q4 2024, Ascend Elements will initially ship $1 billion worth of pCAM under the contract.
- The client for the multi-year contract was not named by the company.
- After the first delivery, the customer has an option to renew and expand the contract worth $5 billion.
- Ascend Elements is building a factory in Kentucky to manufacture standard battery products.
- The Kentucky facility is expected to produce enough cathode material (CAM) and precursors for up to 750,000 electric vehicle batteries per year after the expected end of 2023.
- This annual production capacity is three times more than what the company had previously reported.
- The company had previously only talked about producing CAM and pCAMs for 250,000 electric vehicles.
- Groundbreaking for the Kentucky facility was laid in October 2022.
- The US Department of Energy awarded Ascend Elements two grants totaling $480 million to accelerate the Kentucky facility.
- Mike O’Cronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated that almost 100 percent of the world’s pCAM is produced in Asia and emphasized the need to make critical battery products in North America to secure the supply chain, reduce carbon emissions, and ensure energy independence.
- In March 2023, the company opened its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Georgia, USA.
- The Georgia facility, named Base 1, is described by Ascend Elements as North America’s largest recycling facility for electric vehicle batteries.
- The Georgia facility processes 30,000 tons of batteries every year.
- The annual processing capacity of the Georgia facility corresponds to about 70,000 electric vehicle batteries.
