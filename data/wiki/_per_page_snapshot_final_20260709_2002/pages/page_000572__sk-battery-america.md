# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-06

**Source:** [SK On subsidiary exceeds hiring goal for Georgia battery plants | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/sk-on-subsidiary-exceeds-hiring-goal-for-georgia-battery-plants/641999/)  ·  `page_000572`

## Facts

- SK Battery America will expand its Georgia workforce by several hundred more employees to meet rising demand.
- The company exceeded its goal of hiring 2,600 workers for two assembly plants two years ahead of schedule, according to an announcement from the Georgia governor’s office.
- SK Battery America has spent $2.6 billion on two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County, Georgia since 2019.
- The two facilities started mass production in early 2022.
- The facilities supply batteries to specific EVs built by Ford and Volkswagen.
- Vacant roles at the plants span technical jobs ranging from production operators to senior engineers focused on manufacturing highly sophisticated lithium-ion battery cells at scale, according to spokesperson Stella Kim.
- SK Battery America announced its first project in Georgia in 2018.
- The company partnered with Georgia Quick Start, a job training program through the state’s technical college system, to train qualified candidates.
- SK Battery America CEO Timothy Jeong stated that the employment goal was met ahead of schedule largely due to the partnership with the State of Georgia.
- Timothy Jeong noted that the state’s Quick State program helped attract and train workers with a speed and scale difficult for any company to achieve independently.
- Timothy Jeong claimed that more than 300,000 new electric vehicles a year will be on American roads because of the work being done at the Georgia facilities.
- In July, SK Battery America partnered with the Georgia National Guard’s Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans and other service members.
- SK Battery America collaborated with The Manufacturing Institute, Ford Stewart Heroes MAKE America Logistics Program, and the Georgia Dept. of Labor to recruit workers.
- Starting wages at the plants are $20 per hour.
- Employment benefits include various employment insurance benefits and subsidized meals for all shifts.
- Georgia’s manufacturing industry workforce dropped at the outset of the Covid-19 pandemic but climbed steadily over the following two years, rising from approximately 387,000 workers in January 2021 to 410,000 in November 2022, according to Bureau of Labor Statistics data.
- SK Battery America spokesperson Stella Kim stated that creative hiring approaches were necessary during the pandemic, including Zoom interviews and drive-through job fairs.
