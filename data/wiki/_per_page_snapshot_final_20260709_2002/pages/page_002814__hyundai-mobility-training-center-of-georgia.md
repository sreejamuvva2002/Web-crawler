# Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-05

**Source:** [New Mobility Training Center Showcases the Power of Partnership | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/new-mobility-training-center-showcases-power-partnership)  ·  `page_002814`

## Facts

- The 89,000-square-foot Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia recently opened in Ellabell.
- The center will train up to 824 individuals at a time.
- The training center supports the creation of approximately 8,500 jobs at the adjacent Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) campus by 2031.
- Governor Brian Kemp emphasized the state’s commitment to supporting the “generational project” and its talent pipeline at the Center’s Grand Opening.
- Georgia’s proven track record of workforce solutions attracted Hyundai Motor Group to the state.
- Kia Georgia’s training center has been delivering results for over a decade, providing context for the new partnership model.
- President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company José Muñoz described the partnership as creating access to world-class training that will help Georgia lead in advanced manufacturing.
- The facility is designed and operated by Georgia Quick Start, the state’s workforce development program within the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Georgia Quick Start collaborated directly with Hyundai to simulate real-world production environments, develop custom training lines, and create hands-on labs mirroring next-generation manufacturing processes.
- The center trains future "Meta Pros," Hyundai’s new generation of EV manufacturing professionals, through specialized environments.
- The STEP Line (Safe Transition for Efficient Production) is an introductory line where trainees build timing, awareness, and coordination skills in a controlled, digital environment.
- The IMPACT Line (Immersive Manufacturing Process and Conveyor Training) features a full-length, moving production line with 53 integrated assembly activities and a built-in quality check system replicating real-world EV production.
- The High Voltage Battery and Safety Lab provides specialized hands-on learning to prepare workers to safely handle advanced EV and hybrid vehicle technologies.
- The VR Experience Lab offers a simulated environment for safety and hazard identification.
- The Automation & Mechatronics Lab focuses on programmable logic controllers (PLCs), sensors, motion systems, and industrial automation.
- Additional training spaces include a 5,760-square-foot multipurpose “Momentum Room,” six flexible classrooms, and robotics, welding, and paint labs.
- Training programs are designed to adapt as vehicle technology evolves, ensuring companies in Georgia remain at the forefront of innovation.
- Scott McMurray, Deputy Commissioner of Georgia Quick Start, noted that subject-matter experts partnered directly with HMGMA teams to understand exact skills needed for Meta Pros and built matching training environments.
- Tony Heo, President and CEO of HMGMA, stated: “This training Center gives us confidence that we’ll have the trained talent needed to shape the future of mobility and American manufacturing – right here in Southeast Georgia.”
- The center strengthens Georgia’s position as an advanced manufacturing hub by combining workforce development, technical education, and strategic business partnerships.
