# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Invest Additional $2B in Battery Manufacturing in Georgia Bryan County - Batteries News](https://batteriesnews.com/hyundai-motor-group-lg-energy-solution-invest-additional-2b-in-battery-manufacturing-in-georgia-bryan-county/)  ·  `page_056210`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will invest an additional $2 billion in battery cell manufacturing in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The additional investment raises the joint venture's total investment value to more than $4.3 billion.
- This expansion will create another 400 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history, landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai’s Metaplant, and welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the goal is to make Georgia the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp noted that major investments provide paychecks, improved schools, and infrastructure, and thanked Hyundai Motor Group and LGES for recognizing Georgia as the No. 1 state for business.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated the incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- Muñoz stated Hyundai is proud to partner with LGES and is grateful for the support of Governor Kemp and Georgia communities while working to be a global leader in the electrified mobility industry.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) onsite battery cell manufacturing joint venture.
- The EV manufacturing facility and the battery JV represent an estimated $7.59 billion in total investment and will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of LG Energy Solution's Advanced Automotive Battery Division, stated the investment underscores a dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through quality job creation.
- Kim thanked the State of Georgia for its support and expressed excitement to bring top-quality products and operational experience to grow with local communities.
- The 30 GWh facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant and supply them to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, stated the announcement will bring 400 additional well-paying jobs to the Savannah region.
- Infinger noted that continued investment by Hyundai Motor Group and announced suppliers like LGES will be transformative for the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA), Georgia Power, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States.
- Wilson stated that through building an ecosystem supporting the entire EV supply chain, Georgia continues to add to industry-leading success and bring future jobs to Georgians statewide.
- Wilson credited state and community partners like the Savannah-area JDA, utility providers, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start for creating a collaborative, business-friendly environment in Georgia.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted more than $25 billion in investments and created over 30,000 jobs along the entire EV supply chain.
- Battery-related projects have accounted for at least $11 billion of Georgia's total investment.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Hyundai Motor Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide and its mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- The announcement was made in Atlanta on August 31, 2023.
