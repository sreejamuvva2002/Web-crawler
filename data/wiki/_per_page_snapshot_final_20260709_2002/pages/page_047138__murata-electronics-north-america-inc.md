# Murata Electronics North America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** ['Passive' but not inert - EE Times](https://www.eetimes.com/passive-but-not-inert/)  ·  `page_047138`

## Facts

- Top passive component suppliers are investing in new-product development and information technology to automate communications with their supply chain partners despite weak demand, continued price pressure, pockets of excess inventory, and increased competition from China.
- Passives makers implemented cost-reduction initiatives including plant closings, factory consolidation, manufacturing shifts to low-cost-labor regions in China and Eastern Europe, layoffs, and reduced capital spending.
- Glyndwr Smith, assistant to the chief executive and senior vice president at Vishay Intertechnology Inc., Malvern, Pa., stated cost reduction involves examining purchased materials through to component insertion, working with customers on logistics, vendor-managed inventory, global support, and introducing new technologies.
- Inventory levels remain higher than needed for current consumption; some suppliers claim they bottomed out while others cite spot ordering trends and poor EMS channel visibility forcing excess stock.
- Sandy Beck, vice president of sales and marketing at Kemet Corp., Greenville, S.C., noted inventory levels are not as high as before but excess remains.
- A hand-to-mouth environment with four to eight week lead times makes recovery forecasting difficult for OEMs who have no need to queue up before placing production orders.
- Delivery times for specialty products range from about eight to 10 weeks.
- Capacitor and resistor prices have dropped steadily over the past 12 months, with some tags approaching manufacturing costs.
- Pockets of growth exist in automotive, game consoles, cell phones, and laptop computers end markets, while telecommunications and Internet-related markets lag recovery by several months.
- AVX Corp., Myrtle Beach, S.C., posted net sales of $294.9 million and net income of $1.3 million for its fiscal first quarter ended June 30; unit volume sales exceeded expectations as price pressure slowed with increased production capacity utilization.
- Kemet reported $124 million in net sales (a 5% sequential increase) and $3.4 million in net earnings for the June quarter; unit shipments increased for three consecutive quarters while average selling prices declined approximately 5%.
- Kemet plans to close plants in Greenwood, S.C., and Matamoros, Mexico, and lowered raw materials inventories by $11 million and finished goods stores by $7.9 million during the quarter.
- Kemet reduced its workforce from a high of about 16,000 to 6,500 over the past two years.
- Vishay expected aggregate unit shipments for its passives, semiconductors, and optoelectronic devices to rise 3% to 5% compared with the March quarter.
- Vishay will drop capital expenditures, including R&D, to approximately $100 million from $160 million last year.
- Taiwan-based Yageo Corp. is expected to cut its capital budget to just $30 million, down from $160 million in 2001.
- iSuppli Corp., El Segundo, Calif., reported about 30% of the EMS industry's total production is manufactured in low-cost-labor centers including China, Asia, Eastern Europe, and Mexico; top-tier players aim to migrate more than 70% of total production to those regions by 2004.
- The manufacturing shift to low-cost regions has caused declining spending in North America and Europe, leaving the United States as a center for design and new-product development.
- Chinese passives makers are targeting U.S., Europe, and Japan markets; expected to achieve $1.6 billion in resistor and capacitor sales in 2003, capturing an 8.9% share of the global market per iSuppli.
- Key Chinese players to watch include Chengdu, Fenghua, Man Yue, Nanyang, and Shenzhen, according to iSuppli analyst Shawn A. Wood.
- Global suppliers retain a competitive edge because China's passives vendors lack advanced products, manufacturing tools, global support services, and brand recognition, though this may change as China gains logistical advantages for local OEMs and EMS providers.
- John Denslinger, senior vice president of marketing at Murata Electronics North America Inc., Smyrna, Ga., stated the company is well-equipped to handle expansion in China with a global structure for sales, marketing, and supply chain to navigate customer moves.
- Rohm Co. Ltd. shifted manufacturing to China and is making investments to increase production capacity.
- AVX is entrenched in China via sales offices, warehouses, and parent company Kyocera Corp., Kyoto, Japan; it is establishing a facility in China that will come online in the third quarter to support multinational companies moving to the region.
- Willie King, division vice president of product marketing at AVX, stated operations are moving to low-cost-labor centers while actively supporting design efforts in North America and Europe.
- Kemet has no manufacturing presence in China but maintains distribution hubs in Asia including China; it expects a future Asian manufacturing presence to support existing customers moving to China and engage local customers.
- Epcos A.G., Munich, Germany, is moving production to low-cost areas in both China and Eastern Europe, including Hungary and the Czech Republic, per Denny Salmang, president and chief executive of Epcos Inc., Iselin, N.J.
- Leading passive component manufacturers share a strategy of developing new technology to bolster earnings even in down markets.
- KOA Speer, Vishay, and Yageo utilize acquisition strategies; Vishay acquired Tansitor and Mallory last year to build its passives portfolio.
- Vishay plans to roll out 200 new products this year, of which 60 will be passive devices.
- Yageo acquired two passive component divisions from Royal Philips Electronics N.V. in 2000, marketed under Ferroxcube and Phycomp; about 8% of its sales are derived from North America, and it seeks to expand U.S. presence to guide customer designs.
- KOA Corp. of Japan acquired specialty resistor manufacturer Tama Electric Co. Ltd. in 2001 and broadened its filtering product line to include metal oxide varistors, resettable fuses, three-pole capacitors, inductors, and ferrite beads to tap into emerging gigahertz designs.
- Jeff Rice, vice president of sales and marketing at KOA Speer Electronics Inc., Bradford, Pa., noted the new product lines provide access to emerging gigahertz designs not available with commodity resistors.
- Kemet differentiates itself with a full complement of surface-mount capacitor technologies including solid-state aluminum, ceramic, and tantalum dielectrics; it expanded high-frequency tantalum and high-capacitance ceramic products and generated significant revenue from new-product introductions while targeting the medical sector.
- Yageo's new-product development focuses on lead-free components, a new line of miniaturized solid-state antennas, and X2Y attenuators primarily used for filtering in high-frequency applications.
- Suppliers are expanding vendor-managed inventory programs and creating customer and supplier Web portals to facilitate business.
- AVX, Kemet, and Vishay enable distributors to check inventory via the Web.
- Taiyo Yuden (U.S.A.), Schaumburg, Ill., launched an inventory check function on its website in July for franchised distributor inventory information updated daily, allowing users to view quantities, update dates, download contacts, send RFQs, and place orders.
- Suppliers offer online design support; Vishay provides an MLCC modeling program for capacitor/circuit behavior and a parametric search engine, while AVX and Kemet provide SPICE models and software.
- Vishay supported its EMS customers with senior account managers to coordinate component requirements.
- Murata Electronics North America's strategy focuses on design issues and strengthening engineering and product management as business shifts from North America and Eastern Europe to Asia; it also bolstered its EMS organization to address the production shift to China.
- Kemet endorses systems for supply chain visibility and uses RosettaNet Partner Interface Process 3A1 with distributor TTI Inc., Fort Worth, Texas, for automated quoting to reduce cycle time and increase information accuracy.
