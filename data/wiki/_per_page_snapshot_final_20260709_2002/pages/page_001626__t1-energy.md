# T1 Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-19

**Source:** [FREYR rebrands after killing its $2.6B Georgia battery factory plans | Electrek](https://electrek.co/2025/02/19/freyr-rebrands-t1-energy/)  ·  `page_001626`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery scrapped plans to build a $2.6 billion battery energy storage system factory in Georgia.
- FREYR Battery has rebranded itself as T1 Energy (NYSE: TE).
- T1 Energy is committed to becoming a vertically integrated US solar + battery storage leader from its new corporate headquarters in Austin, Texas.
- T1 Energy expects to start trading under its new NYSE symbols on March 3.
- In August, then-CEO Tom Einar Jensen told investors that a surplus of cheap Chinese batteries had made it harder to raise money to manufacture batteries.
- The company switched its focus to solar panel manufacturing.
- On December 24, FREYR announced closing its acquisition of China-headquartered Trina Solar’s 5-gigawatt (GW), 1.35 million-square-foot solar panel factory in Wilmer, Texas.
- The acquired factory was renamed G1 Dallas.
- The G1 Dallas factory employs more than 1,000 people.
- T1 says the solar panel production ramp is on schedule.
- Daniel Barcelo serves as T1’s chairman of the board and chief executive officer.
- Daniel Barcelo stated that more than 80% of new US electricity capacity in 2024 was solar and batteries.
- T1 plans to lead in the solar and battery markets by enabling the growth in energy supply required to support AI, data centers, reshoring, and electrification to restore American industrial capacity and leadership in future advanced industries.
- T1 is moving forward with site selection for its upcoming US solar cell factory, G2.
- T1 expects to lock in a location for the G2 factory by Q1 2025.
- Construction for the G2 factory is still on track to kick off in mid-2025.
