# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-31

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: New Georgia Economic Development Records Set for Jobs, Investment in Just Nine Months | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-04-07/gov-kemp-new-georgia-economic-development-records-set-jobs-investment)  ·  `page_003849`

## Facts

- Through the third quarter of fiscal year 2022 (July 1, 2021 - March 31, 2022), GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 251 project locations resulting in more than 35,400 new jobs and $12.9 billion in investment for the state.
- Job creation totals account for a 48% increase compared to three-quarters through the previous fiscal year and a 6% increase compared to the prior year’s overall total.
- Total investment shows a recorded 54% increase compared to the same time period last year.
- Nearly 78% of both projects and investments were located outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area, with over half of the jobs created also outside this region.
- Key industry sectors for recent economic development include automotive, advanced manufacturing, logistics and distribution, software and technology, and food processing.
- Georgia is actively recruiting the entire EV supply chain to ensure an effective, efficient, and affordable environment for EV production.
- Rivian plans to develop a $5 billion campus at Stanton Springs North and an electric vehicle service center in Atlanta that will provide training on EVs.
- SK Battery America has partnered with American lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup Ascend Elements to recycle both cell and module lithium-ion battery manufacturing scrap from SK’s Jackson County facility.
- Ascend Elements announced its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington in January.
- Aurubis, a leading recycler of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials worldwide, will invest $340 million in a ‘first-of-its-kind in the U.S.’ state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- Electrification solutions and sustainability leader Aspen Aerogels announced plans to locate a new 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility in Statesboro.
- Aspen Aerogels will produce PyroThin materials at its new facility, which provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems.
- In March, Moderna announced it will establish a new Enterprise Solutions Hub in Atlanta.
- Last August, Intuitive announced it is expanding its Gwinnett County campus.
- The Moderna and Intuitive projects represent a combined investment of more than $547.8 million and 1,425 new jobs.
- Georgia Quick Start, the No. 1-ranking workforce training program administered through the Technical College System of Georgia for over 40 years, helped secure Pepsi Co.’s expansion in DeKalb County, Remington Firearms’ world-class research and development center in LaGrange, and Titan Steel Doors’ expansion in Hart County.
- The Pepsi Co., Remington Firearms, and Titan Steel Doors projects alone represent 1,082 new jobs and $373 million in investment.
- Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) deepwater and regional port projects played a key role in securing Serena & Lily Inc.’s new facility in Effingham County.
- The GPA has been reporting record-breaking numbers for the past 19 consecutive months as it expedites plans for long-term growth.
- The Savannah Harbor Expansion Project (SHEP) was recently completed, deepening a 40-mile stretch between the port and the Atlantic Ocean to accommodate super-sized freighters traveling from the expanded Panama Canal.
- Inland ports are attracting investments, such as the Appalachian Regional Port driving Roper Corporation’s decision to invest $118 million in expanding its facilities in Walker County.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and the state’s extensive rail network serve as key assets driving Georgia’s growth.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation maintains more than 1,200 miles of highway connecting companies to 80% of the country in two days or less of driving time.
- This transportation system was integral to landing Ryder System Inc.’s new facility in Henry County that will create more than 250 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia continues to be the preferred place to do business and emphasized the state's pro-business approach and strong partnership with homegrown and global companies.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted the department's mission to target strategic industries, support existing business expansions, and maintain momentum through the end of the year.
