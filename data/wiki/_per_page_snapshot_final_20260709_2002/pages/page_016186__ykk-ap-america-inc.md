# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-08

**Source:** [YKK AP America Expands Production with New Facility in Macon - Georgia_CEO](https://goldenislesceo.com/news/2022/08/governor-kemp-building-materials-manufacturer-ykk-ap-america-expands-residential-window-production-capabilities-new-facility-bibb-county/)  ·  `page_016186`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that YKK AP America Inc. will invest up to $125 million in an expansion that will include a new manufacturing facility and additional machinery and equipment in Bibb County.
- The new facility will create 100 new jobs in addition to the 250 jobs YKK AP currently supports in Macon, Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that ensuring companies already operating in Georgia can grow and thrive is a priority alongside attracting new investments.
- Governor Kemp noted YKK AP recognizes the advantages of doing business in middle Georgia and has chosen to expand its relationship with the Macon community through 100 new jobs.
- YKK AP specializes in architectural products for commercial and residential buildings.
- YKK AP entered the U.S. market in 1991 with a vertically integrated, commercial products factory in Dublin, Georgia.
- YKK AP maintains its headquarters in Atlanta.
- YKK AP and its Ontario-based subsidiary Erie Architectural Products employ over 1,200 employees in North America.
- Nearly 900 of the company's employees are located within the State of Georgia.
- Oliver Stepe, President of YKK AP America Inc., stated the company is excited to increase its investment in Macon, the hometown of their residential products business.
- The new facility will expand the workforce, introduce further vertical integration of vinyl extrusion, glass, and window production, and allow for enhanced efficiencies to manufacture high-quality windows and doors for homebuilders and homeowners.
- Oliver Stepe stated this investment builds toward a long-term vision to be the leader in the residential window and door market and positions the company to expand its business in and beyond the Southeast region.
- YKK AP’s new state-of-the-art facility will be located at the Macon-Bibb I-75 Industrial Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified site.
- After completion, the company will relocate its operations and employees from its current site on Ocmulgee Boulevard to the new facility.
- The Macon facility will become home to the company’s residential division, which manufactures vinyl windows and doors for builders and homeowners.
- The facility will serve as a model plant for future North American manufacturing facilities, featuring new technologies, advanced sustainable manufacturing processes, and enhanced work environments for employees.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- The company is currently hiring for numerous positions.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester M. Miller stated it is a great day when a long-running business partner decides to make another investment and bring an additional 100 jobs to the area.
- Mayor Miller noted that using the new state-of-the-art facility as a blueprint for future facilities illustrates trust in Macon-Bibb County and Georgia as the best place to do business.
- Senior Project Manager Candice Scott represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated YKK AP has a strong history of growth and success in Georgia, opening its first branch in Atlanta in 1986 and establishing its U.S. manufacturing presence in middle Georgia five years later.
- Commissioner Wilson noted the company has since expanded its presence in Georgia and built valuable relationships with local communities.
- Commissioner Wilson stated companies in the advanced manufacturing industry, like YKK AP, have played a key role in bringing jobs and opportunities to Georgia, creating the second most new jobs in the state last year.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Completing GRAD certification with GDEcD is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development for their regions.
- Through the GRAD program, Georgia has more than 60 industrial certified sites that are ready for fast-track industrial projects.
