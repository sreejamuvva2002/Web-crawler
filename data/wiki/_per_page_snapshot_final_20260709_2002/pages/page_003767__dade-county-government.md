# Dade County Government

## Facts

- Every person engaged in any business, trade, profession or occupation in the unincorporated areas of Dade County must apply each year for an occupational tax certificate, commonly referred to as a business license.
- The fee for the occupational tax certificate is based on the number of employees and calculated using a tax rate adopted by the county commissioners.
- Specific local, state, or federal statutes and regulations may apply to projects in Dade County, including but not limited to requirements for alcoholic beverages, animal inoculation, county road acceptance, distribution of controlled substances, driveway pipe installation, electrical inspection, emergency 911 address, flood damage prevention, health and sanitation, land subdivision, massage business services, mobile home permits and decals, signs, soil erosion and sedimentation control, solid waste, timber cutting and harvesting, utility accommodations on rights-of-way, and wireless telecommunications.
- New construction in Dade County requires obtaining an electrical permit, a septic system permit in most cases, a 911 address, coordination with Georgia Power, and state permits.
- The Dade County Commission Office is located at 71 Case Avenue (2nd floor) in Trenton, GA.
- The Dade County Health Department is located at 71 Case Avenue (1st floor) in Trenton, GA.
- The Dade County 911 Office is located at 71 Case Avenue (2nd floor) in Trenton, GA.
- The Georgia Environmental Protection Division handles state permit questions for the county.
- The Georgia Secretary of State's Administrative Offices of the Professional Licensing Boards handles all other state license questions.
- The notice provides general information concerning permits currently required in Dade County and should not be relied upon for legal advice regarding county, state, or federal statutes.
- Dade County reserves the right to adopt new or different ordinances or regulations in the future.
