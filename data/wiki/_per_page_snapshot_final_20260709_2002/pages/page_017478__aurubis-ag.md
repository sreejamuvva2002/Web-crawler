# Aurubis AG

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Aurubis will invest $340 million (€300 million) in a state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- The new facility will be the very first of its kind in the U.S.
- The facility will create 125 jobs in the Augusta area.
- Governor Kemp called the project the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Aurubis' commitment to sustainable development and raw material security will play a role in building Georgia's electric mobility value chain.
- From Augusta, the company plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated the investment sets an example of sustainable growth and makes the company a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S., a role it already holds in Europe.
- Aurubis will build its facility on 150 acres at Augusta Corporate Park.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a "Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified" site.
- Operations are expected to begin by the first half of 2024.
- The company plans to begin hiring for positions as soon as summer 2022.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Development Authority of Augusta, welcomed Aurubis and noted they will build a world-class advanced manufacturing facility bringing skilled jobs to Augusta.
- The Augusta Economic Development Authority stands ready to provide a foundation for helping Aurubis succeed over the next several decades.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated exports from the Aurubis recycling operation will strengthen Georgia's global position via Savannah.
- Savannah is already one of the nation's most balanced ports in terms of import-export trade.
- Fernanda Kirchner, Senior Project Manager for GDEcD's Global Commerce division, represented the department on this project.
- The project involved partnerships with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Sérgio Domingues, Managing Director for the State of Georgia's Europe Office, played a supporting role in attracting Aurubis to Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed Aurubis and noted it will be the first multi-metal recycling plant in the United States in Richmond County.
- Pat Wilson thanked partners in Augusta and leadership for supporting one of the largest German investments in the state.
- Augusta Corporate Park completed due diligence and specific certification requirements prior to visits by prospective businesses as part of GRAD certification.
- Receiving GRAD certification with GDEcD helps catalyze economic growth and industrial development for regions served.
- Thousands of Georgia jobs have been created on GRAD sites since the program's inception in 2008.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into high-quality metals.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually.
- From copper cathodes, Aurubis makes wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, and flat rolled products made of copper and copper alloys.
- Aurubis also produces precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc.
- The company's portfolio includes additional products such as sulfuric acid and iron silicate.
