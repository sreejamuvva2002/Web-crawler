# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-07-15

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies Bringing Repurposed Rubber Tech to Europe - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/lehigh-technologies-bringing-repurposed-rubber-tech-to-europe/)  ·  `page_057059`

## Facts

- Atlanta, Ga.-based Lehigh Technologies produces sustainable raw materials from end-of-life tires and post-industrial rubber.
- Lehigh Technologies entered a joint venture with HERA Holding, a European waste resources company, to build the first micronized rubber powder (MRP) production facility in Europe.
- The joint venture, named Lehigh Spain, will construct the MRP plant as a HERA facility in Spain.
- The new facility will service Russia and the entire regions of Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.
- Lehigh Technologies’ MRP is a sustainable raw material made from old tires and other post-industrial rubber that can be repurposed back into a tire and other goods to replace oil- and rubber-based materials.
- The proprietary process grinds the material down so small that it has the consistency of flour.
- The MRP plant will utilize proprietary technology Lehigh Technologies has been using in the U.S. since 2006.
- Construction of the plant is currently underway, with first shipments expected by the end of the first quarter 2017.
- Dr. Alan Barton, CEO of Lehigh Technologies, stated that HERA’s shared vision for creating sustainable solutions makes them an ideal partner for Lehigh’s next phase of international expansion.
- Dr. Alan Barton noted strong market reception for MRP as companies learn that micronized rubber powder delivers a strong value proposition, marking a critical step to meet rapidly growing demand.
- The company currently offers a range of repurposed tire products, including PolyDyne, MicroDyne and EkoDyne.
- Lehigh Technologies ships its products to more than 18 countries.
