# Great Dane Trailers

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2010-10-18

**Source:** [Great Dane Trailers Breaks Ground on New Southeast Plant - AftermarketNews](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/great-dane-trailers-breaks-ground-on-new-southeast-plant/)  ·  `page_055297`

## Facts

- Great Dane Trailers officially broke ground on the site of its new refrigerated trailer manufacturing plant in Statesboro, Ga.
- The groundbreaking ceremony was attended by more than 200 attendees, including local business representatives, state and local political leaders, and company executives.
- Georgia Gov. Sonny Perdue, Statesboro Mayor Joe Brannen, and Bulloch County Commission Chairman Garrett Nevil hailed the cooperation among the state, local economic developers, and Great Dane as key reasons for the success of the site selection process.
- Company executives were particularly drawn to the location just northwest of Savannah because it remained close to where Great Dane was founded in 1900.
- Bill Crown, president and CEO of CC Industries and CEO of Great Dane Limited Partnership, stated that the site selection allowed the company to continue its legacy by keeping operations close to its deep roots in Savannah.
- The selected site covers 118 acres.
- The plant will encompass 450,000 square feet, including office and showroom space.
- The facility will have the capacity to produce more than 5,000 trailers annually.
- The plant will employ more than 400 people.
- Phill Pines, president and COO of Great Dane Trailers, said the refrigerated trailers that will be built there will be complex and detailed, emphasizing the importance of this to the local workforce.
- The new reefer plant will feature the latest technology, making it among the most modern, efficient plants in the world.
- Construction is expected to get underway later in 2010.
- The plant is scheduled to bring production online in 2012.
- Great Dane manufactures dry van, refrigerated, and platform trailers.
- The company has division offices in Savannah, Ga., and Chicago, Ill., with manufacturing plants strategically located throughout the United States.
- Great Dane utilizes a network of company-owned branches and full-line independent dealers as well as parts-only independent dealers, with distribution points across North and South America.
