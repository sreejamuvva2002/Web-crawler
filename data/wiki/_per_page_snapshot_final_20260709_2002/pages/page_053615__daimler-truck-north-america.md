# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-10

**Source:** [DTNA rolls out 850,000th truck at Cleveland facility - Truck News](https://www.trucknews.com/equipment/dtna-rolls-out-850000th-truck-at-cleveland-facility/1003199403/)  ·  `page_053615`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America (DTNA) has produced its 850,000th truck at its Cleveland, N.C., manufacturing plant.
- The milestone vehicle is a Western Star 47X.
- The milestone truck was delivered to Alamo Group.
- The truck will be provided to Alamo Group’s snow and ice team, a manufacturer of heavy-duty snow and ice removal equipment.
- David Carson is the senior vice-president of sales and marketing at DTNA.
- David Carson stated that Alamo Group has been a valued partner for many years and expressed pride in supporting their mission with advanced vocational trucks.
- The Western Star 47X is purpose-built for demanding applications like snow and ice management.
- The 47X is powered by a Detroit DD13 engine.
- The 47X is engineered for durability, safety, and performance in extreme conditions.
- Jean Philippe Bourque is the group vice-president of Alamo Group’s Snow and Ice Division.
- Jean Philippe Bourque stated that they are honored to receive the milestone truck and continue their strong relationship with DTNA.
- Jean Philippe Bourque noted that the Western Star 47X is a critical part of their portfolio and that customers value its reliability, performance, and support.
- DTNA’s Cleveland Truck Manufacturing Plant was established in 1989.
- The Cleveland plant is DTNA’s largest heavy-duty truck plant in the U.S.
- The facility spans 1.2 million square feet on 178 acres.
- The facility employs approximately 2,000 people.
- The facility currently assembles the Fifth Generation Freightliner Cascadia.
- The facility currently assembles Western Star 47X and 49X models.
- Jeff Allen is the senior vice-president of operations and specialty vehicles at DTNA.
- Jeff Allen stated that reaching 850,000 trucks is a testament to the dedication of the Cleveland team and the strength of manufacturing processes.
