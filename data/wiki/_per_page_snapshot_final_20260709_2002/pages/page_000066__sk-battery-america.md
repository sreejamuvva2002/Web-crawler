# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-26

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: SK Battery America to Open U.S. IT Center, Create 200 High-Tech Jobs in Roswell | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-01-26/gov-kemp-sk-battery-america-open-us-it-center-create-200-high-tech-jobs)  ·  `page_000066`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on January 26, 2023 that SK Battery America will open a regional IT hub facility in Roswell.
- The new IT center will create 200 high-tech jobs.
- SK Battery America is investing approximately $19 million over the next few years for the IT center.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is the No. 1 state for business and praised SK Battery America's continued growth, noting it brings jobs of the future that benefit Georgians in manufacturing and taps into tech talent from top-ranking universities.
- SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- The Jackson County facilities will supply electric vehicles such as the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The SK Battery America facility is one of SK Group’s three major investments in the State of Georgia.
- SK Group was founded in 1976 and is the second-largest conglomerate in South Korea.
- Jason Choi, Head of Information Technology at SK Battery America, said the decision to open the IT regional center in Roswell aims to strengthen SK’s position as a leader in the battery industry and help build and operate an advanced manufacturing system per further expansion of battery production bases in the United States.
- The new IT center will serve as an integrated IT management center for the company’s battery manufacturing facilities across the United States, including its Georgia facilities.
- The IT center is located at 1110 Sanctuary Parkway in Roswell.
- Operations at the IT center are expected to begin in January.
- SK Battery America will be hiring IT experts for battery manufacturing execution system development and operations, as well as data analysts.
- City of Roswell Mayor Kurt Wilson welcomed SK Battery America to the community, stating his priority since taking office a year ago has been recruiting businesses that provide high-quality job opportunities and enhance economic strength.
- Mayor Kurt Wilson called Roswell the perfect location for the IT Regional Center and expressed pride in SK Battery America's choice.
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, welcomed SK Battery America to Fulton County, calling it a destination for the best and brightest.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the City of Roswell, Fulton County, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that SK On established SK Battery America as its U.S. production base in 2019, and the new IT regional center is a natural result of the company’s commitment to Georgia.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted the ripple effects of economic development, noting suppliers, support centers, and expansions create opportunities beyond a company's original scope, location, and industry.
- Electric mobility related projects have contributed more than $21 billion in investments and more than 26,700 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- SK Battery America’s new IT regional center will support battery production operations, build on the state’s existing automotive assets and developing workforce, and help ensure a domestic supply chain for the electric mobility industry.
- SK On is the parent company of SK Battery America and is a global leading electric vehicle battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider.
- SK Battery America produces safe, fast charging, and long-lasting batteries currently installed in the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The company will have a combined capacity to make 22 GWh of battery cells a year, enough to power the equivalent of about 300,000 EVs annually.
- Since 2019, SK Battery America has invested a total of $2.6 billion to build EV battery plants in Commerce, Georgia, creating 2,600 high-skilled jobs.
