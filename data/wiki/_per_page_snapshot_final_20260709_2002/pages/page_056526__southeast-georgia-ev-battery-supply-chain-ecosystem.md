# Southeast Georgia EV & Battery Supply Chain Ecosystem

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-29

**Source:** [Southeast Georgia: Blossoming Ecosystem - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/03/29/2023-economic-yearbook-southeast-georgia/)  ·  `page_056526`

## Facts

- South Korea’s Hyundai Motor Group announced in May 2022 its intention to build a $5.4 billion plant to produce electric vehicles in Bryan County.
- The plant will be built on the 2,923-acre megasite located less than 31 miles from the Port of Savannah, with access to I-95 and I-16.
- The plant will have capacity to produce 300,000 vehicles a year and offer 8,100 jobs.
- The state’s original prediction of $1 billion in additional investment has already been exceeded by $600 million.
- Georgia Department of Transportation and county governments are working on highway and local road improvements, including redesigning the I-95 and I-16 interchange.
- Water and sewer infrastructure is being upgraded in some counties to support project growth.
- A task force has been set up to identify strategies to increase the regional skilled workforce pipeline.
- In 2014, Bryan, Chatham, Effingham, and Bulloch counties established the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) to attract a landmark investment to the Bryan County site.
- All four JDA counties have an equal voice and work together to secure Hyundai-related projects, while competing independently for non-Hyundai projects.
- The JDA structure discourages potential investors from playing one county off against the other.
- Bryan County was the fastest-growing county in Georgia and the sixth-fastest-growing county in the nation as of the 2020 census.
- St. Joseph’s/Candler health system plans to add two new medical facilities in Bryan County to accommodate population influx.
- Plug Power announced an $84 million investment to open a green-power production plant in Kingsland.
- Plug Power’s zero-emission fuel production plant will help serve industrial customers across the Southeastern U.S. and will create 24 jobs.
- Green Power EMC reached an agreement to bring an additional 252 megawatts of solar energy online to serve 16 subscribing electric membership corporations across Georgia.
- Construction of the first of three South Georgia Green Power sites will begin in Jeff Davis County later this year.
- The three South Georgia Green Power sites together will produce enough low-cost, renewable power to help serve more than 44,000 EMC households.
- Effingham County has seen strong growth in logistics over the last six years due to Port of Savannah expansion and commercial property scarcity.
- Trammell Crow Company located a 477-acre site in Effingham County to build Coastal Trade Center, featuring 4.7 million square feet of logistics space.
- Bulloch County has attracted two Hyundai suppliers: auto parts manufacturers Joon Georgia and Ecoplastic.
- Chatham County’s economy is supported by the port, tourism, and an entertainment production industry that generated $207 million in direct spend in 2022.
- Hyundai supplier Seoyon E-Hwa announced it will build a second $76 million facility in Chatham County, where it already has a plant in LaGrange.
- Commercial real estate company Jones Lang LaSalle reported that warehousing rents rose 28% in the fourth quarter of 2022 due to strong demand.
- Brunswick’s port in Glynn County is the nation’s second-busiest hub for Ro/Ro cargo like cars and tractors.
- A $150 million development plan at the Brunswick port will be completed this year, raising annual capacity from 1.2 million vehicles to 1.4 million.
- Glynn County is fielding requests for information from Hyundai suppliers.
- Tourism in Glynn County was up 23% compared to pre-pandemic 2019 in 2022, bringing $229 million in lodging revenue.
- Downtown Brunswick is experiencing growth with new restaurants and a historic hotel undergoing restoration.
- Broadband is being installed in rural sections of Glynn County that still lack it.
- A serious worker shortage exists in the region, according to Ralph Staffins III, CEO of the Brunswick-Golden Isles Chamber of Commerce.
- Camden County hosts Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay, which generates a former-military workforce of skilled engineers and technicians.
- Camden County hosts two green hydrogen production plants owned by Latham, NY-based Plug Power.
- The Camden County Joint Development Authority provided land and local site support to attract Plug Power’s $84 million investment.
- The Camden County hydrogen plants are capable of producing 15 tons of liquefied hydrogen a day, with plans to expand to 30 tons.
- A 721-acre, $500 million coastal project called Cumberland Inlet is being developed in St. Marys, featuring an ecotourism park, marina, boutique hotel, retail, restaurants, and vacation rentals.
- Atlanta-based Jacoby Development Inc. broke ground on the Cumberland Inlet project in August 2022.
- The real estate market in Camden County has exploded with people moving from the West, Northeast, and Florida, attracted by lower land and insurance costs compared to Florida.
- Liberty County shares Fort Stewart-Hunter Army Airfield with Bryan County and can offer businesses an ex-military workforce.
- Liberty County has a strong industrial base in distribution, e-commerce, and manufacturing, with four new industrial zones recently designated.
- Foreign direct investment accounts for 70% of Liberty County’s industrial companies.
- Coffee County aims to diversify its economy by adding value to its agricultural base alongside new industry.
- Pilgrim’s Pride recently expanded its poultry operations in Douglas with a $75 million facility to produce pet food ingredients.
- Water, sewer, and natural gas expansions at Barrington Agri-Business Park are intended to generate $45 million in private investments.
- Coffee County faces tight available housing, especially for low-income and middle-income families, prompting calls to encourage residential construction.
- Ware County has several planned expansions and new industrial projects in the works, some in the solar area.
- Alabama-based Twin Pines Minerals plans to mine titanium on the edge of the Okefenokee National Wildlife Refuge, a plan that claims no harm to the swamp but faces opposition from residents and environmentalists.
- The Twin Pines Minerals mining plan could impact neighboring Charlton County’s economic fortunes.
