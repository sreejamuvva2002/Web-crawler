# Nidec Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-09

**Source:** [Nidec is gearing up its electric motor business in Europe - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2021/04/09/strategy-brief-nidec-gears-up-european-electric-motor-business/)  ·  `page_058109`

## Facts

- Japan’s Nidec Corporation opened new offices in Serbia and is setting up two new factories in Novi Sad to bring the company to the forefront of the global electric car market.
- CEO Shigenobu Nagamori aims for “domination” in the global e-motor business within the next five years.
- By 2025, Nidec plans to supply five million electric motors to the global automotive industry, targeting the line where electric cars become the majority in the industry.
- Reports from November 2020 indicated about one million of these motors were destined for Europe, prompting additional offices and production facilities in Serbia’s Novi Sad, located about 90 km northwest of Belgrade.
- Nidec initially set the start of construction for September 2021 and hoped to reach full capacity by mid-2022, with the foundation laid in January.
- Nidec Electric Motor Serbia’s principal business is manufacturing and selling automotive motors and related products.
- Jun Seki, President and Chief Operating Officer, represented Nidec Corporation at the press event following the new offices’ opening.
- Nidec declined to disclose specific figures regarding clients and capacity due to confidentiality policies.
- The company established Nidec Elesys Europe LLC in Novi Sad to manufacture and sell automotive inverters and electronic control units (ECUs).
- The Elesys site will cover 36,000 square meters once completed on the same schedule as the e-motor facility.
- Nidec intends to employ 200 people at the Elesys facility.
- The larger e-drive business factory will cover almost 60,000 square meters and will employ 1,000 people.
- Nidec chose Serbia to recruit locally from a workforce abundant in science and engineering who are fluent in English.
- Serbia’s status as a non-EU member was another factor for establishing operations there, with COO Jun Seki noting that being first in a pre-accession market provides a strategic headstart.
- The company claims “tremendous support” through the Western Balkans Cooperation Initiative promoted by the Japanese government.
- Upon opening the new offices, Nidec entered a strategic alliance with the University of Novi Sad to actively engage in technological exchanges via industry-academia partnerships.
- The new facilities will consolidate the Nidec Group’s production activities in the East European region and allow the Automotive Motor & Electronic Control Business Unit to launch multiple future businesses.
- This strategy mirrors a successful cluster of twelve businesses Nidec established in China’s Kudong region.
- The Serbian business base will supply products to the European market while eventually designing and developing products locally, creating synergies by sharing production infrastructure and back-office operations across multiple firms at the same site.
- Nidec plans to invest up to ten billion US dollars in the automotive segment over the next five years, announced in November 2020.
- CEO Nagamori intends to “dominate the market for electric vehicle engines,” targeting a quarter of the market by 2025 and between 40 and 45 percent by 2030.
- The company estimates electric cars will reach price parity with combustion or hybrid vehicles within the next five years.
- Nidec works with three client types: Type A (outsources motor business, e.g., Foxconn for their EV platform MIH), Type B (understands outsourcing benefits, e.g., Stellantis), and Type C (OEMs developing motors in-house).
- COO Seki stated it is only “a matter of time” until OEMs will source components from suppliers like Nidec once they scale up to be cost-competitive.
- Nidec holds a large contract for hybrid vehicles from an unnamed manufacturer.
- The company forecasts growing demand for motors in the compact EV segment, citing Chinese Mini EV sales of around 35,000 units a month as evidence of high market potential.
- An update in December 2021 confirmed construction started in December 2021 (delayed from September) but maintained the mid-2022 completion schedule for the 59,760-square-meter production facility.
- A July 2022 update reported ground was broken for the Nidec Elesys plant, which will produce inverters and ECUs in an area of around 36,000 square meters, with completion planned for early 2023.
- An October 2022 update confirmed construction of the electric motor factory was completed, with series production scheduled to start in 2023 and a first-phase employment target of around 1,000 people.
