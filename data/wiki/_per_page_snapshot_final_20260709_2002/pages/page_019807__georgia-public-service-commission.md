# Georgia Public Service Commission

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kemp picks new PSC member as Eaton leaves for superior court judgeship | News From The States](https://www.newsfromthestates.com/article/kemp-picks-new-psc-member-eaton-leaves-superior-court-judgeship)  ·  `page_019807`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp appointed Cobb County businessman Fitz Johnson to the Georgia Public Service Commission (PSC) to replace outgoing Chairman Chuck Eaton.
- The PSC regulates Georgia’s public utilities and plays a major role in deciding what Georgians pay for electricity and other utility services.
- The commission will decide how much of Plant Vogtle’s cost overruns should fall to Georgia Power’s customers.
- Commissioner Tim Echols, the panel’s vice chair, plans to nominate Commissioner Tricia Pridemore to replace Eaton as chair.
- Chuck Eaton is leaving the PSC this summer to become a superior court judge in the Atlanta Judicial Circuit.
- Fitz Johnson is an Army veteran and Cobb County businessman who previously ran for state schools superintendent and has served on the State Charter Schools Commission.
- The PSC is an all-Republican, five-member commission that regulates public utilities.
- A pending lawsuit challenges the PSC’s at-large election system as unfair to Black voters, noting only one African American commissioner has served since statewide elections began in 1906.
- Eaton earned his law degree by taking night classes at Georgia State University after being elected to the commission and recognizing its judicial nature.
- Eaton was narrowly reelected in 2018, edging out Democrat Lindy Miller by about 2 percentage points.
- Eaton represents Fulton, DeKalb, Clayton and Rockdale counties on the commission, with his term set to expire in 2024.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Fitz Johnson’s record of service, private sector leadership, and community dedication uniquely qualify him to serve on the PSC.
- Commissioner Tim Echols noted that Chuck Eaton’s calm nature, judicial competency, and sense of humor made him a great Commission Chair.
