# VinFast

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Vietnam, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Vietnamese automaker VinFast announced plans to build its first North American electric-vehicle manufacturing plant in Chatham County, North Carolina, with production slated for 2024 and up to 7,500 jobs by 2027. The company considered a site in Savannah, Georgia before selecting the NC location.

## EV supply-chain relevance

VinFast evaluated a potential plant site in Savannah, Georgia, indicating direct relevance to Georgia’s EV supply-chain landscape.

## Key facts

- VinFast will build its first North American plant on a nearly 2,000-acre site in Chatham County, NC.
- The plant is planned to have an annual capacity of 150,000 electric vehicles.
- State incentives include up to $316 million in job-development grants and up to $450 million for site preparation and infrastructure.
- VinFast expects to create 7,500 jobs by 2027 with average salaries of $51,000.
- Production at the plant is expected to begin in 2024.
- The company considered a potential site in Savannah, Georgia before selecting North Carolina.
- Governor Roy Cooper highlighted the project’s job creation and greenhouse-gas reduction benefits.
- UAW President Ray Curry said the union plans to organize workers at the new VinFast plant and other EV/battery factories in the South.
- VinFast is part of Vietnamese conglomerate Vingroup, which funds its U.S. expansion.
- In November, VinFast introduced the VF e35 midsize and VF e36 large electric SUVs at the Los Angeles Auto Show.
