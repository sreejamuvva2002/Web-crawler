# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-04

**Source:** [I visited Hyundai's $12.6 Billion Metaplant and now I understand why Tesla should be worried](https://autos.yahoo.com/ev-and-future-tech/articles/visited-hyundais-12-6-billion-154517497.html)  ·  `page_001030`

## Facts

- Hyundai is investing $12.6 billion in Georgia for its Metaplant America facility.
- The HMGMA campus spans nearly 3,000 acres, comprises 11 buildings, and covers 7.5 million square feet.
- The plant is located in Savannah, Georgia, with a new hydrogen refueling station under development in nearby Pooler, Georgia.
- HMGMA currently employs more than 1,600 Hyundai team members, referred to as Meta Pros.
- When fully staffed, including affiliate and supplier personnel, the facility will employ 8,500 workers.
- Hyundai utilizes 21 XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks for zero-emission logistics operations at the site.
- A new HTWO Energy Savannah hydrogen refueling station for the XCIENT trucks is in development in Pooler, Georgia.
- The plant's buildings feature rounded corners to harmonize with the surrounding natural environment.
- HMGMA is highly automated and uses robots, including Boston Dynamics Spot robot dogs, to scan vehicles on the assembly line for proper part installation.
- Robotic devices at the facility both assemble cars and transport parts throughout the factory.
- The sound generated by the plant's machines is pumped underground, allowing workers to converse at normal volume without hearing protection.
- The plant currently manufactures the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 electric vehicles.
- Hyundai plans to expand HMGMA production to a total of 14 models, which will include Kia and Genesis vehicles.
- HMGMA initially targeted an annual output of up to 300,000 vehicles.
- Hyundai recently updated the plant's target to build as many as 500,000 vehicles per year when fully operational.
- Hyundai aims to build 80 percent of the electric vehicles it sells in America domestically at this facility.
- Electrified vehicles now account for one-third of all Hyundai sales globally.
- In 2021, Hyundai ranked fifth in overall EV sales in the United States.
- In 2025, Hyundai surpassed Ford and Volkswagen to move into third place behind Tesla and GM in U.S. pure EV market share.
- Hyundai is developing extended range electric vehicles (EREVs) that combine high-performance battery and motor technology with less than half the battery capacity of standard EVs.
- The EREV technology promises a driving range of more than 600 miles and lower vehicle costs due to reduced battery capacity.
- Every vehicle built at HMGMA undergoes testing on a roughly one-and-a-half-mile test track before shipping.
- The HMGMA test course subjects each car to 16 different evaluations, including rough-road, emergency-braking, turning-radius, and acceleration tests.
- Professional drivers at the facility test between 30 and 40 cars daily on the test track.
- Hyundai rented a private farm near HMGMA to set up an off-road rallycross course for testing the IONIQ 5 XRT model.
