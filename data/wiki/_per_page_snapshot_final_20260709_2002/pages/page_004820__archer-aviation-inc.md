# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Selects Site in Georgia Adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport for its Manufacturing Facility](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20221114006079/en/)  ·  `page_004820`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation selected a site in Covington, Georgia adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport for its manufacturing facility.
- The site selection represents a key next step as Archer works towards being first in the industry to bring up a high-capacity eVTOL production facility.
- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site.
- The initial facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer intends to invest $118M over the next 10 years.
- Archer will receive an incentive package totaling 33% of its capital investment commitment.
- The incentive package includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Archer has entered into a letter of intent with Synovus Bank to obtain financing for the project.
- Synovus Bank is the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential manufacturing sites across the United States for its first facility.
- Key site selection considerations included local labor market talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer's CEO, stated that after many months of discussion and evaluation, they are excited to select Georgia for their manufacturing facility.
- Goldstein noted that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Goldstein added that eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp commented that Georgia has made nurturing innovation a priority as it works with businesses to define the future.
- Kemp stated that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Kemp welcomed Archer to Georgia, noting they can take advantage of the state's signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 jobs in Covington long-term.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline.
- The local education pipeline includes a dozen high schools that offer training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has been cultivating opportunities for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer's team is based in Santa Clara, California.
