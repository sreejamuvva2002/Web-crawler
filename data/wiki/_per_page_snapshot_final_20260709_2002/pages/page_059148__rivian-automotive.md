# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-03

**Source:** [Georgia sets record-breaking incentives for Rivian's electric vehicle plant | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/georgia-rivian-electric-vehicle-plant-incentive-package)  ·  `page_059148`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia and local governments will give Rivian Automotive $1.5 billion of incentives to build a 7,500-job, $5 billion electric vehicle plant east of Atlanta.
- Documents outlining the incentive package were signed on Monday.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the package reflects the largest single industrial announcement in Georgia history.
- Rivian pledged to reach full investment and job targets by the end of 2028.
- The 7,500 jobs will pay an average of $56,000 a year plus benefits.
- The state hopes Rivian will anchor an entire electric vehicle industry in Georgia.
- Wilson stated it is absolutely appropriate because the project is creating more jobs.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, called it by far the largest incentive package Georgia has ever offered to a company.
- LeRoy noted it is also the largest incentive package any American state has ever given to an auto plant.
- LeRoy stated this is very significant and the biggest auto assembly subsidy package in US history.
- Rivian is based in Irvine, California.
- Rivian is a startup manufacturer of electric trucks and commercial delivery vans.
- The company challenges established automakers like Ford and General Motors as well as electric vehicle leader Tesla.
- Rivian is already producing vehicles at its facility in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian hopes to break ground on the Georgia plant as early as summer 2022.
- Rivian plans to begin production in Georgia in 2024.
- The Georgia plant aims to produce 400,000 vehicles a year.
- A company statement said the long-term economic partnership promises to deliver value to Rivian, the people of Georgia and their kids’ kids’ kids.
- The project faces fierce local opposition from residents concerned about spoiling their rural quality of life.
- The plant site covers 2,000 acres.
- The site is located between Social Circle and Rutledge, approximately 45 miles east of downtown Atlanta.
- The state took over planning and zoning for the project after opponents overwhelmed Morgan County officials.
- Residents voiced concerns about possible well-water contamination, light pollution, and disruption of wildlife and farmland.
- A released site plan shows Rivian shifting the plant away from wetlands and agreeing to limit light pollution in response to concerns.
- Opposition has become entangled in politics with former U.S. Sen. David Perdue challenging Governor Kemp in the May 24 Republican primary.
- Perdue emphasized the role of liberal billionaire George Soros in his opposition.
- Soros bought $2 billion worth of Rivian shares around the time the company chose Georgia but owns only 2% of the company.
- There is no evidence that Soros influenced the plant location decision.
- Perdue stated to think about how many small businesses in Georgia could be helped for this kind of money instead of padding George Soros’ pockets.
- Perdue also said Kemp gave away the farm to a woke corporation for something locals do not want, leaving hardworking Georgians footing the bill.
- Governor Kemp reiterated his backing for Rivian during a Sunday debate.
- Kemp stated he supports 7,500 great-paying jobs going to rural Georgia to an automobile manufacturing facility and will always be for that.
- Local governments agreed to $700 million in property tax breaks for the project.
- Rivian plans to make more than $300 million in payments in lieu of taxes over 25 years beginning in 2023.
- The state will spend $200 million to buy the site, grade it, build road improvements including a new Interstate 20 interchange, and extend utilities.
- Georgia will spend $62.5 million to build a dedicated training center for the plant.
- Georgia projected spending $27 million on providing job training.
- The incentive package includes a $200 million income tax credit calculated at $5,250 per job over five years.
- If Rivian does not owe that much state corporate income tax, Georgia will give personal income taxes collected from workers instead.
- Sales tax exemptions are estimated to save Rivian $175 million on machinery and $105 million on construction materials.
- Kia received more than $450 million in incentives for its plant in West Point, southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia promised SK Innovation $300 million in incentives for a $2.6 billion, 2,600-worker battery plant northeast of Atlanta.
- Wilson stated the state has strong protections to claw back money if Rivian falls below 80% of its promised investment or employment.
- The state touts a projected $420 million annual payroll from the plant.
- An analysis shows there will be 8,000 more jobs created elsewhere in the state with a total economic impact of more than $7 billion.
- LeRoy stated the incentives will cost $200,000 per job and that state and local governments will never collect enough increased taxes to cover it.
- LeRoy stated the state can never break even on the subsidies.
- LeRoy stated there is no way the average worker in this place is going to pay $200,000 more in state and local taxes.
- Rivian currently plans two consumer models: the R1T pickup with a base price of $67,500 and the R1S SUV with a base price of $70,000.
- Amazon owns 18% of Rivian.
- Amazon has ordered 100,000 delivery vans from Rivian, launching the company into commercial vehicles.
- Rivian raised $11.9 billion through a stock offering on Nov. 10 to finance the new plant.
- Rivian shares have fallen 70% since the stock offering as investors worry about production delays.
