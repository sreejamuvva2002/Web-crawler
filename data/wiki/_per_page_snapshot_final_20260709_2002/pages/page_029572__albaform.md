# Albaform

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Albaform – An American Story in the Making | Embassy of the Czech Republic in Washington, D.C.](https://mzv.gov.cz/washington/en/economy_trade/news/albaform_an_american_story_in_the_making.html)  ·  `page_029572`

## Facts

- Since 2013, the Czech company Albaform has grown to almost 70 employees in the US state of Georgia.
- Thanks to orders from BMW and others, Albaform is investing $1.7 million into its development.
- Albaform is a family-owned and operated company that has become increasingly successful over time.
- Albaform produces a wide range of wire-forming components and custom wire frames for the automotive industry, primarily for use in car seats.
- The company operates an American branch located in Flowery Branch, Georgia.
- Albaform secured three new contracts for wire frames for the American factories of BMW, Daimler, and Volkswagen.
- The company is investing $1.7 million into production.
- Albaform will create 15 new jobs to add to its current 51 employees.
- Albaform promotes the good name of the Czech Republic by presenting itself as a Czech company.
- The company has 8 experts stationed in Georgia who are based at its parent company's headquarters in Ladná near Břeclav in the Czech Republic.
- Tomáš Hart, Head of the Economic Department at the Czech Embassy, stated that "The story of Albaform is really the American dream."
- Tomáš Hart noted that he has been in contact with the Vintrlik family since they began their business and was happy to help them along the way.
- The Vintrlik family is involved with the Czech school in Atlanta.
- Last year in October, the Vintrlik family assisted the Czech Embassy and CzechInvest in organizing a trade mission from the Czech automotive industry to South Carolina and Georgia.
- The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Czech Republic financially supported the trade mission visit as part of its economic diplomacy.
- Honorary Consul of the Czech Republic for North and South Carolina Petr Vasicko and Honorary Consul of the Czech Republic for Georgia George Novak assisted in organizing the trade mission.
- Tomáš Hart noted that "Successful in business, actively involved in the community and supportive of other ventures – Albaform has really shown how a middle sized Czech company can make it on the competitive American market."
