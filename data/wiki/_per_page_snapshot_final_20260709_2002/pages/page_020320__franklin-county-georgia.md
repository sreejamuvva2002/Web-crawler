# Franklin County, Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-31

**Source:** [Franklin County | Leveraging Growth - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/01/31/franklin-county-leveraging-growth/)  ·  `page_020320`

## Facts

- Franklin County Industrial Building Authority (IBA) executive director Tonya Powers identifies manufacturing companies, particularly in the automotive sector, as top prospects for future industrial growth in the county.
- An automotive parts manufacturing company is investing $29 million and creating 60 new jobs in Franklin County's industrial park.
- Franklin County has a pipeline of prospects that are suppliers to SK Battery, which is located nearby.
- The IBA purchased a 93-acre site on rail for a new industrial park capable of supporting a 1 million-square-foot building with combined manufacturing and warehousing components.
- The Hartwell Railroad connects to CSX and Norfolk Southern railroads and runs directly across the front of the new 93-acre industrial site.
- Sheet metal manufacturer Alliance Supply and Piping was sold a 20-acre site at Gerrard Road Industrial Park for a new product line, creating at least 30 jobs.
- Carry-On Trailers is adding a larger facility in the Central Franklin Industrial Park as one of the area's largest employers.
- Fox Factory manufactures performance products for ATVs, snowmobiles, and motorcycles in Franklin Springs and operates as a billion-dollar business.
- Rocky Ridge custom-built trucks are manufactured in Franklin Springs.
- Atlanta Rod and Manufacturing employs 100 people at its 200,000-square-foot Franklin County headquarters, producing 150,000 pounds of bolts daily (nearly 45 million annually).
- Atlanta Rod and Manufacturing successfully lobbied the EPA to sunset a waiver allowing foreign fasteners in federally funded projects, securing domestic steel requirements for water infrastructure.
- Franklin Springs mayor Levy "Lee" Moore is purchasing close to 300 acres for industrial use to attract technology-based and agriculture-based industry.
- Emmanuel College (now Emmanuel University) serves as a local workforce pipeline, with new hires from the school and UGA supporting regional business growth.
- Lavonia remains the commercial and industrial hub of Franklin County due to historical infrastructure foresight by previous administrations.
- The county is building a 3 million-gallon-a-day wastewater treatment facility costing more than $30 million to support incoming industrial growth over the next 20 years.
