# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yamaha saves Newnan High Marching Band Season – Winters Media](https://www.wintersmedia.net/yamaha-saves-newnan-high-marching-band-season/)  ·  `page_015753`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) is a manufacturing facility located in Newnan, Georgia.
- YMMC assembles recreational vehicles like WaveRunners, Side-by-Sides, ATVs and golf carts.
- On Friday, March 26 an EF4 tornado struck Newnan with a path that passed right over Newnan High School.
- Dr. Joshua Boyd, Director of Bands, stated the tornado leveled their building, deemed it a total loss, and they lost every instrument, uniform, and piece of band history.
- The roof shifted and water poured in for three weeks, leaving woodwind instruments sitting in water to rot.
- Band students volunteered to help clear debris for the community; 30 kids showed up initially, and all 130 showed up three days later.
- A tornado-impacted student told Dr. Boyd that band was critical emotional support because they had lost their home and belongings.
- YMMC local leadership connected Newnan High School's band to Yamaha Corporation of America (YCA), based in Southern California near Yamaha Motor's Corporate Headquarters.
- Dr. Boyd aimed to get the Marching Band back up and running by fall to help students deal with the emotional roller coaster caused by the tornado.
- Over several weeks, YMMC staff worked in unison with a YCA team to develop a plan to assist the band for the fall opening of school and marching season.
- David Jewell, Partnerships and Alliances Manager for YCA, immediately asked what his company could do to help upon hearing about the tornado destruction in Newnan.
- Insurance for the school was not yet settled after the March storm, and instrument ordering typically requires a six-month lead time due to complex manufacturing and international shipping.
- Yamaha acquired and loaned marching brass, low reeds, and marching drums, coordinating inventory from across the country to arrive together at YMMC in Newnan because packages could not be received at the high school yet.
- The entire leadership team from YMMC personally delivered the instruments and unboxed them with the band.
- Band member Diego Sanchez described the delivery as hopeful, encouraging, and exciting, comparing unboxing to Christmas day.
- YMMC President Takahiro Imanishi helped with the unboxing and stated that 'Two Yamahas, One Passion' reflects how the two companies work together to make passion for music and memories possible.
- Dr. Boyd observed instrument division staff collaborating with ATV manufacturing staff to secure the band's return to the field.
- Approximately 60% of YMMC staff live in Coweta County, with many being Newnan High School graduates or having children who attended.
- YMMC is Coweta County's largest private employer and continues to grow as demand for products is high.
