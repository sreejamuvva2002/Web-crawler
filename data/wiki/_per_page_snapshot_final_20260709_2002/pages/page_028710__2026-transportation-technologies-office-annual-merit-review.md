# 2026 Transportation Technologies Office Annual Merit Review

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-02

**Source:** [2026 Transportation Technologies Office Annual Merit Review | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/events/2026-transportation-technologies-office-annual-merit-review)  ·  `page_028710`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy Transportation Technologies Office (formerly the Vehicle Technologies Office) held its 2026 Annual Merit Review (AMR) on June 1-4, 2026.
- The event was held at the Hyatt Regency Crystal City hotel in Arlington, Virginia.
- The purpose of the AMR is to evaluate TTO-funded projects for their contributions to DOE’s mission and goals.
- During the TTO AMR, advanced vehicle technologies projects funded by TTO are presented and reviewed for their merit.
- The event provides an opportunity for lab experts, industry stakeholders, and DOE staff to meet and exchange ideas and perspectives on related technologies.
- Reviewers come from a variety of backgrounds, including current and former members of the vehicles industry, academia, national laboratories, and government.
- Each reviewer evaluates a set of projects based on how much they contribute to or advance the Department of Energy’s missions and goals.
- Reviewers consider each project’s breadth, depth, appropriateness, accomplishments, and potential.
- The final reviews are described in TTO’s Annual Merit Review Results Reports.
- Presentations from 2009–2025 are available in the Merit Review Presentations Database.
- The 2025 TTO Annual Merit Review plenary session was held on June 2, 2025.
- Invited speakers and TTO staff set the stage for the AMR technical presentations.
- Reports from 2008-2025 and other subprogram reports are available on the Annual Progress Reports page.
