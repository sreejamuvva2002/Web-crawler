# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. Department of Energy Selects 11 Projects to Advance Domestic Manufacturing of Next-Generation Batteries | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/us-department-energy-selects-11-projects-advance-domestic-manufacturing-next-generation)  ·  `page_004448`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced an investment of $25 million across 11 projects to advance materials, processes, machines, and equipment for domestic manufacturing of next-generation batteries.
- The announcement was made on December 19, 2024, in Washington, D.C.
- These projects will advance platform technologies upon which battery manufacturing capabilities can be built, enabling flexible, scalable, and highly controllable battery manufacturing processes.
- Batteries are critical for next-generation technologies that will enhance energy affordability and increase America’s overall energy security and independence.
- For grid storage, these innovations will advance the resilience of homes and businesses, the electrification of the transportation sector, and the success of the domestic industrial manufacturing sector.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated that under the Biden-Harris Administration, a foundation has been built to keep momentum growing into the next generation of battery innovation.
- Granholm noted that the announced projects build on this success, enhancing national security while delivering jobs for American workers for decades more to come.
- Since President Biden took office, companies have announced more than $140 billion in investments in battery and critical mineral supply chains.
- DOE recently announced over $3 billion for selected projects to boost the domestic production of advanced batteries and battery materials nationwide.
- Those previously selected projects will retrofit, expand, and build new domestic facilities for battery-grade processed critical minerals, battery components, battery manufacturing, and recycling.
- The next-generation batteries projects are managed by DOE’s Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO).
- Subtopic 1 focuses on advanced processes and/or high-performance processing machines for low cost, large-scale, sustainable, commercial manufacture of sodium-ion batteries.
- AM Batteries, Inc. (Billerica, Massachusetts) received $2.8 million under Subtopic 1.
- Argonne National Laboratory (Lemont, Illinois) received $1.5 million under Subtopic 1.
- Clean Republic SODO LLC d/b/a Dakota Lithium Materials (Seattle, Washington) received $2 million under Subtopic 1.
- Subtopic 2 focuses on design and manufacturing of flow battery membranes, as well as system design and manufacturing for scale-up of flow battery production and cost-effective integration of flow battery systems.
- Quino Energy, Inc. (San Leandro California) received $2.6 million under Subtopic 2.
- Arkema, Inc. (King of Prussia, Pennsylvania) received $2.1 million under Subtopic 2.
- University of Akron (Akron, Ohio) received $1.6 million under Subtopic 2.
- Subtopic 3 focuses on scalable manufacturing processes and equipment for nanolayered films.
- University of Maryland (College Park, Maryland) received $2.6 million under Subtopic 3.
- Illinois Institute of Technology / Spraying System Co. (Chicago, Illinois / Glendale Heights, Illinois) received $2.6 million under Subtopic 3.
- Smart Manufacturing Platforms for Battery Production emphasizes development of broadly applicable smart manufacturing platforms that can be leveraged to improve the production of a variety of battery technologies.
- Charge CCCV (Vestal, New York) received $2.6 million under Smart Manufacturing Platforms.
- American Lithium Energy Corp. (Carlsbad, California) received $2.6 million under Smart Manufacturing Platforms.
- Titan Advanced Energy Solutions (Salem, Massachusetts) received $2.6 million under Smart Manufacturing Platforms.
- Selection for award negotiations is not a commitment by DOE to issue an award or provide funding.
- Before funding is issued, DOE and the applicants will undergo a negotiation process, and DOE may cancel negotiations and rescind the selection for any reason during that time.
- Award amounts are rounded and subject to change pending negotiations.
