# Reliance New Energy Battery Limited

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-17

**Source:** [Reliance Secures 10 GWh Battery Capacity Under PLI Scheme](https://currentaffairs.adda247.com/reliance-secures-10-gwh-battery-capacity-under-pli-scheme/)  ·  `page_060376`

## Facts

- Reliance New Energy Battery Limited, a subsidiary of Reliance Industries Limited, secured a 10 gigawatt-hour (GWh) capacity under the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Advanced Chemistry Cell (ACC) Battery Storage.
- The Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI) signed a Programme Agreement with the company on February 17, 2025.
- The PLI ACC Scheme was approved by the Indian government in May 2021 with a financial outlay of ₹18,100 crore.
- The scheme aims to establish a total manufacturing capacity of 50 GWh for advanced chemistry cells essential for electric vehicles and renewable energy storage.
- The scheme is technology-agnostic, allowing companies to use different technologies to set up battery manufacturing plants.
- The first phase of the scheme in March 2022 awarded 30 GWh capacity to three companies, with agreements signed in July 2022.
- With this new allocation to Reliance, the total awarded capacity under the scheme now stands at 40 GWh.
- In January 2024, MHI invited bids for the remaining 20 GWh capacity under the scheme.
- Seven companies submitted proposals for a total of 70 GWh: ACME Cleantech Solutions, Amara Raja Advanced Cell Technologies, Anvi Power Industries, JSW Neo Energy, Lucas TVS, Reliance Industries, and Waaree Energies.
- After evaluation, Reliance emerged as the successful bidder for 10 GWh capacity, while the remaining bidders were placed on a waiting list.
- The company has been investing in clean energy and battery storage solutions as part of its broader renewable energy strategy.
- The Indian government announced exemptions on 35 additional capital goods related to EV battery manufacturing in the Union Budget for FY2025-26.
- Only 10 GWh capacity remains to be allocated under the scheme as India moves closer to achieving its 50 GWh target.
