# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Wabash expands southeast presence with new Atlanta service center By Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/wabash-expands-southeast-presence-with-new-atlanta-service-center-93CH-4256423)  ·  `page_014451`

## Facts

- Wabash announced it has strengthened its presence in the Southeast with three strategic moves in the Atlanta region.
- The company opened a new Parts & Service center in the Atlanta area that began operations on September 18.
- The facility provides parts, service, and truck body upfitting services.
- Wabash describes the facility's location as strategic within the Southeast’s trucking corridor.
- The center will offer Wabash’s Ready-to-Mount truck bodies for dry freight and platform applications.
- Dave Hill, vice president of Parts & Services at Wabash, stated that the Atlanta expansions mean faster turnaround, more coverage, and better reliability for fleets operating across Georgia, Alabama, South Carolina, and Tennessee.
- The Atlanta service center joins existing Wabash facilities in California, Florida, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Texas, with additional locations planned.
- Fleetco, which has worked with Wabash for over 14 years, will now represent the company for trailer sales in the greater Atlanta market.
- The company’s Preferred Partner Network grew with the addition of three CS Truck and Trailer locations in Georgia.
- These three Georgia locations provide authorized third-party dealership services for maintenance and Wabash Genuine Parts.
- Wabash designs and manufactures products including dry and refrigerated trailers, truck bodies, platform trailers, and tank trailers.
- Wabash announced a new partnership with Global Tank to expand its tank trailer network across eight new states, including Kansas and Missouri.
- Trailer orders increased 18% year-over-year in August, though analysts cautioned against overinterpreting the data.
