# S&P Global Mobility

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-02

**Source:** [May 2026 US auto sales remain relatively steady in current environment | Mobility Global](https://www.spglobal.com/automotive-insights/en/blogs/2025/02/us-auto-sales-2025)  ·  `page_017232`

## Facts

- May 2026 US auto sales are projected to reach approximately 1.44 million units, according to S&P Global Mobility.
- This translates to an estimated sales pace of 15.8 million units (seasonally adjusted annual rate: SAAR), down slightly from the March (16.2M) and April (15.9M) readings but above the year-ago result (May 2025 – 15.6M) for the first time in seven months.
- S&P Global Mobility projects US auto sales volumes to reach 15.8 million units in 2026, representing an estimated decline of more than 3% from the 2025 level of 16.38 million units.
- The 15.8-million-unit setting is slightly downgraded from previous expectations.
- Chris Hopson, manager of North American light vehicle sales forecasting for S&P Global Mobility, stated that auto sales remain steady and solid in wake of swirling geopolitical and new vehicle affordability conditions.
- Sales pace over the past three months has remained relatively constant, with underlying movement towards more fuel-efficient vehicles, specifically hybrid electric vehicles (HEV) as gasoline prices remain high, while consumer spending retrenchment signals are still being watched.
- A notable downshift for BEV sales and market share was expected through the first half of 2026 as automakers and consumers adjust to post-incentive conditions.
- Rising fuel prices since March have renewed interest in fuel efficient vehicles.
- BEV share of new sales in March and April has not reflected a meaningful advance, though BEV sales are up from immediate post-incentive expiration results realized from September 25-February 26 but remain down from year-ago levels.
- OEM adjustments regarding inventory and strategy assessments could be playing a role in new vehicle BEV share, while reports of a rise in BEV activity in the used vehicle sector may limit stronger new BEV sales advances despite surging fuel prices.
- The roll out of new BEV products over the course of 2026 should provide additional momentum for BEV sales alongside uncertainty on how high and long fuel prices will remain.
- The 2026 US auto sales market will continue to contend with affordability pressures and the impact of shifting EV policies, with S&P Global Mobility monitoring the evolving environment as manufacturers and consumers adapt to changing incentive landscapes.
