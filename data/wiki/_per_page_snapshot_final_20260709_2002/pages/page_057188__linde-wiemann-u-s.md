# Linde + Wiemann U.S.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Linde Wiemann Manufacturing Expansion | Automotive Production](https://www.mcmillanpazdansmith.com/project/linde-wiemann-u-s-manufacturing-facilty-phases-i-ii/)  ·  `page_057188`

## Facts

- Linde + Wiemann is a German-based automotive supplier that established and expanded its US manufacturing operations through a multi-phase facility designed to support long-term growth.
- The project utilized a design-build partnership approach, collaborating closely with the owner, plant manager, and contractors to align scope, budget, and schedule.
- Phase 1 delivered 44,300 SF of production, warehouse, and distribution space.
- Phase 1 included a 7,000 SF two-story office building.
- The facility was designed to support a larger multi-phase buildout totaling approximately 290,000 SF.
- The total buildout plan includes a future freestanding office building.
- Phase 2 expanded and renovated an existing 51,333 SF facility to support Linde + Wiemann’s growing manufacturing operations.
- Phase 2 increased the building size to more than 138,000 SF.
- The expansion created additional space for metal stamping, welding, storage, and distribution.
- Phase 2 introduced new manufacturing and warehouse areas while upgrading office and utility spaces to better support daily operations.
- A mezzanine level was added for maintenance storage during Phase 2.
- Future expansions are planned for the site.
- The design and construction were carefully coordinated to prevent disruptions to Phase 1 operations during future expansions.
- The design-build team maintained a highly coordinated process from concept through construction, enabling rapid decision-making and minimizing delays.
- The initial phase was completed within a seven-month construction schedule.
- The project demonstrated the team’s ability to deliver complex industrial facilities on time and within budget.
