# Georgia Manufacturing Alliance

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-01

**Source:** [Georgia Manufacturing Alliance looks forward to a 2024 packed with opportunities - Hybrid Cars News Today - EIN Presswire](https://hybridcars.einnews.com/pr_news/685207147/georgia-manufacturing-alliance-looks-forward-to-a-2024-packed-with-opportunities)  ·  `page_010237`

## Facts

- Georgia Manufacturing Alliance closed out 2023 with a record number of networking opportunities, educational events and plant tours for its members.
- More than 50 such experiences were offered throughout the state for GMA members in 2023 alone.
- Founder and CEO Jason Moss started the Georgia Manufacturing Alliance in 2008 to grow the state's manufacturing community and keep jobs in the state.
- Two business-to-business networking events were held in January, along with a plant tour of Iron Age Office that sold out and grew a waiting list as the date approached.
- A plant tour of Aristotle Metal Works is scheduled for February 12.
- The Georgia Manufacturing Alliance will celebrate its 16th anniversary at Creative Comforts in Athens on February 20.
- An educational panel discussion on the challenges and opportunities for local sourcing will be held on February 22.
- GMA members are scheduled to tour the Kia Georgia plant in West Point in March.
- A tour of the Gulf Stream plant in Savannah is planned for May.
- Twice-monthly networking and educational events are scheduled throughout the year for manufacturing professionals across the state.
- Jason Moss stated that Georgia’s manufacturing community is strong and positioned to grow even stronger.
- Moss cited Georgia's geographical location, climate, a major shipping port, airport, other transportation options, and a strong available workforce as factors making the state ideal for manufacturers.
- The GMA calendar will continue to grow as more plant tours and educational and networking events are planned.
