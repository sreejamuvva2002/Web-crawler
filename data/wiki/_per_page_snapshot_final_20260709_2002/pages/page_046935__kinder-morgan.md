# Kinder Morgan

**Source:** [Kinder Morgan Proposes 290-Mile Gas Pipeline Expansion Spanning Three States](https://pgjonline.com/news/2025/may/kinder-morgan-proposes-290-mile-gas-pipeline-expansion-spanning-three-states)  ·  `page_046935`

## Facts

- Kinder Morgan is planning a multibillion-dollar expansion of its South System 4 pipeline network.
- The proposal calls for the addition of 14 new pipeline segments totaling approximately 290 miles stretching across Georgia, South Carolina, and Alabama.
- The plan includes upgrades to 13 compressor stations.
- The plan includes the installation of four new metering facilities to manage and measure gas flow.
- Allen Fore, vice president of Kinder Morgan, stated the project is an expansion of an existing natural gas pipeline system operated in Georgia for over 50 years.
- The expansion is designed to increase natural gas supply for the region.
- The project is branded as the “South System 4 Expansion.”
- Kinder Morgan positioned the project as necessary to meet long-term energy demand, even for those who don’t directly use natural gas at home.
- Allen Fore noted that natural gas is a key source for generating electricity and likely powers lights for residents without direct gas usage.
- Some property owners in Jefferson County have expressed concern about how the project might interfere with future land use.
- In recent public forums, Kinder Morgan has emphasized its willingness to work with landowners to accommodate specific needs.
- If regulatory approvals are granted, construction could begin in the coming years.
- Operations are expected to start between 2028 and 2029.
