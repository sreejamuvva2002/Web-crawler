# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-24

**Source:** [German auto parts maker bringing 200 jobs to Whitfield County](https://newschannel9.com/news/local/german-autoparts-maker-bringing-200-jobs-to-whitfield-county)  ·  `page_020540`

## Facts

- GEDIA Automotive Group announced it is bringing 200 jobs to Whitfield County with the building of a manufacturing facility that focuses on parts for electric vehicles.
- GEDIA will invest $85 million in this project.
- The facility will deliver 200 jobs to the greater Dalton area.
- The site is located on a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified property in Whitfield County.
- GEDIA has been in business for over 100 years.
- GEDIA supplies lightweight structural automotive parts to Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Volvo, and several other world-renowned companies.
- The company will build and occupy a 180,000-square-foot facility on a GRAD Certified property in Carbondale Business Park.
- GEDIA is headquartered in Germany.
- This will mark the company’s first site in the Southeast.
- This will be the company’s second facility in the United States.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that GEDIA is a family-owned business and expressed confidence it would be pleased with its decision to join Georgia's growing electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that the state’s consistent investment in its Germany-based European office is paying off.
- GEDIA will use state-of-the-art press hardening, tempering, and automated welding processes to produce parts for electric vehicles.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the third quarter of 2021.
- GEDIA will offer opportunities for management and staff positions, highly skilled technicians, and production workers.
- Additional information about the project will be available by the beginning of the fourth quarter of 2020.
