# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2016-01-27

**Source:** [Georgia Electric Car Sales Drop ~90% In ~6 Months - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2016/01/27/georgia-driving-incentives-evs-another-murder-attempt-electric-cars/)  ·  `page_053933`

## Facts

- At one time, Georgia was leading the country in incentives for EVs.
- Approximately a year ago, Atlanta, Georgia was second in the nation in electric car use.
- New registrations of electric vehicles have fallen ~90% since the summer of 2015.
- State law provided those who bought or leased an EV a $5,000 tax credit.
- A Georgian with an EV could divide that credit over a two-year lease and recoup about $200 a month.
- In 2015, Georgia state lawmakers ended the tax incentive to find savings that offset a transportation spending bill.
- Don Francis is the coordinator of the federal Clean Cities program in the state.
- Tim Echols said the timing for approval of new tax incentives might be better in a few years.
- Manufacturers like Chevy and Tesla promise to develop electric cars that can drive far longer distances on a single charge.
- California's Charge Ahead program includes a $2,500 ZEV rebate and ZEV mandate.
- California aims to be emissions free by 2025.
