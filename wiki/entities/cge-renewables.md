# CGE Renewables

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Social Circle, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

CGE Renewables offers demand-response and load-management programs for commercial customers in Social Circle, GA, helping them reduce peak demand charges, earn incentive payments, and optionally pair with battery storage without disrupting operations.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides demand-response services that manage EV charging loads, flatten peak demand, and enable dual revenue streams for businesses with electric-vehicle fleets.

## Key facts

- CGE Renewables uses load monitoring, automated controls, and strategic scheduling to flatten peak demand without disrupting Social Circle operations.
- Utilities charge commercial customers for the highest 15-minute power draw in a billing period; CGE helps reduce that spike.
- Enrolled customers receive bill credits or direct payments of $50-$200 per demand-response event, typically 10-20 events per year lasting 1-4 hours.
- ISO capacity markets (PJM, NYISO, ISONE) pay businesses to commit load-reduction capacity in advance, providing a predictable annual revenue stream.
- Shifting flexible loads such as EV charging, pre-cooling, and batch processing to off-peak windows reduces both energy and demand costs automatically.
- Interruptible rates give permanent discounts on demand charges in exchange for allowing utilities to curtail load during emergencies.
- Proactive load management (staggering equipment startups, pre-cooling buildings) can achieve 20-40% demand reduction each month.
- Pairing battery storage with demand response creates a dual revenue stream: batteries charge off-peak and discharge during peak events.
- CGE provides end-to-end services including data analysis, enrollment paperwork, automated real-time controls, and monthly reporting.
- Manufacturers with variable loads can realize $30-$100K per year in combined demand savings and DR payments.
- Hotel HVAC systems can be pre-cooled to maintain guest comfort while participating in demand response events.
- Logistics facilities, refrigerated warehouses, and EV fleet charging stations are natural candidates for demand-response programs.
- School campuses can shift HVAC, lighting, and EV-charging loads to earn incentive payments that offset operating budgets.
