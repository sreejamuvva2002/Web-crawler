# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-02

**Source:** [Accelerated Investment in Aurubis’ Augusta Multi-metal Recycling Facility Strengthens Georgia’s Position in EV, Sustainability - Augusta CEO](https://augustaceo.com/features/2023/02/accelerated-investment-aurubis-augusta-multi-metal-recycling-facility-strengthens-georgias-position-ev-sustainability/)  ·  `page_017382`

## Facts

- An influx of ancillary companies is forming to support Georgia's booming electric mobility industry.
- German multi-metal provider and recycling company Aurubis is among the ancillary companies forming to support Georgia's electric mobility sector.
- Aurubis is one of the world’s leading recyclers of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials.
- Aurubis was drawn to Georgia for its pro-business environment, spirit of innovation, and focus on building out a world-class electric mobility value chain.
- Aurubis announced in November 2021 that it would build a recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta, Georgia.
- Leaders from the Georgia Department of Economic Development were at Aurubis headquarters in Hamburg, Germany, when the investment was announced.
- The announcement followed a series of meetings between Aurubis and local/state officials held on the home soil of each partner.
- In December 2022, Aurubis announced it would increase its investment total to €640 million.
- Aurubis projects the facility will double its throughput volume from 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year.
- The facility is expected to create 200 jobs.
- The December 2022 announcement marked the single-largest investment by a German company in Georgia.
- The Augusta facility is the first facility of its kind in the U.S.
- The Augusta facility is the first greenfield project in the U.S. for Aurubis.
- Aurubis plans to produce materials that can be used in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics valuable in the EV industry.
- Processing complex metal-bearing materials and returning them to use is essential for maintaining a sustainable battery materials industry.
- Not long after the December 2022 announcement, Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and state leaders traveled to Germany to celebrate Aurubis's commitment to Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia's ports system, highly skilled workforce, and pro-business environment attracted Aurubis to Augusta.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis, stated that today's high recycling demand cannot be met by existing facilities.
- Roland Harings stated that copper can be recycled without loss of quality or performance and is needed for cars, mobile phones, batteries, cables, and computers.
- Roland Harings said Aurubis's increased commitment to the U.S. will be a game-changer for the environment, manufacturers, and the transition to a renewable economy.
- Aurubis broke ground on the recycling plant in June 2022.
- The first phase of the facility is expected to come online in mid-2024.
- The expansion of the facility is expected to be completed in 2026.
- The company was able to quickly start work on the site due to its status as a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- GRAD certification is a proactive way for landowners and local developers to catalyze economic growth and increase speed to market for companies.
- Cal Wray, president of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, expressed excitement over the speed of construction and Aurubis's plans for further expansions.
- Cal Wray stated that Aurubis will provide great jobs and be a wonderful addition to the Augusta community.
- Aurubis's presence in Georgia indicates the state's status as a national leader in electric mobility and sustainable battery production.
- The project evidences a strong partnership between Georgia and Germany.
- Germany is one of Georgia’s top three investment partners.
- Germany was Georgia’s second-largest job-creating country for Foreign Direct Investment in FY22.
- At least 513 German facilities operate in Georgia, employing more than 25,000 Georgians.
- Georgia is celebrating its 50th year of continuous representation in Germany.
