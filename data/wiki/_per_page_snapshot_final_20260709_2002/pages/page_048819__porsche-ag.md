# Porsche AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-04

**Source:** [1,000 miles in the Porsche Taycan 4S Cross Turismo EV: The good and bad - AOL](https://www.aol.com/articles/1-000-miles-porsche-taycan-150022818.html)  ·  `page_048819`

## Facts

- 2025 Porsche Taycan 4S Cross Turismo was tested on a 1,000-mile road trip from New York City to Raleigh, North Carolina.
- The Taycan has an EPA-estimated range of around 280 miles, up from the prior generation's 220-230 mile range.
- Porsche has a deal with Electrify America that allows plug-and-charge compatibility without needing multiple apps.
- ChargePoint chargers in Bethesda, Maryland were found but did not offer fast enough speeds for the trip.
- An EVgo fast charger was used in a parking structure in Henderson, North Carolina, requiring credit card payment due to app connectivity issues.
- Tesla Superchargers are plentiful in the Raleigh/Chapel Hill area, and Porsche is part of the Tesla network, allowing access using a Porsche-supplied adapter.
- A Tesla charging session cost $43.79 for nearly 80 kWh of charge ($0.55/kWh).
- An Electrify America fast charger in Emporia, Virginia featured two plugs but only allowed one plug to be used at a time.
- The Taycan was originally released in 2019 and received an update in 2025 with new motors and an improved battery.
- The updated Taycan produces nearly 600 horsepower and 523 lb-ft of torque.
- The Cross Turismo body style features fender cladding and a ride height roughly 20 millimeters higher than the Sport Turismo, with additional lift capability via air suspension.
- Optional Porsche Active Ride system uses adaptive dampers to keep the vehicle level during braking, steering, and acceleration, and can raise the car when doors open for easier entry.
- The Taycan has a starting price of around $130,000.
