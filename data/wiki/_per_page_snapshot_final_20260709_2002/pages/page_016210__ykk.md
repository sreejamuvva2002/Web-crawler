# YKK

## Facts

- The Dublin City Council approved a memorandum of understanding with YKK to upgrade its Dublin plant.
- The Laurens County Commission approved a tax abatement for YKK’s planned $50 million renovation at its Dublin plant.
- County Administrator Bryan Rogers described the project as a “modernization” of the YKK plant that will include new technology and machinery and improvements to the heating-and-air conditioning system.
- Rogers stated, “It’s more of a renovation, not an expansion.”
- The tax abatement is included in a memorandum of understanding with the company that has already been approved by the Dublin City Council and has the backing of the Dublin-Laurens Development Authority.
- Under the agreement, YKK will receive a five-year tax break on the plant improvements.
- The company will pay no taxes in the first year of the abatement.
- Taxes will increase by 20 percent each subsequent year until reaching 100 percent in the sixth year.
- Rogers clarified that the tax abatement applies only to the renovated part of the plant.
- Commissioner Trae Kemp called the tax abatement for the $50 million investment a “no-brainer,” and the board passed the measure unanimously.
