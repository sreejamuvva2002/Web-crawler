# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-28

**Source:** [Copper foil manufacturer making Augusta its North American HQ](https://www.postandcourier.com/northaugusta/news/copper-foil-manufacturer-making-augusta-its-north-american-hq/article_d1c77258-0db8-11ed-93a1-0f97a3e9958f.html)  ·  `page_019290`

## Facts

- Japanese firm Nippon Denkai’s American subsidiary announced plans for a new copper foil facility and establishment of its North American headquarters at Augusta Corporate Park.
- In a joint announcement on July 20 with Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s office, Denkai America committed an initial $150 million to the project, with the total investment growing to $430 million.
- The project will create 250 jobs within five years.
- Parent company Nippon Denkai envisions the new Augusta facility’s manufacturing output tripling within those 5 years, from 10,500 tons to 31,500 tons.
- Denkai America is the only North American manufacturer of high-quality electrodeposited (ED) copper foil used in printed electronics.
- Denkai’s materials have been used in both the aerospace and defense industries but are now used in lithium-ion batteries powering the electric vehicle market.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated the investment would further strengthen the state's status as a leader in emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Gov. Kemp emphasized a focus on bringing quality jobs to Georgians and praised Georgia’s top-ranking pro-business environment.
- Construction on the 150-acre site is expected to begin in September, with the project completed and ready for operations by December 2024.
- Denkai originally announced in December (prior to this article) plans to build a facility adjacent to its existing plant in Camden, S.C., but chose the Augusta site due to future expandability and cost competitiveness.
- Nippon Denkai cited a key cost-competitive edge in electricity prices, local quality team members, and business-friendly local government support as reasons for choosing Augusta over Camden.
- Through the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Denkai secured a $1.85 million EDGE grant from the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
- EDGE grants are designed for cross-state expansion and relocation projects to help Georgia compete for economic investment that might otherwise go to neighboring states.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner for Georgia’s Department of Economic Development, stated Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem.
- Wilson noted Georgia is committed to harnessing electric mobility adoption to provide future jobs for Georgians currently employed in the automotive industry.
- Gov. Kemp announced that Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain intended to cut inefficiencies and reduce reliance on imported materials.
- Georgia is actively incentivizing EV market investment, including Hyundai’s May announcement for a $5.5 billion EV plant in Bryan County, located about 30 minutes west of Savannah.
- Steven Kendrick, chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, called Denkai America a "great corporate citizen" that will bring high-skilled jobs to Augusta and the Augusta Corporate Park.
- Denkai's settlement in Augusta Corporate Park follows less than a month after groundbreaking for German company Aurubis’ copper smelting and metals recycling plant at the same industrial park on Valencia Way.
- Aurubis’ plant is expected to generate 125 jobs and received local financial backing via a Richmond County judge's May authorization for up to $760 million in revenue bonds for its construction on 150 acres.
