# Internal Revenue Code Section 30D New Clean Vehicle Credit

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-04

**Source:** [30D New Clean Vehicle Credit | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/manufacturing/30d-new-clean-vehicle-credit)  ·  `page_003069`

## Facts

- The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (P.L. 119-21) or Working Families Tax Cut was enacted on July 4, 2025, and revised dates and provisions in the Internal Revenue Code affecting tax credits.
- Internal Revenue Code § 30D provides a credit of up to $7,500 to qualified buyers of new clean vehicles.
- To be eligible for the credit, the Qualified Manufacturer (QM) must meet requirements related to qualifying content of critical minerals and battery components (CMBC).
- Beginning in 2024, eligible vehicles cannot contain battery components manufactured or assembled by a Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC).
- Beginning in 2025, eligible vehicles also cannot contain applicable critical minerals extracted, processed, or recycled by a FEOC.
- In May 2024, DOE published its final interpretive guidance on the definition of a Foreign Entity of Concern.
- The Treasury Department concurrently released a final rule on clean vehicle tax credits, including 30D, in May 2024 that cross-references the DOE finalized FEOC interpretive guidance.
- DOE is supporting the IRS in conducting an upfront review to ensure vehicles are eligible for the § 30D credit.
- The upfront review ensures QMs are compliant with FEOC and CMBC requirements for the calendar year in which the QM anticipates submitting a periodic written report to the IRS.
- The qualified manufacturer must submit a Compliance Report to the DOE, including supporting documentation and calculations regarding qualifying content of battery components and applicable critical minerals, as well as associated constituent materials, contained in the clean vehicle battery.
- This Compliance Report will be used to establish a compliant battery ledger.
- The IRS will make a determination with respect to the submission, with analytical assistance from the DOE, and notify the qualified manufacturer of its determination.
- If a Qualified Manufacturer submits information to DOE by July 1 of the year prior to the calendar year for which the compliant battery ledger is being established, DOE and the IRS will complete their review and determination by October 31.
- If a Qualified Manufacturer submits information to DOE after July 1, the upfront review will be conducted on a rolling basis but may not be complete prior to the beginning of the calendar year.
- IRS Revenue Procedure 2023-38 and Revenue Procedure 2024-26 specify content requirements for submissions.
- The 30D Compliance Report Submission Portal (30D Portal) allows Qualified Manufacturers to submit the Annual Compliance Report to the Department of Energy for upfront review.
- This review covers FEOC and Critical Mineral and Battery Component (CMBC) compliance requirements.
- Qualified Manufacturers are encouraged but not required to use provided templates for their submissions.
- Available submission resources include the 2024 FEOC Compliance Report template, 2025 and beyond FEOC and CMBC Compliance Report template, Data sheet template, Data sheet template - Unlocked, Frequently Asked Questions (March 17, 2025), and the 30D Compliance Report Submission Portal User Guide (Revised August 2025).
- DOE hosted a virtual informational webinar for Qualified Manufacturers on Thursday, October 3, 2024, at 12:00 p.m. Eastern Time to provide comprehensive guidance on viewing IRS determinations through the portal.
- By providing data for the program, manufacturers voluntarily disclose proprietary data to determine eligibility of clean vehicles for IRC Section 30D tax credit purposes.
- Manufacturers represent and warrant that they are authorized to disclose this data per the Terms of Use.
- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), and supporting contractors will have access to the provided data.
- The data will be used by DOE, IRS, and contractors to evaluate compliance with FEOC requirements set forth in IRC Section 30D(d)(7).
- Compliance evaluation criteria include eligibility requirements, definitions for qualified manufacturer, description of the proposed supply chain, battery components, critical minerals, allocation-based accounting, and other factors described in IRS Section 30D guidance.
- Based on this data, the IRS will make a determination on clean vehicle eligibility for IRC Section 30D tax credit purposes with analytical assistance from DOE and notify the manufacturer.
- Provided data will be treated as confidential and proprietary to the fullest extent allowable under federal law.
- DOE and IRS reserve the right to disclose data if they believe in good faith it is appropriate or necessary to take precautions against liability, protect against fraudulent/abusive/unlawful activity, investigate/defend against third-party claims, comply with audit/inspection/copying requirements, or comply with open records laws.
- Data may also be disclosed in any other manner to which the manufacturer consents.
