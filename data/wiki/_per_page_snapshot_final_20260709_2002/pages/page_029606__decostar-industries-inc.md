# Decostar Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-10-27

**Source:** [Decostar Industries to Pay $38,500 to Settle EEOC Religious Discrimination Suit | U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission](https://www.eeoc.gov/newsroom/decostar-industries-pay-38500-settle-eeoc-religious-discrimination-suit)  ·  `page_029606`

## Facts

- Decostar Industries, Inc. is a manufacturer and supplier of automotive parts based in Carrollton, Ga.
- The company will pay $38,500 to settle a religious discrimination lawsuit filed by the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC).
- An employee made numerous requests to be excused from mandatory overtime on Saturdays due to religious beliefs prohibiting work during the Sabbath.
- Decostar initially approved the accommodation request until January 2014, when a new supervisor took over and repeatedly denied ongoing accommodation requests.
- The company discharged the employee on Oct. 27, 2014, after she refused to violate her religious beliefs.
- A consent decree settling the lawsuit requires Decostar to adopt and implement a new policy for employees to request religious accommodations.
- The decree mandates that the company provide annual equal employment opportunity training to its managers.
- The two-year decree requires Decostar to post a notice about the lawsuit to employees and provide periodic reporting to the EEOC regarding disability discrimination complaints.
- Antonette Sewell, regional attorney for the EEOC's Atlanta District Office, stated that forcing workers to choose between livelihood and religion is unconscionable and unlawful.
