# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-21

**Source:** [Aurubis Invests € 530 Million --  Trade Fair](https://www.metec-tradefair.com/en/Media_News/News/Aurubis_Invests_€_530_Million)  ·  `page_017347`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG's Supervisory Board resolved further capital expenditure of about €530 million to double processing capacity at its Richmond recycling site in Georgia, US.
- About €90 million in additional capital expenditure adjustments for infrastructure requirements and inflation have been approved specifically for the Richmond plant.
- The Richmond plant will receive a second recycling module with a capital expenditure volume of about €250 million.
- The expansion stage at the Richmond site is scheduled to start production in 2026.
- The previous throughput volume of 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials will be raised to 180,000 tons due to the doubling of the expansion stage.
- The expansion follows the highest environmental standards and enables environmentally friendly processing of the input materials.
- Aurubis is creating about 80 new jobs in Richmond as part of the expansion.
- After the completion of both recycling modules, the Richmond site will employ about 200 people.
- Starting in the fiscal year of 2025/26, Aurubis expects the expansion stage to make an additional EBITDA contribution of about €90 million per year.
- The Richmond plant will profit from low electricity and gas prices as a locational benefit.
- Construction work for the first construction stage has already started at the Richmond site.
- Combined with the second expansion stage, Aurubis expects an EBITDA contribution of about €170 million from the fiscal year of 2026/27 with a total investment volume of €640 million for the Richmond project.
- The rapidly growing availability of complex recycling materials in the USA, which lies at more than 5 percent per year, creates favorable conditions for expanding Aurubis' market position and supports the Georgia expansion.
- US investments will be highly profitable due to high synergies, low requirements of additional space, and the use of existing infrastructure.
- Roland Harings, Chairman of the Aurubis Executive Board, stated that Aurubis is accelerating growth in recycling with the next expansion stage of its US plant and strengthening its core business by investing in innovative metallurgy.
- Aurubis plans to finance the planned projects largely from current cash flow without needing a capital increase.
