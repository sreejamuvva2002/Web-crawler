# Murata Manufacturing Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Murata banks on ceramic technology - EE Times](https://www.eetimes.com/murata-banks-on-ceramic-technology/)  ·  `page_047090`

## Facts

- Murata Manufacturing Co. Ltd. was founded in 1940 and follows a strategy of organic growth using research and development to build capacitors, filters, resistors, microwave parts, and other components.
- The company has become a multibillion-dollar global supplier.
- A prolonged industry downturn caused Murata's revenue and earnings to tumble, forcing workforce trimming and lower capital investment.
- Increasing OEM pressure forced Murata to continue reducing commodity part prices.
- The Kyoto, Japan-based company is leveraging core ceramic expertise to develop higher-margin products such as Bluetooth modules and higher-performance capacitors for emerging applications.
- The company is increasing its presence in China.
- John Denslinger serves as senior vice president of Murata Electronics North America Inc., located in Smyrna, Ga.
- Murata Electronics North America Inc. is one of the company's 47 wholly owned subsidiaries across Asia, Europe, and North and South America.
- Murata remains the world's leading manufacturer of multilayer ceramic capacitors (MLCCs), accounting for more than 20% of the global market by revenue.
- The company considers itself a market leader in SAW filters with a 20% share and dielectric filters with a 40% share.
- Murata remains vertically integrated, forming its own ceramic materials and building its own machines instead of outsourcing.
- The company consistently reinvests 5% to 8% of its revenue in research and development.
- Murata's last notable acquisition occurred in 1981.
- For the fiscal year ended March 31, 2002, Murata's revenue was $2.97 billion, down 32.4% from the previous year, with net income of $394 million, down 66%.
- The company projects revenue and earnings for fiscal 2002 to be roughly flat with fiscal 2001.
- Capital investment for fiscal 2002 is projected at $200 million, down from $260 million in fiscal 2001.
- Denslinger expects Murata's revenue to slowly improve over the next year but cites price erosion in commodity parts as the main hurdle to recovery.
- A lot of OEMs now negotiate price contracts on a quarterly rather than yearly basis.
- Capacitors have experienced 12 consecutive quarters of price reductions.
- Denslinger expects prices to erode 10% in the first half of 2003.
- A weak end market for communications products, Murata's largest market segment, saw its percentage of revenue slide to 37% in fiscal 2001 from 41% in fiscal 2000.
- Murata remains committed to the communications sector long-term and expects to supply EMC filters, thermistors, and DC/DC converters to OEMs building broadband, home networking, and digital access equipment.
- Capacitors account for 40% of Murata's revenue.
- The company is expanding its capacitor offerings to include low-equivalent-series resistance and capacitance values exceeding 1 microfarad.
- Murata hopes to target the RF market and applications in which tantalum capacitors now dominate.
- The company is leveraging low-temperature co-fired ceramic technology to manufacture integrated Bluetooth modules.
- Murata announced a newest Bluetooth module measuring 9.3 x 7.8 x 1.8mm, which is 25% smaller than the previous version introduced in August.
- The Bluetooth module for wireless handset and PDA applications sells for $15 to $20, which is two to three times more than component-based solutions from suppliers like Texas Instruments Inc.
- Murata's group product manager Scott Klettke stated the higher price is justified because the module speeds time-to-market for designers of Bluetooth products.
- Revenue percentage from China was 14% in fiscal 2001 and is expected to increase as more OEM and EMS customers locate there.
- Last month, the company began mass production of capacitors, isolators, and thermistors at a plant in Wuxi.
- Murata's other China plants are located in Beijing, Suzhou, and Shenzhen.
