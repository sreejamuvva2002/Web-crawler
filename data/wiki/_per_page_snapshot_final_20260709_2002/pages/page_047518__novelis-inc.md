# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-11

**Source:** [Novelis Inc. 10-Q Quarterly Report February 2026](https://last10k.com/sec-filings/1304280/0001304280-26-000008.htm)  ·  `page_047518`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is a leading sustainable aluminum solutions provider and the world leader in aluminum rolling and recycling.
- Novelis reported a net loss attributable to common shareholders of $160 million for the third quarter of fiscal year 2026, compared to a net income of $110 million in the prior year.
- The Q3 FY2026 net loss was significantly impacted by fires at the Oswego, US plant that occurred in September and November.
- Production interruptions at the Oswego facility caused rolled product shipments to be approximately 72 kilotonnes lower than expected.
- The Oswego production disruptions resulted in an estimated negative pre-tax $54 million impact on Adjusted EBITDA and Net loss.
- Novelis recorded an additional $327 million in pre-tax losses directly related to the Oswego fires.
- Adjusted EBITDA for Q3 FY2026 was $348 million, representing a 5% year-over-year decrease.
- The decline in Adjusted EBITDA was impacted by an estimated negative $54 million from the Oswego fires and $34 million from tariffs.
- Rolled product shipments totaled 809 kilotonnes in Q3 FY2026, down 11% year-over-year.
- Adjusted EBITDA per tonne shipped reached $430 in Q3 FY2026, an increase of 6% year-over-year.
- Novelis is recovering from the Oswego production disruption and anticipates restarting the hot mill in late Q2 calendar year 2026.
- Novelis received a $750 million equity contribution from its common shareholder in December.
- Steve Fisher, president and CEO of Novelis Inc., stated that underlying performance remains strong despite short-term capacity constraints due to the Oswego disruption.
- The company's resilient business model is supported by strategic investments in new capacity, effective cost management initiatives, and favorable market conditions, particularly in beverage packaging which is its largest product end market.
- Cost efficiencies and favorable recycling benefits contributed to a 6% year-over-year increase in Adjusted EBITDA per tonne to $430 in the third quarter.
- Net sales for Q3 FY2026 increased 3% versus the prior year period to $4.2 billion, primarily driven by higher average aluminum prices.
- Higher net sales were partially offset by an 11% decrease in total rolled product shipments to 809 kilotonnes.
- Lower shipments to the automotive, beverage packaging, and specialties markets were primarily driven by the estimated 72 kilotonne negative shipment impact from the Oswego production disruption.
- Higher aerospace shipments partially offset the lower shipments to other end markets.
- The decrease in net income was also due to unrealized losses on derivatives in the current year compared to gains in the prior year, partially offset by favorable metal price lag from rising average local market aluminum premiums.
- Adjusted EBITDA per tonne increased despite absorbing tariffs and the impact of the Oswego fires, highlighting the robustness of the core business according to management.
- Net cash used in operating activities was an outflow of $90 million for the first nine months of fiscal year 2026, compared to a net cash inflow of $263 million in the prior year period.
- The operating cash flow decrease was largely related to impacts from the Oswego fires.
- Adjusted free cash flow was an outflow of $1,641 million for the first nine months of fiscal year 2026, compared to an outflow of $915 million in the prior year period.
- The adjusted free cash flow decrease was impacted by an estimated negative $485 million related to the Oswego fires.
- Total capital expenditures increased by 34% to $1,577 million for the first nine months of fiscal year 2026.
- Capital expenditures were mainly allocated to strategic investments in new capacity under construction.
- A notable strategic investment is a greenfield rolling and recycling plant located in Bay Minette, Alabama.
