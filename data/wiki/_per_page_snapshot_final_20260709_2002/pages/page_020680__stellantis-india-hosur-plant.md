# Stellantis India Hosur Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-18

**Source:** [Stellantis’ Hosur plant emerges as a global powertrain hub, ETManufacturing](https://manufacturing.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/automotive/stellantis-hosur-plant-emerges-as-a-global-powertrain-hub-gears-up-for-hydrogen-tech/125413308)  ·  `page_020680`

## Facts

- Stellantis India’s CEO and MD is Shailesh Hazela.
- The company's next 10 years will be shaped by flexible manufacturing, localisation, and future-ready platforms.
- Hydrogen is already part of Stellantis India’s present-day planning.
- Stellantis has started working on a hydrogen pilot project in Europe.
- If the hydrogen product meets Indian requirements, it can be manufactured at the Hosur plant.
- The hydrogen solution could fit into Citroën or Jeep brands where customers are looking for that solution.
- India needs everything at the right value, which matters for adoption.
- Hydrogen is considered the next chapter in Stellantis’ multi-energy strategy, not a disruption.
- Stellantis platforms must be adaptive, future-proof, multi-energy, and scalable to define the next decade.
- The Hosur plant has flexible production lines capable of making multiple products on the same line.
- In Thiruvallur, all four Citroën products are manufactured on one flexible line.
- Stellantis often stretches existing production line capabilities instead of immediately building new ones.
- The company sometimes increases worker hours and optimizes lines to get more production out.
- It is too early to comment on new lines or products beyond the recently launched three X-series.
- Stellantis India operates a multi-city engineering ecosystem with over 4,000 engineers across Chennai, Pune, Hyderabad, and Bengaluru.
- These engineers work on global software, cloud systems, connectivity layers, and platform programmes.
- The teams work on programmes across brands, platforms, and continents, covering engineering, manufacturing, IT, and supply chain.
- The Hosur plant is the physical heartbeat of Stellantis India.
- The Hosur plant spans 18 acres.
- The plant operates on three shifts a day, six days a week.
- It produces up to 374,000 gearboxes annually.
- It produces up to 300,000 engines annually.
- Nearly 1,500 employees work at the Hosur plant.
- The plant produced its first 6-speed manual gearbox in 2018.
- The plant builds ED engines, the 1.2-litre naturally aspirated engine, and the 1.2-litre turbo Gen 3.
- The plant produces multiple gearbox models.
- The gearboxes serve five major Stellantis brands: Citroën, Peugeot, Opel, DS, and South American models.
- Citroën connects India and export markets for these components.
- Part of the 5-speed gearbox production is exported to South Africa and Morocco.
- The plant has achieved 90 per cent localisation.
- Stellantis works with 138 suppliers, most located in Tamil Nadu.
- Ninety-five percent of Hosur plant production goes to plants outside India.
- The plant is committed to supporting every domestic requirement.
- Hosur has become an indispensable node in Stellantis’ global supply chain across Europe, the Middle East, Africa, Brazil, and Argentina.
- Hosur is the global centre of competence for 6-speed and 5-speed manual gearboxes.
- Hosur is also the global centre of competence for the 1.2-litre engine.
- The plant functions as an engineering campus with design, simulation, and calibration happening on-site.
- Exports from India’s supplier base are expected to rise from about ₹4,000 crore to ₹10,000 crore next year.
- Stellantis nurtures suppliers and builds their capability to supply locally and globally.
- Stellantis has invested nearly ₹11,000 crore in India over the years, with more in the pipeline.
- The company’s investment approach is demand-driven, targeting capacity, capability, or new technology needs.
- The Hosur plant uses AI-enabled vision systems to scan for imperfections.
- Machine-learning robotics adjust to production changes in real time at the plant.
- These technologies are used as everyday tools rather than futuristic experiments.
- The plant has the lowest energy consumption per gearbox unit.
- Hosur uses 92 per cent renewable energy.
- The plant saved 10,622 tonnes of CO₂ in 2024.
- The plant is targeting a CO₂ saving of 14,000 tonnes in 2025.
- Employees at the plant come from 16 states.
- Eighteen percent of the plant's employees are women.
- Stellantis built its own training academy for plant employees.
- Directly and indirectly, the plant has created 4,500 jobs.
- The plant optimizes space inward using vertical storage, compact machining, and condensed pathways rather than expanding outward.
- Stellantis operates a global map of 44 engine and gearbox plants.
- Hosur’s capacity is comparable to 45–50 per cent of those 44 plants.
- If Stellantis produces 6 million vehicles globally, the 300,000 engines from Hosur represent 5 per cent of the world’s engine capacity.
- Hosur will continue to support Stellantis with all internal combustion engines and manual gearboxes for as long as needed.
