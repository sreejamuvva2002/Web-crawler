## Facts

- On July 2, 2026, Lucid Group, Inc. issued a press release announcing its production and delivery totals for the quarter ended June 30, 2026, alongside several organizational and leadership changes.
- On July 1, 2026, the Company’s Board of Directors appointed Alexander De Bock as its incoming Chief Financial Officer.
- Mr. De Bock is expected to join the Company in the coming weeks and will report to Silvio Napoli, the Company’s Chief Executive Officer.
- Taoufiq Boussaid, the Company’s current Chief Financial Officer, will leave after a transition period following Q2 2026 earnings; his departure is not related to any disagreements on operations, policies, or financial disclosures.
- Mr. De Bock, age 49, has served as CFO of Metalsa, a leading manufacturer of automotive components specializing in chassis structures for light and commercial vehicles, since June 2026.
- Previously, Mr. De Bock served as CFO of TI Automotive, a global automotive Tier 1 supplier of safety and performance critical products, from April 2025 to February 2026.
- Prior to TI Automotive's formation following ABC Technologies' acquisition of TI Fluid Systems, he served as CFO and Executive Director of TI Fluid Systems from April 2023 to April 2025.
- From June 2020 to March 2023, Mr. De Bock served as CFO of the Commercial Vehicle Division of ZF Group and as a board member of ZF Commercial Vehicle Control System India Ltd.
- Prior to ZF Friedrichshafen AG's acquisition of WABCO Holdings Inc. in May 2020, Mr. De Bock held various finance leadership positions at WABCO from July 2007 to May 2020, including Corporate Treasurer and Vice President, Financial Planning & Analysis.
- Earlier in his career, Mr. De Bock held internal audit positions with American Standard and Audit & Assurance roles with Arthur Andersen & Co.
- Mr. De Bock holds an M.A. in Applied Economics from the University of Antwerp, Belgium.
- In connection with his appointment, Mr. De Bock will receive an annual base salary of $750,000.
- He will receive a target incentive bonus opportunity of 150% of his base salary for calendar year 2026, paid out based on the greater of 150% of base salary and actual achievement, prorated based on his start date.
- He will receive a cash signing bonus of $1,100,000 within thirty days of his start date and another $600,000 in January 2027, subject to continued employment.
- If terminated without Cause or experiencing a Constructive Termination, he is eligible under the Executive Severance Benefit Plan for 75% of his annual target bonus and immediate vesting of one-third of his then-unvested stock awards, with unvested PSUs deemed service-vested based on actual performance achievement.
- He will receive reimbursement of up to $15,000 per month for six months for temporary housing expenses, grossed up for taxes.
- He will receive reimbursement for flights between the Bay Area and the Detroit metropolitan area between his start date and February 1, 2027.
- He will also receive a Company vehicle, tax and financial planning, and relocation support.
- Mr. De Bock will receive initial equity grants with an aggregate target grant value of $7,500,000, allocated as $2,500,000 in RSUs vesting over four years (25% on a one-year cliff, remainder quarterly) and $5,000,000 in PSUs subject to the Company’s 2026 PSU program.
- He will be eligible for future annual long-term incentive awards beginning in 2027 with a target grant value of $2,000,000, subject to Board discretion.
- He is eligible for a performance-based cash bonus segmented into five equal tranches of $500,000, with a maximum payout of $2,500,000 if achieved between his start date and the fourth anniversary.
- Each tranche's performance vesting condition requires achievement of a Market Capitalization Performance Hurdle of $5.0 billion, $7.5 billion, $10.0 billion, $12.5 billion, and $17.5 billion for tranches 1 through 5, respectively.
- The Market Capitalization Performance Hurdle is defined as the volume-weighted average of the Company’s market capitalization for 60 consecutive trading days per his offer letter.
- Each tranche vests upon the later of its time-based vesting date (second anniversary for tranche 1, third for tranches 2 and 3, fourth for tranches 4 and 5) and certification of the market cap hurdle, subject to continued employment.
