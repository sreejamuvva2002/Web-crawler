# Georgia Made® Certification Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Made® | Georgia](https://georgia.org/georgia-made)  ·  `page_002509`

## Facts

- The Georgia Made® program celebrates manufacturers across Georgia whose products are used in daily life and globally.
- To be Georgia Made® certified, at least 50% of the final product must be assembled and produced in Georgia.
- The certification conveys a point of pride for products used in the state or carried via Georgia’s robust logistics network anywhere else in the world.
- Choosing Georgia Made® products offers Georgians a fulfilling way to support their state's business community health.
- Georgia works closely with manufacturing companies under the program to understand their needs and remove barriers, ensuring that prosperity benefits everyone.
- The program provides opportunities for businesses to connect with leading logistics, research, and educational institutions throughout the state to explore partnership opportunities.
- Upon certification, a company will be registered in the Georgia Made® database of companies.
- Certified companies receive usage rights to the Georgia Made® logo on products, packaging, and promotional materials.
- Eligibility requires at least 50% of the final product to be assembled or produced in Georgia, excluding food/agricultural products and those eligible for the Georgia Grown program.
- Manufacturing under the program refers to human activities creating tangible or intangible goods, including product design, proprietary code, engineering, and industrial production.
- Raw materials adapted into finished goods include wood, metal, minerals, recycled paper, plastic, and glass.
- Finished goods may also be component parts incorporated into or assembled into other products or goods.
- The program covers Georgia’s wide range of non-industrial products that meet the definition outside industrial or commercial facilities.
- Companies whose products display the Georgia Made® label operate in sectors including aerospace, automotive, defense, and life sciences.
