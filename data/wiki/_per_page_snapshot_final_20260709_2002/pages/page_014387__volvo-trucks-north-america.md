# Volvo Trucks North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Trucks Expands Certified EV Dealer Network into Arizona, Georgia and North Carolina as Vanguard Truck Centers Adds Five Locations](https://www.volvotrucks.us/news-and-stories/press-releases/2023/december/volvo-trucks-expands-certified-ev-dealer-network-into-arizona-georgia-and-north-carolina-as-vanguard-truck-centers-adds-five-locations/)  ·  `page_014387`

## Facts

- Vanguard Truck Centers has completed its Volvo Trucks Certified Electric Vehicle (EV) Dealer program at five locations.
- The new certified locations are in Phoenix, Arizona; Atlanta, Georgia (Forest Park); Charlotte, North Carolina; and Austin and Houston, Texas.
- These locations provide comprehensive sales and service support for customers’ electromobility journeys.
- With Vanguard's five additions, Volvo Trucks has expanded its Certified EV Dealer network into three additional states: Arizona, Georgia, and North Carolina.
- Volvo's total certified dealership locations now number 50 across 24 U.S. states and three Canadian provinces in North America.
- Peter Voorhoeve serves as president of Volvo Trucks North America.
- Cost remains a significant barrier to electrification, particularly at scale.
- Volvo Financial Services (VFS) introduced new financing options for customers and dealers to support the transition to zero-emission solutions.
- Vanguard utilized a VFS dealer financing program to install onsite charging stations and dedicated EV service bays at its locations.
- The financing also covered diagnostic tools, personal protective equipment for technicians working with high-voltage systems, and parts inventory for the VNR Electric model.
- VFS recently introduced new financing options for fleets to support charging infrastructure installation costs, as well as service and maintenance plans.
- Christopher Roback is vice president of sales and customer experience at Volvo Financial Services.
- VFS now offers holistic solutions that combine the entire EV ecosystem into a single payment to distribute transition costs instead of requiring upfront capital.
- Vanguard Truck Centers was founded in 1989.
- Vanguard operates 26 modern facilities across Texas, Arizona, Missouri, Illinois, South Carolina, North Carolina, and Georgia.
- Vanguard has trained 10 technicians, with two at each of the five new locations, to perform routine battery-electric truck maintenance and repairs.
- The first five locations are now staffed with certified EV sales and service representatives to support growing fleet adoption of zero-emission vehicles.
- Vanguard plans to continually evaluate each location for additional training and certification opportunities as market demand for zero-tailpipe emission solutions grows.
- John Thomas is executive vice president of new truck sales at Vanguard Truck Centers.
- Customers are submitting daily inquiries about electric vehicles, including those who have never purchased a Volvo Truck before but are interested in the VNR Electric model.
- Volvo Trucks North America is headquartered in Greensboro, North Carolina.
- It is one of the leading heavy-duty truck manufacturers in North America.
- Uptime Services are delivered by a network of nearly 400 authorized dealers across North America and the 24/7 Volvo Trucks Uptime Center.
- Every Volvo truck is assembled at the Volvo Trucks New River Valley manufacturing facility in Dublin, Virginia.
- The Dublin facility meets ISO 9001 standards for quality and ISO 14001 standards for environmental care.
- The Dublin facility holds dual ISO 50001/Superior Energy Performance certification at the platinum level for sustained excellence in energy management.
- Volvo Trucks North America offers a full range of diesel, alternative-fuel, and all-electric vehicles as part of its complete transport solutions.
- Volvo Trucks provides medium- and heavy-duty trucks globally through a dealer network with 2,200 service points in about 130 countries.
- Volvo trucks are assembled in 12 countries across the globe.
- In 2025 approximately 120,000 Volvo trucks were delivered worldwide.
- Volvo Trucks is part of the Volvo Group, which also provides complete solutions for financing and service.
