# NVH Korea

**Source:** [News | A transaction worth the wait: Korean autoparts maker buys distribution center](https://www.costar.com/article/1855061448/a-transaction-worth-the-wait-korean-autoparts-maker-buys-distribution-center)  ·  `page_047651`

## Facts

- NVH Korea, a Korean autoparts manufacturer, purchased a distribution center in Phenix City, Alabama.
- The facility is located at 510 Brickyard Road, just over the border from Columbus, Georgia.
- The property sits on approximately 22 acres and contains a 150,420-square-foot warehouse and distribution center.
- NVH Korea paid nearly $6.7 million for the building.
- The building was originally constructed in 1991 and underwent renovation in 2023.
- The property was put up for sale during a period of rising interest rates and buyer caution.
- It took 18 months, including a four-month negotiation period, for the facility to find a new owner after being listed.
- Jack Hayes, CEO for brokerage NAI G2 Commercial, described the project as significant in its size and economic impact to the east Alabama and west Georgia region.
- Missy Kendrick, chief executive of the business booster group Choose Columbus, stated that the acquisition was 'a perfect example of an impactful acquisition' that 'allowed a neighboring county's industry to expand without leaving the market,' filled a long vacant facility, and provided jobs and capital investment in a growing area that benefits the entire region.
- The transaction was judged by real estate professionals in the area and garnered a CoStar Impact Award.
- Brokers Jack Hayes (CEO) and David Johnson (chief of brokerage operations) handled the listing.
