# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Breaking Ground on the Future | Kia](https://www.kia.com/us/en/kia-collective/vision/breaking-ground-on-the-future)  ·  `page_003119`

## Facts

- Construction has commenced on a new EV plant in Georgia.
- On October 25, 2022, leaders from the local community grabbed their shovels and ceremonially broke ground on the plant.
- The facility is expected to begin production in the first half of 2025.
- Kia hopes the plant will establish a stable supply chain for EV batteries in the U.S.
- In 2006, Kia opened an auto-assembly facility in West Point, Georgia.
- The West Point facility accounts for 40% of Kia sales in the U.S.
- The new EV plant is expected to bring in 8,100 jobs.
- Alongside the groundbreaking, Kia will donate to four charities across Bryan County, Georgia, where the factory will be built.
- Kia is supporting the community through an extension of its Accelerate the Good Program.
- Donations will go to Family Promise of the Coastal Empire, America’s Second Harvest of Coastal Georgia, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital, and Inspiredu.
- The K5, Sportage, Sorento, and Telluride (excluding HEV and PHEV models) are assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
