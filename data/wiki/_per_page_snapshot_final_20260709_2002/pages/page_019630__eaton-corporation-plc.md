## Facts

- Eaton Corporation plc is headquartered at Eaton House, 30 Pembroke Road, Dublin D04 Y0C2, Ireland.
- The company operates in the Industrials sector within the Specialty Industrial Machinery industry.
- Eaton has 92,000 full-time employees globally.
- Eaton's Vehicle and eMobility operations are now aligned within the broader Mobility focus.
- Through its Vehicle and eMobility segment, Eaton delivers components and intelligent power electronics for conventional and electric vehicles.
- These EV-related components cover transmissions, engine management, and high-voltage power distribution and protection.
- The company focuses on electrification, digitalization, and energy transition trends to drive growth across mobility end markets.
- Eaton is making significant investments in research and development driving advancements in energy storage, electrical vehicle charging infrastructure, and renewable energy integration.
- Craig Arnold served as Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Eaton Corporation until 2025, having assumed the role in 2020 after succeeding Alexander Cutler.
- Arnold joined Eaton in 2000 as Vice President and General Manager of the Cutler-Hammer's electrical business unit.
- Craig Arnold was appointed President and Chief Operating Officer (COO) of Eaton Corporation in 2015.
- Paulo Ruiz became President and Chief Executive Officer effective June 1, 2025.
- Eaton’s organizational structure in 2026 is a multi-segment, matrixed hierarchy built around business sectors, regions, and corporate functions.
- Core business segments include Electrical Americas, Electrical Global, Aerospace, Vehicle, and eMobility.
- Electrical Americas and Electrical Global focus on regional electrical products, systems, and services for North America and international regions.
- Aerospace, Vehicle, and eMobility are grouped under an Industrial/ Mobility-focused leadership structure that consolidates mechanical and transportation businesses.
- The company leverages a common innovation and sustainability framework, investing heavily in R&D, digital technologies, and grid-interactive solutions to help customers improve efficiency and reduce emissions.
