# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Daechang Seat Corp. USA To Create 500 Jobs In Chatham County, GA | Textile World](https://www.textileworld.com/textile-world/2023/10/automotive-supplier-daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county-ga/)  ·  `page_018890`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames.
- The new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The investment will create more than 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee serves as the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- DSC maintains a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- DSC will be hiring for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- Recruitment will occur through local job fairs and website postings.
- Griff Lynch serves as the Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO.
- The facility is located only minutes from the Port of Savannah.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Trip Tollison serves as the Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson serves as the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Since 2018, Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs pursuing its entire EV supply chain.
- In fiscal year 2023, automotive industry job creation in Georgia increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- The announcement was posted on October 24, 2023.
