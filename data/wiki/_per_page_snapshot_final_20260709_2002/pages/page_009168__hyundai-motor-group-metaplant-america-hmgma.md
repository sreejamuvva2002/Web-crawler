# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-02

**Source:** [Hyundai bets $21B on Atlas humanoid robots for US car assembly](https://interestingengineering.com/innovation/hyundai-to-deploy-humanoid-atlas-robots)  ·  `page_009168`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group plans to deploy Atlas humanoid robots at its Metaplant America facility in Georgia.
- The Atlas robots are developed by Boston Dynamics and are designed to perform tasks traditionally carried out by humans.
- This initiative marks a significant milestone in Hyundai’s ongoing partnership with Boston Dynamics, which is aimed at scaling up robotic manufacturing and design at its facilities.
- Last month, Hyundai committed to purchasing tens of thousands of robots from Boston Dynamics, a company it acquired from SoftBank in 2021.
- At the Georgia plant, Hyundai already uses Spot robots for industrial inspections.
- The facility features a dedicated robot that removes car doors before vehicles enter General Assembly.
- A fixed robot reinstalls the doors toward the end of the process, a technology unique to the Georgia facility.
- Hyundai has not disclosed how many Atlas robots will be deployed or what specific tasks they will perform.
- The company plans to further expand the use of robots across its global manufacturing facilities to streamline processes and enhance efficiency.
- The automation drive is part of Hyundai’s $21 billion investment plan in the U.S.
- $6 billion of the investment is specifically earmarked for innovation, automation, and strategic partnership.
- U.S. President Donald Trump’s tariffs on imported autos has also forced Hyundai to ramp up these efforts.
- North America remains Hyundai’s largest market.
- Despite a global sales slump of 2 percent last year, Hyundai’s sales in the U.S. rose by 4 percent, reaching 1.9 million units.
- The company’s strong performance in North America was a key driver behind its record consolidated revenue last year.
- Hyundai’s vehicles are known for their price competitiveness, especially in the midrange segment, and continue to appeal to U.S. consumers.
- Hyundai aims to manufacture 300,000 electric and hybrid vehicles annually at the new Metaplant America facility.
- At its recent Grand Opening Ceremony, the company announced plans to ramp up production to 500,000 units over time, without specifying a timeline.
- Hyundai expects to produce 1.2 million vehicles annually on U.S. soil in total.
- This represents a 70 percent increase from the estimated production volume of roughly 700,000 vehicles in 2024.
- The automaker is mulling building capacity at its Alabama plant.
- Lee Jae-il, an analyst at Eugene Investment & Securities in South Korea, stated that hybrid vehicles with favorable cost performance have contributed significantly to Hyundai’s brand strength.
- The company remains vulnerable to the impact of tariffs on imports from South Korea, which make up a large portion of Hyundai’s U.S. sales.
- Hyundai President Jose Munoz confirmed in April that the company will not raise prices until June.
- A correction dated May 5, 2025, clarified that the correct annual production target for HMGMA is 300,000 units, not 100,000 as previously stated.
- A further correction clarified that the doors at HMGMA are installed robotically but not by Atlas robots.
