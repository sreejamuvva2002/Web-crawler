# Mine of the Future Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. Department of Energy Launches Mine of the Future Initiatives to Bolster the U.S. Mining Industry | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/hgeo/articles/us-department-energy-launches-mine-future-initiatives-bolster-us-mining-industry)  ·  `page_005383`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Fossil Energy (FE) announced two new programs under its Mine of the Future initiative.
- The Mine of the Future initiative aims to transform U.S. mining practices and speed the development of a secure and resilient domestic critical mineral and material supply chain by employing cutting-edge mining technologies and processes.
- FE announced its intent to provide up to $80 million in federal funding for mining technology proving grounds that will support and accelerate innovative technology development and commercialization.
- FE also announced up to $15 million in federal funding for DOE’s National Laboratories to conduct critical research, development, and demonstration activities that will advance DOE strategies to strengthen the domestic critical mineral supply chain.
- Through both programs, the initiative aims to help deliver on President Trump’s commitment to expand the nation’s critical materials portfolio, reduce reliance on foreign sources, and re-establish the United States as a global leader in the mining sector.
- Assistant Secretary of Fossil Energy Kyle Haustveit stated that DOE is committed to fostering a new generation of skilled miners and equipping them with tools to lead the world in mineral production and processing technology.
- The $80 million funding opportunity will establish field sites for real-world testing, optimization, and deployment of next-generation mining technologies.
- These proving ground sites will serve as vital training grounds for a new generation of skilled American miners.
- The Proving Ground initiative will de-risk the adoption of new mining technologies for commercialization and widespread industry adoption.
- The initiative includes development of Mine Technology Proving Grounds to enable the development, demonstration, and maturation of advanced mining technologies at a large scale for deployment at domestic mine sites.
- It includes accelerated Research & Development Projects for up to two signature mining technologies to be developed and proven at their proposed proving ground facility.
- It involves collaborative partnerships between government, industry/private sector, and academia to leverage diverse expertise, infrastructure, and support for technology.
- DOE expects to issue the Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO) for the Proving Grounds during the fourth quarter of calendar year 2025.
- Under the National Laboratory Call for Proposals, FE announced up to $15 million in federal funding available to DOE’s National Laboratories for technologies capable of revolutionizing the mining industry.
- Selected laboratory performers will advance DOE’s critical minerals and materials supply chain strategies through activities that bridge the gap between bench-scale research and commercial scale demonstration.
- The lab call solicits proposals for Mining Innovation, focusing on developing technologies capable of innovating and modernizing the critical mineral mining industry to develop better, novel mining techniques for conventional ores containing critical minerals.
- The lab call solicits proposals for Beneficiation, focusing on developing novel beneficiation technologies that can preconcentrate at the mine site along with novel separation techniques to enhance efficiency, reduce environmental impact, and improve resource recovery.
- The lab call solicits proposals for In-situ, focusing on in-situ extraction and recovery of critical minerals using technologies that emphasize advanced technological integration with a strong commitment to environmental and social responsibility.
- Letters of intent for the National Laboratory Call are due by October 27, 2025 at 5:00 PM ET.
- Full applications for the National Laboratory Call are due by January 27, 2026 at 5:00 PM ET.
- FE’s Division of Minerals Sustainability focuses on the production and processing of critical minerals, developing an economically sustainable and secure domestic supply chain, and conducting technology innovations on new advanced mining technologies.
