# Georgia Power

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-16

**Source:** [Georgia Power welcomes Rivian to Georgia - Dec 16, 2021](https://southerncompany.mediaroom.com/2021-12-16-Georgia-Power-welcomes-Rivian-to-Georgia)  ·  `page_001694`

## Facts

- Georgia Power joined Governor Brian Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development to welcome Rivian, an electric sport utility vehicle and pickup company, to Georgia.
- Rivian was named the 2022 MotorTrend Truck of the Year and selected Georgia for a nearly 13 million square foot plant for vehicle and battery operations along Interstate 20 near Social Circle.
- The Rivian project brings an estimated 7,500 jobs and a $5 billion investment to Georgia.
- The announcement highlights the success of targeted statewide economic development efforts such as the Electric Mobility Innovation Alliance (EMIA), which was launched in July by Governor Kemp.
- The EMIA aims to bring together public and private sector leaders, researchers, and utilities like Georgia Power to ensure state recruitment efforts and regulations welcome the growing electric transportation and battery charging industry and workforce development opportunities.
- Chris Womack, president, chairman and CEO of Georgia Power, stated that the announcement is a tremendous victory for the state and an example of the longstanding partnership between state government and the business community on bringing new jobs and growing the economy.
- Womack noted that Georgia has a network of nearly 1,000 public charging stations from multiple partners available across the state to support electric vehicle investments.
- Original equipment manufacturers in Georgia and throughout the Southeast region are shifting their product lines to keep up with increasing electric vehicle production.
- Georgia Power supports the EV needs of several OEMs including Kia, Mercedes-Benz, BMW, and Volkswagen.
- Other EV industry leaders that have moved to or expanded in Georgia include battery producer SK Innovation, EnChem, modules and storage systems producer Duckyang, charging station leader Heliox, and all-electric bus manufacturer Blue Bird.
- Georgia Power continues proactive efforts with the State of Georgia to attract multibillion-dollar companies involved in auto manufacturing production, recycling, and EV supply chain development.
- Georgia Power partnered with the University of Georgia's College of Engineering to create an e-Mobility Certificate program covering engineering, public policy, public health, and business acumen to prepare the workforce for an electrified transportation future.
- Georgia Power is working closely with design students at Georgia Tech to evaluate the environmental benefits and future workforce opportunities of transportation electrification.
- Earlier in 2021, Georgia Power joined Southern Company and utilities across the South, Midwest, Gulf Coast, Central Plains, and mid-Atlantic to enable seamless EV driver travel through a coordinated charging network.
- The Electric Highway Coalition will enable increased charging options along major highway routes from Texas to Washington, DC to provide easier access to EV options for drivers.
