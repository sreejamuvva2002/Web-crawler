# LGES-JSW Energy Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [LG Energy Solution in Talks with JSW Energy for $1.5 Billion Battery Joint Venture in India](https://evmagz.com/lg-energy-solution-in-talks-with-jsw-energy-for-1-5-billion-battery-joint-venture-in-india/)  ·  `page_056193`

## Facts

- South Korea’s LG Energy Solution (LGES) is in discussions with India’s JSW Energy to establish a joint venture for manufacturing batteries for electric vehicles (EVs) and renewable energy storage.
- The proposed joint venture would require an investment of over $1.5 billion.
- Under the agreement terms, LGES would contribute technology and equipment for battery production.
- JSW Energy would invest the necessary capital for the venture.
- The joint venture is expected to set up a plant in India with a total capacity of 10 gigawatt hours.
- JSW plans to use approximately 70% of the plant's capacity for energy storage and electric vehicles.
- LGES will take the remaining share of the plant's capacity.
- The companies have signed an initial agreement, but the deal remains non-binding.
- Final discussions for the joint venture are expected to conclude in the coming months.
- One source stated that LGES wanted a partner in India and JSW is interested because it is developing its own brand of EVs starting with buses and trucks, and later on cars.
- The venture represents a significant move for LGES, which supplies batteries to major automakers like Tesla, General Motors, and Hyundai.
- The joint venture would expand LGES’s footprint in India, where it already supplies batteries to e-scooter makers like Ola Electric and TVS Motor.
- For JSW, the joint venture offers an opportunity to secure local battery supplies for its energy business and reduce costs for its upcoming EVs.
- JSW is also engaged in other talks with battery suppliers.
- JSW has proposed a 25-year agreement to LGES.
- Both companies are aiming to have the plant operational before the end of 2026.
- A final decision on the joint venture is expected within 3 to 4 months.
- The joint venture would boost India’s push to increase local manufacturing of clean energy products.
- India's local manufacturing push is supported by government incentives for companies investing in EV production.
