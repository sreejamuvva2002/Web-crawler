# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-28

**Source:** [TI Fluid Systems Introduces New Integrated Thermal Manifold Assemblies For Electric Vehicles](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ti-fluid-systems-introduces-new-integrated-thermal-manifold-assemblies-for-electric-vehicles-301301220.html)  ·  `page_003450`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems (LSE:TIFS), a leading global supplier of automotive fluid systems technology, has introduced an innovative approach for coolant fluid management on electrified vehicles.
- The new technology is an Integrated Thermal Manifold assembly (ITMa) that provides a one-piece, light-weight, blow-molded plastic manifold to optimize complex line design and replaces multiple line bundle assemblies of thermal loops for heating and cooling on next generation of electric vehicles.
- Current EVs typically use multiple complex rubber, aluminum or multi-layer plastic lines that are packaged tightly together under hood or chassis mounted.
- TI Fluid Systems has developed the ability to evolve their Fuel Tank and Delivery Systems (FTDS) division fuel tank blow molding technology to produce a one piece integrated thermal 'manifold' (ITMa) approach.
- This single piece blow molded plastic manifold provides significant OEM EV value including improved flow performance for efficiency gains, lower part count for sub and final vehicle assembly, lower weight, and reduced investment tooling costs.
- TI Fluid Systems' FTDS division has demonstrated further integration potential for 'fluid storage' (coolant reservoir expansion tank), 'fluid temp control' (heat exchangers/sensors) and 'fluid delivery' (pumps) to create a very compact module solution.
- These integrated thermal modular assemblies are bespoke to each OEM vehicle or application and can be engineered for various system types of multiple thermal loops.
- Hans Dieltjens, COO stated that the company's innovations have captured the attention of OEMs as they move from 'build-to-print' components to 'innovative collaboration' of EV sub-systems.
- Hans Dieltjens, COO also noted that TI Fluid Systems is leveraging its fluid know-how on new thermal EV challenges and successfully demonstrating the ability to pivot fluid competencies, capabilities and assets to provide a family of new break-through EV products with various levels of integration.
- TI Fluid Systems has received their first ITMa global EV platform application release for model year 2023 introduction with a leading high-volume European OEM.
- TI Fluid Systems is a leading global manufacturer of fluid storage, carrying and delivery systems primarily for the light duty automotive market.
- The company possesses nearly 100 years of automotive fluid systems experience.
- TI Fluid Systems operates manufacturing facilities in 107 locations across 28 countries serving all major global OEMs.
