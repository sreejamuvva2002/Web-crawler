# Vista Metals

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-09

**Source:** [Vista Metals Completes Aluminum Casthouse Expansion in Georgia - Light Metal Age Magazine](https://www.lightmetalage.com/news/industry-news/casthouse/vista-metals-completes-aluminum-casthouse-expansion-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_013567`

## Facts

- Vista Metals completed the construction of a seventh aluminum casthouse at its facility in Adairsville, Georgia.
- This milestone represents the second phase of the company’s ongoing $100 million long-range capital plan.
- The new Georgia casthouse includes the installation of melting furnaces, homogenizing ovens, and support equipment.
- President Kelly Thomas stated that Vista’s investment will further support growth in core markets including outer space, commercial aviation, defense, and industrial applications.
- The company is completing final equipment installation in a new world-class finishing facility, which is a critical part of this expansion phase.
- Vista Metals announced a $60 million investment in a new location to serve the aerospace industry with specialty aluminum products.
- The new Kentucky facility will be located in Bowling Green, Kentucky, and expands upon existing capabilities in California and Georgia.
- CEO Andy Primack stated the company plans to bring over 50 new jobs to the Bowling Green area.
- Bowling Green is described as a growing city with a strong industrial business base, a proven track record of attracting qualified manufacturing talent, close interaction between business and education, and a long-term community development plan.
- Initial site preparation is now underway at the Kentucky location.
- Vista Metals will commence a phased build-out to support growth in its core markets.
- The new Kentucky site will primarily support downstream processing and value-added products serving aerospace extrusion, forging, and rolling markets.
- In addition to commercial aviation, the Kentucky location will support defense, automotive, and general industrial applications.
- President Kelly Thomas stated that the opening of the Kentucky facility underscores a commitment to the aerospace industry and delivering robust supply chains.
- OEMs are looking to more than double commercial aircraft build rates over the next two decades.
- The company intends to invest in a manner that enables customers to test and qualify the facility ahead of the projected growth surge.
- Ongoing investments in people, processes, and equipment at existing facilities will continue alongside the expansion.
