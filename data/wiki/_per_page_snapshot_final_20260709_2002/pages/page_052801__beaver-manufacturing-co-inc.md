# Beaver Manufacturing Co. Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Fiber reinforcement firm Beaver expanding into Mexico](https://www.americanindustriesgroup.com/news/fiber-reinforcement-firm-beaver-expanding-into-mexico-news/)  ·  `page_052801`

## Facts

- Beaver Manufacturing Co. Inc. is a supplier of technical fiber reinforcements for hoses in North America.
- The company plans to open a new plant in Mexico.
- The new plant will not impact operations at its headquarters facility in Mansfield, Ga.
- The new facility will be located in Tepeji, about 45 miles northwest of Mexico City.
- Mike Dubin is Beaver's senior vice president and chief operating officer.
- The site will expand the company's global presence.
- The site will increase converting capacity of meta- and para-aramid, rayon and polyester products.
- These products support automotive, oil and gas, and other industrial applications in Mexico and South America.
- Aramid fibers are a class of heat-resistant and strong synthetic fibers.
- Aramid fibers provide high strength-to-weight ratios with greater flexibility not available in steel wire.
- Strong growth in Mexico prompted the need for the Mexican factory.
- The share of Beaver's revenues derived from Mexico has increased substantially over the past five years.
- The new plant will be in a prime location for expanding the company's aramid product market.
- Major growth is projected in Mexico and South America for the automotive and oil and gas industries.
- Dubin stated that business transferred from the U.S. to Mexico or from other parts of the world into Mexico has really high growth rates.
- Mexico has become a large percentage of Beaver's business.
- The company felt like localizing that business with all the growth they are working on was an important way to handle it.
- Serving South America from Mexico is much better for the company.
- Beaver's factory will be located at an industrial park in Tepeji.
- The firm's section in the industrial park will be in excess of 50,000 square feet.
- Equipment will begin arriving in January.
- Dubin hopes to have the plant operational by the end of 2018's first quarter.
- Initial employment is planned at between 30 and 40 workers.
- Employment will expand from there.
- Beaver did not disclose investment or capacity figures for the operation.
- The new plant will be headed by General Manager Enrique Quirarte.
- Quirarte has 13 years of experience in sales and marketing.
- Quirarte has managed sales force teams in Mexico and South America.
- Quirarte has specialized in working in the aramid market.
- Quirarte is accustomed to selling in diverse markets such as automotive, electrical, energy, pharma and others.
- Dubin stated they are excited about having an expert with sales and marketing in the meta- and para-aramid markets.
- Quirarte's extensive knowledge of heat-resistant and strong synthetic fibers will be a key asset in supporting development into the automotive, oil, gas and other specialty industries in Mexico and South America.
- Quirarte will oversee both the operations of the factory and business in the region.
- Quirarte will have full responsibility for the facility.
- Quirarte will have full bottom-line responsibility for everything.
- To this point, Beaver has serviced Mexico from its main facility in Mansfield.
- The Mansfield site will continue to operate at full capacity and employment.
- The addition of the new Mexico plant will complement operations at the main facility without disruption.
- The new plant will help the company efficiently handle projections for growth.
- Dubin stated there are no plans to change anything being done in their current facility in Mansfield.
- This is an expansion of the business.
- There will be no change in employment at the Georgia facility.
- No equipment will be moved from the Georgia facility.
- Growth has been so strong that the new capacity cannot come on-stream soon enough, according to Dubin.
- This is Beaver's second major move in the past two months.
- In October, Beaver reached an agreement where Germany's Mehler Engineered Products Group will represent Beaver's product line in Europe beginning Jan. 1.
- The agreement with Mehler will eventually lead to manufacturing of the goods in Europe.
