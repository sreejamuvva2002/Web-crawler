# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-19

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group Sign MoU for Future Mobility Collaboration | Hyundai Motor Group](https://www.hyundaimotorgroup.com/en/news/CONT0000000000114163)  ·  `page_002770`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding on Sept. 19, 2023.
- The MoU covers collaboration on research and applications to support sustainable mobility, developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $12.6 billion in new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- The investment includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The HMGMA site will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- The investment also includes a $5 billion battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On joint venture facility will employ approximately 3,500 people.
- Proximity to Georgia Tech was one of the important considerations in deciding the locations of these facilities.
- Kia Georgia’s West Point assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009 under the leadership of Honorary Chairman Mong-Koo Chung and Executive Chair Euisun Chung.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue, who was Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia Georgia plant’s opening, participated in the HMG-Georgia Tech MOU ceremony.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals Hyundai Motor’s automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2021 alone.
- According to the study, Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that Hyundai is a global brand synonymous with quality, innovation, and a commitment to advancing technology, and noted an alignment of values between the organizations.
- Cabrera expressed gratitude for Hyundai's transformative investments in the state and pride that the Hyundai brand will feature prominently on campus.
- Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, said Georgia is like a second home to the company and emphasized their mission-driven focus on sustainable mobility and the hydrogen economy.
- Chang stated the partnership with Georgia Tech includes research and applications for sustainable mobility, workforce development, and smart cities, calling it the beginning of a decades-long partnership and a reason for choosing Georgia for EV investments.
- José Muñoz, President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company, and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, said Hyundai’s investments will create tens of thousands of jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Muñoz noted that proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for its new EV manufacturing facility.
- Muñoz envisioned zero emissions hydrogen-powered vehicles on campus, advanced air mobility shuttles to Hartsfield International Airport, and hands-free autonomous vehicles on I-75 and I-85.
- The expanded relationship with Georgia Tech will include opportunities for student professional development and cooperative work programs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia stands at the forefront of the mobility sector's transformation toward sustainable transportation alternatives and is building an ecosystem to support it.
- Hyundai will collaborate with Georgia Tech on joint research and development programs focused on sustainable mobility, battery technology, and expanding the hydrogen economy.
- Georgia Tech and Hyundai will create technical training and leadership development programming for Hyundai employees.
- The partnership will initiate engagement activities to stimulate interest in STEM degrees among students.
- Hyundai will actively participate in career recruitment events at Georgia Tech.
- Hyundai will create learning programs for both undergraduate and graduate students.
- Hyundai’s partnership includes field-naming recognition at Bobby Dodd Stadium, which is now known as Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field.
- The partnership provides a pathway for Georgia Tech to expand its stature as a leading research entity in automotive electrification through world-class hydrogen research.
- The partnership gives Georgia Tech an opportunity to contribute to the state of Georgia’s commitment as a hub for electric vehicle production.
- The partnership brings new revenue for Georgia Tech Athletics to provide resources for student-athletes and teams.
