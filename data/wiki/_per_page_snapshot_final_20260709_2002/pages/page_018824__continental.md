# Continental

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-16

**Source:** [Continental Marks Key U.S. Tire Plant Anniversaries and Continued Investment Commitment to Customers](https://www.continental-tires.com/us/en/about-us/newsroom/truck-and-bus/continental-marks-key-us-tire-plant-anniversaries-and-continued-investment-commitment-to-customers/)  ·  `page_018824`

## Facts

- 2024 marks a significant milestone for Continental's tire manufacturing operations in the United States, celebrating the anniversaries of three key plants: 5 years in Clinton, Miss.; 10 years in Sumter, S.C. and 50 years in Mount Vernon, Ill.
- Continental's U.S. tire plants have an annual production capacity of more than 16 million tires.
- The U.S. tire manufacturing operations employ a team of more than 6,700 local manufacturing employees.
- Continental is amongst the top tire manufacturers in the U.S.
- In the last decade alone, Continental has invested more than $1.5 billion USD in its American tire manufacturing operations.
- Jochen Etzel serves as CEO of Continental Tire the Americas.
- Since 1988, when Continental first started manufacturing operations in the U.S., the company has strategically and consistently enhanced its manufacturing footprint to support growth in the American market.
- Benny Harmse serves as Head of Manufacturing for Continental Tire the Americas.
- The Mt. Vernon plant produced its first tire in 1974 and employs a team of over 3,900 people.
- The Mt. Vernon plant's product portfolio includes both Passenger Light Truck Tires (PLT) and truck tires (TT).
- The Sumter plant officially opened in 2014, employs approximately 1,300 employees, and focuses on producing PLT tires.
- The newest Continental tire plant is located in Clinton, officially opened in 2019, employs around 700 people, and produces TT tires.
- Continental serves both original equipment and replacement customers from its U.S. tire plants.
- Additional manufacturing locations include Aldora Mills, Barnesville, GA, which produces tire cords, and Plymouth, Ind., where Hoosier produces racing tires.
- The company was recognized as one of America’s Best Large Employers by Forbes for the eighth time.
- Continental was named one of the World’s Top Companies for Women by Forbes in 2023.
- Founded in 1871, Continental generates sales of €39.7 billion in 2024 and currently employs around 190,000 people in 55 countries and markets.
- In fiscal 2024, the Tires group sector generated preliminary sales of 13.9 billion euros.
- Continental's tire division employs more than 57,000 people worldwide and operates 20 production and 16 development sites.
