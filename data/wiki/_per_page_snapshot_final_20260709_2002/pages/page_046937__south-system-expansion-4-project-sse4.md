# South System Expansion 4 Project (SSE4)

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-30

**Source:** [South System Expansion 4](https://www2.kindermorgan.com/Operations/Projects/SSE4-Project-Page)  ·  `page_046937`

## Facts

- Southern Natural Gas Company, L.L.C. (SNG) and Elba Express Company, L.L.C. (EEC) submitted an application to FERC on June 30, 2025 seeking a certificate of public convenience and necessity for the South System Expansion 4 Project (Docket No. CP25-517-000).
- The project followed a successful pre-filing process in October 2024 (Docket No. PF25-1-000).
- On December 31, 2025, FERC issued a Notice of Schedule for the certificate order after adding the project to the Permitting Council's FAST-41 Dashboard.
- On January 30, 2026, FERC issued a Draft Environmental Impact Statement (DEIS), and the public comment period has concluded.
- FERC expects to issue an order granting a certificate of public convenience and necessity in July 2026.
- The project is valued at approximately $3.5 billion.
- SSE4 will increase SNG's South Main Line capacity by approximately 1.3 Bcf/d.
- The project aims to meet growing power generation and local distribution company demand in the Southeast.
- SSE4 will be completed in two phases, almost entirely comprising brownfield looping and horsepower compression additions on SNG and EEC pipeline systems.
- KMI's share of the cost is approximately $1.8 billion, including EEC.
- Assuming timely permits, KMI expects to place phase one in service in Q4 2028 and phase two in Q4 2029.
