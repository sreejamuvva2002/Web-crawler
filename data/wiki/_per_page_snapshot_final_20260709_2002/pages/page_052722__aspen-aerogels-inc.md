# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels Inc. is a leading provider of high-performance aerogel insulation known for innovative thermal barrier solutions in the electric vehicle market.
- The company developed and commercialized PyroThin® aerogel thermal barriers specifically for use in EV battery packs.
- Sales of PyroThin® reached $306.8 million in 2024, up from $110.1 million in 2023 and $55.6 million in 2022.
- Aspen Aerogels secured multi-year production contracts with major automotive OEMs including GM and Toyota to supply thermal barriers for EV battery systems.
- The company converted its East Providence facility to support the growth of its thermal barrier program.
- Aspen Aerogels plans to utilize external manufacturing capabilities in China to meet expected demand.
- The company established a maquiladora in Mexico for assembling PyroThin products.
- Aspen Aerogels launched a carbon aerogel initiative aimed at improving lithium-ion battery performance.
- The company anticipates achieving its target revenue capacity by 2025 through productivity improvements and external manufacturing support.
- Aspen Aerogels plans to reduce capital expenditures in 2025 following the cessation of the Statesboro plant construction.
- In October 2024, the company engaged in an underwritten offering raising approximately $93.2 million.
- Aspen Aerogels entered into a Credit Agreement with MidCap securing a $125 million term loan and a $100 million revolving credit facility.
- Proceeds from the MidCap agreement were used to repurchase $123.9 million of Convertible Senior PIK Toggle Notes.
- The company relies on third-party manufacturing facilities in China, exposing it to risks related to political, social, and economic instability.
- Aspen Aerogels is dependent on a single manufacturing facility in East Providence, RI, and any significant disruption there could adversely affect operations.
- Trends in EV battery technologies may reduce demand for the company's thermal barrier products.
- The company faces significant competition in the insulation markets which could impact market share and revenues.
- Changes in U.S.-China relations could materially harm business operations due to regulatory compliance uncertainties in China.
- Management acknowledges challenges posed by the need for substantial capital investment to expand production capacity and maintain operations.
- Aspen Aerogels is exposed to foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations affecting financial results from international sales.
- Total revenue reached $452.7 million, reflecting a 90% increase from the previous year driven by growth in both energy industrial and thermal barrier segments.
- Gross profit was $182.9 million, representing a 221% increase attributed to higher revenue and improved cost management.
- Income from operations was $54.5 million, a significant improvement from a loss of $49.2 million in the prior year.
- Net income was $13.4 million, compared to a net loss of $45.8 million in the previous year.
- In 2024, 57% of product revenue was generated in the United States, with significant contributions from Latin America (22%), Europe (10%), Asia (6%), and Canada (4%).
- Aspen Aerogels has sold over $1.5 billion of insulation products globally, representing an installed base of more than 500 million square feet of insulation.
- The Energy Industrial Segment has seen a compound annual growth rate of 15% since 2008.
- The company plans to leverage its Aerogel Technology Platform to expand into new markets including hydrogen energy and filtration while continuing to grow its presence in the EV market.
