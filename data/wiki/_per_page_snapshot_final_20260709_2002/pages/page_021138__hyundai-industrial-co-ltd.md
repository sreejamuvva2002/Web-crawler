# Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [Hyundai Industrial Opening New Facility In Georgia, Creating 100 New Jobs And Investing $24 Million | Doc and the Block](https://docandtheblock.com/hyundai-industrial-opening-new-facility-in-georgia-creating-100-new-jobs-and-investing-24-million/)  ·  `page_021138`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. would be opening a new manufacturing facility near Newnan.
- The new facility will create 100 new jobs.
- Hyundai Industrial will invest more than $24 million in the new facility.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that partners like Kia and Hyundai are attracting a large network of suppliers, creating jobs and opportunity for Georgians across the state.
- Suppliers have committed to bringing nearly 6,000 jobs to Georgia since last year’s Hyundai Metaplant announcement.
- Nearly 6,000 new jobs will be located mostly in communities outside of large urban areas.
- Hyundai Industrial was established in 1978.
- Hyundai Industrial is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- The company specializes in manufacturing car seats, arm rests, and head rests.
- Woosuck Kim serves as Team Leader at HDI America Inc.
- Woosuck Kim stated that Hyundai Industrial has more than four decades of experience providing comfortable and safe experiences for drivers and passengers with some of the world’s biggest automotive brands.
- Hyundai Industrial is excited to be part of the e-mobility transition happening in Georgia.
- Hyundai Industrial looks forward to working with Coweta County to create mutually beneficial growth for the community and future employees.
- A press release from Governor Kemp’s office stated that Hyundai Industrial purchased an existing building at 83 Amlajack Way in Coweta County.
- The building will be retrofitted to produce arm and head rests for OEMs.
- One of the OEM customers is Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Industrial will focus on filling manufacturing production roles.
- Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry for over a century.
- Georgia has pursued the entire EV supply chain, creating more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs since 2020.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia’s automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
