# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-04

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies Taking Rubber Modified Asphalt Round the Curve - Scrap Tire News: Tire and Rubber Recycling News and Information](https://scraptirenews.com/2023/01/04/lehigh-technologies-taking-rubber-modified-asphalt-round-the-curve/)  ·  `page_057058`

## Facts

- In 2022, Georgia-based Lehigh Technologies collaborated with Michelin to resurface 6.3 miles of Georgia’s Lawrenceville Highway with Lehigh’s Rubber Modified Asphalt, made with recycled tire rubber powder.
- Jason Stravinski serves as President and CEO of Lehigh Technologies.
- The paving project began in September 2022 and was in its final stages at the time of publication.
- Kara Fulcher serves as Director of Sustainability and Government Affairs for Michelin North America, Inc.
- The Rubber Modified Asphalt uses tires at the end of their first life, which are already available in the North American market.
- The recycled material replaces a polymer derived from the oil-refining process.
- RMA is a locally sourced replacement for an ingredient that is generally imported from Asia.
- The use of RMA provides a highly resilient response to a long, complex supply chain.
- Michelin and Lehigh are proud to take end-of-life tires and give them new life in other products through innovation to keep valuable materials in service as long as possible with progressively less ecological impact.
- Over one million tons of US asphalt mix have been paved utilizing the Rheopave + MicroDyne 400-TR system which is comprised of all US sourced materials.
- Lehigh’s rubber powder alone over the past 10 years has been employed to produce over 5,000 miles of asphalt.
- Lehigh’s sustainable product blend can be used to modify liquid bitumen at an asphalt terminal or directly added at an asphalt contractor’s site via the Dry Process.
- Lehigh is one of several companies that have been at the forefront of developing a unique, cost-saving modification process to produce sustainable and long-lasting roadways.
- Rubber Modified Asphalt produced via the Dry Process with Lehigh’s state-of-the-art technologies provides a paving solution that is equally effective as oil-derived polymer modified asphalt, yet it’s cheaper and more friendly to the environment.
- With oil and gas prices growing higher each day, importing polymer is less attractive compared to utilizing better, more sustainable options available in the U.S.
