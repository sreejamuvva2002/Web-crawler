# Daimler Truck North America

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America recently announced two investments at its headquarters complex on Swan Island in Portland, Oregon: a new electric vehicle supply equipment training center near Electric Island and a new engineering facility at its campus.
- The addition to Electric Island represents the next phase of testing and development of charging infrastructure for commercial vehicles since the first-of-its-kind public charging station was built in partnership with Portland General Electric and opened in 2021.
- The anticipated project for the new training center will cost $3 million, with half of the costs funded through a forgivable loan from Prosper Portland, the City of Portland’s economic development agency.
- The new training center will be used by DTNA dealers and as a resource center for community stakeholders interested in clean transportation.
- John O’Leary, president and CEO of DTNA, stated that Daimler Truck North America appreciates the city and state’s support of its expansion plans to promote the development and proper testing of advanced technology and looks forward to collaborating on training and employing a future-ready workforce.
- The new DTNA engineering facility will be approximately 110,000 square feet in size and will consolidate testing activities under one roof with a focus on research and development for emerging propulsion technologies, such as battery-electric and hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- The anticipated project for the new engineering facility will cost $40 million and will be partially funded by the State of Oregon with a $700,000 forgivable loan from the state’s Strategic Reserve fund.
- Business Oregon Director Sophorn Cheang stated that Oregon is the location of choice for businesses leading clean technology innovation in the U.S., and that the partnership to support DTNA’s investment adds to the region’s competitiveness and will provide opportunities for a skilled workforce in manufacturing and engineering.
