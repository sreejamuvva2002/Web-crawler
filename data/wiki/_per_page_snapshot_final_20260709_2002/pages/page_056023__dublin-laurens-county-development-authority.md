# Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Incentives | Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority](https://business.jeffersoncountyalliance.com/business-advantages/incentives/)  ·  `page_056023`

## Facts

- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority serves as the primary conduit for business incentives offered to qualified industrial prospects relocating or expanding in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Incentives require signed performance agreements mandating companies to meet specific job and investment expectations.
- Manufacturers, distributors, and qualifying businesses may receive local property tax exemptions for a negotiated period based on commitments to new jobs and capital investment.
- Development Authority staff assists companies in structuring tax incentives to comply with local, state, and federal law.
- The Authority acts as a conduit for low-interest Industrial Revenue Bonds (IRBs) for qualifying projects that provide external letters of credit for collateral.
- Local leadership commits to expedite permitting and plan review for certain projects as allowed by law.
- Qualifying projects are eligible for grant funds through the Laurens County Closing Fund (LCCF) for real property improvements and other Georgia-law-allowed expenditures.
- Qualifying companies may receive donations of land in established industrial parks based on projected jobs and investment.
- Companies generating significant economic impact may receive assistance with building pad preparation.
- In 2005, Georgia became the first Southeast state to adopt a Single Factor Gross Receipts apportionment formula for corporate income tax.
- Georgia's corporate income tax rate is six percent, and it is one of only 13 states using Single Factor Apportionment.
- The Single Factor formula treats gross receipts in Georgia as the only factor for determining taxable income, significantly reducing effective rates for companies selling outside Georgia.
- Georgia does not use a Throw Back Rule to tax income from sales to out-of-state customers whose states lack the corresponding tax.
- Companies in strategic industries (manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, processing, telecommunications, broadcasting, tourism, and R&D) may qualify for the Job Tax Credit Program.
- Qualifying companies must create between five and 25 net new full-time jobs in year one depending on the community tier.
- Job Tax Credits accrue for additional jobs created in years two through five.
- The Job Tax Credit value ranges from $750 to $3,500 per job per year starting in the first taxable year.
- An additional $500 credit is available in counties participating in a multi-county Join Development Authority (JDA).
- Increased tax credits equal to Tier 1 credits are allowed for jobs created in Less Developed Census Tracts, Opportunity Zones, Military Zones, and Georgia's 40 least developed counties.
- Job Tax Credits may offset 100 percent of state corporate income tax liability in Tier 1 and 2 counties, or 50 percent in Tier 3 and 4 counties.
- Unused Job Tax Credits may be carried forward for 10 years from the close of the taxable year qualified jobs were established.
- In Tier 1 counties, excess Job Tax Credits may offset Georgia payroll withholding taxes with a $3,500 per job per year limitation.
- The Port Tax Credit Bonus is available to taxpayers increasing imports or exports through a Georgia port by 10 percent over the base year.
- Base year port traffic must be at least 75 net tons, five containers, or 10 TEUs, or these figures are used as the base if actual traffic is lower.
- Port Tax Credits can offset up to 50 percent of corporate income tax liability and may be carried forward for 10 years.
- The Port Tax Credit Bonus cannot be utilized with the Quality Jobs Tax Credit and is limited to Opportunity Zones, Military Zones, and Less Developed Census Tracts for existing large distribution centers.
- An additional $1,250 per job credit is added to the Job Tax Credit for taxpayers with qualified increases in port shipments.
- The Quality Jobs Tax Credit requires creating at least 50 jobs in a 12-month period paying at least 110 percent of the county average wage.
- The Quality Jobs Tax Credit ranges from $2,500 to $5,000 per job per year for up to five years, with new jobs within seven years qualifying.
- Quality Jobs Tax Credits may offset payroll withholding after other tax liability is exhausted and can be carried forward for 10 years.
- Georgia offers a 10 percent tax credit on increased research and development expenses subject to a base amount calculation.
- The R&D base amount equals current year Georgia gross receipts multiplied by the average ratio of qualified Georgia R&D expenses to gross receipts for the preceding three years, or 0.300, whichever is less.
- For new companies or those with no prior Georgia R&D expenditures, the base amount is 30 percent of current year Georgia gross receipts.
- The R&D credit applies to 50 percent of net Georgia income tax liability after other credits and can be applied to state payroll withholding in the first five years for new entities.
- Unused R&D credits may be carried forward for up to 10 years.
- Companies employing at least 1,800 new employees and investing a minimum of $450 million or having a $150 million annual payroll qualify for the Mega Project Tax Credit.
- The Mega Project Tax Credit provides $5,250 per job per year for the first five years, applied to corporate income tax with excess eligible for payroll withholding.
- Employers purchasing or building qualified child care facilities receive Georgia income tax credits equal to 100 percent of construction costs, spread over 10 years at 10 percent annually.
- Unused child care construction credits can be carried forward three years, and the facility must be state-licensed.
- Employers providing or sponsoring child care receive a credit against Georgia income tax equal to 75 percent of direct operating costs, with unused credits carried forward five years.
- All child care credits can be used against 50 percent of a taxpayer's income tax liability in a given year.
- The Work Opportunity Tax Credit Program (WOTC) is coordinated by the Georgia Department of Labor for hiring individuals from nine target groups facing employment barriers.
- WOTC reduces federal income tax liability by $1,200 to $9,000 per qualified employee, with $2,400 being the most frequent credit for adult new hires.
- Employers must receive Georgia Department of Labor certification before claiming WOTC on federal returns.
- Qualified equipment purchases or leases used in manufacturing are exempt from sales tax.
- Primary material handling equipment, computer equipment, and Class 100 (or less) clean room machinery, equipment, and materials can also be exempted under certain conditions.
- Effective January 1, 2011, business inventory is exempt from state property taxes at 0.25 mills.
- Many Georgia counties exempt up to 100 percent of qualified raw material, work-in-process, and finished goods inventory under the local-option Freeport law.
- Distribution and warehouse inventories are exempt if destined to be shipped out of state.
- Georgia's Foreign-Trade Zone (FTZ) program allows companies to defer, decrease, or eliminate duties on imported materials used in products assembled in Georgia.
- The Georgia Department of Labor assists recruitment by posting job notices, screening applications, providing interview space and scheduling, and hosting job fairs.
- GDOL works with private employment agencies that list jobs with the state.
- Quick Start is a nationally-ranked employee training program providing customized skill-based job training at no cost to qualifying companies.
- Quick Start provides training space, instructors, and materials, potentially saving companies millions in training costs.
- A retraining tax credit allows companies to claim 50 percent of direct training costs, up to $500 per full-time employee per approved program, totaling a maximum of $1,250 per employee per year.
- Retraining programs must be approved by the Technical College System of Georgia for quality and productivity enhancements or software technologies.
- The retraining tax credit offsets up to 50 percent of state corporate income tax liability and can be carried forward 10 years or combined with other credits.
- The HOPE Scholarship provides tuition assistance at one of Georgia's 35 public colleges or universities for graduating high school seniors with a B or better average.
- The HOPE Grant allows all Georgians to receive degrees or certificates at low cost through technical colleges and schools, aiding relocating families and company training initiatives.
- Worksource Heart of Georgia covers 17 counties including Appling, Bleckley, Candler, Dodge, Emanuel, Evans, Jeff Davis, Johnson, Laurens, Montgomery, Tattnall, Telfair, Toombs, Treutlen, Wayne, Wheeler, and Wilcox.
- The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) funds provide On-The-Job training, Youth Services, and Individual Training Accounts across the 17-county area.
