# Woory Industrial Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-07

**Source:** [EV parts supplier to build plant in Georgia - Rough Draft Atlanta](https://roughdraftatlanta.com/2023/06/07/new-ev-plant/)  ·  `page_015389`

## Facts

- South Korea-based auto parts maker Woory Industrial Co. will build its first U.S. manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday.
- Woory Industrial Co. will invest an estimated $18 million in a facility in Dublin.
- The new facility will create more than 130 jobs.
- The company specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components for both electric and internal combustion vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated: “We’re proud to welcome Woory Industrial as the latest job creator to help us on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp added: “This emerging industry continues to bring historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities all over the state, thanks to our highly skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.”
- Unlike internal combustion engines, EVs do not have a heating source, making heaters required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Pioneering development by Woory has made the company a leader in EV heating technology.
- The company is also working to develop other new products for EVs as well as hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- Jungwoo Kim, Woory’s CEO, stated: “We are facing a revolution in the automobile industry for the first time in 100 years.”
- Jungwoo Kim added: “The transition to electric vehicles is the future, and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automobile markets.”
- Woory will be hiring for positions from all fields necessary for an automotive parts plant.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program to land the project.
- The new plant is expected to begin operations in November.
