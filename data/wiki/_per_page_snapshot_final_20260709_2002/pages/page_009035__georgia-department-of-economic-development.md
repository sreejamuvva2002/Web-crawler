# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-31

**Source:** [GA sustains momentum for economic development](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/01/ga-sustains-momentum-for-economic-development/)  ·  `page_009035`

## Facts

- Georgia sustained economic development momentum during the first half of Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1 through December 31, 2022).
- GDEcD’s Global Commerce division supported 218 locations or expansions expected to create approximately 17,500 new jobs and more than $13 billion in investment for the state.
- Both the number of locations and total investment amount increased over the same period last year.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the sustained momentum through economic development projects in key industries.
- Areas outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region accounted for 85 percent of jobs created and 92 percent of investments from economic development projects during the period.
- Advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments resulting from these projects increased by more than 38 percent compared to the same time last year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted Georgia Quick Start job training and GRAD certified sites as proven solutions for accelerated project timelines.
- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment, after advanced manufacturing.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives for the groundbreaking of the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho attended the HMGMA groundbreaking.
- The HMGMA EV facility will support more than 8,100 direct jobs in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham Counties.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following HMGMA’s groundbreaking.
- These supplier additions swiftly surpassed the initial $1 billion in investment predicted to come from such additions.
- International companies have been valued partners in creating a closed-loop electric mobility supply chain and accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022.
- SK On and Hyundai Motor Group formed a partnership in Bartow County.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery announced a Coweta County facility.
- Both the SK On/Hyundai and FREYR Battery projects are in the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Battery recyclers Sung-Il Hitech and Denkai America joined Georgia’s growing electric mobility ecosystem.
- The aerospace and defense industries created more than 1,500 new jobs.
- Archer Aviation Inc. announced a facility near airports to advance sustainable urban air mobility.
- Anduril Industries announced a facility near airports for defense technology.
- Both Archer Aviation and Anduril will benefit from nearby top-ranked technical colleges and four-year universities.
- Advanced manufacturing is a cornerstone industry sector in Georgia’s middle and coastal regions.
- YKK AP announced a major investment or expansion in Macon.
- Beretta Holding S.A. announced a new facility in Bryan County through its subsidiary Norma Precision Inc.
- The advanced manufacturing sector has announced more than 7,700 new jobs in the state since July 1, 2022.
- Germany-based Haering Precision celebrated the grand opening of its Lavonia facility in November.
- Haering Precision's opening event included a “Future Day” for approximately 400 local high school students and community members.
- Companies in the food processing industry announced more than $695 million in investments, largely through expansions.
- King’s Hawaiian announced another expansion of its Oakwood facility, marking its second expansion in less than a year.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman emphasized workforce challenges and the Georgia Quick Start program for upskilling Georgians.
- Georgia Tech hosted a groundbreaking for its Science Square purpose-built district in August.
- The Rowen Foundation’s 2,000-acre science and learning campus broke ground in early December.
- Athena Studios announced it will add an additional 350,000 square feet to its Athens facility.
- Athena Studios' expansion includes a structure dedicated to training the local workforce through a partnership with the University of Georgia and Georgia Film Academy.
- BlueStar Studios announced more than 600,000 square feet of new stage space at the former Fort Gillem outside of Atlanta.
- BlueStar Studios will also add an additional 45,000 square feet dedicated to technical resources and amenities, including refurbished buildings preserving historic architecture.
