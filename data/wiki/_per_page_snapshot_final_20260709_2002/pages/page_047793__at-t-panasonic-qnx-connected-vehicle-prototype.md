# AT&T-Panasonic-QNX Connected Vehicle Prototype

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-01-09

**Source:** [AT&T's Watson coming to cars: won't mind if you ask it if you're there yet](https://www.engadget.com/2012-01-09-atandt-watson-for-cars-announced.html)  ·  `page_047793`

## Facts

- On Jan. 9, 2012, AT&T announced at CES that it is partnering with Panasonic and QNX to bring its Watson speech-recognition technology to cars.
- The technology will be integrated into cloud-connected vehicles to respond to voice commands for controlling GPS or changing radio channels.
- Initial testing of the technology is currently taking place in Peachtree City, Georgia.
- AT&T is providing the network services for the project.
- Panasonic and QNX are supplying the hardware for the prototype.
- The initiative operates within the Peachtree "Connected" City, GA collaboration framework.
- AT&T's Watson speech engine is a multimodal and multilingual speech and language engine with best-in-class performance across applications.
- AT&T holds more than 500 speech-related patents that can process speech, text, and gesture inputs for calls, navigation, and web browsing.
- Glenn Lurie, president of emerging devices, resale and partnerships at AT&T, stated that wireless innovations in the automotive space continue to expand and are great news for consumers.
- The prototype aims to demonstrate a connectivity strategy for automotive virtual assistance and limit driver distraction by focusing on customer needs and system interface usability.
- Derek Kuhn, vice president of sales and marketing at QNX Software Systems, noted that natural and intuitive user experiences are key to consumer adoption of connected vehicles.
- AT&T and Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America formed a working agreement in 2011 to explore and test connectivity solutions for consumers and commuters.
- The collaboration framework allows the two companies to jointly create customized products for global automotive manufacturers in North America.
- Initial concept testing for in-car infotainment systems and emerging mobile devices interface is currently taking place in the Peachtree City testing community.
