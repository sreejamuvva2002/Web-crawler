# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-06

**Source:** [Dongwon to invest US$30m in Georgia facility | Automotive World](https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/dongwon-to-invest-us30m-in-georgia-facility/)  ·  `page_053955`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology will invest US$30m in a new production facility in Emanuel County, Georgia.
- The investment will create 200 jobs over the coming years.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology is a South Korean automotive parts supplier that manufactures doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The new facility will be constructed at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- Construction is set to begin in 2026.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971 and already operates a facility in Hogansville, Georgia, which will remain operational.
- The new site represents the company's second Georgia location and is part of its expansion across the Southeast, where it currently supports more than 500 direct jobs.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has been operating in the United States since 2003.
- Heather Hollstein serves as the Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology.
- The company will hire for positions including management, system leads, and production personnel, with recruitment expected to begin in 2026.
- Pat Wilson is a Commissioner at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development stated that the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers choose strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia's ports.
