# Georgia EV & Clean Energy Supply Chain & Workforce Initiatives

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-11

**Source:** [Major investment in electric buses and training - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/major-investment-in-electric-buses-and-training/)  ·  `page_001260`

## Facts

- Students in the Richmond County School System will benefit from a $1 billion federal investment in clean energy buses.
- Goodwill prepares to train the workforce needed to support the electric vehicle industry.
- Senator Raphael Warnock (D-Ga) and EPA Administrator Michael Regan announced the funding through Warnock’s Clean Commute for Kids Act, which was included in the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Nearly $60 million will fund 25 buses for Richmond County School System, along with buses for DeKalb, Bibb, Clayton, and Glynn County Schools, as well as Marietta and Carrollton City Schools.
- Senator Warnock stated: “This is about converting fleets of diesel-powered school buses into clean energy vehicles for the future. This is a ride to the future, and this investment demonstrates the power of bipartisan cooperation to deliver tangible results for our communities.”
- Electric buses can cost more than twice that of a diesel bus, but the investment balances when factoring in future costs for fuel, parts, and maintenance.
- Electric energy to refuel the buses costs less than diesel, and with fewer moving parts, the cost to maintain electric buses is lower.
- Finding a trained workforce to service and maintain EVs is a challenge, particularly in Georgia, which has seen a shift toward facilities that manufacture EVs along with supporting industries.
- Goodwill Industries International partnered with Accenture to launch “The Goodwill Clean Tech Accelerator.”
- The workforce training program addresses the most in-demand, entry-level, clean energy jobs.
- Participants will gain skills for jobs in electric vehicle charging and other industries, including solar.
- Steve Preston, President and CEO of Goodwill Industries International, stated: “As our labor market transitions, we see important opportunities for people to move into more promising roles with better pay. It is essential that we provide the training and other support needed to ensure people capture these opportunities.”
- Preston added: “The Goodwill Clean Tech Accelerator will open doors for people in an expanding industry and provide support to employers who are helping us transition to a more sustainable world.”
- The accelerator will provide skills and certifications for job opportunities with higher wages and better career advancement.
- The program will target under and unemployed individuals.
- Nearly 25 million children ride more than 500,000 predominantly diesel buses to school in the U.S., exposing children to polluted air.
- Warnock noted: “Exposure to diesel exhaust from these school buses negatively affects the health and well-being of children, especially those with asthma or other pulmonary ailments. This federal funding will help support and grow jobs at Georgia companies like Fort Valley’s Blue Bird Corporation, which has been an industry leader in green commute technology.”
- Augusta is jumping into green energy technology with the construction of Aurubis Richmond, LLC, currently under construction in the Augusta Corporate Park off Mike Padget Highway.
- The Aurubis plant will recycle copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials nationwide once operational.
- Operation is expected to be early this year.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated during a recent visit: “The world needs metal which can be recycled without loss of quality or performance for cars, mobile phones, batteries, cables, and computers.”
- The plant will have the capacity to process up to 90,000 tons of complex metals, including printed circuit boards.
- Aurubis will be joined by Denkai America, which will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American headquarters in Richmond County.
- Denkai America’s initial investment will be $150 million to establish an ED copper foil production facility, with a focus on electric vehicle (EV) battery foil.
- The facility will be located on a 115-acre site.
- Denkai America aims to invest a total of $430 million and create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
