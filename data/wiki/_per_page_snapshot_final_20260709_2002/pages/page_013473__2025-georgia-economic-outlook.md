# 2025 Georgia Economic Outlook

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-06

**Source:** [Georgia’s economy will grow in 2025, but more slowly](https://news.uga.edu/georgias-economy-will-grow-in-2025-but-more-slowly/)  ·  `page_013473`

## Facts

- Georgia’s economy is projected to grow by 2.4% in 2025, a slowdown from the 3.1% growth seen in 2024.
- Georgia’s GDP is projected to grow faster than the nation’s, which forecasters project to grow by 1.6% in 2025.
- The economic slowdown is attributed to a deliberate policy shift rather than an external economic shock or factor outside state control.
- Inflation successfully decreased from 8% in 2022 to 3% in 2024 and is projected to remain at 3% in 2025.
- The Federal Reserve began reducing monetary policy restraint in September by cutting interest rates, with additional rate cuts expected in 2025.
- Quantitative tightening is expected to end, leading to moderate expansion of credit and money supply.
- The probability of Georgia slipping into a recession is 25%.
- The number of jobs in Georgia is projected to rise by 1% in 2025, slower than the 1.5% gain estimated for 2024 but higher than the national job growth rate of 0.6%.
- Georgia’s unemployment rate is expected to increase from 3.7% to 4%, remaining lower than the projected U.S. level of 4.3%.
- The rise in unemployment will be driven by slowed hiring rather than layoffs.
- Health care and housing are projected to lead Georgia’s economic growth in 2025.
- A growing population and increasing number of senior residents will continue to boost health care hiring across the state.
- Homebuilders are expected to ramp up construction of single-family homes due to slightly lower mortgage rates, favorable demographics, and a post-pandemic housing shortage.
- Georgia homebuilders will increase the number of single-family homes built in 2025 by 9%.
- Home prices, which have increased 65% since before the COVID-19 pandemic, are expected to hold steady.
- Georgia is likely to experience job losses in retail and information sectors due to technological advances and competition from online retailers.
- Georgia has broken records in attracting new jobs and industries, though this pace will slow due to the national and global economic slowdown.
- The build-out of existing projects is expected to create thousands of new jobs over the next few years.
- Advantages for economic development include a favorable mix of industries, supportive demographics, excellent transportation and logistics infrastructure, a competitive tax climate, and low costs of living and doing business.
