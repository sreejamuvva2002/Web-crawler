# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Strategic EV Battery Partnerships: How Hyundai and LG Energy Solution's $4.3 Billion JV Positions Them for IRA-Driven Growth](https://www.ainvest.com/news/strategic-ev-battery-partnerships-hyundai-lg-energy-solution-4-3-billion-jv-positions-ira-driven-growth-2507/)  ·  `page_056174`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) have formed a $4.3 billion joint venture to build an EV battery cell manufacturing plant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) of 2022 offers a $7,500 tax credit for eligible vehicles.
- To qualify for the IRA tax credit, 60% of a vehicle's battery components must be assembled in North America, and critical minerals must come from the U.S. or its trade partners.
- The Georgia plant is set to begin production by late 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours (GWh), enough to power 300,000 EVs.
- The facility will supply battery packs to Hyundai's U.S. plants.
- The IRA tax credit is projected to generate $41.7 billion in investments and 72,300 jobs in facilities already producing eligible vehicles.
- An additional $20.2 billion in future investments is tied to upcoming projects related to the IRA.
- Hyundai's Georgia plant is part of a $61.9 billion surge in IRA-driven investments.
- Tax-credit-eligible EVs are expected to command 60% of U.S. sales by 2030.
- The U.S.-China trade war has exposed vulnerabilities in global supply chains, particularly in the EV battery sector.
- In January 2025, the Pentagon added CATL to its “Chinese military companies” list.
- The IRA's sourcing requirements are forcing automakers to diversify their supply chains.
- The partnership aligns with the Trump administration's aggressive tariff policies.
- Volkswagen, BMW, and Ford are also shifting battery production to North America.
- Hyundai and LGES already collaborate on battery production in Indonesia and South Korea.
- The Georgia facility is part of Hyundai's broader $21 billion U.S. investment plan from 2025 to 2028.
- The plant will supply battery packs to the Hyundai Metaplant America, which is set to produce 1.2 million EVs annually.
- The Hyundai Metaplant America will produce vehicle models including the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai's parts subsidiary, will assemble battery packs on-site at the Georgia facility.
- The project is expected to create 3,000 direct jobs by 2030.
- The project is expected to create over 8,500 indirect jobs by 2030.
- The project reinforces Georgia's emergence as the “e-mobility capital” of the U.S.
- Hyundai's U.S. EV sales are projected to grow from 150,000 units in 2024 to 500,000 by 2030.
- The IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 are expected to qualify for the full $7,500 tax credit.
- LGES is expanding in the U.S. with a $5.5 billion Arizona plant.
- The EV battery market is growing at 15% annually.
- By 2026, IRA criteria will require 80% of critical minerals to be sourced domestically.
