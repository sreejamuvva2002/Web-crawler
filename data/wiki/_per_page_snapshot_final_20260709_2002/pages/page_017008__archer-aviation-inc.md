# Archer Aviation, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-08

**Source:** [Archer Nears Completion of eVTOL Manufacturing Facility - Advanced Air Mobility Intl](https://www.aaminternational.com/2024/08/archer-nears-completion-of-evtol-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_017008`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation provided an update on the construction progress of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA, reaffirming an expected 2024 completion date.
- In preparation for the facility’s completion, Archer showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport.
- Guests at the showcase included government officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- Archer completed the first build phase earlier this year, inclusive of grading and foundation work.
- The facility is nearing completion of phase two, which involves the erection of the exterior walls and roof.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Once complete, the facility will be nearly 400,000 square feet.
- The facility will support Archer’s planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer’s buildout is taking place in close partnership with Stellantis.
- Archer and Stellantis are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft together.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and Founder of Archer, stated that thanks to the hard work of the Archer and Stellantis teams, they are tracking towards completing this manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Once complete, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year.
- Archer aims to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- Midnight is Archer’s electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft.
- The Midnight aircraft is built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation.
- The Midnight travels at speeds up to 150mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- The Midnight is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The Midnight is 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes, nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- The Midnight features redundant systems across the aircraft allowing Archer to target similar levels of safety as commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
