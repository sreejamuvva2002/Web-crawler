# Volvo Technology of America

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2031-09-30

**Source:** [Volvo to receive $208M to accelerate heavy-duty electric truck production | WardsAuto](https://www.automotivedive.com/news/volvo-funding-domestic-manufacturing-conversion-plants/721737/)  ·  `page_014259`

## Facts

- The Energy Department awarded $208 million to Volvo Technology of America to help fast-track the production of heavy-duty electric trucks and related powertrain components.
- The funding will assist Volvo Group’s Lehigh Valley Operations truck assembly site in Macungie, Pennsylvania.
- The funding will assist Volvo Group’s New River Valley truck location in Dublin, Virginia.
- Dublin, Virginia is Volvo’s largest truck manufacturing plant in the world.
- Facilities will be upgraded and move toward mixed model assembly, allowing for scalability and flexibility.
- Pennsylvania Sen. Bob Casey stated that the upgrades enable a novel manufacturing approach that will significantly increase the production capacity potential of battery electric vehicles (BEV)/fuel cell electric vehicles (FCEV).
- A project summary noted that the changes will help improve BEV and FCEV production processes.
- The money will help convert the facilities to zero-emission sites, part of Volvo Group’s goal to be 100% fossil-free by 2040.
- A Hagerstown, Maryland, plant will get upgrades to support powertrain operations, according to a Volvo spokesperson.
- Stephen Roy, chairman of Volvo Group North America and president of Mack Trucks, stated that through facility upgrades and employee training, this grant will help our U.S. plants more efficiently produce the innovative trucks and zero-emission powertrain components essential to this transition.
- Funding comes through the Domestic Manufacturing Conversion grant program.
- The program will create 295 new union jobs for Volvo and Mack, according to Sen. Casey.
- The $2 billion program is available through Sept. 30, 2031.
- The program supports domestic production of hybrid, electric and hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, per a federal website.
- ZF Axle Drives Marysville was awarded nearly $158 million to convert part of its Marysville, Michigan, facility from internal combustion engine driveline component production to EV components for potential new customers.
- Cummins will spend $75 million and receive a matching amount to convert an approximately 360,000-square-foot portion of an engine plant in Columbus, Indiana.
- The conversion is for the manufacturing of zero-emission components and electric powertrain systems.
- Cummins stated that the $75 million grant is the largest federal grant ever awarded solely to Cummins and is part of appropriations related to the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Cummins plans to reach net-zero emissions by 2050.
