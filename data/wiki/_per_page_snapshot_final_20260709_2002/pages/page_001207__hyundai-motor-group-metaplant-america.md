# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-05

**Source:** [Hyundai to supply Georgia EV plant via fuel cell trucks - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2024/12/05/hyundai-to-supply-georgia-ev-plant-via-fuel-cell-trucks/)  ·  `page_001207`

## Facts

- Hyundai announced it will supply fuel cell trucks to its Georgia EV plant in cooperation with its subsidiary Glovis America.
- Glovis America provides logistics operations for the operation and manages project cargoes and project freight logistics involving oversized, heavy-lift or dangerous goods in various amounts and types.
- When in operation, new Xcient hydrogen fuel-cell Class 8 heavy-duty trucks will transport vehicle parts from HMGMA suppliers across the region to the factory in Georgia.
- The 21 Xcient trucks will represent more than one-third of the Glovis America truck fleet at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- When introduced to the US market, the Xcient fuel cell truck was revealed to run on two 90 kW hydrogen fuel cell systems (totaling 180 kW power) and a 350 kW electric motor.
- The Xcient fuel cell truck allows for over 450 miles of range.
- A mobile hydrogen refuelling station will be set up at the Megasite in Georgia to fuel the trucks.
- A more permanent hydrogen production and refueling station is planned for the Megasite, though it is being developed and will be announced at a later date.
- Oscar Kwon serves as the CEO of HMGMA.
- Oscar Kwon stated that HMGMA is committed to creating sustainable transportation solutions.
- Oscar Kwon noted that the Hyundai Xcient hydrogen fuel-cell trucks supporting the Metaplant release zero emissions and offer a cleaner alternative to traditional freight logistics methods used at other manufacturing facilities.
