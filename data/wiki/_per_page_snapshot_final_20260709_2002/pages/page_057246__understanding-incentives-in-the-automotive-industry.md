# Understanding Incentives in the Automotive Industry

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Understanding Incentives in the Automotive Industry](https://www.blsstrategies.com/insights-press/understanding-incentives-in-the-automotive-industry)  ·  `page_057246`

## Facts

- Economic development incentives play an integral role in influencing corporate location decisions for new automotive jobs and investment.
- Incentives can take the form of cash grants; tax credits, exemptions or abatements; free or discounted land and buildings; and employment training assistance.
- These incentives are able to materially offset large upfront investments required by production facilities, mitigate risks, and/or reduce projected operating costs.
- State legislatures set aside large sums called “deal closing funds” or “governor’s funds” to attract new jobs and investment.
- Stipulations such as pay-for-performance measures are commonplace for cash grant programs, requiring job creation and investment milestones to be achieved before payments are received.
- Cash grant awards can range from several hundred thousand dollars to $10 million (or more) depending on the state and project.
- The Texas Enterprise Fund (TEF) provides typical awards ranging from $5,000 to $10,000 per job based on jobs created, average annual wage, capital investment, and fund availability.
- TEF requires a minimum of 25 new jobs for rural areas and at least 75 new jobs for urban areas.
- In 2016, the TEF program attracted CGT US Limited to New Braunfels, Texas, awarding a $2 million grant after the company committed to reshoring manufacturing from China, creating 275 jobs and investing $80 million.
- Toyota received a $40 million TEF award for its North American Headquarters relocation to Plano in 2014.
- The Michigan Business Development Program (MBDP) is a discretionary cash grant program offering pay-per-performance awards approaching $10,000 per new job created.
- MBDP requires at least 50 new jobs in developed counties and at least 25 new jobs in rural counties.
- In 2016, Magna Seating Inc. received a $740,000 MBDP grant for its Highland Park manufacturing facility expansion after committing to creating 148 new jobs and investing $6 million.
- While most states offer statutory sales and use tax exemptions on machinery and equipment, only a limited number offer discretionary exemptions or refunds on building or construction materials.
- Forgiveness of sales tax on construction materials can save projects millions in construction costs during early cash-flow-sensitive phases.
- Kentucky Enterprise Initiative Act (KEIA) offers a discretionary refund of 6% sales and use taxes paid on building and construction materials.
- KEIA requires a minimum investment of $500,000 in building and construction expenditures and provides the incentive for up to seven years.
- In 2016, MACA Plastics received a $33,000 KEIA award to renovate a 122,600 square foot facility in Maysville, KY, as part of a $7.9 million investment while committing to creating 156 new jobs supplying automotive components.
- Real and personal property tax abatements typically extend for up to ten years at varying percentages and are approved at the local government level.
- Some states require a public ownership model utilizing bond issuances where a government body leases property back to the company, with lease payments equivalent to a reduced property tax levy (FILOT/PILOT).
- Dana Light Axle Products used Missouri’s “Chapter 100” program in 2016 to abate half of its personal property taxes for seven years, saving $1.4 million while committing to create 135 new jobs and invest $39 million at its Columbia, MO plant.
- Illinois, Michigan, and Ohio do not levy property taxes on manufacturing personal property.
- Some states offer discretionary tax incentives based on incremental employee payroll, allowing retention or refund of withholding taxes or offering refundable/non-refundable corporate income tax credits.
- The Missouri Works Program offers a payroll benefit of up to 7% of new employee payroll for up to six years, combining withholding tax retention with transferable tax credits.
- In 2014, Martinrea received a $3.2 million award from the Missouri Works Program for a new 275,000 square foot facility in Riverside, committing to invest $51.6 million and supply the nearby GM OEM plant.
- Grants for public infrastructure improvements are widely used throughout the Midwest and Southeast, with state governments transferring funds to local governments for water/sewer, road, rail, and site preparation improvements.
- The Mississippi Development Infrastructure Grant Program allows counties or municipalities to apply on behalf of companies for state funding based on job creation, investment, and infrastructure needs.
- Tennessee’s FastTrack Infrastructure Development Program provided a $5 million grant to DENSO Manufacturing Inc. in 2015 for land acquisition and site prep as part of a $400 million expansion creating 500 new jobs in Maryville, TN.
- Georgia and South Carolina provide non-refundable job creation tax credits that vary in amount by county.
- In 2016, LINDE + WIEMANN received an estimated $3 million in Georgia Jobs Tax Credit for a new 63,500 square foot manufacturing facility and office in Hart County, investing $35 million and committing to creating 200 new jobs.
- Indiana’s Hoosier Business Investment Tax Credit offers a non-refundable credit equal to ten percent of qualifying capital investment with a carry forward for up to nine years.
- Non-refundable tax credits only have value if the company has sufficient tax liability against which to use them.
- Many electric providers offer “economic development riders” establishing lower rates per kWh for qualified projects, typically ranging from two to five years.
- Utility incentives often include minimum kW demand requirements (ranging from 10-25 kW to 1,000 kW, with most requiring 200 kW or more) and minimum job creation/investment targets.
- Operations with high load factors may be eligible for additional savings on utility rates.
- The Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) can offer performance grants and low-interest loans for qualifying projects.
- Additional utility incentives include energy efficiency programs, renewable energy programs, and renewable electric commodity options.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program provides extremely flexible and customized workforce training to prospective workers, mitigating talent risk for manufacturing start-ups.
- South Carolina’s readySC program similarly provides customized training to mitigate talent risk in the automotive industry.
- Special legislation can be used to create customized incentive offers outside statutory menus for projects with significant job creation, capital investment, or emerging technology.
- In 2014, Tesla announced a gigafactory near Sparks, Nevada, creating 6,500 jobs and investing $10 billion, secured by an estimated $1.3 billion incentive package requiring special state legislation including twenty-year sales tax exemptions, ten-year property/corporate tax reductions, and direct-to-consumer sales authorization.
- Mississippi used a special legislative deal valuing $596 million to attract Continental Tire’s commercial vehicle tire plant in Clinton, securing 2,500 jobs and $1.45 billion in investment through grants, twenty-five-year reduced corporate income tax, property abatements, and payroll retention.
- Realizable incentive value depends on flexibility, usability, scalability, and compatibility with other programs rather than just the total dollar amount awarded.
- Legal prohibitions often exist against combining certain incentive programs for the same project.
- Companies must maintain “but for” status until all incentive approvals are achieved, as moving forward too quickly risks ineligibility.
- Timing of real estate acquisitions, equipment purchase orders, hiring, and public announcements is critical to maintaining eligibility for most incentive programs.
