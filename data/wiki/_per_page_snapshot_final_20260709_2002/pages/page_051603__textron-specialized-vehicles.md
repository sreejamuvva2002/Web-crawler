# Textron Specialized Vehicles

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2017-04-06

**Source:** [The Masters Tournament Helps Highlight Augusta-Based Textron Specialized Vehicles | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/blogs/georgia-at-work/2017/04/06/the-masters-tournament-helps-highlight-augusta-based-textron)  ·  `page_051603`

## Facts

- Textron is the umbrella company that manufactures E-Z-Go golf cars, which have been produced in Augusta since 1954.
- Brandon Haddock serves as Textron Specialized Vehicles’ director of communications.
- Textron employs between 1,400 and 1,500 people in Georgia, with approximately 1,200 working daily in Augusta.
- Textron operates a plant in Kennesaw that manufactures ground support equipment for the aviation industry.
- Textron manufactures hundreds of different models of vehicles and equipment in Augusta for the golf industry, commercial businesses, and consumers.
- Textron acquired the former Proctor & Gamble facility located near its headquarters in South Augusta.
- The acquired site contains about 600,000 square feet of manufacturing space on a 240-acre plot.
- Textron has been building at the new site for over a year as of the interview date.
- Textron announced it would integrate and relocate the manufacturing and operations of its sister company Jacobsen from Charlotte to Augusta.
- The first Jacobsen equipment rolled off the new Augusta assembly line last month, with full relocation from Charlotte expected by the end of the year.
- Jacobsen manufactures professional turf care equipment for golf courses, professional landscapers, and municipal and commercial turf care providers.
- The expansion and Jacobsen relocation were originally announced in 2016.
- Textron expects to create between 500 and 600 new jobs over the next five years in Augusta.
- New positions will include assemblers, specialized skilled labor (welders, machinists), product engineers, and finance, HR, and administrative support staff.
- Approximately one month before the interview, Textron partnered with the Georgia Department of Labor and the Georgia Department of Economic Development for a job fair that drew over 1,000 applicants for approximately 70 positions.
- Textron primarily seeks employees with a high school degree, a strong work ethic, and a willingness to contribute ideas to improve operations.
- Skilled roles like welders and engineers require additional training and experience, with Textron recruiting actively from regional universities and colleges.
- Based on industry trends, Textron expects to add between 150 and 250 additional jobs within the next 18 to 24 months.
- During The Masters Tournament week, Textron hosts plant tours and operates a hospitality house to entertain customers and partners.
