# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-03-18

**Source:** [OSHA: Georgia Goodyear plant lapses put employees at serious risk - Salon.com](https://www.salon.com/2018/03/18/osha-georgia-goodyear-plant-lapses-put-employees-at-serious-risk_partner/)  ·  `page_055152`

## Facts

- A Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. plant in Georgia faces about $70,000 in fines for failing to follow federal workplace safety laws that endangered workers.
- Investigators from the federal Occupational Safety and Health Administration imposed seven serious citations against the company in January after inspecting its plant last year in Social Circle, Georgia.
- Federal officials found the company failed to provide proper protective gear to workers processing rubber through hot metal presses with temperatures exceeding 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
- Investigators discovered hazards from unguarded machines, rainwater and leaking equipment that put workers at risk of slipping on the production floor.
- William Fulcher, the OSHA area office director in Atlanta, stated that the inspection found multiple safety deficiencies that put employees at risk of serious injury or death.
- Goodyear has contested the fines, according to an OSHA spokesman.
- Laura Duda, a Goodyear spokeswoman, wrote that the company cooperated with OSHA during its inspection and is following the standard administrative process for responding to citations.
- A December investigation by Reveal showed how Goodyear’s lax approach to safety contributed to the deaths of motorists on the road and workers in its plants.
- Goodyear ranked among the top five manufacturers in the United States for worker deaths since 2009, according to Reveal’s analysis.
- At least four motorists over the last seven years have died in accidents after tires made at Goodyear plants failed.
- Reveal’s investigation found that intense production demands and leaks in the roof of Goodyear’s plants have endangered both workers and consumers.
- Court documents and former workers from Goodyear’s plants in Danville, Virginia, and Fayetteville, North Carolina, detailed how water leaked through the roof and onto machines.
- Workers are trained to avoid moisture in tire production because it can prompt treads to separate, causing a blowout.
- Former employees of Goodyear’s Danville and Fayetteville plants reported feeling pressure to put production before workplace safety.
- Some former employees recalled a quota-driven motto invoked on the shop floor: “Round and black and out the back.”
- Hours after Reveal’s investigation was published, Goodyear issued a statement acknowledging it fell short of its own expectations for safety over the past two years.
- Barbara Hatala, Goodyear’s communications manager for the Americas Operations, called the safety failures unacceptable and mourned the loss of valued coworkers at two U.S. manufacturing plants.
- The Trump administration has rolled back and postponed Obama-era workplace safety protections in keeping with goals laid out by the National Association of Manufacturers.
- Richard Kramer, Goodyear’s chairman, chief executive officer and president, serves on the board of the National Association of Manufacturers.
- Since October 2008, Goodyear has been fined more than $1.9 million for more than 200 health and workplace safety violations, far more than its four major competitors combined.
- Besides the most recent proposed OSHA fines of $69,058, Goodyear’s plant in Social Circle, Georgia, has been previously cited for three workplace safety violations, two of them serious.
- Jordan Barab, former deputy assistant secretary of OSHA under Obama, described the most recent fines as “deeply discouraging.”
- Barab stated that despite multiple preventable deaths and serious injuries in plants across the country, Goodyear never seems to learn that it is legally and morally required to provide safe workplaces for its employees.
