# TriNet

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-18

**Source:** [TriNet to Create 750 Jobs in Metro Atlanta | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2025/trinet-create-750-jobs-metro-atlanta)  ·  `page_003715`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on March 18, 2025 that TriNet plans to create 750 new jobs at a new corporate center in Dunwoody over the next five years.
- The expansion represents an estimated $15.4 million in investment in DeKalb County.
- TriNet provides comprehensive HR solutions, technology, expertise, and access to world-class benefits that enable small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) to attract and develop top-tier talent.
- Mike Simonds serves as TriNet President and CEO.
- Catherine Wragg serves as TriNet Chief People Officer.
- The new corporate center will occupy approximately 150,000 square feet of space in Dunwoody.
- TriNet will immediately begin hiring for technology, HR consulting, client management, and sales roles.
- The company plans to leverage its increased local presence to grow its regional Atlanta and Southeast customer base.
- Dunwoody Mayor Lynn Deutsch highlighted the city's access to a talented workforce and vibrant mixed-use environment as ideal for TriNet's location.
- DeKalb County CEO Lorraine Cochran-Johnson stated that the investment reinforces DeKalb as a premier destination for corporate growth and innovation.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, noted TriNet's unique position to connect businesses with emerging talent from metro Atlanta’s renowned universities.
- John Soper, Assistant Director of Statewide Projects, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the City of Dunwoody, Decide DeKalb, Metro Atlanta Chamber, University System of Georgia, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized that strong partnerships between industry, communities, and higher education drive economic growth in Georgia.
- TriNet’s suite of products includes services and software-based solutions to streamline workflows by connecting HR, benefits, employee engagement, payroll, and time and attendance for SMBs.
