# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies Santa Ana

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2025 IndustryWeek Best Plants: Freudenberg-NOK Santa Ana Leads with Lean | IndustryWeek](https://www.industryweek.com/members/article/55327370/2025-industryweek-best-plants-freudenberg-nok-santa-ana-leads-with-lean)  ·  `page_020454`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Santa Ana produces gaskets and O-rings that fit between two surfaces to form seals under radically different circumstances with serious consequences for failure.
- The company's gaskets are used in jet engines, landing gear, hydraulic systems, aircraft fuel controls, power transmissions, gas pumps, construction and agriculture equipment, and soda dispensers.
- Freudenberg-NOK Santa Ana operates on a low-volume high-stakes production model that requires impeccable quality standards.
- The plant won the 2025 IndustryWeek Best Plants Award.
- The plant received the 2022 and 2024 Raytheon/RTX Supplier Platinum Award, as well as a Best Lean Award and Six Sigma Award.
- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies Santa Ana is located in Santa Ana, California.
- The facility employs 175 people.
- The plant occupies 39,000 square feet of space.
- Primary products include O-rings and injection/compression molded gaskets.
- The site started up in 1975 as International Seal Co. before being purchased by Freudenberg-NOK in 1998.
- Eugen Amann serves as the Application Center Manager at Freudenberg-NOK Santa Ana.
- Gaskets used aboard aircraft must withstand high-energy systems, temperature variance, and chemical interactions.
- Landing gear gaskets maintain an aircraft's tire pressure under landing pressure while withstanding temperature differentials between negative 45 degrees Fahrenheit and 158 degrees Fahrenheit.
- O-rings inside jet engines handle long-term continuous operating temperatures close to 400 degrees Fahrenheit as well as chemical compatibility with jet engine turbine lubricants.
- Gaskets for heat exchanger systems handle high-pressure, high-temperature air from the engine and low-pressure, low-temperature air in the wings and cabin.
- The plant produces O-rings and other gasket shapes for about 250 customers using 90 different compounds.
- Uncured rubber is closely inspected before entering inventory and then prepared for molding via compression or injection molds.
- Molded parts are sometimes trimmed by hand or de-flashed cryogenically, where excess material is snap-frozen and cleanly removed.
- Finished rings are inspected, packaged, and shipped after the manufacturing process.
- The plant uses visual cues, labels, and poka-yoke error prevention methods throughout the factory floor, testing labs, quality control area, offices, bathrooms, and cafeteria.
- Raw material for gaskets is stored in blue bins, newly-formed gaskets go in yellow boxes, scrap goes into red boxes, and finished gaskets go into green bins.
- To prevent mixing up similar parts like 10-mm and 11-mm rubber O-rings, the plant uses a to-scale watermark of each O-ring on digital or paper orders for quick visual comparison.
- Employee work teams have autonomy over production scheduling, skills certification, safety and environmental compliance, quality assurance, daily job assignments, performance reviews, training, hiring, and materials management.
- Vacation spending is handled by work teams on a first-come, first-served basis, and team leads weigh in on disciplinary actions and terminations.
- The plant offers gain-sharing rewards for individual performance, rewards for team performance, profit-sharing, and paid knowledge and skills training including lean management system training.
- Leadership at the plant is expected to come from all levels of the organization rather than just the management team.
