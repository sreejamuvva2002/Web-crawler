# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: EV parts supplier halts $325M project, 250 jobs lost - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/aspen-aerogels-register-georgia-ev-plant/63798304)  ·  `page_000468`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels would have been one of several EV parts suppliers near the Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- President & CEO Donald Young stated on Wednesday's earnings call: "We are taking decisive actions to navigate an evolving environment."
- Donald Young explained that the approach allows them to create capacity in a modular fashion that can more closely anticipate the demand curve, and to do so with minimal capital.
- Instead of focusing on a plant in Southeast Georgia, Aspen Aerogels is targeting upgrades on its already existing plant in East Providence, Rhode Island.
- The Bulloch County Development Authority says they are disappointed with the news.
- Bulloch County Development Authority CEO Benjy Thompson stated: "Businesses often reevaluated their strategies. The Development Authority will continue to pursue long-term projects that bring good jobs, smart investment, and economic opportunities to the citizens of Bulloch County."
- Hyundai told WJCL 22 that Aspen Aerogels is not a supplier of HMGMA, and will not affect production.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp is in Munich for an economic development mission, and is unable to provide a statement at this time.
- In 2022, Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced that an electric vehicle parts supplier was coming to Bulloch County and, once operational, would employ more than 250 people.
- Nearly three years later to the day, those plans have changed.
- Aspen Aerogels, recognized as a technology leader in aerogel-based sustainability and electrification solutions, intended to invest at least $325 million at its site at the Southern Gateway Commerce Park near Statesboro.
- Aspen Aerogels announced Wednesday that it is halting construction on the 500,000-square-foot manufacturing facility on a 90-acre parcel.
- The company will be assessing options to derive value from the assets in Statesboro, including relocating some equipment to upgrade and expand its existing plant (in Rhode Island).
- Aspen Aerogels previously announced back in 2015 plans to bring 100 jobs and a $70 million investment to the same area.
