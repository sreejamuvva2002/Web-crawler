# Polk County Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Business Climate - Polk County Georgia](https://www.choosepolk.com/business-climate/)  ·  `page_056475`

## Facts

- Polk County offers fast-track permitting, aggressive financing methods, business advocacy, and creative problem-solving to qualified projects.
- The business-friendly environment of Polk County is available for existing businesses as well.
- Polk County’s Development Authority Team is committed to earning and keeping a company’s trust and investment through the experience, workforce, and lifestyle Polk County provides.
- Advanced manufacturing companies benefit from convenient access to eastern markets including Atlanta, Birmingham, and Chattanooga through I-75, I-20, and I-59.
- Advanced manufacturing companies utilize four-lane highways US 27, US 278, and GA 113 for logistics.
- Advanced manufacturing companies have access to CSX and Norfolk Southern rail networks.
- Advanced manufacturing companies utilize the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick.
- Advanced manufacturing companies have a long history of success in Polk County and Northwest Georgia.
- Polk County is perfectly situated in the ABC (Atlanta-Birmingham-Chattanooga) triangle and in the heart of Northwest Georgia’s Automotive Alley.
- Businesses in Polk County have easy access to 28 car, light & heavy vehicle OEMs such as Honda, BMW, Mercedes, and VW within a 500-mile radius.
- Jefferson Southern Corporation is an existing automotive industry presence in Polk County.
- Polk County is less than 8 miles from the new Silver Comet Field at Paulding Northwest Atlanta Airport.
- Polk County is included within one of America’s largest aerospace clusters.
- Approximately 13,100 workers are already employed in the aerospace industry within 60 miles of Paulding Northwest Atlanta Airport.
- Meggitt Polymers & Composites is an existing aerospace products and parts industry presence in Polk County.
- Metal fabrication is a top growth sector for Northwest Georgia with a presence in Polk County.
- Metal fabrication has become a focus of training at Polk County College & Career Academy.
- Local industry works with Polk County College & Career Academy to train effective and efficient welders coming out of high school.
- The pipeline for metal fabrication employees continues to flow into Polk County from local high schools.
- Advance Storage Products, The HON Company, and Miura Boiler are existing metal fabrication industry presences in Polk County.
- Contact centers and back office operations that do not need to be tied to a specific location can benefit from Polk County’s low cost, available labor, proximity to Atlanta, bilingual workforce, and fiber optics networks.
- AT&T Telecommunications – Mobility Services is an existing contact center/back office industry presence in Polk County.
