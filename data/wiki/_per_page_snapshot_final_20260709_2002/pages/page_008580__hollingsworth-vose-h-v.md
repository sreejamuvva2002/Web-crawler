# Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Hollingsworth & Vose to Expand Virginia Site - Nonwovens Industry](https://www.nonwovens-industry.com/contents/view_breaking-news/2023-01-05/hollingsworth-vose-to-expand-virginia-site/page_2?widget=trending)  ·  `page_008580`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V) is a global manufacturer of advanced materials including nonwovens used in filtration, battery and industrial applications.
- H&V will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County, VA.
- The expansion will add more than 28,000 square feet to an existing facility.
- The added space will accommodate new production equipment to meet increased demand.
- Virginia successfully competed with Georgia for the project.
- The project will create 25 new jobs.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced the investment and job creation.
- H&V has generated positive economic impact and job opportunities in Floyd County for more than four decades.
- This significant investment further solidifies the company’s commitment to Virginia, according to Governor Youngkin.
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Caren Merrick stated H&V has played a role in the success of Virginia’s advanced materials industry over the past 46 years.
- Merrick noted the company’s continued investment testifies to the New River Valley’s strategic location and business-friendly environment.
- Josh Ayer, CEO of H&V, said the facility is essential to serving both global and domestic customers.
- Ayer stated they chose Virginia for this expansion because of its positive business environment and strong support from the Commonwealth of Virginia and Floyd County.
- H&V has been part of the Floyd, VA community since 1976.
- Joe Turman, Chairman of the Floyd County Board of Supervisors, said the new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county’s 191-year history.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
