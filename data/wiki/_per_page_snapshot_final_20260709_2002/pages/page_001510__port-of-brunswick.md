# Port of Brunswick

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia’s Port of Brunswick in bid for top auto gateway | AJOT.COM](https://www.ajot.com/premium/ajot-georgias-port-of-brunswick-in-bid-for-top-auto-gateway)  ·  `page_001510`

## Facts

- $260 million in investments gives Port of Brunswick room to grow.
- INEOS announced that the Port of Brunswick, Georgia, would be its gateway for the delivery of its new Grenadier off-road vehicle to markets in the U.S. Southeast and Texas.
- Nineteen dealers in the region are slated to handle the high-end SUV in Atlanta, Ft. Lauderdale, St. Petersburg, Houston, Dallas, and High Point, N.C.
- The Grenadier vehicles are assembled in France and shipped from Belgium to U.S. markets via Wallenius Wilhelmsen Logistics.
- The Grenadier is the first foray into auto manufacturing for INEOS, a France-based global chemical company that operates in 29 countries.
- Brunswick aims to become the nation’s number one gateway for roll-on/roll-off cargoes.
- INEOS is joining two dozen auto manufacturers at Brunswick’s Colonel’s Island terminal.
- The top five auto manufacturers at the terminal are Mercedes, Hyundai, Kia, Nissan, and GM.
- Machinery producers at the terminal include John Deere, Caterpillar, Wirtgen, and Hamm.
- Gabriel Pernia, planning and logistics director at INEOS Automotive Americas, stated Brunswick is their tactical and geographical preferred location to cover their sales distribution network.
- Pernia noted the port provides extensive capacity for vehicle storage, processing, and maritime cargo, which helps minimize total transit time and improve customer order deliveries.
- Georgia is attracting companies from the electric vehicle ecosystem, including the Hyundai Metaplant, a $9.8 billion manufacturing site that will open later this year in Bryan County, 85 miles north of Brunswick.
- Automotive parts supplier DAS Corp. opened a $35 million facility in Candler County, 100 miles north of Brunswick.
- Synthetic graphite manufacturer Anovion Technologies opened for business last year in Decatur County, 200 miles west of Brunswick.
- All-electric automaker Rivian is building a manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs, Georgia, around 250 miles northeast of Brunswick.
- The Port of Brunswick’s capacity has been enhanced by the Georgia Ports Authority’s investments of $262 million in the Colonel’s Island facility and additional surrounding acreage.
- The investments went toward 350,000 square feet of on-dock warehousing.
- The investments included 108,000 square feet of warehousing on the south end of the island.
- The investments included 80 additional acres for ro/ro storage.
- Buildings totaling 182,000 square feet are still under construction.
- An additional 42 storage acres are still under construction.
- A fourth ro/ro berth is in the engineering phase.
- A new rail yard is in the planning stage.
- The Port of Brunswick handled a record 775,565 units of autos and machinery in 2023.
- This volume represents an increase of 15.6% over 2022.
- Cliff Pyron, GPA’s chief commercial officer, cited population growth in the Southeast driving increased demand for autos across the region as a factor for the cargo increase.
- Pyron noted Brunswick’s central location to Southeastern markets favors Colonel’s Island.
- Importers can get their finished goods to dealers quicker due to Brunswick’s proximity, reducing time and costs associated with overland transportation.
- GPA expects organic growth and its unmatched ability to take on new trade to boost Brunswick volumes well into the future.
- GPA employs a two-pillar strategy where the Port of Savannah focuses on container trade while Brunswick specializes in ro/ro.
- Last month, non-containerized cargo at Savannah’s Ocean Terminal completed a transition to Brunswick.
- Griff Lynch, GPA’s CEO, told an audience at the Brunswick Chamber of Commerce last fall that concentrating on containers in Savannah and autos/machinery in Brunswick streamlines operations and provides more efficient service as cargo volumes continue to grow.
- Several new short-sea services linking Mexico to the Port of Brunswick have been launched recently to facilitate import of vehicles assembled south of the border.
- In November 2023, the first shipment of over 1,000 vehicles was delivered to Brunswick by Zim’s Gold Star service on the maiden vessel Sebring.
- In July, shipping line CMA-CGM started a new short-sea service carrying vehicles from Mexico to the Port of Brunswick.
- NYK and Hoegh Autoliners have both recently added routes from Brunswick to the Middle East.
- GPA’s investments are part of an ambitious plan to establish Brunswick as the nation’s number one ro/ro gateway.
- Lynch stated that at its current rate of growth, the Port of Brunswick is poised to become the nation’s busiest gateway for ro/ro cargo.
- Lynch said they will be ready to serve this growth with capital improvement projects underway and available land to expand to demand.
- Lynch added that organic growth, a steady pipeline of new customers, and the ability to take on new trade will boost Brunswick’s ro/ro volumes, which are already expanding at a strong rate.
- GPA initiated an aggressive infrastructure plan to strengthen Colonel’s Island for auto and machinery processing.
- The planned new railyard on the southside of Colonel’s Island will enter the construction phase next year.
- Kent Fountain, GPA Board Chairman, said this rail capacity improvement will extend Brunswick’s reach deeper into interior markets to capture more business.
- The port features a new fumigation facility onsite which is required for cars and machinery being shipped to Australia and New Zealand.
- It is the largest facility of its size for autos and machinery.
- Federally funded maintenance dredging is ongoing in the Brunswick River shipping channel.
- The U.S. Army Corps of Engineers is awaiting funding for a project to widen the channel and expand the turning basin at Colonel’s Island.
- Colonel’s Island encompasses 1,700 acres.
- Fountain noted Brunswick’s gateway port model features four on-site auto processors.
- The model includes room for customers to grow their business with three available parcels of land totaling 264 acres.
- The model includes direct access to Interstate 95 for car carrier and machinery trucks.
- With on-terminal rail, Brunswick offers the fastest East Coast rail connections to inland markets.
