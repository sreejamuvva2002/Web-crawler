# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-07

**Source:** [New auto production facility bringing 200 new jobs to Georgia](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/auto-production-facility-bringing-200-210431376.html)  ·  `page_053957`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology will invest $30 million in a new automotive manufacturing facility in Emanuel County, Georgia.
- The new production facility is located at the Highway 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The project is expected to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Hiring for management, system leads, and production personnel at the new facility is expected to begin in 2026.
- This initiative marks the largest economic development project for Emanuel County in more than 30 years.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971 and supplies automotive components including door, seat, and battery frames for original equipment manufacturers.
- The company has operated in the United States since 2003 and currently supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology's existing facilities in Hogansville will remain operational.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and job creation plans on Wednesday.
- Heather Hollstein, human resources manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, stated the company is proud to be part of growing the state's automotive industry and remains committed to supplying high-quality products to internal and external customers.
- State Representative Butch Parrish, chairman of the House Rules Committee, highlighted the project's focus on bringing high-quality jobs to rural communities in southeast Georgia.
- Will Donaldson, chairman of the Emanuel County Development Authority, expressed community excitement over the economic development payoff for the area.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, noted his team worked closely with company leadership on the site selection process.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson of the Georgia Department of Economic Development stated that the automotive industry is expanding outward along the Interstate 16 corridor toward Georgia's ports.
- The project was a collaborative effort involving the Georgia Department of Economic Development Global Commerce team, Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Alyce Thornhill, director of project implementation and supplier strategy for the Georgia Department of Economic Development Global Commerce team, represented the state on the project.
