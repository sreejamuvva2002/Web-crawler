# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Breaks Ground on $5B EV Plant in Georgia | Engineering News-Record](https://www.enr.com/articles/61374-rivian-breaks-ground-on-5b-ev-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_001044`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive has broken ground on its long-planned electric-vehicle factory in Georgia.
- The project is valued at $5 billion.
- The project is supported by one of the state’s largest incentive packages and a multibillion-dollar federal loan designed to anchor EV manufacturing in the Southeast.
- The company and state leaders held a groundbreaking ceremony on Sept. 16 at the Stanton Springs North site.
- The site is located about 45 miles east of Atlanta.
- The ceremony marked the official start of site development.
- Construction is expected to mobilize in 2026.
- Vehicle production is expected to begin in 2028, according to the automaker.
- The facility will be completed in two phases.
- The facility will span roughly 9 million sq ft.
- The production capacity aims to top 400,000 vehicles annually.
- Rivian says its R2 and R3 crossover models will anchor production at the plant.
- Rivian founder and CEO RJ Scaringe said at the groundbreaking that they are excited to build their next plant in Georgia and inspired by the state’s innovative spirit.
- Scaringe stated the Georgia facility will support global expansion and provide the scale necessary to get millions of future drivers in all-electric vehicles in the United States and overseas.
- Georgia officials estimate the combined value of state and local incentives at about $1.5 billion.
- The Economic Development Agreement was signed by Rivian and the state in May 2022.
- The agreement outlines statutory tax credits, property tax abatements, workforce training, land and infrastructure improvements, and discretionary “speed-to-market” spending.
- Georgia Dept. of Economic Development (GDED) Commissioner Pat Wilson said approximately one-quarter of the total incentive package comes in the form of state incentives.
- Wilson added that the final 20% of the estimated value is delivered through site work such as wetlands mitigation, a 500-acre pad ready for development, and road improvements.
- The state legislature appropriated $112.6 million for land acquisition and site preparation.
- Site preparation includes a new interchange on Interstate 20, a rail spur, and a Georgia Quick Start training center.
- Rivian must invest $5 billion to qualify for the full incentives.
- Rivian must employ 7,500 workers at an average salary of $56,000 by the end of 2028 to qualify for the full incentives.
- The agreement requires Rivian to maintain those employment and investment levels through 2047.
- If Rivian’s performance drops below 80% of the commitments, clawbacks require pro-rata repayment of incentives.
- The deal includes a payments-in-lieu-of-taxes schedule beginning with $1.5 million annually from 2023 through 2028.
- Payments will rise to $12 million in year seven.
- Estimated revenues from the schedule are more than $900 million over 25 years to benefit local schools and governments.
- The U.S. Dept. of Energy Loan Programs Office announced in January it had closed on a loan of up to $6.57 billion to Rivian New Horizon LLC.
- Rivian New Horizon LLC is the entity building the Georgia plant.
- DOE described the financing as support for “Project Horizon,” encompassing site development, construction and manufacturing facilities.
- DOE Loan Programs Office Director Jigar Shah said investing in American innovation is securing supply chains, strengthening the workforce, and ensuring the U.S. leads the clean transportation future.
- Rivian’s Securities and Exchange Commission filings describe the loan as including capitalized interest.
- The loan is explicitly intended “for the construction of its next manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North.”
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe, and GDED Commissioner Pat Wilson broke ground on Sept. 16.
- Early work at the site includes electrical and water utility hookups and road access.
- Local media reports indicate early work has since commenced.
- ENR has not yet independently verified those reports.
- Rivian has not confirmed its pick for general contractor.
- The car company temporarily paused the Georgia project in 2024 to conserve cash while launching its R2 line at its Normal, Ill., plant.
- Rivian restarted the Georgia build after securing federal financing and reconfirming its long-term strategy.
- The factory represents one of the largest single industrial investments in Georgia history.
- It adds to a growing cluster of EV and battery plants in the Southeast.
- State leaders see the Rivian facility as a keystone for attracting suppliers and bolstering the region’s automotive sector.
- State officials project the plant will reach the mandated 7,500-employee target by 2030.
- Another 2,000 construction jobs are projected to be created during the build-out.
- Wilson highlighted that clawback provisions protect taxpayers “in the extremely unlikely event of company goals not being met.”
