# G-TEKT Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-03-31

**Source:** [G-TEKT Corporation Business Report FY2012 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_163_hl2011)  ·  `page_056473`

## Facts

- Prior to March 2011, the Company operated as a single entity, Kikuchi Co., Ltd.
- From the 2012 fiscal year, figures reflect the Company's performance after its merger with Takao Kinzoku Kogyo., Co. Ltd.
- Sales for the FY ended Mar. 31, 2012 were 135,376 million JPY.
- Operating income for the FY ended Mar. 31, 2012 was 9,779 million JPY.
- Ordinary profit for the FY ended Mar. 31, 2012 was 8,864 million JPY.
- Net income for the FY ended Mar. 31, 2012 was 13,727 million JPY.
- Production volumes significantly dropped after the Great East Japan Earthquake and flooding in Thailand but recovered as customer production increased.
- Revenue for operations in Japan was 47,517 million yen with a profit of 3,613 million yen.
- Four companies were added as consolidated subsidiaries in North America after the merger, generating revenue of 43,649 million yen and profit of 1,196 million yen.
- One company was added as a consolidated subsidiary in Europe, generating revenue of 9,545 million yen and profit of 1,158 million yen.
- Four companies were added as consolidated subsidiaries in Asia, generating revenue of 15,684 million yen and profit of 2,475 million yen.
- Operations of Global Auto-Parts Alliance India Private Ltd. were transferred to G-TEKT India Private Ltd. due to a reorganization of Indian operations, resulting in an extraordinary loss.
- One company was added as a consolidated subsidiary in China, generating revenue of 24,719 million yen and profit of 1,122 million yen.
- Sales of automotive body parts in South America decreased due to the Great East Japan Earthquake, resulting in revenue of 4,316 million yen and a loss of 38 million yen.
- The Company announced it will form a new manufacturing subsidiary in Karawang, West Java, Indonesia named PT. G-TEKT Indonesia Manufacturing.
- The Indonesian subsidiary will produce automotive body structural parts and thick precision parts.
- G-TEKT plans to invest 4 billion yen in the new Indonesian company.
- The land area of the Indonesian facility is 73,078 square meters and the building will cover 11,209 square meters.
- The Indonesian company is scheduled to be operational in January 2013 with estimated annual sales of 3.2 billion yen in 2015.
- The Company established a joint venture, G-One auto Parts De Mexico, S.A. De C.V., with H-One in March 2012.
- Operations at the Mexican joint venture are scheduled to start from January 2014.
- The Mexican JV will be established in Celaya, Guanajuato, Mexico, with an operating officer of G-TEKT serving as its representative.
- Total investment for the Mexican JV is approximately US$46 million (approx. 3,528 million yen).
- Planned sales for the Mexican JV are approximately US$66 million (approx. 5.06 billion yen) in 2015.
- The Mexican JV will utilize large-size transfer press and welding facilities to produce automotive parts for 200,000 vehicles per year.
- The JV is a fifty-fifty partnership expected to be operational in April 2014.
- R&D expenditure for the FY ended Mar. 31, 2012 was 199 million JPY.
- G-TEKT will launch development of a hot-stamping method for ultra-high-tensile steel sheets to further increase material strength levels.
- By the end of 2012, dedicated production equipment will be installed at its Hamura Plant in Tokyo to develop new mass production technology.
- Honda Motor Co., Ltd., a major customer, has adopted structural components made from hot stamped ultra-high-tensile steel sheets in its new mini passenger vehicle.
- G-TEKT aims to engineer a highly competitive hot-stamping method to help customers introduce hot-stamped, ultra-high-tensile steel sheets to a wider variety of vehicle models globally.
- R&D activities involving new technologies and products are conducted at the Company's main Technology Development Department.
- Fifteen engineers work at the Product Development Section as of March 2012.
- Major FY R&D achievements include developing hot-press technology, forming technology for high tensile strength steel sheets, non-destructive inspection technology for spot-welded points, precision stamping technology for heavy sheet-metal, forming simulation technology, and vehicle weight reduction technology.
- Capital expenditure for the FY ended Mar. 31, 2012 was 12,698 million JPY.
- Main capital investments included acquiring production facilities at the Saitama Plant, expanding the Tochigi Plant, enhancing Chinese operations, and increasing die production capacity.
- Planned capital investment for Jefferson Southern Corporation in Georgia, USA includes a new facility (353 million yen from Jan. 2013 to Feb. 2013) and equipment for manufacturing auto parts including stamping/welding equipment (1,157 million yen from Mar. 2012 to Mar. 2014) and dies/jigs/tools (197 million yen).
- The purpose of investments at Jefferson Southern Corporation in Georgia is increasing production capacity and dealing with model changes.
- Planned capital investment for Jefferson Industries Corporation in Ohio, USA includes a new facility (429 million yen), stamping/welding equipment (1,052 million yen), and dies/jigs/tools (747 million yen) from Apr. 2012 to Mar. 2014.
- Planned capital investment for Austin Tri-Hawk Automotive Inc. in Indiana, USA includes a new facility (35 million yen), stamping/welding equipment (1,151 million yen), and dies/jigs/tools (1,405 million yen) from Aug. 2011 to Jul. 2012.
- Jefferson Industries Corporation, Jefferson Southern Corporation, Austin Tri-Hawk Automotive Inc., Jefferson Elora Corporation, G-KT do Brasil Ltda., Auto Parts Alliance (China) Ltd., Wuhan Auto Parts Alliance Co., Ltd., G-TEKT Europe Manufacturing Ltd., G-TEKT (Thailand) Co., Ltd., G-TEKT Eastern Co., Ltd., and G-TEKT India Private Ltd. hold nonexclusive licenses from G-TEKT for production, use, and sales rights regarding automotive parts, stamping dies, and jigs expertise.
- Technology contract with Jefferson Industries Corporation runs from 2003.10.01 with automatic annual extension.
- Technology contract with Auto Parts Alliance (China) Ltd. runs from 2011.07.01 to 2016.06.30.
- Technology contract with G-TEKT India Private Ltd. runs from 2008.06.26 to 2013.06.25 with automatic annual extension.
