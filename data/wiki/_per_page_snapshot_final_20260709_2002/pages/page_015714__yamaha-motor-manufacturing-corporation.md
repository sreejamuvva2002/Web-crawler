# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yamaha breaks ground on Innovative SMART Warehouse in Newnan – Winters Media](https://www.wintersmedia.net/yamaha-breaks-ground-on-innovative-smart-warehouse-in-newnan/)  ·  `page_015714`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) broke ground last week on a new, advanced, and innovative “SMART” Warehouse in Newnan, Georgia.
- The new SMART Warehouse is the first-of-its-kind for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- The facility will be 200,000 square feet and use state-of-the-art automated storage and retrieval systems.
- The warehouse will reduce response time for fulfilling dealer orders for Yamaha WaveRunners, Golf Cars, ATVs, and Side-by-Sides by more than 50 percent.
- SMART is an acronym for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The medium parts automated storage and retrieval system will be capable of retrieving and putting away 240 containers per hour, compared to the current rate of 170 containers per hour.
- The small parts automated system will be capable of handling 2400 containers per hour, in comparison to the current capability of 550 containers per day.
- The new systems will enable Yamaha to reduce forklifts by 50 percent using automatic conveyors and tuggers to move parts through the SMART center to the factories.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President and CEO of Yamaha Motor Corporation, stated that the warehouse will improve overall operational efficiency and the velocity of material flow to factory assembly lines to get product to dealers and customers faster.
- Demand for all outdoor recreational products produced at this factory continues to exceed supply.
- The investment underscores Yamaha’s long-term commitment to YMCC employees, the City of Newnan, and Coweta County.
- The facility will bring higher paid and more skilled jobs into Coweta County, including robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
- Designed by SSI SCHAEFER, the warehouse will include an observation platform for groups such as students from local schools to learn about manufacturing and automated processes.
- Yamaha expects to complete the SMART Warehouse in 2024.
- Due to strong product demand and continuous growth, Yamaha is currently hiring for various roles.
