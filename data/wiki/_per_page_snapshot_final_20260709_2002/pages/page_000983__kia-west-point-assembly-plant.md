# Kia West Point Assembly Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-13

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Production Plant Expanding For EV9 - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2023/07/13/kia-georgia-production-plant-expanding-for-ev9/)  ·  `page_000983`

## Facts

- Kia is expanding its West Point assembly plant in Georgia to produce the fully electric EV9 SUV.
- The facility expansion represents a $200 million investment.
- Production of the model-year 2024 EV9 will begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Expanding the production line will create around 200 jobs in Troup County, Georgia.
- The Kia Georgia plant currently produces the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, and K5 models, comprising three gasoline SUVs and one gasoline sedan.
- The EV9 will mark Kia's first entry into electric vehicle production across North America.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that the EV9 has the potential to be another 'game changer' for Kia, similar to the Telluride.
- Yoon noted that the EV9 will be the most sophisticated vehicle Kia has ever built and will stand out in the EV market and on the road.
- The EV9 combines design elements from the award-winning Telluride SUV with electric vehicle features from the award-winning EV6, aiming to mix capability, technology, and refined elegance.
- Assuming a $10,000 profit per unit, Kia would need to sell 20,000 units to break even on the expansion investment.
