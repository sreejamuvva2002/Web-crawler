# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-11

**Source:** [YKK USA Celebrates 50 Years of Innovation and Excellence at Macon Factory - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/features/2024/11/ykk-usa-celebrates-50-years-innovation-and-excellence-macon-factory/)  ·  `page_016044`

## Facts

- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. announced the 50th anniversary of its Macon Factory on November 9, 2024, with a Family Day event for employees.
- Speakers at the anniversary event included Mayor of Macon Lester Miller, Consul General of Japan Mio Maeda, YKK Corporation Chairman Masayuki Sarumaru, President of YKK Corporation of America Jim Reed, and President of YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. John Smith.
- John Smith, President of YKK (U.S.A.) Inc., highlighted the pivotal role of the Macon Factory in embodying the company’s philosophy and commitment to the community.
- Smith stated that the company expresses deep gratitude not only to its dedicated employees, whose tireless efforts have been instrumental, but also to the city of Macon for their unwavering support over the past fifty years, and is enthusiastic about extending this legacy of excellence and community partnership well into the future.
- Japan-based YKK first entered the U.S. market in 1960 as a zipper distributor in New York City near the garment district.
- Founder Tadao Yoshida envisioned a vertical integration model controlling the entire production process to ensure superior quality and cost efficiency.
- A site selection team explored locations in Tennessee, North and South Carolina, Alabama, and Georgia as the U.S. textile industry shifted southward.
- Georgia was selected due to its robust infrastructure, including four interstate highways, the deep-water port of Savannah, and a major international airport.
- Cloyd Hall, a Macon native and Special Assistant to then-Governor Jimmy Carter, arranged a meeting that facilitated a connection between Yoshida and Carter.
- YKK established its manufacturing facility just south of Macon in the newly developed Ocmulgee East Industrial Park.
- Prominent Macon leaders visited YKK’s manufacturing center in Kurobe, Japan, to initiate the partnership.
- Under the leadership of Yoshinori Kitano, construction and operation strategies were crafted for the new facility.
- Newly hired engineers, including future YKK Corporation of America CEO Alex Gregory, were trained in Japan to transfer specialized manufacturing techniques to Macon.
- The Macon plant opened in 1974.
- The facility initially produced zippers tailored to the American market for clothing, furniture, and outdoor equipment.
- Over the decades, the factory expanded dramatically and now employs over 600 local residents.
- The Macon facility diversified into advanced solutions for industries including automotive, government, and medical sectors.
- In 1977, YKK played a pivotal role in establishing a sister city relationship between Kurobe, Japan, and Macon, Georgia.
- Over the past four decades, more than 1,100 citizens from both cities have visited each other's countries as part of the partnership.
- YKK provides substantial sponsorships and active participation for the annual Macon Cherry Blossom Festival.
- In 1990, YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. donated $300,000 towards the construction of a dedicated building for festival organizers.
- The city of Macon donated $60,000 worth of land to complement the 1990 donation.
- Chairman Tadahiro Yoshida personally attended the 1990 groundbreaking ceremony for the festival building.
- YKK continues its annual support as a sponsor of the Macon Cherry Blossom Festival.
