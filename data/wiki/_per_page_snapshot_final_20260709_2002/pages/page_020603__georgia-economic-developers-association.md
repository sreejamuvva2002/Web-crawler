# Georgia Economic Developers Association

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GEDA Recognizes Top Deals & Excellence By Individuals | AM 1180 Radio](https://chattooga1180.com/geda-recognizes-top-deals-excellence-by-individuals/)  ·  `page_020603`

## Facts

- GEDA held its Annual Awards luncheon on a Monday to recognize top deals of the year and excellence by individuals.
- Awards presented included Deals of the Year, Volunteer of the Year, Rip Wiley Award for Professional Excellence, and Committee Chair and Member of the Year.
- Chris Womack, Chairman, President & CEO of Georgia Power, spoke and conferred the awards.
- Over 200 economic developers from around the state attended the ceremony in-person at the Georgia Tech Hotel at Tech Square in Midtown Atlanta.
- The GEDA Deal of the Year Awards recognize outstanding achievement in the location or expansion of new businesses in Georgia that meet community economic development goals and involve significant community program support, such as financial or permitting assistance, workforce development, and state financial support.
- Winners were selected in three community categories: Small, Mid-Size, and Large.
- The Small category Deal of the Year winner was Bacon County Development Authority – La Regina Atlantica.
- The Mid-Size category Deal of the Year winner was Camden County Joint Development Authority – Plug Power.
- The Large category Deal of the Year winner was Dalton-Whitfield County Joint Development Authority – GEDIA Georgia.
- Volunteer of the Year recipient is Alan Verner, Chairman of the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton & Walton Counties.
- Tim Evans, Vice President – Economic Development of the Greater Hall County Chamber of Commerce, was named the 2021 recipient of the Rip Wiley Award for Professional Excellence.
- The Rip Wiley Award is named after H. W. “Rip” Wiley, who worked for the Georgia Department of Industry, Trade and Tourism (now the Department of Economic Development) as Director of the Industry Division when he passed in October 1978.
- The award was established upon his death in 1978 to be presented to the person in economic development who best represents his spirit of excellence.
- Jason Dunn, GEDA Chair of the Professional Development Committee and Executive Director of the Fitzgerald-Ben Hill County Development Authority, received the Committee Chair of the Year award.
- Rachel Barnwell, Manager of Economic Development Programs for the Development Authority of Bulloch County, was recognized as Member of the Year for her efforts on the 2021 Annual Conference Committee.
- GEDA is a nonprofit association of professionals, volunteers and supporters that provides networking and professional development opportunities for its members and supports public policies that promote quality job creation and sustainable economic development throughout the state.
