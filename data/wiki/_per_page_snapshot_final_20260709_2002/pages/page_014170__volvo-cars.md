# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-03

**Source:** [Volvo Cars' new CEO vows to produce more cars in US](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/volvo-cars-ceo-vows-produce-142335388.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_014170`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars' new top executive said on Thursday that the company would produce more cars in the U.S. while ramping up its regionalization efforts.
- The announcement comes as a 25% U.S. tariff on global autos takes effect.
- European companies are scrambling to find solutions as President Donald Trump's 25% duties on foreign cars became effective and a bigger-than-expected wall of import tariffs was unveiled on Wednesday.
- Volvo Cars made an unexpected decision on Sunday to replace its CEO with former CEO Hakan Samuelsson.
- The company has struggled lately, with its share price falling to record lows amid a slowdown in demand for electric vehicles and global uncertainty.
- During the company's annual general meeting in Gothenburg on Thursday, investors awaited plans from Samuelsson, who led the company for more than 10 years until 2021, to revive the share price and improve the company.
- Samuelsson stated that the share price has not developed as shareholders expected, indicating they are not fully invested in Volvo Cars.
- He said he would look at ways to cut costs and improve strategy, which would include ramping up regionalization efforts with hubs in China, the U.S., and Europe.
- Samuelsson noted that while Volvo is well prepared in China and Europe, it needs to be better in the U.S. to get around import tariffs.
- He expects to ramp up production at its Charleston plant in South Carolina.
- His comments mirrored those made by former CEO Jim Rowan to Reuters in March.
- The company currently produces a pricier electric SUV, the EX90, in the South Carolina factory.
- The South Carolina factory also produces Polestar's Model 3.
- Volvo's cheaper model, the EX30, will start production soon in Europe.
- Samuelsson said he thinks the next car should be some car with a high volume in the U.S., though he does not know exactly what that car would be.
- He added that what might be a best-selling car today would perhaps not be the same in five years, so they have to think carefully.
- Samuelsson told investors he would take some time to look at what had already been done in his absence.
- He expects to return in a month or two with a turnaround plan for the company and a strategy focused on continued electrification, profitability, and regionalization.
- He told investors that producing one type of car in one place and sending it across the world is a thing of the past.
