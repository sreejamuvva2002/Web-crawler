# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-04

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America Expands Electric Vehicle Use for Logistics Decarbonization](https://electriccarsreport.com/2024/05/daimler-truck-north-america-expands-electric-vehicle-use-for-logistics-decarbonization/)  ·  `page_053617`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America (DTNA) is expanding the use of electric trucks for logistics operations across multiple business units and regions, including North Carolina, South Carolina, Arizona, Ohio, Michigan, and Saltillo, Mexico.
- DTNA started integrating Freightliner eCascadia battery electric semi-trucks into their logistics operation in the Pacific Northwest in December 2023.
- The Freightliner eCascadia was launched in 2022.
- DTNA began integrating operations at Daimler Truck Mexico’s truck manufacturing plant in Santiago in January 2024.
- For short-haul operations, DTNA will begin using its Freightliner eM2 electric box trucks that were introduced to customers last year.
- DTNA's strategy expands electrification within regional logistics networks serving its manufacturing plants and parts distribution centers (PDCs) within a 250-mile radius.
- This logistics approach is strategically aligned with its supplier landscape to fully utilize truck capacity and optimize multi-stop schedules.
- The strategy enables precise planning of transportation routes and charging points for DTNA's operations.
- DTNA leverages existing charging infrastructure, such as “Electric Island”, the nation’s first-of-its-kind public charging station in Portland, Oregon, designed specifically for commercial vehicles.
- DTNA plans to utilize future charging infrastructure, including Duke Energy’s Mount Holly Microgrid Center in the Carolinas and Michigan’s Mobility Charging Hub.
- The company supports strategic partners in their electrification journey by offering Detroit eConsulting services, which include implementing depot charging at partner facilities.
- Jeff Allen serves as senior vice president of operations and specialty vehicles at DTNA.
- Jeff Allen stated: “Our commitment to sustainability extends across our entire value chain. As we witness the adoption of electric vehicles, such as our Freightliner eCascadia and Freightliner eM2 trucks, within our logistics network, we are not only driving sustainability but also laying the groundwork for adaptable and robust electric logistics solutions.”
- Sustainability is an integral part of DTNA’s core business.
- The company conducts life cycle carbon assessments for all of its activities to understand environmental impacts from production to disposal.
- DTNA aims to achieve carbon neutrality for all new products and services from direct suppliers in Europe, the United States, and Japan by 2039.
- As early as 2020, the Portland Truck Manufacturing Plant achieved CO₂-neutral production by reducing energy consumption and offsetting on-site emissions.
- The Portland Truck Manufacturing Plant is where the Freightliner eCascadia and eM2 are built.
- DTNA plans to achieve CO₂-neutral production at all of its remaining truck manufacturing plants by 2025.
- DTNA is implementing sustainable supply chain measures and views suppliers as partners in its sustainability journey.
- DTNA is proactively developing solutions to optimize the entire lifecycle of the batteries used in its electric trucks.
- Battery lifecycle optimization strategies include remanufacturing, repurposing, and end-of-life recycling.
- The integration of electric vehicles into DTNA’s logistics network exemplifies the company’s commitment to real-world sustainability initiatives.
- By implementing such measures, DTNA solidifies its position as a leader in sustainable operations and demonstrates its belief that environmental responsibility is not only good for business, but essential for the future.
