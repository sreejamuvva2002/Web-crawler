# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-01-23

**Source:** [Kemp Visits Porsche Electric Vehicle Plant on Germany Trip - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/kemp-visits-porsche-electric-vehicle-plant-on-germany-trip/)  ·  `page_048907`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp visited Porsche’s new electric vehicle plant in Stuttgart, Germany.
- Detlev von Platen, who led Porsche Cars North America’s Atlanta operation for seven years, served as the tour guide at the Stuttgart plant.
- Von Platen presided over a site selection process that considered 73 locations before choosing a 53-acre complex with a test track near the Atlanta airport for Porsche.
- Klaus Zellmer succeeded von Platen as president and CEO of Porsche Cars North America in 2015.
- Von Platen is now a member of the Porsche AG executive board for sales and marketing.
- The Porsche Taycan launched in December with a six-figure starting price.
- Gov. Kemp had just announced a new foreign investment to supply a $1.7 billion vehicle battery factory in Jackson County, Georgia.
- In a news release, Gov. Kemp noted a desire to see a hub for future-facing vehicles continue growing in Georgia, already an automotive magnet.
- Georgia is home to 800 German-owned facilities when including retail outlets.
- Hundreds of German manufacturers have made Georgia home, contributing to the 36,000 jobs German firms support here.
- Gov. Kemp visited Munich and met with Florian Herrmann, Bavaria’s state minister for domestic, European affairs and media, reciprocating a June 2019 visit by Herrmann to Georgia.
- Georgia and Bavaria are partners in the Regional Leaders Alliance, a group of international states and provinces including Shandong, China; Sao Paulo, Brazil and others that meet annually to share best practices.
- Gov. Kemp opened Georgia’s new trade and investment office at Max Weber Platz in Munich.
- The Georgia office in Munich employs five full-time staff for trade and investment promotion, plus two contractors for tourism promotion.
- Frank Sportolari, president of UPS Germany and AmCham Germany, attended the ribbon-cutting for Georgia’s Munich office.
- Discussions at the meeting included concerns about tariffs, following President Trump's threat to impose tariffs on European cars if trade negotiations are unsatisfactory.
- Bavaria state minister Florian Herrmann stated that tariffs and trade restrictions are wrong and unnecessary in fair competition between partners like Bavaria and the USA.
