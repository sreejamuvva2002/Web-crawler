# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-09

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Halts Plans for $2.6B Georgia Plant, Coweta County's Job](https://hoodline.com/2025/02/freyr-battery-halts-plans-for-2-6b-georgia-plant-coweta-county-s-job-growth-on-hold/)  ·  `page_055007`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery withdrew plans to establish a nearly $2.6 billion battery manufacturing plant in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The abandoned project was forecast to generate more than 700 jobs in the region.
- FREYR Battery sent a letter to the Coweta County Development Authority in January detailing the withdrawal.
- The company stated the decision was necessary to realign its near-term strategic goals.
- FREYR Battery noted that the Coweta County Development Authority, Coweta County, and the State of Georgia provided extraordinary assistance and support.
- Shifting market forces and internal changes influenced the cancellation.
- Contributing factors to the cancellation included rising interest rates, declining battery prices, and corporate leadership adjustments.
- The cancellation is a setback for Coweta County's economic expansion prospects.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development spokesperson stated the state's incentive arrangement is designed to protect taxpayers.
- Efforts are underway to recuperate funds promptly allotted for the project's inception.
- FREYR Battery is currently focusing on a solar module manufacturing facility in Texas.
- The Coweta County project was initially announced in 2022.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp had previously heralded the project as a major boost to Georgia's sustainable technology ecosystem.
- The facility was intended to produce lithium-ion battery cells for vehicles and energy storage solutions.
- Coweta County officials stated that the incentives were structured with safeguards to protect taxpayers.
- Coweta County officials noted that the property will remain valuable for creating jobs and investment to benefit the community.
