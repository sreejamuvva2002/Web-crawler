# Hyundai Motor Group Meta Plant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Metaplant America: Georgia’s $7.6B EV Megafactory Nears Production – Everything You Need to Know](https://vfuturemedia.com/electric-vehicles/hyundai-metaplant-america-nears-production-inside-georgias-7-6b-ev-manufacturing-megafactory/)  ·  `page_014306`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Meta Plant America (HMGMA) is the largest single economic development project in Georgia history.
- The facility is entering the final phases of commissioning as of November 2025.
- The $7.6 billion EV and battery metaplant is located in Bryan County.
- Genesis, Hyundai, and Kia electric vehicle production remains on track to begin in Q1 2025.
- Battery pack assembly operations are starting even earlier than vehicle production.
- The facility is Hyundai’s strategic centerpiece to secure full $7,500 U.S. federal EV tax credit eligibility under the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) by localizing both vehicle and battery production.
- Unlike legacy automakers relying on retrofitted factories, Hyundai built a fully modernized “Metaplant” from the ground up with the E-GMP (Electric-Global Modular Platform) as the backbone of manufacturing.
- A strategic goal is to qualify Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EVs for the full $7,500 tax credit, which requires North American final assembly plus battery content.
- A strategic goal is to reduce reliance on China-centered battery supply chains.
- The facility aims to reach 300,000+ annual vehicle capacity by 2028.
- The project will generate 8,500 direct jobs plus tens of thousands in supplier network employment.
- The campus spans 2,900 acres.
- The Vehicle Assembly Plant has a capacity of 300,000 units per year, expandable to 500,000.
- Battery Pack Assembly (HMGMA + LG Energy Solution JV) has an initial capacity of 30 GWh per year, equivalent to approximately 300,000–350,000 packs.
- The Stamping, Body, Paint, and Trim Shops utilize over 500 robots on fully automated lines.
- Trials for the Stamping, Body, Paint, and Trim Shops were completed in Q4 2024.
- An on-site Supplier Park hosts 12 Tier-1 suppliers including Glovis, Seohan, and DAS.
- Confirmed launch models for 2025–2026 include the Hyundai IONIQ 7 (three-row E-GMP SUV), Kia EV9 GT (high-performance variant), Genesis GV90 (flagship luxury electric SUV), and next-gen Hyundai IONIQ 9 (expected sibling to IONIQ 7 for select markets).
- All vehicles will feature 800V architecture.
- All vehicles will feature ultra-fast charging capable of reaching 10–80% in approximately 18 minutes.
- All vehicles will support Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) and Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G).
- The battery plant is a 50/50 Hyundai–LG Energy Solution joint venture featuring NCMA high-nickel pouch cells with low cobalt content.
- Battery cell capacity is expandable to over 50 GWh.
- Cells are sourced from U.S. LG plants in Michigan and an upcoming Arizona plant.
- Assembling packs in Georgia qualifies them for the full IRA battery credit.
- This battery strategy mirrors successful North American partnerships used by Ford–SK On and GM–LG Ultium.
- The State of Georgia provides $1.8 billion in cash and tax incentives conditioned on job creation and investment milestones.
- Bryan County and local entities provide over $400 million in land and infrastructure support.
- Federal IRA leverage provides indirect funding totaling hundreds of millions annually via consumer tax credits, conditioned on final assembly and battery content.
- Total public support is approximately $2.2–2.5 billion.
- The project cost grew to $7.6 billion due to expanded battery operations and higher construction costs.
- More than 6,000 workers are on-site at peak construction.
- Body and paint shop equipment installation is 95% complete.
- First pilot vehicles were produced in September–October 2025.
- Mass-production trial runs are set for December 2025.
- A grand opening is expected in January 2025.
- Hyundai reports progress is “on track or slightly ahead of schedule.”
- Full tax credit eligibility will allow Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EVs to compete directly with Tesla, Ford, GM, and Rivian on price.
- Models like the IONIQ 7 could launch below $50,000 after incentives.
- Georgia is becoming a Korean EV manufacturing hub combined with Kia’s West Point expansion and SK On–Hyundai battery plants in Bartow County.
- Georgia is positioned as a major North American EV manufacturing corridor.
- The Hyundai Metaplant represents a highly integrated EV ecosystem engineered specifically for IRA compliance, 800V E-GMP scaling, and next-generation manufacturing flexibility.
- Once the first IONIQ 7 rolls off the line in early 2025, Hyundai’s evolution from rapid follower to genuine North American EV volume leader becomes official.
