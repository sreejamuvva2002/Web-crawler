# Peterson American Corporation

## Facts

- Peterson American Corporation's mission is to work with customers to provide the best products available to the world market through practical, innovative thinking.
- The company maintains an equitable relationship with its customers, employees, shareholders, and communities.
- Peterson Spring's performance culture emphasizes discipline, hard work, integrity, operational excellence, innovation, and quality/precision.
- The company's history begins in the 1880s with the immigration of founder August Christian Peterson, a Norwegian blacksmith.
- August studied blacksmithing and rose through spring manufacturing ranks in the early 1900s before managing plants across the Midwest.
- In 1914, August moved his family to Detroit to form a company targeting demand from the railroad industry and the budding automobile industry.
- August worked with his eldest son Alfred, who left school after eighth grade to work in steel mills' spring plants.
- Other sons Sigurd, Conrad, and Harold joined later; Sigurd made significant contributions as a mechanic and developed innovative machines.
- The company expanded overseas during the 1960s and 1970s through the purchase of foreign firms and joint ventures.
- In 1978, the company split into two halves, with one renamed Peterson American Corporation.
- The departed half's assets were repurchased in 1991, bringing the entire family of companies back under the PAC umbrella.
- Peterson Spring is a founding member of the Spring Makers Institute (SMI) and has been an active member since its inception in 1933.
- Former employees Bud and Pete Peterson, grandson and great-grandson of founder August Christian Peterson, both served two-year terms as SMI president.
- Bud received the SMI's Golden Coil Award in 1989 for contributions of lasting importance to the spring industry.
- The company operates 13 facilities worldwide, all TS/ISO/QS certified.
- Decades of innovating thinking honed in the high-performance automotive market provide knowledge for thriving in fields implementing springs.
- Peterson's metallurgists, engineers, and designers create springs used daily in products ranging from lawnmowers to military applications.
