# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-13

**Source:** [Rivian plans new SUV model for facility near Social Circle - The Covington News](https://www.covnews.com/news/business/rivian-plans-new-suv-model-facility-near-social-circle/)  ·  `page_000745`

## Facts

- Rivian’s planned production facility near Social Circle will produce a new SUV model expected to be more affordably priced than its current vehicles.
- The company plans for the Georgia facility to cover 2,000 acres in Walton and Morgan counties.
- The Georgia facility will serve as the production site for the future R2 platform, a more accessibly priced mid-sized SUV targeting global markets.
- Prices are in the $70,000 range for new Rivian-produced vehicles.
- A lower-priced model could allow it to better compete with other automakers that are planning or already offer electric vehicles in the $30,000 range.
- CEO CJ Scaringe stated the California-based company was “on a clear path” to the beginning of production at its planned Georgia facility despite its stock falling to an all-time low recently.
- The company announced that production would begin in 2024.
- Scaringe said the company plans for its next new model, based on the R2 platform, to be produced beginning in 2025 at the new facility.
- Analysts attributed the stock price decline to prominent investor Ford Motor Co. announcing it was selling around 8% of its Rivian shares.
- The company reported a $1.59 billion net loss on revenue of $95 million in the first quarter of 2022.
- Scaringe stated the company had $17 billion in cash on hand.
- The company is working to overcome supply chain issues that have lowered vehicle production numbers at its Normal, Illinois, plant.
- Supply chain issues at the Normal, Illinois, plant have lowered the company's profits.
- Based on demonstrated production rates and progress, if all supply constraints were resolved, the Normal plant would run at two times the currently expected output for the remainder of 2022.
- The company is producing vehicles at low volumes on production lines designed for higher volumes, experiencing negative gross profit related to significant labor and overhead costs.
- The company expects negative gross profit dynamics to continue in the near term but improve on a per vehicle basis as production volumes ramp up faster than future labor and overhead cost increases.
- The company is investing in technologies and capabilities at its current Normal, Illinois, facility that are designed to benefit future platforms, including R2.
- The company signed an Economic Development Agreement with the state of Georgia and the four-county Joint Development Authority (JDA).
- The agreement allowed Rivian to move forward with development of the Georgia plant.
- The deal included $1.5 billion in local and state incentives for the project.
- Rivian expects the new facility to have a transformative impact on the local economy and believes the investment represents compelling benefits for all stakeholders.
- The agreement requires the company to produce at least 80% of the 7,500 jobs it promised by 2028.
- The agreement requires the company to maintain the 7,500 jobs for 20 years or face the loss of its tax breaks.
- The company will give the JDA payments in lieu of taxes (PILOTs) that the JDA will redistribute to the four counties that are part of the agency, including Newton.
- There was no mention in the report of local opposition to construction from an organized group centered on Morgan County and the nearby city of Rutledge.
- The opposing group has opposed construction for a variety of reasons: lack of transparency in negotiations, environmental concerns, potential loss of the area’s rural nature, and the state’s control of the project which allows it to bypass local reviews.
- The state has vowed to comply with all environmental regulations and constraints and be as transparent as possible.
- Opponents have regularly scoffed at the state's claim regarding compliance and transparency.
