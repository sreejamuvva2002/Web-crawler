# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Strengthening Europe’s raw materials security: Aurubis inaugurates globally one-of-a-kind recycling plant for strategic metals](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2026/strengthening-europes-raw-materials-security-aurubis-inaugurates-globally-one-of-a-kind-recycling-plant-for-strategic-metals)  ·  `page_017339`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- Aurubis inaugurated a globally unique plant at its Hamburg site on July 3, 2026.
- The new facility is named Complex Recycling Hamburg (CRH).
- The CRH facility integrates the capabilities of multiple smelting processes into a single unit.
- For the first time, raw materials containing copper, lead and sulfur can be processed in a single plant at this facility.
- Production was officially kicked off after around two-and-a-half years of construction.
- The project involved an investment of €190 million.
- The €190 million investment is expected to deliver a considerable positive financial contribution once fully ramped up.
- CRH is one of the most significant growth projects under Aurubis’ current strategy, “Aurubis Performance 2030: Forging resilience. Leading in multimetal.”
- The new facility enables Aurubis to process attractive recycling materials for which there are only a limited number of buyers worldwide.
- CRH will allow the company to treat more than 30,000 additional tons of recycling material per year.
- CRH will also process significantly larger volumes of high-value smelter intermediates with exceptional efficiency.
- The facility enhances Aurubis’ ability to recover important metals, such as copper, lead and precious metals.
- The facility produces sulfuric acid, which is indispensable for key industrial applications.
- The objective of the process innovation is to keep more materials and value within the Aurubis smelter network, optimizing the utilization of existing assets and further enhancing production resilience.
- The new plant strengthens Aurubis’ business model with its diversified earnings drivers.
- At the heart of the plant is a specially engineered converter that separates the individual elements.
- The unit processes batches of around 45 tons at temperatures of up to 1,400 degrees Celsius.
- CRH is equipped with cutting-edge technology and sets a new benchmark in efficient, environmentally friendly multimetal production.
- Around a third of total investment went towards air pollution control measures, underscoring the plant’s high environmental standards.
- The facility is highly automated, enabling very efficient staffing levels while ensuring best-in-class health and safety standards.
- Aurubis COO Tim Kurth stated that global demand for critical raw materials like copper is growing rapidly.
- Tim Kurth emphasized the need for better access to recycling materials and more innovation in complex material processing.
- The CRH project received funding from the European Union.
- The project directly contributes to advancing the EU Critical Raw Materials Act.
- Project financing was backed by the first investment loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) under its comprehensive raw materials strategy.
- EIB Vice President Nicola Beer stated that securing critical raw materials such as copper and expanding recycling capacities in Europe are central to resilience, sustainable development, and Europe’s industrial competitiveness.
- The EIB loan to Aurubis reinforces European value chains and shows quick and decisive action in alignment with the Critical Raw Materials Act.
- Projects like CRH support an increase in the processing of complex materials in Europe.
- Stefan Rouenhoff, Parliamentary State Secretary at the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy, said securing critical raw materials is one of the key economic policy challenges of our time.
- Dr. Christopher Schwieger, State Councilor of the Hamburg Authority for Economic Affairs, Labor and Innovation, noted that Aurubis continues to invest in industrial hub Hamburg and that Complex Recycling Hamburg showcases how modern industrial production, innovation and sustainability can successfully work in tandem.
- The investment is important for the city’s economic future and for securing high-quality jobs.
