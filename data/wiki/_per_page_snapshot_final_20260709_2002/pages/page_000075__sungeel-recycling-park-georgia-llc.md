# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-18

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Korean Lithium-Ion Battery Recycler SungEel HiTech to Build First U.S. Recycling Facility, SungEel Recycling Park Georgia | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-08-18/gov-kemp-korean-lithium-ion-battery-recycler-sungeel-hitech-build-first)  ·  `page_000075`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that lithium-ion battery recycler and raw materials provider SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC., will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia is a subsidiary of the Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility.
- The project will create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia announced record-breaking numbers for fiscal year 2022 and that companies like SungEel are evidence of a rapidly growing electric mobility ecosystem generating new jobs across the state.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Korea has been a key partner in Georgia’s growing sustainable technology industry and expressed excitement that SungEel chose Georgia for their first U.S. facility.
- SungEel has more than 10 years of patented technology.
- SungEel specializes in complete recycling technology using a 100 percent full circulatory system.
- The recycling system processes end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap.
- The system achieves a metal recovery rate of more than 95 percent.
- Recovered metals include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- Suk Jae Yim, Representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, stated that SungEel HiTech's entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will fulfill its responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- The new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park in Toccoa.
- Hayestone Brady Business Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop a portfolio of available sites ready for fast-track development.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will be hiring for technicians, operators, administrative, and sales roles.
- Brian Akin, Chairman of the Stephens County Development Authority, stated that investments in infrastructure, workforce development, and education were significant factors in SungEel's selection process.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Stephens County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that SungEel is at the cutting edge of sustainable technology and that its proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to success.
- Pat Wilson emphasized that preparing key industries, such as the auto industry, for the next technological revolution means creating jobs of the future and protecting opportunities of today.
- GDEcD expressed gratitude to partners in Stephens County for their investment in speed to market solutions like site preparation.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it an attractive location for diverse rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain that will help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $13 billion in investments to Georgia since 2020.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than 18,100 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd. (KOSDAQ, KQ 365340) is headquartered in Korea and operates as a global leader in lithium ion battery recycling and raw material provision.
- SungEel HiTech currently operates facilities in South Korea, China, Malaysia, India, and Hungary.
- New SungEel HiTech facilities are underway in Poland and Germany.
