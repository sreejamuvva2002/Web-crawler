# Georgia Department of Transportation, The Ray, Kia Georgia, and Panasonic Partnership

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-05

**Source:** [Georgia Department of Transportation Demonstrates Safer, Connected Roadways with Kia Georgia and Panasonic Smart Mobility Technology on "The Ray Highway"](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2022/05/05/1139378-georgia-department-transportation-demonstrates-safer-connected-roadways-with-kia-georgia.html)  ·  `page_048018`

## Facts

- On May 5, 2022, the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT), The Ray, Kia Georgia, and Panasonic demonstrated a fully operational connected vehicle environment along 18 miles of Interstate 85 known as "The Ray Highway."
- The demonstration utilized the Cirrus by Panasonic® connected vehicle data management platform that detects real-time roadway events and communicates safety-critical messages to travelers about driving conditions.
- The partnership between the four organizations is focused on raising awareness of the life-saving potential of connected vehicle technology as well as deploying real-world use cases with measurable benefits.
- GDOT and Kia Georgia vehicles equipped with vehicle-to-everything (V2X) technology were showcased to deliver real-time situational awareness to drivers along this key logistics and manufacturing corridor in West Georgia.
- GDOT, The Ray, and Panasonic are providing "ride-along" experiences for stakeholders where drivers will be warned of hazardous conditions ahead on the roadway before they come into view.
- These ride-along experiences showcase how connected vehicle technology can detect and warn drivers about weather events, work zones, hard braking, and vehicle crashes in a real-world highway environment.
- The experience includes an in-vehicle visualization of traveler warnings, V2X technology capabilities installed on and along roadways, digital communication enabling connected roadway intelligence, and the Cirrus cloud platform managing data for GDOT to detect events and provide real-time alerts.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, stated that Georgia DOT leads in deploying transportation technology to benefit drivers, move freight across the region more efficiently and safely, and advance Georgia as the technology epicenter of the east coast.
- McMurry emphasized that connected vehicle technology will save lives and expressed determination to see it implemented in Georgia as soon as possible, highlighting the power of partnerships with Kia, The Ray, and Panasonic.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that customer safety is the number one priority for Kia Georgia team members and that the V2X program is a forward-thinking initiative putting Kia at the heart of solutions where vehicles and infrastructure communicate seamlessly to enhance highway safety and efficiency.
- Countess noted Kia Georgia's pride in partnering with these organizations while showcasing products built by the Kia team in West Point, Georgia.
- Allie Kelly, Executive Director of The Ray, stated that connected vehicle technology creates an opportunity to increase travel efficiency and driver safety simultaneously, requiring investments in physical and digital infrastructure for connected and autonomous vehicles.
- Kelly noted that Georgia DOT and The Ray have made investments to improve and modernize transportation infrastructure and offer real-time warnings about crashes, construction work zones, hard braking, and weather impacts like black ice, flooding, and hydroplaning.
- Chris Armstrong, Vice President of Cirrus at Panasonic's Smart Mobility Office, stated that the project solves transportation challenges by bringing perspectives from public, private, and philanthropic organizations united in improving safety and championing wide-scale adoption of life-saving technology.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is a Newark, NJ-based company and the principal North American subsidiary of Osaka, Japan-based Panasonic Holdings Corporation.
- Panasonic is a leading provider of Consumer Lifestyle technologies, Smart Mobility, Sustainable Energy, Immersive Experiences, and Integrated Supply Chain solutions, and was named one of Interbrand's Top 100 Best Global Brands of 2021.
- Panasonic serves as a technology partner and integrator to businesses, government agencies, and consumers across the region.
