# Hyundai Mobis

## Facts

- The announcement was made in Seoul on March 14th, 2024.
- Hyundai Mobis announced the development of an “Integrated Front Face Module” that maintains traditional grille design while reducing air resistance and securing design diversity.
- The module incorporates an aerodynamic system that reduces air resistance and improves electric consumption efficiency (ECE).
- The integrated module features a LiDAR sensor that protrudes outward only during driving for design and sensor protection.
- The module includes an automatic charger retrieval feature that retrieves the charger after charging is completed.
- A system automatically opens and closes parts like the grille and hood to reduce air resistance during high-speed driving and improve ECE.
- This combined aerodynamic system extends the electric vehicle’s range by approximately 20 km.
- The technology allows for the intake of external air and exhaust of air after heat exchange, improving battery cooling efficiency and controlling airflow.
- Hyundai Mobis differentiated itself by maintaining a traditional grille design while improving aerodynamic performance, unlike typical streamlined EV designs that eliminate grilles.
- Integrating design elements such as LED lighting has enhanced marketability for the module.
- The technology is particularly beneficial for SUV and CUV electric vehicles where lowering vehicle height is challenging.
- Technologies prevent external contamination and impact on sensors by hiding the LiDAR inside the grille when not in use.
- A semi-automatic charging system initiates upon plugging in the charger, then automatically retrieves the charger and closes the charging area’s cap/cover after complete charging.
- This automatic charging feature reduces user inconvenience and offers better cost-effectiveness than charging robots.
- Hyundai Mobis is reinventing itself as a ‘Mobility Solution Provider’ focusing on future mobility core technologies including electrification, autonomous driving, and IVI.
- The company achieved USD 9.22 billion in orders from global automakers last year.
- Hyundai Mobis is intensifying efforts to penetrate foreign markets and expand its order book as part of its long-term growth strategy.
