# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America | Georgia.org | Georgia](https://georgia.org/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america)  ·  `page_000021`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is reshaping Georgia’s manufacturing landscape with its first dedicated mass-production EV and hybrid plant.
- At full production, the cutting-edge Metaplant will be able to support the production of 500,000 units of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis electric and hybrid vehicles annually.
- This historic $7.59 billion investment will create at least 8,500 new jobs on-site at the Metaplant.
- The project has spurred nearly 6,900 additional new jobs through direct, offsite suppliers in Georgia.
- The project has spurred $2.5 billion in investments through direct, offsite suppliers in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- These investments are expected to generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- The job and earnings projections are according to a report from the Center for Automotive Research at the time of HMGMA’s grand opening.
- On May 20, 2022, Governor Brian P. Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Chairman Euisun Chung announce Hyundai’s selection of Georgia for its dedicated mass-production EV and hybrid vehicle manufacturing plant.
- The original commitment was $5.54 billion in investment and the creation of 8,100 new jobs.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai breaks ground at the Bryan County Megasite, marking the start of construction on this state-of-the-art facility.
- On May 26, 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution formalize their joint venture for battery production.
- On August 31, 2023, Hyundai expands the battery facility’s scope, adding $2 billion in investment.
- The August 31, 2023 expansion adds 400 more jobs.
- On June 18, 2024, Hyundai announces the all-electric IONIQ 5 SUV as the first vehicle to be produced at HMGMA.
- On October 7, 2024, HMGMA begins production on the first of its Georgia Made® EVs, the 2025 Hyundai IONIQ 5.
- On December 4, 2024, Hyundai deploys the first XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks as part of its Clean Logistics Project to reduce internal logistics emissions.
- As of March 26, 2025, at the time of its official opening, HMGMA has hired 1,200 Meta Pros.
- On March 26, 2025, HMGMA announces plans to expand manufacturing capacity to 500,000 vehicles annually.
- Hyundai’s investment solidifies Georgia’s position as a leader in sustainable innovation and advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia is paving the way for a fully integrated, closed-loop EV supply chain with a growing network of suppliers and partners.
- Doowon Climate Control Co., Ltd. is listed among the partners in Georgia's e-mobility ecosystem.
- Hyundai Motor Company cited speed-to-market, workforce, and the state’s ability to meet energy requirements for choosing Georgia.
- The close proximity to the Port of Savannah was central to the selection of the Bryan County Megasite.
- Connections to the port are available by both rail and highway.
- Hyundai will be able to access global markets quickly and efficiently through these connections.
- Georgia Quick Start, the No. 1 workforce training program in the nation, is constructing a customized training center at the Metaplant site.
- HMGMA has partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses.
- EVP TCC training is also available at Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- Euisun Chung, Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group, stated: “Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America not only represents the Group’s advanced manufacturing capabilities and commitment to innovation, but also our investment in relationships with our partners and communities right here in Georgia.”
- Euisun Chung added: “With the rich history of craftsmanship and manufacturing in this community, together with the talented workforce at HMGMA we are building the future of mobility with America, in America.”
