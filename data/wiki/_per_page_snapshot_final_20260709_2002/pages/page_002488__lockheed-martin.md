# Lockheed Martin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-17

**Source:** [Georgia Applauds Lockheed Martin’s 75 Years of Production, Impact | Top State for Talent](https://topstatefortalent.georgia.gov/news/2026-06-17/georgia-applauds-lockheed-martins-75-years-production-impact)  ·  `page_002488`

## Facts

- Lockheed Martin is celebrating 75 years of continuous operations in Marietta, Georgia.
- The company's Cobb County manufacturing facility supports more than 5,600 Georgia jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined company officials and state, local, and federal leaders to celebrate the milestone on June 17, 2026.
- Lockheed Martin announced 1,200 new Marietta-based jobs as part of this milestone.
- Since 2019, Lockheed Martin has increased its Georgia workforce by more than 25%.
- The company added over 1,200 jobs at its 8-million-square-foot Marietta facility alone since 2019.
- Statewide, Lockheed Martin employs more than 6,300 people in Georgia.
- In 2025, Lockheed Martin contributed $4.5 billion to Georgia’s economy.
- An additional 550 employees are based at Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay.
- More than 100 employees are based at Robins Air Force Base.
- The C-130J Super Hercules is Georgia’s top export and anchors the state’s second-largest manufacturing industry.
- The aerospace and aviation industries generated more than $23.9 billion in GDP in 2024.
- At its Marietta center of excellence, Lockheed Martin produces the C-130J Super Hercules and the F-35 Lightning II Center Wing Assembly.
- The facility supports the ongoing sustainment of the F-22 Raptor, C-5 Super Galaxy, and P-3 Orion.
- The Marietta facility is home to the Skunk Works® division.
- The C-130J and F-35 programs alone support more than 20,000 direct and indirect Georgia jobs through robust supplier networks.
- Marietta has been the global home of C-130 Hercules production since 1955.
- More than 2,700 C-130s have been built at the site and delivered to over 70 operators worldwide.
- The manufacturing line is America’s last remaining production capacity for building aircraft for the airlift mission.
- Careers at Lockheed Martin in Marietta span advanced manufacturing, engineering, AI, data analytics, IT, finance, operations, and more.
- Lockheed Martin invests in Georgia’s future workforce through partnerships with the University System of Georgia, Technical College System institutions, and K-12 STEM education.
- These workforce efforts align Georgia’s education pipeline with employer needs as part of Governor Kemp’s Top State for Talent initiative.
- Nearly 2,000 veterans, reservists, and military spouses are employed directly by Lockheed Martin in Georgia.
- Marietta employees give back 21,000 volunteer hours to schools and organizations each year.
- Georgia’s nine military bases offer competitive advantages to Lockheed Martin and other defense and advanced manufacturing companies.
