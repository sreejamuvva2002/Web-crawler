# 2025 Collegiate Wind Competition (CWC)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Twelve Teams Selected To Advance to 2025 Collegiate Wind Competition Final Event | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/collegiatewindcompetition/articles/twelve-teams-selected-advance-2025-collegiate-wind)  ·  `page_003974`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced the 12 winning teams for Phase 2 of the 2025 Collegiate Wind Competition (CWC) on January 17, 2025.
- The CWC is an annual competition held since 2014 that prepares students from colleges and universities across the United States for jobs in the wind energy industry by giving them real-world experience in technical design and project management.
- Phase 2 winners include Brigham Young University, California Maritime Academy, California Polytechnic State University, Iowa State University, Johns Hopkins University, Kansas State University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Pennsylvania State University, Rice University, University of Massachusetts Lowell, The University of Texas at Dallas, and Virginia Tech.
- Finalists were selected from 35 Phase 1 winners based on submissions in prototype wind turbine design, turbine testing, hypothetical offshore wind farm planning, and community/industry outreach.
- Each winning team receives a cash prize of up to $15,000 per team and advances to Phase 3 to refine wind turbine prototypes, present hypothetical wind farm sites, and illustrate community outreach plans.
- Teams not selected can still participate as learn-along teams but are ineligible for any awards.
- The final event will be held at the American Power Association's CLEANPOWER 2025 Conference and Exhibition in Phoenix, Arizona, where overall first-, second-, and third-place teams will be selected.
- According to the National Laboratory of the Rockies' 2024 National Wind Energy Workforce Assessment, total wind energy workers are projected to reach about 258,000 by 2030, requiring the industry to train and hire up to 125,000 new workers.
- The competition is funded by DOE's Wind Energy Technologies Office and managed by the National Laboratory of the Rockies.
