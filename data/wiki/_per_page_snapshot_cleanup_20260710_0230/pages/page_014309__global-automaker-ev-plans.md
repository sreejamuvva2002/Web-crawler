# Global Automaker EV Plans

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-09

**Source:** [Automakers Are Adding Electric Vehicles to Lineups](https://www.yahoo.com/news/why-electric-cars-may-soon-192805219.html)  ·  `page_014309`

## Facts

- Many automakers have detailed plans to electrify large portions of their fleets over the next decade, with some announcing goals for fully electrified lineups in as little as five years.
- A record number of almost 100 pure battery electric vehicles (BEVs) are set to debut by the end of 2024 if all goes according to plan.
- Gabe Shenhar, associate director of Consumer Reports' Auto Test Center, states that more affordable models have the potential to sway a significant percentage of car-buyers toward EVs due to efficiency, performance, and lower ownership costs.
- American Honda aims for all its sales to be zero-emissions, electrified vehicles by 2040, with a projected ramp-up of 40 percent of sales by 2030 and 80 percent by 2035.
- Honda sells popular Honda- and Acura-branded cars, SUVs, and pickups to American consumers.
- Honda has previously sold electrified models ranging from the Civic hybrid, CRZ, and Insight to more recent models like the Accord hybrid, CR-V hybrid, and Clarity hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and electric.
- Honda signed a memorandum of understanding with General Motors in late 2020 allowing Honda to use GM's upcoming Ultium batteries.
- GM states its Ultium technology will allow long-range electric travel among other benefits.
- The Honda-GM accord also calls for mutual vehicle platform-sharing, allowing either automaker to use the basic building blocks of GM's EV architecture.
- Honda announced it will have its first electric vehicles based on the e:Architecture platform by model year 2024.
- The Honda Prologue is scheduled to launch in early 2024 in North America.
- Automotive News reported in January that Honda plans to build a Honda EV in 2023 at a GM plant in Mexico.
- Honda also plans to build an Acura EV in 2024 at the GM plant in Tennessee where GM plans to build the Cadillac Lyriq EV.
- BMW entered the EV market early with its i3 electric city car, available at U.S. dealerships for the 2014 model year.
- BMW has teased concept EVs including the i4, iX, and Vision.
- BMW recently announced plans to introduce electric versions of the 5 Series and 7 Series sedans, and the X1 SUV.
- BMW says it will bring roughly a dozen new EVs to market by 2025.
- Mini and Rolls-Royce fall under the BMW umbrella, with a Mini Cooper electric model already available.
- Rumors suggest an upcoming Rolls-Royce EV that could be called the Silent Shadow.
- Ford says its passenger vehicles will be 100 percent emissions-free in Europe by 2030 but has not made the same commitment in the U.S.
- Ford sells an electric SUV called the Mustang Mach-E and an electric version of its commercial Transit van.
- Ford plans to introduce an electric version of its popular F-150 pickup, dubbed F-150 Lightning, for sale in spring 2022.
- Ford's Lincoln luxury brand projects that half its global sales will be zero-emissions models by the middle of this decade and will electrify its entire portfolio by 2030.
- GM includes the Buick, Cadillac, Chevrolet, and GMC brands and has a long history with EVs dating back to the late 1990s with the EV1.
- GM previously produced the Chevrolet Volt plug-in hybrid and the all-electric Chevrolet Bolt, which is currently the most reliable EV according to Consumer Reports' reliability survey.
- GM announced in January that it aspires to have an all-electric model lineup by 2035, with 40 percent of its models being pure EVs by the end of 2025.
- GM stressed it will not be making hybrids or plug-in hybrids, considering them transitional technology.
- GM recently introduced a redesigned Bolt and the Bolt EUV SUV variant.
- The GMC Hummer EV off-road truck is scheduled to arrive early in 2022, followed by a Hummer SUV, an electric Silverado-based pickup, and a pair of Cadillac EVs: the Lyriq SUV and the Celestiq sedan.
- Hyundai already has the Kona Electric and Ioniq Electric models on the market and recently unveiled the first of three planned models in its all-electric Ioniq sub-brand, the Ioniq 5 SUV.
- Genesis, Hyundai's premium brand, is expected to unveil its own EVs soon, and Hyundai aims to sell 1 million pure electric vehicles worldwide by 2025.
- Jaguar and Land Rover have been a subsidiary of Tata Motors since 2008, with the Jaguar I-Pace electric SUV already in production.
- In early 2021, the company announced all Jaguar and Land Rover models will have an electric version by the end of the decade, including six new electric Land Rover SUVs over the next five years and an all-electric Jaguar lineup by 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group owns a minority stake in Kia, which plans to release EVs riding on the same E-GMP platform, a modular EV-specific substructure with floor-mounted batteries that underpins Ioniq-branded EVs.
- Kia already has an electric version of its Niro and will have 11 EVs—seven dedicated electric models—in its lineup by 2026, starting with the EV6.
- Kia wants to sell 1.6 million "eco-friendly vehicles" globally by the end of the decade.
- Mazda does not yet offer any electric cars in the U.S. but recently started selling the MX-30 EV and plug-in hybrid models in other markets.
- Mazda plans to introduce an EV-only platform within the next few years, with all its nameplates available with some level of electrification by 2030, though it has not specified the proportion of pure electric versus plug-in or mild hybrids.
- Mercedes' electric push began in 2007 with an EV version of the Smart ForTwo city car, and the automaker plans to introduce 10 new EV models by 2022.
- The U.S. market will see the EQS, an electric version of the S-Class sedan, in fall 2021, while other EV models like the EQA and EQB SUVs are on hold for the U.S. but expected to debut overseas.
- The EQS will have a 435-mile range based on European test standards, which tend to be more optimistic than EPA numbers.
- Mitsubishi has a limited presence in North America but has been partially owned by Nissan since 2016 and has access to significant technical resources through the Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi Alliance.
- The company announced plans to expand its electrified lineup to 50 percent of global sales by 2030, having previously introduced the short-lived i-MiEV electric hatchback around the same time as the Nissan Leaf and already selling plug-in hybrid models.
- Over the past decade, Nissan has sold half a million Leaf EVs worldwide, with its next North America-bound electric model being the Ariya small SUV scheduled to go on sale late in 2021.
- Nissan has a hybrid technology called e-Power that uses a gasoline engine to generate electricity for an electric motor-driven powertrain, akin to how the Chevrolet Volt operated.
- Peyman Kargar, chairman of Infiniti (Nissan's luxury marque), said in mid-2020 that his division would add a performance-oriented hybrid and a pure BEV to Infiniti's lineup, though he did not specify when.
- Stellantis formed in early 2021 when Fiat Chrysler Automobiles merged with Groupe PSA, encompassing Alfa Romeo, Chrysler, Dodge, Fiat, Jeep, Maserati, and Ram truck brands in North America.
- Stellantis announced a major worldwide EV initiative in July 2021, declaring all its brands committed to offering fully electric vehicles, key to which is the introduction of four electric platforms with ranges from 300 to 500 miles.
- Stellantis aims for 40 percent of its U.S. models sold to be low-emissions vehicles and expects battery costs to drop significantly over the next few years.
- By 2024, Stellantis expects to have two battery technologies in place: a high-density option and a nickel cobalt-free version, with solid state batteries available by 2026.
- Jeep promises a "zero emission vehicle in every segment" by 2025, Dodge pledges an electric muscle car (a Charger) in 2024, and Ram plans a full-sized electric truck by 2024.
- Subaru announced in early 2021 that it would offer some form of electrification on all its models by mid-decade but did not specify the proportion of vehicles that would be EVs, plug-in hybrids, or hybrids.
- Tesla launched with a roadster, followed by the Model S hatchback and Model X SUV, known for impressive driving range and technological innovation particularly in over-the-air updates and driver assistance systems.
- The Model 3 sedan has broadened the brand's appeal, while the line includes the recent Model Y crossover delivering a driving range of 300-plus miles but a stiff ride.
- The Model S recently received a significant update with a freshened exterior and interior as well as a new Plaid high-performance version, and Tesla expects to see a pickup truck and second-generation roadster in the near future.
- Along with its luxury division Lexus, Toyota has built cars and SUVs with hybrid powertrains for decades, selling more than 6 million Prius hybrids in 20 years of production.
- Toyota moved into hydrogen fuel-cell-powered cars with its redesigned Mirai, which generates electricity to power the car motor without needing recharging but requires a steady supply of hydrogen from an onboard tank.
- According to the Department of Energy Alternative Fuels Data Center, there are only a handful of hydrogen filling stations all in California, leading to slow adoption.
- Toyota recently announced a partnership with Shell to build more hydrogen stations while simultaneously developing a dedicated EV platform.
- Toyota will add two EVs and a plug-in hybrid to its selection in 2021 and will offer electrified versions of various models between now and 2025.
- Volkswagen committed to becoming an EV-focused manufacturer following the "dieselgate" scandal, with its Group including Audi, Bentley, Bugatti, Lamborghini, Porsche, and Volkswagen.
- The corporation says it will launch 70 pure electric vehicles and 60 hybrids by the end of the decade, some already in production.
- VW's most recent addition is the ID.4 small electric SUV aimed at mass market consumers, while Audi already had an E-Tron model in production and recently introduced its electric flagship E-Tron GT built on the same platform as the Porsche Taycan EV.
- Bentley CEO says all Bentleys will be plug-ins (hybrid or pure electric) by 2026 and all-electric by the end of the decade, while Lamborghini unveiled the Terzo Millennio concept in 2017 inviting speculation into its electrification plans.
- Volvo plans to build only pure electric vehicles by the end of the decade, having been owned by Zhejiang Geely Holding Group (which also owns Lotus) since 2010.
- Volvo already has one electric model out, the XC40 Recharge, and Polestar once its performance sub-brand is slated to be all-electric soon.
