# Aurubis

**Source:** [Germany's Aurubis to bring jobs to Augusta, Georgia with planned large metals recycling plant.](https://www.ajc.com/news/business/metals-recycler-with-ties-to-ev-industry-bringing-jobs-to-augusta/KAIHMCXBGVHG7ITGBF6CJV55A4/)  ·  `page_017328`

## Facts

- A German company intends to invest $340 million and create 125 jobs by building a recycling and smelting facility in Augusta.
- Aurubis is a recycler of copper and other metals.
- Aurubis plans to produce materials at the plant that will be used in electronic products including lithium-ion batteries.
- The project represents the first multi-metal recycling operation of its kind in the United States.
- It is the largest single investment by a German company in Georgia, according to Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- Constrained supplies of crucial metals needed for batteries in electric cars and other products have raised worries in industry and government.
- Aurubis plans to build at the Port of Augusta Industrial Park.
- The facility will be located on a former military base.
- The plant will be the second largest metals recycler operation in the world.
- State officials stated that Aurubis will be able to attract more than 60% of its workforce from the local community.
