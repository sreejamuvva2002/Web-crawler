# Clayton County

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-30

**Source:** [Clayton County | Aiming for New Heights - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/04/30/clayton-county-aiming-for-new-heights/)  ·  `page_008357`

## Facts

- Clayton County has access to 19 exits and four major interstates (I-75, I-85, I-285 and I-675), providing logistical benefits for businesses.
- GlassesUSA is investing $10 million to move its U.S. headquarters from New York to College Park, adding 75 jobs, with access to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and the Port of Savannah strongly influencing its decision.
- Automotive parts producer GSC Steel Stamping added 75 jobs following a $150,000 million investment to acquire the assets of parts manufacturer Dixien.
- GSC Steel Stamping operates a 200,000-square-foot facility in Lake City that it claims is the largest minority-owned automotive stamping plant globally.
- TOTO invested $238 million and added more than 300 skilled workers to its production floor in Morrow.
- Clayton County serves as a pipeline from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport to the Georgia Ports Authority for moving products.
- Victory Landing Logistics Center is a 90-acre, five-building industrial site just off I-285, representing a $400 million project offering 700,000 square feet of warehouse space with an additional 335,000 square feet under construction.
- Construction on MARTA’s bus rapid-transit expansion in Clayton County is expected to begin this year to improve metro area mobility.
- MARTA expects to spend approximately $338 million on dedicated bus lanes and permanent stations for a 15.5-mile route with 13 stations extending from College Park MARTA Station to Southlake Mall.
- The transit expansion's base of operations will be the $150 million Clayton County Operations and Maintenance Facility, funded partly by $33 million in federal funds, two Federal Transit Administration grants, and a $5 million earmark secured by U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock.
- The MARTA expansion is expected to generate $174,000 in annual revenue through the hiring of hundreds of construction workers and creation of more than 700 permanent jobs.
- Clayton County Public School System (CCPS) plans to spend almost a billion dollars to renovate or replace 36 aging schools, supported by a $440 million ESPLOST passed by voters last year.
- CCPS is partnering with Georgia Power, Delta Air Lines, Atlanta Gas Light, and Construction Ready to get 700 graduating seniors gainfully employed through its Student Employment Services Division.
- Clayton State University's Launchpad Leadership Academy provides four-year scholarships, pairs students with corporate partners for hands-on learning experiences, and certifies completers as workforce ready.
- Workforce-friendly majors at Clayton State University include cyber technology and information technology, which are popular among incoming students.
- BlueStar Studios purchased 53 acres of the former Fort Gillem Army post in Forest Park to create a film production campus with two 20,000-square-foot stages and 100,000 square feet of combined stage, office, and flex space.
- BlueStar Studios cited state tax credits for film businesses locating to Georgia as an incentive for its location decision.
- The Urban Redevelopment Authority facilitated the sale of the former Fort Gillem land to BlueStar Studios along with a 10-year property tax abatement.
