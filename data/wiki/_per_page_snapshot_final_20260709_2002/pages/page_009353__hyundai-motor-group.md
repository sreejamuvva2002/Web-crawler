## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group released a promotional video titled “Ballet Parking: when robots dance to park your car.” featuring robots parking a Kia EV3.
- The promotional video was first released in October of last year, with viewership surging since April and continuing to climb.
- The video has been shared by over 48 global media platforms, communities and social media channels, accumulating combined views exceeding 5.8 million.
- Viewer reactions include comments such as “This is truly impressive technology” and “I can’t believe these tiny robots are moving cars.”
- The 40-second YouTube Shorts clip includes a “5x” marker indicating the parking sequence is shown at five times the actual speed.
- A caption on the video states: “This is a dramatized video and may differ from reality.”
- Hyundai unveiled a commercialized version of its robotic parking technology in June last year at the Factory Seoul building in Seongsu-dong, eastern Seoul.
- The robotic parking device consists of two flat, rectangular robots that slide under a vehicle, secure the wheels and body using robotic arms, and transport it freely.
- These parking robots are currently used in vehicle manufacturing at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore.
- The spinning movements and speed of the robots in the promotional video were created using computer graphics and AI rather than being real-life footage.
- Hyundai stated that combining actual technologies with AI enhanced realism and engagement, likely boosting the video’s promotional impact.
- A related article headline notes that Hyundai's Georgia plant will use Boston Dynamics' Atlas humanoid robot from October.
