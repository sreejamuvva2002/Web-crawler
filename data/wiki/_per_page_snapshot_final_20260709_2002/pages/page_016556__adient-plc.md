# Adient plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-30

**Source:** [Adient plc - Annual Report: 2022 (Form 10-K) | Annual Statements](https://annual-statements.com/company/adient-plc/annual-report-2022-form-10k-49250)  ·  `page_016556`

## Facts

- Adient plc became an independent company on October 31, 2016, as a result of the separation of Johnson Controls International plc's automotive seating and interiors businesses.
- Adient is a global leader in the automotive seating supply industry with leading market positions in the Americas, Europe, and China.
- The company operates more than 200 wholly- and majority-owned manufacturing or assembly facilities across 31 countries.
- Adient employs approximately 75,000 people worldwide.
- The company produces and delivers automotive seating for all vehicle classes and major OEMs, delivering into more than 20 million vehicles every year.
- Adient's proprietary technologies extend into complete seating systems, frames, mechanisms, foam, head restraints, armrests, and trim covers.
- The company utilizes a Global Core Product Portfolio (CPP) strategy for part and design reuse to reduce complexity, inventory, floor space, development time, product cost, and investment.
- Adient's worldwide engineering network includes ten core development centers located in Plymouth (USA), Trencin (Slovakia), Burscheid (Germany), Yokohama (Japan), Solingen (Germany), Chongqing (China), Kaiserslautern (Germany), Ceska Lipa (Czech Republic), Ansan (South Korea), and Pune (India).
- In China, Adient operates through 8 joint ventures with 35 manufacturing locations in 22 cities.
- Adient supplies seating to major global OEMs including BMW, Mercedes-Benz Group, Ford Motor Company, General Motors Company, Honda Motor Company, Hyundai Motor Company, Jaguar Land Rover, Kia Corporation, Mazda Motor Corporation, Mitsubishi Motor Corporation, Nissan Motor Corporation, Renault Group, Subaru, Stellantis N.V., Suzuki Motor Corporation, Toyota Motor Corporation, Volkswagen Group, and Volvo Car Group.
- The company also supplies regional OEMs such as BAIC Motor Co., Ltd., Changan Automobile (Group) Co., Ltd., Chery Automobile Co. Ltd., FAW Group Corporation, Proton Holdings Berhad, Ashok Leyland, Tata Motors Limited, and Zhejiang Geely Holding Group Co., Ltd.
- Adient supplies newer automotive manufacturers including Tesla Motors, Inc., NIO, and Xpeng Motors.
- The company has more than 8 joint venture partnerships with key OEMs, including Guangzhou Automobile Group Co., Ltd., Beijing Automobile Works Co., Ltd., and FAW Group Corporation.
- Adient faces competition from traditional seating suppliers, component specialists, and competitors partnered with OEMs through ownership or interlocking business relationships.
- Key independent competitors include Lear Corporation, Toyota Boshoku Corporation, Faurecia SA, and Magna International Inc.
- Raw materials used by Adient include steel, aluminum, polyurethane chemicals, fabrics, leather, vinyl, and polypropylene.
- The adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) is accelerating globally due to product offerings from legacy manufacturers and new entrants, government incentives, and consumer acceptance.
- While seating systems are not largely impacted by the shift to EVs, key attributes of seat design are evolving as the market pivots toward EVs.
- This EV market shift provides Adient with opportunities to provide value-added solutions through its Evolution of Seating Systems Sustainability (ES3) initiative and capture market share through new entrants.
- Adient has developed an interiors concept for autonomous driving that addresses major seating and interior trends for the future automotive industry.
- The company aims to reduce scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions by 75% by 2030, using 2019 as the base year.
- Adient demonstrates material content compliance through the International Material Data System (IMDS) to meet national and international standards, laws, and regulations.
