# Rivian Automotive

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive aims to secure close to $15 billion in debt to facilitate the construction of an electric vehicle manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The EV manufacturer announced this aim on Monday.
- An agreement dated November 9 outlines that taxable bonds will be issued by the Georgia Department of Economic Development and the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties.
- This bond issuance was outlined in a securities filing.
- Rivian has committed to acquiring the bonds as they are released.
- Under the agreement, Rivian will make property tax payments of at least nearly $300 million until 2047.
- The property tax payment amount may escalate if the automaker surpasses its $5 billion investment threshold.
- Rivian initiated its plans for the Georgia plant in 2021.
- Rivian disclosed its intention to establish the Georgia plant, which is scheduled for commissioning by 2024.
- The prospective facility is projected to provide employment for over 7,500 individuals.
- The prospective facility is ultimately projected to produce 400,000 vehicles annually.
