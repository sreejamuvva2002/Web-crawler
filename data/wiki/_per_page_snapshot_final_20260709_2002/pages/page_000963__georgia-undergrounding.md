# Georgia Undergrounding

## Facts

- Georgia Undergrounding provides LaGrange, GA with underground utility and infrastructure construction services via tunnel construction services, horizontal auger boring and (HDD) horizontal directional drilling more commonly called directional boring.
- The company specializes in the underground boring of utility pipelines for satellite, cable, TV, Internet, wi-fi, fiber optic, security, electric power, solar, wind, telephone, oil, gas, petroleum, steam, sewer, drain, water and irrigation crossings.
- LaGrange, GA directional boring experts excavate an opening beneath the ground without disturbing the surface to bore underground pipes and conduit for utility lines of all types of lengths and diameters whether large or small.
- The company has over 25 years of directional drilling experience and has bored and installed utility pipelines under trees, under city roads, under bridges, under railway tracks and even under rivers.
- Most directional boring machines use drilling fluid while a few machines are designed to use either air or air and foam for drilling in rock.
- Directional drilling fluid is generally a mixture of bentonite clay and water, with additives used to improve borehole performance.
- Drilling fluid (mud) is classified as non-toxic and can be disposed of accordingly.
- Upon reaching the exit point, the drill bit is detached and the end of the drill pipe is attached to a back reamer or hole opener to enlarge the borehole with consecutive passes.
- When the borehole is between 25% - 50% larger than the pipe to be installed, the utility product pipe is pulled in while rotating the drill pipe and reamer using a swivel to prevent rotation of the pipe.
- For some power, cable and telecommunications projects, the drill pipe itself becomes the conduit and is left in the ground upon reaching the exit point, known as “drill and leave”.
- Directional boring is used for utility installation due to lower cost, directional capabilities, no access pit required, shorter project completion times, less traffic disruption, deeper product pipe installation possible, longer product pipe length installation possible, and being safer for the environment.
- Underground boring preserves the integrity of existing landscape, allows curved or straight lines to avoid underground obstacles, is quicker than open cut excavation requiring fewer workers, and can drill underneath roads, buildings and waterways.
- The company has bored telecommunications & power cable conduits, water lines, sewer lines, gas lines, oil lines, product pipelines and environmental remediation casings from 2” to 48” in size via directional drilling under city, county and state roads.
- Additional methods for boring underneath roads include slick bore, dry bore, jack and bore, hammering, bentonite boring, rotary drilling, wet boring, mud jacking, slurry boring, and rotary boring.
- Services in LaGrange, GA are categorized under NAICS Code 238910 (Boring for building construction), NAICS Code 237990 (Horizontal Directional Drilling), and various CSI Codes for trenchless utility installation and tunnel excavation.
