# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-30

**Source:** [Kia Georgia begins assembly of the 2025 Kia EV9 All-Electric SUV - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/kia-georgia-begins-assembly-of-the-2025-kia-ev9-all-electric-suv/)  ·  `page_000291`

## Facts

- The 2025 Kia EV9 is the first electric vehicle assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The EV9 joins the award-winning Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage SUVs currently assembled at Kia’s West Point facility.
- Assembly of the all-electric 2025 EV9 three-row SUV began on Thursday, May 30, 2024, at Kia Georgia’s assembly plant in West Point.
- The vehicle was introduced by SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon, President and CEO for Kia North America and Kia America.
- The EV9 was driven off the assembly line by Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp and James Watson, Kia Georgia’s Vice President of Production.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the expansion of Kia’s operations in Georgia and the addition of the new product line is an example of state and local partners working with a valued job creator.
- Governor Kemp thanked Kia Georgia team members for choosing the Peach State as they work to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- The EV9 joins the lineup of SUVs currently being assembled on Kia Georgia’s assembly line: Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage.
- This launch is the culmination of a more than $200 million expansion.
- The expansion added approximately 200 new jobs at the plant.
- The expansion provides flexibility to assemble both EVs and traditional products along the same assembly line.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon stated that the launch is the latest example of Kia’s commitment to the U.S. market and Georgia.
- Through Plan S, Kia aims to be a leader in the e-mobility sector for years to come.
- The EV9 has won World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- Stuart Countess is President and CEO of Kia Georgia.
- Countess noted that almost 15 years ago, on Nov. 16, 2009, the first Kia vehicle rolled off the same assembly line.
- Countess credited the Kia Georgia team with driving milestones and turning challenges into opportunities.
- Kia Georgia is adding its flagship EV to the facility to enter a new era with a focus on assembling high-quality vehicles in West Point.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- Kia Georgia has an annual capacity of 350,000 units.
- Kia Georgia is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Ga.
- Kia Georgia began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Kia Georgia produces the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV (excludes HEV and PHEV models), Sportage SUV (excludes HEV and PHEV models), and the all-electric EV9 SUV.
