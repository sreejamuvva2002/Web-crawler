# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-14

**Source:** [Georgia breaks international trade record for second consecutive year](https://fox28savannah.com/news/local/georgia-ports-trade-record-economic-development-money-united-states-value-international-brian-kemp)  ·  `page_012923`

## Facts

- For the second year in a row, Georgia had a record-breaking year for international trade.
- GDEcD's 2022 end-of-year report showed that Georgia’s total trade reached $196 billion in 2022.
- Georgia ranked seventh in the U.S. for the dollar value of trade.
- The state’s top five trading partners were Korea, Mexico, Canada, Germany and China.
- Aerospace products remained the state’s number one export.
- Motor vehicles were the top imported product into Georgia.
- $148 billion in imports passed through Georgia in 2022, representing a 20% growth from the previous year.
- GDEcD leaders attribute the trade increase to the state’s appeal as a desirable location for businesses.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that 85 percent of exporters were small businesses, spanning from the Port of Savannah to the North Georgia mountains and everywhere in between.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Savannah’s Port plays a major role in the state’s economic growth by providing infrastructure and capacity for businesses of all sizes to participate in the global economy.
- Griff Lync, director of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated the GPA is working to fulfill last year’s promise to achieve a 60% increase in port capacity by 2025.
