# Hanwha Solutions’ Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Hanwha Solutions’ Qcells is one of the world’s largest and most recognized photovoltaic (PV) manufacturers, known for its high-quality solar cells and modules.
- The company selected Gray to build its first U.S. facility, an award-winning solar module assembly plant in Dalton, GA.
- Qcells soon selected Gray for a second project for a new facility with 420,000 s.f. of production space, a 400,000 s.f. raw and finished goods warehouse, and a corporate office complex.
- Qcells announced plans to partner with Gray on the construction of a production facility that President Biden described as “the largest solar investment in U.S. history.”
- The new facility will create thousands of good-paying jobs.
- Gray aims to strengthen its relationship with Qcells for the benefit of their companies, communities, and American-made renewable energy.
- The newest production facility is located in White, GA.
- The project timeline indicates it will be operational in 26 months.
- Qcells has a customer relationship with Gray that stretches back to 2005.
- The newest manufacturing complex is located in White, GA, just outside of Cartersville and only 35 miles from the Dalton campus.
- The complex will be the first in the U.S. to execute the total manufacture of silicon ingots and wafers, solar cells, and finished solar modules.
- The facility will boast an annual production capacity of 3.3 gigawatts (GWDC).
- This capacity solidifies Qcells' status as the market leader in domestic solar energy.
- The project has received national recognition as the largest investment in domestic solar energy production.
- The endeavor was largely made possible by the passing of the Inflation Reduction Act and its related incentives for manufacturers of advanced technology to begin U.S. operations.
- The Gray team developed a close partnership with Qcells to meet aggressive schedules and deliver a first-class facility.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in mid-2025.
