# Sabic

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-11

**Source:** [Phase-change Thermoplastics Can Prevent EV Battery Thermal Runaway](https://www.plasticstoday.com/automotive-mobility/phase-change-thermoplastics-can-prevent-ev-battery-thermal-runaway)  ·  `page_011571`

## Facts

- Engineering plastics provider Sabic recently reported test results underscoring the potential of thermoplastic-based thermal runaway barrier solutions to prevent fire spread in electric vehicle (EV) batteries.
- Sabic’s sub-system level test series showed that a battery module box made from its Stamax 30YH570 long-glass-fiber polypropylene (PP) resin with an intercellular thickness as thin as 1 mm has potential to prevent thermal runaway propagation in 18650 cylindrical cells.
- The Stamax grade provides the necessary thermal insulation and flame resistance to reduce the chances of cell-to-cell propagation in a thermal runaway scenario and mitigate risks of catastrophic safety incidents.
- This material in combination with compressible foams has shown promising results in containing thermal runaway propagation in prismatic and pouch cells.
- In 2023, the Stamax 30YH570 resin earned the UL Verified Mark from Underwriters Laboratories (UL) for effective flame-delay performance.
- The UL assessment was based on UL 2596, Test Method for Thermal and Mechanical Performance of Battery Enclosure Materials, using the thermal runaway box method.
- Thermal runaway propagation tests are performed using a cluster of 15 commercial 18650 and 11 21700 lithium-ion battery cells enclosed in a steel chamber, with and without injection-molded Stamax 30YH570 resin blocks serving as a thermal barrier.
- Initiating cells were thermally abused by heating them through an electrical tape to begin thermal runaway during testing.
- In tests without the thermal barrier, electric and chemical energies stored in the cells were discharged as high-temperature gas, open-flame, high-speed particles, and sudden pressure increases, causing immediate propagation to all neighboring cells and severe damage to the EV battery pack.
- In tests using the thermal barrier made from Stamax 30YH570 resin, heat transfer and thermal propagation from thermal abuse of initiating cells were effectively suppressed, leaving all remaining cells safe and intact.
- The flame-retardant material changes phase from solid to liquid and gas and removes heat via latent heat and intumescent capabilities during severe thermal events, acting as an effective thermal shield to mitigate heat transfer and fire spread.
- At the recent NPE show, Sabic highlighted EV battery applications successfully employing its advanced thermoplastics.
- One application is the battery module from the 2023 Hyundai Ioniq 6, which uses Sabic’s H1030 PP compound for module enclosures.
- The H1030 material is a 30% glass-fiber-reinforced, non-halogenated, flame-retardant compound that meets China’s GB/T 41467.3-2015 fire safety regulation for EVs surviving five minutes at 1000°C.
- Additional EV battery components displayed at the show included enclosures, end plates, and busbars, either in production or developed as prototypes.
- Thermoplastics used for these applications offer benefits including weight and cost reduction, increased functional integration, and enhanced electrical and thermal insulation over conventional metals.
- Fahad Al-Harthi, global director of automotive at Sabic, stated that their portfolio of flame-retardant materials and expertise in application design can help enable new thermal runaway barrier approaches to improve EV battery safety, efficiency, and performance.
