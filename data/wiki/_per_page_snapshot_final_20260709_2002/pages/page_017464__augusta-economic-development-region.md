# Augusta Economic Development Region

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-28

**Source:** [Augusta | Richmond County: From Obstacles to Opportunity - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/07/28/augusta-richmond-county-from-obstacles-to-opportunity/)  ·  `page_017464`

## Facts

- Aurubis, a German-based multimetal recycling company, has invested at least $800 million in a first-of-its-kind facility in the U.S.
- The company is a global provider of non-ferrous metals used in electric vehicles, power plants, mobile phones, and computers.
- Aurubis is one of the largest copper recyclers in the world.
- Construction at the Aurubis facility is close to completion, with hopes to start producing product in the fourth quarter.
- The Aurubis project will create 240 jobs and currently employs roughly 200 people.
- Aurubis is located in the Augusta Corporate Park.
- GF Casting Solutions, a Swiss-based auto parts supplier, is expected to begin operations by the end of 2026.
- Phase One of the GF Casting facility is almost finished with equipment currently being installed.
- The GF Casting investment totals $224 million and will create 343 jobs.
- GF Casting Solutions is located in the Augusta Corporate Park, across from Aurubis.
- Manufacturing is one of the top industries in the region, with 438 manufacturers employing roughly 25,000 people in the MSA.
- The regional manufacturing industry is expected to grow by 2,000 more jobs by 2026.
- From 2013 to 2023, the seven-county MSA added 74 new manufacturing operations.
- As of the fourth quarter of 2024, manufacturers in the region are estimated to have made $8.1 billion in purchases from suppliers in the U.S.
- Of those supplier purchases, 27% or $2.2 billion were made within the Augusta MSA.
- Cal Wray, president of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, highlighted the growth and momentum in the regional manufacturing sector and provided key investment data.
- House Bill 880 allows military spouses to use an existing professional license in good standing from another state to get a job in Georgia, overcoming a six- to eight-month licensing hurdle.
- Military retirees living in Georgia will no longer have to pay income taxes on part of their retirement income, with up to $65,000 exempted beginning with the 2026 tax year.
- The Greater Augusta Black Chamber of Commerce has provided $577,000 in grants to small businesses and about $45,000 in loans to address access to capital challenges for local entrepreneurs.
- Accelerate Augusta, a microenterprise incubator housed in a city-owned building leased by the Downtown Development Authority, offers boot camps and resources for aspiring entrepreneurs and small businesses.
- The Accelerate Augusta center was made possible by a $2.35 million appropriations grant through Sen. Raphael Warnock’s office and a partnership with the city.
