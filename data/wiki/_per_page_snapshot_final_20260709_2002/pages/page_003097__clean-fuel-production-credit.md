# Clean Fuel Production Credit

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-01

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Clean Fuel Production Credit](https://afdc.energy.gov/laws/13321)  ·  `page_003097`

## Facts

- Beginning January 1, 2025, the Treasury Department will offer tax credits for the production and sale of low emission transportation fuels, including sustainable aviation fuel (SAF).
- The tax credit amount is $0.20 per gallon for non-aviation fuel and $0.35 per gallon for SAF.
- For facilities that satisfy the prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements, the credit amount is $1.00 per gallon for non-aviation fuel and $1.75 per gallon for SAF.
- For any taxable year, the Clean Fuel Production Credit is equal to the applicable credit amount per gallon multiplied by the fuel’s carbon dioxide emissions factor.
- Emissions factors will be published annually by the Secretary of the Treasury.
- Beginning January 1, 2025, tax credits will be adjusted for inflation.
- The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) released guidance on registration requirements to claim the credit in May 2024.
- The IRS released frequently asked questions about applying for registration in July 2024.
- Further guidance is forthcoming.
- The program references Public Law 117-169 and 26 U.S. Code 45Z.
- Jurisdiction is Federal.
- Type is Incentives.
- Administering agency is the U.S. Internal Revenue Service.
- Applicable technologies include Biodiesel, Ethanol, Natural Gas, and Renewable Diesel.
