# Novelis Inc.

## Facts

- Novelis, a subsidiary of India’s Hindalco Industries, will restart its Oswego hot mill in December, having previously anticipated starting it in the March quarter.
- Teams have been working around-the-clock to restore operations at Oswego quickly and safely while leveraging alternative resources to minimize customer disruption.
- Novelis recognized charges of $21 million related to the Oswego incident during the quarter.
- The plant is insured for property damage and business interruption losses related to such events, with about 70-80% recoverable through insurance in future periods.
- Novelis reported a 27% year-on-year growth in net income for the September quarter at $163 million.
- Excluding special items, the bottomline was 37% lower compared to the previous year at $113 million.
- The US-based aluminum-maker’s net sales were 10% higher compared to the previous year at $4.7 billion, mainly driven by higher average aluminum prices.
- Total rolled product shipments for the quarter were in line with the previous year at 941 kilotonnes.
- Slightly higher automotive and aerospace shipments were offset by lower beverage packaging and specialty shipments.
- Adjusted earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization (EBITDA) fell 9% on year to $422 million.
- The adjusted EBITDA made on each tonne of aluminum stood at $448, down 8% on year.
- The fall in EBITDA was primarily on account of a net negative tariff impact and higher aluminum scrap prices, which was partially offset by higher product pricing and cost efficiency actions.
- CEO Steve Fisher stated that the second quarter financial performance was in line with expectations for sequential improvement, reflecting solid execution in a continued dynamic environment.
- CEO Steve Fisher stated that the company’s diverse global footprint will be further strengthened with a significant investment in the U.S. to construct a state-of-the-art plant in Bay Minette to bring needed capacity to an undersupplied domestic market.
- Novelis spent $913 million on capital expenditure in the six months of the current fiscal so far.
- This capital expenditure was primarily for strategic investments in new rolling and recycling capacity under construction, including the company's new greenfield rolling and recycling plant in Bay Minette, Alabama.
- The company announced its earnings after market hours in India, and shares of parent company Hindalco Industries ended down 1.8% at 830.70 rupees on the BSE.
