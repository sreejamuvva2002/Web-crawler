# Storion Energy LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-31

**Source:** [Largo and Stryten Energy Finalize Formation of Storion Energy to Strengthen U.S. Energy Resilience and Address Long Duration Energy Storage Needs - The Globe and Mail](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/investing/markets/stocks/LGO/pressreleases/30756435/largo-and-stryten-energy-finalize-formation-of-storion-energy-to-strengthen-us-energy-resilience-and-address-long-duration-energy-storage-needs/)  ·  `page_050558`

## Facts

- Effective Friday, January 31, 2025, Largo Clean Energy Corp. and Stryten Critical E-Storage, LLC finalized the formation of a joint venture named Storion Energy LLC.
- Storion Energy is focused on removing barriers to entry for battery manufacturers by providing domestically sourced, price-competitive components for the flow battery industry, including vanadium electrolyte.
- The partnership leverages Largo’s high-quality vanadium supply, Largo Physical Vanadium Corp.’s innovative electrolyte leasing model, and Stryten’s U.S.-based manufacturing expertise.
- Francesco D’Alessio, President of LCE, stated that the transaction closing represents a transformational step for Largo as it advances its investment strategy into the fast-growing vanadium flow battery market.
- Storion Energy now provides a direct channel for Largo’s high-quality vanadium products into the long duration energy storage sector.
- Through Storion, the partners are establishing a fully integrated vanadium electrolyte supply chain in North America to address key challenges for vanadium flow battery adoption.
- Stryten paid US$1 million in cash directly to Largo and will contribute an additional US$6 million over time to fund Storion’s operations.
- LCE and Stryten each hold a 50% equity interest in Storion, with customary pre-emption rights and anti-dilution protections.
- Storion’s board composition will be generally proportional to ownership, with Stryten holding one additional seat.
- Largo amended its Safekeeping and Supply Agreements with LPV and assigned its Safekeeping Agreement to Storion.
- Largo and Storion entered a separate supply agreement granting Storion a right of first offer for vanadium products.
- Required third-party consents were received as closing conditions for the transaction.
- Storion Energy intends to support scalable domestic electrolyte production to establish a fully integrated vertical supply chain for utility-scale vanadium redox flow battery long-duration energy storage solutions in the U.S.
- Vanadium redox flow battery technology is described as a safe and reliable option for Battery Energy Storage Systems that need to provide energy storage of four or more hours.
- Storion has locations in Alpharetta, Georgia and Wilmington, Massachusetts.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Stryten Energy’s stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Largo Physical Vanadium Corp.’s strategy includes owning and actively supplying vanadium to end users of vanadium flow batteries to advance the integration of renewable energy in long duration storage.
- Vanadium is non-degrading and fully recyclable when used as electrolyte in vanadium flow batteries and offers carbon reducing attributes when used in steel alloying applications.
- Largo Inc. is one of the world’s largest primary vanadium producers, sourcing materials from its Maracás Menchen Mine in Brazil.
- Largo maintains a 50% ownership stake in Storion Energy as part of its strategic investment in the clean energy storage sector.
