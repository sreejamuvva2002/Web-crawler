# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [SK Battery Cuts Nearly 1,000 Georgia Jobs Amid Declining EV Demand - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/sk-battery-cuts-nearly-1000-georgia-jobs-amid-declining-ev-demand/)  ·  `page_000303`

## Facts

- SK Battery is cutting nearly 1,000 jobs at its Commerce plant amid tepid demand for battery-electric vehicles.
- SK Battery America announced March 6 that it would lay off 958 workers “to align operations to market conditions.”
- SK Battery America remains committed to Georgia and to building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The company is pursuing future customers, including the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- The company will retain about 1,600 workers at the $2.6 billion site.
- The Commerce plant received an incentives package valued at a combined $300 million from state and local governments.
- SK Battery announced the layoffs in a WARN notice filed with the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Top roles affected are assembly stacking operators (137 jobs), assembly nothing operators (130) and formation operators (129).
- Employees’ last day worked is Friday, but they will receive full pay for another 60 days.
- Chief Human Resources Officer Chuck Moore sent a letter regarding the layoffs and continued pay.
- The SK project was announced in 2017 during the waning days of the Nathan Deal administration.
- Newly elected Gov. Brian Kemp picked up the baton and pushed for the state to press the accelerator in its efforts to drive supplier investments.
- The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 created a complex landscape for SK, as it was still relying heavily on materials from China.
- To qualify for the full $7,500 tax credit under the IRA, a battery had to be both assembled in the U.S. and made from a minimum 50 percent content sourced from the U.S. or select trading partners.
- President Trump’s One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed July 4, removed the purchase incentives.
- Demand for new electric vehicles has cratered since, with Cox Automotive research pointing to a 30 percent decline year over year in January.
- SK On is working with Hyundai on a $5 billion battery joint-venture plant in Cartersville.
- An SK spokesperson said the company is “unaware of any changes” regarding the Cartersville JV.
- Work continues on a $4.3 billion LG-Hyundai site on the footprint of the Korean auto maker’s Metaplant near Savannah.
- The Savannah site was the subject of a September Immigration and Customs Enforcement raid that created a diplomatic spat with Korea after nearly 500 people were detained, causing work to pause for two months.
- Georgia’s U.S. senators, both Democrats, blamed Trump policies for Friday’s job cuts.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock said the administration put politics over people and slashed clean energy investments that helped bring good-paying manufacturing jobs to the state, and made manufacturing in Georgia more expensive with harmful tariff taxes.
- U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff warned that Trump’s policies threatened the EV boom in Georgia.
- Ossoff stated: “Let’s be clear: these were battery manufacturing jobs and now they’re gone... As predicted, Trump’s war on electric vehicles is hurting Georgia’s economy. We were booming and building new plants. Now Georgians are losing our jobs.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp traveled to Korea in October to calm nerves after the ICE raid.
- Kemp has enjoyed a strong relationship with Hyundai, SK and other Korean investors, often pointing out that their commitments were made before the IRA’s passage.
- Kemp told Global Atlanta just after meetings in Seoul that Biden-era incentives were an inflationary market distortion that artificially propped up electric-vehicle demand.
- Kemp added that stalled EV ecosystem projects across the state were too reliant on government largesse.
- Kemp said: “Their whole business model up on incentives, versus, if we let the market work with the companies that were coming before, they would have still built the supply chains out.”
- The SK plant in Commerce spans 2.7 million square feet.
- The SK plant represents $2.6 billion in capital investment.
- The SK plant maintains 22 GW in production capacity.
- The plant's capacity is enough to power 300,000 all-electric vehicles.
