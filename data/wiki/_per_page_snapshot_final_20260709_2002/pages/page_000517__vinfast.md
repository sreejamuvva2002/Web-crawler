# VinFast

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-04

**Source:** [The Economic Future Of Chatham Is Looking Brighter (And More Eco-Friendly) Than Ever](https://www.boldcre.com/post/the-economic-future-of-chatham-is-looking-brighter-and-more-eco-friendly-than-ever)  ·  `page_000517`

## Facts

- A $4 billion business development project is coming to Chatham County.
- VinFast, an electric vehicle and battery maker based in Vietnam, chose Triangle Innovation Point in Moncure for a new production facility.
- The site is expected to bring 7,500 local jobs to the area.
- Chatham County landed the largest economic development package in North Carolina’s history.
- The Chatham Economic Development Corporation (EDC) has been pitching the idea of an auto manufacturer in Chatham for more than 10 years.
- Chatham Park, a mixed-use development, is set to open in 2045 with 60,000 residents and 22,000 homes.
- The region covers 710 square miles in the heart of North Carolina with over 70,000 residents spanning the towns of Pittsboro, Siler City and Goldston.
- A majority of the population lives in rural areas.
- VinFast supports North Carolina’s goal of shifting to electric vehicles.
- Chatham County Manager Dan LaMontagne stated the investment will benefit the community for decades by employing thousands to build environmentally responsible vehicles that reduce the carbon footprint.
- Central Carolina Community College has educational pipelines in place to support the workforce needed for the EV industry.
- Chris Ehrenfeld, owner and CEO of BOLD and former chairman, noted Chatham County's growing residential and commercial real estate markets signal a bright economic future.
- The 64 corridor running east-west across Chatham is expected to see incredible growth and job creation from this project, Chatham Park, and the eventual development of the CAM site.
