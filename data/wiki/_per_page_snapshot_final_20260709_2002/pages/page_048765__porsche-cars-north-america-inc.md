# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-09-24

**Source:** [Porsche Commences New MultiMillion Dollar Development In Georgia Adjacent To Its North American Head](https://www.conceptcarz.com/a38867/porsche-georgia-north-american-headquarters.aspx)  ·  `page_048765`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (PCNA) announced a new multi-million dollar investment to further develop its North American headquarters and U.S. footprint in Georgia.
- The new 33-acre development is located at the Northeast corner of Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport in Atlanta, Georgia, adding to PCNA's existing 27-acre headquarters.
- Phase one of the development begins construction in September 2020 with a standalone Porsche Service Center South Atlanta.
- The service center will be independently developed, owned, and operated jointly by Hennessy Porsche North Atlanta and Porsche Atlanta Perimeter.
- PCNA will invest in a new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility and a track extension for the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta over the next three years.
- Kjell Gruner, newly appointed President and CEO of PCNA, stated that Porsche is committed to the United States and Atlanta, and sees a prime opportunity to enhance the customer experience by expanding the campus for faster service next to the world's busiest airport.
- The Porsche Experience Center Atlanta has already welcomed over 270,000 visitors since opening in 2015.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia's automotive industry is a $3 billion economic driver in the state and praised Porsche's continued commitment to Georgia.
- Mayor Alan Hallman of Hapeville, Georgia, expressed excitement over the expansion, stating it symbolizes tremendous growth and opportunities for the city.
- Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms thanked Porsche for investing in Atlanta and stated her administration looks forward to strengthening the local economy through highly skilled and middle-wage opportunities.
- The Porsche Service Center South Atlanta will be a 20,815 square foot facility on 2.5 acres of land.
- The new service facility will include 13 lift bays for customer vehicles, a Porsche Driver's Selection boutique, and a reception area with a waiting lounge.
- It is one of two standalone service centers for Porsche in the U.S., offering greater service capacity and convenience for Atlanta and surrounding cities.
- The expected completion date for the service center is the third quarter of 2021.
- Phase two of development is expected to begin in 2021 with construction of a parking structure, followed by the Classic Factory Restoration facility and Experience Center track extension.
- This second phase of development is expected to be completed in mid-2023.
- Established in 1984, PCNA is the exclusive U.S. importer of the Porsche 911, 718 Boxster and Cayman, Macan and Cayenne, Panamera, and Taycan.
- PCNA has been headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, since 1998.
- The company operates the first Porsche Experience Center in North America featuring a module-based 1.6 mile driver development track, business center, and Restaurant 356.
- PCNA also operates a second Porsche Experience Center near Los Angeles, which is a 53-acre complex featuring a 4.1 mile driver development track, business center, and Restaurant 917.
- PCNA supports 192 independently owned and operated Porsche dealerships in the U.S., supplying parts, service, marketing, and training.
- PCNA is an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Porsche AG, which is headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany.
