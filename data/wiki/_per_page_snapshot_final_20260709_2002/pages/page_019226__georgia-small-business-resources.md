# Georgia Small Business Resources

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Small Business Resources in Georgia - SME CPA](https://www.smecpa.com/small-business-resources-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_019226`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia defines a small business as one that is independently owned and operated with less than 300 employees or a revenue of less than $30 million.
- Up to 99% of companies in Georgia fall under this small business category and are vital to the state’s economy.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) offers extensive suggestions and assistance for enhancing business operations.
- GDEcD provides resources on its website for business licenses, suppliers, contractors, loans, certifications, and connections for entrepreneurs.
- Businesses may be eligible for certification as a women-owned or minority-owned business through GDEcD.
- Georgia Centers of Innovation (COI) is divided into five centers that assist the industries of Aerospace, Energy Technology, IT, Logistics, and Manufacturing.
- COI helps businesses navigate the business environment, find eligible research grants, and handle legalities for working with universities on projects.
- The Georgia Mentor Protégé Connection (MPC) offers a one-year partnership program between corporate mentors and aspiring entrepreneurs.
- MPC corporate mentors come from companies with headquarters in Georgia, and eligible small businesses must be nominated by past mentors.
- The Georgia Chamber of Commerce is the state’s largest business advocacy group, with 73% of its members being small businesses.
- The Chamber provides tools including a Small Business Advisory Council that offers a Webinar Series and other benefit programs.
- Local chambers serve almost every city and county in Georgia to support small businesses in their respective areas.
