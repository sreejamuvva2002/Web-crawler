# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Technical Targets for Liquid Alkaline Electrolysis | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/fuels/technical-targets-liquid-alkaline-electrolysis)  ·  `page_005493`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) has established technical targets for liquid alkaline electrolysis to guide technology development.
- The central goal is low-cost hydrogen production of $2/kg H2 by 2026 and $1/kg H2 by 2031.
- Many combinations of performance, efficiency, lifetime, and cost targets can achieve the central hydrogen production cost goals.
- The target combinations were developed with input from experts from industry and national laboratories.
- The targets are intended as a starting guidepost for technology developers.
- Separate technical target tables have been developed for electrolyzer technologies other than low-temperature liquid alkaline electrolyzers.
- All performance, durability, and capital cost targets must be met simultaneously on the same stack or system to achieve the levelized cost of hydrogen targets.
- The ultimate goal is to demonstrate these targets in stacks and systems at relevant commercial scales.
- Current status values are based on traditional liquid alkaline electrolyzer technology available in commercial products for many years.
- The 2026 performance target for electrolyzer stacks is 1.0 A/cm2 @ 1.8 V/cell, with an ultimate target of 2.0 A/cm2 @ 1.7 V/cell.
- The electrical efficiency target for stacks is 45 kWh/kg H2 (74% LHV) ultimately, down from a 2026 target of 48 kWh/kg H2 (69% LHV).
- The average degradation rate target for stacks is 2.1 mV/kh (0.13%/1,000 h) ultimately, with a 2026 target of 2.3 mV/kh (0.13%/1,000 h).
- The lifetime target for electrolyzer stacks is 80,000 hours of operation for both 2026 and ultimate goals.
- The stack capital cost target is $50/kW ultimately, with a 2026 target of $100/kW.
- The system energy efficiency target is 48 kWh/kg H2 (70% LHV) ultimately, down from a 2026 target of 52 kWh/kg H2 (64% LHV).
- The uninstalled system capital cost target is $150/kW ultimately, with a 2026 target of $250/kW.
- The ultimate hydrogen production cost target is $1.00/kg H2, with a 2026 target of $2.00/kg H2.
- Efficiency calculations include a 2.5% efficiency loss due to H2 crossover, used as an upper limit for safety margins.
- Status and targets assume an end-of-life performance loss of 10%.
- Degradation rate evaluation must account for operation near the stack’s nominal operating point, dynamic operation for duty cycle requirements, and on-off cycling if required by applications.
- End-of-life is defined as a 10% voltage loss from beginning-of-life operations following any break-in or conditioning period, measured at the same current density.
- Cost targets assume economies of scale have been achieved through high-volume manufacturing.
- Installation costs, including engineering, procurement, construction, and permitting, are excluded from system cost values.
- Production cost targets assume utilization of low-cost electricity (e.g., ≤$0.03/kWh) with high availability (e.g., >90%).
- Scenarios utilizing lower-cost electricity at lower capacity factors, such as from wind or solar, may also meet production cost targets.
