## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation has reported record fiscal 2024 third-quarter earnings and an increase in profitability.
- The company has raised its full-year guidance following robust growth in net orders for buses, including a surge in electric bus sales.
- Blue Bird's backlog stands at over 5,200 units, representing approximately $775 million in potential revenue.
- The company is also expanding its production capacity, backed by an $80 million grant from the Department of Energy.
- This expansion will enable Blue Bird to produce up to 14,000 buses annually and will create about 400 new jobs.
- Blue Bird projects revenue growth and higher adjusted EBITDA in the coming years.
- The company expects fiscal '24 revenue to reach $1.315 billion with adjusted EBITDA of $175 million.
- Projections for fiscal '25 indicate revenue between $1.4 billion and $1.5 billion, with adjusted EBITDA of $180 million to $200 million.
- CEO Phil Horlock is set to retire with Britton Smith taking over as CEO.
- Blue Bird is also developing a chassis business with a commercial product expected on the road in 2025.
