# State of Alabama

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electricity Laws and Incentives in Alabama](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/ELEC?state=AL)  ·  `page_028778`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Transportation’s NEVI Formula Program required the Alabama Department of Transportation to submit an annually updated EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan for fiscal years 2022 through 2026 describing how the state intended to distribute NEVI funds and their physical and cybersecurity strategies, including a Community Engagement Outcomes Report.
- The submitted NEVI plans must address updated NEVI Guidance.
- The Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs offers grants for light-duty EV chargers and the replacement of qualified medium- and heavy-duty diesel vehicles with new diesel or alternative fuel vehicles.
- Rebate grants are available for EV chargers; medium- and heavy-duty trucks; school, shuttle, and transit buses; freight switchers; airport ground support equipment; and forklifts and port cargo handling equipment.
- Vehicles must meet model year requirements to qualify for the rebate grants.
- Funding amounts for the rebates are based on vehicle type, fuel type, and applicant type.
- The rebate grants are funded by Alabama’s portion of the Volkswagen Environmental Mitigation Trust.
- The Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs administers the EV Charging Infrastructure Program allowing government and non-government entities to receive funding for installing publicly accessible direct current fast chargers and Level 2 EV chargers along non-interstate corridors.
- Additional terms and conditions apply to the EV Charging Infrastructure Program grants.
- The Alabama Department of Transportation is authorized to administer the Electric Transportation Infrastructure Grant Program to distribute grants for EV charging infrastructure expansion under Code of Alabama 40-12-242.
- EV owners must pay an annual fee in addition to standard registration fees.
- All-electric vehicle owners in Alabama must pay an annual fee of $203.
- Plug-in hybrid electric vehicle owners in Alabama must pay an annual fee of $103.
- Beginning in 2023, EV fees in Alabama increase by $3 every four years.
- A portion of the EV fees contributes to the Electric Transportation Infrastructure Grant Program, which provides grants for EV charging stations.
- The Alabama Legislature established a Green Fleets Review Committee and Green Fleets Policy outlining a procurement procedure for state vehicles based on criteria that includes fuel economy and life cycle costing.
- State fleet managers must classify their vehicle inventory for compliance with the policy and submit annual plans for procuring fuel-efficient vehicles.
- Annual procurement plans must reflect a 4% annual increase in average fleet fuel economy for light-duty vehicles, a 3% annual increase for medium-duty vehicles, and a 2% annual increase for heavy-duty vehicles per fiscal year.
- Government entities must manage and operate their fleets in a manner that is energy efficient, minimizes emissions, and reduces petroleum dependency by using specified proven technology identified by the Committee.
- An entity that owns, operates, leases, or controls electric vehicle charging stations is not defined as a public utility under Alabama Public Service Commission Docket No. 32694.
