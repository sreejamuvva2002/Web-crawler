# Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Ecosystem in India

**Type:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2019-04

**Source:** [I love my electric vehicle, but where do I charge it? This and other problems facing EV users in India | YourStory](https://yourstory.com/2019/04/electric-vehicle-infrastructure-india-charging-fame)  ·  `page_009739`

## Facts

- Electric vehicles are in the limelight with more than a handful of startups getting ready to launch their EVs.
- Startups like GoGreenBov, Tork, Ultraviolette, and Strom are ready to showcase/sell vehicles later this year (2019).
- Early mover Ather Energy is already present on roads and expanding.
- Revolt Intellicorp announced big investments to speed up release of electric bikes.
- Yulu, Vogo, Bounce, and ONN Bikes operate in last-mile electric mobility solutions.
- EVs primarily consist of bikes and three-wheelers growing in popularity.
- Charging infrastructure supply has not kept pace with EV supply growth.
- Worldwide EV charging options are fixed charging stations and swappable batteries.
- Fixed charging systems use permanent docking systems set up in malls, homes, offices, or fast-charged depending on technology.
- Batteries are increasingly lithium-ion rather than lead acid due to lead acid draining faster after eight months.
- Saurabh Markandeya, Co-founder of Adarin (Bengaluru), states EVs running on Li-Ion batteries must operate in temperatures over 40 degrees Celsius, causing poor function in Indian summer cities.
- Markandeya notes charging infrastructure lacks a consistent national standard.
- Adarin manufactures motors, electric drivetrains, and batteries.
- Fast charging infrastructure reduces battery life from five years to three years.
- Adarin built fast charging solutions in India that do not reduce battery life.
- Mahindra & Mahindra announced a partnership with SmartE to launch 100,000 electric three-wheelers by 2022.
- Delhi-NCR-based SmartE already has charging infrastructure for 800 vehicles and plans expansion.
- Mahindra & Mahindra is releasing the Treo range of electric two-wheelers.
- Tata AutoComp Systems (subsidiary of Tata Group) signed an MoU with Australia-based Tritium to bring 50kW DC fast chargers to India for all EV types.
- ABB, Sun Mobility, and Hero Future Energies are in advanced stages of setting up EV infrastructure.
- Central government's FAME scheme allocated close to Rs 1,500 crore for 2019-20, Rs 5,000 crore for 2020-21, and Rs 3,500 crore for 2021-22.
- Of the FAME allocation, Rs 1,000 crore is allocated for setting up EV charging stations in India.
- FAME policy proposes establishing 2,700 charging stations along highways at intervals of about 25 km.
- Close to 278,000 EVs are on India’s roads, mostly two-wheelers.
- Private charging stations will be permitted at residences with DISCOMs facilitating them.
- Setting up public charging stations (PCS) is a de-licensed activity open to individuals meeting government technical and performance standards.
- DISCOM licensees must prioritize providing connectivity for PCS applications.
- Charging stations may obtain electricity from any generation company via open-access protocols.
- Guidelines provide minimum infrastructure requirements for charging stations for light and heavy vehicles.
- Bengaluru has 32 charging stations built by Ather Energy alone.
- Ather Energy plans to add more stations in Bengaluru this year and expand to four cities soon.
- Ather Energy manufactures/sells electric scooters and makes its charging infrastructure available for other e-bikes and cars.
- Tork is readying commercial launch later this year (2019) in Pune.
- Tork's bike reaches 100 km per hour with a range of 100 kilometres on a single charge.
- Kapil Shelke, Founder of Tork Motors, states the company will set up its own charging infrastructure starting in Pune.
- Fixed charging infrastructure setup costs Rs 1 lakh to Rs 4 lakh for businesses, per draft policy specifications.
- Swappable batteries remove battery cost from vehicle ownership, charging only for used range.
- SUN Mobility (Chetan Maini-led, Bengaluru startup) is setting up its own charging infrastructure and plans 100 EV charging stations in the first phase this financial year.
- SUN Mobility supports two-wheelers and three-wheelers with long-term plans for cars and buses.
- SUN Mobility announced a partnership with SmartE to deploy swappable batteries at SmartE Park and Charge Hubs across Delhi-NCR.
- SUN Mobility conducted trials in Gurugram last year and currently supports 500 electric three-wheelers in Phase 1.
- Goldie Srivastava, Co-Founder and CEO of SmartE, states the partnership underscores commitment to easy, safe, affordable, eco-friendly last-mile commute.
- Mumbai-based Magenta Power will use solar power to proliferate charging stations across India.
- Magenta aims to set up over 450 charging stations by next year from current 32.
- Magenta set up a solar power-based charging station for EVs on the Mumbai-Pune highway.
- GoGreenBov is readying to sell 10,000 electric scooters soon.
- Dhivik Ashok, Founder of GGBov, believes B2B industries should place EVs close to fixed infrastructure.
- GGBov plans to set up fixed charging infrastructure at offices and later homes for efficiency and cost benefits.
- GGBov has a manufacturing plant in Kolar with capacity to make 10,000 vehicles a year, expandable per FY 2020 growth.
- India's EV charging infrastructure area currently has no subsidies available.
- India must figure out what works for citizens and create impact beyond just launching new EV types.
