# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-06

**Source:** [Volvo Group Achieves Energy Efficiency - FINCHANNEL](https://finchannel.com/volvo-group-achieves-energy-efficiency/45070/oil-auto/2015/06/)  ·  `page_014210`

## Facts

- Volvo Group North America achieved its goal of reduced energy consumption in the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Better Buildings, Better Plants Challenge five years earlier than anticipated.
- Volvo Group’s goal had been a 25 percent reduction in energy consumption at its eight U.S. manufacturing plants between 2009 and 2020.
- By the end of 2014, Volvo Group had reduced energy consumption by 26.8 percent compared with its 2009 baseline.
- Volvo Group was one of only 11 companies to meet its goal early.
- Rick Robinson serves as the director of health, safety and environment for the Volvo Group North America.
- Rick Robinson stated that environmental care is one of the Volvo Group’s core values and that reaching the milestone required the diligence and dedication of all employees.
- The eight U.S. manufacturing facilities include: Volvo Trucks in Dublin, Virginia; Volvo Group Powertrain in Hagerstown, Maryland; Mack Trucks in Macungie, Pennsylvania; Volvo Construction Equipment in Shippensburg, Pennsylvania; Volvo Penta in Lexington, Tennessee; Volvo Bus in Plattsburgh, New York; Volvo Group Remanufacturing in Charlotte, North Carolina; and Volvo Group Remanufacturing in Middletown, Pennsylvania.
- Energy Secretary Ernest Moniz commented that as the Better Buildings Initiative enters its fourth year, leaders continue to showcase how saving energy saves money, creates jobs, accelerates the nation’s competitiveness in the clean energy economy, and preserves the environment.
- Three of Volvo Group’s manufacturing sites – Macungie, New River Valley, and Hagerstown – held the top three positions in the DOE’s Superior Energy Performance program among Platinum level partners at the time.
- Since the DOE launched the Better Buildings Challenge in 2011, more than 250 partners have saved $840 million and saved 94 TBTUs of energy, representing six million tons of greenhouse gas emissions.
- The Volvo Group is one of the world’s leading manufacturers of trucks, buses, construction equipment, and marine and industrial engines.
- The company employs about 100,000 people globally.
- The Volvo Group has production facilities in 19 countries and sells its products in more than 190 markets.
- In 2014, the Volvo Group’s sales amounted to about $38.2 billion.
- The Volvo Group is a publicly-held company headquartered in Gothenburg, Sweden.
- Volvo shares are listed on Nasdaq Stockholm and are traded OTC in the U.S.
