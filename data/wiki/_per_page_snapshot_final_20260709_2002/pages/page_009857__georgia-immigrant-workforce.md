# Georgia Immigrant Workforce

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [What you need to know about the Georgia economy and immigrant workers - Georgia Budget and Policy Institute](https://gbpi.org/what-you-need-to-know-about-the-georgia-economy-and-immigrant-workers/)  ·  `page_009857`

## Facts

- 1.3 million immigrants reside in Georgia, comprising 11.6% of the total population in the state.
- 49% of immigrants in Georgia are naturalized citizens.
- 653,000 U.S.-born Georgians live with at least one immigrant parent, accounting for 6.7% of the total U.S.-born resident population in the state.
- Immigrant workers fill 15% of all jobs in Georgia and contribute 14.4% of the state’s economic output.
- Manufacturing accounts for 17% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Construction accounts for 27% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Agriculture, forestry, fishing, and hunting account for 19% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Other services account for 19% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Professional and business services account for 17% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Manicurists and pedicurists account for 78% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Taxi drivers account for 43% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Construction laborers account for 41% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Carpenters account for 40% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- Software developers account for 40% of immigrant workers in Georgia.
- 108,000 immigrants in Georgia are entrepreneurs, representing 19.9% of the state’s total entrepreneurs.
- Immigrant household income in Georgia totals $52.4 billion, which is funneled directly back into the Georgia economy through consumer spending.
- Deporting Georgian workers would rob city and state coffers of nearly $929 million in tax revenue, making it more difficult to provide basic services to all residents.
- Pursuing mass deportation would tank economic growth, lead to massive job losses, disrupt key industries, spike inflation, separate families, and create a culture of fear in workplaces and communities.
- The fact sheet was developed with the Economic Policy Institute (EPI) and the Latino Community Fund (LCF Georgia).
- EPI is a nonprofit, nonpartisan think tank working to counter rising inequality, low wages, weak benefits for working people, slower economic growth, unacceptable employment conditions, and a widening racial wage gap.
- LCF Georgia is a 501(c)3 organization supporting Latino-serving organizations and families with advocacy, capacity building, program development, and collective investments to act as a catalyst for investment and positive narrative within the Latinx/Hispanic Community in Georgia.
