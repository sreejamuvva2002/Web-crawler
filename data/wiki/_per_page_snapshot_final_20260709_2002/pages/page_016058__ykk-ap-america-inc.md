# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. is an innovation-driven manufacturer of commercial façade systems and residential windows and doors.
- The company operates a subsidiary named ERIE Architectural Products.
- Founder Tadao Yoshida established the “Cycle of Goodness” philosophy as the central tenet of all business activities.
- The company adheres to 25 Fundamental Behaviors to operationalize its corporate values.
- YKK AP designs and manufactures high-quality fenestration solutions for commercial and residential markets.
- Products are utilized on buildings across the United States.
- The STUDIO 101 Design Center provides hands-on product demonstrations and training events, with locations in downtown Atlanta, GA and Rancho Cucamonga, CA.
- YKK AP Inc. (Japan) focuses on Architectural Products including windows, doors, curtain walls, and exterior products.
- Open positions are available in sales, estimating, customer service, marketing, application/design/mechanical engineering, operations/production, and business support areas like IT, HR, finance, and purchasing.
- YKK AP America Inc. is the North American arm of the global YKK AP Inc. (Japan).
- Products are proudly made in the USA and Canada.
- The company’s philosophy centers on creating mutual benefit for customers, partners, employees, and society, guided by Tadao Yoshida’s principle that “no one prospers without rendering benefit to others.”
- Sustainability is integrated into responsible sourcing, transparent manufacturing, fair employee treatment, and customer value delivery.
- The company aligns with the YKK AP Group’s Evolution 2030 global initiative and the YKK Group Environmental Vision 2050 to reduce carbon intensity and environmental footprint.
- YKK AP controls every stage of manufacturing from billet to finished product across ISO 14001-certified facilities in the U.S. and Canada.
- The company produces its own aluminum billets, cuts its own dies, operates multiple extrusion lines, and applies advanced anodizing, painting, and thermal break technologies, including one of the industry’s only vertical anodizing lines in the U.S.
- YKK AP introduced BetterBillet™, a proprietary aluminum billet made from recycled scrap and low-carbon primary aluminum, integrated into most commercial products.
- To support Western U.S. demand, YKK AP established the Los Angeles Sales and Manufacturing Center in Rancho Cucamonga, California, adding engineering support.
- A 400,000-square-foot greenfield facility manufactures residential windows and doors for homebuilders and homeowners throughout the Southeast, integrating vinyl extrusion, fabrication, and glazing under one roof.
- Tim Thomas was promoted to President of Erie Architectural Products, leveraging over 38 years in the glass and glazing industry.
- A new showroom dubbed STUDIO 101 was opened at the downtown Atlanta HQ facility, housing corporate staff, engineering, and a training center for product demos.
- A second regional manufacturing center in Cincinnati, Ohio, opened in 2019 in over 100,000 square feet to increase local production capabilities and finished goods inventory for the Midwest.
- Pearson advanced from a temp agency receptionist to a top sales representative, becoming the first female recipient of the company’s President’s Award.
- In 2019, YKK AP acquired high-performance facades solution provider Erie Architectural Products to grow its offering for prefabricated, pre-assembled products.
- In 2018, the company appointed its first female vice president, who created an organizational excellence department focused on employee engagement, talent acquisition, and human capital technology.
- In 2017, YKK AP led a local business challenge to complete Martin Luther King, Jr. Monument Park in Dublin, GA, and recognized Martin Luther King, Jr. Day as a paid company holiday.
- Oliver Stepe was promoted to President of YKK AP America Inc. in 2016 after joining in 1992; he appointed the company’s first minority director and expanded business to the west coast.
- In 2015, YKK AP became the first fenestration manufacturer to obtain third-party certified environmental product declarations for its entire portfolio, offering seven categories meeting LEED v4 requirements.
- Also in 2015, a direct freight program was launched to reduce damage and increase scheduling predictability for commercial customers coast-to-coast.
- In July 2014, the company opened its first regional manufacturing facility in Coppell, Texas, a 105,000-square-foot site serving as a key supply and distribution center for south central and western regions.
- In 2013, YKK AP debuted the “I am an Architect” video series at AIA Denver, which gained two million views and built an AEC Facebook community of over 64 thousand people.
- The company hosted its first “Chillin’ with YKK AP” event in Las Vegas to build relationships with customers and industry partners.
- In 2011, at AIA New Orleans, the company demonstrated ProTek® impact-resistant glazing systems by having pitcher Trey Shields throw pitches at an estimated 80 mph.
- In 2010, YKK AP debuted myThermalAssistant, a thermal performance calculator enabling building professionals to calculate energy-saving metrics using AAMA 507 standard based on NFRC methods.
- In 2009, the company launched enerGfacade, the industry’s first energy-efficient line of framing systems under a single brand name, using patented technologies for advanced thermal performance.
- In 2008, ThermaShade® Sun Control system was launched, winning an IDEA design award in 2009, featuring ease-of-installation with a hanger bolt and slotted receptor design.
- In 2007, the company diversified into another market and began plans for a vinyl window manufacturing facility in Macon, GA, which remains the hub of its residential business.
- In 2006, YKK AP held a groundbreaking ceremony for phase 1 of an $80M expansion of its Dublin plant, opening nearly 200 jobs, and was named one of the industry’s top ten employers by US Glass Magazine.
- In 2005, YKK AP officially entered the residential market with the first shipment of vinyl windows to Quality Craft, Inc.
- In 2004, YKK AP achieved $100 million in sales for the first time.
- The Dublin commercial manufacturing plant is ISO 14001 certified and maintains a 73% recycling rate.
- In 2002, a grand opening was held for a new fabrication plant next to the Dublin manufacturing plant to increase capacity for commercial entrances and windows.
- In 2001, YKK AP began attending national trade shows starting with NGA (now Glassbuild) and has frequently won Best In Show at AIA Conference, including in 2015.
- In 2000, the company received the Region 9 Existing Industry Award from the Georgia Economic Developers Association for exceptional community and state contributions.
- In 1999, YKK AP released the first version of Ycalc® estimating software, which evolved into Ycalc® X5 powered by Logikal® for structural design, shop drawings, material optimization, and fabrication utilities.
- In 1998, YKK AP partnered with the City of Dublin, GA to establish a “Sister Cities” relationship and student exchange program with Osaki Japan, alongside supporting initiatives like Dublin FIRST Robotics Competition and river cleanups.
- In 1997, YKK AP was granted a patent for its ProTek® entrance design’s water resistance, later expanding the line into blast mitigation; additional patents cover StyleView® residential window sash and ThermaShade® Sunshades.
- In 1996, the company opened its Cincinnati branch (sixth satellite location) and expanded the Dublin manufacturing facility by 100,000 square feet; it now operates ten branches coast-to-coast.
- In 1995, YKK AP launched the “Project Center” team to support medium to large-scale projects with estimating, engineering, and project management services.
- Following Hurricane Andrew, YKK AP developed ProTek®, a complete integrated entrance and framing solution for impact resistance, later expanded into blast mitigation.
- The company employs Anodized Plus®, a unique vertical anodizing process meeting AAMA 612 standards for enhanced durability.
- The Dublin, GA manufacturing plant opened in September 1992 as a vertically integrated operation handling melting, casting, die manufacturing, extrusion, finishing, fabrication, and shipping.
- YKK AP entered the U.S. market with aluminum products from YKK AP Japan in 1984, opened an Atlanta branch in 1986, and incorporated YKK AP America Inc. on April 24, 1991, choosing Dublin, GA as its manufacturing base.
- High-performance aluminum facade, window, and door systems are supported by design tools and technical expertise for architects and glaziers.
- Premium residential windows and patio doors serve the Southeastern U.S. through a trusted dealer network for homebuilders and homeowners.
- Erie AP delivers custom unitized curtain wall solutions via its ENVIRO|FACADES® line, combining design assist, performance testing, and advanced manufacturing.
