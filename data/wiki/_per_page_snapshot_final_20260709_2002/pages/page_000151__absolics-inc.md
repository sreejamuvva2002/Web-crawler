# Absolics, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-01

**Source:** [Absolics Breaks Ground On Planned $600M Investment | News | SK](https://eng.sk.com/news/absolics-breaks-ground-on-planned-600-million-manufacturing-site-in-georgia-for-breakthrough-semiconductor-material)  ·  `page_000151`

## Facts

- Absolics, Inc., a subsidiary of SKC Co. Ltd., broke ground on November 1, 2022, on a planned $600 million manufacturing facility in Covington, Ga.
- The investment increases total planned investment plans to more than $600 million, up from the $473 million originally announced in October 2021.
- SKC and Absolics raised the investment projections based on the need for more advanced manufacturing equipment to meet anticipated demand.
- The facility will create more than 400 high-skilled jobs in Georgia.
- Phase 1 is planned for completion by the end of 2023, representing a $240 million investment expected to create 140 jobs and focus on small-volume manufacturing.
- Mass production at the facility is expected to start in the second quarter of 2024.
- Phase 2 is expected to be an additional $360 million investment that creates another 270 jobs and ramps up to high-volume manufacturing, anticipated to be completed over the next 3-5 years.
- The Georgia site will manufacture a glass substrate, which is a thin layer of glass on which processing and memory chips can be mounted together to create the brains of a computing system.
- The glass substrate reduces the space required for a multi-chip package, allowing more chips to be packed into a single device.
- The material is considered a breakthrough because it can significantly increase the performance and energy efficiency of chipsets.
- SKC and Absolics originally developed the technology as part of a research consortium with the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- Absolics is expected to be the first in the world to mass produce the glass substrate material.
- The groundbreaking ceremony was attended by officials including Senator Jon Ossoff and Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- Dr. Woncheol Park, President and Chief Executive Officer of SKC, stated that Absolics will be an integral part of the semiconductor ecosystem in the U.S. and expressed pleasure at building a factory to create hundreds of jobs impacting Covington and Georgia.
- Senator Jon Ossoff stated he is working every day to bring jobs and business to Georgia, noting the plant will create Georgia jobs, support U.S. national security, and bring advanced manufacturing to the state.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted the development as an opportunity to grow the semiconductor industry in Georgia and the U.S., noting it came about through research at Georgia Tech and praising the long-term relationship between Georgia and SK Group.
- Absolics was created last year by SKC, a majority owner of the new business.
- The inaugural product will be a thin piece of glass, approximately 3-by-3 inches, used to package different semiconductor components together.
- Processing, memory, and logic chips can be mounted next to each other on the glass to create the core of a computing system.
- The new glass material replaces the standard silicon imposer on a plastic substrate, simplifying manufacturing and reducing package thickness by almost half.
- Multi-layer ceramic capacitors (MLCC) can be embedded in the glass substrate, freeing more surface space for chips.
- Absolics’ product allows larger processors and up to four times more chips to be mounted in the same space compared to standard packaging.
- The energy efficiency of the glass substrate reduces overall power consumption by up to 50 percent, driving down costs and environmental impact at scale in large data centers.
- Absolics expects Phase 1 to have an annual production capacity of 12,000 m2.
- Phase 2 is expected to ramp up to an annual production capacity of 72,000 m2.
- The investment and facility are part of SK Group’s broader commitment to increase U.S. investments and assets to more than $50 billion by the end of 2025, focusing on clean energy, semiconductors, and life sciences.
- Earlier in 2022, SK Siltron CSS opened a new facility in Michigan as part of a $300 million investment to increase manufacturing capacity for semiconductor wafers used in EV components.
- SK On is investing $2.6 billion in a site in Commerce, Ga., to make batteries for electric vehicles.
- SKC was founded in 1976 and established a presence in Georgia in 1996, growing into one of the largest manufacturers of polyester films (PET film) in North America.
- SKC’s Semiconductor Material Business develops and manufactures semiconductors and specialty materials, including chemical mechanical polishing (CMP) pads and slurry.
- SKC is securing future growth drivers by producing copper foil, an essential material for electric vehicle (EV) battery anodes.
- SK Group, South Korea’s second-largest conglomerate, drives innovations in semiconductors, sustainable energy, telecommunications, and life sciences.
- SK Group is based in Seoul and invests globally with a commitment to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and increasing renewable energy use.
- SK companies combined have $139 billion in global annual revenue and employ more than 100,000 people worldwide.
- SK companies are investing billions of dollars in expanding their U.S. presence through operations or partnerships in hydrogen energy, fuel cells, EV battery manufacturing, energy storage, pharmaceuticals, semiconductors, and advanced materials.
