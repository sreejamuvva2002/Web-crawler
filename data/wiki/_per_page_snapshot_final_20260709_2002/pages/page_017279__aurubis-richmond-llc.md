# Aurubis Richmond, LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-22

**Source:** [Large Augusta manufacturer completes phase one; 80 more jobs to come - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/large-augusta-manufacturer-completes-phase-one-80-more-jobs-to-come/)  ·  `page_017279`

## Facts

- The $800 million Aurubis Richmond, LLC plant in the Augusta Corporate Park officially joined the Richmond County business community with a ribbon-cutting ceremony marking the completion of phase one.
- Construction on the project has been underway since 2022.
- Toralf Haag, CEO of Aurubis AG, described the ribbon-cutting as a landmark day celebrating professionals who worked together to achieve the accomplishment.
- Aurubis has spent more than 150 years developing technology and skills as Europe’s leading producer and one of the world’s largest power recyclers.
- The plant will recycle copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials.
- The facility will be part of the growing electric mobility chain in Georgia.
- The company will produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, stated the facility will process 90,000 tons of recycling materials every year during phase one.
- Phase two of the project is already under construction.
- With phase two complete, the facility will have the capacity to process over 180,000 tons of materials annually.
- Aurubis invested in training and intends to bring more than 230 direct jobs to the Augusta area.
- The facility already has 150 staff on site.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp (R) participated in the 2022 groundbreaking ceremony and returned for the ribbon-cutting to mark the plant's opening.
- Gov. Kemp stated that projects like this highlight assets making Georgia the number one state to do business for the 11th year in a row, per an Area Development Magazine announcement made a week earlier.
- Since taking office over five and a half years ago, Gov. Kemp announced 3,000 new jobs and $2.36 billion in investment in Augusta and Richmond County.
- Over the same period, the broader region of the state saw over 6,300 new jobs and over $3.2 billion in investment from projects the state worked on.
- These announced figures do not count organic growth happening daily in local communities with local chambers and economic developers.
- Inge Hofkens, COO of Multimetal Recycling at Aurubis AG, noted that economic factors over the last two years were heavily influenced by several challenges but emphasized the company's clear vision and strong will to see the project reach full potential.
- Hofkens summarized the company's commitment to Richmond County by stating they are here to stay, fully committed, and this is only the beginning.
