# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-15

**Source:** [The future of electric cars in Coweta | Local News | times-herald.com](https://www.times-herald.com/news/local/the-future-of-electric-cars-in-coweta/article_ac0b2543-60d5-5d77-a0d2-00c0bc0686d2.html)  ·  `page_000833`

## Facts

- In 2018, 6,000 electric vehicles were sold in Georgia, an increase of 147 percent compared to the prior year.
- The majority of EV charging stations are located in dense, urban areas like Atlanta where there are hundreds of stations dotting the streets.
- EV charging stations are also popping up in more rural areas like Coweta County.
- Newnan currently has 16 EV charging stations, with 13 of them providing a free charge.
- Drivers can find EV charging stations by City Hall, Carl Miller Park, and the Target off Bullsboro Drive.
- Other cities in Coweta County don’t have any EV charging stations yet.
- Most charging stations are located near major highways, interstates or throughout urban areas, according to a 2019 report by the Center for Rural Affairs.
- The Center for Rural Affairs stated that the lack of charging stations throughout many rural areas restricts the viability of electric vehicles for people who live in rural communities or tourists who wish to visit.
- The Center for Rural Affairs stated that any plans to expand charging infrastructure must include rural areas.
- A Jan. 7 state news release stated that Georgia is committed to strengthening its position as a leader in the EV market with support of cutting-edge innovations emerging out of universities, a highly skilled workforce, and a relationship approach to economic development.
- International companies SK Innovations, TEKLAS, and GEDIA Automotive Group have partnered with the Georgia Department of Economic Development to invest in EV supply chain-related production in Georgia.
- Georgia companies and universities are researching projects including improving driving range, increasing recharging speeds, enhancing connectivity systems, improving AI, and adding nanotech materials into production.
- Coweta-Fayette EMC provides a Drive Free for A Year Plug-in Electric Vehicle Program where qualified members who lease or purchase a new plug-in 100 percent EV on or after April 22, 2019 may qualify for a monthly credit on their power bill to cover the cost of charging the EV for one year.
- The Coweta-Fayette EMC website states that today’s EVs offer longer driving ranges, have extended warranties, and can use charging points located along major roadways, places of employment, shopping, and entertainment.
- General Motors is planning to become an all-electric vehicle company by 2035.
- GM plans to release two Chevrolet Bolt models starting under $34,000 this summer.
- GM will release an electric version of their iconic Hummer amounting to $113,000 later this year.
- Volkswagen plans to build 28 million electric vehicles by 2028.
- Volkswagen Group is investing nearly $37 into its electric car program.
- The Yale School of Environment predicts that if the private sector and the U.S. government make a serious commitment to EVs, the majority of new automobiles sold in the U.S. will be electric within the next two decades.
- A Yale article claims that a more extensive nationwide network of charging stations and improved EV batteries are needed before EVs become more prevalent.
- Trae Westmoreland with the Coweta County Development Authority stated that Coweta has been a finalist for several potential battery manufacturers.
- Westmoreland stated that Coweta is getting more looks from the industry and has the attributes that fit the model for what they are trying to do.
- Westmoreland stated that Coweta has good sites, workforce, and skill set, with local employees well positioned.
- Westmoreland stated that through the German apprenticeship model, Coweta is a hub for advanced manufacturing.
- Westmoreland stated that when someone needs 400 acres within driving distance of Atlanta and the airport, Coweta is really the only game in town right now.
- LG Batteries was the last company to seriously consider Coweta County but is likely to set up shop in Tennessee due to General Motors’ desire to utilize an incentive package provided by the state.
