# State Electric Vehicle Tax Credits & Rebates Guide

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [List of State Electric Vehicle Tax Credits & Rebates in 2025 & 2026](https://20somethingfinance.com/electric-hybrid-tax-credits-by-state/)  ·  `page_015993`

## Facts

- The federal electric clean vehicle tax credits were prematurely ended in 2025 with OBBBA legislation, making any EV/PHEV purchased after 9/30/25 ineligible for the federal credit.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (signed in 2022 and effective in 2023) renamed PHEV and electric vehicle federal tax credits to the "Clean Vehicle Tax Credit".
- Major vehicle requirements for federal credits include a battery capacity of at least 7 kilowatt hours, manufacture by a qualified manufacturer, final assembly in North America, meeting battery and minerals sourcing criteria, and a maximum MSRP of $80,000 for vans/SUVs/trucks or $55,000 for other vehicles.
- The Inflation Reduction Act removed automaker vehicles-sold volume phaseouts and added taxpayer modified adjusted gross income (MAGI) eligibility caps.
- Used electric vehicles became potentially eligible for up to a $4,000 tax credit under the new federal rules.
- Starting in 2024, dealerships were authorized to offer the value of the federal tax credit up front to consumers.
- A "Clean Commercial Vehicle Credit" was created, allowing auto lessors to pass along a $7,500 EV lease tax credit to consumer lessees.
- California proposed $200 million in new funds for EV tax credits in 2026 to backfill for the expired federal EV tax credits.
- Colorado extended its electric vehicle tax credits with a phaseout schedule: 2025 offers $3,500 on vehicles up to $80,000 plus an additional $2,500 if under $35,000 for both purchases and leases; 2026 reduces the base to $750 with the same $2,500 low-price add-on.
- Connecticut's Hydrogen and Electric Automobile Purchase Rebate (CHEAPR) program offers rebates for residents purchasing or leasing new eligible electric, fuel cell, or plug-in hybrid vehicles up to a maximum MSRP of $50,000.
- Connecticut expanded "Rebates+" credits are available for applicants residing in Environmental Justice communities, distressed municipalities, participating in qualifying income programs, or earning less than 300% of the Federal Poverty Level.
- Connecticut allocated its rebate funds on a first-come, first-served basis and lowered base amounts beginning on 8/1/25.
- Delaware offers electric vehicle cash rebates totaling up to $2,500 depending on vehicle type, requiring applications within 90 days of purchase.
- The Illinois Electric Vehicle Rebate Program, funded by the Illinois Environmental Protection Agency, provides rebates for residents purchasing new or used all-electric vehicles from licensed dealers.
- Illinois approved a funding round from 10/28/25 to 1/41/26 offering a $4,000 rebate for low-income applicants, $2,000 for non-low-income applicants, and $1,500 for all-electric motorcycles.
- Illinois vehicle rebates are capped at vehicles with a selling price not exceeding $80,000.
- Maine suspended electric vehicle rebates starting November 16, 2024, except for low-income residents, though previously listed enhanced rebates included $7,500 for new EVs, $3,000 for new PHEVs, and $2,500 for used EVs/PHEVs.
- Maryland offered an excise tax credit of $3,000 on vehicles $50,000 and less through 2027 for qualifying zero-emission plug-in or fuel cell vehicles, though funding appeared inactive as of early 2026.
- Massachusetts MOR-EV program is funded through 2027 and offers rebates for new or used electric and fuel-cell vehicles.
- Massachusetts provides a $3,500 rebate for new vehicle purchases/leases under $55,000, an additional $1,500 (MOR-EV+) for below-specified income levels, and an extra $1,000 for trading in a vehicle at least 12 years old.
- New Jersey offers a $1,500 "Charge Up" point-of-sale incentive on eligible EVs and a $4,000 pre-approved "Charge Up+" incentive for below-specified income levels.
- New Jersey also provides a $250 in-home EV charger incentive.
- New York offers tax rebates for new electric vehicles based on range ($2,000 for >200 miles, $1,000 for 40-199 miles, $500 for <40 miles) and an additional $500 rebate for MSRPs greater than $42,000.
- Oregon's "Charge Ahead Rebate" provided up to $7,500 for new EVs and $5,000 for used EVs but was suspended on 9/9/25, with new funding anticipated in 2026.
- Pennsylvania offers EV rebates for new and used vehicles with a final purchase price of $45,000 or less: $3,000 for battery electric vehicles, $1,500 for plug-in hybrids, and $500 for other alternative fuel/electric motorcycles.
- Pennsylvania limits household income to 300% of the federal poverty level for standard rebates, with an additional $1,000 available for lower-income applicants meeting the 150% FPL threshold.
- Rhode Island's DRIVE EV Rebate program offers up to $3,000 for new BEVs/fuel-cell vehicles and up to $2,000 for new PHEVs (under $75,000), plus up to $2,500 for used BEVs/fuel-cell and up to $1,750 for used PHEVs (under $55,000).
- Rhode Island provides rebates of up to $800 or 100% of the purchase price for Level 2 Residential EV Chargers, and up to $1,000 or 50% of installation costs, with higher amounts for income-qualified applicants.
- Rhode Island offers an additional rebate of up to $1,500 for applicants in state or federal income-qualified programs (DRIVE+), awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.
- Federal energy tax credits and rebates related to solar panels, electrical panels, battery storage, and EV charging were updated via the Inflation Reduction Act for 2025 but were mostly ended prematurely with OBBBA legislation for 2026 and beyond.
