# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Georgia Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, LGES to spend $2 billion more on Georgia battery plant - 매일경제 영문뉴스 펄스(Pulse)](https://pulse.mk.co.kr/news/all/10819952)  ·  `page_056215`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) announced plans to increase their joint investment in a battery manufacturing facility located in Bryan County, Georgia by an additional $2 billion.
- The joint venture aims to strengthen the production capabilities of electric vehicle (EV) batteries and create more job opportunities in the region.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp expressed his gratitude to Hyundai Motor Group and LGES for their investment in the state.
- The announcement brings the companies’ total investment to $7.59 billion in the electric vehicle manufacturing facility and battery joint venture in the region.
- The project will create 8,500 new jobs over the next eight years, according to the governor's press release.
- José Muñoz, president and global chief operating officer of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that the incremental investment in Bryan County reflects their continued commitment to creating a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- David Kim, head of Advanced Automotive Battery Division at LGES, stated that the investment demonstrates their commitment to working with Hyundai Motor Group to lead electrification in the U.S. while strengthening the local economy by creating quality jobs.
- Back in May, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES announced their joint venture plant in Georgia to supply EV batteries for the North American market.
- At the time of the initial announcement, the companies stated they would invest $4.32 billion by 2028 to build a battery cell production plant to cover about 300,000 EVs per year.
