# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech Sign MOU to Further Collaborate on Hydrogen Mobility Development](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-motor-group-and-georgia-tech-sign-mou-to-further-collaborate-on-hydrogen-mobility-development-302756387.html)  ·  `page_002790`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Institute of Technology announced an expansion of their collaboration to advance hydrogen-powered transportation, deepen applied research and education, and accelerate zero-emissions vehicles in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor North America is donating four NEXO fuel cell electric SUVs exclusively for campus use.
- Hyundai Motor Group is donating a hydrogen electrolyzer project exclusively for campus use.
- The partnership brings NEXO hydrogen fuel cell SUVs and fueling infrastructure to the Georgia Tech campus, positioning it as one of the nation's most prominent campus-based examples for hydrogen mobility.
- The hydrogen electrolyzer will be installed at Georgia Tech's North Avenue Research Area (NARA).
- The donated vehicles and infrastructure will support campus operations and interdisciplinary research across engineering, sustainability, energy systems, and public policy.
- Engineering research will focus on exploring hydrogen-based systems and mobility solutions.
- Sustainability research will assess the environmental benefits of hydrogen technologies.
- Energy systems research will understand the integration of hydrogen fuel cells into current infrastructure.
- Public policy research will evaluate the regulatory and social implications of hydrogen adoption.
- The university campus will serve as a living laboratory for clean transportation technologies.
- Faculty and students will study the real-world performance of hydrogen technology, infrastructure requirements for large-scale deployment, and environmental impacts of hydrogen energy systems.
- Insights from the initiative aim to inform and accelerate the widespread use of hydrogen technology in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The initiative supports Georgia Tech's strategic plan goal of expanding the use of zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Beyond hydrogen energy, the partnership aims to advance innovation in autonomous driving, electric vehicle batteries, charging infrastructure, materials science, and cybersecurity.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is located in Georgia.
- HTWO Logistics operates as a clean logistics partnership running Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks in logistics operations near Savannah.
- The collaboration reinforces the link between education, research, and Hyundai's long-term goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2045.
- The partnership aims to generate workforce talent and position Georgia as a national leader in hydrogen and zero-emissions transportation.
- Additional announcements regarding the partnership's research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
- Ken Ramírez, Executive Vice President and Head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group, stated that combining university research with Hyundai's global experience creates a foundation for real-world solutions to drive the energy transition.
- Ken Ramírez is a Georgia Tech alumnus (class of 1991) and currently serves on the Georgia Tech Advisory Board.
- Randy Parker, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, stated that donating NEXO fuel cell SUVs will expand the campus clean mobility footprint and provide real-world experiences with zero-emissions transportation technology.
- Ángel Cabrera, President of the Georgia Institute of Technology, stated that expanding work with Hyundai advances hydrogen research, reduces campus emissions, and strengthens Georgia's role in the future of clean mobility.
- Georgia Tech has more than 57,000 undergraduate and graduate students representing 54 U.S. states and territories and more than 146 countries.
- Georgia Tech conducts more than $1 billion in research annually for government, industry, and society.
- Hyundai Motor Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide and its mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
