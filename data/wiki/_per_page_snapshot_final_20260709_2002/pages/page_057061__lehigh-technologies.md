# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-19

**Source:** [Michelin Acquires Specialty Materials Company Lehigh Technologies](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/michelin-acquires-specialty-materials-company-lehigh-technologies/)  ·  `page_057061`

## Facts

- Michelin announced it has acquired Lehigh Technologies, a specialty materials company that uses patented cryogenic turbo mill technology to transform rubber from end-of-life tires and industrial goods into materials for new tires and other products.
- The acquisition reduces the amount of raw materials initially needed, such as elastomers and fillers from oil- and rubber-based sources.
- Pete Selleck, chairman and president of Michelin North America, stated that the company is always looking for ways to achieve safer and more sustainable mobility by using high-technology recycled materials without compromising safety or performance while consuming less finite natural resources.
- Christophe Rahier, senior vice president of strategic planning materials for the Michelin Group, said the acquisition demonstrates Michelin’s strategic intent to extend its materials expertise beyond tires and foster advanced rubber recycled materials in tire and non-tire industries.
- Lehigh Technologies is based in Tucker, Georgia, near Atlanta.
- Lehigh Technologies employs about 100 people.
- The firm produces highly engineered, versatile raw materials called micronized rubber powder (MRP).
- MRP is a low-cost, high-performance sustainable material that can be used as a substitute for other oil- and rubber-based materials used in manufacturing tires, plastics, asphalt and construction materials.
- Lehigh’s customers include some of the largest tire companies in the world, as well as companies in construction materials, asphalt modification and other markets.
- Alan Barton, CEO of Lehigh Technologies, stated that the deal provides an incredible opportunity to continue growth with the full backing of Michelin to expand capabilities, execute a global growth strategy and pursue new market opportunities.
- Michelin completed the acquisition on Oct. 13.
